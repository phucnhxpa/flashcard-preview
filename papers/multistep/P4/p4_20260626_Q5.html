Q5 · Vector lines intersection in 3D

Question

The lines $L_1$ and $L_2$ are given by

$L_1:\; \mathbf{r}_1 = (2,\, -1,\, 3) + \lambda(1,\, 4,\, 2)$

$L_2:\; \mathbf{r}_2 = (-1,\, \beta,\, 6) + \mu(2,\, -1,\, 7)$

where $\beta$ is a constant.

Given that $L_1$ and $L_2$ intersect at the point $P$, (a) find the value of $\beta$, and (b) find the coordinates of $P$.

[8 marks total: (a) 5 marks, (b) 3 marks]

3D vector lines L₁ and L₂ intersecting at P(−7, −37, −15) with β = −40

(a) Find the value of $\beta$ [5 marks] · 5 steps

At the intersection $P$, $\mathbf{r}_1 = \mathbf{r}_2$. Equating the three components gives three scalar equations in $\lambda$, $\mu$, and $\beta$. Pick any two to solve for $\lambda$ and $\mu$; use the third to find $\beta$ and as a consistency check.
STEP 1/5equate components

Strategy. Set $\mathbf{r}_1 = \mathbf{r}_2$ and equate the $x$-, $y$-, $z$-components. Number each equation — Edexcel awards a mark for the setup.

Three scalar equations

$$(1)\quad 2 + \lambda = -1 + 2\mu$$ $$(2)\quad -1 + 4\lambda = \beta - \mu$$ $$(3)\quad 3 + 2\lambda = 6 + 7\mu$$
Trap (exam technique). Not numbering simultaneous equations when asked to "solve" loses a method mark. Always label equations (1), (2), (3) on the page.

Sense check. Equation (1) links $\lambda$ and $\mu$ (no $\beta$). Equation (3) also links $\lambda$ and $\mu$ (no $\beta$). Equation (2) contains $\beta$ — so solve $\lambda, \mu$ from (1) and (3), then find $\beta$ from (2).

Three equations: (1) $\lambda - 2\mu = -3$; (2) $4\lambda + \mu = \beta + 1$; (3) $2\lambda - 7\mu = 3$.
↳ from STEP 1: (1) $\lambda - 2\mu = -3$; (3) $2\lambda - 7\mu = 3$.
STEP 2/5rearrange for solver

Strategy. Rearrange equations (1) and (3) into the standard form for the calculator's simultaneous-equation solver: unknown-coefficient, unknown-coefficient, constant.

Rearrange

$$(1):\quad \lambda - 2\mu = -3.$$ $$(3):\quad 2\lambda - 7\mu = 3.$$
Calculator layout (lesson tip). Memorise the layout the calculator demands: $\lambda$-coefficient, $\mu$-coefficient, constant. Rearrange into that order BEFORE entering — this is where most sign errors happen. Even when calculator-solving is allowed, write the equations out and number them.

Sense check. (1): $\lambda - 2\mu = -3$ — coefficient of $\lambda$ is $+1$, $\mu$ is $-2$, constant $-3$. (3): $2\lambda - 7\mu = 3$ — coefficients $+2, -7$, constant $+3$.

Solver input: (1) $1, -2, -3$; (3) $2, -7, 3$.
↳ from STEP 2: solver input ready.
STEP 3/5solve for λ and μ

Strategy. From (1): $\lambda = 2\mu - 3$. Substitute into (3) and solve for $\mu$, then back-substitute to get $\lambda$.

From (1): $\lambda = 2\mu - 3$

$$\text{Sub into (3):}\quad 2(2\mu - 3) - 7\mu = 3.$$ $$4\mu - 6 - 7\mu = 3 \quad\Longrightarrow\quad -3\mu = 9 \quad\Longrightarrow\quad \mu = -3.$$

Back-substitute

$$\lambda = 2(-3) - 3 = -9.$$

Sense check. Plug $\lambda = -9, \mu = -3$ into (1): $-9 - 2(-3) = -9 + 6 = -3$. ✓ Into (3): $2(-9) - 7(-3) = -18 + 21 = 3$. ✓

$\lambda = -9$, $\mu = -3$.
↳ from STEP 3: $\lambda = -9$, $\mu = -3$.
STEP 4/5find β from equation (2)

Strategy. Use the second equation $-1 + 4\lambda = \beta - \mu$ with the now-known $\lambda$ and $\mu$ to solve for $\beta$.

Substitute into (2)

$$-1 + 4(-9) = \beta - (-3).$$ $$-1 - 36 = \beta + 3 \quad\Longrightarrow\quad -37 = \beta + 3.$$ $$\beta = -40.$$

Sense check. $4 \times (-9) = -36$; $-1 - 36 = -37$; $-37 - 3 = -40$. $\beta = -40$.

$\beta = -40$.
↳ from STEP 4: $\beta = -40$.
STEP 5/5verify consistency

Strategy. The third equation was used to find $\lambda$ and $\mu$, so verify that equation (2) with $\beta = -40$ is consistent. All three equations should agree.

Verify (2) with $\beta = -40$

$$-1 + 4(-9) = -40 - (-3) \;\Rightarrow\; -37 = -37.\;\checkmark$$
Consistency check. In 3-D, three component equations overdetermine the two parameters $\lambda, \mu$. If the third equation were NOT consistent, the lines would be skew (no intersection). The fact that all three agree confirms the lines do meet at a single point.

Sense check. If the lines were skew, we would get contradictory values of $\beta$ from different pairs of equations. Here $\beta = -40$ is unique and consistent.

$\boxed{\beta = -40}$ — confirmed by all three equations.

(b) Find the coordinates of $P$ [3 marks] · 3 steps

Substitute $\lambda = -9$ into $\mathbf{r}_1$ (or $\mu = -3$ into $\mathbf{r}_2$ — both give the same point). Write the final answer as coordinates $(x, y, z)$, NOT as a column vector.
STEP 1/3substitute λ = -9 into r₁

Strategy. Use $L_1$: $\mathbf{r}_1 = (2, -1, 3) + \lambda(1, 4, 2)$ with $\lambda = -9$.

Component-wise

$$x = 2 + (-9)(1) = 2 - 9 = -7.$$ $$y = -1 + (-9)(4) = -1 - 36 = -37.$$ $$z = 3 + (-9)(2) = 3 - 18 = -15.$$

Sense check. Each component is "base point + $\lambda \times$ direction". $x$: $2 + (-9) = -7$. $y$: $-1 + (-36) = -37$. $z$: $3 + (-18) = -15$.

$\mathbf{r}_1 = (-7,\, -37,\, -15)$.
↳ from STEP 1: $\mathbf{r}_1 = (-7, -37, -15)$.
STEP 2/3verify with r₂

Strategy. Cross-check by substituting $\mu = -3$ into $L_2$: $\mathbf{r}_2 = (-1, \beta, 6) + \mu(2, -1, 7)$ with $\beta = -40$.

Component-wise

$$x = -1 + (-3)(2) = -1 - 6 = -7.\;\checkmark$$ $$y = -40 + (-3)(-1) = -40 + 3 = -37.\;\checkmark$$ $$z = 6 + (-3)(7) = 6 - 21 = -15.\;\checkmark$$

Sense check. Both parametrisations give the same point $(-7, -37, -15)$. ✓ This confirms $\lambda = -9$ and $\mu = -3$ are correct.

$\mathbf{r}_2 = (-7, -37, -15)$ — matches $\mathbf{r}_1$. ✓
↳ from STEP 2: both lines give $(-7, -37, -15)$.
STEP 3/3write as coordinates

Strategy. Write the final answer as coordinates $(x, y, z)$ — a row in parentheses with commas. Do NOT write it as a column vector.

Final answer

$$\boxed{P = (-7,\, -37,\, -15)}.$$
Trap 7 (watch-list) — coordinates vs column vectors. Edexcel's "coordinates" command means row form $(x, y, z)$ with commas. A column vector $\begin{pmatrix} -7 \\ -37 \\ -15 \end{pmatrix}$ loses the mark. After computing $\mathbf{r}_1$, always transcribe to row form on the answer line.

Sense check. $P = (-7, -37, -15)$: three numbers in parentheses, separated by commas — the coordinate format the mark scheme demands.

$\boxed{P = (-7,\, -37,\, -15)}$ — coordinates, not a column vector.
