Worked Ex 5 — Electrical energy converted in a bulb filament

Question

A bulb filament carries a current of $250\ \text{mA}$ with $2.2\ \text{V}$ across it. Find the electrical energy converted in $1$ minute.

(a) Convert the given quantities to SI base units. [1 mark]
(b) Calculate the energy using the direct one-step method. [2 marks]
(c) Confirm via the roundabout charge route, and state why the direct route is preferred. [2 marks]

[5 marks]

Part (a) — convert to SI units [1 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 B1

Expand prefixes and convert time to seconds:

$$I = 250\ \text{mA} = 0.250\ \text{A}, \qquad t = 1\ \text{min} = 60\ \text{s}$$

The p.d. is already in volts: $V = 2.2\ \text{V}$.

Trap: $t$ must be in seconds for joules. Leaving $t = 1$ (minute) gives an answer $60\times$ too small.
$I = 0.250\ \text{A}$, $V = 2.2\ \text{V}$, $t = 60\ \text{s}$

Part (b) — direct method [2 marks] · 1 step

Carry-in from (a): $I = 0.250\ \text{A}$, $V = 2.2\ \text{V}$, $t = 60\ \text{s}$.
↳ from STEP 1: $I = 0.250\ \text{A}$, $V = 2.2\ \text{V}$, $t = 60\ \text{s}$
STEP 1/1 M1 A1

Formula (electrical energy, one step):

$$W = IVt$$

Substitute $I = 0.250$, $V = 2.2$, $t = 60$:

$$W = 0.250 \times 2.2 \times 60 = 33\ \text{J}$$

Phuc's documented pattern (a28d777a 10:29): Alex wrote $E = Pt$, $E = IVt$, $E = I^2Rt$ on the board specifically to push for single-step energy calculations. Reach for $W = IVt$ (or $W = Pt$) first. Always give the unit (J).
$W = 33\ \text{J}$

Part (c) — roundabout check & why avoid it [2 marks] · 1 step

Carry-in from (a): $I = 0.250\ \text{A}$, $V = 2.2\ \text{V}$, $t = 60\ \text{s}$; target $W = 33\ \text{J}$.
↳ from STEP 1: $Q = It$ then $E = QV$; target $33\ \text{J}$
STEP 1/1 M1 A1

Roundabout route (two steps):

$$Q = It = 0.250 \times 60 = 15\ \text{C}$$

$$E = QV = 15 \times 2.2 = 33\ \text{J}\ ✓$$

Revision-guide trap: the roundabout route gives the same $33\ \text{J}$ but uses two substitutions and two chances for a unit error. Only use $E = QV$ when the charge $Q$ is explicitly given. Examiners award method marks — a roundabout route with an arithmetic slip loses them.
$E = 33\ \text{J}$ ✓ (direct route preferred)
