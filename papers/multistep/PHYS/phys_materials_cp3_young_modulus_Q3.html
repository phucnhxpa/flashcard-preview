Vernier caliper: applying a positive zero error

Question

A vernier caliper has resolution 0.05 mm. With the jaws fully closed the vernier zero sits to the right of the main-scale zero with the 1st vernier division aligned — a zero error of $+0.05\,\mathrm{mm}$. A steel ball is then clamped: the vernier zero lies just past the 34 mm main-scale mark and the 11th vernier division aligns with a main-scale line. State the diameter of the ball.

[3 marks]

State the diameter of the ball [3 marks] · 3 steps

STEP 1/3read scales

Read main + vernier. Main scale up to the vernier zero is $34\,\mathrm{mm}$; the aligned 11th division gives $11 \times 0.05\,\mathrm{mm}$.

$$\text{vernier} = 11 \times 0.05 = 0.55\,\mathrm{mm}$$
Vernier part $= 0.55\,\mathrm{mm}$
↳ from STEP 2: main $= 34\,\mathrm{mm}$, vernier $= 0.55\,\mathrm{mm}$
STEP 2/3observed reading

Observed reading.

$$\text{observed} = 34 + 0.55 = 34.55\,\mathrm{mm}$$
Observed $= 34.55\,\mathrm{mm}$
↳ from STEP 3: observed $= 34.55\,\mathrm{mm}$, zero error $= +0.05\,\mathrm{mm}$
STEP 3/3apply zero error

Subtract the positive zero error. A positive zero error means the instrument reads too high, so it is subtracted.

$$\text{actual} = 34.55 - 0.05 = 34.50\,\mathrm{mm}$$
Common trap: adding a positive zero error, or reading each vernier division as $0.5\,\mathrm{mm}$. A positive zero error is subtracted.
Diameter $= 34.50\,\mathrm{mm}$
