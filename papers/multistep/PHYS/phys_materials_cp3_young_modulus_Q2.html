Micrometer: diameter, cross-sectional area and its percentage uncertainty

Question

A micrometer of resolution 0.01 mm is used on a wire. The sleeve shows the 4.5 mm mark (last full millimetre 4, with the half-millimetre mark visible) and the thimble reads 38 divisions. (a) State the diameter. (b) Calculate the cross-sectional area. (c) Estimate the percentage uncertainty in the area from the instrument resolution.

[5 marks]

Part (a) — read the diameter [1 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1combine scales

Combine sleeve and thimble. Sleeve $= 4.5\,\mathrm{mm}$; thimble $= 38 \times 0.01\,\mathrm{mm}$.

$$d = 4.5 + 38(0.01) = 4.88\,\mathrm{mm} = 4.88\times10^{-3}\,\mathrm{m}$$
$d = 4.88\,\mathrm{mm}$

Part (b) — cross-sectional area [2 marks] · 2 steps

Carry-in: $d = 4.88\times10^{-3}\,\mathrm{m}$, so $r = d/2 = 2.44\times10^{-3}\,\mathrm{m}$.
STEP 1/2radius first

Radius first. Halve the diameter before using the area formula.

$$r = \tfrac{d}{2} = 2.44\times10^{-3}\,\mathrm{m}$$
$r = 2.44\times10^{-3}\,\mathrm{m}$
↳ from STEP 2: $r = 2.44\times10^{-3}\,\mathrm{m}$
STEP 2/2area

Area.

$$A = \pi r^2 = \pi(2.44\times10^{-3})^2 = 1.87\times10^{-5}\,\mathrm{m^2}$$
$A = 1.87\times10^{-5}\,\mathrm{m^2}$

Part (c) — percentage uncertainty in the area [2 marks] · 2 steps

Carry-in: resolution $= 0.01\,\mathrm{mm}$ on $d = 4.88\,\mathrm{mm}$; area depends on $d^2$.
STEP 1/2% in d

Percentage uncertainty in the diameter. One resolution step on the measured diameter:

$$\frac{0.01}{4.88}\times100\% = 0.20\%$$
$\pm 0.20\%$ in $d$
↳ from STEP 2: $d$ uncertainty $= 0.20\%$; $A = \pi(d/2)^2 \propto d^2$
STEP 2/2% in A

Propagate to the area. A quantity raised to a power multiplies its percentage uncertainty by that power; $A \propto d^2$, so double it.

$$\%\,\text{in } A = 2 \times 0.20\% = 0.41\%$$
$\pm 0.41\%$ in $A$ (about $0.4\%$)
