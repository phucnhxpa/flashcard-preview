Q3 — Upthrust and the iceberg fraction

Question

(a) State Archimedes' Principle.

(b) A brass cylinder of volume $2.0\times10^{-4}\ \text{m}^3$ is fully submerged in water ($\rho_f = 1000\ \text{kg m}^{-3}$). Find the upthrust. ($g = 9.81\ \text{N kg}^{-1}$.)

(c) Ice has density $920\ \text{kg m}^{-3}$; sea water $1025\ \text{kg m}^{-3}$. Find the fraction of a floating iceberg below the surface.

[6 marks]

Part (a) — Archimedes' Principle [1 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 B1

The upthrust on a body partially or fully immersed in a fluid equals the weight of the fluid that the body displaces.

Exam wording: 'weight of fluid displaced' — not the mass, not the volume.

Part (b) — upthrust on the brass cylinder [2 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2 M1

Formula (upthrust):

$$U = \rho_f V g$$

Fully submerged, so the displaced volume is the whole volume.

STEP 2/2 A1

Substitute $\rho_f=1000$, $V=2.0\times10^{-4}$, $g=9.81$:

$$U = 1000\times(2.0\times10^{-4})\times9.81 = 1.96\ \text{N}$$

Common trap: using the brass density. Upthrust uses the fluid density only — brass never enters.
$U = 1.96\ \text{N}$

Part (c) — submerged fraction of the iceberg [3 marks] · 3 steps

Carry-in: floating $\Rightarrow W=U$; ice $\rho_s=920$, sea water $\rho_f=1025\ \text{kg m}^{-3}$.
STEP 1/3 M1

Flotation condition: $W = U$, i.e.

$$\rho_s V_{\text{tot}}\, g = \rho_f V_{\text{sub}}\, g$$

STEP 2/3 M1

Cancel $g$ and rearrange for the fraction submerged:

$$\frac{V_{\text{sub}}}{V_{\text{tot}}} = \frac{\rho_s}{\rho_f}$$

STEP 3/3 A1

Substitute:

$$\frac{V_{\text{sub}}}{V_{\text{tot}}} = \frac{920}{1025} = 0.898$$

About 90% is below the surface — the 'tip of the iceberg'.

$V_{\text{sub}}/V_{\text{tot}} = 0.90$ (≈90% submerged)
