Designing to a tensile strength: maximum force and minimum diameter

Question

(a) A brass pin has cross-sectional area $0.30\,\mathrm{cm^2}$ and brass has tensile strength $190\,\mathrm{MPa}$. Calculate the maximum tensile force before it breaks. (b) Mild steel has breaking strength $500\,\mathrm{MPa}$. A single mild-steel wire must support a 200 kg piano. Calculate the minimum diameter of the wire. (Take $g = 9.81\,\mathrm{N\,kg^{-1}}$.)

[6 marks]

Part (a) — maximum force on the brass pin [3 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2convert area

Idea. Tensile strength is the maximum stress; rearrange $\sigma = F/A$ to $F = \sigma A$. Convert the area to $\mathrm{m^2}$ first ($1\,\mathrm{cm^2} = 10^{-4}\,\mathrm{m^2}$).

$$A = 0.30\,\mathrm{cm^2} = 0.30\times10^{-4}\,\mathrm{m^2}$$
$A = 3.0\times10^{-5}\,\mathrm{m^2}$
↳ from STEP 2: $\sigma = 190\times10^{6}\,\mathrm{Pa},\; A = 3.0\times10^{-5}\,\mathrm{m^2}$
STEP 2/2force

Substitute.

$$F = \sigma A = (190\times10^{6})(0.30\times10^{-4}) = 5700\,\mathrm{N}$$
$F = 5.7\,\mathrm{kN}$

Part (b) — minimum wire diameter for the piano [3 marks] · 3 steps

Carry-in: independent of (a). Breaking strength $\sigma = 500\times10^{6}\,\mathrm{Pa}$; piano mass $200\,\mathrm{kg}$.
STEP 1/3weight

Weight to support.

$$F = mg = 200 \times 9.81 = 1962\,\mathrm{N}$$
$F = 1962\,\mathrm{N}$
↳ from STEP 2: $F = 1962\,\mathrm{N},\; \sigma = 500\times10^{6}\,\mathrm{Pa}$
STEP 2/3required area

Required area. The minimum area is the load divided by the maximum stress.

$$A = \frac{F}{\sigma} = \frac{1962}{500\times10^{6}} = 3.924\times10^{-6}\,\mathrm{m^2}$$
$A = 3.924\times10^{-6}\,\mathrm{m^2}$
↳ from STEP 3: $A = 3.924\times10^{-6}\,\mathrm{m^2}$
STEP 3/3diameter

Area to diameter. From $A = \pi r^2$, find $r$ then double it.

$$r = \sqrt{\frac{A}{\pi}} = 1.117\times10^{-3}\,\mathrm{m},\quad d = 2r$$
$d = 2.23\,\mathrm{mm}$ (minimum)
