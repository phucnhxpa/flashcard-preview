Q — Identify and explain the R–T behaviour of metal vs thermistor

Question

(a) State, with reason in terms of $I = nqvA$, whether the resistance of an NTC thermistor increases or decreases when its temperature rises. [3 marks]

(b) A copper wire and a thermistor are each heated. Explain why their resistances change in opposite directions. [3 marks]

(c) An examiner shows four $R$–temperature graphs. State which feature identifies the thermistor. [1 mark]

[7 marks]

Part (a) — thermistor R–T direction [3 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 B1 B1 B1

Statement: the resistance decreases.

Reason: rising temperature frees more electrons from the semiconductor lattice, so the number density $n$ increases sharply. In $I = nqvA$, the increase in $n$ outweighs the small decrease in drift velocity $v$, so the current $I$ increases for the same $V$ — hence $R = V/I$ decreases.

Mark-scheme keyword: the rise in $n$ dominates the fall in $v$. Both must be mentioned for full marks.
$R$ decreases: $T\uparrow \Rightarrow n\uparrow$ (dominates $v\downarrow$) $\Rightarrow I\uparrow \Rightarrow R\downarrow$

Part (b) — why metal and thermistor are opposite [3 marks] · 1 step

Carry-in from (a): for the thermistor (semiconductor), heating raises $n$ and lowers $R$.
↳ from STEP 1: thermistor (semiconductor): $T\uparrow \Rightarrow n\uparrow \Rightarrow R\downarrow$
STEP 1/1 B1 B1 B1

Metal (copper): $n$ is already huge ($\sim8.4\times10^{28}\ \text{m}^{-3}$) and barely changes on heating. Instead the lattice ions vibrate more, causing more electron–ion collisions, so the drift velocity $v$ decreases. From $I = nqvA$, $I$ falls for the same $V$, so $R$ increases (positive temperature coefficient, PTC).

Semiconductor (thermistor): $n$ starts tiny and rises sharply on heating; this rise dominates, so $R$ decreases (NTC).

Phuc trap (the single most heavily tested distinction): metal and semiconductor behave oppositely with temperature. Metal: $R$ rises ($v\downarrow$ dominates, $n$ fixed). Thermistor/LDR: $R$ falls ($n\uparrow$ dominates). Memory aid: “Metal heats up, resists more; thermistor heats up, conducts more.”
Metal: $n$ fixed, $v\downarrow \Rightarrow R\uparrow$ (PTC). Thermistor: $n\uparrow \Rightarrow R\downarrow$ (NTC). Opposite.

Part (c) — identifying graph feature [1 marks] · 1 step

Carry-in from (a) & (b): the thermistor's resistance falls as temperature rises (NTC), unlike the metal.
↳ from STEP 1: thermistor $R$ falls with $T$; the fall is steep then flattens
STEP 1/1 B1

The thermistor is identified by a decreasing $R$–$T$ curve that is steep at low temperature and flattens at higher temperature (not a straight line, and falling — opposite to the metal's rising line).

Reason for the shape: $n$ rises approximately exponentially with temperature, so the resistance drops fastest at first then levels off.
Decreasing, steep-then-flattening $R$–$T$ curve
