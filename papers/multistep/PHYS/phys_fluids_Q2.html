Q2 — Four spheres in one tank: pressure & buoyancy

Question

A tank of one uniform liquid holds four spheres: sphere 1 floats at the surface, spheres 2 and 3 hover (suspended) within the liquid, sphere 4 rests on the bottom.

(a) State how the pressure on a sphere depends on its depth, with the formula.

(b) Classify each sphere's density relative to the liquid, with justification.

[6 marks]

Part (a) — pressure with depth [2 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2 B1

Formula (gauge pressure in a fluid):

$$P = \rho g h$$

with $\rho$ the liquid density, $g$ the field strength, $h$ the depth.

STEP 2/2 B1

Pressure increases with depth: the deeper sphere 4 sits at higher pressure than sphere 1. (This depth-dependence of pressure is the very origin of upthrust — the bottom face feels more pressure than the top.)

Deeper $\Rightarrow$ higher $P$, via $P=\rho g h$

Part (b) — buoyancy classification [4 marks] · 3 steps

Carry-in: max upthrust when fully submerged is $U_{\max}=\rho_f V g$; compare $W=\rho_s V g$ with it. Equivalently compare $\rho_s$ with $\rho_f$.
STEP 1/3 M1

Sphere 1 (floats): least dense, $\rho_s < \rho_f$. It settles with part above the surface so the submerged volume displaces a fluid weight equal to $W$.

STEP 2/3 M1

Spheres 2 & 3 (suspended): neutral buoyancy, $\rho_s = \rho_f$. Fully submerged, $W = U$, resultant zero, so they hover at any depth.

STEP 3/3 A1

Sphere 4 (on the bottom): most dense, $\rho_s > \rho_f$, so $W > U_{\max}$. It sinks; the floor supplies the extra upward normal force.

Mark-scheme rule: each state must be justified by comparing object density with fluid density — not merely asserted.
$\rho_1<\rho_f$; $\rho_2=\rho_3=\rho_f$; $\rho_4>\rho_f$
