Pre-strained steel rail: tensile force, stored energy and unstressed temperature

Question

When rails are laid in cold weather they are stretched and fixed in place while still stretched (pre-straining). Typical data for a steel rail: Young modulus $= 2.0\times10^{11}\,\mathrm{Pa}$; cross-sectional area $= 7.5\times10^{-3}\,\mathrm{m^2}$; pre-strain $= 3.0\times10^{-4}$; the strain changes by $2.5\times10^{-5}$ for each kelvin of temperature rise. A rail is laid at 8 °C with a pre-strain of $3.0\times10^{-4}$. (a) Calculate the tensile force needed to produce the pre-strain. (b) Calculate the elastic strain energy stored in a rail of unstressed length 45 m. (c) Calculate the temperature at which the rail becomes unstressed.

[7 marks]

Part (a) — tensile force for the pre-strain [3 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2formula

Strategy. Combine $E = \sigma/\varepsilon$ with $\sigma = F/A$ to get force directly from the modulus, area and strain.

$$E = \frac{\sigma}{\varepsilon} = \frac{F/A}{\varepsilon} \Rightarrow F = E A \varepsilon$$
$F = EA\varepsilon$
↳ from STEP 2: $E = 2.0\times10^{11},\; A = 7.5\times10^{-3},\; \varepsilon = 3.0\times10^{-4}$
STEP 2/2answer

Substitute.

$$F = (2.0\times10^{11})(7.5\times10^{-3})(3.0\times10^{-4}) = 45000\,\mathrm{N}$$
$F = 45\,\mathrm{kN}$

Part (b) — elastic strain energy stored [2 marks] · 2 steps

Carry-in: from (a) $F = 45000\,\mathrm{N}$; rail length $L = 45\,\mathrm{m}$; pre-strain $\varepsilon = 3.0\times10^{-4}$.
STEP 1/2extension

Extension first. Strain is extension over original length, so

$$\Delta x = \varepsilon L = (3.0\times10^{-4})(45) = 0.0135\,\mathrm{m}$$
$\Delta x = 0.0135\,\mathrm{m}$
↳ from STEP 2: $F = 45000\,\mathrm{N},\; \Delta x = 0.0135\,\mathrm{m}$
STEP 2/2energy

Formula. In the linear region the stored energy is the triangular area, $E_e = \tfrac12 F\,\Delta x$ (keep the factor $\tfrac12$).

$$E_e = \tfrac12 (45000)(0.0135) = 303.75\,\mathrm{J}$$
$E_e = 304\,\mathrm{J}$ (3 s.f.)

Part (c) — temperature at which the rail is unstressed [2 marks] · 2 steps

Carry-in: pre-strain $\varepsilon = 3.0\times10^{-4}$; strain per kelvin $= 2.5\times10^{-5}\,\mathrm{K^{-1}}$; laid at $8\,\mathrm{°C}$.
STEP 1/2temperature rise

Idea. The rail becomes unstressed when thermal expansion exactly cancels the pre-strain. Divide the pre-strain by the strain produced per kelvin.

$$\Delta T = \frac{\varepsilon}{2.5\times10^{-5}} = \frac{3.0\times10^{-4}}{2.5\times10^{-5}} = 12\,\mathrm{K}$$
$\Delta T = 12\,\mathrm{K}$
↳ from STEP 2: $\Delta T = 12\,\mathrm{K}$, laid at $8\,\mathrm{°C}$
STEP 2/2final temperature

Add to the laying temperature.

$$T = 8\,\mathrm{°C} + 12\,\mathrm{°C}$$
$T = 20\,\mathrm{°C}$
