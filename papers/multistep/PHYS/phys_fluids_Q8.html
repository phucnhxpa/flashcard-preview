Q8 â€” Ball bearing in oil: weight, upthrust, acceleration, v_t, drag

Question

A steel ball bearing of radius $r = 1.5\ \text{mm}$ and density $\rho_s = 7800\ \text{kg m}^{-3}$ is dropped from rest into a deep tank of oil ($\rho_f = 950\ \text{kg m}^{-3}$, $\eta = 0.99\ \text{Pa s}$). ($g = 9.81\ \text{N kg}^{-1}$.)

(a) Find the weight of the ball.

(b) Find the upthrust on the ball.

(c) Find the initial acceleration just after release.

(d) Find the terminal velocity.

(e) Find the drag at terminal velocity two ways and check they agree.

[10 marks]

Part (a) â€” weight [2 marks] Â· 2 steps

STEP 1/2 M1

Sphere volume, then weight:

$$V = \tfrac{4}{3}\pi r^3 = \tfrac{4}{3}\pi(1.5\times10^{-3})^3 = 1.41\times10^{-8}\ \text{m}^3$$

STEP 2/2 A1

$$W = \rho_s V g = 7800\times(1.41\times10^{-8})\times9.81 = 1.08\times10^{-3}\ \text{N}$$

$W = 1.08\times10^{-3}\ \text{N}$

Part (b) â€” upthrust [1 marks] Â· 1 step

Carry-in from (a): same volume $V = 1.41\times10^{-8}\ \text{m}^3$; now use fluid density $\rho_f=950$.
STEP 1/1 A1

$$U = \rho_f V g = 950\times(1.41\times10^{-8})\times9.81 = 1.31\times10^{-4}\ \text{N}$$

Same $V$ as (a); the fluid density now, not the steel.
$U = 1.31\times10^{-4}\ \text{N}$

Part (c) â€” initial acceleration [3 marks] Â· 3 steps

Carry-in from (a),(b): $W = 1.08\times10^{-3}\ \text{N}$, $U = 1.31\times10^{-4}\ \text{N}$, $m = \rho_s V = 7800\times1.41\times10^{-8}$ kg.
STEP 1/3 M1

At release $v = 0$, so drag $= 6\pi\eta r(0) = 0$. The resultant (down) is

$$F_{\text{net}} = W - U = 1.08\times10^{-3} - 1.31\times10^{-4} = 9.5\times10^{-4}\ \text{N}$$

STEP 2/3 M1

Newton's second law, $a = F_{\text{net}}/m$ with $m = \rho_s V = 7800\times1.41\times10^{-8} = 1.10\times10^{-4}\ \text{kg}$:

$$a = \frac{9.5\times10^{-4}}{1.10\times10^{-4}}$$

STEP 3/3 A1

$$a = 8.6\ \text{m s}^{-2}\ \text{downward}$$

The release condition 'drag = 0 because $v=0$' is the marked idea here.
$a = 8.6\ \text{m s}^{-2}$ down

Part (d) â€” terminal velocity [2 marks] Â· 2 steps

Carry-in: $v_t = \dfrac{2r^2 g(\rho_s-\rho_f)}{9\eta}$; $r=1.5\times10^{-3}$, $\rho_s-\rho_f = 6850$, $\eta=0.99$.
STEP 1/2 M1

Substitute into the derived formula:

$$v_t = \frac{2(1.5\times10^{-3})^2(9.81)(7800-950)}{9(0.99)}$$

Numerator $= 2\times2.25\times10^{-6}\times9.81\times6850 = 0.3024$; denominator $= 8.91$.

STEP 2/2 A1

$$v_t = \frac{0.3024}{8.91} = 3.4\times10^{-2}\ \text{m s}^{-1}$$

$v_t = 3.4\times10^{-2}\ \text{m s}^{-1}$

Part (e) â€” drag at v_t, two ways [2 marks] Â· 2 steps

Carry-in: $W = 1.08\times10^{-3}$, $U = 1.31\times10^{-4}\ \text{N}$, $v_t = 3.394\times10^{-2}\ \text{m s}^{-1}$.
STEP 1/2 M1

Method 1 â€” force balance at $v_t$ ($W = U + F_D$):

$$F_D = W - U = 9.50\times10^{-4}\ \text{N}$$

STEP 2/2 A1

Method 2 â€” Stokes' Law at $v_t$:

$$F_D = 6\pi\eta r v_t = 6\pi(0.99)(1.5\times10^{-3})(3.394\times10^{-2}) = 9.50\times10^{-4}\ \text{N}$$

The two agree, confirming $v_t$.

$F_D = 9.5\times10^{-4}\ \text{N}$ (both methods)
