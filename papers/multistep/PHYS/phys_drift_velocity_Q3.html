Edexcel IAL Physics · Unit 2 (WPH12) · Drift Velocity (I = nqvA) · Worked Example 3 (textbook, 160 mA)

Q — Compare drift velocity in a thin filament and thick leads

Question

A circuit carries $I = 160\ \text{mA}$. The tungsten filament has diameter $d_1 = 0.025\ \text{mm}$ ($n_W = 4.0\times10^{28}\ \text{m}^{-3}$); the copper leads have diameter $d_2 = 0.72\ \text{mm}$ ($n_{Cu} = 8.0\times10^{28}\ \text{m}^{-3}$). Take $q = 1.6\times10^{-19}\ \text{C}$.

(a) Find the filament cross-sectional area $A_1$. [1 mark]
(b) Find the drift velocity in the filament. [2 marks]
(c) Find the drift velocity in the leads, and explain the difference. [3 marks]

[6 marks]

Part (a) — filament area [1 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 B1

Formula (circular cross-section):

$$A = \pi r^2 = \pi\left(\dfrac{d}{2}\right)^2$$

Substitute $d_1 = 0.025\ \text{mm} = 0.025\times10^{-3}\ \text{m}$:

$$A_1 = \pi(0.5\times0.025\times10^{-3})^2 = \pi(1.25\times10^{-5})^2 \approx 4.91\times10^{-10}\ \text{m}^2$$

Trap: halve the diameter and square it. Plugging the diameter straight in as $A$ is the commonest slip here.
$A_1 \approx 4.91\times10^{-10}\ \text{m}^2$

Part (b) — filament drift velocity [2 marks] · 1 step

Carry-in from (a): $A_1 = 4.91\times10^{-10}\ \text{m}^2$, $n_W = 4.0\times10^{28}$, $I = 0.160\,\text{A}$, $q = 1.6\times10^{-19}$.
↳ from STEP 1: $A_1 = 4.91\times10^{-10}\,\text{m}^2$
STEP 1/1 M1 A1

Formula: $v = \dfrac{I}{nqA}$.

Substitute (filament, tungsten):

$$v_1 = \dfrac{0.160}{(4.0\times10^{28})(1.6\times10^{-19})(4.91\times10^{-10})} \approx 0.051\ \text{m s}^{-1} = 51\ \text{mm s}^{-1}$$

Note: the thin, low-$n$ filament drifts surprisingly fast — tens of mm s$^{-1}$.
$v_1 \approx 51\ \text{mm s}^{-1}$

Part (c) — leads, and the comparison [3 marks] · 1 step

Carry-in from (a) & (b): same $I = 0.160\,\text{A}$ everywhere (series). Leads: $d_2 = 0.72\,\text{mm}$, $n_{Cu} = 8.0\times10^{28}$. Filament result $v_1 \approx 51\,\text{mm s}^{-1}$.
↳ from STEP 1: $A_2 = \pi(0.36\times10^{-3})^2 \approx 4.07\times10^{-7}\,\text{m}^2$; same $I$
STEP 1/1 M1 A1 A1

Leads area: $A_2 = \pi(0.5\times0.72\times10^{-3})^2 \approx 4.07\times10^{-7}\ \text{m}^2$.

Drift velocity:

$$v_2 = \dfrac{0.160}{(8.0\times10^{28})(1.6\times10^{-19})(4.07\times10^{-7})} \approx 3.1\times10^{-5}\ \text{m s}^{-1} = 0.031\ \text{mm s}^{-1}$$

Comparison: the same current flows in series, so $v \propto 1/A$. The leads are much wider ($A_2 \gg A_1$), so their drift is $\sim 1600\times$ slower than the filament.

Mark-scheme keyword: “current is constant throughout a series circuit; smaller area $A$ requires larger drift velocity $v$ to maintain the same $I = nqvA$.”
$v_2 \approx 0.031\ \text{mm s}^{-1}$ — leads $\sim1600\times$ slower
