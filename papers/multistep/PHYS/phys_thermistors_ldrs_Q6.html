Self-Test — Output voltage and the effect of heating

Question

A thermistor ($R_1$) and fixed resistor $R_2 = 1.0\ \text{k}\Omega$ are connected in series with a $9.0\ \text{V}$ supply, with $V_{out}$ measured across $R_2$. At room temperature the thermistor resistance is $3.0\ \text{k}\Omega$.

(a) Calculate $V_{out}$ at room temperature. [2 marks]
(b) The thermistor temperature now rises. State and explain the effect on $V_{out}$. [3 marks]

[5 marks]

Part (a) — output at room temperature [2 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 M1 A1

Formula:

$$V_{out} = V_{in}\cdot\dfrac{R_2}{R_1 + R_2}$$

Substitute $V_{in} = 9.0\ \text{V}$, $R_1 = 3.0\ \text{k}\Omega$, $R_2 = 1.0\ \text{k}\Omega$ (kΩ cancels top and bottom):

$$V_{out} = 9.0\times\dfrac{1.0}{3.0 + 1.0} = 9.0\times0.25 = 2.25\ \text{V}$$

Note: because both resistances are in kΩ, the units cancel in the ratio — no need to convert to Ω here.
$V_{out} = 2.25\ \text{V}$

Part (b) — effect of rising temperature [3 marks] · 1 step

Carry-in from (a): at room temperature $V_{out} = 2.25\ \text{V}$, with thermistor $R_1 = 3.0\ \text{k}\Omega$ and fixed $R_2 = 1.0\ \text{k}\Omega$.
↳ from STEP 1: $V_{out} = 2.25\ \text{V}$; $R_1$ (thermistor) currently $3.0\ \text{k}\Omega$
STEP 1/1 B1 B1 B1

Statement: $V_{out}$ increases.

Explanation:

1. Rising temperature frees more electrons ($n\uparrow$), so the thermistor resistance $R_1$ decreases (NTC).

2. Total resistance $R_1 + R_2$ decreases, so the circuit current $I = V_{in}/(R_1 + R_2)$ increases.

3. $V_{out} = IR_2$ with $R_2$ fixed, so $V_{out}$ increases. (Equivalently: a smaller $R_1$ leaves a larger fraction of $V_{in}$ across $R_2$.)

Trap: do not say "the thermistor changes the current" as your first statement. The correct order is: temperature → $n$ changes → resistance changes → then current and $V_{out}$ change.
$V_{out}$ increases ($R_1\downarrow \Rightarrow$ larger share of $V_{in}$ on $R_2$)
