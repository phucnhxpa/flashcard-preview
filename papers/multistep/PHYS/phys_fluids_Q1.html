Q1 — Will it float? A density decision

Question

A sealed plastic sphere has mass 36 g and radius 2.0 cm.

(a) Find the average density of the sphere.

(b) State, with a reason, whether it floats in water ($\rho_f = 1000\ \text{kg m}^{-3}$).

(c) The mass is instead 30 g (same radius). Re-decide.

[5 marks]

Part (a) — average density [3 marks] · 3 steps

STEP 1/3 M1

Convert to SI. $m = 36\ \text{g} = 0.036\ \text{kg}$; $r = 2.0\ \text{cm} = 0.020\ \text{m}$.

Common trap: leaving mass in grams or radius in cm. Volume uses $r$, not the diameter.
STEP 2/3 M1

Formula (sphere volume):

$$V = \tfrac{4}{3}\pi r^3$$

Substitute $r = 0.020$:

$$V = \tfrac{4}{3}\pi (0.020)^3 = 3.35\times10^{-5}\ \text{m}^3$$

STEP 3/3 A1

Formula (density):

$$\rho = \frac{m}{V}$$

Substitute:

$$\rho = \frac{0.036}{3.35\times10^{-5}} = 1.07\times10^{3}\ \text{kg m}^{-3}$$

$\rho_s = 1.07\times10^{3}\ \text{kg m}^{-3}$

Part (b) — float or sink? [1 marks] · 1 step

Carry-in from (a): $\rho_s = 1.07\times10^{3}\ \text{kg m}^{-3}$; fluid $\rho_f = 1000\ \text{kg m}^{-3}$.
STEP 1/1 A1

Decision rule: compare object density with fluid density. Sinks if $\rho_s > \rho_f$; floats if $\rho_s < \rho_f$.

$\rho_s = 1070 > 1000 = \rho_f$, so the sphere sinks.

$\rho_s > \rho_f \Rightarrow$ sinks

Part (c) — mass 30 g instead [1 marks] · 1 step

Carry-in: same $V = 3.35\times10^{-5}\ \text{m}^3$; now $m = 0.030\ \text{kg}$.
STEP 1/1 A1

$$\rho_s = \frac{0.030}{3.35\times10^{-5}} = 896\ \text{kg m}^{-3}$$

Now $\rho_s = 896 < 1000 = \rho_f$, so it floats. The decision is always $\rho_s$ versus $\rho_f$.

$896 < 1000 \Rightarrow$ floats
