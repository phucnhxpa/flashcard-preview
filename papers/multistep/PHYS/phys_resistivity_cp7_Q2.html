Nichrome Q — Find resistivity from measured R, l and diameter

Question

A nichrome wire of diameter $d = 0.2743\ \text{mm}$ has resistance $R = 18.3\ \Omega$ over a length $l = 1.000\ \text{m}$.

(a) Find the radius and cross-sectional area $A$ of the wire. [3 marks]
(b) Hence find the resistivity $\rho$ of nichrome. [2 marks]

[5 marks]

Part (a) — cross-sectional area [3 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 M1 M1 A1

Convert diameter to metres (mm $\to$ m is $\times 10^{-3}$):

$$d = 0.2743 \times 10^{-3}\ \text{m}$$

Radius $r = d/2$:

$$r = \dfrac{0.2743 \times 10^{-3}}{2} = 0.13715 \times 10^{-3}\ \text{m}$$

Formula (area of a circle):

$$A = \pi r^2$$

$$A = \pi (0.13715 \times 10^{-3})^2 = 5.913 \times 10^{-8}\ \text{m}^2$$

Radius trap: $A = \pi r^2$ uses the radius. Putting the diameter into $\pi r^2$ gives an area four times too large. Equivalently use $A = \pi d^2/4$.
$A = 5.913 \times 10^{-8}\ \text{m}^2$

Part (b) — resistivity [2 marks] · 1 step

Carry-in from (a): $A = 5.913 \times 10^{-8}\ \text{m}^2$; measured $R = 18.3\ \Omega$, $l = 1.000\ \text{m}$.
↳ from STEP 1: $A = 5.913 \times 10^{-8}\ \text{m}^2$, $R = 18.3\ \Omega$, $l = 1.000\ \text{m}$
STEP 1/1 M1 A1

Formula (rearrange $R = \rho l / A$ for $\rho$):

$$\rho = \dfrac{RA}{l}$$

Substitute $R = 18.3$, $A = 5.913 \times 10^{-8}$, $l = 1.000$:

$$\rho = \dfrac{18.3 \times 5.913 \times 10^{-8}}{1.000} = 1.08 \times 10^{-6}\ \Omega\,\text{m}$$

Unit trap: resistivity is in $\Omega\,\text{m}$ (ohm-metres), since $\dfrac{\Omega \cdot \text{m}^2}{\text{m}} = \Omega\,\text{m}$. Never write $\Omega/\text{m}$.
Sense-check: $1.08 \times 10^{-6}\ \Omega\,\text{m}$ is the known resistivity of nichrome — a high-$\rho$ alloy used for heating elements.
$\rho = 1.08 \times 10^{-6}\ \Omega\,\text{m}$
