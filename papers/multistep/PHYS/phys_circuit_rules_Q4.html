Q — Two combination networks: solve the parallel section first

Question

Find the total resistance of each combination network. In each case a single resistor is in series with a parallel pair.

(a) $80\ \Omega$ in series with $20\ \Omega \parallel 10\ \Omega$. [3 marks]
(b) $7.0\ \Omega$ in series with $4.0\ \Omega \parallel 12\ \Omega$. [2 marks]

[5 marks]

Part (a) — 80 Ω in series with 20 Ω ∥ 10 Ω [3 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2 M1 A1

Formula (Step 1 — solve the parallel section first):

$$\dfrac{1}{R_p} = \dfrac{1}{R_1} + \dfrac{1}{R_2}$$

Substitute $R_1 = 20\ \Omega$, $R_2 = 10\ \Omega$:

$$\dfrac{1}{R_p} = \dfrac{1}{20} + \dfrac{1}{10} = \dfrac{1}{20} + \dfrac{2}{20} = \dfrac{3}{20}$$

$$R_p = \dfrac{20}{3} = 6.7\ \Omega\ (1\ \text{d.p.})$$

Phuc's mistake (lesson 55:22–56:44): he applied a single parallel formula to the entire combination, skipping this step. Always isolate the parallel sub-circuit first; $R_p$ is smaller than the smallest branch ($< 10\ \Omega$).
$R_p = 6.7\ \Omega$
↳ from STEP 2: parallel section $R_p = 6.7\ \Omega$; series resistor $= 80\ \Omega$
STEP 2/2 A1

Formula (Step 2 — add the series resistor):

$$R_T = R_{\text{series}} + R_p$$

Substitute $R_{\text{series}} = 80\ \Omega$, $R_p = 6.7\ \Omega$:

$$R_T = 80 + 6.7 = 86.7\ \Omega \approx 87\ \Omega$$

Order matters: only add series values after collapsing the parallel block to a single resistance. Adding all three directly (series style) is the classic combination-network error.
$R_T \approx 86.7\ \Omega$

Part (b) — 7.0 Ω in series with 4.0 Ω ∥ 12 Ω [2 marks] · 1 step

Carry-in (method from (a)): use the same two-step approach — collapse the parallel pair first, then add the $7.0\ \Omega$ series resistor.
↳ from STEP 1: same two-step method: parallel $R_p$ first, then add $7.0\ \Omega$
STEP 1/1 M1 A1

Step 1 (parallel section):

$$\dfrac{1}{R_{23}} = \dfrac{1}{4.0} + \dfrac{1}{12} = \dfrac{3}{12} + \dfrac{1}{12} = \dfrac{4}{12} = \dfrac{1}{3}$$

$$R_{23} = 3.0\ \Omega$$

Step 2 (add the series resistor):

$$R_T = 7.0 + 3.0 = 10.0\ \Omega$$

Sense-check: $R_{23} = 3.0\ \Omega < 4.0\ \Omega$ (smallest branch) ✓ — parallel resistance is always less than the smallest branch. Then series adds.
$R_T = 10.0\ \Omega$
