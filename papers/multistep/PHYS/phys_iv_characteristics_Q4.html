Edexcel IAL Physics · Unit 2 (WPH12) · I–V Characteristics · Topic 2.4 · Worked Example 4 (ohmic nichrome wire)

Ohmic Q — Find R from the slope of a straight I–V line

Question

A nichrome wire gives an I–V characteristic that is a straight line through the origin with gradient $54.8\ \text{mA/V}$.

(a) State, with reason, the relationship between the gradient and the resistance for this component. [2 marks]
(b) Calculate the resistance of the wire. [2 marks]

[4 marks]

Part (a) — gradient ↔ resistance [2 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 B1 B1

The wire is ohmic (straight line through the origin, constant $R$). With $I$ on the y-axis and $V$ on the x-axis:

$$\text{gradient} = \dfrac{I}{V} = \dfrac{1}{R}$$

Therefore the resistance is the reciprocal of the gradient:

$$R = \dfrac{1}{\text{gradient}}$$

Axis trap: this only works because the plot is $I$ (y) vs $V$ (x). On a $V$-vs-$I$ plot the gradient would equal $R$ directly.
Ohmic ⇒ gradient $= 1/R$, so $R = 1/\text{gradient}$

Part (b) — compute R [2 marks] · 1 step

Carry-in from (a): $R = 1/\text{gradient}$, with gradient $= 54.8\ \text{mA/V} = 0.0548\ \text{A/V}$ (convert mA → A first).
↳ from STEP 1: gradient $= 54.8\ \text{mA/V} = 0.0548\ \text{A/V}$; $R = 1/\text{gradient}$
STEP 1/1 M1 A1

Convert the gradient to base SI units:

$$54.8\ \text{mA/V} = 54.8\times10^{-3}\ \text{A/V} = 0.0548\ \text{A/V}$$

Take the reciprocal:

$$R = \dfrac{1}{0.0548} = 18.2\ \Omega$$

Reciprocal trap: the gradient is $1/R$, so you must invert it. Quoting $0.0548$ as the resistance is a common error.
Unit check: $1/(\text{A/V}) = \text{V/A} = \Omega$ ✓
$R = 18.2\ \Omega$
