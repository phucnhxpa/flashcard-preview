Effective spring constant: two identical springs

Question

A single spring has spring constant $k = 59\,\mathrm{N\,m^{-1}}$. Two such identical springs are combined. (a) Draw the arrangement and find the effective spring constant when the two springs are joined in series. (b) Draw the arrangement and find the effective spring constant when the two springs are joined in parallel.

[6 marks]

Part (a) — two springs in series [3 marks] · 3 steps

STEP 1/3diagram

Arrangement. Two springs end-to-end (one below the other), the load hanging from the bottom spring. The same force passes through both, so extensions add and the combination is softer than one spring.

Series springs behave like resistors in parallel.
↳ from STEP 2: $k_1 = k_2 = 59\,\mathrm{N\,m^{-1}}$
STEP 2/3formula

Formula. For springs in series the reciprocals add:

$$\frac{1}{k_{\text{eff}}} = \frac{1}{k_1} + \frac{1}{k_2}$$
Reciprocal addition lowers $k_{\text{eff}}$.
↳ from STEP 3: $\dfrac{1}{k_{\text{eff}}} = \dfrac{1}{59} + \dfrac{1}{59} = \dfrac{2}{59}$
STEP 3/3answer

Substitute.

$$\frac{1}{k_{\text{eff}}} = \frac{2}{59} \Rightarrow k_{\text{eff}} = \frac{59}{2}$$
$k_{\text{eff}} = 29.5\,\mathrm{N\,m^{-1}}$

Part (b) — two springs in parallel [3 marks] · 2 steps

Carry-in: single-spring constant $k_1 = k_2 = 59\,\mathrm{N\,m^{-1}}$ (independent of part (a)).
STEP 1/2diagram

Arrangement. Two springs side-by-side, both fixed to the support at the top and to a common bar at the bottom; the load hangs from the bar. The load is shared, so each spring has the same extension and the combination is stiffer.

Parallel springs behave like resistors in series.
STEP 2/2formula + answer

Formula. For springs in parallel the constants add directly:

$$k_{\text{eff}} = k_1 + k_2 = 59 + 59$$
$k_{\text{eff}} = 118\,\mathrm{N\,m^{-1}}$
