Q — Rearrange I = nqvA and find the drift velocity

Question

A copper wire of cross-sectional area $A = 2\ \text{mm}^2$ carries a current $I = 0.5\ \text{A}$. Copper has $n = 6.4\times10^{28}\ \text{m}^{-3}$ and $q = 1.6\times10^{-19}\ \text{C}$.

(a) Rearrange $I = nqvA$ for $v$ and convert $A$. [1 mark]
(b) Calculate the drift velocity $v$. [2 marks]

[3 marks]

Part (a) — rearrange & convert [1 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 B1

Rearrange the transport equation for the drift velocity:

$$I = nqvA \quad\Rightarrow\quad v = \dfrac{I}{nqA}$$

Convert the area: $2\ \text{mm}^2 = 2\times10^{-6}\ \text{m}^2$.

Trap: do the rearrangement symbolically first, in one step — no need to find $\Delta Q$ or $\Delta N$ separately.
$v = \dfrac{I}{nqA},\; A = 2\times10^{-6}\ \text{m}^2$

Part (b) — evaluate v [2 marks] · 1 step

Carry-in from (a): $v = \dfrac{I}{nqA}$ with $I = 0.5\,\text{A}$, $n = 6.4\times10^{28}$, $q = 1.6\times10^{-19}$, $A = 2\times10^{-6}\,\text{m}^2$.
↳ from STEP 1: $v = I/(nqA)$, $A = 2\times10^{-6}\,\text{m}^2$
STEP 1/1 M1 A1

Substitute:

$$v = \dfrac{0.5}{(6.4\times10^{28})(1.6\times10^{-19})(2\times10^{-6})}$$

Denominator: $6.4\times1.6\times2 = 20.48$, powers $28-19-6 = 3$, so denominator $= 20.48\times10^{3} = 2.05\times10^{4}$.

$$v = \dfrac{0.5}{2.05\times10^{4}} \approx 2.4\times10^{-5}\ \text{m s}^{-1} \approx 0.024\ \text{mm s}^{-1}$$

Sense-check: drift is a fraction of a millimetre per second — slower than a snail, as expected for a metal.
$v \approx 2.4\times10^{-5}\ \text{m s}^{-1}$
