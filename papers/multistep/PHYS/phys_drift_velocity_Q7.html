Edexcel IAL Physics · Unit 2 (WPH12) · Drift Velocity (I = nqvA) · Self-test Q8 / Example 5 (3 m wire, 0.25 A)

Q — Electron travel time vs why the lamp lights instantly

Question

A lamp is $3.0\ \text{m}$ from the switch, connected by copper wire of cross-section $A = 0.50\ \text{mm}^2$. The carrier density is $n = 8.5\times10^{28}\ \text{m}^{-3}$, $q = 1.6\times10^{-19}\ \text{C}$, and the lamp draws $I = 0.25\ \text{A}$.

(a) Find the drift velocity of the electrons. [2 marks]
(b) Show that an electron takes almost a day to reach the lamp. [2 marks]
(c) Explain why the lamp nevertheless appears to light instantly. [2 marks]

[6 marks]

Part (a) — drift velocity [2 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 M1 A1

Convert $A = 0.50\ \text{mm}^2 = 5.0\times10^{-7}\ \text{m}^2$.

Formula: $v = \dfrac{I}{nqA}$.

$$v = \dfrac{0.25}{(8.5\times10^{28})(1.6\times10^{-19})(5.0\times10^{-7})} \approx 3.7\times10^{-5}\ \text{m s}^{-1}$$

$v \approx 3.7\times10^{-5}\ \text{m s}^{-1}$

Part (b) — electron travel time [2 marks] · 1 step

Carry-in from (a): drift velocity $v = 3.7\times10^{-5}\ \text{m s}^{-1}$; distance $L = 3.0\ \text{m}$.
↳ from STEP 1: $v = 3.7\times10^{-5}\,\text{m s}^{-1}$, $L = 3.0\,\text{m}$
STEP 1/1 M1 A1

Formula: $t = \dfrac{L}{v}$.

$$t = \dfrac{3.0}{3.7\times10^{-5}} \approx 8.1\times10^{4}\ \text{s} \approx 22\ \text{hours}$$

Result: almost a full day — the electron itself crawls.
$t \approx 8.1\times10^{4}\ \text{s} \approx 22\ \text{h}$

Part (c) — why it lights instantly [2 marks] · 1 step

Carry-in from (a) & (b): individual electrons drift at $\sim10^{-5}\ \text{m s}^{-1}$ (taking $\sim22$ h to cross 3 m), yet $n \sim 10^{28}\ \text{m}^{-3}$ carriers are present everywhere.
↳ from STEP 1: electrons are slow ($\sim10^{-5}\,\text{m s}^{-1}$); $n\sim10^{28}\,\text{m}^{-3}$
STEP 1/1 B1 B1

Explanation (mark-scheme wording):

When the switch closes, the electric field is set up along the whole wire at $\approx 3\times10^{8}\ \text{m s}^{-1}$ (near the speed of light), so every electron in the wire — including those already at the lamp — begins to drift almost simultaneously. With $\sim10^{28}$ carriers per m$^3$, the charge arriving per second is large, so the lamp glows within nanoseconds.

Phuc's lesson question (“Is it the same Charlie?”): no single tagged electron travels end-to-end. The marble-tube picture applies — push one marble in, another pops out the far end at once. It is the field that propagates, not one electron.
The field (≈ c) starts all carriers at once — not one fast electron
