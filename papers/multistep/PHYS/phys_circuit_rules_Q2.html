Q — Combination network: $R_1$ in series with $R_2\parallel R_3$

Question

A $12.0\ \text{V}$ battery is connected to $R_1 = 8.0\ \Omega$ in series with a parallel pair $R_2 = 3.0\ \Omega$ and $R_3 = 6.0\ \Omega$.

(a) Find the total resistance $R_T$ and the total current $I_T$. [3 marks]
(b) Find the p.d. across $R_1$ and across the parallel section. [2 marks]
(c) Find the branch currents $I_2$ and $I_3$, and verify KCL. [2 marks]

[7 marks]

Part (a) — total resistance and current [3 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2 M1 A1

Formula (parallel sub-circuit first — never one formula for the whole):

$$\dfrac{1}{R_{23}} = \dfrac{1}{R_2} + \dfrac{1}{R_3}$$

Substitute $R_2 = 3.0\ \Omega$, $R_3 = 6.0\ \Omega$:

$$\dfrac{1}{R_{23}} = \dfrac{1}{3.0} + \dfrac{1}{6.0} = \dfrac{2}{6} + \dfrac{1}{6} = \dfrac{3}{6} = \dfrac{1}{2.0}$$

$$R_{23} = 2.0\ \Omega$$

Common trap (combination network): never apply a single formula to the whole circuit. Solve the parallel sub-circuit first, then add the series resistor. Also take the reciprocal at the end — don't stop at $1/R_{23} = 1/2$.
$R_{23} = 2.0\ \Omega$
↳ from STEP 2: parallel combination $R_{23} = 2.0\ \Omega$; $R_1 = 8.0\ \Omega$, $\varepsilon = 12.0\ \text{V}$
STEP 2/2 A1

Formula (series adds; then Ohm's law for the whole loop):

$$R_T = R_1 + R_{23}, \qquad I_T = \dfrac{\varepsilon}{R_T}$$

Substitute $R_1 = 8.0\ \Omega$, $R_{23} = 2.0\ \Omega$, $\varepsilon = 12.0\ \text{V}$:

$$R_T = 8.0 + 2.0 = 10.0\ \Omega$$

$$I_T = \dfrac{12.0}{10.0} = 1.2\ \text{A}$$

Note: the parallel block now behaves as a single $2.0\ \Omega$ resistor in series with $R_1$, so resistances simply add.
$R_T = 10.0\ \Omega,\ I_T = 1.2\ \text{A}$

Part (b) — p.d. across R1 and the parallel section [2 marks] · 1 step

Carry-in from (a): $I_T = 1.2\ \text{A}$ (same current through the series resistor $R_1 = 8.0\ \Omega$); $\varepsilon = 12.0\ \text{V}$.
↳ from STEP 1: $I_T = 1.2\ \text{A}$ flows through $R_1 = 8.0\ \Omega$; $\varepsilon = 12.0\ \text{V}$
STEP 1/1 M1 A1

Formula (Ohm's law on the series element, then KVL):

$$V_1 = I_T R_1, \qquad V_{23} = \varepsilon - V_1$$

Substitute $I_T = 1.2\ \text{A}$, $R_1 = 8.0\ \Omega$:

$$V_1 = 1.2 \times 8.0 = 9.6\ \text{V}$$

$$V_{23} = 12.0 - 9.6 = 2.4\ \text{V}$$

Parallel rule: both $R_2$ and $R_3$ have the same $2.4\ \text{V}$ across them — they share the two nodes of the parallel section. KVL gives this p.d. as the e.m.f. minus the series drop.
$V_1 = 9.6\ \text{V},\ V_{23} = 2.4\ \text{V}$

Part (c) — branch currents and KCL check [2 marks] · 1 step

Carry-in from (a) & (b): the parallel section has $V_{23} = 2.4\ \text{V}$ across both branches; $R_2 = 3.0\ \Omega$, $R_3 = 6.0\ \Omega$; total current $I_T = 1.2\ \text{A}$.
↳ from STEP 1: $V_2 = V_3 = V_{23} = 2.4\ \text{V}$; $I_T = 1.2\ \text{A}$
STEP 1/1 A1 A1

Formula (Ohm's law per branch — same p.d. across each):

$$I_2 = \dfrac{V_{23}}{R_2}, \qquad I_3 = \dfrac{V_{23}}{R_3}$$

Substitute $V_{23} = 2.4\ \text{V}$:

$$I_2 = \dfrac{2.4}{3.0} = 0.80\ \text{A}, \qquad I_3 = \dfrac{2.4}{6.0} = 0.40\ \text{A}$$

KCL check: $I_2 + I_3 = 0.80 + 0.40 = 1.20\ \text{A} = I_T$ ✓. The branch currents recombine to the total. The smaller resistor ($R_2$) carries the larger current — current prefers the easier path.
$I_2 = 0.80\ \text{A},\ I_3 = 0.40\ \text{A}$
