Q7 — Terminal velocity: derive and evaluate

Question

(a) State the condition for terminal velocity and explain why a maximum speed exists.

(b) Derive $v_t = \dfrac{2r^2 g(\rho_s-\rho_f)}{9\eta}$ for a sphere of density $\rho_s$, radius $r$, in fluid of density $\rho_f$, viscosity $\eta$.

(c) A steel ball of $r = 1.0\ \text{mm}$, $\rho_s = 7800\ \text{kg m}^{-3}$ falls through oil ($\rho_f = 880\ \text{kg m}^{-3}$, $\eta = 0.85\ \text{Pa s}$). Find $v_t$.

[8 marks]

Part (a) — condition for v_t [2 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2 B1

At terminal velocity the resultant force is zero, so the acceleration is zero and the speed is constant:

$$W = U + F_D$$

STEP 2/2 B1

Drag rises with speed ($F_D = 6\pi\eta r v$). As the body speeds up, drag grows until it (plus upthrust) just cancels the weight; the net force then vanishes, so the body settles onto one stable maximum speed.

Phuc's slip (§9.5): a smaller downward resultant still means speeding up — deceleration needs an upward resultant.
$a=0$ when $W = U + F_D$

Part (b) — derivation [4 marks] · 4 steps

Carry-in from (a): start from $W = U + F_D$ with $V=\tfrac{4}{3}\pi r^3$, $F_D=6\pi\eta r v_t$.
STEP 1/4 M1

Substitute the three force formulas:

$$\rho_s\!\left(\tfrac{4}{3}\pi r^3\right)\!g = \rho_f\!\left(\tfrac{4}{3}\pi r^3\right)\!g + 6\pi\eta r v_t$$

STEP 2/4 M1

Collect the drag term:

$$6\pi\eta r v_t = \left(\tfrac{4}{3}\pi r^3 g\right)(\rho_s-\rho_f)$$

STEP 3/4 A1

Divide by $6\pi\eta r$ and simplify $4/18 = 2/9$, cancel $\pi$ and one power of $r$:

$$\boxed{\,v_t = \frac{2r^2 g(\rho_s-\rho_f)}{9\eta}\,}$$

$v_t = \dfrac{2r^2 g(\rho_s-\rho_f)}{9\eta}$
STEP 4/4 B1

Dependencies (worth a sentence): $v_t \propto r^2$, $v_t \propto 1/\eta$, $v_t \propto (\rho_s-\rho_f)$.

This formula is examinable but NOT on the data sheet — you must derive it.

Part (c) — evaluate v_t [2 marks] · 2 steps

Carry-in from (b): $v_t = \dfrac{2r^2 g(\rho_s-\rho_f)}{9\eta}$; $r=1.0\times10^{-3}$, $\rho_s=7800$, $\rho_f=880$, $\eta=0.85$.
STEP 1/2 M1

Substitute:

$$v_t = \frac{2(1.0\times10^{-3})^2(9.81)(7800-880)}{9(0.85)}$$

Numerator $= 2\times10^{-6}\times9.81\times6920 = 0.1358$; denominator $= 7.65$.

STEP 2/2 A1

$$v_t = \frac{0.1358}{7.65} = 1.8\times10^{-2}\ \text{m s}^{-1}\ (2\ \text{s.f.})$$

Pitfalls: use radius not diameter ($v_t\propto r^2$); use the difference $(\rho_s-\rho_f)$, not $\rho_s$ alone; keep $r$ in metres.
$v_t \approx 1.8\times10^{-2}\ \text{m s}^{-1}$
