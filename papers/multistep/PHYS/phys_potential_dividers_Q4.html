Edexcel IAL Physics · Unit 2 (WPH12) · Potential Dividers · Worked Example 4 (lesson bonus, 3-mark chain)

Thermistor sensor — explain the change in VR (mark per step)

Question

In the thermistor divider of the previous question (thermistor $R_{\text{th}}$ in series with a fixed resistor $R$ across a $9.0\ \text{V}$ supply), the temperature of the thermistor increases.

(a) State and explain what happens to the p.d. across the fixed resistor $R$. [3 marks]

This is a 3-mark “state and explain” — the examiner wants the full chain, one mark per link.

[3 marks]

Part (a) — the three-link chain [3 marks] · 3 steps

STEP 1/3 B1 (link 1)

Link 1 — resistance change (NTC behaviour):

Temperature increases $\Rightarrow$ the thermistor resistance $R_{\text{th}}$ decreases.

$$\uparrow T \;\Rightarrow\; \downarrow R_{\text{th}}$$

Mark-scheme wording: name the behaviour — an NTC (negative temperature coefficient) thermistor loses resistance as it warms, because electrons gain thermal energy and flow more easily.
$\uparrow T \Rightarrow \downarrow R_{\text{th}}$
↳ from STEP 2: from link 1: $\downarrow R_{\text{th}}$ (so total resistance falls, current $I$ rises)
STEP 2/3 B1 (link 2)

Link 2 — voltage across the thermistor:

Total resistance falls $\Rightarrow$ current $I$ rises. Using $V = IR$ for the thermistor, its smaller $R_{\text{th}}$ takes a smaller share, so $V_{\text{th}}$ decreases.

$$\downarrow R_{\text{th}} \;\Rightarrow\; \downarrow V_{\text{th}}$$

Revision-guide trap — “R = IV” is wrong: Ohm's law is $V = IR$, so $R = V/I$ and $I = V/R$. Phuc stated the rearrangement the wrong way round in the lesson before correcting with the “Very Itchy Rash” ($V = IR$) mnemonic. Quote $V = IR$ to justify $V_{\text{th}} \propto R_{\text{th}}$.
$\downarrow R_{\text{th}} \Rightarrow \downarrow V_{\text{th}}$
↳ from STEP 3: from link 2: $\downarrow V_{\text{th}}$; KVL: $V_R = V_{\text{in}} - V_{\text{th}}$ with $V_{\text{in}} = 9.0\ \text{V}$ fixed
STEP 3/3 B1 (link 3)

Link 3 — voltage across the fixed resistor (KVL):

By Kirchhoff's voltage law the two p.d.s add to the fixed supply:

$$V_R = V_{\text{in}} - V_{\text{th}}$$

$V_{\text{in}}$ is constant and $V_{\text{th}}$ has decreased, so $V_R$ increases.

$$\downarrow V_{\text{th}} \;\Rightarrow\; \uparrow V_R$$

Full chain for the 3 marks: $\uparrow T \Rightarrow \downarrow R_{\text{th}} \Rightarrow \downarrow V_{\text{th}} \Rightarrow \uparrow V_R$. All three links must be explicit — stating only the final answer scores 1 of 3.
$V_R$ increases  (full chain: $\uparrow T \Rightarrow \downarrow R_{\text{th}} \Rightarrow \downarrow V_{\text{th}} \Rightarrow \uparrow V_R$)
