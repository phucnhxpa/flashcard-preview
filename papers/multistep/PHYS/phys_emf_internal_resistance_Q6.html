Self-Test Q7 — Parallel resistors with internal resistance

Question

Two resistors of $6.0\ \Omega$ and $3.0\ \Omega$ are connected in parallel and then connected to a battery with e.m.f. $\varepsilon = 12.0\ \text{V}$ and internal resistance $r = 1.0\ \Omega$.

(a) Find the resistance of the parallel combination $R_p$. [2 marks]
(b) Find the current drawn from the battery. [1 mark]
(c) Hence find the terminal p.d. [2 marks]

[5 marks]

Part (a) — parallel combination [2 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 M1 A1

Formula (resistors in parallel):

$$\dfrac{1}{R_p} = \dfrac{1}{R_1} + \dfrac{1}{R_2}$$

Substitute $R_1 = 6.0\ \Omega$, $R_2 = 3.0\ \Omega$:

$$\dfrac{1}{R_p} = \dfrac{1}{6.0} + \dfrac{1}{3.0} = \dfrac{1}{6.0} + \dfrac{2}{6.0} = \dfrac{3}{6.0} = \dfrac{1}{2.0}$$

$$R_p = 2.0\ \Omega$$

Trap: $R_p$ is the reciprocal of the sum of reciprocals — do not stop at $1/R_p = 1/2$ and report $0.5\ \Omega$. The parallel value is always smaller than the smallest resistor ($< 3.0\ \Omega$ here).
$R_p = 2.0\ \Omega$

Part (b) — current from battery [1 marks] · 1 step

Carry-in from (a): parallel load $R_p = 2.0\ \Omega$; $\varepsilon = 12.0\ \text{V}$, $r = 1.0\ \Omega$.
↳ from STEP 1: $R_p = 2.0\ \Omega$, $\varepsilon = 12.0\ \text{V}$, $r = 1.0\ \Omega$
STEP 1/1 A1

Formula (current with internal resistance):

$$I = \dfrac{\varepsilon}{R_p + r}$$

Substitute $\varepsilon = 12.0$, $R_p = 2.0$, $r = 1.0$:

$$I = \dfrac{12.0}{2.0 + 1.0} = \dfrac{12.0}{3.0} = 4.0\ \text{A}$$

Note: this is the total current through the battery (and through $r$), which then splits between the two parallel branches.
$I = 4.0\ \text{A}$

Part (c) — terminal p.d. [2 marks] · 1 step

Carry-in from (a) & (b): $\varepsilon = 12.0\ \text{V}$, $r = 1.0\ \Omega$, current $I = 4.0\ \text{A}$, parallel load $R_p = 2.0\ \Omega$.
↳ from STEP 1: $\varepsilon = 12.0\ \text{V}$, $I = 4.0\ \text{A}$, $r = 1.0\ \Omega$
STEP 1/1 M1 A1

Formula (terminal p.d.):

$$V = \varepsilon - Ir$$

Substitute $\varepsilon = 12.0$, $I = 4.0$, $r = 1.0$:

$$V = 12.0 - (4.0)(1.0) = 12.0 - 4.0 = 8.0\ \text{V}$$

Cross-check: the terminal p.d. is also the p.d. across $R_p$: $V = IR_p = 4.0 \times 2.0 = 8.0\ \text{V}$ ✓.
$V = 8.0\ \text{V}$
