Q — Find $I_C$, $V_A$, $V_B$ and $R_B$ in a series–parallel network

Question

Resistor $C$ is in series with a parallel pair: branch $A$ has $R_A = 12\ \Omega$ carrying $I_A = 0.8\ \text{A}$; branch $B$ carries $I_B = 1.2\ \text{A}$.

(a) Find the current through $C$, $I_C$. [1 mark]
(b) Find the p.d. across $A$ and across $B$. [2 marks]
(c) Hence find $R_B$. [1 mark]

[4 marks]

Part (a) — current through C [1 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 M1

Formula (Kirchhoff's first law — start here, tutor's advice):

$$\sum I_{\text{in}} = \sum I_{\text{out}} \quad\Rightarrow\quad I_C = I_A + I_B$$

The series resistor $C$ carries the total current, which then splits between branches $A$ and $B$. Substitute $I_A = 0.8\ \text{A}$, $I_B = 1.2\ \text{A}$:

$$I_C = 0.8 + 1.2 = 2.0\ \text{A}$$

Why start with KCL: the two branch currents are given, so charge conservation gives $I_C$ immediately. $C$ is in series with the whole parallel block, so it carries the sum.
$I_C = 2.0\ \text{A}$

Part (b) — p.d. across A and B [2 marks] · 1 step

Carry-in from (a): branch currents $I_A = 0.8\ \text{A}$, $I_B = 1.2\ \text{A}$, and $R_A = 12\ \Omega$. $A$ and $B$ are in parallel.
↳ from STEP 1: $I_A = 0.8\ \text{A}$, $R_A = 12\ \Omega$; $A$ and $B$ are parallel
STEP 1/1 M1 A1

Formula (Ohm's law on branch $A$, then parallel rule):

$$V_A = I_A R_A, \qquad V_B = V_A \;(\text{parallel})$$

Substitute $I_A = 0.8\ \text{A}$, $R_A = 12\ \Omega$:

$$V_A = 0.8 \times 12 = 9.6\ \text{V}$$

$$V_B = V_A = 9.6\ \text{V}$$

Parallel rule: $A$ and $B$ share the same two nodes, so they have the same p.d. across them. Compute $V_A$ from the known branch, then carry it straight across to $B$.
$V_A = V_B = 9.6\ \text{V}$

Part (c) — resistance of B [1 marks] · 1 step

Carry-in from (a) & (b): $I_B = 1.2\ \text{A}$ and $V_B = 9.6\ \text{V}$.
↳ from STEP 1: $V_B = 9.6\ \text{V}$, $I_B = 1.2\ \text{A}$
STEP 1/1 A1

Formula (definition of resistance — never $R = IV$):

$$R_B = \dfrac{V_B}{I_B}$$

Substitute $V_B = 9.6\ \text{V}$, $I_B = 1.2\ \text{A}$:

$$R_B = \dfrac{9.6}{1.2} = 8.0\ \Omega$$

Common trap (R = IV): always $R = V/I$. The lower-resistance branch ($B$, $8.0\ \Omega$) carries the larger current ($1.2\ \text{A}$) — consistent with current preferring the easier path.
$R_B = 8.0\ \Omega$
