Edexcel IAL Physics · Unit 2 (WPH12) · Potential Dividers · Worked Example 2 (lesson Q3a-c, potentiometer)

Potentiometer wire — Vout across XB for three slider positions

Question

A uniform resistance wire $AB$ has a $12.0\ \text{V}$ supply connected across it. A sliding contact $X$ taps off the output p.d. across the lower section $XB$. For a uniform wire, resistance is proportional to length, so the divider ratio equals the length ratio.

Find $V_{\text{out}}$ across $XB$ when:
(a) $AX = \tfrac{1}{2}AB$. [1 mark]
(b) $AX = \tfrac{1}{3}AB$. [1 mark]
(c) $AX = \tfrac{3}{4}AB$. [1 mark]

[3 marks]

Part (a) — slider at the midpoint [1 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 B1

Formula (output is the lower-section fraction of $V_{\text{in}}$):

$$V_{\text{out}} = V_{\text{in}} \cdot \dfrac{XB}{AB}$$

Find $XB$: if $AX = \tfrac{1}{2}AB$, then $XB = AB - AX = \tfrac{1}{2}AB$.

Substitute $V_{\text{in}} = 12.0\ \text{V}$, $XB/AB = \tfrac{1}{2}$:

$$V_{\text{out}} = \tfrac{1}{2} \times 12.0 = 6.0\ \text{V}$$

Key idea: the output is across $XB$ (the lower section), so $XB$ goes on top of the fraction — not the tapped-off length $AX$. Slider at the midpoint $\Rightarrow$ half the supply.
$V_{\text{out}} = 6.0\ \text{V}$

Part (b) — AX = ⅓ AB [1 marks] · 1 step

Carry-in: same wire, $V_{\text{in}} = 12.0\ \text{V}$; output is across the lower section $XB$, so $V_{\text{out}} = V_{\text{in}}\,(XB/AB)$.
↳ from STEP 1: $V_{\text{in}} = 12.0\ \text{V}$; $V_{\text{out}} = V_{\text{in}}\,(XB/AB)$
STEP 1/1 B1

Find $XB$: if $AX = \tfrac{1}{3}AB$, then $XB = AB - \tfrac{1}{3}AB = \tfrac{2}{3}AB$.

Substitute $XB/AB = \tfrac{2}{3}$:

$$V_{\text{out}} = \tfrac{2}{3} \times 12.0 = 8.0\ \text{V}$$

Common trap: using $AX = \tfrac{1}{3}$ directly gives $4.0\ \text{V}$, which is the p.d. across $AX$, not the output $V_{\text{out}}$ across $XB$. Convert to the lower-section fraction first: $XB = \tfrac{2}{3}AB$.
$V_{\text{out}} = 8.0\ \text{V}$

Part (c) — AX = ¾ AB [1 marks] · 1 step

Carry-in: same wire, $V_{\text{in}} = 12.0\ \text{V}$; output is across the lower section $XB$, so $V_{\text{out}} = V_{\text{in}}\,(XB/AB)$.
↳ from STEP 1: $V_{\text{in}} = 12.0\ \text{V}$; $V_{\text{out}} = V_{\text{in}}\,(XB/AB)$
STEP 1/1 B1

Find $XB$: if $AX = \tfrac{3}{4}AB$, then $XB = AB - \tfrac{3}{4}AB = \tfrac{1}{4}AB$.

Substitute $XB/AB = \tfrac{1}{4}$:

$$V_{\text{out}} = \tfrac{1}{4} \times 12.0 = 3.0\ \text{V}$$

Sense check: moving the slider down (larger $AX$) leaves a shorter $XB$, so $V_{\text{out}}$ falls. Range is $0$ (slider at $B$) up to $12.0\ \text{V}$ (slider at $A$) — that is the whole point of a potentiometer.
$V_{\text{out}} = 3.0\ \text{V}$
