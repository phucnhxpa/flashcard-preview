The loading-unloading test: reading permanent strain off the graph

Question

A ductile metal sample is loaded past its elastic limit and then the load is removed. (a) Explain how the loading-unloading graph distinguishes elastic from plastic deformation. (b) On the unloading graph shown, describe how to read off the permanent (plastic) strain and the recovered (elastic) strain.

[4 marks]

Part (a) — elastic vs plastic from unloading path [2 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2elastic case

Within the elastic region. Unloading retraces the loading line back to the origin: no permanent extension and all the stored energy is returned.

Elastic: unloading returns to the origin.
STEP 2/2plastic case

Beyond the elastic limit. The unloading line is straight and parallel to the original elastic line, but it meets the strain axis at a non-zero value — a permanent (plastic) strain remains.

Plastic: unloading line is parallel, with an offset intercept.

Part (b) — reading the two strains [2 marks] · 2 steps

Carry-in: the unloading line is parallel to the elastic line and starts at the release point.
STEP 1/2permanent strain

Permanent strain. Follow the parallel unloading line down to zero load; the strain-axis intercept it leaves behind is the permanent (plastic) strain.

Permanent strain = strain-axis intercept of the unloading line.
↳ from STEP 2: release-point strain minus the permanent-strain intercept
STEP 2/2recovered strain

Recovered (elastic) strain. This is the horizontal distance from the release point back to the unloading line — i.e. the total strain at release minus the permanent strain.

Recovered strain = total strain at release − permanent strain.
