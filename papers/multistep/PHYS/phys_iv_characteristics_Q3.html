Edexcel IAL Physics · Unit 2 (WPH12) · I–V Characteristics · Topic 2.4 · Worked Example 3 (filament lamp)

Filament Lamp Q — Show R increases as the lamp heats up

Question

A tungsten filament lamp is rated $12\ \text{V}, 24\ \text{W}$.

(a) Find the current and hence the resistance at its rated operating conditions. [2 marks]
(b) From the I–V graph, at $V = 8.0\ \text{V}$ the current is $I = 1.62\ \text{A}$; at $I = 0.80\ \text{A}$ the p.d. is $V = 2.0\ \text{V}$. Find the resistance at each point. [2 marks]
(c) State what the trend in $R$ shows and explain it. [2 marks]

[6 marks]

Part (a) — R at rated 12 V, 24 W [2 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 M1 A1

Formula (current from power):

$$P = IV \quad\Rightarrow\quad I = \dfrac{P}{V}$$

Substitute $P = 24\ \text{W}$, $V = 12\ \text{V}$:

$$I = \dfrac{24}{12} = 2.0\ \text{A}$$

Then $R = V/I$:

$$R = \dfrac{12}{2.0} = 6.0\ \Omega$$

$I = 2.0\ \text{A}$, $R = 6.0\ \Omega$ (rated)

Part (b) — R at two dimmer points [2 marks] · 1 step

Carry-in from (a): rated $R = 6.0\ \Omega$ at $12\ \text{V}$. Graph points: $(8.0\ \text{V},\,1.62\ \text{A})$ and $(2.0\ \text{V},\,0.80\ \text{A})$. Use $R = V/I$ at each point (non-ohmic — never the gradient).
↳ from STEP 1: rated $R = 6.0\ \Omega$; points $(8.0\,\text{V},1.62\,\text{A})$, $(2.0\,\text{V},0.80\,\text{A})$
STEP 1/1 A1 A1

At $V = 8.0\ \text{V}$, $I = 1.62\ \text{A}$:

$$R = \dfrac{V}{I} = \dfrac{8.0}{1.62} = 4.9\ \Omega$$

At $I = 0.80\ \text{A}$, $V = 2.0\ \text{V}$:

$$R = \dfrac{V}{I} = \dfrac{2.0}{0.80} = 2.5\ \Omega$$

Read R at the point: each $R$ comes from the actual $(V,I)$ pair, $R=V/I$ — not from the slope of the curved characteristic.
$R = 4.9\ \Omega$ at $8.0\ \text{V}$; $R = 2.5\ \Omega$ at $2.0\ \text{V}$

Part (c) — trend and physical reason [2 marks] · 1 step

Carry-in from (a) & (b): $R$ values are $6.0\ \Omega \to 4.9\ \Omega \to 2.5\ \Omega$ as the lamp gets dimmer (smaller $V$ and $I$).
↳ from STEP 1: $R: 6.0 \to 4.9 \to 2.5\ \Omega$ as the lamp dims
STEP 1/1 B1 B1

Trend: as the lamp dims (lower $V$, $I$) the resistance falls; equivalently, as it brightens the resistance rises. The lamp is non-ohmic.

Physical explanation (2 marks):

(1) A larger current heats the tungsten filament ($I^2R$ resistive heating), raising its temperature.

(2) At higher temperature the lattice ions vibrate with greater amplitude, so conduction electrons collide with them more frequently — this increases the resistance.

Direction trap: a filament lamp's $R$ increases with temperature (curve bows toward the $V$-axis). A thermistor does the opposite.
$R$ rises with temperature → lamp is non-ohmic (more frequent electron–ion collisions)
