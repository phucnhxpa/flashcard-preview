CP3: determining the Young modulus of a wire (Searle's apparatus)

Question

Describe how Searle's apparatus is used to determine the Young modulus of a metal in the form of a wire. (a) State the measurements taken and the instrument for each. (b) State how stress and strain are obtained and how the Young modulus is found from a graph. (c) State the purpose of the control wire and one safety precaution.

[6 marks]

Part (a) — measurements and instruments [2 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2diameter & length

Diameter. Measure the wire's diameter at several points and orientations with a micrometer screw gauge; average it. The original length between the clamps is measured with a metre rule.

Diameter: micrometer; original length: metre rule.
STEP 2/2load & extension

Load and extension. Add known slotted masses; the force is $F = mg$. The small extension of the test wire is read on the vernier scale (levelled with a spirit level) against the control wire.

Force from $F = mg$; extension from the vernier scale.

Part (b) — stress, strain and the graph [3 marks] · 3 steps

Carry-in: from (a) — area $A = \pi(d/2)^2$, original length $L$, force $F = mg$, extension $\Delta x$.
STEP 1/3stress

Stress. Use the original area from the averaged diameter:

$$A = \pi\left(\tfrac{d}{2}\right)^2,\qquad \sigma = \frac{F}{A}$$
$\sigma = F/A$ with $A = \pi(d/2)^2$.
STEP 2/3strain

Strain. Divide each extension by the original length:

$$\varepsilon = \frac{\Delta x}{L}$$
$\varepsilon = \Delta x / L$
↳ from STEP 3: paired $(\varepsilon, \sigma)$ values
STEP 3/3gradient = E

Graph. Plot stress against strain. The gradient of the straight (linear) part is the Young modulus:

$$E = \frac{\Delta\sigma}{\Delta\varepsilon}$$

Taking a gradient from many points is more reliable than a single pair.

$E = $ gradient of the linear part of the $\sigma$–$\varepsilon$ graph.

Part (c) — control wire and safety [1 marks] · 1 step

Carry-in: two identical wires hang from the same support; only one is loaded.
STEP 1/1control + safety

Control wire. It compensates for sagging of the support and for thermal expansion, so the vernier measures only the extension of the test wire due to the load.

Safety. Wear safety goggles (the wire can snap) and place a sand tray beneath the masses to cushion any fall.

Control wire cancels support sag & temperature; goggles + sand tray for safety.
