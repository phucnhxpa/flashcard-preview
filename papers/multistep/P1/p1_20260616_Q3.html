Part (a) — Find A from f''(4) = 0 [4 marks] · 5 steps

$f'(x) = 2x^{-1/2} + Ax^{-2} + 3$, where $A$ is a constant. Given that $f''(4) = 0$, find the exact value of $A$. You must show all stages of your working; solutions relying on calculator technology are not acceptable.
STEP 1/5differentiate f'(x) term by term M1

Strategy. To use $f''(4) = 0$, first find $f''(x)$ by differentiating $f'(x) = 2x^{-1/2} + Ax^{-2} + 3$ term by term. Apply the power rule: multiply by the power, subtract 1 from the power.

Differentiate each term

$$\frac{d}{dx}\bigl[2x^{-1/2}\bigr] = 2\cdot\!\left(-\tfrac{1}{2}\right)\!x^{-3/2} = -x^{-3/2}.$$$$\frac{d}{dx}\bigl[Ax^{-2}\bigr] = A\cdot(-2)x^{-3} = -2Ax^{-3}.$$$$\frac{d}{dx}[3] = 0.$$

Combine

$$f''(x) = -x^{-3/2} - 2Ax^{-3}.$$

STEP 2/5substitute x = 4 M1

Strategy. Set $f''(4) = 0$ and substitute $x = 4$ into each term. Compute the powers of 4 without a calculator using the rules of indices.

Evaluate powers

$$4^{-3/2} = \frac{1}{4^{3/2}} = \frac{1}{(\sqrt{4})^3} = \frac{1}{2^3} = \frac{1}{8}.$$$$4^{-3} = \frac{1}{4^3} = \frac{1}{64}.$$
Index rules, no calculator. The question states “you must show all stages of your working; solutions relying on calculator technology are not acceptable.” Write each index expansion explicitly: $4^{3/2} = (4^{1/2})^3 = 2^3 = 8$.
↳ from STEP 2: $4^{-3/2}=\tfrac18,\; 4^{-3}=\tfrac{1}{64}$
STEP 3/5set up equation in A A1

Strategy. Substitute the evaluated powers into $f''(4) = 0$ to get a linear equation in $A$.

Substitute

$$f''(4) = -\frac{1}{8} - 2A\cdot\frac{1}{64} = 0$$$$-\frac{1}{8} - \frac{A}{32} = 0.$$

Sense check. Both terms are negative (since $A$ will turn out negative), so the equation can balance at zero.

↳ from STEP 3: $-\tfrac18 - \tfrac{A}{32} = 0$
STEP 4/5solve for A A1

Strategy. Rearrange: move the constant term to the RHS, then multiply by $-32$ to isolate $A$.

Rearrange

$$-\frac{A}{32} = \frac{1}{8} \quad\Longrightarrow\quad A = -32 \cdot \frac{1}{8} = -4.$$

Sense check. With $A = -4$: $f''(4) = -\tfrac18 - \tfrac{-4}{32} = -\tfrac18 + \tfrac18 = 0$ — balances.

↳ from STEP 4: $A = -4$
STEP 5/5verify and box A1

Strategy. Box the answer and verify by re-substitution. Note the conceptual lesson: even though $A = -4$ is numerically correct, Phuc scored 0/4 because the method was wrong.

Final answer

$$\boxed{A = -4}.$$
