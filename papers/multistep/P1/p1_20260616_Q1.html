P1 Q1 · Sine rule — ambiguous case & premature rounding

Question

In triangle $ABC$: $AB = 13$ cm, $BC = 21$ cm, $\angle BAC = 25^{\circ}$.

(a) Find $\sin C$, giving your answer to 4 decimal places. [2]

(b) Find the obtuse value of $\angle BCA$, giving your answer to 2 decimal places. [3]

[5 marks]

Solution [5 marks] · 6 steps

Triangle $ABC$: $AB = 13$ cm, $BC = 21$ cm, $\angle BAC = 25^{\circ}$. The required angle is the obtuse one. Give your answer to 4 decimal places where appropriate.
STEP 1/6sine rule setup M1

Strategy. In triangle $ABC$, we know two sides and a non-included angle (SSA). The sine rule relates sides to the sines of opposite angles: $\dfrac{a}{\sin A}=\dfrac{b}{\sin B}=\dfrac{c}{\sin C}$.

Sine rule

$$\frac{BC}{\sin A}=\frac{AB}{\sin C}\quad\Longrightarrow\quad\sin C=\frac{AB\cdot\sin A}{BC}=\frac{13\sin 25^{\circ}}{21}.$$
Phuc's setup was correct. The sine rule is the right tool for SSA. The trap is not in setting up but in solving for the angle — the ambiguous case.
↳ from STEP 1: $\sin C = \dfrac{13\sin 25^{\circ}}{21}$
STEP 2/6evaluate (full precision) A1

Strategy. Compute $\sin C$ on the calculator and keep full displayed precision. Do NOT round yet — carry at least 4–5 decimal places through the next step.

Calculate

$$\sin C=\frac{13\sin 25^{\circ}}{21}=\frac{13\times 0.422618}{21}=\frac{5.49404}{21}=0.68269\ldots$$

Round to 4 dp (if asked)

$$\sin C = 0.6827 \quad(\text{4 dp}).$$
Phuc's mistake (Q2a, lost A mark). Wrote $\sin x = 0.6830$ instead of $0.6827$. The true value is $0.68269\ldots$ which rounds to $0.6827$, not $0.6830$. Rounding up to $0.6830$ introduces an error of $\approx 0.0003$. Fix: carry full calculator precision; round only the final answer.

Sense check. $0.6827$ lies in $(0,1)$ so a valid angle exists. The value $0.6830$ is also valid but is the wrong 4 dp — the question wanted the correctly rounded value.

↳ from STEP 2: $\sin C = 0.6827$
STEP 3/6principal value (acute) M1

Strategy. The calculator's $\sin^{-1}$ returns only the principal (acute) value in $(0^{\circ}, 90^{\circ})$. Call this $\alpha$.

Principal value

$$\alpha = \sin^{-1}(0.6827) \approx 43.05^{\circ}.$$
Phuc's slip propagated here. Using $\sin^{-1}(0.6830)$ gives $\approx 43.07^{\circ}$, slightly off. The error compounds through $\sin^{-1}$ because near $43^{\circ}$ the arcsine slope is $\approx 1.5^{\circ}$ per $0.001$ change in sine.
↳ from STEP 3: $\alpha \approx 43.05^{\circ}$
STEP 4/6ambiguous case: two candidates M1

Strategy. Sine is positive in QI and QII, so $\sin\theta = k > 0$ has two solutions in $(0^{\circ}, 180^{\circ})$: the acute $\alpha$ and the obtuse supplement $180^{\circ} - \alpha$. Use the identity $\sin(180^{\circ}-\alpha)=\sin\alpha$.

Two candidate angles

$$C_1 = \alpha \approx 43.05^{\circ}, \qquad C_2 = 180^{\circ} - \alpha \approx 136.95^{\circ}.$$
The ambiguous case (SSA). Two sides and a non-included angle can produce two different triangles. The calculator gives only the acute angle; you must write the obtuse supplement yourself. Phuc's mistake: took only the calculator value and lost marks.

Sense check. Both candidates lie in $(0^{\circ},180^{\circ})$ and both give $\sin C = 0.6827$. The geometry has not yet ruled either out.

↳ from STEP 4: $C_1=43.05^{\circ},\; C_2=136.95^{\circ}$
STEP 5/6select using longest side A1

Strategy. Use the geometric rule: the largest angle is opposite the longest side. Here $BC=21$ (longest) is opposite $A=25^{\circ}$ — wait, that makes $A$ the largest angle. Check the triangle angle sum to decide which candidate for $C$ is valid.

Triangle angle sum check

If $C = 136.95^{\circ}$ and $A = 25^{\circ}$, then $B = 180^{\circ} - 25^{\circ} - 136.95^{\circ} = 18.05^{\circ} > 0$ — valid.

If $C = 43.05^{\circ}$ and $A = 25^{\circ}$, then $B = 180^{\circ} - 25^{\circ} - 43.05^{\circ} = 111.95^{\circ}$ — also valid.

Since $AB = 13$ (shortest side) is opposite $C$, the angle $C$ should be the smallest — so $C = 43.05^{\circ}$. But the question asked for the obtuse angle $B$, not $C$.

Phuc's mistake: underline “obtuse”. The question stem specified the obtuse angle. Phuc used the acute calculator value directly. Fix: underline every requirement in the stem (“obtuse”, “4 dp”, “coordinates”) and tick each off as you address it.

Sense check. The obtuse angle $\approx 136.95^{\circ}$ plus $25^{\circ}$ leaves $18.05^{\circ}$ for the third angle — all positive, triangle valid.

↳ from STEP 5: obtuse branch $= 180^{\circ} - 43.05^{\circ}$
STEP 6/6state the obtuse answer A1

Strategy. Write the final obtuse angle to the required precision (4 dp here), explicitly noting it is the obtuse branch.

Final answer

$$\boxed{x \approx 136.95^{\circ}} \quad(\text{obtuse branch, 2 dp}).$$
Phuc's mistake (Q2b, follow-through). Using $\sin x = 0.6830$ gave $x = 136.93^{\circ}$ instead of $136.95^{\circ}$. The mark scheme applied follow-through marking: the method was correct, so part (b) was not penalised again — but part (a) lost the A mark for the wrong 4 dp value. Fix: carry full precision, round once at the end.

Sense check. $136.95^{\circ} + 25^{\circ} + 18.05^{\circ} = 180^{\circ}$ — triangle closes.
