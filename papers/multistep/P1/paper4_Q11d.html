Q11(d) — Define $R$ by inequalities (4 boundaries)

Question

Curve $C\!:\; y = 2x^2 - 12x + 14$. From earlier parts: completing the square gives $2(x-3)^2 - 4$, so the minimum is $P = (3, -4)$. The curve also passes through $(-1, 28)$. Line $l$ passes through $(-1, 28)$ and $P$, with equation $y = -8x + 20$.

The region $R$ is bounded by four things: the $x$-axis, the line $l$, the $y$-axis, and the curve $C$ — and lies entirely in the first quadrant.

(d) Define $R$ by four inequalities. [3]

[3 marks]

Solution [3 marks] · 3 steps

From (a)–(c): $C\!:\, y = 2(x-3)^2 - 4$, $P = (3, -4)$, line $l\!:\, y = -8x + 20$. Curve $y$-int $(0, 14)$, line $y$-int $(0, 20)$, line meets $x$-axis at $(2.5, 0)$, curve meets $x$-axis at $x = 3 \pm \sqrt{2}$.
STEP 1/3

Strategy: before writing inequalities, identify what the four boundary pieces ARE and where they meet. Trace the boundary of $R$ in order, listing each piece.

Boundary trace (counter-clockwise, starting at $(0, 14)$ where curve meets $y$-axis):

$\;\;\;(i)$ Travel up along the $y$-axis from $(0, 14)$ to $(0, 20)$ — meets line $l$.

$\;\;\;(ii)$ Travel down-right along line $l: y = -8x + 20$ from $(0, 20)$ to $(2.5, 0)$ — meets the $x$-axis.

$\;\;\;(iii)$ Travel left along the $x$-axis from $(2.5, 0)$ to $(3 - \sqrt{2}, 0) \approx (1.586, 0)$ — meets curve $C$.

$\;\;\;(iv)$ Travel up along curve $C$ back to $(0, 14)$.

Four boundary pieces:

$\;\;\;\bullet\;\;y$-axis ($x = 0$)

$\;\;\;\bullet\;\;$line $l$ ($y = -8x + 20$)

$\;\;\;\bullet\;\;x$-axis ($y = 0$)

$\;\;\;\bullet\;\;$curve $C$ ($y = 2x^2 - 12x + 14$)

Four boundaries ⇒ four inequalities. Each piece of the boundary contributes ONE inequality whose direction is determined by which side of that piece $R$ sits on. The next step uses a sample-point test to fix each direction. Why this region is "in the first quadrant": the $y$-axis ($x = 0$) and $x$-axis ($y = 0$) both bound $R$, so $R$ has $x \geq 0$ and $y \geq 0$.
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: 4 boundaries: $y$-axis, line $l$, $x$-axis, curve $C$
STEP 2/3 M1

Pick a clear interior point, e.g. $(1, 6)$ (inside $R$ from the diagram).

BoundaryAt $(1,6)$⇒ inequality
$y$-axis: $x = 0$$x = 1 > 0$$x \geq 0$
$x$-axis: $y = 0$$y = 6 > 0$$y \geq 0$
line $l$: $y = -8x+20$$6 < -8(1)+20 = 12$$y \leq -8x+20$
curve $C$: $y = 2x^2-12x+14$$6 > 2(1)-12+14 = 4$$y \geq 2x^2-12x+14$
Watch the curve direction! Many students write "$y \leq$ curve" by reflex — but $R$ is above the curve here, since the curve dips below the line. Sample-point check is decisive.
↳ from STEP 2:From STEP 2: $x \geq 0$, $y \geq 0$, $y \leq -8x+20$, $y \geq 2x^2-12x+14$
STEP 3/3 A1 A1

Strategy: assemble the four inequalities from the sample-point test into a single system of conditions defining $R$. Verify they all hold at the test point $(1, 6)$.

From the sample-point test:

$\;\;\;y$-axis ⇒ $x \geq 0$

$\;\;\;x$-axis ⇒ $y \geq 0$

$\;\;\;$Line $l$ ⇒ $y \leq -8x + 20$

$\;\;\;$Curve $C$ ⇒ $y \geq 2x^2 - 12x + 14$

Final answer (all four together):

$$\boxed{\;x \geq 0,\quad y \geq 0,\quad y \leq -8x + 20,\quad y \geq 2x^2 - 12x + 14\;}$$

Verify at $(1, 6)$: $1 \geq 0$ ✓, $6 \geq 0$ ✓, $6 \leq -8 + 20 = 12$ ✓, $6 \geq 2 - 12 + 14 = 4$ ✓.

Strict vs non-strict? The standard convention for "region bounded by" includes the boundary, so use $\leq, \geq$ (solid lines on a sketch). If the question said strictly bounded, switch to $<, >$ (dashed). Trap (Phuc's notes had this wrong): writing "$y \leq$ curve" flips $R$ to the wrong side — $R$ sits ABOVE the curve's dip into negative $y$, since the curve has its vertex at $P(3, -4)$ which is BELOW $R$. The correct direction is $y \geq $ curve. Why all four are needed: dropping any one would let $R$ leak out — e.g. without $y \geq 0$, $R$ would extend down past the curve's vertex.
$x \geq 0,\;\, y \geq 0,\;\, y \leq -8x + 20,\;\, y \geq 2x^2 - 12x + 14$
