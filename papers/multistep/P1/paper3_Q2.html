Q2 — Area $= 100$ on $\triangle ABC$, then $BC$ via cosine rule

Question

Triangle $ABC$ has $AB = 15$ cm, $AC = 25$ cm, $\angle BAC = \theta°$, and area $100\ \text{cm}^2$.

(a) Find $\sin\theta°$. [2]

(b) Given $\theta > 90°$, find $BC$ to 3 s.f. [3]

[5 marks]

Part (a) — Find $\sin\theta°$ [2 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 M1 A1

You have two sides ($AB$, $AC$) and the angle between them ($\theta = \angle BAC$) — SAS pattern.

Formula (triangle area, SAS):

$$\text{Area} = \tfrac{1}{2}\, ab\, \sin C$$

Substitute $a = 15$, $b = 25$, $C = \theta°$, area $= 100$:

$$100 = \tfrac{1}{2}(15)(25)\sin\theta° = 187.5\sin\theta°$$

$$\sin\theta° = \dfrac{100}{187.5} = \dfrac{8}{15}$$

Why this and not $\tfrac{1}{2}bh$: you don't have a perpendicular height; you have the included angle. The $\tfrac{1}{2}ab\sin C$ formula is $\tfrac{1}{2}\!\cdot\!\text{base}\!\cdot\!\text{height}$ in disguise — the height equals $b\sin C$.
Keep it exact: $\tfrac{8}{15}$ is the clean form (cancel $100/187.5$ by dividing top and bottom by $12.5$). The decimal $0.5333\ldots$ would lose precision in part (b).
$\sin\theta° = \tfrac{8}{15}$

Part (b) — Find $BC$ [3 marks] · 3 steps

From (a): $\sin\theta° = \tfrac{8}{15}$, with $\theta > 90°$.
STEP 1/3 M1

Formula (Pythagorean identity):

$$\sin^2\theta + \cos^2\theta = 1$$

Substitute $\sin\theta = \tfrac{8}{15}$:

$$\cos^2\theta = 1 - \left(\tfrac{8}{15}\right)^2 = 1 - \tfrac{64}{225} = \tfrac{161}{225}$$

$$\cos\theta = \pm\dfrac{\sqrt{161}}{15}$$

Because $\theta > 90°$ (obtuse), $\cos\theta$ must be negative:

$$\cos\theta° = -\dfrac{\sqrt{161}}{15}$$

Sign rule: in $(90°, 180°)$ sine stays $+$ but cosine flips to $-$. Forgetting the minus would make $BC$ come out far too small (an acute triangle, not obtuse).
$\cos\theta° = -\dfrac{\sqrt{161}}{15}$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $\cos\theta° = -\dfrac{\sqrt{161}}{15}$
STEP 2/3 M1

Formula (cosine rule):

$$a^2 = b^2 + c^2 - 2bc\cos A$$

Substitute $a = BC$, $b = AB = 15$, $c = AC = 25$, $A = \theta°$ with $\cos\theta = -\tfrac{\sqrt{161}}{15}$:

$$BC^2 = 225 + 625 - 2(15)(25)\!\left(-\tfrac{\sqrt{161}}{15}\right)$$

$$BC^2 = 850 + 50\sqrt{161}$$

Watch the double negative: $-2bc\cos\theta$ with $\cos\theta < 0$ becomes $+$, so the term adds to $850$ — consistent with $BC$ being the long side opposite the obtuse angle.
$BC^2 = 850 + 50\sqrt{161}$
↳ from STEP 2:From STEP 2: $BC^2 = 850 + 50\sqrt{161}$
STEP 3/3 A1

$\sqrt{161} \approx 12.6886$, so $50\sqrt{161} \approx 634.43$ and

$$BC = \sqrt{850 + 634.43} = \sqrt{1484.43} \approx 38.528\ldots$$

Sense check: $BC$ should exceed both $15$ and $25$ (longest side opposite biggest angle). $38.5 > 25$ ✓.
$\boxed{BC \approx 38.5\ \text{cm}}$ (3 s.f.)
