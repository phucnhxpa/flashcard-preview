Q3 — Trig graphs: y = 4 sin θ, y = 1 + sin θ, and counting tan θ = 1 + sin θ

Question

Figure shows y = sin θ, 0 ≤ θ ≤ 360°.
(a) State the coordinates of the minimum point of y = 4 sin θ. (2)
(b) On the same axes sketch y = 1 + sin θ and y = tan θ for 0 ≤ θ ≤ 360°.
(c) State the number of solutions of tan θ = 1 + sin θ in (i) 0 ≤ θ ≤ 2160°, (ii) 0 ≤ θ ≤ 1980°.

[2 marks]

Part (a)[2 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2

Coordinates of the minimum of y = 4 sin θ. (2)

Step 1: Apply the vertical stretch. y = 4 sin θ is sin θ stretched ×4 vertically, so range −4 ≤ y ≤ 4. The minimum value is −4.

STEP 2/2

Step 2: Find where it occurs. sin θ is least at θ = 270°, so the minimum of 4 sin θ is at θ = 270°.

Result: Minimum point = (270°, −4) — give it as a COORDINATE PAIR, not just −4.

Part (b)3 steps

STEP 1/3

Sketch y = 1 + sin θ and y = tan θ. (setup for (c))

STEP 2/3

Step 1: Sketch y = 1 + sin θ. The sine wave lifted UP by 1: range 0 ≤ y ≤ 2; it just touches the x-axis at its minimum (θ = 270°) and never goes below.

STEP 3/3

Step 2: Sketch y = tan θ. Period 180°, with vertical asymptotes at θ = 90° and θ = 270° in 0 ≤ θ ≤ 360°; each branch sweeps −∞ to +∞.

Part (c)3 steps

STEP 1/3

Number of solutions of tan θ = 1 + sin θ over large ranges. (2)

Step 1: Count crossings in one 360° period. tan θ has period 180° and 1 + sin θ has period 360°; count the intersections in a single 360° block (the repeating unit). Call this number N.

STEP 2/3

Step 2: Multiply by whole periods. (i) 0 ≤ θ ≤ 2160° = 6 full periods of 360° ⟹ 6N. (ii) 0 ≤ θ ≤ 1980° = 5 full periods + a leftover 180°.

Step 3: Adjust the endpoints. For (ii) add the crossings that occur in the final leftover 180°, and check whether a solution lands exactly on the closed endpoint. This is the SAME one-pattern-per-period skill as the June 12 tan x = 1/x + 1 question.

Result: (i) 6N; (ii) 5N + (crossings in final 180°), with endpoint check.

STEP 3/3

Common trap (this part).

On (a) he gave the value −4 instead of the coordinate pair (270°, −4) — lost a B mark for not reading "coordinates".

Exam checklist.

Underline instruction words ("coordinates", interval ends); count solutions = intersections of the two graphs over one repeating period, then scale and adjust endpoints.

Common pitfalls.

Forgetting that 1 + sin θ only touches the axis (doesn't cross) at its minimum; miscounting near the tan asymptotes; ignoring the half-period leftover at 1980°.
