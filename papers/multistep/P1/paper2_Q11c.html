Q11(c) — Min of $y = 12\sin(x + B)$ at $\left(\tfrac{\pi}{4},\, A\right)$

Question

Curve $C_3\!:\; y = 12\sin(x + B)$, where $B > 0$ is a constant (in radians).

The minimum point on $C_3$ with the smallest positive $x$-coordinate is $\left(\tfrac{\pi}{4},\, A\right)$.

(c)(i) State the value of $A$.
(c)(ii) Find the smallest possible value of $B$. [2]

[2 marks]

Part (c)(i) — Value of $A$ [1 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 B1

Formula (sine extrema):

$$\min_\theta \sin\theta = -1,\quad \max_\theta \sin\theta = +1$$

$\sin\theta = -1$ at $\theta = \tfrac{3\pi}{2}, \tfrac{3\pi}{2}+2\pi, \ldots$.

Substitute at the minimum of $C_3$:

$$y_{\min} = 12 \cdot (-1) = -12$$

Since the minimum has $y$-coordinate $A$:

$$\boxed{\;A = -12\;}$$

Phuc's noted gap: "I confused $\sin = 1$ (max) vs $\sin = -1$ (min)."
Pin it down:
• MAX of $\sin\theta = +1$ at $\theta = \tfrac{\pi}{2} + 2n\pi$ ("top")
• MIN of $\sin\theta = -1$ at $\theta = \tfrac{3\pi}{2} + 2n\pi$ ("bottom")
For $y = R\sin(\ldots)$ with $R > 0$: max $y = +R$, min $y = -R$.
$A = -12$

Part (c)(ii) — Smallest positive $B$ [1 marks] · 1 step

From (c)(i): the minimum gives $\sin(x + B) = -1$. Given: the minimum with smallest positive $x$ occurs at $x = \tfrac{\pi}{4}$.
↳ from STEP 0:From STEP 1 (c)(i): $A = -12$ (minimum value), so $\sin(x+B) = -1$ at the minimum.
STEP 1/1 B1

$\sin\theta = -1 \;\Leftrightarrow\; \theta = \tfrac{3\pi}{2} + 2n\pi$ for integer $n$ (general solution; sine has period $2\pi$).

At the given minimum $x = \tfrac{\pi}{4}$:

$$\tfrac{\pi}{4} + B = \tfrac{3\pi}{2} + 2n\pi$$

Isolate $B$:

$$B = \tfrac{3\pi}{2} - \tfrac{\pi}{4} + 2n\pi = \tfrac{6\pi - \pi}{4} + 2n\pi = \tfrac{5\pi}{4} + 2n\pi$$

For $B > 0$ and as small as possible, take $n = 0$:

$$\boxed{\;B = \tfrac{5\pi}{4}\;}$$

Why "$+ 2n\pi$": sine repeats every $2\pi$, so every minimum is $2\pi$ apart. We don't yet know which $n$ corresponds to the "smallest positive $x$" minimum — solving picks it out.
Sanity check $n = -1$: $B = \tfrac{5\pi}{4} - 2\pi = -\tfrac{3\pi}{4} < 0$ — violates $B > 0$.
Verify: at $x = \tfrac{\pi}{4}$, $x + B = \tfrac{\pi}{4} + \tfrac{5\pi}{4} = \tfrac{3\pi}{2}$, so $\sin(x+B) = -1$ ✓ (a minimum, as required).
$B = \tfrac{5\pi}{4}$
