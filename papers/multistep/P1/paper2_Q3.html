Q3 — Sketch $y = f(x+3)$ and $y = f(-3x)$

Question

The curve $y = f(x)$ passes through $(-1, 0)$ and $(0, 2)$, and touches the $x$-axis at $(3, 0)$.

Sketch, on separate diagrams, showing all intercepts clearly:

(a) $y = f(x + 3)$ [3]   (b) $y = f(-3x)$ [3]

[6 marks]

Part (a) — $y = f(x + 3)$ [3 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2 B1 M1

Strategy: identify which type of transformation $f(x+3)$ represents, write down the rule for moving any labelled point, then apply it to each known point on the original curve.

Transformation rule (inside $f$, additive):

$\;\;y = f(x + a)$  ⇒ horizontal shift LEFT by $a$ units (if $a > 0$).

So $y = f(x+3)$ shifts the original curve 3 units to the LEFT. Coordinate map: $(x, y) \mapsto (x - 3,\, y)$ — only $x$ changes.

Apply to the three labelled points of $y = f(x)$:

$\;\;(-1, 0) \;\to\; (-1 - 3, 0) = (-4, 0)$

$\;\;(0, 2) \;\to\; (0 - 3, 2) = (-3, 2)$

$\;\;(3, 0)$ touch $\;\to\; (3 - 3, 0) = (0, 0)$, still touches

$$\boxed{\;(-4,0),\;(-3,2),\;(0,0)\text{ touch}\;}$$

The counter-intuitive bit: $+3$ inside means LEFT, not right. Why? $f(x+3) = 0$ when $x+3 = $ original-root, i.e. $x = $ original-root $- 3$ — 3 less than before, i.e. shifted left. Touches stays a touch: a double root ("touches") stays a double root under any horizontal shift; the curve still kisses the $x$-axis at the new $x$-coordinate.
$(-4,0),\;(-3,2),\;(0,0)$ — last still touches

STEP 2/2 A1

Strategy: draw the same shape as $y = f(x)$ but slid left by 3 units. The shape is preserved exactly — only its horizontal position changes.

Three key features to show:

$\;\;(-4, 0)$ — leftmost $x$-intercept (the curve crosses).

$\;\;(-3, 2)$ — local max (the original $y$-intercept, now 3 left).

$\;\;(0, 0)$ — tangent contact (curve touches $x$-axis without crossing).

The new curve also passes through the origin $(0, 0)$, which now serves as the new $y$-intercept (since the touch point ended up on the $y$-axis).

Mark scheme cue: label all three intercepts/key points; the touch behaviour at $(0,0)$ must be visibly tangent (curve approaches and leaves on the same side of the axis), not a crossing. Common slip: drawing the curve crossing at $(0,0)$ instead of touching loses the A1.
Curve through $(-4,0)$, $(-3,2)$, tangent at $(0,0)$

Part (b) — $y = f(-3x)$ [3 marks] · 2 steps

From (a): the original three points are $(-1,0)$, $(0,2)$, $(3,0)$ touch — apply a different transformation now.
STEP 1/2 B1 M1

Strategy: $f(-3x)$ has TWO transformations rolled into one: the negative sign and the factor of $3$ both sit inside $f$. Decompose, then apply the coordinate map.

Effect (1) — minus sign inside:

$\;\;y = f(-x)$ is the reflection of $y = f(x)$ in the $y$-axis.

Effect (2) — factor inside:

$\;\;y = f(ax)$ with $|a| > 1$ is a horizontal compression by factor $\tfrac{1}{|a|}$.

Combined transformation rule: for $y = f(ax)$, replace $x$ by $\dfrac{x}{a}$. Coordinate map: $(x, y) \mapsto \bigl(\tfrac{x}{a},\, y\bigr)$.

Here $a = -3$, so divide each $x$-coordinate by $-3$:

$\;\;(-1, 0) \;\to\; \bigl(\tfrac{-1}{-3},\, 0\bigr) = \bigl(\tfrac{1}{3},\, 0\bigr)$

$\;\;(0, 2) \;\to\; \bigl(\tfrac{0}{-3},\, 2\bigr) = (0, 2)$ — unchanged (any horizontal transformation leaves $x = 0$ alone)

$\;\;(3, 0)$ touch $\;\to\; \bigl(\tfrac{3}{-3},\, 0\bigr) = (-1, 0)$, still touches

$$\boxed{\;\bigl(\tfrac{1}{3},0\bigr),\;(0,2),\;(-1,0)\text{ touch}\;}$$

Inside-vs-outside rule: changes inside $f()$ are HORIZONTAL and counter-intuitive (they affect $x$ in the opposite way you might expect: $f(3x)$ compresses, not stretches). Changes outside $f()$ are VERTICAL and match intuition. Here both changes are inside, so both are horizontal. Sanity check: the $y$-intercept $(0, 2)$ is fixed under any horizontal scaling/reflection — only points off the $y$-axis move.
$\bigl(\tfrac{1}{3},0\bigr),\;(0,2),\;(-1,0)$ touch

STEP 2/2 A1

Strategy: sketch the new curve passing through the three transformed points. The shape is the original mirror-flipped left↔right and compressed to $\tfrac{1}{3}$ of its original horizontal extent.

Three key features to show:

$\;\;\bigl(\tfrac{1}{3}, 0\bigr)$ — the new ($x$-intercept that crosses).

$\;\;(0, 2)$ — local extremum, $y$-intercept (unchanged from original).

$\;\;(-1, 0)$ — tangent contact (the curve touches but does not cross).

Visually: original tangent at $(3, 0)$ on the right has migrated to $(-1, 0)$ on the LEFT, and the original crossing at $(-1, 0)$ has migrated to $\tfrac{1}{3}$ on the right.

Common slip: dividing by $3$ instead of $-3$ gives a compression but forgets the reflection — produces a curve that looks similar but lies on the wrong side of the $y$-axis. Always include the sign. Mark scheme cue: the touch at $(-1, 0)$ must be visibly tangent (not a crossing) to earn the A1.
Curve through $\bigl(\tfrac{1}{3},0\bigr)$, $(0,2)$, tangent at $(-1,0)$
