Q7 — Find coordinates of P where $C_1$ and $C_2$ meet in the first quadrant

Question

The curves $C_1$ and $C_2$ meet in the first quadrant at the point P, shown in Figure 1.

Use algebra to find the coordinates of P, given $f(x) = 1.2x(x-4)$ and $g(x) = \frac{3}{10}x(x-4)^2$.

[4 marks]

Solution[4 marks] · 6 steps

STEP 1/6M1

Set the two curves equal.

$$1.2x(x-4) = 0.3x(x-4)^2$$

STEP 2/6M1

Move everything to one side.

$$1.2x(x-4) - 0.3x(x-4)^2 = 0$$

STEP 3/6M1

Factor out the common factor $x(x-4)$.

$$x(x-4)\bigl[1.2 - 0.3(x-4)\bigr] = 0$$

Key technique: Both terms contain $x(x-4)$. Factor it out rather than expanding — this directly reveals the solutions.
STEP 4/6A1

Solve each factor.

  • $x = 0$ (origin)
  • $x = 4$ (meeting at x-axis)
  • $1.2 - 0.3(x-4) = 0 \Rightarrow 0.3(x-4) = 1.2 \Rightarrow x-4 = 4 \Rightarrow x = 8$
STEP 5/6

Select the correct solution.

P is in the first quadrant and is NOT the origin ($x=0$) or the x-axis meeting ($x=4$).

So $x = 8$.

STEP 6/6A1

Find $y$ using $f(x)$:

$$y = f(8) = 1.2 \cdot 8 \cdot (8-4) = 1.2 \cdot 8 \cdot 4 = 38.4$$

Verify using $g(x)$:

$$g(8) = 0.3 \cdot 8 \cdot (8-4)^2 = 0.3 \cdot 8 \cdot 16 = 38.4 \;\checkmark$$

$$\boxed{P = (8,\; 38.4)}$$

Check: Both $f(8)$ and $g(8)$ give 38.4, confirming the intersection point is correct.
