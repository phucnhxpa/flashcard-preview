Q5 — Find $f(x)$: quadratic with roots 0, 4 and minimum at $(2, -4.8)$

Question

$C_1$ has equation $y = f(x)$ where $f(x)$ is a quadratic function. $C_1$ cuts the x-axis at the origin and at $x = 4$, and has a minimum turning point at $(2, -4.8)$.

Find $f(x)$.

[3 marks]

Solution[3 marks] · 4 steps

STEP 1/4M1

Write factored form using the roots.

Roots at $x=0$ and $x=4$:

$$f(x) = k(x - 0)(x - 4) = kx(x-4)$$

Rule: A quadratic with roots $r_1, r_2$ has form $y = k(x-r_1)(x-r_2)$. The constant $k$ is found from a third point.
STEP 2/4M1

Substitute the turning point $(2, -4.8)$ to find $k$.

$$-4.8 = k \cdot 2 \cdot (2-4) = k \cdot 2 \cdot (-2) = -4k$$

$$k = \frac{-4.8}{-4} = 1.2$$

STEP 3/4A1

Write the answer.

$$\boxed{f(x) = 1.2x(x-4)}$$

Optionally expanded: $f(x) = 1.2x^2 - 4.8x$.

STEP 4/4

Check: Substitute all given conditions back.

$f(0) = 1.2 \cdot 0 \cdot (-4) = 0$ \checkmark

$f(4) = 1.2 \cdot 4 \cdot 0 = 0$ \checkmark

$f(2) = 1.2 \cdot 2 \cdot (-2) = -4.8$ \checkmark

Sign check: $k = 1.2 > 0$ means the parabola opens upward. The question says "minimum" — consistent \checkmark.
Factored form from roots, then sub a known point to find k. Check all given conditions back.
