Q6 — Find $g(x)$: cubic cuts at origin, touches at $x=4$, passes through $(6, 7.2)$

Question

$C_2$ has equation $y = g(x)$ where $g(x)$ is a cubic function. $C_2$ cuts the x-axis at the origin and meets the x-axis at $x = 4$, and passes through the point $(6, 7.2)$.

Find $g(x)$.

[3 marks]

Solution[3 marks] · 4 steps

STEP 1/4M1

Write factored form from root types.

"Cuts" the x-axis at origin $\Rightarrow$ simple root $\Rightarrow$ factor $x$.

"Meets" (touches) at $x=4$ $\Rightarrow$ repeated root $\Rightarrow$ factor $(x-4)^2$.

$$g(x) = w \cdot x(x-4)^2$$

Key vocabulary: "meets" or "touches" = even multiplicity = squared factor. "Cuts" = odd multiplicity = linear factor. Three factors ($x \cdot (x-4)^2$) = degree 3 = cubic \checkmark.
STEP 2/4M1

Substitute $(6, 7.2)$ and evaluate each factor numerically.

Harry's efficient method: evaluate each bracket separately:

$$7.2 = w \cdot (6) \cdot (6-4)^2 = w \cdot 6 \cdot 4 = 24w$$

Tip: Keep factors separate ($w \times 6 \times 4$), avoid expanding $(x-4)^2$ first. Fewer calculator keypresses = fewer input errors.
STEP 3/4A1

Solve for $w$.

$$w = \frac{7.2}{24} = \frac{72}{240} = \frac{3}{10}$$

$$\boxed{g(x) = \frac{3}{10}\,x(x-4)^2}$$

STEP 4/4

Check:

$g(6) = 0.3 \cdot 6 \cdot 4 = 7.2$ \checkmark

$g(0) = 0.3 \cdot 0 \cdot 16 = 0$ \checkmark

$g(4) = 0.3 \cdot 4 \cdot 0 = 0$ \checkmark

At $x=4$: the graph touches (not crosses) the x-axis. Check by looking at the sign of $g(x)$ just before and after $x=4$: $g(3.9) > 0$ and $g(4.1) > 0$ — same sign confirms it touches.
'Meets' = touches = repeated root = squared factor. 'Cuts' = crosses = simple root = linear factor.
