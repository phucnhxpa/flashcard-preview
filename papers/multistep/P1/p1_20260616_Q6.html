Solve $2x^4 - 15x^2 - 50 = 0$, giving real solutions in surd form. Use the substitution $u = x^2$.
STEP 1/5spot the even-power pattern M1

Strategy. The equation $2x^4 - 15x^2 - 50 = 0$ contains only even powers of $x$. Let $u = x^2$, so $x^4 = (x^2)^2 = u^2$. The quartic becomes a standard quadratic in $u$.

Substitution

$$\text{Let } u = x^2. \qquad 2u^2 - 15u - 50 = 0.$$
Why this works. A quartic of the form $ax^4 + bx^2 + c = 0$ is a “quadratic in disguise.” The substitution $u = x^2$ reduces it to a quadratic, which factorises or uses the formula. Always check for even powers first.
↳ from STEP 1: $u = x^2$, $\;2u^2 - 15u - 50 = 0$
STEP 2/5factorise the quadratic in u M1

Strategy. Factorise $2u^2 - 15u - 50$ by the split-middle method: find two numbers that multiply to $2 \times (-50) = -100$ and add to $-15$. The pair is $-20$ and $+5$.

Split and factor

$$2u^2 - 20u + 5u - 50 = 0$$$$2u(u - 10) + 5(u - 10) = 0$$$$(2u + 5)(u - 10) = 0.$$

Solve for $u$

$$u = -\frac{5}{2} \quad\text{or}\quad u = 10.$$
Phuc's trap: monic-style shortcut. Attempting $(u - r_1)(u - r_2)$ directly without the split-middle step loses method marks on a non-monic quadratic. Always show the split: $2u^2 - 15u - 50 \to 2u^2 - 20u + 5u - 50$.
↳ from STEP 2: $u = -\tfrac52$ or $u = 10$
STEP 3/5back-substitute u = xÂ˛ M1

Strategy. Replace $u$ with $x^2$ in each candidate root and solve for $x$.

From $u = 10$

$$x^2 = 10 \;\Longrightarrow\; x = \pm\sqrt{10}.$$

From $u = -\tfrac{5}{2}$

$$x^2 = -\frac{5}{2}.$$
Do NOT stop at $u$. The question asks for $x$, not $u$. A common error is to box $u = 10$ and $u = -5/2$ as the final answer — that loses the back-substitution marks. Always return to the original variable.
↳ from STEP 3: $x^2 = 10$ or $x^2 = -\tfrac52$
STEP 4/5reject with reason A1

Strategy. Check each candidate against the substitution $u = x^2$. A real square is always non-negative: $x^2 \geq 0$ for all real $x$. So $u = x^2 = -5/2 < 0$ has no real solution.

Reject $u = -\tfrac{5}{2}$

$$\text{Reject } u = -\frac{5}{2} \text{ since } x^2 \geq 0 \text{ for all real } x.$$
Phuc's mistake. Just crossed out $u = -5/2$ without a reason. Examiners award a mark for the rejection with justification, not just the rejection itself. Always write: “reject $u = -5/2$ since $x^2 \geq 0$” or “reject since a square cannot be negative.” The reason is part of the answer.

Sense check. The other root $u = 10 > 0$ is admissible: $x^2 = 10$ gives two real solutions $x = \pm\sqrt{10}$.

↳ from STEP 4: reject $u=-\tfrac52$; keep $x^2=10$
STEP 5/5state the real solutions A1

Strategy. Collect the admissible real solutions and box them.

Final answer

$$\boxed{x = \sqrt{10} \quad\text{and}\quad x = -\sqrt{10}.}$$

Sense check. Back-substitute $x = \sqrt{10}$: $2(\sqrt{10})^4 - 15(\sqrt{10})^2 - 50 = 2(100) - 15(10) - 50 = 200 - 150 - 50 = 0$ — satisfies. Two real roots, as expected for a quartic with two real and two complex roots.
