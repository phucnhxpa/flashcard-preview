Q6(b) — Define $R$ by inequalities (line, parabola, $x$-axis)

Question

Line $l\!:\, y - 5x = 75$ (i.e. $y = 5x + 75$). Curve $C\!:\, y = 2x^2 + x - 21$. From part (a), $l$ meets $C$ at $P = (-6,\,45)$ and $Q = (8,\,115)$.

The shaded region $R$ is bounded by $l$ above, by $C$ on the lower-left and lower-right, and by the $x$-axis between the two $x$-intercepts of $C$.

(b) Use inequalities to define $R$. [3]

[3 marks]

Solution [3 marks] · 3 steps

From (a): $l \cap C = \{P(-6, 45),\, Q(8, 115)\}$. Curve $x$-intercepts: $2x^2+x-21 = (2x+7)(x-3) = 0 \Rightarrow x = -\tfrac{7}{2}$ or $x = 3$.
STEP 1/3 B1

Strategy: $R$ has three boundary curves; each contributes one inequality. Walk the boundaries in turn, and for each one decide which side of it $R$ lies on by looking at the shaded region in the diagram.

Boundary curve: the $x$-axis has equation $y = 0$.

From the diagram, $R$ sits above (or on) the $x$-axis — every point in $R$ has non-negative $y$-coordinate. The boundary itself ($y=0$, between $x = -\tfrac{7}{2}$ and $x=3$) is included.

Sample-point check at the obvious interior point $(0, 0)$:

$\;\;\;y = 0 \geq 0$ ✓

$$\boxed{\;y \geq 0\;}$$

Mnemonic: $y \geq 0$ means "vertical coordinate $\geq 0$" ⇒ above (or on) the $x$-axis. $y \leq 0$ would be below it. Why non-strict ($\geq$) not strict ($>$)? The shaded region in $R$ touches the $x$-axis at the curve's roots $x = -\tfrac{7}{2}$ and $x = 3$; those boundary points are usually included unless the question says "strictly bounded".
$y \geq 0$

STEP 2/3 B1

Strategy: for a line boundary, the inequality has the form $y \leq mx + k$ or $y \geq mx + k$. Determine the direction by checking a clear sample point inside $R$.

Boundary curve: $l\!:\, y = 5x + 75$ (rearranged from $y - 5x = 75$).

From the diagram, the shaded region $R$ lies below the line — the line caps $R$ from above. So expect $y \leq 5x + 75$.

Sample-point check at the interior point $(0, 0)$ (clearly inside $R$):

$\;\;\;5(0) + 75 = 75$

$\;\;\;0 \leq 75$ ✓   (the sign $\leq$ is verified)

$$\boxed{\;y \leq 5x + 75\;}$$

Direction rule: "below the line $y = mx+k$" ⇒ $y \leq mx+k$. If you got $0 \not\leq 75$ from the sample-point test, you would flip to $\geq$. Sense-check at $P$ and $Q$: at $P(-6, 45)$, line gives $5(-6)+75 = 45$ — exactly equal (point on boundary ✓); at $Q(8, 115)$, line gives $5(8)+75 = 115$ — equal ✓. Both intersection points lie on the line as required.
$y \leq 5x + 75$
↳ from STEP 2:From STEP 2: $y \leq 5x + 75$
STEP 3/3 B1

Strategy: third boundary is the parabola itself. Decide direction with the same sample-point method, then bundle all three inequalities together to define $R$.

Boundary curve: $C\!:\, y = 2x^2 + x - 21$ (roots at $x = -\tfrac{7}{2}$ and $x = 3$ from the factorisation $(2x+7)(x-3) = 0$).

From the diagram, $R$ sits above the parabola: the curve forms a U-shaped floor on the left ($-6 \leq x \leq -\tfrac{7}{2}$) and right ($3 \leq x \leq 8$) sides of $R$. Expect $y \geq 2x^2 + x - 21$.

Sample-point check at the interior point $(0, 0)$:

$\;\;\;2(0)^2 + 0 - 21 = -21$

$\;\;\;0 \geq -21$ ✓   (the direction $\geq$ is verified)

Combine all three boundary inequalities:

$$\boxed{\;y \geq 0,\quad y \leq 5x + 75,\quad y \geq 2x^2 + x - 21\;}$$

Phuc's slip last time: wrote $y \leq 2x^2+x-21$ — wrong direction. The sample-point check at $(0, 0)$ gives $0$ vs $-21$: $0 > -21$, so the correct sign is $\geq$ (above), not $\leq$ (below).
Rule of thumb: $y \geq f(x)$ ⇒ region above curve; $y \leq f(x)$ ⇒ region below. "Above" / "below" refers to vertical position, not which side of the curve looks bigger on the page.
No need for $x$-bounds: the three inequalities together already pin $R$ to $-6 \leq x \leq 8$ — outside that range the line drops below the parabola, leaving no valid $y$ that simultaneously satisfies $y \leq 5x+75$ and $y \geq 2x^2+x-21$.
$y \geq 0,\;\, y \leq 5x + 75,\;\, y \geq 2x^2 + x - 21$
