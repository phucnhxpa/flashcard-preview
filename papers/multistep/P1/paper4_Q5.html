Q5 — Roof frame $\triangle ABD \cup \triangle BCD$ along $BD$

Question

A roof-frame is modelled by two triangles $ABD$ and $BCD$ joined along the common side $BD$. Points $A$, $D$, $C$ are collinear (i.e. $ADC$ is a straight line). $BD = x\,$m, $CD = (1+x)\,$m, $BC = 5\,$m, and $\angle BCD = \theta°$.

(a) Show that $\cos\theta° = \dfrac{13 + x}{5 + 5x}$. [2]

(b) Given $x = 2\sqrt{3}$ and $\angle BAC = 30°$, find the area of $\triangle ABC$ to 1 d.p. [5]

[7 marks]

Part (a) — Show $\cos\theta° = \tfrac{13+x}{5+5x}$ [2 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2 M1

Cosine Rule: $a^2 = b^2 + c^2 - 2bc\cos A$, where $a$ is opposite $A$.

Here $BD$ is opposite $\theta$, with $BC = 5$ and $CD = 1+x$:

$$BD^2 = BC^2 + CD^2 - 2\cdot BC\cdot CD\cdot\cos\theta$$

$$x^2 = 25 + (1+x)^2 - 10(1+x)\cos\theta$$

Why cosine rule? We have all three sides $\{BD, BC, CD\}$ and want the included angle $\theta$ at $C$. Sine rule needs an angle–opposite-side pair we don't have.
$x^2 = 25 + (1+x)^2 - 10(1+x)\cos\theta$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $x^2 = 25 + (1+x)^2 - 10(1+x)\cos\theta$
STEP 2/2 A1

Expand $(1+x)^2 = 1 + 2x + x^2$:

$$x^2 = 25 + 1 + 2x + x^2 - 10(1+x)\cos\theta$$

The $x^2$ on each side cancels:

$$0 = 26 + 2x - 10(1+x)\cos\theta \;\Rightarrow\; 10(1+x)\cos\theta = 26 + 2x$$

$$\cos\theta = \frac{26 + 2x}{10(1+x)} = \frac{2(13+x)}{10(1+x)} = \boxed{\frac{13 + x}{5 + 5x}}\quad\blacksquare$$

Show-that hint: aim for the form on the page — factor a $2$ from the numerator to cancel with $10$ ⇒ gives $5$ in the denominator.
$\cos\theta° = \dfrac{13 + x}{5 + 5x}$ ✓

Part (b) — Area of $\triangle ABC$ when $x = 2\sqrt{3}$ [5 marks] · 4 steps

From (a): $\cos\theta° = \dfrac{13 + x}{5 + 5x}$. Now $x = 2\sqrt{3}$, $\angle BAC = 30°$, $ADC$ collinear.
STEP 1/4 M1

Strategy: substitute $x = 2\sqrt{3}$ into the formula from part (a). The denominator contains a surd, so rationalise by multiplying numerator and denominator by the conjugate.

Substitute into $\cos\theta° = \dfrac{13 + x}{5 + 5x}$:

$\;\;\;\cos\theta° = \dfrac{13 + 2\sqrt{3}}{5 + 5(2\sqrt{3})} = \dfrac{13 + 2\sqrt{3}}{5 + 10\sqrt{3}}$

Rationalise: conjugate of $5 + 10\sqrt{3}$ is $5 - 10\sqrt{3}$. Multiply top and bottom:

$\;\;\;\cos\theta° = \dfrac{(13 + 2\sqrt{3})(5 - 10\sqrt{3})}{(5 + 10\sqrt{3})(5 - 10\sqrt{3})}$

Denominator via $(a+b)(a-b) = a^2 - b^2$:

$\;\;\;5^2 - (10\sqrt{3})^2 = 25 - 100 \cdot 3 = 25 - 300 = -275$

Numerator — FOIL:

$\;\;\;(13)(5) + (13)(-10\sqrt{3}) + (2\sqrt{3})(5) + (2\sqrt{3})(-10\sqrt{3})$

$\;\;\;= 65 - 130\sqrt{3} + 10\sqrt{3} - 20 \cdot 3$

$\;\;\;= 65 - 120\sqrt{3} - 60 = 5 - 120\sqrt{3}$

Combine:

$\;\;\;\cos\theta° = \dfrac{5 - 120\sqrt{3}}{-275} = \dfrac{120\sqrt{3} - 5}{275} = \dfrac{24\sqrt{3} - 1}{55}$

$$\boxed{\;\cos\theta° = \dfrac{24\sqrt{3} - 1}{55} \approx 0.7376 \Rightarrow \theta \approx 42.47°\;}$$

Conjugate rule: for $a + b\sqrt{c}$ in a denominator, multiply top and bottom by $a - b\sqrt{c}$. Their product $(a)^2 - (b\sqrt{c})^2 = a^2 - b^2 c$ is rational. Sign-of-denominator slip: here $25 - 300 = -275$ — negative, which flips the sign of the numerator on simplification. Decimal sanity-check: $24 \cdot 1.732 \approx 41.57$, so $(41.57 - 1)/55 \approx 0.7376$ ✓.
$\cos\theta \approx 0.7376 \Rightarrow \theta \approx 42.47°$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $\cos\theta \approx 0.7376 \Rightarrow \theta \approx 42.47°$
STEP 2/4 M1

$BD = 2\sqrt{3}\approx 3.464$, $BC = 5$, $\theta \approx 42.47°$.

$$\frac{BC}{\sin(\angle BDC)} = \frac{BD}{\sin\theta} \Rightarrow \sin(\angle BDC) = \frac{5\sin 42.47°}{2\sqrt{3}} \approx 0.9748$$

Test acute/obtuse: $5^2 = 25$ vs $BD^2 + CD^2 = 12 + 19.93 = 31.93$. Since $25 < 31.93$, $\angle BDC$ is acute, so $\angle BDC \approx 77.06°$.

Sine ambiguity: $\sin^{-1}(0.9748)$ gives both $77°$ and $103°$. Check the cosine sign or compare to the longest side to pick the right one.
$\angle BDC \approx 77.06°$
↳ from STEP 2:From STEP 2: $\angle BDC \approx 77.06°$
STEP 3/4 M1

$ADC$ is straight $\Rightarrow \angle ADB + \angle BDC = 180°$.

$$\angle ADB = 180° - 77.06° = 102.94°$$

In $\triangle ABD$: $\angle BAD = 30°$, $\angle ADB \approx 102.94°$, so $\angle ABD = 47.06°$.

Sine rule: $\dfrac{BD}{\sin 30°} = \dfrac{AB}{\sin\angle ADB} = \dfrac{AD}{\sin\angle ABD}$:

$$AB = \frac{2\sqrt{3}\,\sin 102.94°}{0.5} \approx 6.752\ \text{m}$$

$$AD = \frac{2\sqrt{3}\,\sin 47.06°}{0.5} \approx 5.072\ \text{m}$$

Supplementary sines: $\sin(180° - \alpha) = \sin\alpha$. So strictly $\sin\angle ADB = \sin\angle BDC$ — no need to recompute, just plug in $\sin 77.06°$.
$AB \approx 6.752$, $AD \approx 5.072$, $AC = AD + DC \approx 9.536\ \text{m}$
↳ from STEP 3:From STEP 3: $AB \approx 6.752$ m, $AC \approx 9.536$ m
STEP 4/4 A1 A1

In $\triangle ABC$ with $\angle BAC = 30°$ and the two adjacent sides $AB$, $AC$ known:

$$\text{Area} = \tfrac{1}{2}\, AB \cdot AC \cdot \sin 30° = \tfrac{1}{2}\cdot 6.752 \cdot 9.536 \cdot \tfrac{1}{2} \approx 16.10\ \text{m}^2$$

Cross-check: Area of $\triangle ABC$ $= $ Area $\triangle ABD$ + Area $\triangle BCD$ $= 8.55 + 7.54 = 16.09$ ✓. Don't round $\theta$ early — keep it on the calculator.
$\boxed{\text{Area} \approx 16.1\ \text{m}^2}$ (1 d.p.)
