Q8 — Platform: outer sector $AOBEA$ + inner sector $DOC$

Question

The platform consists of an inner sector $DOC$ joined to an outer (major) sector $AOBEA$, both centred at $O$. Given $\angle AOB = 0.8$ rad, arc $CD = 9$ m, and $DA : AO = 3 : 5$.

(a) Show $AO \approx 7.03$ m. [3]

(b) Find the perimeter to 3 s.f. [3]

(c) Find the total area to the nearest m². [3]

[9 marks]

Part (a) — Show $AO \approx 7.03$ m [3 marks] · 3 steps

STEP 1/3 M1

Strategy: a ratio $3:5$ tells us the relative lengths but not the actual sizes. Introduce a common scaling factor $x$ so $DA$, $AO$, and the derived $DO$ all sit on the same scale. Then one equation (the given arc length) will pin $x$ down.

Set up the variable:

$\;\;\;DA = 3x$   (smaller part of the ratio)

$\;\;\;AO = 5x$   (larger part — this is the outer-sector radius)

Compute the inner-circle radius $DO$ by adding $DA$ and $AO$ (since $D$, $A$, $O$ are collinear with $A$ between $D$ and $O$ on a single radial line):

$\;\;\;DO = DA + AO = 3x + 5x$

$$\boxed{\;DA = 3x,\;\;AO = 5x,\;\;DO = 8x\;}$$

Why one variable suffices: a ratio gives only the shape, not the size. With one unknown ($x$) and one upcoming equation (arc $CD = 9$), we have a solvable system. Why $DO = 8x$ specifically: $D$ lies on the inner (larger) circle, on the same line as $O$ and $A$, and at distance $DA + AO$ from $O$. Read carefully — "inner sector $DOC$" means inner relative to the platform's shape, but $DO$ is the larger radius (because $D$ is further from $O$ than $A$).
$DA = 3x$, $\ AO = 5x$, $\ DO = 8x$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $DA = 3x$, $AO = 5x$, $DO = 8x$
STEP 2/3 M1

Strategy: the given length "arc $CD = 9$" is what fixes $x$. The arc-length formula relates arc length, radius, and subtended angle (in radians) — apply it to the inner arc.

Formula (arc length, radians):

$$\ell = r\,\theta\quad(\theta\text{ in radians})$$

Identify the radius and angle for arc $CD$:

$\;\;\;r = DO = 8x$  (arc $CD$ lies on the inner circle through $D$ and $C$)

$\;\;\;\theta = \angle DOC = 0.8$ rad  (same angle at $O$ as $\angle AOB$, since $A,D$ share a ray and $B,C$ share a ray)

Substitute into $\ell = r\theta$:

$\;\;\;\text{arc } CD = (8x)(0.8) = 6.4x$

Set equal to the given $9$ m:

$\;\;\;6.4x = 9$

Divide both sides by $6.4$:

$$\boxed{\;x = \dfrac{9}{6.4} = 1.40625\;}$$

Trap: remember to use the inner radius $DO = 8x$, NOT the outer $AO = 5x$. Arc $CD$ lives on the larger circle (through $D$ and $C$). Using $5x$ would give $5x \cdot 0.8 = 4x = 9 \Rightarrow x = 2.25$ — wrong by a factor of $8/5$. Why exact: $9/6.4 = 90/64 = 45/32 = 1.40625$ exactly.
$x = 1.40625$
↳ from STEP 2:From STEP 2: $x = 1.40625$
STEP 3/3 A1

Strategy: the question asks for $AO$, not $x$. Recall $AO = 5x$ from step 1, and substitute the value of $x$ found in step 2.

Compute $AO$:

$\;\;\;AO = 5x$

$\;\;\;\phantom{AO} = 5 \times 1.40625$

$\;\;\;\phantom{AO} = 7.03125\;\text{m}$

Round to 3 s.f. (as required by the "show $\approx 7.03$" target):

$$\boxed{\;AO \approx 7.03\;\text{m}\;(3\text{ s.f.})\;}\quad\blacksquare$$

Keep the exact $7.03125$ for any subsequent parts (b) and (c) to avoid rounding errors propagating. The "3 s.f." rounding is just for the show-that — it's for presentation, not for further calculation.
$AO \approx 7.03$ m ✓

Part (b) — Perimeter [3 marks] · 3 steps

From (a): $x = 1.40625$, $\ AO = 7.03125$ m, $\ DO = 8x = 11.25$ m, $\ DA = BC = 3x = 4.21875$ m, $\ \theta = 0.8$ rad, arc $CD = 9$ m.
STEP 1/3 B1

Strategy: the perimeter is the total length of everything you would walk if you traced the platform's outline. Decompose into named pieces, then sum.

Walk the boundary starting at $A$ and going counter-clockwise:

$\;\;\;A \to E \to B$: the OUTER arc (the major arc, the long way round through the top)

$\;\;\;B \to C$: a straight radial segment of length $BC = 3x$

$\;\;\;C \to D$: the inner arc (already given as $9$ m)

$\;\;\;D \to A$: a straight radial segment of length $DA = 3x$

Sum:

$$P = \underbrace{\text{major arc }AEB}_{\text{outer, long way}} + \underbrace{\text{arc }CD}_{=\,9\text{ m}} + \underbrace{DA}_{=\,3x} + \underbrace{BC}_{=\,3x}$$

Trap (the major-vs-minor confusion): the $0.8$ rad is the angle $\angle AOB$ — the minor angle at $O$. The outer arc on the platform's OUTSIDE goes the long way around (through $E$ at the top), so it subtends the major angle $2\pi - 0.8$. Drawing the picture (or carefully reading the diagram) fixes this every time. Using the wrong angle would give a perimeter that's far too small.
4 pieces: major arc, inner arc, $DA$, $BC$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: 4 boundary pieces: major arc $AEB$, inner arc $CD = 9$ m, $DA = BC = 3x$
STEP 2/3 M1

Strategy: the major arc is the only piece whose length isn't already a number. Apply $\ell = r\theta$ with the major angle.

Formula (arc length, radians):

$$\ell = r\,\theta$$

Compute the major angle (full circle minus the minor):

$\;\;\;\theta_{\text{major}} = 2\pi - 0.8$

$\;\;\;\phantom{\theta_{\text{major}}} \approx 6.2832 - 0.8 = 5.4832$ rad

Substitute $r = AO = 7.03125$ (use the EXACT value from part (a), not $7.03$):

$\;\;\;\text{arc }AEB = (7.03125)(5.4832)$

$\;\;\;\phantom{\text{arc }AEB} \approx 38.555\;\text{m}$

$$\boxed{\;\text{arc }AEB \approx 38.555\;\text{m}\;}$$

Don't round early: keep $AO = 7.03125$ (full value), not $7.03$. Using $7.03$ gives $7.03 \times 5.4832 \approx 38.546$ — losing $0.009$, which would cascade into a 3rd-s.f. error in the final perimeter ($\approx 55.98$ vs $55.99$). Carry full-precision through, round only at the end.
major arc $AEB \approx 38.555$ m
↳ from STEP 2:From STEP 2: major arc $AEB \approx 38.555$ m
STEP 3/3 A1

Strategy: sum the four boundary lengths and round to the requested precision (3 s.f.).

Recall $DA = BC = 3x = 3 \times 1.40625 = 4.21875$ m.

Add the four pieces:

$\;\;\;P = \underbrace{38.555}_{AEB} + \underbrace{9}_{CD} + \underbrace{4.21875}_{DA} + \underbrace{4.21875}_{BC}$

$\;\;\;\phantom{P} \approx 38.555 + 9 + 8.4375$

$\;\;\;\phantom{P} \approx 55.99\;\text{m}$

$$\boxed{\;P \approx 56.0\;\text{m}\;(3\text{ s.f.})\;}$$

Sense-check: the major arc dominates the perimeter ($\approx 69\%$ of $P$), the inner arc is $\approx 16\%$, and the two short radial sides together are $\approx 15\%$. A perimeter of $\approx 56$ m is in the right ballpark for a platform whose larger radius is $\approx 11$ m (gives roughly $C \approx 2\pi r \approx 70$ m for a full circle — and this platform spans most of one).
$P \approx 56.0$ m

Part (c) — Total area [3 marks] · 3 steps

From (a): $AO = 7.03125$ m, $\ DO = 11.25$ m, $\ \theta = 0.8$ rad.
STEP 1/3 M1

Strategy: the platform is bounded by two arcs and two radial sides. Decompose the enclosed area into two SECTORS (each centred at $O$): the outer major sector swept by $OA \to OB$ the long way, plus the inner minor sector swept by $OD \to OC$ the short way. Add them.

Visualize the decomposition: picture a pizza slice (inner minor sector $DOC$) stuck onto an outer pizza ring crescent (outer major sector $AEB$). The shared boundary is the pair of radii $OA$, $OB$ where they meet (at $A$ and $B$ respectively).

Set up the total area:

$$A_{\text{total}} = \underbrace{\tfrac{1}{2}\,AO^2(2\pi - 0.8)}_{\text{outer major sector}} + \underbrace{\tfrac{1}{2}\,DO^2(0.8)}_{\text{inner minor sector}}$$

Why ADD (not subtract): the inner sector and the outer major sector together cover the whole platform without overlap. They share the two radii $OA$ and $OB$ as common edges (which are 1-dimensional and contribute no area). Why two SECTORS, not annulus: an annulus assumes a full $2\pi$ on both circles; here the inner is only $0.8$ rad and the outer is $2\pi - 0.8$ rad — different angles, so we cannot use a simple annular formula.
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $A_{\text{total}} = \tfrac{1}{2}AO^2(2\pi-0.8) + \tfrac{1}{2}DO^2(0.8)$
STEP 2/3 M1

Strategy: apply the standard sector area formula to each piece separately, using the appropriate radius and angle. Keep full precision.

Formula (sector area, radians):

$$A = \tfrac{1}{2}\, r^2\, \theta$$

Outer major sector ($r = AO = 7.03125$, $\theta = 2\pi - 0.8 \approx 5.4832$):

$\;\;\;A_{\text{major}} = \tfrac{1}{2}\,(7.03125)^2\,(5.4832)$

$\;\;\;\phantom{A_{\text{major}}} = \tfrac{1}{2}\,(49.4385)\,(5.4832)$

$\;\;\;\phantom{A_{\text{major}}} \approx 135.54\;\text{m}^2$

Inner minor sector ($r = DO = 8x = 11.25$, $\theta = 0.8$):

$\;\;\;A_{\text{minor}} = \tfrac{1}{2}\,(11.25)^2\,(0.8)$

$\;\;\;\phantom{A_{\text{minor}}} = \tfrac{1}{2}\,(126.5625)\,(0.8)$

$\;\;\;\phantom{A_{\text{minor}}} = 50.625\;\text{m}^2$ (exact)

Calculator habit: store $AO = 7.03125$ and $DO = 11.25$ in memory as full values; round only at the very end. Watch the angle: outer uses the major $5.4832$ (long way around), inner uses the minor $0.8$ — easy to swap by accident, which would give a sector area of around $4.6$ m² for the outer and $\approx 350$ m² for the inner. Numbers should pass a sanity check before adding.
↳ from STEP 2:From STEP 2: $A_{\text{major}} \approx 135.54$ m², $A_{\text{minor}} = 50.625$ m²
STEP 3/3 A1

Strategy: sum the two sector areas and round to the requested precision (nearest m²).

Add:

$\;\;\;A_{\text{total}} = A_{\text{major}} + A_{\text{minor}}$

$\;\;\;\phantom{A_{\text{total}}} \approx 135.54 + 50.625$

$\;\;\;\phantom{A_{\text{total}}} \approx 186.16\;\text{m}^2$

Round to the nearest integer:

$$\boxed{\;A_{\text{total}} \approx 186\;\text{m}^2\;}$$

Sense-check via simple bound: a full circle of radius $11.25$ has area $\pi(11.25)^2 \approx 397$ m². The platform consists of most of an outer thin ring plus a tiny inner sector — total $\approx 186$ m² is plausible (well under the full disc bound). Outer dominates: $135.54/186.16 \approx 73\%$ of the area — matches the visual intuition (outer arc covers most of the angular extent).
$A_{\text{total}} \approx 186\ \text{m}^2$
