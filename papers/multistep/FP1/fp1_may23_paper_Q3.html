Q3 — $H: xy=9$, $P(3t, 3/t)$; normal in form $ty - t^3 x = f(t)$; meets $H$ again

Question

The rectangular hyperbola $H$ has Cartesian equation $xy = 9$. The point $P\!\left(3t,\,\tfrac{3}{t}\right)$, $t \ne 0$, lies on $H$.

(a) Use calculus to determine an equation for the normal to $H$ at $P$, in the form $ty - t^3 x = f(t)$. [4]

Given that $t = 2$:

(b) Determine the coordinates of the point where the normal meets $H$ again, in simplest form. [3]

[7 marks]

Part (a) — Normal at $P(3t, 3/t)$ [4 marks] · 4 steps

Gap-topic primer (MEMORIZE): for $xy=c^2$, parametric form is $(ct,\,c/t)$. Here $c^2=9 \Rightarrow c=3$, so $P=(3t,\,3/t)$.
STEP 1/4 M1

Move (implicit differentiation, or rewrite as $y=9/x$):

$$xy = 9 \;\Rightarrow\; y + x\frac{dy}{dx} = 0 \;\Rightarrow\; \frac{dy}{dx} = -\frac{y}{x}$$

Substitute $x = 3t$, $y = 3/t$:

$$\left.\frac{dy}{dx}\right|_P = -\frac{3/t}{3t} = -\frac{1}{t^2}$$

Memorize this shortcut: at the parametric point $(ct, c/t)$ on $xy=c^2$, the tangent slope is always $-1/t^2$. Derive once, then quote.
$m_{\text{tan}} = -1/t^2$
Carry-in from previous stepFrom STEP 1: $m_{\text{tan}} = -1/t^2$
STEP 2/4 A1

Strategy: the normal at $P$ is by definition perpendicular to the tangent at $P$. Convert tangent slope to normal slope using the perpendicular-gradient relation, then we're ready to write the line equation.

Formula (perpendicular gradients): for two non-vertical lines with gradients $m_1, m_2$:

$$m_1 \cdot m_2 \;=\; -1 \;\;\Longleftrightarrow\;\; m_2 \;=\; -\frac{1}{m_1}$$

Substitute $m_{\text{tan}} = -\dfrac{1}{t^2}$ from the previous step:

$$m_{\text{normal}} \;=\; -\frac{1}{\,-1/t^2\,} \;=\; -1 \cdot \bigl(-t^2\bigr) \;=\; t^2$$

$$\boxed{\;m_{\text{normal}} = t^2\;}$$

Double-negative trap: the formula is $m_n = -1/m_t$ — the leading minus stays even when $m_t$ is itself negative. Here $m_t = -1/t^2$ (negative), so $m_n = -1 \div (-1/t^2) = +t^2$ (positive). Skipping the explicit minus gives $-t^2$ — a sign error that propagates through the whole question. Sanity check: tangent slope $\times$ normal slope $= -1/t^2 \cdot t^2 = -1$ ✓.
$m_{\text{normal}} = t^2$
Carry-in from previous stepFrom STEP 2: $m_{\text{normal}} = t^2$
STEP 3/4 M1

Formula (point-slope line through $P$):

$$y - y_P = m(x - x_P)$$

Substitute $P = (3t,\,3/t)$, $m = t^2$:

$$y - \frac{3}{t} = t^2 \bigl(x - 3t\bigr)$$

Carry-in from previous stepFrom STEP 3: $y - \frac{3}{t} = t^2 \bigl(x - 3t\bigr)$
STEP 4/4 A1

Move (clear the $1/t$ fraction): multiply both sides by $t$:

$$ty - 3 = t^3 x - 3t^4$$

Rearrange to target form $ty - t^3 x = f(t)$:

$$ty - t^3 x = 3 - 3t^4$$

$$\boxed{\;ty - t^3 x = 3(1 - t^4)\;}\quad\text{i.e. } f(t)=3-3t^4$$

Standard form (memorize): normal at $(ct,c/t)$ on $xy=c^2$ is $\;t^3 x - ty = c(t^4-1)$, equivalently $ty-t^3x=c(1-t^4)$. With $c=3$ this is exactly our answer.
$ty - t^3 x = 3(1-t^4)$

Part (b) — Where the normal meets $H$ again ($t=2$) [3 marks] · 3 steps

From (a): normal at $P$ is $ty - t^3 x = 3(1-t^4)$.
STEP 1/3 M1

Substitute $t=2$ into the normal:

$$2y - 8x = 3(1-16) = -45 \;\Rightarrow\; y = 4x - \tfrac{45}{2}$$

Substitute into $xy = 9$:

$$x\!\left(4x - \tfrac{45}{2}\right) = 9 \;\Rightarrow\; 8x^2 - 45x - 18 = 0$$

$8x^2 - 45x - 18 = 0$
Carry-in from previous stepFrom STEP 1: $8x^2 - 45x - 18 = 0$
STEP 2/3 M1

Move (Vieta’s shortcut for “meets again”): if $\alpha,\beta$ are roots of $8x^2 - 45x - 18 = 0$, then $\alpha\beta = -18/8 = -9/4$.

One root is $x_P = 3t = 6$ (the original point). So the OTHER root is:

$$x_{\text{other}} = \frac{-9/4}{6} = -\frac{3}{8}$$

Why this beats re-solving: you already know $P$ is one intersection; Vieta’s product-of-roots $\alpha\beta = c/a$ gives the other coordinate without the quadratic formula. Checks: $6 \cdot (-\tfrac{3}{8}) = -\tfrac{9}{4}$ ✓.
$x = -\tfrac{3}{8}$
Carry-in from previous stepFrom STEP 2: $x = -\tfrac{3}{8}$
STEP 3/3 A1

Find $y$ via $xy=9$ (cleaner than the line):

$$y = \frac{9}{x} = \frac{9}{-3/8} = -24$$

$$\boxed{\;\left(-\tfrac{3}{8},\;-24\right)\;}$$

Sanity check via line: $y = 4(-3/8) - 45/2 = -3/2 - 45/2 = -24$ ✓. Both methods agree.
$\bigl(-\tfrac{3}{8},\;-24\bigr)$
