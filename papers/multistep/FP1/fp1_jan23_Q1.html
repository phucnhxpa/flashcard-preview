Q1 — Standard Results: $\sum (7r-5)^2$

Question

Use the standard results for $\sum_{r=1}^n r$ and $\sum_{r=1}^n r^2$ to find $\sum_{r=1}^n (7r-5)^2$.

[4 marks]

Solution 3 steps

STEP 1/3

Step 1 — Expand the summand:

$$(7r-5)^2 = 49r^2 - 70r + 25$$

$(7r-5)^2 = 49r^2 - 70r + 25$
↳ from STEP 1:Expanded summand
STEP 2/3

Step 2 — Split and apply standard results:

$$\sum_{r=1}^n (7r-5)^2 = 49\sum r^2 - 70\sum r + 25n$$

$$= 49 \cdot \frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6} - 70 \cdot \frac{n(n+1)}{2} + 25n$$

Must memorise: $\sum_{r=1}^n r = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}$ — the ONLY standard result NOT in the formula booklet!
Applied standard results for $\sum r^2$ and $\sum r$
↳ from STEP 2:Standard results applied
STEP 3/3

Step 3 — Factor out and simplify:

Factor out $\frac{n}{6}$:

$$= \frac{n}{6}[49(n+1)(2n+1) - 210(n+1) + 150]$$

Expand: $49(2n^2+3n+1) - 210(n+1) + 150$

$= 98n^2 + 147n + 49 - 210n - 210 + 150 = 98n^2 - 63n - 11$

Verify: $n=1$: LHS $= (7-5)^2 = 4$; RHS $= \frac{1}{6}(98-63-11) = \frac{24}{6} = 4$ ✓

$\boxed{\sum_{r=1}^n (7r-5)^2 = \frac{n}{6}(98n^2 - 63n - 11)}$
