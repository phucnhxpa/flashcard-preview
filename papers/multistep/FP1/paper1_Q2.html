Q2 — $f(z) = 2z^3 + pz^2 + qz - 41$; $5-4i$ is a root

Question

$f(z) = 2z^3 + pz^2 + qz - 41$ with $p,q$ integers. Given that $5-4i$ is a root:

(a) Write down another complex root. [1]

(b) Solve $f(z)=0$ completely. [4]

(c) Determine $p$ and $q$. [2]

(d) The three roots form a triangle on the Argand diagram. Find its area. [2]

[9 marks]

Part (a) — Other complex root [1 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 B1

Rule (MEMORIZE): if a polynomial has REAL coefficients and complex root $z = a+bi$, then $z^* = a-bi$ is also a root.

Coefficients $2, p, q, -41$ are all real, so the conjugate of $5-4i$ is also a root.

Phuc's slip: "I forget this one." Drill: real-coefficient polynomial + one complex root ⇒ conjugate is automatically a second root.
Other complex root $= \boxed{5 + 4i}$

Part (b) — Solve completely [4 marks] · 3 steps

Roots so far: $z_1 = 5-4i$, $z_2 = 5+4i$. Cubic has 3 roots so $z_3 = \alpha$ is real.
STEP 1/3 M1

Strategy: we already have two of the three roots ($5\pm 4i$); the third must be real (a real-coefficient cubic has $1$ or $3$ real roots). Vieta's product-of-roots gets us straight to that third root using only the leading and constant coefficients of $f$.

Formula (Vieta for $az^3 + bz^2 + cz + d = 0$):

$$z_1\, z_2\, z_3 \;=\; -\frac{d}{a}$$

Read off coefficients from $f(z) = 2z^3 + pz^2 + qz - 41$:

$$a = 2,\quad d = -41$$

Substitute:

$$z_1\, z_2\, z_3 \;=\; -\frac{-41}{2} \;=\; \boxed{\;\dfrac{41}{2}\;}$$

Sign-of-$d$ trap: the constant term is $-41$, NOT $+41$. Vieta gives $-d/a = -(-41)/2 = +\tfrac{41}{2}$. A sign slip here propagates: you would compute $\alpha = -\tfrac{1}{2}$ and the wrong values of $p,q$ in part (c). Always copy the constant with its sign first, then negate.
$z_1 z_2 z_3 = \dfrac{41}{2}$
Carry-in from previous stepFrom STEP 1: $z_1 z_2 z_3 = \dfrac{41}{2}$
STEP 2/3 M1

Formula (sum-of-squares shortcut):

$$(a+bi)(a-bi) = a^2 + b^2$$

Substitute $a=5,\ b=4$:

$$(5-4i)(5+4i) = 25 + 16 = 41$$

Don't expand $(5-4i)(5+4i)$ term by term — always recognise the conjugate pattern and jump straight to $a^2 + b^2$.
$z_1 z_2 = 41$
Carry-in from previous stepFrom STEP 2: $z_1 z_2 = 41$
STEP 3/3 A1 A1

From $z_1 z_2 z_3 = 41/2$ and $z_1 z_2 = 41$:

$$41 \cdot \alpha = \frac{41}{2} \;\Rightarrow\; \alpha = \frac{1}{2}$$

The three roots of $f(z) = 0$ are:

$$\boxed{\;z = 5-4i,\ 5+4i,\ \tfrac{1}{2}\;}$$

Verify: $f(1/2) = 2(\tfrac{1}{8}) + p(\tfrac{1}{4}) + q(\tfrac{1}{2}) - 41$. With $p=-21,\ q=92$ (part c): $\tfrac{1}{4} - \tfrac{21}{4} + 46 - 41 = -5 + 5 = 0$ ✓
Roots: $5-4i,\ 5+4i,\ \tfrac{1}{2}$

Part (c) — Find $p$ and $q$ [2 marks] · 2 steps

From (a)/(b): three roots are $z_1 = 5-4i$, $z_2 = 5+4i$, $z_3 = \tfrac{1}{2}$. Use Vieta on $2z^3 + pz^2 + qz - 41$.
STEP 1/2 M1

Formula: $z_1 + z_2 + z_3 = -b/a$.

Substitute:

$$(5-4i) + (5+4i) + \tfrac{1}{2} = 10 + \tfrac{1}{2} = \tfrac{21}{2}$$

$$\tfrac{21}{2} = -\frac{p}{2} \;\Rightarrow\; p = -21$$

$p = -21$
Carry-in from previous stepFrom STEP 1: $p = -21$
STEP 2/2 A1

Formula: $z_1 z_2 + z_1 z_3 + z_2 z_3 = c/a$.

$z_1 z_2 = 41$ (from b). $z_1 z_3 + z_2 z_3 = z_3(z_1 + z_2) = \tfrac{1}{2}(10) = 5$.

$$41 + 5 = \tfrac{q}{2} \;\Rightarrow\; q = 92$$

Pair-sum shortcut: when two roots are conjugate, $z_1 z_3 + z_2 z_3 = z_3 (z_1 + z_2)$ where $z_1+z_2$ is the real number $2\,\text{Re}(z_1)$.
$\boxed{p = -21,\ q = 92}$

Part (d) — Triangle area [2 marks] · 1 step

Argand axes: $x$-axis = REAL, $y$-axis = IMAGINARY.
STEP 1/1 M1 A1

$P_1 = (5,-4)$, $P_2 = (5,+4)$, $P_3 = (\tfrac{1}{2},0)$.

$P_1 P_2$ is vertical (both have $x=5$). Length $= |4-(-4)| = 8$ — use as base.

Height = perpendicular distance from $P_3$ to line $x=5$:

$$h = \left|5 - \tfrac{1}{2}\right| = \tfrac{9}{2}$$

Formula: $\text{Area} = \tfrac{1}{2}\,\text{base}\,\text{height}$.

$$\text{Area} = \tfrac{1}{2}\cdot 8\cdot \tfrac{9}{2} = \boxed{18}$$

$\text{Area} = 18$ square units
