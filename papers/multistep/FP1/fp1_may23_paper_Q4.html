Q4 — (i) area scale factor of 3 → possible $k$; (ii) solve $BC=D$ for $C$

Question

(i) $A = \begin{pmatrix} -3 & 8 \\ -3 & k \end{pmatrix}$, $k$ constant. $A$ takes triangle $T$ to $T'$ where $\text{area}(T') = 3 \cdot \text{area}(T)$. Determine the possible values of $k$. [4]

(ii) $B = \begin{pmatrix} a & -4 \\ 2 & 3 \end{pmatrix}$ with $BC = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 5 & 1 \\ 1 & 4 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$ and $a$ constant. Determine $C$ in terms of $a$. [4]

[8 marks]

Part (i) — Possible values of $k$ [4 marks] · 3 steps

STEP 1/3 M1

Formula (NOT in booklet — MEMORIZE):

$$\text{area scale factor} = |\det A|$$

Substitute $a=-3,b=8,c=-3,d=k$ into $\det = ad - bc$:

$$\det A = (-3)(k) - (8)(-3) = -3k + 24$$

$\det A = -3k + 24$
Carry-in from previous stepFrom STEP 1: $\det A = -3k + 24$
STEP 2/3 M1

The $\pm$ trap: area scale factor is 3 means $|\det A| = 3$, so $\det A = \pm 3$ (TWO cases).

Fuat (Loom): "They say possible values — this could be plus 3 or minus 3 … so $k$ is going to be either 7 or 9." Whenever the question uses possible values (plural), almost always more than one answer.
Carry-in from previous stepFrom STEP 2: $k$
STEP 3/3 A1 A1

Case 1: $-3k + 24 = +3 \Rightarrow k = 7$.

Case 2: $-3k + 24 = -3 \Rightarrow k = 9$.

$$\boxed{\;k = 7 \;\;\text{or}\;\; k = 9\;}$$

Sense check: $\det A = -3(7)+24 = 3$ ✓ and $\det A = -3(9)+24 = -3$ ✓. Both give $|\det| = 3$.
$k = 7$ or $k = 9$

Part (ii) — Solve $BC = D$ for $C$ [4 marks] · 3 steps

Setup: $B$ is $2\times 2$, $D$ is $2\times 3$. So $C$ must be $2\times 3$. Solve $BC=D \Rightarrow C = B^{-1}D$ by left-multiplying by $B^{-1}$.
STEP 1/3 M1

Formula (2x2 inverse — NOT in booklet, MEMORIZE):

$$\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix}^{\!-1} = \frac{1}{ad-bc}\begin{pmatrix} d & -b \\ -c & a \end{pmatrix}$$

Compute $\det B = (a)(3) - (-4)(2) = 3a + 8$.

Substitute:

$$B^{-1} = \frac{1}{3a+8}\begin{pmatrix} 3 & 4 \\ -2 & a \end{pmatrix}$$

Mnemonic: "swap diagonal, negate off-diagonal, divide by det". The negation of $-(-4) = +4$ is the easy slip.
$B^{-1} = \tfrac{1}{3a+8}\begin{pmatrix} 3 & 4 \\ -2 & a \end{pmatrix}$
Carry-in from previous stepFrom STEP 1: $B^{-1} = \tfrac{1}{3a+8}\begin{pmatrix} 3 & 4 \\ -2 & a \end{pmatrix}$
STEP 2/3 M1

Move: $C = B^{-1} D$ — multiply $(2\times 2)\cdot(2\times 3)$, shape $2\times 3$.

$$C = \frac{1}{3a+8}\!\begin{pmatrix} 3 & 4 \\ -2 & a \end{pmatrix}\!\begin{pmatrix} 2 & 5 & 1 \\ 1 & 4 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$$

Row $\times$ column entries:

Row 1: $(3\!\cdot\!2 + 4\!\cdot\!1,\;3\!\cdot\!5 + 4\!\cdot\!4,\;3\!\cdot\!1 + 4\!\cdot\!2) = (10,\,31,\,11)$

Row 2: $(-2\!\cdot\!2 + a\!\cdot\!1,\;-2\!\cdot\!5 + a\!\cdot\!4,\;-2\!\cdot\!1 + a\!\cdot\!2) = (a-4,\;4a-10,\;2a-2)$

Carry-in from previous stepFrom STEP 2: $C = \frac{1}{3a+8}\!\begin{pmatrix} 3 & 4 \\ -2 & a \end{pmatrix}\!\begin{pmatrix} 2 & 5 & 1 \\ 1 & 4 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$
STEP 3/3 A1 A1

$$\boxed{\;C = \frac{1}{3a+8}\begin{pmatrix} 10 & 31 & 11 \\ a-4 & 4a-10 & 2a-2 \end{pmatrix}\;}$$

Always check shapes BEFORE multiplying: $B^{-1}$ is $2\times 2$, $D$ is $2\times 3$ → product is $2\times 3$. If shapes don’t match, you wrote $DB^{-1}$ by mistake.
$C = \tfrac{1}{3a+8}\!\begin{pmatrix}10 & 31 & 11 \\ a-4 & 4a-10 & 2a-2\end{pmatrix}$
