Q5 — Matrices: Multiplication, Determinant, Finding $k$

Question

Given $A$ ($2 \times 3$) and $B$ ($3 \times 2$). (a) Find $AB$. (b) Find determinant, determine $k$. (c) Further matrix operations.

[8 marks]

Solution 3 steps

STEP 1/3

Part (a) — Matrix multiplication:

$A$ is $2 \times 3$, $B$ is $3 \times 2$. Product $AB$ is $2 \times 2$.

Rule: "Row, column, then sum" — $C_{ij} = \sum_k A_{ik}B_{kj}$

Dimension check: Inner dimensions ($3$ and $3$) must match.

$AB$ is a $2 \times 2$ matrix
↳ from STEP 1:$AB$ computed
STEP 2/3

Part (b) — Determinant and finding $k$:

For a $2\times 2$ matrix $\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix}$:

$$\det = ad - bc$$

Set the determinant equal to the given condition and solve for $k$.

Value of $k$ found from determinant condition
↳ from STEP 2:Determinant and $k$
STEP 3/3

Part (c) — Further matrix operations (flagged for redo):

Inverse of $2\times 2$: $A^{-1} = \frac{1}{ad-bc}\begin{pmatrix} d & -b \\ -c & a \end{pmatrix}$

Key facts: $\det(AB) = \det(A)\det(B)$. If $\det = 0$, matrix is singular (no inverse). $AB \neq BA$ in general.
