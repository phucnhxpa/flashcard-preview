Q3 · Unit Cube — 3D Pythagoras to Face Midpoint

Question

A cube has sides of unit length. What is the length of a line joining a vertex to the midpoint of one of the opposite faces?

[3 marks] · Answer: $\sqrt{6}/2$ (confirmed with green checkmark at ~42:00)

Solution [3 marks] · 3 steps

All edges = 1 unit. Vertex at $(0,0,0)$; target = midpoint of opposite face at $(1, \frac{1}{2}, \frac{1}{2})$. Break the 3D path into two 2D Pythagoras steps.
STEP 1/3base diagonal M1

Strategy. Find the diagonal across the base to the point directly below the target midpoint. Travel 1 unit along one edge and $\frac{1}{2}$ unit along the perpendicular edge.

2D Pythagoras on base

$$x = \sqrt{1^2 + \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^2} = \sqrt{1 + \frac{1}{4}} = \sqrt{\frac{5}{4}} = \frac{\sqrt{5}}{2}$$
↳ from STEP 1: $x = \frac{\sqrt{5}}{2}$
STEP 2/3add third dimension M1

Strategy. Now use $x$ (the base diagonal) and the remaining $\frac{1}{2}$ unit in the vertical direction to find the full 3D distance $y$.

Second Pythagoras step

$$y = \sqrt{x^2 + \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^2} = \sqrt{\frac{5}{4} + \frac{1}{4}} = \sqrt{\frac{6}{4}} = \frac{\sqrt{6}}{2}$$
Trap: Do NOT apply 3D Pythagoras once as $\sqrt{1^2+1^2+1^2} = \sqrt{3}$ — that is the body diagonal (corner-to-corner), not vertex-to-face-midpoint. Two successive 2D steps are required.
↳ from STEP 2: $y = \frac{\sqrt{6}}{2}$
STEP 3/3verify and rationalise A1

Strategy. Confirm the answer. $y = \sqrt{6}/2 = \sqrt{3/2}$. Equivalently, rationalise $\sqrt{3}/\sqrt{2} \cdot \frac{\sqrt{2}}{\sqrt{2}} = \frac{\sqrt{6}}{2}$.

Answer: $\dfrac{\sqrt{6}}{2}$ (confirmed with green checkmark at ~42:00)

Sense check: $\sqrt{6}/2 \approx 1.225$. This is NOT $\sqrt{3}/2 \approx 0.866$ (a different value). The body diagonal $\sqrt{3} \approx 1.732$ is a distractor. The two-step method is essential.
