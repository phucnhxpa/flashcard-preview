Q6 · Mean Age with New Members

Question

The mean age of the twenty members of a running club is exactly 28. The mean age increases by exactly 2 years when two new members join.

What is the mean age of the two new members?

[4 marks] · Options: A) 20 years B) 22 years C) 30 years D) 40 years E) 50 years F) 52 years

Solution [4 marks] · 4 steps

Original: 20 members, mean = 28. After 2 join: 22 members, mean = 28 + 2 = 30. Mean = sum ÷ count, so $\Sigma = n\bar{x}$. Parse each sentence into one equation.
STEP 1/4original sum M1

Strategy. Translate "mean of 20 = 28" into a sum. Use $\Sigma = n\bar{x}$.

Original sum

$$\frac{\Sigma_1}{20} = 28 \quad\Longrightarrow\quad \Sigma_1 = 20 \times 28 = 560$$
Technique: Parse word problems line by line. "Mean of 20 = 28" → $\Sigma = 560$. One equation per sentence.
↳ from STEP 1: $\Sigma_1 = 560$
STEP 2/4new sum (22 members) M1

Strategy. Mean increases by 2, so new mean = 30. Two new members join → count = 22 (NOT 20).

New sum

$$\frac{\Sigma_2}{22} = 30 \quad\Longrightarrow\quad \Sigma_2 = 22 \times 30 = 660$$
Trap: Using $\frac{\Sigma}{20} = 30$ instead of $\frac{\Sigma}{22} = 30$. This gives $\Sigma = 600$, extra = 40, mean = 20 — which is option A (a deliberate distractor). After adding 2 members, the denominator is 22, not 20.
↳ from STEP 2: $\Sigma_2 = 660$
STEP 3/4combined age of new members A1

Strategy. The difference between the two sums is the total age added by the two new members.

Sum added

$$\Sigma_{\text{new}} = \Sigma_2 - \Sigma_1 = 660 - 560 = 100$$
↳ from STEP 3: $\Sigma_{\text{new}} = 100$
STEP 4/4mean age of 2 new members A1

Strategy. Divide the combined age by 2 (there are two new members).

Mean of new members

$$\bar{x}_{\text{new}} = \frac{100}{2} = 50 \text{ years}$$
Answer: E — 50 years

Sticky note from Harry: "Recognise that the mean age increases by 2 years, so the new mean is 30. This helps to simplify the sums." Check: $28 + 2 = 30$ ✓. If you mistakenly use mean = 28, you get $22 \times 28 = 616$, extra = 56, mean = 28 — a dead end.
