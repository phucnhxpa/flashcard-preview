Q5 · Clock Angle at 9:45

Question

If you look at a clock and the time is 9:45, what is the angle between the hour and the minute hands?

[3 marks] · Options: A) 0° B) 7.5° C) 15° D) 22.5° E) 30°

Solution [3 marks] · 3 steps

Time = 9:45. Each hour mark = 30° ($360°/12$). The hour hand moves continuously.
STEP 1/3minute hand position M1

Strategy. The minute hand at 45 minutes points to the 9-position. Calculate its angle from 12.

Minute hand

$$\text{Minute angle} = \frac{45}{60} \times 360° = 270° \text{ from 12}$$

The minute hand is at the 9 o'clock position.

↳ from STEP 1: minute hand at 270°
STEP 2/3hour hand (moves continuously!) M1

Strategy. KEY INSIGHT: the hour hand does NOT sit at 9 — it moves continuously. At 9:45 it is $\frac{3}{4}$ of the way from 9 to 10.

Hour hand

$$\text{Hour angle} = 9 \times 30° + \frac{45}{60} \times 30° = 270° + 22.5° = 292.5°$$
Trap 1 (→0°): Assuming the hour hand sits exactly at 9. It doesn't — it moves $\frac{3}{4}$ of the way from 9 to 10.
Trap 2 (→7.5°): Using $\frac{1}{4}$ hour instead of $\frac{3}{4}$ hour. At 45 min = $\frac{3}{4}$ hour, the hour hand moves $\frac{3}{4} \times 30° = 22.5°$ past 9.
↳ from STEP 2: hour hand at 292.5°
STEP 3/3angle between hands A1

Strategy. Subtract the two positions. Always take the minimum (smaller) angle.

Angle between hands

$$\text{Angle} = 292.5° - 270° = 22.5°$$
Answer: D — 22.5°

Formula shortcut: $|30H - 5.5M| = |30(9) - 5.5(45)| = |270 - 247.5| = 22.5°$. If the result exceeds 180°, subtract from 360°.
