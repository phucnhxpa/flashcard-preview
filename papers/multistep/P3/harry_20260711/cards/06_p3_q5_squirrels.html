WMA13/01 October 2025 Q5 — squirrels model and maximum

Question

In this question you must show all stages of your working.

Solutions relying entirely on calculator technology are not acceptable.

The number of squirrels in a forest is being studied.

The number of squirrels, $N$, in the forest, $t$ years after the start of the study, is modelled by the equation $$N=\frac{4000e^{0.1t}}{19+e^{0.2t}},\qquad t\ge0.$$

Use the equation of the model to answer parts (a), (b), (c) and (d).

(a) Find the number of squirrels in the forest at the start of the study. (1)

(b) Find $dN/dt$. (2)

The number of squirrels in the forest is at a maximum when $t=T$.

Using the answer to part (b),

(c) show that $e^{0.2T}=A$, where $A$ is a constant to be found. (2)

(d) Hence find the maximum number of squirrels in the forest.

Show your working and give your answer to the nearest whole number. (3)

Status: solved. The official exponent $0.2t$ and checked maximum 459 are retained; the ledger readings $0.1t$ in the denominator and 959 are rejected.

[8 marks: (a) 1, (b) 2, (c) 2, (d) 3]

Part (a)[1 mark] · 1 step

STEP 1/1

Strategy. Substitute the starting time $t=0$ directly into the model and evaluate every exponential before simplifying the fraction.

Why this method applies. “At the start of the study” translates to $t=0$. Exponentials with zero exponent equal one, so both numerator and denominator can be evaluated exactly.

Formula or definition first. Use $e^0=1$ and the stated model $N(t)=4000e^{0.1t}/(19+e^{0.2t})$.

Full intermediate working. At $t=0$, $$N(0)=\frac{4000e^0}{19+e^0}=\frac{4000}{19+1}=\frac{4000}{20}=\boxed{200\text{ squirrels}}.$$ No rounding is required because the result is exact.

Difficult transition. The difficult transition is evaluating the entire denominator. The constant 19 does not replace the exponential term; $e^0$ contributes an additional one.

Diagnosis / why the rejected route fails. The lesson records that the $+1$ was initially lost. Using 4000/19 fails because it ignores the denominator’s exponential at zero and produces a non-integer value inconsistent with the exact substitution.

Final check / check back. Reinsert 200 into the original equation by multiplying the denominator: $200(20)=4000$, exactly the numerator at zero. The visual starts at 200 and rises toward the later maximum.

Exam trigger / transfer trigger. For every initial-value model, write the time value first, replace each exponential separately, keep parentheses around the denominator, and simplify only after all terms are visible.

Part (b)[2 marks] · 1 step

Carry-in: Source model: $N=4000e^{0.1t}/(19+e^{0.2t})$.
STEP 1/1

Strategy. Define numerator and denominator functions, differentiate each, apply the quotient rule with the subtraction visible, and simplify exponent products without changing the denominator.

Why this method applies. The model is a quotient in which both numerator and denominator depend on $t$. Product or chain differentiation alone is insufficient; quotient structure governs the derivative.

Formula or definition first. Let $u=4000e^{0.1t}$ and $v=19+e^{0.2t}$. Then $u\prime=400e^{0.1t}$ and $v\prime=0.2e^{0.2t}$. Use $(u/v)\prime=(vu\prime-uv\prime)/v^2$.

Full intermediate working. Substitute: $$\frac{dN}{dt}=\frac{(19+e^{0.2t})(400e^{0.1t})-(4000e^{0.1t})(0.2e^{0.2t})}{(19+e^{0.2t})^2}.$$ Expand the numerator: $7600e^{0.1t}+400e^{0.3t}-800e^{0.3t}=7600e^{0.1t}-400e^{0.3t}$. Therefore $$\boxed{\frac{dN}{dt}=\frac{7600e^{0.1t}-400e^{0.3t}}{(19+e^{0.2t})^2}}.$$

Difficult transition. The difficult transition combines the exponent law $e^{0.1t}e^{0.2t}=e^{0.3t}$ with the quotient-rule subtraction. Combining before distributing the minus sign is a common source of the wrong coefficient.

Diagnosis / why the rejected route fails. The lesson records a dropped minus sign. A plus sign would make the derivative numerator always positive and would prevent the stationary maximum required in part (c), so it is both algebraically and conceptually inconsistent.

Final check / check back. At $t=0$, the numerator is $7600-400=7200>0$, so the population initially rises, matching the graph. The squared denominator is positive and keeps the derivative sign controlled by the numerator.

Exam trigger / transfer trigger. When differentiating a quotient model, name $u,v$, write the rule before substitution, bracket both products, preserve the minus, simplify exponentials last, and inspect the derivative sign for model plausibility.

Part (c)[2 marks] · 1 step

Carry-in: From part (b): derivative numerator $7600e^{0.1t}-400e^{0.3t}$; denominator $(19+e^{0.2t})^2>0$.
STEP 1/1

Strategy. At the maximum, set the derivative equal to zero, use positivity of the denominator and exponential factor, and solve only to the requested show-that form $e^{0.2T}=A$.

Why this method applies. A rational derivative is zero exactly when its numerator is zero, provided its denominator is nonzero. Here the denominator is a positive square for every real $T$, and exponentials are also positive.

Formula or definition first. Use the stationary condition $dN/dt=0$ and the carried numerator $7600e^{0.1T}-400e^{0.3T}$. Factor $e^{0.1T}$ after using $e^{0.3T}=e^{0.1T}e^{0.2T}$.

Full intermediate working. Write $$7600e^{0.1T}-400e^{0.3T}=0.$$ Factor: $$e^{0.1T}(7600-400e^{0.2T})=0.$$ Since $e^{0.1T}>0$, the second factor must vanish: $$7600-400e^{0.2T}=0.$$ Divide by 400 to obtain $$\boxed{e^{0.2T}=19},$$ so the requested constant is $\boxed{A=19}$.

Difficult transition. The difficult transition is knowing where to stop. The command says “show that” and asks for $A$, not for a decimal value of $T$. Solving for $T$ here adds unnecessary work and can introduce premature rounding.

Diagnosis / why the rejected route fails. The lesson records an attempt to continue and a base-10 log notation. That route fails the command discipline: the target has already been reached. If $T$ were later needed, the natural logarithm would undo base $e$.

Final check / check back. Substitute $e^{0.2T}=19$ into the numerator factor: $7600-400(19)=0$, so the derivative is exactly zero. The exponential factor and denominator remain positive, validating the cancellation.

Exam trigger / transfer trigger. On a “show that” stationary-point part, set the derivative numerator to zero, state why discarded factors cannot vanish, reach the printed form exactly, identify the constant, and stop.

Part (d)[3 marks] · 1 step

Carry-in: From part (c): $e^{0.2T}=19$, hence $e^{0.1T}=\sqrt{19}>0$.
STEP 1/1

Strategy. Use the exact stationary relation from part (c) directly in the original model. Take the positive square root to obtain $e^{0.1T}$, substitute without first rounding $T$, and round only the final population.

Why this method applies. The model contains both $e^{0.1T}$ and $e^{0.2T}$. Because $e^{0.2T}=(e^{0.1T})^2$ and exponentials are positive, the carried relation determines both factors exactly enough for substitution.

Formula or definition first. From $e^{0.2T}=19$, use $e^{0.1T}=\sqrt{19}$. Then evaluate $N(T)=4000e^{0.1T}/(19+e^{0.2T})$.

Full intermediate working. Substitute: $$N(T)=\frac{4000\sqrt{19}}{19+19}=\frac{4000\sqrt{19}}{38}=458.831467\ldots.$$ The requested nearest whole number is therefore $$\boxed{459\text{ squirrels}}.$$ The exact stationary relation avoids computing and rounding $T$ altogether.

Difficult transition. The difficult transition is taking the positive square root. Although an algebraic square has two roots, $e^{0.1T}$ is strictly positive, so only $+\sqrt{19}$ is possible. Rounding a separately calculated $T$ before substitution would perturb the maximum.

Diagnosis / why the rejected route fails. The passed guide rejects an analysis reading of 959 and an incorrect denominator exponent. The official model and spoken confirmation support 459. A value near 959 also contradicts the plotted scale and exact substitution.

Final check / check back. The curve peaks at about 459 and the initial value was 200, so the magnitude is plausible. The derivative changes from positive to negative as $e^{0.2t}$ crosses 19, confirming a maximum rather than a minimum. Rounding $458.831\ldots$ gives 459.

Exam trigger / transfer trigger. When a previous stationary condition gives an exact exponential relation, substitute that relation into the model instead of solving for time. Preserve exact radicals, check the derivative sign change, and round only the final requested quantity.
