Q2 · Algebraic division of \(\tfrac{2x^{2}-5x+8}{x-2}\), then integrate

Question 2. \(g(x)=\dfrac{2x^{2}-5x+8}{x-2}\). (a) Write \(g(x)\) in the form \(Ax+B+\dfrac{C}{x-2}\), where \(A\), \(B\) and \(C\) are integers to be found.   (b) Show that \(\displaystyle\int_{4}^{8} g(x)\,dx=\alpha+\beta\ln 3\), where \(\alpha\) and \(\beta\) are integers to be found.

No official question-paper PDF or mark scheme was in the source set, so per-part marks are not shown.
Status: fully worked in the lesson — \(A=2,\ B=-1,\ C=6\); \(\int_{4}^{8} g\,dx=44+6\ln 3\). Algebra-only question — no diagram needed.
Part (a) · Write \(g(x)=Ax+B+\dfrac{C}{x-2}\) (two methods)Solved
Hide working
Strategy
Two equivalent routes. Method 1 polynomial long division (Harry's preferred, for speed); Method 2 the identity / substitution method as a cross-check.
Method 1 · long division
Divide \(2x^{2}-5x+8\) by \(x-2\):\[ 2x^{2}\div x=2x;\quad 2x(x-2)=2x^{2}-4x;\quad (-5x)-(-4x)=-x. \]\[ -x\div x=-1;\quad -1(x-2)=-x+2;\quad 8-2=6\ (\text{remainder}). \]\[ g(x)=2x-1+\frac{6}{x-2}. \]
Method 2 · identity / substitution
Multiply up: \(2x^{2}-5x+8\equiv(Ax+B)(x-2)+C\). Then\[ x=2:\ 8-10+8=C\Rightarrow C=6; \]\[ x=0:\ 8=-2B+6\Rightarrow B=-1;\qquad x=1:\ 5=-(A-1)+6\Rightarrow A=2. \]
Result
\( g(x)=2x-1+\dfrac{6}{x-2},\qquad A=2,\ B=-1,\ C=6. \)
Sense check
Both methods agree. Quick check at \(x=3\): LHS \(\tfrac{18-15+8}{1}=11\); RHS \(6-1+6=11\) ✓.
Phuc's slips (recorded): In the division Phuc computed \((-5x)-(-4x)\) as \(-9x\) instead of \(-x\). Harry's rule: always bracket the term you subtract — \((-5x)-(-4x)=-5x+4x=-x\). Phuc also mis-wrote the answer as \(\tfrac{(x-2)(2x-1)+6}{x-2}\); the remainder \(6\) alone sits over \((x-2)\).
Part (b) · Show \(\int_{4}^{8} g(x)\,dx=\alpha+\beta\ln 3\)Solved
Carry-inFrom (a): \(g(x)=2x-1+\dfrac{6}{x-2}\), so \(\int g\,dx=x^{2}-x+6\ln|x-2|\).
Hide working
Strategy
Integrate the part-(a) form term by term. The key fact from the warm-up: \(\int\tfrac{k}{x-2}\,dx=k\ln|x-2|\), because \(\tfrac{d}{dx}\bigl(6\ln|x-2|\bigr)=\tfrac{6}{x-2}\).
Formula · antiderivative
\[ \int\!\Bigl(2x-1+\tfrac{6}{x-2}\Bigr)dx=x^{2}-x+6\ln|x-2|+C. \]
Substitute the limits \(4\) and \(8\)
\[ \Bigl[x^{2}-x+6\ln|x-2|\Bigr]_{4}^{8} =\bigl(64-8+6\ln 6\bigr)-\bigl(16-4+6\ln 2\bigr). \]
Working · simplify with the log law
\[ =(56-12)+6(\ln 6-\ln 2)=44+6\ln\tfrac{6}{2}=44+6\ln 3. \](The modulus signs drop because \(6,2>0\).)
Result
\( \displaystyle\int_{4}^{8} g(x)\,dx=44+6\ln 3, \qquad \alpha=44,\ \beta=6. \)
Sense check
\(\ln 6-\ln 2=\ln\tfrac62=\ln 3\) is the quotient log law; the polynomial part \(x^2-x\) contributes \(56-12=44\).
Phuc's slip (recorded): Phuc paused on \(\int\tfrac{6}{x-2}\,dx\) — recognising it as a \(\ln\)-type integral \(6\ln|x-2|\) was the sticking point; and he used square brackets \(\bigl[\ \bigr]_{4}^{8}\) for the definite-integral evaluation, which is the correct notation.
