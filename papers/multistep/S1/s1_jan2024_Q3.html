Q3 — Probability Tree, Conditional Probability

Question

In a sixth form college each student in Year 12 and Year 13 is either left-handed (L) or right-handed (R). The partially completed tree diagram, where $p$ is a probability, gives information about these students.

[Tree: Year 12 prob $p$, then $\frac{1}{8}$ to L; Year 13 prob $1-p$, then $\frac{1}{10}$ to L.]

(a) Complete the tree diagram, in terms of $p$ where necessary. (1)

The probability that a student is left-handed is 0.11.

(b) Find the value of $p$. (3)

(c) Find the probability that a student selected at random is in Year 12 and left-handed. (2)

Given that a student is right-handed,

(d) find the probability that the student is in Year 12. (2)

[8 marks]

Part (a)[1 mark] · 1 step

STEP 1/1

The missing branches are complements:
- Year 12, R: $1 - \frac{1}{8} = \frac{7}{8}$
- Year 13, R: $1 - \frac{1}{10} = \frac{9}{10}$

Part (b)[3 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2

From the tree, the total probability of L:

$$P(L) = p \cdot \frac{1}{8} + (1-p) \cdot \frac{1}{10} = 0.11$$

STEP 2/2

Multiply both sides by the common denominator 80:

$$10p + 8(1-p) = 0.11 \times 80$$
$$10p + 8 - 8p = 8.8$$
$$2p + 8 = 8.8$$
$$2p = 0.8$$
$$\boxed{p = 0.4 = \tfrac{2}{5}}$$

Target: $\boxed{p = 0.4 = \tfrac{2}{5}}$

Part (c)[2 marks] · 1 step

Carry-in: Part (b) result: $\boxed{p = 0.4 = \tfrac{2}{5}}$
STEP 1/1

$$P(\text{Y12} \cap L) = p \times \frac{1}{8} = \frac{2}{5} \times \frac{1}{8} = \frac{2}{40} = \boxed{\frac{1}{20}}$$

Target: $\boxed{\frac{1}{20}}$

Part (d)[2 marks] · 4 steps

Carry-in: Part (b) result: $\boxed{p = 0.4 = \tfrac{2}{5}}$
Part (c) result: $\boxed{\frac{1}{20}}$
STEP 1/4

Use the conditional probability formula:

$$P(\text{Y12} \mid R) = \frac{P(\text{Y12} \cap R)}{P(R)}$$

Numerator:
$$P(\text{Y12} \cap R) = p \times \frac{7}{8} = \frac{2}{5} \times \frac{7}{8} = \frac{14}{40} = \frac{7}{20}$$

STEP 2/4

Denominator (two equivalent routes --- they MUST agree):

Route 1 (complement): $P(R) = 1 - P(L) = 1 - 0.11 = 0.89 = \dfrac{89}{100}$.

Route 2 (direct from tree):
$$P(R) = p \cdot \frac{7}{8} + (1-p) \cdot \frac{9}{10} = \frac{2}{5} \cdot \frac{7}{8} + \frac{3}{5} \cdot \frac{9}{10} = \frac{7}{20} + \frac{27}{50}$$
$$= \frac{35}{100} + \frac{54}{100} = \frac{89}{100} \quad ✓$$

STEP 3/4

Divide:
$$P(\text{Y12} \mid R) = \frac{7/20}{89/100} = \frac{7}{20} \times \frac{100}{89} = \frac{700}{1780} = \boxed{\frac{35}{89} \approx 0.393}$$

From the Loom (verbatim, Mr. Fuat):

"Common mistake: Writing $P(\text{Y12} \mid R) = p \times \frac{7}{8}$ --- that's $P(\text{Y12} \cap R)$, not the conditional."

Target: $\boxed{\frac{35}{89} \approx 0.393}$
STEP 4/4

Key formula / concept.

$$\boxed{P(A \mid B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)}}$$

The conditioning event $B$ always goes in the denominator. The trigger phrase "given that $B$" demands this formula.

Exam checklist.

  • [ ] Write the conditional formula $P(A|B) = P(A \cap B)/P(B)$ on the page as soon as "given that" appears.
  • [ ] Compute $P(B)$ (the denominator) by two routes if possible --- complement vs direct from tree --- and verify they match.
  • [ ] State the numerator as an intersection probability with the symbol $\cap$ written explicitly.
  • [ ] Final answer either as fraction $\frac{35}{89}$ or 3 s.f. decimal $0.393$.
  • [ ] Branch probabilities on each split must sum to 1.

Common pitfalls.

  • Returning $P(A \cap B)$ when the question asks for $P(A \mid B)$ --- Phuc's exact error.
  • Computing $P(R)$ by complement only and not cross-checking against direct calculation --- risks an arithmetic slip going undetected.
  • Inverting the formula: writing $P(B \mid A) = P(A \cap B)/P(B)$ instead of $/ P(A)$.

See also Q5(b) --- same conditional-probability trigger in a more complex Normal-distribution setting; Q6(a) --- uses $P(A \cap B) = P(B \mid A) P(A)$.
