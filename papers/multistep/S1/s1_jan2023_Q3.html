$$\boxed{0 \le a \le 0.6}$$

Key concept (NOT in formula booklet). For a discrete RV:
$$E(X) = \sum x\,P(X=x), \qquad E(X^2) = \sum x^2\,P(X=x), \qquad \mathrm{Var}(X) = E(X^2) - [E(X)]^2$$

Target: $\boxed{0 \le a \le 0.6}$

(b) Range of $E(X)$

$E(X) = 3.6 - 2a$ is linear and decreasing in $a$, so:
- max at $a = 0$: $E(X) = 3.6$
- min at $a = 0.6$: $E(X) = 3.6 - 1.2 = 2.4$

$$\boxed{2.4 \le E(X) \le 3.6}$$

(c) Variance equation — Phuc's redo

Step 1 — $E(X^2)$:
$$E(X^2) = 4a + 9(0.4) + 16(0.6 - a) = 4a + 3.6 + 9.6 - 16a = 13.2 - 12a$$

Step 2 — variance identity:
$$\mathrm{Var}(X) = E(X^2) - [E(X)]^2 \;\Rightarrow\; 0.56 = (13.2 - 12a) - (3.6 - 2a)^2$$

Step 3 — expand:
$$(3.6 - 2a)^2 = 12.96 - 14.4a + 4a^2$$

Step 4 — substitute (mind the sign flip on every term):
$$0.56 = 13.2 - 12a - 12.96 + 14.4a - 4a^2$$
$$0.56 = 0.24 + 2.4a - 4a^2$$

Step 5 — standard quadratic = 0:
$$4a^2 - 2.4a + 0.32 = 0 \;\Rightarrow\; a^2 - 0.6a + 0.08 = 0$$

Step 6 — solve:
$$a = \frac{0.6 \pm \sqrt{0.36 - 0.32}}{2} = \frac{0.6 \pm 0.2}{2}$$
$$a = 0.4 \quad\text{or}\quad a = 0.2$$

Step 7 — check constraint $0 \le a \le 0.6$. Both $0.2$ and $0.4$ are valid.

$$\boxed{a = 0.2 \text{ or } a = 0.4}$$
