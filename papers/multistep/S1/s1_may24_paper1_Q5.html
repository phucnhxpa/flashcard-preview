Q5 — Round 1: $X \sim N(15, 2^2)$. Round 2: $T \sim N(\mu, \sigma^2)$

Question

Round-1 time $X$ minutes $\sim N(15, 2^2)$.

(a) Find $\Pr(X < 18)$ by standardising. [2]

(b) The fastest 60% of competitors qualify. Find the longest time at which a competitor still qualifies. [3]

(c) Round-2 time $T \sim N(\mu, \sigma^2)$ satisfies $\Pr(\mu - 10 < T < \mu + 10) = 0.95$. Find $\Pr(T > \mu - 5 \mid T > \mu - 10)$. [5]

[10 marks]

Part (a) — $\Pr(X < 18)$ [2 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1standardise M1 A1

Formula: $z = \dfrac{x - \mu}{\sigma}$.

$$z = \frac{18 - 15}{2} = 1.5$$

From the normal table: $\Pr(Z < 1.5) = 0.9332$.

$\boxed{\,\Pr(X < 18) = 0.9332\,}$

Part (b) — Longest qualifying time (fastest 60%) [3 marks] · 2 steps

"Fastest 60% qualify" ⇒ qualifiers have the LOWEST 60% of times. The "longest qualifying time" $t$ is the 60th percentile of $X$.
STEP 1/2translate wording M1

Lowest 60% of times ⇒ $\Pr(X \leq t) = 0.60$, so by symmetry $\Pr\!\left(Z \leq \dfrac{t-15}{2}\right) = 0.60$.

From the inverse table (or $\Phi^{-1}(0.6)$): $z = 0.2533$.

Read carefully: "fastest 60%" "longest qualifying time" $=$ 60th percentile. NOT $\Pr(X > t) = 0.60$ (that gives the 40th percentile = 14.49 min, which would mean 60% of times are slower — the opposite of qualifying).
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $z = 0.2533$ (60th percentile, $\Phi^{-1}(0.60)$).
STEP 2/2solve for $t$ M1 A1

$$\frac{t - 15}{2} = 0.2533 \;\Longrightarrow\; t = 15 + 2(0.2533) = 15.51$$

$\boxed{\,t \approx 15.5\ \text{min}\,(3\,\text{s.f.})\,}$

Part (c) — $\Pr(T > \mu - 5 \mid T > \mu - 10)$ [5 marks] · 4 steps

$T \sim N(\mu, \sigma^2),\ \Pr(\mu-10 < T < \mu+10) = 0.95$.
STEP 1/4symmetric interval ⇒ $\sigma$ M1 A1

Symmetric-95% trick (memorise — NOT in booklet): $\Pr(\mu - k < X < \mu + k) = 0.95 \;\Longleftrightarrow\; k = 1.96\sigma$.

Here $k = 10$:

$$\sigma = \frac{10}{1.96} = 5.102\ldots \approx 5.10$$
