Q2 — Correlation, Coding $^\circ$F $\to$ $^\circ$C

Question

The average minimum monthly temperature, $x$ $^\circ$F, and the average maximum monthly temperature, $y$ $^\circ$F, in Kolkata were recorded for 12 months.

$$\sum x = 862,\qquad \sum x^2 = 62\,802,\qquad S_{yy} = 413.67,\qquad S_{xy} = 512.67,\qquad n = 12$$

(a) (i) Calculate the mean of the 12 values of the average minimum monthly temperature.
(ii) Show that the standard deviation of the 12 values of the average minimum monthly temperature is 8.57 $^\circ$F to 3 significant figures. (3)

(b) Calculate the product moment correlation coefficient between $x$ and $y$. (3)

The formula used was $c = \dfrac{5}{9}(f - 32)$.

(c) Use this formula and the values from part (a) to calculate, in $^\circ$C, the mean and the standard deviation of the 12 values of the average minimum monthly temperature in Kolkata. Give your answers to 3 significant figures. (4)

Given that $u$ is the equivalent in $^\circ$C of $x$ and $v$ is the equivalent in $^\circ$C of $y$,

(d) state, giving a reason, the product moment correlation coefficient between $u$ and $v$. (2)

[12 marks]

STEP 1/3

(a)(i) $$\bar{x} = \frac{\sum x}{n} = \frac{862}{12} = 71.833\overline{3} \approx 71.8\ ^\circ\text{F}$$

What went wrong:. Image shows Phuc consulting the formula sheet for $S_{xx}$ and $r$ --- these should be memorised to save exam time. For (c) the conceptual trap is mixing the additive shift ($-32$, which leaves s.d. alone) with the multiplicative scaling ($\times 5/9$, which scales s.d.).

STEP 2/3

(a)(ii) $$S_{xx} = \sum x^2 - \frac{(\sum x)^2}{n} = 62802 - \frac{862^2}{12} = 62802 - \frac{743044}{12} = 62802 - 61920.333 = 881.667$$

STEP 3/3

$$\sigma_x = \sqrt{\frac{S_{xx}}{n}} = \sqrt{\frac{881.667}{12}} = \sqrt{73.4722} = 8.5716\ldots \approx 8.57\ ^\circ\text{F (3 s.f.)} \quad ✓$$

Part (b)[3 marks] . 1 steps

STEP 1/1

$$r = \frac{S_{xy}}{\sqrt{S_{xx}\,S_{yy}}} = \frac{512.67}{\sqrt{881.667 \times 413.67}} = \frac{512.67}{\sqrt{364752.5}} = \frac{512.67}{603.948} = 0.8489\ldots \approx 0.849$$

Part (c)2 steps

STEP 1/2

Mean in $^\circ$C: apply the formula to $\bar{x}$:

$$\bar{c} = \frac{5}{9}(\bar{x} - 32) = \frac{5}{9}(71.833 - 32) = \frac{5}{9} \times 39.833 = 22.13 \approx 22.1\ ^\circ\text{C}$$

STEP 2/2

Standard deviation in $^\circ$C: the constant $-32$ is an additive shift (does not change s.d.); only the multiplier $5/9$ matters:

$$\sigma_c = \frac{5}{9}\sigma_x = \frac{5}{9} \times 8.5716 = 4.762 \approx 4.76\ ^\circ\text{C}$$

Part (d)[2 marks] . 3 steps

STEP 1/3

PMCC between $u$ and $v$ equals PMCC between $x$ and $y$:

STEP 2/3

$$r_{u,v} = r_{x,y} = 0.849$$

STEP 3/3

Reason: PMCC is invariant under linear coding $u = a + bx$, $v = c + dy$ with $b, d > 0$. Here $b = d = 5/9 > 0$, so $r$ is unchanged.

Key formula / concept.

Key formula / concept:
- Coding rule: $u = a + bx \Rightarrow \bar{u} = a + b\bar{x},\ \sigma_u = |b|\sigma_x$.
- PMCC invariant under positive linear coding on both variables.

Common pitfalls:
