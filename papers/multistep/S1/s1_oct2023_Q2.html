What went wrong. Part (d) --- Phuc wrote a long paragraph mentioning skewness, range and outliers. The Edexcel mark scheme for "compare two distributions, interpret in context" rewards a rigid template: one comparison of location (median), one comparison of spread (IQR), each interpreted with the variable name and units. Anything else is wasted ink.

From the Slack thread (Mr Fuat, verbatim):

"for d, only compare the median and IQR between two distributions. In the median, we just need to say that the median of the data one is greater or less than the median of the data two. That's all. We don't need to say anything else. For the interquartile range, we need to say that the interquartile range of data one is greater or less than the interquartile range of data two. If the interquartile range is less, this means the data is more consistent."

Phuc's incorrect approach (annotated). > "The December distribution is positively skewed compared to February \ldots{} range is bigger \ldots{} more outliers in December \ldots{}"

Too many comparisons; the mark scheme only marks the median + IQR + context. Extra commentary is ignored at best, and if any extra claim is wrong (e.g. miscalled skew) it can cancel a correct claim.

Audit checklist1 steps

STEP 1/1

Key formula / concept.

Key formula / concept

  • Quartile positions for ungrouped/discrete data (Edexcel S1): $Q_1 = \frac{n+1}{4}$, median $= \frac{n+1}{2}$, $Q_3 = \frac{3(n+1)}{4}$.
  • Outlier fences: $Q_1 - 1.5\,\text{IQR}$ and $Q_3 + 1.5\,\text{IQR}$.
  • Compare-in-context template: median + IQR + one context sentence each, with units and variable.

Exam checklist before moving on.

Exam checklist

  • [ ] Median found at $\frac{n+1}{2}$ position; if non-integer, average the two flanking values.
  • [ ] $Q_1$ and $Q_3$ positions calculated and averaged where needed.
  • [ ] Outlier fences shown explicitly: state $Q_3 + 1.5\,\text{IQR}$ and $Q_1 - 1.5\,\text{IQR}$ with their numerical values.
  • [ ] Box plot whiskers go to the most extreme non-outlier; outliers plotted as crosses.
  • [ ] "Compare" answer has exactly 3 statements: median, IQR, context --- no extras.

Common pitfalls.

Common pitfalls

  • Extending whiskers all the way to $16$ or $94$ --- they must stop at the most extreme non-outlier ($23$ and $86$).
  • Forgetting to convert "greater IQR" into a meaningful context statement (e.g. "more variable in February").
  • Mentioning skew or symmetry without being asked --- it eats time and risks cancelling a correct comparison.
