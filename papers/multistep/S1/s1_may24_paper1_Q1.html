Q1 — 35 Birch + 32 Maple seedlings: stem-and-leaf, quartiles, outlier, box plot, range of $a$

Question

The back-to-back stem-and-leaf diagram below shows the heights, in cm, of 35 Birch and 32 Maple seedlings. The median height of the Maple seedlings is 45 cm.

   Totals       Birch              Maple        Totals
    (2)                  9 8 | 2 | 5 7 7 8 9        (5)
    (8)        9 9 9 6 5 3 1 1 | 3 | 0 2 6 6 8 9 9  (7)
    (9)      9 8 8 7 6 3 1 1 1 | 4 | 1 1 1 k 7 8    (6)
    (9)      7 7 7 5 4 3 2 1 0 | 5 | 0 1 2 3 4 4 4  (7)
    (3)                  7 6 5 | 6 | 3 4 6          (3)
    (3)                  6 5 4 | 7 | 0 7            (2)
    (1)                      5 | 8 | 0 0            (2)

Key:  5 | 6 | 3   means 65 cm Birch / 63 cm Maple
Median height of Maple seedlings = 45 cm.

[13 marks]

Part (a) — Find $k$ [1 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1median position M1 A1

Formula (grouped/list median, even n):

$$\text{median} = \tfrac{1}{2}\bigl(x_{(n/2)} + x_{(n/2+1)}\bigr)$$

Maple has $n = 32$ ⇒ median = avg of 16th & 17th values.

Cumulative Maple totals: 5, 12, 18, 25, 28, 30, 32 — so positions 13–18 lie in the row $4|\,1\,1\,1\,k\,7\,8$.

13th = 41, 14th = 41, 15th = 41, 16th = $40+k$, 17th = 47, 18th = 48.

$$\text{median} = \frac{(40+k) + 47}{2} = 45 \;\Longrightarrow\; 87+k = 90$$

Trap: on the LEFT side (Birch) you read the leaves right-to-left — smallest closest to the stem. The Maple side reads left-to-right as normal.
$\boxed{\,k = 3\,}$

Part (b) — Find $Q_1$ and $Q_3$ for the Birch heights [2 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2Edexcel quartile rule M1

Edexcel rule for discrete / stem-and-leaf data (NOT $(n+1)/4$):

position $= \tfrac{n}{4}$ or $\tfrac{3n}{4}$. If integer ⇒ average that and the next; if non-integer ⇒ round UP.

$n = 35$:

$\tfrac{35}{4} = 8.75 \;\Rightarrow\; Q_1$ at position 9.

$\tfrac{3\cdot 35}{4} = 26.25 \;\Rightarrow\; Q_3$ at position 27.

↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $Q_1$ at position 9, $Q_3$ at position 27.
STEP 2/2read positions A1

Read Birch leaves right-to-left, smallest first.

Cumulative Birch totals (top → down): 2, 10, 19, 28, 31, 34, 35.

9th value lies in the 30s row (rows up to here total 10): leaves 31, 31, 33, 35, 36, 39, 39, 39 ⇒ 9th = 39.

27th value lies in the 50s row (cumulative through 40s = 19, through 50s = 28): the 50s row read smallest-first is 50, 51, 52, 53, 54, 55, 57, 57, 57 — so 20th=50, …, 27th = 57.

$Q_1 = 39 \text{ cm},\quad Q_3 = 57 \text{ cm}$

Part (c) — Show exactly one Birch outlier (use $k = 1.5$) [2 marks] · 2 steps

Definition: outlier $= $ value $< Q_1 - 1.5\cdot \text{IQR}$ or $> Q_3 + 1.5\cdot\text{IQR}$.
STEP 1/2fences M1

$\text{IQR} = Q_3 - Q_1 = 57 - 39 = 18$.

$1.5 \cdot \text{IQR} = 27$.

Lower fence: $Q_1 - 27 = 39 - 27 = 12$.

Upper fence: $Q_3 + 27 = 57 + 27 = 84$.

Fences: $[\,12,\;84\,]$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: Fences: $[12,\ 84]$ (lower 12, upper 84).
STEP 2/2count outliers A1

Min Birch = 28 (≥ 12 ✓ — none below).

Birch values $>84$: scan the 80s row → only 85.

So exactly one outlier: 85 cm.

Phrase to memorise: "85 > 84 ⇒ 85 is an outlier; all other values lie within the fences ⇒ exactly one outlier." A "show that" question wants both halves — find AND check no others.
$\boxed{\text{exactly one outlier: } 85\ \text{cm}}$

Part (f) — Range of $a$ if 27, $a$, 48, $2a$ added to Maple and box plot unchanged [3 marks] · 3 steps

Original Maple stats: min 25, $Q_1$ 36, median 45, $Q_3$ 54, max 80. After adding the 4 new values, $n = 32+4 = 36$, and the new box plot must be identical.
STEP 1/3max constraint M1

Max unchanged = 80 ⇒ all new values ≤ 80.

Largest of the four = $2a$ (assuming $a>0$ ⇒ $2a>a$).

$$2a \leq 80 \;\Longrightarrow\; \boxed{a \leq 40}$$

↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $a \leq 40$ (max constraint: $2a \leq 80$).
STEP 2/3$Q_1$ constraint M1

New $n=36$ ⇒ $Q_1$ at position $\tfrac{36}{4}=9$ (avg of 9th & 10th).

Original 8th & 9th values give $Q_1 = 36$. After inserting 4 values, the new 9th & 10th must still both equal 36 ⇒ exactly one new value may be inserted strictly below 36.

The four new values are 27 (already $<36$), $a$, 48, $2a$. We need exactly one $<36$:

27 is the one. So $a \geq 36$ (and $2a \geq 72 \geq 36$ automatic).

$$\boxed{a \geq 36}$$

↳ from STEP 2:From STEP 2: $a \geq 36$ ($Q_1$ constraint). From STEP 1: $a \leq 40$.
STEP 3/3median constraint + combine A1

New median = avg of 18th & 19th values. Originally 16th=43, 17th=47 (median (43+47)/2 = 45). After 4 inserts, the 18th & 19th must average 45 — i.e. still 43 and 47 from the original Maple list ⇒ exactly 2 new values $\leq 43$ and 2 new values $\geq 47$.

27, $a$ go below; 48, $2a$ go above ⇒ $a \leq 43$ and $2a \geq 47$ (both compatible with the earlier $36 \leq a \leq 40$).

$Q_3$ unchanged check: $Q_3$ at position 27 → original 23rd = 53, 24th = 54 unchanged ⇒ auto-OK.
