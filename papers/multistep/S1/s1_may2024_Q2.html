Q2 — Discrete Random Variable

Question

A spinner can land on the numbers 2, 4, 5, 7 or 8 only. The random variable $X$ represents the number that this spinner lands on when it is spun once. The probability distribution of $X$ is given in the table below.

$x$ 2 4 5 7 8
$P(X=x)$ 0.25 0.3 0.2 0.1 0.15

(a) Find $P(2X-3 > 5)$. (1)
Given that $E(X)=4.6$,
(b) show that $\operatorname{Var}(X) = 4.14$. (3)
The random variable $Y = aX - b$ where $a$ and $b$ are positive constants. Given that $E(Y)=13.4$ and $\operatorname{Var}(Y) = 66.24$,
(c) find the value of $a$ and the value of $b$. (4)
In a game Sam and Alex each spin the spinner once, landing on $X_1$ and $X_2$ respectively. Sam's score is $S = X_1$. Alex's score is $R = 2X_2 - 3$. The person with the higher score wins. If the scores are the same it is a draw.
(d) Find the probability that Sam wins the game. (4)

[9 marks]

Part (a)[1 mark] · 3 steps

STEP 1/3

Worked solution

$$2X-3 > 5 \;\Longleftrightarrow\; X > 4.$$

STEP 2/3

$$P(X>4) = P(X=5)+P(X=7)+P(X=8) = 0.2+0.1+0.15 = \boxed{0.45}$$

Key concept: Rearrange the linear inequality first; then sum point probabilities.

Target: $\boxed{0.45}$
STEP 3/3

Exam checklist.

  • [ ] Strict "$>$" — exclude $X=4$.
  • [ ] Sum probabilities for all valid $x$ values.

Common pitfalls.

Including $X=4$ (the inequality is strict); transcribing the wrong probability column.

Part (b)3 steps

Carry-in: Part (a) result: $\boxed{0.45}$
STEP 1/3

Worked solution

$$E(X^2) = 2^2(0.25)+4^2(0.3)+5^2(0.2)+7^2(0.1)+8^2(0.15)$$
$$= 1 + 4.8 + 5 + 4.9 + 9.6 = 25.3$$

STEP 2/3

$$\operatorname{Var}(X) = E(X^2) - [E(X)]^2 = 25.3 - 4.6^2 = 25.3 - 21.16 = \boxed{4.14}.\ ✓$$

Key formula (NOT in formula booklet for some texts, but IS in IAL booklet): $\operatorname{Var}(X) = E(X^2) - [E(X)]^2$. The form $\frac{\sum x^2}{n} - \bar{x}^2$ for raw data is NOT in the IAL booklet — Phuc noted this on Apr 24.

From the Loom (Fuat): "The expectation of $X$-squared is each $x$-squared times its probability — square the $x$, not the probability."

Target: $\boxed{4.14}$
STEP 3/3

Exam checklist.

  • [ ] Show all five $x^2 \cdot p$ terms.
  • [ ] Subtract $E(X)^2$, not $E(X)$.
  • [ ] Box the final $4.14$ to match the question's "show that".

Common pitfalls.

Squaring the probability instead of the $x$; arithmetic slip when accumulating five terms; forgetting to subtract $E(X)^2$.

Part (c)[4 marks] · 3 steps

Carry-in: Part (a) result: $\boxed{0.45}$
Part (b) result: $\boxed{4.14}$
STEP 1/3

Worked solution

Using variance first (cleanest):
$$\operatorname{Var}(Y) = a^2 \operatorname{Var}(X) \;\Longrightarrow\; 66.24 = a^2(4.14) \;\Longrightarrow\; a^2 = 16 \;\Longrightarrow\; a = 4\ (\text{positive}).$$

STEP 2/3

Then use expectation:
$$E(Y) = aE(X) - b \;\Longrightarrow\; 13.4 = 4(4.6) - b = 18.4 - b \;\Longrightarrow\; b = 5.$$

$$\boxed{a = 4,\quad b = 5}$$

Key formulas: $E(aX+b) = aE(X)+b$ and $\operatorname{Var}(aX+b)=a^2\operatorname{Var}(X)$ — both in the booklet.

Target: $\boxed{a = 4,\quad b = 5}$
STEP 3/3

Exam checklist.

  • [ ] Start with Var — eliminates the constant $b$ so you get $a$ first.
  • [ ] Choose the positive root of $a^2$.
  • [ ] Substitute $a$ back into $E(Y)$ to solve for $b$.
  • [ ] $a$ and $b$ should be tidy integers; if not, recheck arithmetic.

Common pitfalls.

Choosing $a=-4$ ("positive constants" stated); writing $\operatorname{Var}(Y)=a\operatorname{Var}(X)$ (forgot the square); applying the sign wrong on $b$ since $Y=aX-b$.

Part (d)[4 marks] · 5 steps

Part (b) result: $\boxed{4.14}$
Part (c) result: $\boxed{a = 4,\quad b = 5}$
STEP 1/5

Worked solution — Probability Sam wins

Compute $R$-values from $R=2X_2-3$:
| $X_2$ | 2 | 4 | 5 | 7 | 8 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| $R$ | 1 | 5 | 7 | 11 | 13 |
| $P(R)$ | 0.25 | 0.3 | 0.2 | 0.1 | 0.15 |

STEP 2/5

$S$ and $R$ are independent (different spins). Sam wins ⇔ $S > R$.

Condition on $S$:
