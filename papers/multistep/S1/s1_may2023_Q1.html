Q1 — Histogram

Question

The histogram shows the distances, in km, that 274 people travel to work. Given that 60 of these people travel between 10 km and 20 km to work, estimate
(a) the number of people who travel between 22 km and 45 km to work, (3)
(b) the median distance travelled to work by these 274 people, (2)
(c) the mean distance travelled to work by these 274 people. (3)

Histogram readings (calibrated against the named class): since 60 people lie in $[10,20)$ and the class width is 10, $\text{FD}_{[10,20)} = 6$. Reading the remaining bars against this scale:

Class (km) Width FD Frequency $f$ Midpoint
$[0,10)$ 10 0.5 5 5
$[10,20)$ 10 6 60 15
$[20,30)$ 10 7 70 25
$[30,40)$ 10 10 100 35
$[40,50)$ 10 3 30 45
$[50,60)$ 10 0.9 9 55
Total 274

Check: $5+60+70+100+30+9 = 274$ ✓

Needed before revealing this part

Calibrated FD scale: $[10,20)$ has frequency 60 and width 10, so FD $=6$. Needed slices: $[22,30)$ width 8 at FD 7, $[30,40)$ frequency 100, and $[40,45)$ width 5 at FD 3.

STEP 1/1Part (a)

Formula first. State the rule symbolically, then substitute values and box the exam answer.

(a) People in $[22, 45)$: slice across three bars.

  • $[22,30)$: width 8, FD 7 $\Rightarrow$ $f = 8 \times 7 = 56$
  • $[30,40)$: full, $f = 100$
  • $[40,45)$: width 5, FD 3 $\Rightarrow$ $f = 5 \times 3 = 15$

$$\text{Total} = 56 + 100 + 15 = \boxed{171}.$$

Mistake guard from the attempt/revision notes.

What went wrong (potential pitfalls noted from img_00.png).. Phuc began part (b) by writing "$274/2 = 137$" and "$274 - 60 = 214$", indicating an attempt to subtract bar frequencies sequentially rather than build a cumulative-frequency column. Risk: losing track of which class contains the 137th value.

Revision-guide trap.. A common error is to ignore the partial-bar pro-rating and treat $[20,40)$ as a clean two-class slice, then subtract a guess for the $[40,45)$ slice --- e.g. computing $70 + 100 + 30 = 200$ for $[20,50)$ and trying to remove "the bit between 20 and 22" by eye. This conflates full bars and partial bars and loses 2 marks in (a).

Target: $\boxed{171}$

Needed before revealing this part

$n/2=137$. Cumulative frequencies: $5,65,135,235,\ldots$ so the 137th value is in $[30,40)$ with $L=30$, $F=135$, $f_m=100$, $w=10$.

STEP 1/1Part (b)

Formula first. State the rule symbolically, then substitute values and box the exam answer.

(b) Median. Position $= n/2 = 137$. Cumulative frequencies: $5, 65, 135, 235, \ldots$ The 137th value lies in $[30,40)$ with $L=30$, $F=135$, $f_m=100$, $w=10$:

$$\text{median} = 30 + \frac{137-135}{100}\times 10 = 30 + 0.2 = \boxed{30.2 \text{ km}}.$$

Target: $\boxed{30.2 \text{ km}}$

Part (c)[3 marks] · 1 step

Needed before revealing this part

Use frequencies $5,60,70,100,30,9$ with midpoints $5,15,25,35,45,55$. Total $\sum f=274$.

STEP 1/1Part (c)

Formula first. State the rule symbolically, then substitute values and box the exam answer.

(c) Mean.
$$\sum f x_{\text{mid}} = 5(5)+60(15)+70(25)+100(35)+30(45)+9(55)$$
$$= 25 + 900 + 1750 + 3500 + 1350 + 495 = 8020.$$
$$\bar{x} = \frac{8020}{274} = 29.27\ldots \approx \boxed{29.3 \text{ km (3 s.f.)}}.$$

Target: $\boxed{29.3 \text{ km (3 s.f.)}}$

Common traps to actively avoid.

Common pitfalls.

- Pro-rating but using the wrong sub-width (e.g. 7 instead of 8 for $[22,30)$).
- Using $(n+1)/2$ for the median position (that's discrete data --- for continuous grouped data it is $n/2$).
- Treating "between 22 and 45" as inclusive of full bars only.
