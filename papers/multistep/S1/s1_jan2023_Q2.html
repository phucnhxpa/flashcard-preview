Q2 — Marbles transferred between bags — tree, P(diff), P(same), conditional

Question

Two bags, $X$ and $Y$, each contain green marbles (G) and blue marbles (B) only.
- Bag $X$ contains 5 green marbles and 4 blue marbles
- Bag $Y$ contains 6 green marbles and 5 blue marbles

A marble is selected at random from bag $X$ and placed in bag $Y$.
A second marble is selected at random from bag $X$ and placed in bag $Y$.
A third marble is then selected, this time from bag $Y$.

(a) Use this information to complete the tree diagram shown on page 7. (3)
(b) Find the probability that the 2 marbles selected from bag $X$ are of different colours. (2)
(c) Find the probability that all 3 marbles selected are the same colour. (2)

Given that all three marbles selected are the same colour,

(d) find the probability that they are all green. (3)

[10 marks]

Part (a)[3 marks] . 2 steps

STEP 1/2

Tree diagram probabilities

1st draw from X (9 marbles, 5G/4B): $P(G_1) = \tfrac{5}{9}$, $P(B_1) = \tfrac{4}{9}$.

From the source notes — common traps for this question.

What went wrong. Two specific slips, both flagged in the thread:

  1. He treated bag Y as having 11 marbles for the 3rd draw — the two transferred marbles push the total to 13.
  2. He tried to use a single value of $P(G_3)$ regardless of branch. The numerator of $P(G_3)$ is path-dependent: 8 (after GG), 7 (after GB/BG), 6 (after BB). For (c)/(d) only the pure GG and pure BB branches contribute, which keeps it tractable.

Key formula / concept When sampling without replacement and items move between containers, both the numerator and denominator of subsequent draws change. Always recompute composition for each branch.

STEP 2/2

2nd draw from X (now 8 marbles):
- After $G_1$ (4G, 4B): $P(G_2\mid G_1) = \tfrac{4}{8}$, $P(B_2\mid G_1) = \tfrac{4}{8}$.
- After $B_1$ (5G, 3B): $P(G_2\mid B_1) = \tfrac{5}{8}$, $P(B_2\mid B_1) = \tfrac{3}{8}$.

3rd draw from Y (13 marbles after 2 transferred in):
- After $GG$ added → Y has 8G/5B: $P(G_3) = \tfrac{8}{13}$, $P(B_3) = \tfrac{5}{13}$.
- After $GB$ or $BG$ added → Y has 7G/6B: $P(G_3) = \tfrac{7}{13}$, $P(B_3) = \tfrac{6}{13}$.
- After $BB$ added → Y has 6G/7B: $P(G_3) = \tfrac{6}{13}$, $P(B_3) = \tfrac{7}{13}$.

Part (b)[2 marks] . 2 steps

STEP 1/2

Two from X different colours

STEP 2/2

$$P(G_1B_2) + P(B_1G_2) = \tfrac{5}{9}!\cdot!\tfrac{4}{8} + \tfrac{4}{9}!\cdot!\tfrac{5}{8} = \tfrac{20}{72} + \tfrac{20}{72} = \tfrac{40}{72} = \boxed{\tfrac{5}{9} \approx 0.556}$$

Target: $\boxed{\tfrac{5}{9} \approx 0.556}$

Part (c)[2 marks] . 2 steps

Part (b) result: $\boxed{\tfrac{5}{9} \approx 0.556}$
STEP 1/2

All three same colour

All green (must come via $GG$ path):
$$P(GGG) = \tfrac{5}{9}!\cdot!\tfrac{4}{8}!\cdot!\tfrac{8}{13} = \tfrac{160}{936}$$

STEP 2/2

All blue (must come via $BB$ path):
$$P(BBB) = \tfrac{4}{9}!\cdot!\tfrac{3}{8}!\cdot!\tfrac{7}{13} = \tfrac{84}{936}$$

$$P(\text{same}) = \tfrac{160+84}{936} = \tfrac{244}{936} = \boxed{\tfrac{61}{234} \approx 0.261}$$

Target: $\boxed{\tfrac{61}{234} \approx 0.261}$

Part (d)[3 marks] . 2 steps

Part (b) result: $\boxed{\tfrac{5}{9} \approx 0.556}$
Part (c) result: $\boxed{\tfrac{61}{234} \approx 0.261}$
STEP 1/2

Conditional — all green given all same

$$P(\text{all G}\mid\text{same}) = \frac{P(\text{all G})}{P(\text{same})} = \frac{160/936}{244/936} = \frac{160}{244} = \boxed{\tfrac{40}{61} \approx 0.656}$$

Target: $\boxed{\tfrac{40}{61} \approx 0.656}$

Exam checklist
- Bag X totals 9 → 8 across draws 1 and 2. Bag Y totals 13 (not 11) for draw 3.
- For (c) you only need the $GG!\to!G_3$ and $BB!\to!B_3$ paths.
- Keep a common denominator (936) so (d) collapses cleanly to $\frac{160}{244}$.
- Conditional probability formula is $P(A\mid B) = P(A\cap B)/P(B)$ — here $A\subset B$ so $P(A\cap B) = P(A)$.
- Final answers to 3 s.f. or as exact fractions.

Common pitfalls.

Common pitfalls Forgetting to update Y's total; using $\tfrac{6}{13}$ for $P(G_3)$ on the GG branch (it should be $\tfrac{8}{13}$); answering (d) with $P(\text{all G})$ alone, ignoring the conditioning.
