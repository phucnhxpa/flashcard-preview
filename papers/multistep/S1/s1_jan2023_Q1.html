Q1 — Histogram — frequencies, mean, sd, IQR by interpolation

Question

The histogram shows the times taken, $t$ minutes, by each of 100 people to swim 500 metres.

(a) Use the histogram to complete the frequency table for the times taken by the 100 people to swim 500 metres.

Time taken (t minutes) 5–10 10–14 14–18 18–25 25–40
Frequency ($f$) 10 16 24

(1)

(b) Estimate the number of people who took less than 16 minutes to swim 500 metres. (2)

(c) Find an estimate for the mean time taken to swim 500 metres. (2)

Given that $\sum ft^2 = 41{,}033$

(d) find an estimate for the standard deviation of the times taken to swim 500 metres. (2)

Given that $Q_3 = 23$

(e) use linear interpolation to estimate the interquartile range of the times taken to swim 500 metres. (3)

[10 marks]

Setup2 steps

STEP 1/2

Reconstructing the missing frequencies (a)

Histogram frequencies = frequency density × class width. The 5–10, 10–14, 14–18 classes give frequencies 10, 16, 24, which fix the frequency-density scale. The remaining bars read off as:

STEP 2/2
Time $t$ 5–10 10–14 14–18 18–25 25–40
Frequency $f$ 10 16 24 28 22

Check: $10 + 16 + 24 + 28 + 22 = 100$ ✓

Part (b)[2 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2

People with $t < 16$ (linear interpolation inside 14–18)

The class 14–18 has 24 people spread uniformly. The portion $14 \le t < 16$ is $\dfrac{16-14}{18-14} = \dfrac{1}{2}$ of the class:

STEP 2/2

$$\text{frequency in } 14 \le t < 16 \;=\; \tfrac{2}{4}\times 24 \;=\; 12$$

Cumulative below 16:

$$10 + 16 + 12 \;=\; \boxed{38 \text{ people}}$$

Target: $\boxed{38 \text{ people}}$

Part (c)[2 marks] · 2 steps

Carry-in: Part (b) result: $\boxed{38 \text{ people}}$
STEP 1/2

Mean

Use class midpoints $\bar t_i = 7.5,\; 12,\; 16,\; 21.5,\; 32.5$.

STEP 2/2

$$\sum ft = 7.5(10) + 12(16) + 16(24) + 21.5(28) + 32.5(22)$$
$$= 75 + 192 + 384 + 602 + 715 = 1968$$

$$\bar t = \frac{\sum ft}{n} = \frac{1968}{100} = \boxed{19.68 \text{ minutes}}$$

Target: $\boxed{19.68 \text{ minutes}}$

Part (d)[2 marks] · 3 steps

Carry-in: Part (b) result: $\boxed{38 \text{ people}}$
Part (c) result: $\boxed{19.68 \text{ minutes}}$
STEP 1/3

Standard deviation

STEP 2/3

$$\sigma = \sqrt{\frac{\sum ft^2}{n} - \bar t^{\,2}} = \sqrt{\frac{41033}{100} - 19.68^2}$$

STEP 3/3

$$= \sqrt{410.33 - 387.2024} = \sqrt{23.1276} = \boxed{4.81 \text{ minutes (3 s.f.)}}$$

Target: $\boxed{4.81 \text{ minutes (3 s.f.)}}$

Part (e)[3 marks] · 3 steps

Carry-in: Part (b) result: $\boxed{38 \text{ people}}$
Part (c) result: $\boxed{19.68 \text{ minutes}}$
Part (d) result: $\boxed{4.81 \text{ minutes (3 s.f.)}}$
STEP 1/3

IQR — Phuc's redo focus

Correct method. Position $\dfrac{n}{4} = 25$. The 25th value falls in 10–14 (cumulative jumps 10 → 26 across this class).

STEP 2/3
Class $f$ Cumulative $F$
5–10 10 10
10–14 16 26
14–18 24 50

$$Q_1 = L + \frac{\tfrac{n}{4} - F_{\text{before}}}{f_{\text{class}}}\times w = 10 + \frac{25-10}{16}\times 4 = 10 + \frac{60}{16} = 13.75$$

$$\boxed{\text{IQR} = Q_3 - Q_1 = 23 - 13.75 = 9.25 \text{ minutes}}$$

Target: $\boxed{\text{IQR} = Q_3 - Q_1 = 23 - 13.75 = 9.25 \text{ minutes}}$
STEP 3/3

What went wrong (Phuc, this part).

Phuc skipped reconstructing the missing class frequencies first, which left the cumulative-frequency table incomplete; without F_before = 10 and f_class = 16 you cannot set up the interpolation at all. He also flirted with the $\frac{n+1}{4}$ rule from discrete data — IAL grouped data uses $\frac{n}{4}$ directly (25, not 25.25).

Key formula / concept Linear interpolation formula for the $k$-th quartile in grouped data:
$$Q = L + \frac{\tfrac{kn}{4} - F_{\text{before}}}{f_{\text{class}}}\times w$$
The question hands you $Q_3 = 23$ on purpose — they did not want you to interpolate the awkward 18–25 class.

Exam checklist.

  • Class frequencies sum to 100 before doing anything else.
  • Midpoints use the lower + upper boundary halves: $\frac{5+10}{2}=7.5$, $\frac{18+25}{2}=21.5$.
  • Use $\frac{n}{4}=25$ for grouped data — never $\frac{n+1}{4}$.
  • After locating the class, the interpolation denominator is the class frequency, not the cumulative one.
  • State final answers to 3 s.f. with units (minutes).

Common pitfalls.

Reading the histogram by bar height instead of area; using $\sigma^2$ instead of $\sigma$ in part (d); choosing the wrong quartile class (counting up to 25 lands you inside 10–14, not 5–10).
