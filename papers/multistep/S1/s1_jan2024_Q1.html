Q1 - Histogram, Median, Skew, Probability

Question

The histogram below shows the distribution of the heights, to the nearest cm, of 408 plants.

(a) Use the histogram to complete the following table.

Height ($h$ cm) $5 \le h < 9$ $9 \le h < 13$ $13 \le h < 15$ $15 \le h < 17$ $17 \le h < 25$
Frequency 32 152 120

(2)

(b) Use interpolation to estimate the median. (2)

The mean height of these plants is 13.2 cm correct to one decimal place.

(c) Describe the skew of these data. Give a reason for your answer. (1)

Two of these plants are chosen at random.

(d) Estimate the probability that both of their heights are between 8 cm and 14 cm. (3)

[8 marks]

STEP 1/5Part (a)

Strategy. Active recall: choose the method for Part (a) before unmasking. Reproduce exam-safe working, not just the final answer.

(a) Use frequency density (fd) = frequency / class width to read the histogram. For the three given classes:

Class width freq fd
$5 \le h < 9$ 4 32 8
$9 \le h < 13$ 4 152 38
$13 \le h < 15$ 2 120 60

The histogram shows fd = 32 for $15 \le h < 17$ and fd = 5 for $17 \le h < 25$:

  • $15 \le h < 17$: freq $= 32 \times 2 = 64$.
  • $17 \le h < 25$: freq $= 5 \times 8 = 40$.

Check total: $32 + 152 + 120 + 64 + 40 = 408$ ✓

STEP 2/5Part (b)

Strategy. Active recall: choose the method for Part (b) before unmasking. Reproduce exam-safe working, not just the final answer.

(b) Median position $= n/2 = 408/2 = 204$.

Cumulative frequencies: $32,\ 184,\ 304,\ 368,\ 408$. The 204th value lies in $13 \le h < 15$ (since $184 < 204 \le 304$).

$$\text{Median} = 13 + \frac{204 - 184}{120} \times 2 = 13 + \frac{20}{120} \times 2 = 13 + \frac{1}{3} = 13.33\ldots \approx 13.3\ \text{cm}$$

STEP 3/5Part (c)

Strategy. Active recall: choose the method for Part (c) before unmasking. Reproduce exam-safe working, not just the final answer.

(c) Mean = 13.2, Median $\approx$ 13.3. Since mean $<$ median, the data are negatively skewed.

STEP 4/5Part (d)

Strategy. Active recall: choose the method for Part (d) before unmasking. Reproduce exam-safe working, not just the final answer.

(d) Estimate the count of plants in $[8, 14)$ assuming uniform distribution within each class.

  • From class $[5,9)$: $\dfrac{9-8}{4} \times 32 = \dfrac{1}{4} \times 32 = 8$
  • All of $[9,13)$: $152$
  • From class $[13,15)$: $\dfrac{14-13}{2} \times 120 = \dfrac{1}{2} \times 120 = 60$

Total in $[8,14)$: $8 + 152 + 60 = 220$.

Selecting two plants without replacement:

$$P(\text{both in }[8,14)) = \frac{220}{408} \times \frac{219}{407} = \frac{48180}{166056} = 0.2901\ldots \approx 0.290$$

Formula anchor.

Key formula / concept:
- Frequency density $= \dfrac{\text{frequency}}{\text{class width}}$; area of bar = frequency.
- Median by interpolation: $L + \dfrac{(n/2) - F_{<}}{f_{\text{class}}} \times w$.
- Skew test: mean $<$ median $\Rightarrow$ negative skew; mean $>$ median $\Rightarrow$ positive skew.
- Uniform-within-class assumption when boundaries split a class.
