Q4 — 8 bears (length $d$, girth $g$): $S_{dg},\ S_{gg},\ r,\ g=a+bd$, interpret & predict

Question

For 8 bears, length $d$ cm and girth $g$ cm satisfy the totals shown.

(a) Find $S_{dg}$ and $S_{gg}$. [3]

(b) Find PMCC $r$ between $d$ and $g$. [2]

(c) Show that the regression line of $g$ on $d$ is $g = -42.3 + 0.722d$. [3]

(d) Interpret the gradient. [1]

(e) (i) Estimate $g$ when $d = 2.5$ m. (ii) Why is $d = 0.5$ m unreliable? [2]

(f) A change in girth of 17.3 cm corresponds to a change in length of $x$ cm. Find $x$. [2]

n = 8 bears.   d = length (cm),  g = girth (cm).
Σd  = 1456.8        Σg  = 713.2
Σdg = 141 978.84    Σg² = 72 675.98
S_dd = 16 769.78    (given)

[13 marks]

Part (a) — $S_{dg}$ and $S_{gg}$ [3 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2$S_{dg}$ M1 A1

Formula (booklet): $S_{xy} = \Sigma xy - \dfrac{(\Sigma x)(\Sigma y)}{n}$.

$$S_{dg} = 141\,978.84 - \frac{1456.8 \times 713.2}{8} = 141\,978.84 - 129\,873.72$$

$\boxed{\,S_{dg} = 12\,105.12\,}$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $S_{dg} = 12{,}105.12$.
STEP 2/2$S_{gg}$ A1

$$S_{gg} = \Sigma g^2 - \frac{(\Sigma g)^2}{n} = 72\,675.98 - \frac{(713.2)^2}{8} = 72\,675.98 - 63\,581.78$$

$\boxed{\,S_{gg} = 9094.20\,}$

Part (b) — PMCC $r$ [2 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1$r$ formula M1 A1

Formula (booklet): $r = \dfrac{S_{xy}}{\sqrt{S_{xx}\,S_{yy}}}$.

$$r = \frac{12\,105.12}{\sqrt{16\,769.78 \times 9094.20}} = \frac{12\,105.12}{\sqrt{1.5249 \times 10^8}} = \frac{12\,105.12}{12\,349.4\ldots}$$

Coding invariance: $r$ is unchanged by any linear coding of $x$ or $y$. The regression coefficients $a$ and $b$ are NOT.
$\boxed{\,r = 0.980\,(3\,\text{s.f.})\,}$

Part (c) — Show $g = -42.3 + 0.722d$ [3 marks] · 2 steps

$\bar d = 1456.8/8 = 182.1,\ \bar g = 713.2/8 = 89.15$.
STEP 1/2gradient $b$ M1

Formula (booklet): $b = \dfrac{S_{xy}}{S_{xx}}$.

$$b = \frac{S_{dg}}{S_{dd}} = \frac{12\,105.12}{16\,769.78} = 0.72184\ldots \approx 0.722$$

Direction matters: regression of $g$ on $d$ uses $S_{dd}$ in the denominator (variance of the predictor). Reversing $d \leftrightarrow g$ gives the wrong line.
$b \approx 0.722$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $b \approx 0.722$.
STEP 2/2intercept $a$ M1 A1

$a = \bar g - b\,\bar d$:

$$a = 89.15 - 0.72184(182.1) = 89.15 - 131.45 = -42.30 \approx -42.3$$

$\boxed{\,g = -42.3 + 0.722d\,}$ ✓

Part (d) — Interpret the gradient [1 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1units + context B1

For each extra 1 cm of bear length, the model predicts the girth increases by about 0.722 cm.

Mark scheme demands BOTH units AND context. "Increases by 0.722 per unit" = B0. Must say "1 cm of length", "girth in cm", "of a bear", and "increases" (positive direction).
Each extra 1 cm of length ⇒ +0.722 cm of girth (within the sampled range).

Part (e)(i) — Estimate $g$ when $d = 2.5$ m [1 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1convert m → cm B1

Convert! $2.5\ \text{m} = 250\ \text{cm}$.

$$g = -42.3 + 0.722(250) = -42.3 + 180.5 = 138.2\ \text{cm}$$

Sanity check: $\bar d = 182$ cm, so 250 cm is on the upper edge of the sample but still plausible. (Compare with (e)(ii) where 50 cm is way below.)
$g \approx 138$ cm (or 138.2 cm)

Part (e)(ii) — Why is $d = 0.5$ m unreliable? [1 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1extrapolation B1

$d = 0.5\ \text{m} = 50\ \text{cm}$ is far outside the sampled range (sample mean $\bar d \approx 182$ cm; bears in the data are clearly large).

The model gives $g = -42.3 + 0.722(50) = -6.2$ cm — a negative girth, physically impossible. Extrapolation outside the data range is unreliable.

50 cm is outside the data range (extrapolation) ⇒ model unreliable.

Part (f) — Find $x$ if girth changes by 17.3 cm [2 marks] · 1 step

Gradient = 0.722 cm girth per 1 cm length. We want $0.722 \cdot x = 17.3$.
STEP 1/1invert gradient M1 A1

$$0.722\,x = 17.3 \;\Longrightarrow\; x = \frac{17.3}{0.722} = 23.96\ldots$$

(Using unrounded $b = 0.72184$: $x = 17.3/0.72184 = 23.97$.)

$\boxed{\,x \approx 24.0\ \text{cm}\,}$ (or 23.96)
