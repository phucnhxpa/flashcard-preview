Part (B) — CDF as a table [2 marks] · 2 steps

The command word is "specify" — the answer must be in a particular form. For a discrete RV, the CDF $F(x) = \mathrm{P}(X \le x)$ must be presented as a two-row table: $x$ on top, $F(x)$ underneath.
STEP 1/2cumulative sum

Strategy. $F(x) = \mathrm{P}(X \le x) = \sum_{x_i \le x} p_i$. Add the probabilities cumulatively from left to right.

Cumulative probabilities

$$F(1) = 0.2, \quad F(2) = 0.2+0.3 = 0.5,$$ $$F(3) = 0.5+0.1 = 0.6, \quad F(4) = 0.6+0.2 = 0.8,$$ $$F(5) = 0.8+0.2 = 1.0.$$

Sense check. The last entry must be $F(5) = 1.0$ (the total probability). If it is not 1, an earlier entry is wrong.

↳ from STEP 1: $F(1)=0.2,\; F(2)=0.5,\; F(3)=0.6,\; F(4)=0.8,\; F(5)=1.0$.

Part (D) — Var(X) and Var(5 − 3X) [5 marks] · 4 steps

Given $\mathrm{E}(X) = 1.8$ and the distribution table. Need $\mathrm{Var}(5 - 3X)$. Use the variance formula $\mathrm{Var}(X) = \mathrm{E}(X^2) - [\mathrm{E}(X)]^2$, then the linear-transform rule $\mathrm{Var}(aX+b) = a^2\,\mathrm{Var}(X)$.
STEP 1/4E(X²) — no 1/n

Strategy. Compute $\mathrm{E}(X^2) = \sum x_i^2 \, p_i$. There is no $1/n$ factor — the $p_i$ already encode the relative weight.

Substitute

$$\mathrm{E}(X^2) = 1^2(0.2) + 2^2(0.3) + 3^2(0.1) + 4^2(0.2) + 5^2(0.2)$$ $$= 0.2 + 1.2 + 0.9 + 3.2 + 5.0 = 4.6.$$
Phuc's mistake (forgot the variance formula). Phuc wrote $\mathrm{E}(X^2) = \frac{1}{n}\sum x_i^2 p_i$, adding a spurious $1/n$. Harry: "this is something I would put on a flashcard… and revise." The formula is $\mathrm{E}(X^2) = \sum x_i^2 p_i$ — no division.

Sense check. Each term $x_i^2 p_i$ is at most $25 \times 0.2 = 5$; the total $4.6$ is in the right ballpark and is less than $5^2 = 25$ (the max $x^2$), as expected.

↳ from STEP 1: $\mathrm{E}(X^2) = 10.5$ (no 1/n).
STEP 2/4Var(X) formula

Strategy. The variance formula (the one Phuc keeps forgetting): $\mathrm{Var}(X) = \mathrm{E}(X^2) - [\mathrm{E}(X)]^2$. No division by $n$ anywhere.

Apply

$$\mathrm{Var}(X) = 4.6 - (1.8)^2 = 4.6 - 3.24 = 1.36.$$

Sense check. $\mathrm{Var}(X) \ge 0$ always (1.36 > 0 ✓); the variance has units of $X^2$ (squared units).

↳ from STEP 2: $\mathrm{Var}(X) = 7.26$.
STEP 3/4linear transform rule

Strategy. For a linear transformation $aX + b$: $\mathrm{Var}(aX + b) = a^2 \cdot \mathrm{Var}(X)$. The shift $b$ does not enter; the sign of $a$ does not matter (squared). Here $a = -3$, $b = 5$.

Apply

$$\mathrm{Var}(5 - 3X) = (-3)^2 \cdot \mathrm{Var}(X) = 9 \times 1.36.$$
Phuc's mistake (kept the $b$ / lost the square). Common errors: writing $\mathrm{Var}(5-3X) = (-3)\mathrm{Var}(X)$ (forgot to square) or $= 9\mathrm{Var}(X) + 5$ (kept the shift). Correct: the 5 shift is invisible to variance; the $-3$ scale is squared.

Sense check. $\mathrm{Var}(5-3X) = 9 \times 1.36 > 1.36$ — scaling by $|-3| = 3$ inflates the spread by $3^2 = 9$, as expected.

↳ from STEP 3: $\mathrm{Var}(5-3X) = 9 \times 7.26$.
STEP 4/4final answer 3 s.f.

Strategy. Multiply out and round to at least 3 significant figures.

Compute

$$\mathrm{Var}(5 - 3X) = 9 \times 1.36 = 12.24 \approx 12.2 \;\text{(3 s.f.)}.$$

Sense check. The answer is positive (variance is always $\ge 0$), larger than $\mathrm{Var}(X)$ by a factor of 9, and given to 3 s.f. — all consistent.

Target: $\mathrm{Var}(5 - 3X) = 12.24$ (3 s.f. $\approx 12.2$).
