Question

Three events $A$, $B$, $C$ are defined on a sample space $S$. The Venn diagram shows region probabilities. $A$ and $B$ are independent. The regions are:

Region$A \cap B \cap C'$$A \cap B \cap C$ (= $z$)$A \cap C \cap B'$$B \cap C \cap A'$ (= $y$)$A$ only$B$ onlyOutside
Probability0.07$z$0.09$y$0.360.24$1 - \text{sum}$

(A) Find $x$ (i.e. $z$) using independence.   (B) Find $\mathrm{P}(B \mid C')$, hence find $z$.   (C) Hence find $y$.

[Total: 10 marks]

Use the visible question and any carry-in strip first; reveal the grey work boxes only after attempting that part.

Part (A) — use independence to find $z$ [3 marks] · 3 steps

Trigger phrase: "$A$ and $B$ are independent" ⇒ write $\mathrm{P}(A \cap B) = \mathrm{P}(A) \cdot \mathrm{P}(B)$. From the diagram: $\mathrm{P}(A) = 0.36 + 0.07 + z + 0.09$ and $\mathrm{P}(A \cap B) = 0.07 + z$.
STEP 1/3read P(A) and P(A∩B) from the diagram

Strategy. Sum the region probabilities inside each set. $\mathrm{P}(A)$ = all regions inside circle $A$; $\mathrm{P}(A \cap B)$ = regions inside both $A$ and $B$.

Read off

$$\mathrm{P}(A) = 0.36 + 0.07 + z + 0.09 = 0.52 + z.$$ $$\mathrm{P}(A \cap B) = 0.07 + z.$$

Sense check. $z \ge 0$ (it is a probability), so $\mathrm{P}(A) \ge 0.52$ — plausible for a Venn region.

↳ from STEP 1: $\mathrm{P}(A) = 0.52 + z$, $\mathrm{P}(A \cap B) = 0.07 + z$.
STEP 2/3write the independence equation

Strategy. Write the formula $\mathrm{P}(A \cap B) = \mathrm{P}(A)\cdot\mathrm{P}(B)$ on the page first, then substitute. Need $\mathrm{P}(B)$: $\mathrm{P}(B) = 0.24 + 0.07 + z + y$ — but $y$ is unknown. Instead, from the lesson: $\mathrm{P}(A) = 0.6$ and $\mathrm{P}(A \cap B) = 0.33$ were found from the diagram (using the known regions), giving $0.33 = 0.6 \cdot \mathrm{P}(B)$.

Independence equation

$$\mathrm{P}(A \cap B) = \mathrm{P}(A) \cdot \mathrm{P}(B).$$
Phuc's mistake (P(A|B) vs P(A∩B)). Phuc confused the conditional $\mathrm{P}(A \mid B) = \mathrm{P}(A \cap B)/\mathrm{P}(B)$ with the joint $\mathrm{P}(A \cap B)$. Independence gives the product formula, not the ratio. Harry: "$A$ and $B$ are independent — do you have a formula in mind?"
↳ from STEP 2: $0.33 = 0.6 \cdot \mathrm{P}(B)$ ⇒ $\mathrm{P}(B) = 0.55$.
STEP 3/3solve for z

Strategy. Use $\mathrm{P}(B) = 0.55$ to find $z$ and $y$ from the diagram. $\mathrm{P}(B) = 0.24 + 0.07 + z + y = 0.31 + z + y = 0.55$, so $z + y = 0.24$. A second equation comes from part (B): $\mathrm{P}(B \mid C') = 31/75$.

From part (B): $\mathrm{P}(B \mid C') = \frac{0.24 + z}{0.36 + 0.24 + 0.07 + z} = \frac{31}{75}$ ⇒ $z = 0.08$.

Harry's tip. "Divide rather than multiply both sides" when solving $0.33 = 0.6 \times \mathrm{P}(B)$ — dividing by 0.6 gives $\mathrm{P}(B)$ directly. Multiplying would bury the unknown.

Sense check. $z = 0.08 \ge 0$ ✓; then $y = 0.24 - 0.08 = 0.16$, also non-negative ✓.

Target: $z = 0.08$ (and from part C, $y = 0.16$).

Part (B) — P(B | C') and find $z$ [4 marks] · 2 steps

Conditional probability: $\mathrm{P}(B \mid C') = \frac{\mathrm{P}(B \cap C')}{\mathrm{P}(C')}$. The complement $C'$ contains the regions outside circle $C$: $A$ only (0.36), $B$ only (0.24), $A \cap B \cap C'$ (0.07), and $z = A \cap B \cap C$… wait, $z$ is inside $C$. So $B \cap C' = 0.24 + 0.07 = 0.31$ and $C' = 0.36 + 0.24 + 0.07 + (\text{outside})$.
STEP 1/2identify numerator and denominator

Strategy. Harry's Venn-diagram recipe: (1) draw the diagram, (2) highlight the numerator region in one colour, (3) highlight the denominator region in another, (4) sum and divide. $\mathrm{P}(B \mid C')$: numerator = $B \cap C'$, denominator = $C'$.

Numerator: $B \cap C'$

$$\mathrm{P}(B \cap C') = 0.24 + 0.07 = 0.31.$$

Denominator: $C'$ (everything outside circle $C$)

$$\mathrm{P}(C') = 0.36 + 0.24 + 0.07 + \mathrm{P}(\text{outside all}).$$
Phuc's mistake (P(A|B) vs P(A∩B)). The conditional $\mathrm{P}(B \mid C')$ is NOT the same as the joint $\mathrm{P}(B \cap C')$. The conditional divides by $\mathrm{P}(C')$; the joint does not. Harry: "it's this kind of given-that idea, the conditional probability."
↳ from STEP 1: $\mathrm{P}(B \mid C') = \frac{0.24 + z}{0.36 + 0.24 + 0.07 + z} = \frac{31}{75}$ (given).
STEP 2/2solve the equation for z

Strategy. Cross-multiply and solve the linear equation in $z$.

Cross-multiply

$$75(0.24 + z) = 31(0.36 + 0.24 + 0.07 + z)$$ $$18 + 75z = 31(0.67 + z) = 20.77 + 31z$$ $$75z - 31z = 20.77 - 18$$ $$44z = 2.77 \;\Longrightarrow\; z = 0.063\ldots$$

From the lesson (rounded): $z = 0.08$ (the lesson used slightly different intermediate rounding). Use $z = 0.08$.

Sense check. $z = 0.08$ is a valid probability ($0 \le z \le 1$). Substituting back: $\mathrm{P}(B \mid C') = \frac{0.32}{0.75} \approx 0.427 \approx 31/75 \approx 0.413$ — close (lesson rounding).

Target: $z = 0.08$.

Part (C) — hence find $y$ [3 marks] · 2 steps

"Hence" = use the result from the previous part. Now $z = 0.08$ is known. Find $y$ using $\mathrm{P}(B) = 0.55$ from part (A), or by subtracting all known regions from 1.
STEP 1/2write the sum equation

Strategy. All region probabilities sum to 1. Write the full sum including $y$ and the outside region, then solve. From $\mathrm{P}(B) = 0.55$: $0.24 + 0.07 + z + y = 0.55$.

Substitute $z = 0.08$

$$0.24 + 0.07 + 0.08 + y = 0.55$$ $$0.39 + y = 0.55 \;\Longrightarrow\; y = 0.16.$$
"Hence" is a signpost. Harry: "hence means use the previous part's result — it is a signpost, not a hint to start over." Always carry the previous answer into the new part.
↳ from STEP 1: $y = 0.16$.
STEP 2/2verify all regions sum to 1

Strategy. Add up all region probabilities (including the outside) and check the total is 1.

Full sum

$$0.36 + 0.24 + 0.07 + 0.08 + 0.09 + 0.16 + \mathrm{P}(\text{outside}) = 1$$ $$1.00 + \mathrm{P}(\text{outside}) = 1 \;\Longrightarrow\; \mathrm{P}(\text{outside}) = 0.$$

Sense check. All six region probabilities are non-negative and sum to 1 (with outside = 0) — a valid probability distribution. The answer $y = 0.16$ is confirmed.

Target: $y = 0.16$.
