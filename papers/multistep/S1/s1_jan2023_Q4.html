(ii) Mutual exclusivity proof — Phuc's redo

If $C$ and $D$ are mutually exclusive then $P(C\cup D) = P(C) + P(D)$, and since any probability is at most 1 we would need $P(C) + P(D) \le 1$.

$$P(C) + P(D) = \frac{x}{x+5} + \frac{5}{x}$$

Common denominator $x(x+5)$:
$$= \frac{x\cdot x + 5(x+5)}{x(x+5)} = \frac{x^2 + 5x + 25}{x^2 + 5x}$$

The numerator is the denominator plus 25:
$$= \frac{(x^2 + 5x) + 25}{x^2 + 5x} = 1 + \frac{25}{x^2 + 5x}$$

For $x > 0$: $x^2 + 5x > 0$, so $\dfrac{25}{x^2+5x} > 0$, hence
$$P(C) + P(D) = 1 + \frac{25}{x^2+5x} > 1$$

But mutual exclusivity requires $P(C) + P(D) = P(C\cup D) \le 1$ — contradiction. Hence $C$ and $D$ cannot be mutually exclusive. $\blacksquare$
