Q5 — Normal Distribution

Question

A competition consists of two rounds. The time, in minutes, taken by adults to complete round one is modelled by a normal distribution with mean 15 minutes and standard deviation 2 minutes.
(a) Use standardisation to find the proportion of adults that take less than 18 minutes to complete round one. (2)
Only the fastest 60% of adults from round one take part in round two.
(b) Use standardisation to find the longest time that an adult can take to complete round one if they are to take part in round two. (3)
The time, $T$ minutes, taken by adults to complete round two is modelled by a normal distribution with mean $\mu$. Given that $P(\mu - 10 < T < \mu+10) = 0.95$,
(c) find $P(T > \mu - 5\ |\ T > \mu - 10)$. (5)

[10 marks]

Part (a)[2 marks] · 3 steps

STEP 1/3

Worked solution

STEP 2/3

$X \sim N(15, 2^2)$. Standardise:
$$P(X<18) = P\!\left(Z < \frac{18-15}{2}\right) = P(Z < 1.5) = \boxed{0.9332}.$$

Target: $\boxed{0.9332}$
STEP 3/3

Exam checklist.

  • [ ] Standardise using $Z=(X-\mu)/\sigma$ — show this line.
  • [ ] Quote $P(Z<1.5)$ from tables to 4 d.p.

Common pitfalls.

Dividing by $\sigma^2$; using the wrong tail.

Worked solution — Cutoff time for round 2

STEP 2/4

"Fastest 60%" = the 60% with shortest times. Their times are below some cutoff $t$, so:
$$P(X < t) = 0.60.$$

Part (c)[5 marks] · 5 steps

Carry-in: Part (a) result: $\boxed{0.9332}$
Part (b) result: $\boxed{15.5\text{ minutes (3 s.f.)}}$
STEP 1/5

Worked solution — Conditional probability

$T \sim N(\mu, \sigma^2)$ with $P(\mu - 10 < T < \mu + 10) = 0.95$.

STEP 2/5

Step 1 — find $\sigma$. By symmetry, $P(T < \mu-10) = (1 - 0.95)/2 = 0.025$. Standardising:
$$\frac{\mu - 10 - \mu}{\sigma} = -1.96 \;\Longrightarrow\; \sigma = \frac{10}{1.96} = 5.102\ldots$$

Step 2 — conditional. Since $\{T>\mu-5\} \subset \{T>\mu-10\}$:
$$P(T > \mu-5 \mid T > \mu-10) = \frac{P(T > \mu-5)}{P(T > \mu-10)}$$

STEP 3/5

Numerator: standardise $T > \mu - 5$:
$$P\!\left(Z > \frac{-5}{5.102}\right) = P(Z > -0.98) = P(Z < 0.98) = 0.8365$$

Denominator: $P(T > \mu - 10) = 1 - 0.025 = 0.975$.

STEP 4/5

$$P(T > \mu - 5 \mid T > \mu - 10) = \frac{0.8365}{0.975} = \boxed{0.858\ (3\text{ s.f.})}$$

Target: $\boxed{0.858\ (3\text{ s.f.})}$
STEP 5/5

Common trap (this part).

Key formulas (IN booklet): Conditional probability $P(A|B) = P(A \cap B)/P(B)$. Standard normal $\Phi^{-1}(0.975) = 1.96$ (from tables).

Exam checklist.

  • [ ] Verify $\sigma$ by re-checking $10/1.96 \approx 5.10$ (not 0.51).

  • [ ] Numerator: $P(Z > -0.98)$, look up $\Phi(0.98) = 0.8365$.
  • [ ] Denominator: use 0.975, not 0.95.
  • [ ] Final answer 3 s.f.
