Q3 — 50 pebbles: histogram bar dimensions, median, coded mean/SD, effect of new pebble

Question

50 pebbles, length $x$ mm — frequency table below. Weight $w$ g is coded by $10y = w - 20$.

(a) Width and height of the bar for $30 \leq x < 32$. [3]

(b) Estimate the median length using linear interpolation. [2]

(c) Show that $\bar w = 40.8$. [2]

(d) Find $\sigma_w$ (standard deviation of weight). [4]

(e) A pebble of weight 40.8 g is added. State the effect on (i) the mean (ii) the standard deviation, with reason. [3]

Length x (mm)   20–<30  30–<32  32–<36  36–<40  40–<45  45–<50
Frequency f       2       16      20       8        3        1

Reference bar (32 ≤ x < 36) drawn 2.5 cm wide × 7.5 cm tall.
Weight (w g) of pebbles coded by  10y = w − 20.
Σy = 104,   Σy² = 233.54  (over n = 50).

[14 marks]

Part (a) — Width & height of the $30 \leq x < 32$ bar [3 marks] · 3 steps

Reference: bar for $32 \leq x < 36$ (width 4 mm, freq 20) drawn 2.5 cm × 7.5 cm.
STEP 1/3width M1

Rule: page-width $\propto$ class-width.

Reference: 4 mm class ↔ 2.5 cm on page ⇒ $\tfrac{2.5}{4} = 0.625$ cm/mm.

Target class width = 2 mm:

$$\text{width} = 2 \times \tfrac{2.5}{4} = 1.25\ \text{cm}$$

width $= 1.25$ cm
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: width $= 1.25$ cm.
STEP 2/3area = freq M1

Rule: area-on-page $\propto$ frequency.

Reference area $= 2.5 \times 7.5 = 18.75$ cm² ↔ 20 pebbles.

Target frequency = 16:

$$\text{target area} = \tfrac{16}{20} \times 18.75 = 15\ \text{cm}^2$$

↳ from STEP 2:From STEP 2: target area $= 15$ cm$^2$.
STEP 3/3height A1

$$\text{height} = \frac{\text{area}}{\text{width}} = \frac{15}{1.25} = 12\ \text{cm}$$

Don't confuse "frequency density" (cm/mm units) with "cm on the printed page". The IAL question gives you cm-on-page directly via the reference bar — use area-proportional reasoning, not frequency density formulas.
$\boxed{\,\text{width} = 1.25\ \text{cm},\ \text{height} = 12\ \text{cm}\,}$

Part (b) — Estimate the median length [2 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2median class M1

Grouped median uses $n/2$ (NOT $(n+1)/2$).

$n/2 = 25$. Cumulative frequencies: 2, 18, 38, 46, 49, 50.

25 falls in the 32 ≤ $x$ < 36 class ($F = 18$ before, $f = 20$ in class, $c = 4$).

↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: Median falls in class $32 \leq x < 36$ ($F=18$, $f=20$, $c=4$).
STEP 2/2linear interpolation A1

Formula (booklet): $L + \dfrac{n/2 - F}{f}\cdot c$.

$$\text{median} = 32 + \frac{25 - 18}{20}\cdot 4 = 32 + \frac{7}{20}\cdot 4 = 32 + 1.4$$

$\boxed{\,\text{median} = 33.4\ \text{mm}\,}$

Part (c) — Show $\bar w = 40.8$ [2 marks] · 1 step

$10y = w - 20 \;\Longleftrightarrow\; w = 10y + 20$. Σ$y$ = 104.
STEP 1/1$\bar y$ then decode M1 A1

$$\bar y = \frac{\Sigma y}{n} = \frac{104}{50} = 2.08$$

Decode: $\bar w = 10\bar y + 20 = 10(2.08) + 20 = 20.8 + 20 = 40.8$ ✓

$\boxed{\,\bar w = 40.8\ \text{g}\,}$

Part (d) — Find $\sigma_w$ [4 marks] · 3 steps

$\Sigma y = 104,\ \Sigma y^2 = 233.54,\ n = 50,\ \bar y = 2.08,\ w = 10y+20$.
STEP 1/3Var($y$) M1

Computational formula: $\text{Var}(y) = \dfrac{\Sigma y^2}{n} - \bar y^{\,2}$.

$$\text{Var}(y) = \frac{233.54}{50} - (2.08)^2 = 4.6708 - 4.3264 = 0.3444$$

↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $\text{Var}(y) = 0.3444$.
STEP 2/3$\sigma_y$ M1

$$\sigma_y = \sqrt{0.3444} = 0.5869\ldots$$

↳ from STEP 2:From STEP 2: $\sigma_y = 0.5869$.
STEP 3/3decode SD (×10, NOT +20) M1 A1

Coding rule: for $w = 10y + 20$, $\sigma_w = |10|\cdot \sigma_y$. The $+20$ shift does not affect spread.

$$\sigma_w = 10 \times 0.5869 = 5.869\ldots \approx 5.87\ \text{g}\ (3\,\text{s.f.})$$

Trap: writing $\sigma_w = 10\sigma_y + 20$ — standard deviation is NEVER shifted by an additive constant. Only the multiplier matters.
$\boxed{\,\sigma_w \approx 5.87\ \text{g}\,}$

Part (e) — Effect of adding a pebble of weight 40.8 g on (i) mean (ii) SD [3 marks] · 2 steps

The new pebble has weight equal to the current mean ($\bar w = 40.8$ g). New $n = 51$.
STEP 1/2(i) mean B1

New mean $= \dfrac{\Sigma w + 40.8}{n+1} = \dfrac{50(40.8) + 40.8}{51} = \dfrac{51 \cdot 40.8}{51} = 40.8$.

⇒ Mean unchanged. Adding a value equal to the current mean preserves the mean.

(i) Mean is unchanged (still 40.8 g).
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: Mean is unchanged (adding value equal to mean).
STEP 2/2(ii) SD B1 B1

$\text{Var} = \dfrac{\Sigma(w_i - \bar w)^2}{n}$.

The new term contributes $(40.8 - 40.8)^2 = 0$ to the numerator, so $\Sigma(w_i - \bar w)^2$ unchanged, but $n$ grows from 50 to 51.

$$\text{Var}_\text{new} = \frac{\text{old numerator}}{51} \;<\; \frac{\text{old numerator}}{50} = \text{Var}_\text{old}$$

⇒ SD decreases.

Watch the textbook trap: "SD stays the same" feels right because the new value is "the average" — but the denominator $n$ increases, shrinking the variance and hence the SD.
(ii) SD decreases (deviation 0 added but $n$ grew).
