Q7 - Discrete RV from CDF, Variance Scaling

Question

The cumulative distribution function of a discrete random variable $X$ is given by

$x$ 1 2 3 4
$F(x)$ $\dfrac{1}{13}$ $\dfrac{2k-1}{26}$ $\dfrac{3k+1}{26}$ $\dfrac{k+4}{8}$

where $k$ is a positive constant.

(a) Show that $k = 4$. (1)
(b) Find the probability distribution of the discrete random variable $X$. (3)
(c) Using your answer to part (b), write down the mode of $X$. (1)
(d) Calculate $\operatorname{Var}(13X - 6)$. (5)

[10 marks]

STEP 1/5Part (a)

Strategy. Active recall: choose the method for Part (a) before unmasking. Reproduce exam-safe working, not just the final answer.

(a) $F(4) = 1$ (largest value of the CDF):

$$\frac{k+4}{8} = 1 \Rightarrow k + 4 = 8 \Rightarrow \boxed{k = 4} \quad ✓$$

Mistake guard from the attempt/revision notes.

What went wrong:. Sequential-step question --- easy to make an arithmetic slip when computing $F(x) - F(x-1)$ across fractions with different denominators. The big trap in (d): forgetting that $\operatorname{Var}(aX + b) = a^2 \operatorname{Var}(X)$ (the additive constant disappears; the multiplier is squared).

Target: $\boxed{k = 4}$
STEP 2/5Part (b)

Strategy. Active recall: choose the method for Part (b) before unmasking. Reproduce exam-safe working, not just the final answer.

(b) Substitute $k = 4$:

$$F(1) = \frac{1}{13} = \frac{2}{26},\quad F(2) = \frac{7}{26},\quad F(3) = \frac{13}{26},\quad F(4) = 1 = \frac{26}{26}$$

Probabilities by $P(X=x) = F(x) - F(x-1)$:

$$P(X=1) = \frac{2}{26}$$
$$P(X=2) = \frac{7}{26} - \frac{2}{26} = \frac{5}{26}$$
$$P(X=3) = \frac{13}{26} - \frac{7}{26} = \frac{6}{26}$$
$$P(X=4) = \frac{26}{26} - \frac{13}{26} = \frac{13}{26}$$

Check: $\frac{2+5+6+13}{26} = \frac{26}{26} = 1$ ✓

$x$ 1 2 3 4
$P(X=x)$ $\tfrac{2}{26}$ $\tfrac{5}{26}$ $\tfrac{6}{26}$ $\tfrac{13}{26}$
STEP 3/5Part (c)

Strategy. Active recall: choose the method for Part (c) before unmasking. Reproduce exam-safe working, not just the final answer.

(c) Largest probability is $\tfrac{13}{26}$ at $x = 4$, so $\boxed{\text{mode} = 4}$.

Target: $\boxed{\text{mode} = 4}$
STEP 4/5Part (d)

Strategy. Active recall: choose the method for Part (d) before unmasking. Reproduce exam-safe working, not just the final answer.

(d) Compute $E(X)$ and $E(X^2)$:

$$E(X) = \frac{1 \cdot 2 + 2 \cdot 5 + 3 \cdot 6 + 4 \cdot 13}{26} = \frac{2 + 10 + 18 + 52}{26} = \frac{82}{26} = \frac{41}{13}$$

$$E(X^2) = \frac{1 \cdot 2 + 4 \cdot 5 + 9 \cdot 6 + 16 \cdot 13}{26} = \frac{2 + 20 + 54 + 208}{26} = \frac{284}{26} = \frac{142}{13}$$

$$\operatorname{Var}(X) = E(X^2) - (E(X))^2 = \frac{142}{13} - \left(\frac{41}{13}\right)^2 = \frac{142 \times 13}{169} - \frac{1681}{169} = \frac{1846 - 1681}{169} = \frac{165}{169}$$

Apply the variance transformation:

$$\operatorname{Var}(13X - 6) = 13^2 \operatorname{Var}(X) = 169 \times \frac{165}{169} = \boxed{165}$$

Target: $\boxed{165}$

Formula anchor.

Key formula / concept:
- For a discrete CDF: $F(x_{\max}) = 1$ and $P(X=x) = F(x) - F(x-1)$.
- $\operatorname{Var}(X) = E(X^2) - (E(X))^2$.
- $\operatorname{Var}(aX + b) = a^2 \operatorname{Var}(X)$ --- constant vanishes, multiplier squared.
