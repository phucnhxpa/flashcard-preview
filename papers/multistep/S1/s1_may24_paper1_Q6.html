Q6 — Teenagers play Chess ($C$), Scrabble ($S$), Go ($G$): find $p,\,q$ and youth-club estimate

Question

Probabilities for a randomly chosen teenager:

  • $C$ only $= 0.04$   ·   $S$ only $= 0.23$   ·   $G$ only $= 0$
  • $C \cap S$ only (not $G$) $= 0.15$   ·   $C \cap S \cap G = 0.12$   ·   $S \cap G$ only $= 0.10$
  • $C \cap G$ only $= p$   ·   outside (none) $= q$

(a) $\Pr(C \cap G')$. [1]

(b) Given $C, S$ are independent, find $p$. [4]

(c) Hence find $q$. [2]

(d) (i) $\Pr((C \cup S) \cap G')$ (ii) $\Pr(C \mid S \cap G)$. [3]

(e) A youth club has 76 teenagers who play both Chess and Go. Estimate the number who do not play Scrabble. [3]

[13 marks]

Part (a) — $\Pr(C \cap G')$ [1 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1C but not G B1

$C \cap G'$ = the parts of $C$ outside $G$ = ($C$ only) + ($C \cap S$ only):

$$\Pr(C \cap G') = 0.04 + 0.15 = 0.19$$

$\boxed{\,\Pr(C \cap G') = 0.19\,}$

Part (b) — Find $p$ given $C, S$ independent [4 marks] · 3 steps

STEP 1/3sum = 1 M1

All 8 regions sum to 1:

$$0.04 + p + 0 + 0.15 + 0.12 + 0.10 + 0.23 + q = 1$$

$$\Longrightarrow\; p + q = 0.36 \quad (\star)$$

↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $p + q = 0.36$.
STEP 2/3$\Pr(C),\,\Pr(S),\,\Pr(C\cap S)$ M1

$\Pr(C)$ = sum of all 4 $C$-regions:

$\Pr(C) = 0.04 + p + 0.15 + 0.12 = 0.31 + p$

$\Pr(S) = 0.15 + 0.12 + 0.10 + 0.23 = 0.60$

$\Pr(C \cap S) = 0.15 + 0.12 = 0.27$ (both $C \cap S$-only and triple)

↳ from STEP 2:From STEP 2: $\Pr(C) = 0.31 + p$, $\Pr(S) = 0.60$, $\Pr(C \cap S) = 0.27$.
STEP 3/3independence ⇒ solve M1 A1

Independence: $\Pr(C \cap S) = \Pr(C)\cdot \Pr(S)$:

$$0.27 = (0.31 + p)\cdot 0.60 \;\Longrightarrow\; 0.31 + p = \frac{0.27}{0.60} = 0.45$$

$$p = 0.45 - 0.31 = 0.14$$

$\boxed{\,p = 0.14\,}$

Part (c) — Hence find $q$ [2 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1use $(\star)$ M1 A1

From $(\star)$: $p + q = 0.36$, so $q = 0.36 - 0.14 = 0.22$.

$\boxed{\,q = 0.22\,}$

Part (d)(i) — $\Pr((C \cup S) \cap G')$ [1 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/13 regions B1

$(C \cup S) \cap G'$ = parts of $C$ or $S$ that are outside $G$:

= ($C$ only) + ($C \cap S$ only) + ($S$ only)

$$= 0.04 + 0.15 + 0.23 = 0.42$$

Trap: don't confuse $(C \cup S) \cap G'$ with $(C \cup S)'$ or $C' \cap S' \cap G'$. Always shade the Venn first.
$\boxed{\,\Pr((C \cup S) \cap G') = 0.42\,}$

Part (d)(ii) — $\Pr(C \mid S \cap G)$ [2 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1conditional M1 A1

Formula: $\Pr(A \mid B) = \dfrac{\Pr(A \cap B)}{\Pr(B)}$.

Numerator: $\Pr(C \cap S \cap G) = 0.12$.

Denominator: $\Pr(S \cap G) = (S\cap G\text{ only}) + (C\cap S\cap G) = 0.10 + 0.12 = 0.22$.

$$\Pr(C \mid S \cap G) = \frac{0.12}{0.22} = \frac{6}{11} \approx 0.545$$

$\boxed{\,\Pr(C\mid S\cap G) = \tfrac{6}{11} \approx 0.545\,}$

Part (e) — Youth club: 76 play $C$ AND $G$. Estimate # who do NOT play $S$ [3 marks] · 3 steps

76 = number who play both $C$ and $G$ (i.e. event $C \cap G$, including the triple-overlap and $C \cap G$ only).
STEP 1/3$\Pr(C \cap G)$ M1

$\Pr(C \cap G)$ = ($C \cap G$ only) + ($C \cap S \cap G$) = $p + 0.12 = 0.14 + 0.12 = 0.26$.

$\Pr(C \cap G) = 0.26$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $\Pr(C \cap G) = 0.26$.
STEP 2/3population $N$ M1

Proportion-scaling rule (IAL favourite): $N = \dfrac{\text{observed count}}{\text{probability}}$.

$$N = \frac{76}{0.26} = 292.31$$

(Estimated total number of teenagers in the youth club.)

$N \approx 292.31$
↳ from STEP 2:From STEP 2: $N \approx 292.31$ (total teenagers).
STEP 3/3multiply by $\Pr(S')$ — use complement A1

Easiest path: complement. $\Pr(S') = 1 - \Pr(S) = 1 - 0.60 = 0.40$.

$$\#\,\text{not-}S = 0.40 \times 292.31 = 116.92 \approx 117$$
