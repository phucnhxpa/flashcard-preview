Q7 — Year 1: 300t; Year 12: 4000t. (a) Linear yr 4; (b) exp yr 2; (c) total difference

Question

A farm produces $300$ tonnes of wheat in year 1 and $4000$ tonnes in year 12.

Model A: production increases by the same amount each year.

Model B: production increases by the same percentage each year.

(a) Using Model A, find production in year 4. [3]

(b) Using Model B, find production in year 2. [3]

(c) Find the difference between the total amounts produced from year 1 to year 12 inclusive under the two models. [3]

[9 marks]

Part (a) — Model A (linear, arithmetic) [3 marks] · 1 step

"Same amount" ⇒ arithmetic: $u_n = a + (n-1)d$.
STEP 1/1 M1 M1 A1

Strategy: "same amount each year" ⇒ arithmetic sequence with first term $a = u_1 = 300$ and common difference $d$. Use the year-12 data to find $d$, then apply $u_n = a + (n-1)d$ at $n = 4$.

Find $d$: from year 1 to year 12 there are $12 - 1 = 11$ INCREMENTS:

$$u_{12} = a + 11d \;\Longrightarrow\; 300 + 11d = 4000$$

$$11d = 3700 \;\Longrightarrow\; d = \dfrac{3700}{11} \approx 336.36$$

Formula (arithmetic $n$th term — NOT in booklet):

$$u_n = a + (n-1)d$$

Apply at $n = 4$:

$$u_4 = 300 + 3d = 300 + 3 \cdot \dfrac{3700}{11} = 300 + \dfrac{11100}{11}$$

$$= 300 + 1009.09\ldots \approx 1309.09\ldots$$

$$\boxed{\;u_4 \approx 1310\text{ tonnes}\;}$$

Off-by-one trap: 12 years from year 1 to year 12 = ELEVEN gaps, not 12. $u_{12} = a + 11d$. Common slip: writing $u_n = ar^n$ for arithmetic — that's geometric. $u_n = a + (n-1)d$ for arithmetic.
$\boxed{\approx 1310\text{ tonnes}}$

Part (b) — Model B (exponential, geometric) [3 marks] · 1 step

"Same percentage" ⇒ geometric: $u_n = ar^{n-1}$.
STEP 1/1 M1 M1 A1

Strategy: "same percentage each year" ⇒ geometric sequence with first term $a = u_1 = 300$ and common ratio $r$. Use year-12 to find $r$, then apply $u_n = ar^{n-1}$ at $n = 2$.

Find $r$: from year 1 to year 12 there are $11$ MULTIPLICATIONS by $r$:

$$u_{12} = ar^{11} \;\Longrightarrow\; 300 r^{11} = 4000$$

$$r^{11} = \dfrac{4000}{300} = \dfrac{40}{3}$$

Take the 11th root:

$$r = \!\left(\dfrac{40}{3}\right)^{\!1/11} \approx 1.260$$

Formula (geometric $n$th term — NOT in booklet):

$$u_n = ar^{n-1}$$

Apply at $n = 2$:

$$u_2 = 300 \cdot r^{1} = 300 \cdot 1.260 \approx 378$$

$$\boxed{\;u_2 \approx 380\text{ tonnes}\;}$$

Common slip: writing $u_n = ar^n$ gives the $(n+1)$-th term. The $n$-th term is $u_n = ar^{n-1}$. Sense check: $r \approx 1.260$ means a $\approx 26\%$ annual growth — aggressive but plausible.
$\boxed{\approx 380\text{ tonnes}}$

Part (c) — total difference [3 marks] · 2 steps

From (a)/(b): Model A is arithmetic with $a = 300$, $u_{12} = 4000$, $d = \tfrac{3700}{11}$. Model B is geometric with $a = 300$, $r \approx 1.260$, $r^{11} = \tfrac{40}{3}$. Sum each from $n=1$ to $n=12$ and subtract.
STEP 1/2 M1

Strategy: Model A is arithmetic. Sum from year 1 to year 12 using the arithmetic series formula. We know both endpoints ($u_1 = 300$, $u_{12} = 4000$) — easiest form is $S_n = \tfrac{n}{2}(a + \ell)$.

Formula (arithmetic sum — IN booklet):

$$S_n = \tfrac{n}{2}(a + \ell) = \tfrac{n}{2}\big[2a + (n-1)d\big]$$

where $\ell = u_n$ is the last term.

Substitute $n = 12$, $a = 300$, $\ell = u_{12} = 4000$:

$$S_{12}^{(A)} = \tfrac{12}{2}(300 + 4000) = 6 \times 4300$$

$$\boxed{\;S_{12}^{(A)} = 25\,800\text{ tonnes}\;}$$

Why $\tfrac{n}{2}(a + \ell)$? Pair up first-and-last, second-and-second-last, etc. — every pair sums to $a + \ell$, and there are $n/2$ pairs. (Gauss's schoolboy trick.)
Carry-in from previous stepFrom STEP 1: $\boxed{\;S_{12}^{(A)} = 25\,800\text{ tonnes}\;}$
STEP 2/2 M1 A1

Strategy: Model B is geometric with $r \approx 1.260$ ($> 1$). Use the geometric sum formula, compute $r^{12}$ via $r \cdot r^{11}$ (we already know $r^{11} = 40/3$), then subtract $S_{12}^{(B)}$ from $S_{12}^{(A)}$.

Formula (geometric sum — IN booklet):

$$S_n = \dfrac{a(r^n - 1)}{r - 1}, \quad r \ne 1$$

Compute $r^{12}$ reusing $r^{11} = 40/3$:

$$r^{12} = r \cdot r^{11} = 1.260 \cdot \dfrac{40}{3} \approx 16.80$$

Substitute $a = 300$, $r = 1.260$, $r^{12} = 16.80$, $n = 12$:

$$S_{12}^{(B)} = \dfrac{300(16.80 - 1)}{1.260 - 1} = \dfrac{300 \cdot 15.80}{0.260} \approx 18\,230\text{ tonnes}$$

Difference (using $S_{12}^{(A)}$ from STEP 1):

$$S_{12}^{(A)} - S_{12}^{(B)} = 25\,800 - 18\,230 = 7570\text{ tonnes}$$

$$\boxed{\;\text{Difference} \approx 7570\text{ tonnes}\;}$$

Modelling cheat sheet: "same amount" → linear/arithmetic; "same percentage" → exponential/geometric. Memorise. Sense check: Model A grows fast initially and slows; Model B grows slowly initially and accelerates. Both end at year 12 = 4000, but Model A's middle is bigger — total is bigger ✓.
$\boxed{\approx 7570\text{ tonnes}}$
