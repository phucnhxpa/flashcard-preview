Q7 — Centre/radius of $C_1: x^2+y^2+8x-10y=29$; prove no overlap with $C_2$

Question

Circle $C_1$ has equation $x^2 + y^2 + 8x - 10y = 29$.

(a)(i) Find the centre of $C_1$. [2] (a)(ii) Find the exact radius of $C_1$. [1]

Circle $C_2$ has equation $(x - 5)^2 + (y + 8)^2 = 52$.

(b) Show that $C_1$ and $C_2$ neither touch nor intersect. [3]

[6 marks]

Part (a) — centre & radius of $C_1$ [3 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 M1

Formula (circle — memorise, NOT in booklet):

$$(x - a)^2 + (y - b)^2 = r^2 \;\Rightarrow\; \text{centre } (a,b),\ \text{radius } r$$

Group $x$-terms and $y$-terms; complete the square:

$$x^2 + 8x = (x+4)^2 - 16$$

$$y^2 - 10y = (y-5)^2 - 25$$

Substitute back:

$$(x+4)^2 - 16 + (y-5)^2 - 25 = 29$$

$$(x+4)^2 + (y-5)^2 = 70$$

Phuc's slip: "I didn't remember the circle equation formula." Memorise the standard form — completing the square is the bridge.
(i) Centre $(-4,\ 5)$    (ii) Radius $r_1 = \sqrt{70}$

Part (b) — non-intersection [3 marks] · 2 steps

$C_2: (x - 5)^2 + (y + 8)^2 = 52$ — centre $(5, -8)$, radius $r_2 = \sqrt{52}$.
STEP 1/2 M1

Formula (distance):

$$d = \sqrt{(x_2-x_1)^2 + (y_2-y_1)^2}$$

Substitute centres $(-4,5)$ and $(5,-8)$:

$$d = \sqrt{(5-(-4))^2 + (-8-5)^2} = \sqrt{81 + 169} = \sqrt{250}$$

$d = \sqrt{250} \approx 15.81$
Carry-in from previous stepFrom STEP 1: $d = \sqrt{250} \approx 15.81$
STEP 2/2 M1 A1

$r_1 + r_2 = \sqrt{70} + \sqrt{52} \approx 8.367 + 7.211 = 15.578$.

Using $d$ from STEP 1 ($d = \sqrt{250} \approx 15.81$):

$$d > r_1 + r_2 \;(15.81 > 15.58)$$

Since the distance between the centres exceeds the sum of the radii, the circles are too far apart to touch or intersect. ∎

Quick reference: $d > r_1+r_2$: separate; $d = r_1+r_2$: external touch; $|r_1-r_2| < d < r_1+r_2$: 2 intersections; $d = |r_1-r_2|$: internal touch; $d < |r_1-r_2|$: one inside the other.
$C_1$ and $C_2$ neither touch nor intersect ∎
