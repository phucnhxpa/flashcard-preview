P2 Q4 · Geometric series — find $a$ and $r$ from $S_3$ and $S_\infty$

Question

A geometric series has sum of the first three terms $S_3 = 70.2$ and sum to infinity $S_\infty = 75$.

Find the first term $a$ and the common ratio $r$.

[5 marks]

Solution [5 marks] · 4 steps

A geometric series has sum of the first three terms $S_3 = 70.2$ and sum to infinity $S_\infty = 75$. Find the first term $a$ and the common ratio $r$.
STEP 1/4S∞ gives a/(1−r) M1

Strategy. Use $S_\infty = \dfrac{a}{1-r}$ (valid for $|r|<1$) to express $a$ in terms of $r$.

From $S_\infty = 75$

$$\frac{a}{1-r} = 75 \quad\Longrightarrow\quad a = 75(1-r). \tag{*}$$
Phuc's mistake. Treated $70.2$ as the third term $a_3 = ar^2$, not the sum of the first three terms $S_3$. Read wording carefully: “sum of the first three terms” = $S_n$, not $a_n$.
↳ from STEP 1: $a = 75(1-r)$
STEP 2/4S₃ and substitute M1

Strategy. Use $S_n = \dfrac{a(1-r^{n})}{1-r}$ with $n=3$, then substitute $(*)$ to eliminate $a$.

From $S_3 = 70.2$

$$\frac{a(1-r^{3})}{1-r} = 70.2.$$

Substitute $a = 75(1-r)$

$$75(1-r^{3}) = 70.2 \quad\Longrightarrow\quad 1 - r^{3} = \frac{70.2}{75} = \frac{117}{125}.$$
↳ from STEP 2: $1 - r^{3} = 117/125$
STEP 3/4solve for r A1

Strategy. Rearrange to $r^{3}$ and take the real cube root.

Solve for r

$$r^{3} = 1 - \frac{117}{125} = \frac{8}{125} \quad\Longrightarrow\quad r = \sqrt[3]{\frac{8}{125}} = \frac{2}{5}.$$

Sense check. $|r| = 0.4 < 1$ ✓ — convergence condition for $S_\infty$ satisfied.

↳ from STEP 3: $r = 2/5$
STEP 4/4back-substitute for a A1

Strategy. Insert $r = 2/5$ into $(*)$ to recover $a$.

Back-substitute

$$a = 75\!\left(1 - \frac{2}{5}\right) = 75 \cdot \frac{3}{5} = 45.$$

Sense check. $S_3 = 45 + 18 + 7.2 = 70.2$ ✓; $S_\infty = 45/(1-0.4) = 75$ ✓.
