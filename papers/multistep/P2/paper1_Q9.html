Q9 — Curve $y = 8x^{3/2} - 2x^{5/2}$: stationary point; find $k$ such that $R_1 = R_2$

Question

Curve $C: y = 8x^{3/2} - 2x^{5/2}$ for $x \ge 0$. Curve crosses the $x$-axis at $x = 0$ and $x = 4$.

(a) Find the $x$-coordinate of the stationary point. [3]

Region $R_1$ lies between $C$ and the $x$-axis from $x=0$ to $x=4$ (above axis). Line $x = k$ ($k > 4$) creates region $R_2$ below the $x$-axis from $x = 4$ to $x = k$, between $C$ and the $x$-axis.

(b) Given $R_1 = R_2$, find $k$. [5]

[8 marks]

Part (a) — stationary point [3 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2 M1

Formula (power rule):

$$\dfrac{d}{dx}(x^n) = nx^{n-1}$$

Differentiate term by term:

$$\dfrac{dy}{dx} = 8 \cdot \tfrac{3}{2}x^{1/2} - 2 \cdot \tfrac{5}{2}x^{3/2} = 12x^{1/2} - 5x^{3/2}$$

Carry-in from previous stepFrom STEP 1: $\dfrac{dy}{dx} = 8 \cdot \tfrac{3}{2}x^{1/2} - 2 \cdot \tfrac{5}{2}x^{3/2} = 12x^{1/2} - 5x^{3/2}$
STEP 2/2 M1 A1

$$12x^{1/2} - 5x^{3/2} = 0 \Rightarrow x^{1/2}(12 - 5x) = 0$$

So $x = 0$ (boundary) or $12 - 5x = 0 \Rightarrow x = \tfrac{12}{5}$.

$\tfrac{12}{5} = 2.4$, which lies in $[0, 4]$ — valid stationary point in $R_1$.

$\boxed{x = \tfrac{12}{5}}$

Part (b) — solve $R_1 = R_2$ [5 marks] · 3 steps

Key insight: $R_1$ above axis (positive integral); $R_2$ below axis (integral is negative). Equal areas ⇒ signed integrals cancel.
STEP 1/3 M1

$R_1 = \int_0^4 y\,dx$    (positive, above axis).

$R_2 = -\int_4^k y\,dx$    (positive area; integral is negative).

$R_1 = R_2 \Rightarrow \int_0^4 y\,dx + \int_4^k y\,dx = 0$

$$\Rightarrow \int_0^k y\,dx = 0$$

Phuc's insight: "R2 should be minus." Correct — and that means the total signed integral from 0 to k is zero. Saves computing two separate integrals.
Carry-in from previous stepFrom STEP 1: $\Rightarrow \int_0^k y\,dx = 0$
STEP 2/3 M1 A1

$$\int (8x^{3/2} - 2x^{5/2})\,dx = 8 \cdot \tfrac{x^{5/2}}{5/2} - 2 \cdot \tfrac{x^{7/2}}{7/2}$$

$$= \tfrac{16}{5}x^{5/2} - \tfrac{4}{7}x^{7/2}$$

Carry-in from previous stepFrom STEP 2: $= \tfrac{16}{5}x^{5/2} - \tfrac{4}{7}x^{7/2}$
STEP 3/3 M1 A1

$\left[\tfrac{16}{5}x^{5/2} - \tfrac{4}{7}x^{7/2}\right]_0^k = \tfrac{16}{5}k^{5/2} - \tfrac{4}{7}k^{7/2} = 0$.

Factor $k^{5/2}$ (non-zero since $k > 4$):

$$k^{5/2}\!\left(\tfrac{16}{5} - \tfrac{4k}{7}\right) = 0 \Rightarrow \tfrac{4k}{7} = \tfrac{16}{5}$$

$$k = \tfrac{16 \cdot 7}{5 \cdot 4} = \tfrac{112}{20} = \tfrac{28}{5}$$

$\boxed{k = \tfrac{28}{5} = 5.6}$
