P2 Q7 · Trapezium rule + log form + log equation with rejected root

Question

(a) The trapezium rule is applied to $\displaystyle\int f(x)\,dx$ and the intermediate result is $\frac{1}{2}\log_{10} M$. Express the answer in the form $\log_{10} K$, giving $K$ in terms of $M$. [5]

(b) A log equation factorises to $(a - 5)(a + 25) = 0$. The logs require $a + 25 > 0$ and $5 - a > 0$. Identify the candidate roots and state which are rejected, with reasons. [4]

[9 marks]

Part (a) — Trapezium rule → log form [5 marks] · 2 steps

↳ from STEP 1: $\frac12 \log_{10} M$
STEP 1/2trapezium rule setup M1

Strategy. Apply the trapezium rule $\displaystyle\int_a^b f(x)\,dx \approx \frac{h}{2}\bigl[y_0 + y_n + 2(y_1 + \cdots + y_{n-1})\bigr]$ with $h = (b-a)/n$, then express the result as a logarithm.

Trapezium rule (after computing the $y_i$ values)

The strip sum evaluates to a single number $M$, and the integral estimate is $\frac12 M$.

Intermediate form. After summing the $y$-values and multiplying by $h/2$, the answer appears as $\frac{1}{2}\log_{10} M$ — a coefficient $\frac12$ still sits in front of the log.
STEP 2/2absorb the 1/2 via power law A1

Strategy. The target form is $\log_{10} K$ with no leading coefficient. Use the log power law $\log_b(x^{n}) = n\log_b x$ in reverse to fold the $\frac12$ inside as a power.

Power law (reverse)

$$\frac{1}{2}\log_{10} M = \log_{10}\!\left(M^{1/2}\right) = \log_{10}\!\bigl(\sqrt{M}\bigr).$$
Phuc's mistake. Left the answer as $\frac{1}{2}\log_{10} M$. Whenever the target form is $\log_b K$ with no coefficient, use the power law to absorb it: $\boxed{K = \sqrt{M}}$.

Sense check. $\log_{10}\sqrt{M} = \log_{10} M^{1/2} = \frac12 \log_{10} M$ ✓ — the two forms are equal, but only the latter matches the required “$\log_{10} K$” format.

Part (b) — Log equation with rejected root [4 marks] · 3 steps

STEP 1/3domain restrictions first M1

Strategy. Before solving, note the domain: every $\log_b(g(x))$ requires $g(x) > 0$. If the logs contain $\log(a+25)$ and $\log(5-a)$, then both $a + 25 > 0$ AND $5 - a > 0$.

Natural domain

$$a + 25 > 0 \;\text{AND}\; 5 - a > 0 \quad\Longrightarrow\quad -25 < a < 5.$$
Habit. Always write the domain BEFORE solving. Any candidate root outside $(-25,\, 5)$ must be rejected with a written reason.
↳ from STEP 1: $-25 < a < 5$
STEP 2/3combine logs and solve M1

Strategy. Combine the logs using product/quotient laws, drop the log, and solve the resulting polynomial.

Factorise the resulting equation

The log equation reduces to a quadratic in $a$ that factorises as:

$$(a - 5)(a + 25) = 0.$$

Candidate roots: $a = 5$ and $a = -25$.

↳ from STEP 2: $(a-5)(a+25) = 0$
STEP 3/3reject with reason A1

Strategy. Check each candidate against the domain $-25 < a < 5$. Reject any that violate it, stating the restriction that kills the root.

Reject $a = 5$

$$a = 5 \;\text{fails because}\; 5 - a = 5 - 5 = 0 \ngtr 0.$$

Reject $a = 5$: need $5 - a > 0$.

Reject $a = -25$

$$a = -25 \;\text{fails because}\; a + 25 = 0 \ngtr 0.$$

Reject $a = -25$: need $a + 25 > 0$.

Phuc's mistake. Just crossed out the invalid root without a reason. Always write the rejection reason: “reject $a = 5$: need $5 - a > 0$”.

Sense check. Both candidates fall on the boundary of the domain (excluded, since $>$ not $\ge$), so both are rejected — the equation has no valid solution in the natural domain.
