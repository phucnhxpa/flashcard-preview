Q4 тАФ $(3+px)^5 = A + Bx + Cx^2 + Dx^3 + \ldots$; given $B = 18D$, $p<0$, find $p$ and $C$

Question

The expansion of $(3 + px)^5$ in ascending powers of $x$ begins $A + Bx + Cx^2 + Dx^3 + \ldots$, where $p$ is a non-zero constant.

(a) Find $A$. [1]

(b) Given $B = 18D$ and $p < 0$, find (i) $p$, (ii) $C$. [6]

[7 marks]

Part (a) тАФ find $A$ [1 marks] ┬╖ 1 step

STEP 1/1 B1

Strategy: the constant term $A$ in $(3 + px)^5$ corresponds to $x^0$ тАФ i.e. the term where $px$ does not appear. In the binomial expansion, that is the $r = 0$ term, where $b^r = (px)^0 = 1$.

Formula (binomial expansion тАФ IN booklet):

$$(a + b)^n = \sum_{r=0}^{n}\binom{n}{r} a^{n-r} b^r$$

Substitute $a = 3$, $b = px$, $n = 5$, $r = 0$:

$$T_1 = \binom{5}{0} \cdot 3^{5} \cdot (px)^{0} = 1 \cdot 3^5 \cdot 1$$

Compute $3^5 = 3 \cdot 3 \cdot 3 \cdot 3 \cdot 3 = 9 \cdot 27 = 243$.

$$\boxed{\;A = 243\;}$$

Why $\binom{5}{0} = 1$: "choose 0 things from 5" has exactly one way (the empty selection). And anything to the power $0$ is $1$. So the $r = 0$ term is always just $a^n$.
$\boxed{A = 243}$

Part (b) тАФ find $p$, $C$ [6 marks] ┬╖ 2 steps

Use $a = 3$, $b = px$, $n = 5$. Compute $B$, $C$, $D$ in terms of $p$.
STEP 1/2 M1 M1 A1

Strategy: general binomial term $T_{r+1} = \binom{n}{r}a^{n-r}b^r$. Here $a = 3$, $b = px$, $n = 5$. $B$ is the coefficient of $x^1$ ($r = 1$), $C$ of $x^2$ ($r = 2$), $D$ of $x^3$ ($r = 3$). Pattern: powers of $3$ decrease while powers of $p$ increase.

Coefficient $B$ ($r = 1$):

$$T_2 = \binom{5}{1} \cdot 3^{4} \cdot (px)^{1} = 5 \cdot 81 \cdot p\,x = 405\,p\,x$$

$\;\;\Rightarrow B = 405\,p$.

Coefficient $C$ ($r = 2$):

$$T_3 = \binom{5}{2} \cdot 3^{3} \cdot (px)^{2} = 10 \cdot 27 \cdot p^2 x^2 = 270\,p^2 x^2$$

$\;\;\Rightarrow C = 270\,p^2$.

Coefficient $D$ ($r = 3$):

$$T_4 = \binom{5}{3} \cdot 3^{2} \cdot (px)^{3} = 10 \cdot 9 \cdot p^3 x^3 = 90\,p^3 x^3$$

$\;\;\Rightarrow D = 90\,p^3$.

$$\boxed{\;B = 405p,\quad C = 270p^2,\quad D = 90p^3\;}$$

Binomial coefficient values: $\binom{5}{1} = 5$, $\binom{5}{2} = 10$, $\binom{5}{3} = 10$ (Pascal's row 5: $1,5,10,10,5,1$). Trap: writing $(px)^r = px^r$ тАФ wrong. $(px)^r = p^r x^r$, so the $p$ also gets the power.
$B = 405p,\ C = 270p^2,\ D = 90p^3$

STEP 2/2 M1 A1 A1

Strategy: use the given relation $B = 18D$ to form an equation purely in $p$. Both sides have a common factor of $p$ (so we can divide by it, since $p \ne 0$ given). The remaining equation is in $p^2$ тЗТ two roots $\pm$, and the constraint $p < 0$ picks the negative one.

Substitute $B = 405p$ and $D = 90p^3$ from STEP 1 into $B = 18D$:

$$405\,p = 18 \cdot 90\,p^3$$

$$405\,p = 1620\,p^3$$

$$405 = 1620\,p^2$$

$$p^2 = \dfrac{405}{1620} = \dfrac{1}{4}$$

$$p = \pm\tfrac{1}{2}$$

$$\boxed{\;p = -\tfrac{1}{2}\;}$$

Compute $C$: $C = 270\,p^2$, with $p^2 = \tfrac{1}{4}$:

$$C = 270 \cdot \tfrac{1}{4} = \tfrac{270}{4} = \tfrac{135}{2} = 67.5$$

$$\boxed{\;C = \tfrac{135}{2} = 67.5\;}$$
