P2 Q6 · Circle — centre on $y = -3x + 19$, through $A(11, 6)$

Question

The centre of a circle lies on the line $y = -3x + 19$. The centre is the point $P(7,\, k)$ and the point $A(11,\, 6)$ lies on the circle.

Find the value of $k$ and the equation of the circle.

[5 marks]

1 · Centre P(7,−2) on y = −3x + 19
2 · Radius = |PA| = √80 = 4√5
3 · Circle (x−7)²+(y+2)²=80

Solution [5 marks] · 3 steps

The centre of a circle lies on the line $y = -3x + 19$. The point $P(7,\, k)$ is the centre, and the point $A(11,\, 6)$ lies on the circle.
↳ from STEP 1: centre $= (7,-2)$
STEP 1/3find centre from line M1

Strategy. The centre lies on the line $y = -3x + 19$. Given the $x$-coordinate is $7$, substitute to find $k$.

Substitute $x = 7$

$$k = -3(7) + 19 = -21 + 19 = -2.$$

Centre $= (7,\, -2)$.

Phuc's mistake. Wrote $y - 4$ where $y + 4$ was needed (which produced $+20$ instead of $+19$ in the line). Fix: rewrite the whole line equation neatly before substituting: $y = -3x + 19$.
↳ from STEP 2: $r = \sqrt{80} = 4\sqrt{5}$
STEP 2/3radius via distance formula M1

Strategy. The radius is the distance from the centre $(7,-2)$ to the point $A(11, 6)$ on the circle: $r = \sqrt{(\Delta x)^{2} + (\Delta y)^{2}}$.

Distance formula

$$r = \sqrt{(11 - 7)^{2} + \bigl(6 - (-2)\bigr)^{2}} = \sqrt{4^{2} + 8^{2}} = \sqrt{16 + 64} = \sqrt{80}.$$

Simplify surd

$$\sqrt{80} = \sqrt{16 \cdot 5} = 4\sqrt{5}.$$

Sense check. $\sqrt{80} \approx 8.94$; the point $(11, 6)$ is $\sqrt{16+64} \approx 8.94$ from $(7,-2)$ ✓.

STEP 3/3equation of circle A1

Strategy. Circle with centre $(a, b)$ and radius $r$: $(x-a)^{2} + (y-b)^{2} = r^{2}$. Use $r^{2} = 80$ (no need to square $4\sqrt{5}$ separately).

Write equation

$$\boxed{(x - 7)^{2} + (y + 2)^{2} = 80}.$$
Notation trap. $(x - 7)^{2}$ means $a = +7$, NOT $-7$. The $y$-term is $(y + 2)^{2}$ because the centre's $y$-coordinate is $-2$: $(y - (-2))^{2} = (y + 2)^{2}$.
