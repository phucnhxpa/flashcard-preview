Q1 · Geometric transformations — $y=2f(3x-1)+4$

Question

Given $y=f(x)$ has a maximum at $(2,\,5)$ and a minimum at $(6,\,-1)$,

(a) sketch $y=2f(3x-1)+4$ and state the new coordinates of the max and min. [4]

(b) find the transformation that maps $y=f(3x)$ back to $y=f(x)$. [1]

[5 marks]

Solution [5 marks] · 4 steps

$y=f(x)$ has a maximum at $(2,5)$ and a minimum at $(6,-1)$. Apply $y=2f(3x-1)+4$ in the correct inside-out order.
STEP 1/4split the composite M1

Strategy. Break $y = 2f(3x-1)+4$ into atomic transformations applied from inside out. Read the bracket first, then scale, then the outside.

Inside-out order

1. $f(x) \to f(x-1)$ — translate right by $1$
2. $f(x-1) \to f(3x-1)$ — horizontal stretch $\times \tfrac13$
3. $f(3x-1) \to 2f(3x-1)$ — vertical stretch $\times 2$
4. $2f(3x-1) \to 2f(3x-1)+4$ — translate up by $4$

Phuc's mistake. Tried to combine all four mentally in one step and lost track of the order. Harry's rule #1: always sketch the intermediate image after each atomic move.
↳ from STEP 1: composite split into 4 atomic moves
STEP 2/4translate right by 1 A1

Strategy. The bracket $3x-1 = 3\bigl(x-\tfrac13\bigr)$, so the $x$-shift is $+\tfrac13$ (NOT $+1$). $y=f(x-a)$ translates by $(a,0)$.

Rewrite the bracket

$$3x-1 = 3\Bigl(x - \frac13\Bigr) \quad\Rightarrow\quad \text{translate right by } \frac13.$$
Common trap (Harry). $f(2x+3) \neq f(2(x+3))$. Factor the coefficient of $x$ before reading the shift: $f(2x+3)=f\bigl(2(x+\tfrac32)\bigr)$, so shift is $-\tfrac32$, not $-3$.

Sense check. A positive $x$-shift moves the graph right; verify by testing the new location of the maximum.

↳ from STEP 2: shift right by $\tfrac13$
STEP 3/4stretches (h then v) M1

Strategy. After the shift: $y=f\bigl(3(x-\tfrac13)\bigr)$ is a horizontal stretch $\times \tfrac13$. Then $2f(\cdot)$ is a vertical stretch $\times 2$.

Stretches

Horizontal: $y=f(ax)$ stretches $\times \tfrac1a$ in $x$. Here $a=3 \Rightarrow$ factor $\tfrac13$.
Vertical: $y=af(x)$ stretches $\times a$ in $y$. Here $a=2 \Rightarrow$ factor $2$.

Phuc's mistake. Inverted the horizontal factor, writing $\times 3$ instead of $\times \tfrac13$. The coefficient inside the bracket divides the $x$-scale.
↳ from STEP 3: h-stretch $\times\tfrac13$, v-stretch $\times2$
STEP 4/4final vertical translate A1

Strategy. The trailing $+4$ is applied last (outermost): translate the whole graph up by $4$.

Apply $+4$

$$y = 2f(3x-1) + 4 \quad\Rightarrow\quad \text{translate up by } (0,4).$$

Sense check. Maximum $(2,5) \to$ after shift $(2+\tfrac13,\,5)=(\tfrac73,5) \to$ after h-stretch $(\tfrac79,5) \to$ after v-stretch $(\tfrac79,10) \to$ after up-translate $(\tfrac79,\,14)$. Minimum $(6,-1) \to (\tfrac{19}{9},\,3)$.

Sequence: shift $+\tfrac13$ right → h-stretch $\times\tfrac13$ → v-stretch $\times2$ → up $4$. New max $\bigl(\tfrac79,\,14\bigr)$, new min $\bigl(\tfrac{19}{9},\,3\bigr)$.

Step-by-step transformation sequence

Step 1: Start with the parent f(x) — max at (2, 5), min at (6, -1)

Step 2: f(3x) — horizontal compression by 1/3 (max moves to 2/3, min to 2)

Step 3: f(3x-1) = f(3(x-1/3)) — translate right by 1/3 (max at 1, min at 7/3)

Step 4: 2f(3x-1) — vertical stretch by 2 (max at (1, 10), min at (7/3, -2))

Step 5: 2f(3x-1)+4 — translate up by 4 [FINAL] (max at (1, 14), min at (7/3, 2))
