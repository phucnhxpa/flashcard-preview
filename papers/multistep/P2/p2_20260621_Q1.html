Q1 · Geometric transformations — $y=2f(3x-1)+4$

Question

Given $y=f(x)$ has a maximum at $(2,\,5)$ and a minimum at $(6,\,-1)$,

(a) sketch $y=2f(3x-1)+4$ and state the new coordinates of the max and min. [4]

(b) find the transformation that maps $y=f(3x)$ back to $y=f(x)$. [1]

[5 marks]

Solution [5 marks] · 4 steps

$y=f(x)$ has a maximum at $(2,5)$ and a minimum at $(6,-1)$. Apply $y=2f(3x-1)+4$ in the correct inside-out order.
STEP 1/4split the composite M1

Strategy. Break $y = 2f(3x-1)+4$ into atomic transformations applied from inside out. Read the bracket first, then scale, then the outside.

Inside-out order

1. $f(x) \to f(3x)$ — horizontal stretch $\times\tfrac13$ (compress)
2. $f(3x) \to f(3x-1)=f\bigl(3(x-\tfrac13)\bigr)$ — translate right $\tfrac13$
3. $f(3x-1) \to 2f(3x-1)$ — vertical stretch $\times 2$
4. $2f(3x-1) \to 2f(3x-1)+4$ — translate up by $4$

Phuc's mistake. Tried to combine all four mentally in one step and lost track of the order. Harry's rule #1: always sketch the intermediate image after each atomic move.
↳ from STEP 1: composite split into 4 atomic moves
STEP 2/4horizontal stretch (compress) A1

Strategy. Deal with the $\times 3$ inside the bracket first: $y=f(3x)$ is a horizontal stretch $\times\tfrac13$ — a compression toward the $y$-axis.

Effect on key points

$$\text{max }(2,5)\to\Bigl(\tfrac23,5\Bigr),\qquad \text{min }(6,-1)\to(2,-1).$$
Phuc's mistake. Inverted the horizontal factor, writing $\times 3$ instead of $\times \tfrac13$. Inside the bracket $\times a$ means a stretch of $\times\tfrac1a$ in $x$ — it divides the $x$-scale.

Sense check. Multiply every $x$-coordinate by $\tfrac13$; the $y$-values are unchanged.

↳ from STEP 2: compressed to $f(3x)$
STEP 3/4translate right by 1/3 M1

Strategy. Now the $-1$: $f(3x-1)=f\bigl(3(x-\tfrac13)\bigr)$, so translate the compressed curve right by $\tfrac13$ — the shift is applied after the compression.

Effect on key points

$$\text{max }\Bigl(\tfrac23,5\Bigr)\to(1,5),\qquad \text{min }(2,-1)\to\Bigl(\tfrac73,-1\Bigr).$$
Common trap (Harry). $f(2x+3)\neq f(2(x+3))$ — factor the coefficient before reading the shift: $f(2x+3)=f\bigl(2(x+\tfrac32)\bigr)$. Shifting the compressed curve by $\tfrac13$ (not $1$) lands the max at $x=1$.
↳ from STEP 3: shifted to $f(3x-1)$
STEP 4/4vertical stretch ×2, then up 4 A1

Strategy. Apply the outside operations: $2f(\cdot)$ is a vertical stretch $\times2$, then $+4$ translates up by $4$ (both act on $y$).

Foolproof check — solve $3x-1=$ old $x$

$$\text{max: }3x-1=2\Rightarrow x=1,\ y=2(5)+4=14;\qquad \text{min: }3x-1=6\Rightarrow x=\tfrac73,\ y=2(-1)+4=2.$$

Sense check. Max $(2,5)\to(\tfrac23,5)\to(1,5)\to(1,10)\to(1,14)$; min $(6,-1)\to(2,-1)\to(\tfrac73,-1)\to(\tfrac73,-2)\to(\tfrac73,2)$. In general $x_{\text{new}}=\dfrac{x_{\text{old}}+1}{3}$.

Sequence: h-compress $\times\tfrac13$ → right $\tfrac13$ → v-stretch $\times2$ → up $4$. New max $\bigl(1,\,14\bigr)$, new min $\bigl(\tfrac73,\,2\bigr)$.
