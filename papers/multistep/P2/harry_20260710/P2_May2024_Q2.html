Q2 · Arithmetic series and an inequality in \(n\)

Total 7 marksQuestion 2. In this question you must show all stages of your working. Solutions relying entirely on calculator technology are not acceptable.
In an arithmetic series, • the sixth term is 2 • the sum of the first ten terms is –80
For this series, (a) find the value of the first term and the value of the common difference.  (4)
(b) Hence find the smallest value of \(n\) for which \[S_{n}>8000\]  (3)
Source: Pearson Edexcel IAL WMA12/01, 14 May 2024, Question 2 (verbatim).
Part (a) · First term and common difference[4] · worked in lesson
Hide working
Strategy
The 6th term gives \(a+5d=2\). The sum of the first 10 terms gives \(S_{10}=\tfrac{10}{2}(2a+9d)=-80\). Solve simultaneously.
Formula · set up two equations
\[ a+5d=2,\qquad S_{10}=\frac{10}{2}\big(2a+9d\big)=-80\;\Rightarrow\; 2a+9d=-16. \]
Working · solve
From the first equation \(a=2-5d\). Substitute into the second: \(2(2-5d)+9d=-16\Rightarrow 4-10d+9d=-16\Rightarrow -d=-20\).
Result
\( d=20,\quad a=-98 \)
Sense check
6th term: \(-98+5(20)=-98+100=2\)  ✓. \(S_{10}=5(-196+180)=5(-16)=-80\)  ✓.

Part (b) · Smallest \(n\) with \(S_{n}>8000\)[3] · worked in lesson (lost 1 mark)
Carry-inFrom (a): \(a=-98,\ d=20\). Use \(S_{n}=\tfrac{n}{2}\big(2a+(n-1)d\big)>8000\).
Hide working
Strategy
Substitute \(a=-98,\ d=20\) into the sum formula, form a quadratic inequality in \(n\), solve for the positive root, then take the smallest integer above it.
Substitute
\[ \frac{n}{2}\big(2(-98)+20(n-1)\big)>8000 \;\Rightarrow\; \frac{n}{2}\big(-196+20n-20\big)>8000 \]
Working · simplify to a quadratic
\[ \frac{n}{2}(20n-216)>8000 \;\Rightarrow\; n(10n-108)>8000 \;\Rightarrow\; 10n^{2}-108n-8000>0 \]\[ \Rightarrow\; 5n^{2}-54n-4000>0 \]
Working · solve the quadratic
Positive root: \(n=\frac{54+\sqrt{54^{2}+4\cdot5\cdot4000}}{2\cdot5}=\frac{54+\sqrt{2916+80000}}{10}=\frac{54+\sqrt{82916}}{10}\approx 34.2\).
Result
\( n=35 \)
Sense check
\(S_{34}=\tfrac{34}{2}(-196+33\times20)=17(460)=7820<8000\). \(S_{35}=\tfrac{35}{2}(-196+34\times20)=17.5(484)=8470>8000\)  ✓.
