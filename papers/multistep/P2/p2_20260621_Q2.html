Q2 · Conditional probability — tree diagram, Bayes

Question

Bag A has 3 red and 2 blue balls. Bag B has 4 red and 1 blue. A fair die is rolled: if it shows 1–3, pick from Bag A; otherwise Bag B. One ball is drawn.

(a) Draw a tree diagram. (b) Find $\Pr(\text{red})$. (c) Given the ball is red, find $\Pr(\text{from Bag A})$.

[5 marks]

Solution [5 marks] · 4 steps

Bag A: 3 red, 2 blue. Bag B: 4 red, 1 blue. Fair die: 1–3 → Bag A, else Bag B. One ball drawn.
STEP 1/4first-stage branches (die) M1

Strategy. The first stage is the die: $1$–$3 \to$ Bag A, $4$–$6 \to$ Bag B. Each has probability $\tfrac12$.

First-stage probabilities

$$\Pr(A) = \frac36 = \frac12, \qquad \Pr(B) = \frac36 = \frac12.$$
Harry's tree rule #1. Label EVERY branch with its probability before computing anything downstream. The two first-stage branches must sum to $1$: $\tfrac12+\tfrac12=1$ ✓
↳ from STEP 1: $\Pr(A)=\Pr(B)=\tfrac12$
STEP 2/4second-stage branches (colour) M1

Strategy. Bag A: $3$R, $2$B so $\Pr(R|A)=\tfrac35$, $\Pr(B|A)=\tfrac25$. Bag B: $4$R, $1$B so $\Pr(R|B)=\tfrac45$, $\Pr(B|B)=\tfrac15$. Each pair sums to $1$.

Conditional probabilities

$$\Pr(R|A)=\frac35,\ \Pr(B|A)=\frac25; \qquad \Pr(R|B)=\frac45,\ \Pr(B|B)=\frac15.$$
Phuc's mistake. Wrote $\Pr(R|A)=\tfrac13$ (red out of ALL 5 balls in both bags). The ball is drawn from Bag A only: denominator is $3+2=5$ inside that bag.
↳ from STEP 2: $\Pr(R|A)=\tfrac35,\ \Pr(R|B)=\tfrac45$
STEP 3/4law of total probability A1

Strategy. Multiply along branches for “AND”, then add the red endpoints for $\Pr(R)$: $\Pr(R) = \Pr(R|A)\Pr(A) + \Pr(R|B)\Pr(B)$.

Total probability

$$\Pr(R) = \frac35\cdot\frac12 + \frac45\cdot\frac12 = \frac{3+4}{10} = \frac{7}{10}.$$

Sense check. Four endpoint probabilities: $\tfrac35\tfrac12+\tfrac25\tfrac12+\tfrac45\tfrac12+\tfrac15\tfrac12 = \tfrac{3+2+4+1}{10}=1$ ✓

↳ from STEP 3: $\Pr(R)=\tfrac{7}{10}$, $\Pr(A\cap R)=\tfrac{3}{10}$
STEP 4/4Bayes (reverse direction) A1

Strategy. Use the conditional probability formula $\Pr(A|R)=\dfrac{\Pr(A\cap R)}{\Pr(R)}$ with $\Pr(A\cap R)=\Pr(R|A)\Pr(A)$.

Bayes

$$\Pr(A|R) = \frac{\Pr(R|A)\Pr(A)}{\Pr(R)} = \frac{\frac35\cdot\frac12}{\frac{7}{10}} = \frac{3/10}{7/10} = \frac{3}{7}.$$
Phuc's mistake. Used $\Pr(A\cap R)=\Pr(A)\Pr(R)$, i.e. assumed independence. Bags are NOT independent of the colour — the die selects the bag. Use $\Pr(A\cap R)=\Pr(R|A)\Pr(A)$.

Sense check. $\tfrac37\approx0.429 < \tfrac12$: given a red ball, Bag A is now less likely than prior $\tfrac12$ because Bag B is richer in red. Reasonable ✓
