Double Angle Formula + Solve in Range

Question

(a) Show that the equation $(3\cos\theta - \tan\theta)\cos\theta = 2$ can be written as $3\sin^2\theta + \sin\theta - 1 = 0$ [3]

(b) Hence solve, for $-\frac{\pi}{2} \leqslant x \leqslant \frac{\pi}{2}$, the equation $(3\cos 2x - \tan 2x)\cos 2x = 2$, giving your answers to 2 decimal places. [5]

[8 marks]

Part (a) — Show that the equation becomes a quadratic in sin θ [3 marks] · 3 steps

STEP 1/3 M1

Expand and convert tan to sin/cos:

$$(3\cos\theta - \tan\theta)\cos\theta = 2$$

$$3\cos^2\theta - \frac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta} \cdot \cos\theta = 2$$

$$3\cos^2\theta - \sin\theta = 2$$

Pitfall: Don't forget to multiply through by $\cos\theta$ — the tan term simplifies to just $\sin\theta$.
$\boxed{3\cos^2\theta - \sin\theta = 2}$
Carry-in from previous step$3\cos^2\theta - \sin\theta = 2$
STEP 2/3 M1

Apply Pythagorean identity: $\cos^2\theta = 1 - \sin^2\theta$

$$3(1 - \sin^2\theta) - \sin\theta = 2$$

$$3 - 3\sin^2\theta - \sin\theta = 2$$

Pitfall: This is a 'show that' — you must show every algebraic step clearly.
$\boxed{3 - 3\sin^2\theta - \sin\theta = 2}$
Carry-in from previous step$3 - 3\sin^2\theta - \sin\theta = 2$
STEP 3/3 A1

Rearrange to standard form:

$$3 - 3\sin^2\theta - \sin\theta - 2 = 0$$

$$-3\sin^2\theta - \sin\theta + 1 = 0$$

$$3\sin^2\theta + \sin\theta - 1 = 0 \quad \square$$

Pitfall: Multiply through by $-1$ to match the required form with positive leading coefficient.
$\boxed{3\sin^2\theta + \sin\theta - 1 = 0}$

Part (b) — Solve the equation for x [5 marks] · 5 steps

$3\sin^2\theta + \sin\theta - 1 = 0$ from part (a)
STEP 1/5 M1

Substitute $\theta = 2x$ and solve quadratic:

$$3\sin^2 2x + \sin 2x - 1 = 0$$

Let $u = \sin 2x$:

$$u = \frac{-1 \pm \sqrt{1 + 12}}{6} = \frac{-1 \pm \sqrt{13}}{6}$$

Pitfall: The range for $2x$ is $-\pi \leqslant 2x \leqslant \pi$ — double the original range!
$\boxed{\sin 2x = \frac{-1 + \sqrt{13}}{6} \text{ or } \frac{-1 - \sqrt{13}}{6}}$
Carry-in from previous step$\sin 2x = \frac{-1 + \sqrt{13}}{6} \approx 0.4343$ or $\sin 2x = \frac{-1 - \sqrt{13}}{6} \approx -0.7676$
STEP 2/5 A1

Case 1: $\sin 2x = \frac{-1 + \sqrt{13}}{6} \approx 0.4343$

$$2x = \arcsin(0.4343) \approx 0.449$$

$$x = \frac{0.449}{2} \approx 0.22$$

Pitfall: Remember to divide by 2 to get $x$, not $2x$.
$\boxed{x \approx 0.22}$
Carry-in from previous step$2x = 0.449$ (principal value)
STEP 3/5 A1

Second solution from Case 1:

$$2x = \pi - 0.449 \approx 2.693$$

$$x = \frac{2.693}{2} \approx 1.35$$

Pitfall: For $\sin 2x = k > 0$, there's a second solution at $\pi - \alpha$ within $[-\pi, \pi]$.
$\boxed{x \approx 1.35}$
Carry-in from previous step$\sin 2x \approx -0.7676$
STEP 4/5 A1

Case 2: $\sin 2x = \frac{-1 - \sqrt{13}}{6} \approx -0.7676$

$$2x = \arcsin(-0.7676) \approx -0.875$$

$$x = \frac{-0.875}{2} \approx -0.44$$

Pitfall: Don't reject negative values of $\sin 2x$ — check if they're in $[-1, 1]$.
$\boxed{x \approx -0.44}$
Carry-in from previous step$2x = -0.875$ (principal value)
STEP 5/5 A1

Second solution from Case 2:

$$2x = -\pi - (-0.875) = -\pi + 0.875 \approx -2.267$$

$$x = \frac{-2.267}{2} \approx -1.13$$

All four solutions: $x \approx -1.13, -0.44, 0.22, 1.35$

Pitfall: For $\sin 2x = k < 0$, the second solution is at $-\pi - \alpha$. Ensure answers are in radians, not degrees!
$\boxed{x \approx -1.13, -0.44, 0.22, 1.35}$
