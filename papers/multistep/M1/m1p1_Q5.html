Q5 — Parachutist: free-fall then parachute opens

Question

A parachutist with parachute (combined mass $250$ kg) is released from rest at height $520$ m. She falls freely for $5$ s, then the parachute opens. Once the parachute is open, the air resistance on the system is constant at $3200$ N. Take $g = 9.8\,\text{m/s}^2$.

(a) Find the distance fallen in the first $5$ s. [2]

(b) Find her speed when she lands. [7]

(c) Find the total time of the descent. [3]

(d) Sketch a speed-time graph for the whole motion. [2]

[14 marks]

Part (a) — Distance fallen in first 5 s [2 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 M1 A1

Strategy: 'free fall' → the only force is gravity → $a = g$ downward. ‘Released from rest’ → $u = 0$. With time $t$ given, choose the suvat formula that maps $(u, a, t) \to s$.

List the knowns for Phase 1 (free fall):

• $u = 0$ (from rest)

• $a = g = 9.8$ m/s$^2$ (down)

• $t = 5$ s

• Want: $s_1$ (distance fallen).

Formula (suvat — displacement from $u$, $a$, $t$):

$$s = ut + \tfrac{1}{2}at^2$$

Substitute:

$$s_1 = 0 \cdot 5 + \tfrac{1}{2}(9.8)(5)^2$$

$$s_1 = \tfrac{1}{2}(9.8)(25) = \tfrac{245}{2} = 122.5\ \text{m}$$

$$\boxed{\;s_1 = 122.5\ \text{m}\;}$$

Sense check: after $5$ s of free fall, speed reached is $v = gt = 49$ m/s (≈110 mph) — a serious skydive speed. Distance $122.5$ m ≈ height of a 40-storey building. Both reasonable for a parachute opening.
$\boxed{\,s_1 = 122.5\ \text{m}\,}$

Part (b) — Landing speed [7 marks] · 2 steps

From (a): $s_1 = 122.5$ m. Speed at end of Phase 1: $v_1 = u + gt = 0 + 9.8(5) = 49$ m/s.
STEP 1/2 M1 A1

Strategy: once the parachute opens, the force balance changes. Air resistance is now significant ($3200$ N) and acts upward. Apply N2L with both forces.

Free-body for Phase 2 (parachutist + parachute, mass $250$ kg). Take down as positive (consistent with motion):

• Weight: $W = mg = 250 \cdot 9.8 = 2450$ N down $\Rightarrow +2450$.

• Air resistance: $3200$ N up $\Rightarrow -3200$.

Formula (Newton’s 2nd law):

$$\Sigma F = m\, a$$

Substitute:

$$2450 - 3200 = 250\, a$$

$$-750 = 250\, a$$

$$a = \frac{-750}{250} = -3\ \text{m/s}^2$$

$$\boxed{\;a_2 = -3\ \text{m/s}^2\;}$$

(Negative sign $\Rightarrow$ acceleration is upward $\Rightarrow$ she’s decelerating.)

Common error: using $a = g$ in Phase 2 — but gravity is no longer the only force, so $a \ne g$. Sense check: air resistance ($3200$ N) > weight ($2450$ N), so net force is upward and she slows down. The negative sign confirms this: $a < 0$ in our convention. Sign convention: down = positive (matches direction of motion), so $-3$ means "$3$ m/s$^2$ in the upward direction" — i.e. deceleration.
$a_2 = -3\ \text{m/s}^2$ (deceleration)
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $a_2 = -3\ \text{m/s}^2$ (deceleration after parachute opens).
STEP 2/2 M1 A1

Strategy: for Phase 2, I know the initial speed (from end of Phase 1: $u_2 = 49$ m/s) and the deceleration (from STEP 1: $a_2 = -3$ m/s$^2$). I want the final speed at landing. The Phase-2 distance is what’s left of the $520$ m drop.

List the knowns for Phase 2:

• $u_2 = 49$ m/s (speed at end of Phase 1: $v = u + gt = 0 + 9.8 \cdot 5 = 49$).

• $a_2 = -3$ m/s$^2$ (from STEP 1).

• $s_2 = 520 - s_1 = 520 - 122.5 = 397.5$ m (distance still to fall).

• Want: $v$ (landing speed).

Formula (suvat — connects $u$, $a$, $s$, $v$ without time):

$$v^2 = u^2 + 2as$$

Substitute:

$$v^2 = (49)^2 + 2(-3)(397.5)$$

$$v^2 = 2401 + (-6)(397.5)$$

$$v^2 = 2401 - 2385 = 16$$

$$v = \sqrt{16} = 4\ \text{m/s}$$

$$\boxed{\;v = 4\ \text{m/s}\;}$$

Sense check: $4$ m/s on landing is about walking pace — a soft, survivable parachute landing. If we had ignored the parachute (used $a = g$ throughout), $v$ would be over $100$ m/s. The parachute did a huge amount of work — cutting the speed from $49$ to $4$ m/s.
$\boxed{\,v = 4\ \text{m/s}\,}$

Part (c) — Total time [3 marks] · 1 step

From (b): $v = 4$ m/s on landing; Phase 1 = 5 s; $a_2 = -3$.
STEP 1/1 M1 A1 A1

Strategy: Phase 1 took $5$ s (given). For total time, I need Phase 2’s duration. I have $u_2$, $v_2$, $a_2$ — the suvat formula linking $(u, v, a, t)$ is $v = u + at$.

List the knowns for Phase 2:

• $u_2 = 49$ m/s (start of Phase 2)

• $v_2 = 4$ m/s (from part b)

• $a_2 = -3$ m/s$^2$

• Want: $t_2$.

Formula (suvat — velocity from $u$, $a$, $t$):

$$v = u + at$$

Substitute:

$$4 = 49 + (-3)\, t_2$$

$$4 - 49 = -3\, t_2$$

$$-45 = -3\, t_2 \;\Rightarrow\; t_2 = \frac{-45}{-3} = 15\ \text{s}$$

Total time: $t_\text{total} = t_1 + t_2 = 5 + 15$.

$$\boxed{\;t_\text{total} = 20\ \text{s}\;}$$

Cross-check using $s = ut + \tfrac{1}{2}at^2$ for Phase 2: $s_2 = 49(15) + \tfrac{1}{2}(-3)(15)^2 = 735 - 337.5 = 397.5$ m ✓ (matches Phase 2 distance).
$\boxed{\,t_{\text{total}} = 20\ \text{s}\,}$

Part (d) — Speed-time sketch [2 marks] · 1 step

From (a)/(b)/(c): Phase 1: $0 \to 5$ s, $v$ rises $0 \to 49$ m/s (slope $+9.8$). Phase 2: $5 \to 20$ s, $v$ falls $49 \to 4$ m/s (slope $-3$).
STEP 1/1 B1 B1

Strategy: on a $v$-$t$ graph, constant acceleration produces a straight line; the gradient equals $a$. Two phases with different constant $a$ → two straight segments joined at the boundary $t = 5$ s.

Segment 1 (free fall, $0 \le t \le 5$):

• Start: $(t, v) = (0, 0)$ (released from rest).

• End: $(t, v) = (5, 49)$ (since $v = u + gt = 0 + 9.8 \cdot 5$).

• Slope $= +9.8$ m/s$^2$ (positive → line goes up to the right).

Segment 2 (parachute, $5 \le t \le 20$):

• Start: $(5, 49)$ (continuous — speed doesn’t jump).

• End: $(20, 4)$ (from parts (b) and (c)).

• Slope $= -3$ m/s$^2$ (negative → line goes down to the right).

Key features to mark: the kink at $(5, 49)$, the endpoint at $(20, 4)$, axes labelled $t$ (s) and $v$ (m/s).

Common error: drawing a curve instead of straight segments. Constant $a$ → straight line, every time. Also speed should never go negative — she’s descending the whole time.
