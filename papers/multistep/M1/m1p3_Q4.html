Q4 — Particle $P$: $\mathbf{a} = (-\lambda\mathbf{i} + 2\lambda\mathbf{j})$, find $\lambda$ and bearing

Question

Particle $P$ moves with constant acceleration $\mathbf{a} = (-\lambda\mathbf{i} + 2\lambda\mathbf{j})\ \text{m/s}^2$, where $\lambda > 0$. At $t = 0$, $P$ has velocity $(5\mathbf{i} - 8\mathbf{j})$ m/s. At $t = 5$ s, the speed of $P$ is $13$ m/s.

(a) Find $\mathbf{v}(5)$ in terms of $\mathbf{i}, \mathbf{j}$ and $\lambda$. [2]

(b) Show that $25\lambda^2 - 42\lambda - 16 = 0$. [3]

(c) Find the bearing on which $P$ moves at $t = 4$ s. [5]

[10 marks]

Part (a) — $\mathbf{v}(5)$ [2 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 M1 A1

Formula (constant acceleration):

$$\mathbf{v}(t) = \mathbf{v}_0 + \mathbf{a}\, t$$

Substitute $t = 5$:

$$\mathbf{v}(5) = (5\mathbf{i} - 8\mathbf{j}) + 5(-\lambda\mathbf{i} + 2\lambda\mathbf{j})$$

$\mathbf{v}(5) = (5 - 5\lambda)\mathbf{i} + (-8 + 10\lambda)\mathbf{j}$

Part (b) — Show the quadratic [3 marks] · 1 step

From (a): $\mathbf{v}(5) = (5-5\lambda)\mathbf{i} + (-8+10\lambda)\mathbf{j}$. Speed = $13$.
STEP 1/1 M1 M1 A1

Formula (speed):

$$|\mathbf{v}|^2 = v_i^2 + v_j^2$$

Substitute:

$$(5-5\lambda)^2 + (-8+10\lambda)^2 = 169$$

Expand each square:

$(5-5\lambda)^2 = 25 - 50\lambda + 25\lambda^2$

$(-8+10\lambda)^2 = 64 - 160\lambda + 100\lambda^2$

Sum: $125\lambda^2 - 210\lambda + 89 = 169$, so $125\lambda^2 - 210\lambda - 80 = 0$.

Divide by $5$:

$$\boxed{\,25\lambda^2 - 42\lambda - 16 = 0\,} \quad\blacksquare$$

Sign check: $(-8)^2 = 64$, cross-term $2(-8)(10\lambda) = -160\lambda$. Don’t drop the negative sign.
$25\lambda^2 - 42\lambda - 16 = 0$ ✓

Part (c) — Bearing at $t = 4$ [5 marks] · 3 steps

From (b): $25\lambda^2 - 42\lambda - 16 = 0$.
STEP 1/3 M1

Formula (quadratic):

$$\lambda = \frac{42 \pm \sqrt{42^2 + 4 \cdot 25 \cdot 16}}{2 \cdot 25} = \frac{42 \pm \sqrt{1764 + 1600}}{50} = \frac{42 \pm \sqrt{3364}}{50} = \frac{42 \pm 58}{50}$$

$\lambda > 0$ → take $+$: $\lambda = \tfrac{100}{50} = 2$.

$\lambda = 2$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $\lambda = 2$.
STEP 2/3 A1

Strategy: apply $\mathbf{v}(t) = \mathbf{v}_0 + \mathbf{a}t$ at $t = 4$, then substitute $\lambda = 2$ from STEP 1.

Formula:

$$\mathbf{v}(4) = \mathbf{v}_0 + 4\, \mathbf{a}$$

Substitute $\mathbf{v}_0 = 5\mathbf{i} - 8\mathbf{j}$, $\mathbf{a} = -\lambda\mathbf{i} + 2\lambda\mathbf{j}$:

$$\mathbf{v}(4) = (5\mathbf{i} - 8\mathbf{j}) + 4(-\lambda\mathbf{i} + 2\lambda\mathbf{j})$$

$$= (5 - 4\lambda)\mathbf{i} + (-8 + 8\lambda)\mathbf{j}$$

Using $\lambda = 2$ from STEP 1:

$\mathbf{i}$-component: $5 - 4(2) = 5 - 8 = -3$.

$\mathbf{j}$-component: $-8 + 8(2) = -8 + 16 = +8$.

$$\boxed{\;\mathbf{v}(4) = -3\mathbf{i} + 8\mathbf{j}\;}$$

Sense check: $|\mathbf{v}(4)| = \sqrt{9 + 64} = \sqrt{73} \approx 8.54$ m/s. Speed at $t=4$ is between $v_0$ ($\sqrt{89} \approx 9.43$) and $v(5) = 13$ — actually it’s decreasing then increasing as the velocity reverses sign in $\mathbf{i}$. Realistic.
$\mathbf{v}(4) = -3\mathbf{i} + 8\mathbf{j}$
↳ from STEP 2:From STEP 2: $\mathbf{v}(4) = -3\mathbf{i} + 8\mathbf{j}$.
STEP 3/3 M1 A1 A1

Strategy: convert $\mathbf{v}(4) = -3\mathbf{i} + 8\mathbf{j}$ to a bearing (measured clockwise from N). First identify the quadrant from the signs of the components, then compute the angle from N.

Quadrant: $\mathbf{i}$-component negative ($-3$, west) and $\mathbf{j}$-component positive ($+8$, north) → NW quadrant.

Angle from N (going west):

$$\tan\phi = \frac{|\mathbf{i}\text{-comp}|}{|\mathbf{j}\text{-comp}|} = \frac{3}{8}$$

$$\phi = \arctan\left(\tfrac{3}{8}\right) \approx 20.56°$$

Bearing for NW direction (going clockwise from N to the W-side direction means going almost all the way around):

$$\text{bearing} = 360° - \phi \approx 360° - 20.56° \approx 339.44°$$

$$\boxed{\;\text{bearing} \approx 339°\;}$$

Quadrant rules for bearing: NE → $\arctan(|i|/|j|)$; SE → $180° - $; SW → $180° + $; NW → $360° - $. Always sketch the arrow first — a wrong quadrant gives a wrong bearing by 90° or 180°, and that’s an A1 lost.
$\boxed{\,\text{bearing} \approx 339°\,}$
