Q2 — Rod with Two Strings (Largest Mass)

Question

Uniform rod AB, length $2a$, mass $M$, horizontal. Strings at C and D. Particle $m$ at B. (a) Find largest possible $m$. (b) Given $m = M/2$, find $T_C$.

[7 marks]

Solution 3 steps

STEP 1/3

Setup — Understand "largest possible mass":

Uniform rod AB, length $2a$, mass $M$, horizontal. Strings at C ($AC = \frac{2a}{5}$) and D ($DB = \frac{3a}{5}$). Particle of mass $m$ at B.

Critical insight: "Largest/smallest possible mass" = one tension goes to zero (limiting equilibrium). At the limit, one string is about to go slack.

Phuc's error: (1) Used wrong moment arm — $\frac{2}{5}a$ for particle at B about C, should be $\frac{8}{5}a$. (2) Did not recognise that "largest mass" ⟹ $T_D = 0$.
Key: Largest $m$ ⟹ $T_D = 0$ (string at D about to go slack)
↳ from STEP 1:$T_D = 0$ at limiting case
STEP 2/3

Part (a) — Moments about D with $T_D = 0$:

Distances from D:

• Centre of rod: at $a$ from A, so $\frac{2a}{5}$ left of D (D is at $\frac{7a}{5}$ from A)

• Point B: at $2a$ from A, so $\frac{3a}{5}$ right of D

Taking moments about D:

$$Mg \cdot \frac{2}{5}a = mg \cdot \frac{3}{5}a$$

$$m = \frac{2M}{3}$$

$m_{\max} = \boxed{\frac{2M}{3}}$
↳ from STEP 2:$m = M/2$, distances from D
STEP 3/3

Part (b) — With $m = M/2$, find $T_C$:

Moments about D (distance from C to D = $a$):

$$T_C \cdot a = Mg \cdot \frac{2}{5}a - \frac{M}{2}g \cdot \frac{3}{5}a$$

$$T_C = \frac{2Mg}{5} - \frac{3Mg}{10} = \frac{Mg}{10}$$

Moment arm rule: Always measure from the pivot point. Draw ALL distances from the pivot. Distance = |position of force − position of pivot|.
$T_C = \boxed{\frac{Mg}{10}}$
