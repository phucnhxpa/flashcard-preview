Q6 — Ships P and Q: verify 13:30 collision; new path; distance at 14:30

Question

Ship $P$ has position $(15\mathbf{i} + 36\mathbf{j})$ km at 12:00 and $(20\mathbf{i} + 34\mathbf{j})$ km at 12:30. Ship $Q$ moves with $\mathbf{q}(t) = (42 - 8t)\mathbf{i} + (9 + 14t)\mathbf{j}$ km, with $t$ in hours after 12:00.

(a) Show $\mathbf{v}_P = (10\mathbf{i} - 4\mathbf{j})$ km/h. [2]

(b) Find $\mathbf{p}(t)$. [2]

(c)(i) Verify the ships collide at 13:30. (ii) Find collision position. [4]

(d) At 12:30, $Q$ instead changes to due North at $15$ km/h ($P$ unchanged). Find the exact distance between $P$ and $Q$ at 14:30. [7]

[15 marks]

Part (a) — Show $\mathbf{v}_P$ [2 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 M1 A1

From 12:00 to 12:30 = $0.5$ h.

Formula:

$$\mathbf{v}_P = \dfrac{\mathbf{r}_P(0.5) - \mathbf{r}_P(0)}{0.5}$$

Substitute:

$$\mathbf{v}_P = \frac{(20-15)\mathbf{i} + (34-36)\mathbf{j}}{0.5} = \frac{5\mathbf{i} - 2\mathbf{j}}{0.5}$$

$\mathbf{v}_P = 10\mathbf{i} - 4\mathbf{j}$ km/h ✓

Part (b) — $\mathbf{p}(t)$ [2 marks] · 1 step

From (a): $\mathbf{v}_P = 10\mathbf{i} - 4\mathbf{j}$ km/h. $P$’s position at 12:00 (from question): $\mathbf{r}_P(0) = 15\mathbf{i} + 36\mathbf{j}$ km.
STEP 1/1 M1 A1

Formula:

$$\mathbf{p}(t) = \mathbf{r}_P(0) + \mathbf{v}_P\, t$$

Substitute:

$$\mathbf{p}(t) = (15 + 10t)\mathbf{i} + (36 - 4t)\mathbf{j}$$

$\mathbf{p}(t) = (15+10t)\mathbf{i} + (36-4t)\mathbf{j}$

Part (c) — Collision at 13:30 [4 marks] · 1 step

From (b): $\mathbf{p}(t) = (15+10t)\mathbf{i} + (36-4t)\mathbf{j}$. From question: $\mathbf{q}(t) = (42-8t)\mathbf{i} + (9+14t)\mathbf{j}$. 13:30 → $t = 1.5$ h.
STEP 1/1 M1 A1 A1 A1

$\mathbf{p}(1.5) = (15 + 15)\mathbf{i} + (36 - 6)\mathbf{j} = 30\mathbf{i} + 30\mathbf{j}$

$\mathbf{q}(1.5) = (42 - 12)\mathbf{i} + (9 + 21)\mathbf{j} = 30\mathbf{i} + 30\mathbf{j}$ ✓

Equal vectors at $t = 1.5$ → they collide at $(30\mathbf{i} + 30\mathbf{j})$ km.

$\boxed{\,\text{Collision at } (30\mathbf{i} + 30\mathbf{j})\ \text{km},\ t = 1.5\ \text{h}\,}$

Part (d) — Distance at 14:30 (avoidance) [7 marks] · 2 steps

At 12:30 ($t=0.5$): $\mathbf{q}(0.5) = (42-4)\mathbf{i} + (9+7)\mathbf{j} = 38\mathbf{i} + 16\mathbf{j}$. After 12:30, $Q$ goes due N at $15$ km/h. From 12:30 to 14:30 = $2$ hours.
STEP 1/2 M1 A1 M1 A1

$Q$ at 14:30 (due N at 15 km/h for 2 h from $(38, 16)$):

$$\mathbf{q}(14\!:\!30) = 38\mathbf{i} + (16 + 30)\mathbf{j} = 38\mathbf{i} + 46\mathbf{j}$$

$P$ at 14:30 ($P$ unchanged, $t = 2.5$ from 12:00):

$$\mathbf{p}(2.5) = (15 + 25)\mathbf{i} + (36 - 10)\mathbf{j} = 40\mathbf{i} + 26\mathbf{j}$$

$Q_\text{14:30} = 38\mathbf{i} + 46\mathbf{j}$, $P_\text{14:30} = 40\mathbf{i} + 26\mathbf{j}$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $Q_{14:30} = 38\mathbf{i} + 46\mathbf{j}$, $P_{14:30} = 40\mathbf{i} + 26\mathbf{j}$.
STEP 2/2 M1 A1 A1

$\mathbf{p} - \mathbf{q} = (40-38)\mathbf{i} + (26-46)\mathbf{j} = 2\mathbf{i} - 20\mathbf{j}$

Formula (magnitude):

$$d = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}$$

Substitute:

$$d = \sqrt{4 + 400} = \sqrt{404} = \sqrt{4 \cdot 101} = 2\sqrt{101}\ \text{km}$$

$\boxed{\,d = 2\sqrt{101}\ \text{km} \approx 20.1\ \text{km}\,}$
