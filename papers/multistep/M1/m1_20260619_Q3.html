Q3 — Speed-Time Graphs & SUVAT

Question (technique block)

Three connected items covering speed-time graph interpretation and SUVAT, the skills behind the kinematics question on this paper.

(a) State what the gradient and the area represent on a speed-time graph, and label the key quantities.   (b) A car accelerates from rest at $2\;\text{m/s}^2$ for $5$ s, then decelerates at $4\;\text{m/s}^2$ to rest. Sketch the speed-time graph and find the total distance.   (c) A particle is projected upward at $15\;\text{m/s}$. How long until it returns to the start? ($g = 9.8\;\text{m/s}^2$)   (d) When is SUVAT valid and when is it NOT?

[Technique · 6 steps total]

Harry emphasised LABELING forces and diagrams. On a speed-time graph the axes, peak speed, segment accelerations, and durations must all be labelled — unlabelled sketches lose method marks.
Annotated Diagram — Speed-Time Graph

Part (a) — Read a speed-time graph [1 step]

A speed-time graph plots speed on the vertical axis against time on the horizontal axis. Two geometric quantities have direct physical meanings.
STEP 1/1gradient & area

Strategy. Gradient of a segment = acceleration ($a = \Delta v / \Delta t$). Area under the graph = distance travelled ($\int v\,dt$). A horizontal line = constant speed ($a=0$); a straight sloped line = constant acceleration.

Labelling checklist (Harry's rule)

  • Label the axes: speed vs time (not velocity, unless direction matters).
  • Label key values on each segment: the acceleration value, the time duration, the peak speed.
  • State the shape of each segment (constant $a$ $\Rightarrow$ straight line).
Phuc's mistake. Unlabelled sketches — no peak speed, no segment durations. Harry: "make sure you label the diagram… that's where the method marks come from."
Key: gradient $= a$; area $= s$ (distance).

Part (b) — Two-phase motion: sketch & total distance [2 steps]

Phase 1: from rest, $a = +2\;\text{m/s}^2$ for $t = 5$ s. Phase 2: decelerate at $4\;\text{m/s}^2$ to rest. The speed-time graph is two triangles back-to-back.
STEP 1/2phase 1 — accelerate

Strategy. Use $v = u + at$ to find the peak speed, then $s = \tfrac12(u+v)t$ for the area of the first triangle.

Phase 1

$$v = 0 + 2(5) = 10\;\text{m/s},\qquad s_1 = \tfrac12(0+10)(5) = 25\;\text{m}.$$
↳ from STEP 1: peak speed $10\;\text{m/s}$, $s_1 = 25$ m.
STEP 2/2phase 2 — decelerate to rest

Strategy. Use $v = u + at$ with $v=0$, $u=10$, $a=-4$ to find the deceleration time, then the second triangle area.

Phase 2

$$0 = 10 - 4t \;\Rightarrow\; t = 2.5\;\text{s},\qquad s_2 = \tfrac12(10+0)(2.5) = 12.5\;\text{m}.$$

Total

$$s_{\text{total}} = s_1 + s_2 = 25 + 12.5 = 37.5\;\text{m}.$$

Sense check. Two triangles on the graph: a tall one (area 25) and a shorter, steeper one (area 12.5). The steeper second triangle reflects the larger deceleration magnitude.

Answer (b): $\;s_{\text{total}} = 37.5\;\text{m}$ (two triangles).

Part (c) — Projectile returns to start [2 steps]

Upward projection at $15\;\text{m/s}$, $g = 9.8\;\text{m/s}^2$, returns to the start height. Take $a = -9.8$ (downward positive convention reversed for upward motion).
STEP 1/2time to the top

Strategy. At the top, $v = 0$. Use $v = u + at$ with $u = 15$, $a = -9.8$.

Time up

$$t_{\text{up}} = \frac{v - u}{a} = \frac{0 - 15}{-9.8} \approx 1.53\;\text{s}.$$
↳ from STEP 1: $t_{\text{up}} \approx 1.53$ s.
STEP 2/2double (symmetry) — or solve $s=0$

Strategy. By symmetry the time down equals the time up. Alternatively, solve $s = 0 = 15t - 4.9t^2$ directly.

Total time

$$t_{\text{total}} = 2\,t_{\text{up}} \approx 2(1.53) = 3.06\;\text{s}.$$

Sense check. Direct route: $t(15 - 4.9t) = 0 \Rightarrow t = 0$ (start) or $t = 15/4.9 \approx 3.06$ s (return). Both routes agree ✓.

Answer (c): $\;t \approx 3.06\;\text{s}$.

Part (d) — When SUVAT is valid [1 step]

SUVAT (the five kinematic equations) all assume a single constant acceleration throughout the motion.
STEP 1/1constant-a assumption

Strategy. State the assumption and the alternative.

SUVAT requires CONSTANT acceleration. If $a$ varies with time (e.g. air resistance $\propto v$, a rocket burning fuel), SUVAT is invalid — use calculus: $a = dv/dt$, $v = ds/dt$, and integrate. Using SUVAT with variable $a$ gives wrong answers.

Five equations, one link. $v=u+at$; $s=ut+\tfrac12 at^2$; $v^2=u^2+2as$; $s=\tfrac12(u+v)t$; $s=vt-\tfrac12 at^2$. The linking variable is TIME — it appears in all five. Identify which 3 of $\{s,u,v,a,t\}$ you know, then pick the equation with the other two.
Rule: constant $a$ $\Rightarrow$ SUVAT; variable $a$ $\Rightarrow$ calculus.
