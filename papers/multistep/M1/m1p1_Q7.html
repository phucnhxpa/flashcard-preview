Q7 — Ships A and B: collision check + bearing 225°

Question

At noon, ship $A$ has position $\mathbf{r}_A = 20\mathbf{j}$ km and velocity $\mathbf{v}_A = 40\mathbf{i}$ km/h (due east). Ship $B$ has position $\mathbf{r}_B = (10\mathbf{i} + 5\mathbf{j})$ km and moves at $20$ km/h on a bearing $\alpha$, where $\tan\alpha = 4/3$.

(a) Find $\mathbf{v}_B$ in $\mathbf{i},\mathbf{j}$ form. [2]

(b) Position of $B$ at time $t$ hours after noon. [2]

(c) Show $\overrightarrow{AB} = (10-24t)\mathbf{i} + (12t-15)\mathbf{j}$ km. [2]

(d) Show that the ships do not collide. [3]

(e) Distance between them when bearing of $B$ from $A$ is $225°$. [4]

[13 marks]

Part (a) — $\mathbf{v}_B$ in components [2 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 M1 A1

Strategy: a bearing $\alpha$ tells me the direction $B$ moves (clockwise from N). I need $\sin\alpha$ and $\cos\alpha$ to split velocity into $\mathbf{i}$ (east) and $\mathbf{j}$ (north) components.

Find $\sin\alpha$, $\cos\alpha$ from $\tan\alpha = 4/3$: build a right triangle with opposite $= 4$, adjacent $= 3$. Hypotenuse:

$$h = \sqrt{4^2 + 3^2} = \sqrt{25} = 5$$

So: $\sin\alpha = \tfrac{\text{opp}}{\text{hyp}} = \tfrac{4}{5}$, $\cos\alpha = \tfrac{\text{adj}}{\text{hyp}} = \tfrac{3}{5}$ (a 3–4–5 triangle).

Formula (bearing → components):

$$\mathbf{v} = v\sin(\text{bearing})\,\mathbf{i} + v\cos(\text{bearing})\,\mathbf{j}$$

(Bearings are measured clockwise from N → east-component uses $\sin$, north-component uses $\cos$ — opposite of standard trig.)

Substitute $v = 20$, $\sin\alpha = 4/5$, $\cos\alpha = 3/5$:

$$\mathbf{v}_B = 20 \cdot \tfrac{4}{5}\,\mathbf{i} + 20 \cdot \tfrac{3}{5}\,\mathbf{j} = 16\mathbf{i} + 12\mathbf{j}$$

$$\boxed{\;\mathbf{v}_B = 16\mathbf{i} + 12\mathbf{j}\ \text{km/h}\;}$$

Trap (recurs from Q2): bearings are clockwise from N, so the $\mathbf{i}$-component (east) uses $\sin$ and $\mathbf{j}$ (north) uses $\cos$ — opposite of standard trig. Sense check: $|\mathbf{v}_B| = \sqrt{16^2 + 12^2} = \sqrt{400} = 20$ km/h ✓ (matches given speed).
$\boxed{\,\mathbf{v}_B = 16\mathbf{i} + 12\mathbf{j}\ \text{km/h}\,}$

Part (b) — Position of $B$ at time $t$ [2 marks] · 1 step

From (a): $\mathbf{v}_B = 16\mathbf{i} + 12\mathbf{j}$ km/h. Initial position (from question): $\mathbf{r}_B(0) = 10\mathbf{i} + 5\mathbf{j}$ km.
STEP 1/1 M1 A1

Strategy: with constant velocity, position is linear in time. Just plug into the standard kinematics formula — then collect $\mathbf{i}$ and $\mathbf{j}$ components separately.

Formula (constant velocity):

$$\mathbf{r}(t) = \mathbf{r}_0 + \mathbf{v}\,t$$

Substitute $\mathbf{r}_0 = 10\mathbf{i} + 5\mathbf{j}$ (from question) and $\mathbf{v}_B = 16\mathbf{i} + 12\mathbf{j}$ (from STEP 1):

$$\mathbf{s}(t) = (10\mathbf{i} + 5\mathbf{j}) + (16\mathbf{i} + 12\mathbf{j})\, t$$

Collect $\mathbf{i}$ and $\mathbf{j}$ components:

$\;\;\mathbf{i}$-component: $10 + 16t$

$\;\;\mathbf{j}$-component: $5 + 12t$

$$\boxed{\;\mathbf{s}(t) = (10 + 16t)\mathbf{i} + (5 + 12t)\mathbf{j}\;}$$

Sense check: at $t = 0$, $\mathbf{s}(0) = 10\mathbf{i} + 5\mathbf{j}$ ✓ (matches initial position). The $t$-coefficients are $\mathbf{v}_B$ ✓.
$\mathbf{s}(t) = (10 + 16t)\mathbf{i} + (5 + 12t)\mathbf{j}$

Part (c) — Show $\overrightarrow{AB}$ [2 marks] · 1 step

$\mathbf{r}_A(t) = (0 + 40t)\mathbf{i} + 20\mathbf{j} = 40t\,\mathbf{i} + 20\mathbf{j}$.
STEP 1/1 M1 A1

Strategy: the displacement from $A$ to $B$ is $\mathbf{r}_B - \mathbf{r}_A$ (head minus tail). Compute each ship’s position vector at time $t$, then subtract component-wise.

Position of $A$ at time $t$: $\mathbf{r}_A(0) = 20\mathbf{j}$, $\mathbf{v}_A = 40\mathbf{i}$, so $\mathbf{r}_A(t) = 40t\,\mathbf{i} + 20\mathbf{j}$.

Position of $B$ at time $t$ (from STEP 2 of part b): $\mathbf{r}_B(t) = (10 + 16t)\mathbf{i} + (5 + 12t)\mathbf{j}$.

Formula:

$$\overrightarrow{AB}(t) = \mathbf{r}_B(t) - \mathbf{r}_A(t)$$

Subtract component-wise:

$\;\;\mathbf{i}$-component: $(10 + 16t) - 40t = 10 - 24t$

$\;\;\mathbf{j}$-component: $(5 + 12t) - 20 = 12t - 15$

$$\boxed{\;\overrightarrow{AB}(t) = (10 - 24t)\mathbf{i} + (12t - 15)\mathbf{j}\;} \quad\blacksquare$$

Sense check: at $t = 0$, $\overrightarrow{AB}(0) = 10\mathbf{i} - 15\mathbf{j}$. That’s $B$ to the east and south of $A$ — consistent with $B$’s initial position $(10, 5)$ vs $A$’s $(0, 20)$ ✓.
$\overrightarrow{AB} = (10-24t)\mathbf{i} + (12t-15)\mathbf{j}$ ✓

Part (d) — No collision [3 marks] · 1 step

From (c): $\overrightarrow{AB}(t) = (10-24t)\mathbf{i} + (12t-15)\mathbf{j}$ km.
STEP 1/1 M1 A1 A1

Strategy: at the moment of collision, $A$ and $B$ are at the same point, so $\overrightarrow{AB}(t) = \mathbf{0}$. For a 2D vector to be zero, BOTH components must vanish at the SAME $t$. Solve each component separately and check if $t$ matches.

Set $\mathbf{i}$-component $= 0$:

$$10 - 24t = 0 \;\Rightarrow\; t = \tfrac{10}{24} = \tfrac{5}{12}\ \text{h}$$

Set $\mathbf{j}$-component $= 0$:

$$12t - 15 = 0 \;\Rightarrow\; t = \tfrac{15}{12} = \tfrac{5}{4}\ \text{h}$$

Compare: $\tfrac{5}{12} \ne \tfrac{5}{4}$ — no single $t$ makes both components zero simultaneously.

$$\boxed{\;\text{Ships do NOT collide.}\;} \quad\blacksquare$$

Geometric interpretation: the $\mathbf{i}$ and $\mathbf{j}$ components represent the east-west and north-south separations. They both reach zero, but at different times — i.e. the ships cross each other’s east-west line first ($t = 5/12$) and then later cross north-south ($t = 5/4$), but never at the same instant.
No common $t$ → never collide.

Part (e) — Bearing $225° \Rightarrow$ distance [4 marks] · 2 steps

From (c): $\overrightarrow{AB}(t) = (10-24t)\mathbf{i} + (12t-15)\mathbf{j}$ km.
STEP 1/2225° = SW

Strategy: 'bearing of $B$ from $A$ is $225°$' fixes the direction of $\overrightarrow{AB}$. Convert this bearing to a constraint on the components, then solve for $t$.

Decode bearing $225°$:

$\;\;225° = 180° + 45°$ → due SW (45° west of south).

SW direction: both $-\mathbf{i}$ (west) and $-\mathbf{j}$ (south), with equal magnitudes (since the angle to S equals the angle to W, both 45°).

Constraint: $\mathbf{i}$-component = $\mathbf{j}$-component, both negative:

$$10 - 24t \;=\; 12t - 15$$

Solve for $t$:

$$10 + 15 = 12t + 24t$$

$$25 = 36t \;\Rightarrow\; t = \tfrac{25}{36}\ \text{h}$$

$$\boxed{\;t = \tfrac{25}{36}\ \text{h}\;}$$

Quick alternative check: "bearing $\beta$" means $\tan\beta = (\mathbf{i}\text{-comp})/(\mathbf{j}\text{-comp})$ with sign care. For $\beta = 225°$, $\tan = 1$, giving $\mathbf{i}$-comp $= \mathbf{j}$-comp — same equation. And both should be negative, confirmed in the next step.
$t = 25/36$ h
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $t = 25/36$ h (bearing $225°$ constraint).
STEP 2/2 M1 A1 A1 A1

Strategy: with $t$ from STEP 1, substitute back to get the actual components of $\overrightarrow{AB}$ at that moment, then compute the magnitude (distance) via Pythagoras.

Using $t = 25/36$ from STEP 1, compute $\mathbf{i}$-component:

$$10 - 24 \cdot \tfrac{25}{36} = 10 - \tfrac{600}{36} = 10 - \tfrac{50}{3} = \tfrac{30 - 50}{3} = -\tfrac{20}{3}$$

Compute $\mathbf{j}$-component:

$$12 \cdot \tfrac{25}{36} - 15 = \tfrac{300}{36} - 15 = \tfrac{25}{3} - \tfrac{45}{3} = -\tfrac{20}{3}$$

Both components equal to $-\tfrac{20}{3}$ ✓ (matches the SW-bearing constraint, both negative and equal).

Formula (magnitude):

$$|\overrightarrow{AB}| = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}$$

Substitute:

$$|\overrightarrow{AB}| = \sqrt{\left(-\tfrac{20}{3}\right)^2 + \left(-\tfrac{20}{3}\right)^2} = \sqrt{2 \cdot \tfrac{400}{9}} = \tfrac{20}{3}\sqrt{2}$$

$$\boxed{\;|\overrightarrow{AB}| = \tfrac{20\sqrt{2}}{3} \approx 9.43\ \text{km}\;}$$

Sense check: at $t = 25/36 \approx 0.69$ h, both ships have been moving for about 41 minutes. The distance between them is roughly 9.4 km — plausible for ships travelling at 20–40 km/h. Tip: when both components are equal, the magnitude is just $\sqrt{2}$ times one of them — saves a step.
$\boxed{\,|\overrightarrow{AB}| = \dfrac{20\sqrt{2}}{3} \approx 9.43\ \text{km}\,}$
