Q4 — Moments: Two Tilting Scenarios

Question

A non-uniform branch $AB$ has length $1.5$ m and weight $W$. It rests on supports at $C$ and $D$, with $AC = 0.24$ m and $DB = 0.36$ m. The centre of mass of the branch is $x$ m from $C$ (measured along the branch towards $B$).

(i) When a $150$ N force is applied vertically UPWARD at $B$, the branch is on the point of tilting about $C$. Find $Wx$.

(ii) When a $225$ N force is applied vertically DOWNWARD at $B$, the branch is on the point of tilting about $D$. Find $W(0.9 - x)$.

(iii) Hence find $W$ and $x$.

[Total: 12 marks]

Phuc's mistakes: (1) wrong sign handling in moment equations — mixing CW and ACW; (2) using ONE diagram for both tilting cases. Harry's rule: draw a FRESH diagram for each case, labelled with which reaction is zero.

Part (i) — Tilting about C, 150 N UP at B [4 marks] · 2 steps

At the point of tilting about $C$, the reaction at the OTHER support ($D$) becomes ZERO. Only the pivot's reaction, the weight, and the applied force contribute to the moment equation about $C$.
STEP 1/2draw a fresh diagram, identify which R = 0

Strategy. For tilting about $C$: set $R_D = 0$. Draw a SEPARATE diagram showing only $R_C$, $W$ (down, at distance $x$ right of $C$), and the $150$ N upward force at $B$ (distance $1.5 - 0.24 = 1.26$ m right of $C$).

Phuc's mistake (shared diagram). He reused one diagram for both tilting cases and mixed up which reaction was zero. Harry: "separate diagrams per case — each case has different forces and a different pivot." Draw a fresh diagram, labelled "$R_D = 0$" for this case.
↳ from STEP 1: $R_D = 0$; pivot at $C$; arm to $W$ is $x$; arm to 150 N is $1.26$ m.
STEP 2/2take moments about C with signed directions

Strategy. Choose a rotation convention (e.g. clockwise = positive). Weight $W$ down at $x$ right of $C$ $\Rightarrow$ CW moment $= +Wx$. The 150 N up at $1.26$ m right of $C$ $\Rightarrow$ ACW moment $= -150(1.26)$. Equilibrium $\Rightarrow$ sum $= 0$.

Moments about C

$$Wx - 150(1.26) = 0 \;\Rightarrow\; Wx = 150 \times 1.26 = 189.$$
Phuc's mistake (sign handling). He confused which force is CW vs ACW. Systematic method: write each moment $=$ force $\times$ perpendicular distance, with sign from CW $(+)$ / ACW $(-)$. Never mix CW and ACW signs in the same equation.

Sense check. The 150 N upward force at $B$ lifts the right end, rotating the branch ACW about $C$; the weight resists with a CW moment. They balance at the tipping point. ✓

Equation (i): $\;Wx = 189$.

Part (ii) — Tilting about D, 225 N DOWN at B [4 marks] · 2 steps

Now the branch tilts about $D$, so $R_C = 0$ (NOT $R_D$). Draw a FRESH diagram — do not reuse the part (i) diagram. Distances are measured from $D$, the new pivot.
STEP 1/2fresh diagram, R_C = 0, distances from D

Strategy. Set $R_C = 0$. Pivot is now $D$. Compute the position of $D$ along the branch: $AD = AB - DB = 1.5 - 0.36 = 1.14$ m, so $CD = 1.14 - 0.24 = 0.9$ m. The CoM is $x$ m right of $C$, hence $(0.9 - x)$ m LEFT of $D$. The 225 N force at $B$ is $DB = 0.36$ m RIGHT of $D$.

Why a fresh diagram. The pivot changed ($C \to D$) and the zero reaction changed ($R_D \to R_C$). Reusing the old diagram is the single biggest source of sign errors here. Label the new diagram "$R_C = 0$, pivot $D$".
↳ from STEP 1: $R_C = 0$; pivot $D$; $W$ is $(0.9-x)$ m left of $D$; 225 N is $0.36$ m right of $D$.
STEP 2/2take moments about D with signs

Strategy. Choose CW = positive again. Weight $W$ down LEFT of $D$ $\Rightarrow$ ACW moment $= -W(0.9 - x)$. The 225 N down at $0.36$ m right of $D$ $\Rightarrow$ CW moment $= +225(0.36)$. Sum $= 0$.

Moments about D

$$-W(0.9 - x) + 225(0.36) = 0 \;\Rightarrow\; W(0.9 - x) = 225 \times 0.36 = 81.$$
Phuc's mistake (sign handling again). He mixed up which force gives CW vs ACW about $D$. A force to the LEFT of the pivot pulling DOWN gives ACW; a force to the RIGHT pulling DOWN gives CW. Always label direction with the moment, then assign the sign.

Sense check. The 225 N down at $B$ pushes the right end down, rotating CW about $D$; the weight to the left of $D$ resists with an ACW moment. They balance at the tipping point. ✓

Equation (ii): $\;W(0.9 - x) = 81$.

Part (iii) — Solve the two equations for W and x [4 marks] · 2 steps

Two equations, two unknowns: $Wx = 189$ and $W(0.9 - x) = 81$. Solve by substitution.
STEP 1/2substitute W = 189/x

Strategy. From equation (i), $W = 189/x$. Substitute into equation (ii) and solve for $x$.

Substitute

$$\frac{189}{x}(0.9 - x) = 81 \;\Rightarrow\; 189(0.9 - x) = 81x.$$ $$170.1 - 189x = 81x \;\Rightarrow\; 170.1 = 270x.$$ $$x = \frac{170.1}{270} = 0.63\;\text{m}.$$
↳ from STEP 1: $x = 0.63$ m.
STEP 2/2back-substitute for W; sense-check

Strategy. Substitute $x = 0.63$ into $W = 189/x$, then run the sense checks.

Solve for W

$$W = \frac{189}{0.63} = 300\;\text{N}.$$

Sense check. CoM is $0.63$ m right of $C$, i.e. $0.24 + 0.63 = 0.87$ m from $A$ — between the supports at $C$ ($0.24$ m) and $D$ ($1.14$ m) ✓. $W = 300$ N is positive ✓. If $x$ had fallen outside $[0, 1.5]$, something would be wrong.

Answer (iii): $\;x = 0.63\;\text{m}$, $\;W = 300\;\text{N}$.
