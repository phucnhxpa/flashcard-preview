Q3 — Inclined Plane with Horizontal Force

Question

Rough plane, angle $\alpha$, $\tan\alpha = 3/4$. Particle P, 2 kg, held by horizontal force $X$ N. $\mu = 0.5$. (a) Show $F = 0$ when $X = 14.7$. (b) Find smallest $X$.

[8 marks]

Solution 3 steps

STEP 1/3

Setup — Horizontal force on inclined plane:

Rough plane at angle $\alpha$ ($\tan\alpha = 3/4$). Particle P, 2 kg, held by horizontal force $X$ N. $\mu = 0.5$.

Key decomposition: A horizontal force $X$ has two components on the incline:

• Along plane: $X\cos\alpha$

• Perpendicular (into plane): $X\sin\alpha$ — this ADDS to the normal reaction

$\sin\alpha = 3/5$, $\cos\alpha = 4/5$ (from $\tan\alpha = 3/4$, Pythagorean triple)
↳ from STEP 1:Resolve along and perpendicular to plane
STEP 2/3

Part (a) — Show $F = 0$ when $X = 14.7$:

Along plane: $X\cos\alpha - 2g\sin\alpha - F = 0$

$$14.7 \times \frac{4}{5} - 2(9.8) \times \frac{3}{5} = 11.76 - 11.76 = 0$$

Therefore $F = 0$ ✓

$F = 0$ when $X = 14.7$ N ✓
↳ from STEP 2:$F = 0$ ⟹ need friction to prevent sliding
STEP 3/3

Part (b) — Find smallest $X$:

Perpendicular: $R = 2g\cos\alpha + X\sin\alpha = \frac{4(19.6)}{5} + \frac{3X}{5}$

Along plane (limiting friction, about to slide down):

$$\frac{4X}{5} - 11.76 = \mu R = \frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{4(19.6)}{5} + \frac{3X}{5}\right)$$

$$\frac{4X}{5} - 11.76 = 7.84 + \frac{3X}{10}$$

$$\frac{X}{2} = 19.6$$
