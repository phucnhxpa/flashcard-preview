Q1 — Uniform beam, two vertical ropes; find $T_D$ and $x$

Question

A uniform beam $AB$ has weight $24$ N and length $6$ m. It is suspended in a horizontal position by two vertical ropes attached at $C$ and $D$, where $AC = x$ m and $CD = 2$ m. The tension at $C$ is double the tension at $D$.

(i) Find the tension at $D$. [2]

(ii) Find the value of $x$. [3]

[5 marks]

Part (i) — Tension at $D$ [2 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 M1 A1

Let $T_D = R$, so $T_C = 2R$. Beam is in equilibrium vertically.

Formula (vertical equilibrium):

$$T_C + T_D = W$$

Substitute:

$$2R + R = 24 \;\Rightarrow\; 3R = 24 \;\Rightarrow\; R = 8$$

$\boxed{\,T_D = 8\ \text{N},\ T_C = 16\ \text{N}\,}$

Part (ii) — Find $x$ [3 marks] · 2 steps

From (i): $T_C = 16$ N, $T_D = 8$ N. Uniform beam → weight at midpoint, $3$ m from $A$.
STEP 1/2 M1

Strategy: take moments about a clean point. $A$ is convenient because no force acts there, so any unknown force at $A$ is automatically eliminated. Identify each force’s moment arm and direction (CW vs ACW) about $A$.

Distances from $A$ to each force’s line of action:

• $T_C$ (up at $C$): $AC = x$ m → turns the beam anticlockwise about $A$ (ACW).

• $T_D$ (up at $D$): $AD = AC + CD = x + 2$ m → ACW about $A$.

• Weight $24$ N (down at midpoint, since beam is uniform): $AG = 3$ m (half of $6$ m). A downward force to the right of $A$ turns the beam clockwise (CW).

Formula (moments balance, take moments about $A$):

$$\Sigma(\text{CW}) = \Sigma(\text{ACW})$$

Substitute:

$$24 \cdot 3 = T_C \cdot x + T_D \cdot (x+2) = 16x + 8(x+2)$$

$$\boxed{\;72 = 16x + 8(x+2)\;}$$

Why pick $A$? No force acts there — so it kills no unknown directly, but writing the equation is cleanest. Any pivot works; we just need three measurable distances.
$24 \cdot 3 = 16x + 8(x+2)$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $72 = 16x + 8(x+2)$.
STEP 2/2 M1 A1

Strategy: expand the bracket, collect like terms, isolate $x$. Watch the sign when moving the constant across.

From STEP 1: $72 = 16x + 8(x+2)$.

Distribute the $8$:

$$72 = 16x + 8x + 16$$

$$72 = 24x + 16$$

Subtract $16$ from both sides:

$$72 - 16 = 24x$$

$$56 = 24x$$

$$x = \tfrac{56}{24} = \tfrac{7}{3}\ \text{m}$$

$$\boxed{\;x = \tfrac{7}{3}\ \text{m} \approx 2.33\ \text{m}\;}$$
