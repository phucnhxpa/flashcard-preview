Q4 — Non-uniform rod on a step, find smallest $M$ and $R_C$

Question

A non-uniform rod $AB$ of length $6.5$ m and mass $1.2$ kg has its centre of mass $3$ m from $A$. The rod rests horizontally on a step at point $C$ where $CB = 1.5$ m (so $AC = 5$ m). A $4$ kg particle is attached at $B$, and a particle of mass $M$ kg is attached at $D$ where $AD = 0.5$ m. ($g = 9.8\,\text{m/s}^2$.)

(a) Find the smallest possible value of $M$ for the rod to remain in equilibrium. [3]

(b) The particles at $B$ and $D$ are removed. A new particle is placed at $E$, where $EB = 0.9$ m, and the rod is on the point of tilting about $C$. Find the magnitude of the force exerted on the rod at $C$. [3]

[6 marks]

Part (a) — Smallest $M$ [3 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2pivot rule

Strategy: 'smallest $M$' means I'm looking for the boundary between equilibrium and the rod tipping over. At that limit, the rod is on the verge of tilting — the reaction at the support that’s about to lift off becomes zero.

Which way does it tilt as $M$ shrinks? The mass at $D$ (very close to $A$) pulls the left end down. The mass at $B$ pulls the right end down. The pivot is $C$. If $M$ is too small, the right side wins and the rod tilts clockwise about $C$ — the $A$ end lifts up.

Limiting condition: rod just about to tilt clockwise about $C$ $\Rightarrow$ no contact at $A$ $\Rightarrow$ only support is the step at $C$.

Why pivot at $C$? Two reasons — see Phuc’s rule below.

$$\boxed{\;\text{Pivot} = C,\ R_A = 0\;}$$

Phuc's rule (verbatim): “(1) The pivot point should not include the force we want to find. (2) Pick a point that eliminates a variable.” Here pivoting at $C$ kills $R_C$ (no moment arm) — leaves only $M$ as unknown in the moments equation. ✓
Pivot = $C$. Reaction at $A$ = 0 at the limit.
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: Pivot $= C$, $R_A = 0$ at the tilt limit.
STEP 2/2 M1 A1 A1

Strategy: at the tilt limit, $R_A = 0$ and $R_C$ has zero moment arm about $C$ — so the moments equation about $C$ involves only the three weights (rod, $D$, $B$) and the single unknown $M$. Solve directly.

Compute distances from $C$ (which is $AC = 5$ m right of $A$):

• Centre of mass (CoM at $3$ m from $A$): $5 - 3 = 2$ m left of $C$. Weight $1.2g$ acts down on the left side $\Rightarrow$ tends to rotate rod clockwise (CW) about $C$.

• Particle at $D$ (at $0.5$ m from $A$): $5 - 0.5 = 4.5$ m left of $C$. Weight $Mg$ down on the left $\Rightarrow$ CW.

• Particle at $B$ (at $6.5$ m from $A$): $6.5 - 5 = 1.5$ m right of $C$. Weight $4g$ down on the right $\Rightarrow$ rotates anti-clockwise (ACW) about $C$.

Formula (moments balance, since rod is in equilibrium):

$$\Sigma(\text{CW moments}) = \Sigma(\text{ACW moments})$$

where each moment $= \text{force} \times \text{perpendicular distance}$.

Substitute:

$$\underbrace{Mg \cdot 4.5}_{D,\,\text{CW}} + \underbrace{1.2g \cdot 2}_{\text{CoM},\,\text{CW}} = \underbrace{4g \cdot 1.5}_{B,\,\text{ACW}}$$

Cancel $g$ on both sides:

$$4.5M + 2.4 = 6$$

$$4.5M = 6 - 2.4 = 3.6$$

$$M = \frac{3.6}{4.5} = 0.8$$

$$\boxed{\;M = 0.8\ \text{kg}\;}$$

Sense check: $D$ is far from $C$ (lever arm 4.5 m) so a small $M$ gives a big moment — making $M = 0.8$ kg believable. If we got $M$ negative or huge, that would signal a sign or distance error.
$\boxed{\,M = 0.8\ \text{kg}\,}$

Part (b) — Force on rod at $C$ (tilting at $E$) [3 marks] · 2 steps

Setup: particles at $B$ and $D$ removed. New particle (mass $m_E$) at $E$, $EB = 0.9$ m → $AE = 5.6$ m → $E$ is $0.6$ m right of $C$. On point of tilting about $C$ → reaction only at $C$.
STEP 1/2 M1

Strategy: on the point of tilting about $C$ means the rod is rotating (in the limit). Balance the only two moments about $C$.

Setup: particles at $B$ and $D$ are removed, so only:

• Rod’s own weight $1.2g$ at the CoM ($2$ m left of $C$).

• New particle (mass $m_E$) at $E$, which is $AE = 6.5 - 0.9 = 5.6$ m from $A$, so $5.6 - 5 = 0.6$ m right of $C$.

Rotation tendency about $C$:

• Rod’s weight on the left side, pulling down $\Rightarrow$ rotates CW about $C$.

• Particle on the right side, pulling down $\Rightarrow$ rotates ACW about $C$.

Formula (moments balance at tilt limit):

$$\Sigma(\text{CW}) = \Sigma(\text{ACW})$$

Substitute:

$$1.2g \cdot 2 = m_E\, g \cdot 0.6$$

$$2.4g = 0.6\, m_E\, g$$

$$m_E = \frac{2.4}{0.6} = 4\ \text{kg}$$

$$\boxed{\;m_E = 4\ \text{kg}\;}$$

Tilting rule: on the point of tilting about $X$ → reaction at all other supports = 0. Here $A$ has no contact at all (it’s in free space), so $C$ is the sole support — but $C$ contributes zero moment about itself. So the moments equation has only the weights.
$m_E = 4$ kg
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $m_E = 4$ kg.
STEP 2/2 M1 A1

Strategy: with $m_E$ found from STEP 1, the rod is now fully specified. The reaction $R_C$ at the step is the only upward force — it must support the entire weight of the rod plus particle. Use vertical equilibrium.

Forces on the rod (vertical):

• Rod’s weight $1.2g$ down.

• Particle’s weight $m_E\, g = 4g$ down (using $m_E = 4$ from STEP 1).

• Reaction $R_C$ at the step, up.

• No contact at $A$ (it’s overhanging) $\Rightarrow$ $R_A = 0$.

Formula (vertical equilibrium):

$$\Sigma F_y = 0 \;\Rightarrow\; R_C = (M_\text{rod} + m_E)\, g$$

Substitute $M_\text{rod} = 1.2$, $m_E = 4$, $g = 9.8$:

$$R_C = (1.2 + 4) \cdot 9.8 = 5.2 \cdot 9.8 = 50.96\ \text{N}$$

$$\boxed{\;R_C = 5.2g \approx 51\ \text{N}\;}$$

Sense check: $R_C$ holds up $5.2$ kg total — about the weight of a small bag of sugar plus a brick. $51$ N (≈5.2 kgf) is reasonable for a rod resting on a step.
$\boxed{\,R_C = 5.2g \approx 51\ \text{N}\,}$
