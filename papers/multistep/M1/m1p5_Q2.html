Q2 — Symmetric collision: A unchanged direction $\Rightarrow$ infer B

Question

Particles $A$ (mass $3m$) and $B$ (mass $m$) move along the same straight line in opposite directions, each at $1.5$ m/s. They collide directly. After the collision:

• The direction of motion of $A$ is unchanged.

• The difference between the speeds of $A$ and $B$ after the collision is $1$ m/s.

(a)(i) Find the speed of $A$ after the collision. (ii) Find the speed of $B$ after the collision. [5]

(b) Find the magnitude of the impulse exerted on $B$ by $A$, in terms of $m$. [3]

[8 marks]

Part (a) — Speeds after collision [5 marks] · 3 steps

STEP 1/3 M1

Strategy: 1D collision \u2014 set up a sign convention before any calculation. Pick a positive direction and write each velocity with a sign that reflects its direction.

Choose $A$’s initial direction as the positive direction ($+$).

Before the collision:

$\;\;u_A = +1.5$ m/s (positive: moving in chosen $+$ direction)

$\;\;u_B = -1.5$ m/s (negative: moving opposite to $A$)

The problem says $A$’s direction is unchanged after, so:

$\;\;v_A = V$ where $V > 0$ (still moving in $+$ direction)

$$\boxed{\;u_A = +1.5,\ u_B = -1.5,\ v_A = V > 0\;}$$

Sign discipline: momentum conservation involves vector quantities. Always assign signs based on direction \u2014 if you treat $u_B$ as $+1.5$ here, conservation will give nonsense.
$u_A = +1.5$, $u_B = -1.5$; $v_A = V > 0$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $u_A = +1.5$, $u_B = -1.5$, $v_A = V > 0$.
STEP 2/3physics check

If $A$’s direction is unchanged, $A$ pushed $B$ forward. So $B$ must now move in $A$’s direction (same sign, $+$).

Also, $B$ must travel at least as fast as $A$; otherwise $A$ would overtake $B$ — unphysical (they’d collide again).

So $v_B > v_A$, and $v_B - v_A = 1 \Rightarrow v_B = V + 1$.

Phuc's reasoning (verbatim): “The reason I choose $V + 1$: initially the two objects have opposite directions, and after the collision the direction of $B$ is the same as $A$’s. $A$ has to push $B$, so $B$ has to accelerate a little bit (i.e. faster than $A$).” That logic is correct — picking $V - 1$ would give $A$ catching up to $B$, which is unphysical.
$v_B = V + 1$ (same direction, faster than $A$)
↳ from STEP 2:From STEP 2: $v_B = V + 1$ (same direction as $A$, faster).
STEP 3/3 M1 A1 A1 A1

Strategy: momentum is conserved in any collision (no external impulse). Sub the four velocities (two pre, two post) into the conservation equation and solve for $V$.

Formula (conservation of momentum): $m_A u_A + m_B u_B = m_A v_A + m_B v_B$.

Substitute $m_A = 3m$, $m_B = m$, $u_A = 1.5$, $u_B = -1.5$, $v_A = V$, $v_B = V + 1$:

$\;\;3m(1.5) + m(-1.5) = 3m \cdot V + m(V + 1)$

Compute LHS:

$\;\;4.5m - 1.5m = 3m$

Expand RHS:

$\;\;3mV + mV + m = 4mV + m$

So:

$\;\;3m = 4mV + m$

Subtract $m$ from both sides, then divide by $m$:

$\;\;2 = 4V$

$\;\;V = 0.5$

So $v_A = 0.5$ m/s and $v_B = V + 1 = 1.5$ m/s, both in $A$’s original direction.

$$\boxed{\;v_A = 0.5\ \text{m/s},\ v_B = 1.5\ \text{m/s}\;}$$

Cancellation check: the $m$ should cancel from every term \u2014 if it doesn\u2019t, you\u2019ve dropped a mass. Also, $v_B > v_A$ ✓ (consistent with the physical reasoning that $B$ moves away faster).
$\boxed{\,v_A = 0.5\ \text{m/s},\ v_B = 1.5\ \text{m/s}\,}$

Part (b) — Impulse on $B$ [3 marks] · 1 step

From (a): taking $A$’s initial direction as $+$, $u_B = -1.5$ m/s (opposite to $A$ before), $v_B = +1.5$ m/s (now in $A$’s direction). $B$ has mass $m$.
STEP 1/1 M1 A1 A1

Strategy: impulse equals change in momentum. Compute it for $B$ alone (mass $m$), using the same sign convention as part (a).

Formula (impulse-momentum): $I = m(v - u)$.

Substitute $m_B = m$, $u_B = -1.5$, $v_B = +1.5$:

$\;\;I_B = m(v_B - u_B)$

$\;\;I_B = m(1.5 - (-1.5))$

$\;\;I_B = m(1.5 + 1.5)$

$\;\;I_B = 3m$ Ns (positive \u2014 in $A$’s direction)

Magnitude:

$$\boxed{\;|I_B| = 3m\ \text{Ns}\;}$$

Don\u2019t drop the sign on $u_B$: $B$\u2019s initial velocity is $-1.5$, not $+1.5$. The change is $\Delta v = 1.5 - (-1.5) = 3$, giving $|I_B| = 3m$. If you used $u_B = +1.5$ you\u2019d get $0$ \u2014 obviously wrong.
$\boxed{\,|I_B| = 3m\ \text{Ns}\,}$
