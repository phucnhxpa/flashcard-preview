Q1 — ESAT Modulus Inequalities & Interval Counting

ESAT warm-up (taken before M1 practice)

Three short ESAT-style items targeting Phuc's recurring modulus-inequality and integer-interval counting slips.

(a) Solve $|x-3| < 5$.   (b) Solve $|2x+1| \geq 7$.   (c) Count the integers $x$ with $|x-2| < 5.5$.

[ESAT warm-up · 5 steps total]

Phuc tends to drop the lower bound on $<$ inequalities and confuse $<$ with $\leq$ at the endpoints. Use the carry-in strip between steps; reveal each grey box only after attempting that part.

Part (a) — $|x-3| < 5$: double-inequality [2 steps]

The modulus inequality $|A| < b$ (with $b>0$) is equivalent to $-b < A < b$ — a single AND (between) region, NOT a single bound.
STEP 1/2split into a double inequality

Strategy. Apply $|A| < b \iff -b < A < b$ directly with $A = x-3$, $b = 5$.

Rewrite

$$|x-3| < 5 \;\iff\; -5 < x-3 < 5.$$
Phuc's mistake. He wrote only $x < 8$, dropping the lower bound. A modulus inequality always produces a DOUBLE inequality — two bounds joined by AND, never one. Harry red-boxed the missing $-5$.
↳ from STEP 1: $-5 < x-3 < 5$.
STEP 2/2add 3 to all three parts

Strategy. Add $3$ to the left, middle, and right of the double inequality simultaneously.

Solve

$$-5+3 < x < 5+3 \;\iff\; -2 < x < 8.$$

Sense check. The interval is symmetric about $3$ (radius $5$): $3-5=-2$, $3+5=8$. ✓

Answer (a): $\;-2 < x < 8$.

Part (b) — $|2x+1| \geq 7$: OR (union) of two regions [2 steps]

For $\geq$ the modulus splits into an OR (union), not an AND: $|A|\geq b \iff A\geq b \;\text{OR}\; A\leq -b$. This gives two outside regions, not a between region.
STEP 1/2split into two inequalities with OR

Strategy. Use the $\geq$ rule (union), keeping the sign of $A = 2x+1$ intact on each branch.

Split

$$|2x+1|\geq 7 \;\iff\; 2x+1 \geq 7 \;\;\text{OR}\;\; 2x+1 \leq -7.$$
Trap. Using a between-region ($-7 < 2x+1 < 7$) here is wrong — that is the $<$ rule, not the $\geq$ rule. $\geq$ always gives an OR / two outside pieces.
↳ from STEP 1: $2x+1 \geq 7$ OR $2x+1 \leq -7$.
STEP 2/2solve each branch

Strategy. Subtract $1$, divide by $2$ on each branch separately.

Solve

$$2x+1\geq 7 \Rightarrow 2x\geq 6 \Rightarrow x\geq 3.$$ $$2x+1\leq -7 \Rightarrow 2x\leq -8 \Rightarrow x\leq -4.$$

Sense check. Test $x=4$: $|9|=9\geq7$ ✓. Test $x=-5$: $|-9|=9\geq7$ ✓. Test $x=0$ (inside the gap): $|1|=1 \not\geq 7$ ✓ — confirms the union of two outside regions.

Answer (b): $\;x\geq 3 \;\text{OR}\; x\leq -4$.

Part (c) — Count integers with $|x-2| < 5.5$ [1 step]

First convert the modulus inequality to a between-region, then LIST the integers explicitly before counting. Phuc's recurring slip is to confuse $<$ with $\leq$ at the endpoints.
STEP 1/1convert, list, count

Strategy. $|x-2| < 5.5 \iff -5.5 < x-2 < 5.5 \iff -3.5 < x < 7.5$. The endpoints $-3.5$ and $7.5$ are NOT integers, so both $-3$ and $7$ are included — no boundary ambiguity here.

List the integers in $(-3.5,\,7.5)$

$$-3,\,-2,\,-1,\,0,\,1,\,2,\,3,\,4,\,5,\,6,\,7.$$
Phuc's mistake (interval counting). He tends to miss boundary integers — confusing $<$ with $\leq$. Fix: always WRITE OUT the integer list explicitly, then count. Underline whether each endpoint is included ($\leq$) or excluded ($<$) before listing.

Sense check. Count: $-3$ to $7$ inclusive is $7-(-3)+1 = 11$ integers. ✓

Answer (c): $\;11$ integers.
