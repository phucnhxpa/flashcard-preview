Q5 — Ring on rough vertical rod: friction direction + min/max

Question

A small ring of mass $0.2$ kg is threaded on a fixed rough vertical rod. A light inextensible string is attached to the ring; the string makes angle $\theta$ with the rod, where $\tan\theta = 5/12$ (so $\sin\theta = 5/13$, $\cos\theta = 12/13$). The string is taut. Take $g = 9.8\,\text{m/s}^2$.

For parts (a) and (b): tension $T = 10$ N; ring is in equilibrium.

(a) Find the magnitude of the friction force. [3]

(b) State the direction of the friction force. [1]

For part (c): $\mu = 1/4$. Find the range of $T$ for equilibrium. [8]

[12 marks]

Part (a) — Friction magnitude with $T = 10$ [3 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2 M1

Resolve along rod (vertical). String pulls along the rod with $T\cos\theta$:

$T\cos\theta = 10 \cdot \tfrac{12}{13} = \tfrac{120}{13} \approx 9.23$ N up.

Weight: $W = 0.2 \cdot 9.8 = 1.96$ N down.

$T\cos\theta > W$ → ring would slide up without friction → friction acts down.

Ring tends UP → friction DOWN.
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: Ring tends UP → friction acts DOWN.
STEP 2/2 M1 A1

Formula (vertical equilibrium along rod):

$$T\cos\theta - W - F = 0$$

Substitute:

$$F = T\cos\theta - W = \tfrac{120}{13} - 1.96$$

$$F \approx 9.23 - 1.96 \approx 7.27\ \text{N}$$

$\boxed{\,F \approx 7.27\ \text{N}\,}$

Part (b) — Friction direction [1 marks] · 1 step

From (a): $T\cos\theta = 120/13 \approx 9.23$ N up exceeds $W = 1.96$ N down → ring tends to slide UP the rod.
STEP 1/1 B1

Friction opposes the tendency of motion. Since the ring tends to move up the rod, friction acts down the rod.

$\boxed{\,\text{Friction acts down the rod}\,}$

Part (c) — Range of $T$ for equilibrium ($\mu = 1/4$) [8 marks] · 2 steps

$R = T\sin\theta$ (string’s horizontal pull = normal). $F_{\max} = \mu R = \tfrac{1}{4}T\sin\theta$.
STEP 1/2 M1 A1 M1 A1

For $T_{\min}$, gravity dominates. Ring tends DOWN → friction acts UP (helping tension):

Formula (vertical equilibrium, $F = \mu T\sin\theta$ at limit):

$$T\cos\theta + \mu T\sin\theta = W \;\Rightarrow\; T_{\min} = \dfrac{W}{\cos\theta + \mu\sin\theta}$$

Substitute $W = 1.96$, $\cos\theta = 12/13$, $\mu = 1/4$, $\sin\theta = 5/13$:

$$T_{\min} = \frac{1.96}{12/13 + (1/4)(5/13)} = \frac{1.96 \cdot 52}{53} \approx 1.92\ \text{N}$$

$T_{\min} \approx 1.92\ \text{N}$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $T_{\min} \approx 1.92$ N (lower bound found).
STEP 2/2 M1 A1

For $T_{\max}$, tension dominates. Ring tends UP → friction acts DOWN:

$$T\cos\theta - \mu T\sin\theta = W \;\Rightarrow\; T_{\max} = \dfrac{W}{\cos\theta - \mu\sin\theta}$$

Substitute:

$$T_{\max} = \frac{1.96}{12/13 - 5/52} = \frac{1.96 \cdot 52}{43} \approx 2.37\ \text{N}$$

$$\boxed{\,1.92 \le T \le 2.37\ \text{N}\,}$$
