Q4 — Non-uniform branch: two tilting cases give two equations

Question

A non-uniform branch $AB$ of length $1.5$ m and weight $W$ N rests in a horizontal position on two supports at $C$ and $D$, where $AC = 0.24$ m and $DB = 0.36$ m. The centre of mass of the branch is at distance $x$ m from $C$ (right of $C$).

(i) When a $150$ N upward force is applied at $B$, the branch is on the point of tilting about $C$.

(ii) When a $225$ N downward force is applied at $B$, the branch is on the point of tilting about $D$.

Find $W$ and $x$.

[8 marks]

Solution [8 marks] · 4 steps

STEP 1/4

Distances from $A$: $C$ at $0.24$, $D$ at $1.5 - 0.36 = 1.14$, $B$ at $1.5$. CoM at $0.24 + x$ from $A$.

Distances from $C$: CoM at $x$ right, $D$ at $0.9$ right, $B$ at $1.26$ right.

Distances from $D$: CoM at $(0.9 - x)$ left, $A$ at $1.14$ left, $B$ at $0.36$ right.

Distances tabulated above.
STEP 2/4 M1 A1 A1

Strategy: on the point of tilting about $C$, the support at $D$ loses contact \u2192 reaction $R_D = 0$. Take moments about $C$ \u2014 only the branch weight and the applied force at $B$ contribute.

Formula (moments balance about $C$): $\Sigma\text{CW} = \Sigma\text{ACW}$.

Forces about $C$ (with $R_D = 0$):

$\;\;\bullet$ Branch weight $W$ down at $x$ m right of $C$ \u2192 CW moment $= Wx$

$\;\;\bullet$ Applied $150$ N up at $B$, which is $1.26$ m right of $C$ \u2192 ACW moment $= 150 \cdot 1.26 = 189$

Balance:

$\;\;Wx = 189$

$$\boxed{\;Wx = 189\;}\quad\text{(eq 1)}$$

Tilting rule: on point of tilting about $C$ \u2192 reaction at $D$ = 0. Only branch weight + applied $150$ N enter the moments. The support at $C$ also has zero arm so it doesn\u2019t enter the equation.
$Wx = 189$ — (eq 1)
↳ from STEP 2:From STEP 2: $Wx = 189$ — (eq 1).
STEP 3/4 M1 A1 A1

Strategy: in case (ii), the branch tilts about $D$, so the support at $C$ loses contact \u2192 reaction $R_C = 0$. Take moments about $D$, with only the branch weight and the applied force at $B$ contributing.

Formula (moments balance about $D$): $\Sigma\text{CW} = \Sigma\text{ACW}$.

Forces about $D$ (with $R_C = 0$):

$\;\;\bullet$ Branch weight $W$ down at $(0.9 - x)$ m left of $D$ \u2192 ACW moment $= W(0.9 - x)$

$\;\;\bullet$ Applied $225$ N down at $B$, $0.36$ m right of $D$ \u2192 CW moment $= 225 \cdot 0.36 = 81$

Balance:

$\;\;W(0.9 - x) = 81$

$$\boxed{\;W(0.9 - x) = 81\;}\quad\text{(eq 2)}$$

Phuc\u2019s insight (verbatim): "At the point of tilting, we assume the contact at the other point is not there anymore. This question is different from earlier ones: we have to write two equations and solve." Two unknowns ($W$, $x$) \u2192 need two independent moment equations \u2014 one from each tilting case.
$W(0.9 - x) = 81$ — (eq 2)
↳ from STEP 3:From STEP 3: $W(0.9-x) = 81$ (eq 2). From STEP 2: $Wx = 189$ (eq 1).
STEP 4/4 M1 A1

Strategy: two equations, two unknowns. Solve (1) for $W$ in terms of $x$, substitute into (2), get a single equation in $x$, solve, then back-substitute.

From eq (1): $Wx = 189 \Rightarrow W = \dfrac{189}{x}$.

Substitute into eq (2):

$\;\;\dfrac{189}{x}(0.9 - x) = 81$

Multiply both sides by $x$:

$\;\;189(0.9 - x) = 81x$

Expand the LHS:

$\;\;170.1 - 189x = 81x$

Rearrange:

$\;\;170.1 = 81x + 189x = 270x$

Divide by $270$:

$\;\;x = \dfrac{170.1}{270} = 0.63$ m

Back-substitute into $W = 189/x$:

$\;\;W = \dfrac{189}{0.63} = 300$ N

$$\boxed{\;W = 300\ \text{N},\ x = 0.63\ \text{m}\;}$$

Sense check: $x = 0.63$ m places the CoM well within the rod (which is $1.5$ m long, with $C$ at $0.24$ m and $D$ at $1.14$ m from $A$) \u2014 between the two supports, as expected for a stable rest position.
$\boxed{\,W = 300\ \text{N},\ x = 0.63\ \text{m}\,}$
