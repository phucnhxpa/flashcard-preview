Q3 — Book ($0.2$ kg) falls $1.5$ m: Model A (free) vs Model B (resistance $R$)

Question

A book of mass $0.2$ kg is held at rest at a point $1.5$ m above a horizontal floor and then released.

(a) Model A: free fall (no resistance). Find the time to reach the floor. [3]

(b) Model B: constant resistance $R$ N opposes motion. The book reaches the floor in $0.6$ s. Find $R$. [5]

Take $g = 9.8\,\text{m/s}^2$.

[8 marks]

Part (a) — Time in free fall [3 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 M1 A1 A1

Strategy: Model A is free fall, so the only force is gravity, giving $a = g = 9.8\ \text{m/s}^2$. Released from rest means $u = 0$. Use the suvat that links $s$, $u$, $a$, $t$.

Formula (suvat): $s = ut + \tfrac{1}{2}at^2$.

List knowns: $u = 0$, $a = 9.8$, $s = 1.5$, find $t$.

Substitute:

$\;\;1.5 = (0)(t) + \tfrac{1}{2}(9.8)t^2$

$\;\;1.5 = 4.9\,t^2$

Divide by $4.9$:

$\;\;t^2 = \dfrac{1.5}{4.9} \approx 0.3061$

Take the positive square root:

$$\boxed{\;t \approx 0.553\ \text{s}\;}$$

Sign / root pitfall: $t^2 = 0.306$ has two roots $\pm 0.553$ \u2014 only the positive value makes physical sense for elapsed time. Also, do not use $g = 10$ unless the question says so; this paper specifies $g = 9.8$.
$\boxed{\,t \approx 0.553\ \text{s}\,}$

Part (b) — Find $R$ [5 marks] · 2 steps

Model B: time = $0.6$ s, $s = 1.5$, $u = 0$.
STEP 1/2 M1 A1

Strategy: the resistance reduces the net downward force, so $a < g$. We don’t know $a$ yet \u2014 but we know $s = 1.5$, $u = 0$, $t = 0.6$. Use suvat to back out $a$ first, then plug into N2L.

Formula (suvat): $s = ut + \tfrac{1}{2}at^2$, with $u = 0$ this becomes $s = \tfrac{1}{2}at^2$.

Substitute $s = 1.5$, $t = 0.6$:

$\;\;1.5 = \tfrac{1}{2}a(0.6)^2$

$\;\;1.5 = \tfrac{1}{2}a(0.36)$

$\;\;1.5 = 0.18\,a$

Divide by $0.18$:

$\;\;a = \dfrac{1.5}{0.18} = \dfrac{25}{3}$

$$\boxed{\;a = \dfrac{25}{3} \approx 8.33\ \text{m/s}^2\;}$$

Phuc\u2019s gap (per redo): set up $R = mg - ma$ correctly but didn\u2019t know how to find $a$ under Model B. Trick: use the given time + distance to compute $a$ via suvat, then N2L gives $R$. Sanity-check: $a < g$ as expected (resistance slows the fall).
$a = 25/3 \approx 8.33\ \text{m/s}^2$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $a = 25/3 \approx 8.33$ m/s$^2$.
STEP 2/2 M1 A1 A1

Strategy: the book has two forces on it \u2014 weight $mg$ down and resistance $R$ up. Net downward force = mass $\times$ acceleration.

Formula (Newton’s 2nd law, taking down positive): $mg - R = ma$.

Rearrange for $R$:

$\;\;R = mg - ma = m(g - a)$

Substitute $m = 0.2$, $g = 9.8$, $a = 25/3$:

$\;\;R = 0.2\,(9.8 - 25/3)$

Convert $9.8 = 29.4/3$:

$\;\;R = 0.2 \cdot \dfrac{29.4 - 25}{3} = 0.2 \cdot \dfrac{4.4}{3}$

$\;\;R = \dfrac{0.88}{3}$

$$\boxed{\;R \approx 0.293\ \text{N}\;}$$

Direction trap: resistance opposes motion. Book falls down \u2192 resistance acts up. So in the equation, $R$ enters with a minus sign on the LHS (or equivalently as a negative force in the down-positive convention).
$\boxed{\,R \approx 0.293\ \text{N}\,}$
