Q2 — Momentum & Impulse: Symmetric Collision

Question

Particles $A$ (mass $3m$) and $B$ (mass $m$) move in opposite directions, each at speed $1.5\;\text{m/s}$. They collide. After the collision $A$'s direction of motion is UNCHANGED. The speed of $B$ after the collision exceeds the speed of $A$ by $1\;\text{m/s}$.

(a) Find the speed of $A$ immediately after the collision.   (b) Find the magnitude of the impulse exerted on $B$ by $A$, and state the impulse exerted on $A$ by $B$.

[Total: 9 marks]

Phuc's mistake: dropping the sign on $u_B = -1.5$ in the impulse calculation, which makes the change in momentum appear to be zero. Assign a sign convention FIRST and keep it throughout.
Annotated Diagram — Before / After Collision

Part (a) — Conservation of momentum [5 marks] · 4 steps

Momentum is a VECTOR — conservation requires signs. Pick $A$'s initial direction as $+$. Then $u_A = +1.5$, $u_B = -1.5$ (opposite). After the collision $A$'s direction is unchanged, so $v_A = +V$ with $V>0$.
STEP 1/4set the sign convention

Strategy. Declare a positive direction BEFORE writing any equation. Choose the direction of $A$'s initial motion as $+$.

Sign convention

$$u_A = +1.5,\qquad u_B = -1.5 \quad(\text{opposite direction}).$$
Why this is non-negotiable. Momentum is a vector. Without signs, conservation gives nonsense. Harry: "you've got to be very careful with the signs." Assign signs before any calculation and never change mid-problem.
↳ from STEP 1: $u_A=+1.5$, $u_B=-1.5$; $v_A = +V$.
STEP 2/4express $v_B$ in terms of $V$

Strategy. $A$ pushes $B$ forward, so $B$ now moves in $A$'s ($+$) direction. Also $B$ must be FASTER than $A$ — otherwise $A$ would overtake $B$ and they would collide again (unphysical).

Use $v_B - v_A = 1$

$$v_B = V + 1 \quad (v_B > v_A,\;\text{both } +).$$
Phuc's reasoning (correct here). "A has to push B, so B has to accelerate a little bit." Choosing $V-1$ would make $A$ faster than $B$ — they'd collide again — unphysical. So $v_B = V+1$, not $V-1$.
↳ from STEP 2: $v_A = V$, $v_B = V+1$.
STEP 3/4apply conservation of momentum

Strategy. Total momentum before = total momentum after. Keep the signed velocities on both sides.

Conservation

$$3m(1.5) + m(-1.5) = 3m\cdot V + m\,(V+1).$$ $$4.5m - 1.5m = 3mV + mV + m.$$ $$3m = 4mV + m.$$
↳ from STEP 3: $3m = 4mV + m$.
STEP 4/4solve for V

Strategy. Cancel $m$ (it is non-zero) and solve the linear equation.

Solve

$$3m = 4mV + m \;\Rightarrow\; 2m = 4mV \;\Rightarrow\; V = \tfrac{2}{4} = 0.5.$$

Sense check. $v_A = 0.5$ m/s, $v_B = 1.5$ m/s. $m$ cancels from every term ✓. $v_B > v_A$ ✓ (no second collision). Both positive ✓ (same direction as $A$'s initial motion).

Answer (a): $\;v_A = 0.5\;\text{m/s}$ (direction unchanged), $v_B = 1.5\;\text{m/s}$.

Part (b) — Impulse on B by A (and on A by B) [4 marks] · 2 steps

Impulse $I = m(v-u)$ — change in momentum. For $B$: $u_B = -1.5$, $v_B = +1.5$, mass $m$. Keep the sign on $u_B$.
STEP 1/2impulse on B

Strategy. Apply $I_B = m(v_B - u_B)$ with the signed velocities from part (a).

Substitute

$$I_B = m\bigl(1.5 - (-1.5)\bigr) = m(3.0) = 3m\;\text{N\,s}.$$
Phuc's critical mistake (dropping the sign). Using $u_B = +1.5$ instead of $-1.5$ gives $I_B = m(1.5-1.5) = 0$ — saying no impulse at all, which is obviously wrong for a collision! The sign on the initial velocity determines the change. Always preserve the sign convention.

Sense check. Positive $I_B$ means the impulse on $B$ is in $A$'s direction ($+$) — consistent with $A$ pushing $B$ forward. ✓

↳ from STEP 1: $I_B = 3m\;\text{N\,s}$ (positive).
STEP 2/2Newton's 3rd Law

Strategy. By Newton's 3rd Law the impulse on $A$ by $B$ is equal in magnitude, opposite in direction: $I_A = -I_B$. Verify by computing directly.

Verify

$$I_A = 3m(v_A - u_A) = 3m(0.5 - 1.5) = 3m(-1) = -3m\;\text{N\,s}.$$

Sense check. $|I_A| = |I_B| = 3m$ ✓, opposite signs ✓. Both impulses sum to zero — internal forces cancel, as required by Newton's 3rd Law.

Answer (b): $\;|I_{B}| = 3m\;\text{N\,s}$; $I_A = -3m\;\text{N\,s}$ (opposite direction).
