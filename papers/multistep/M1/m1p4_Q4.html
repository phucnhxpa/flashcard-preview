Q4 — Uniform beam $AB$, ropes at $C$/$D$; particle at $A$

Question

A uniform beam $AB$ has length $2$ m and mass $m$ kg. It is suspended horizontally by two vertical ropes attached at $C$ and $D$, where $AC = 0.6$ m and $DB = 0.2$ m. A particle of mass $pm$ kg is attached at $A$.

(a) Tension at $C$ is four times tension at $D$. Find $p$. [7]

Now the particle at $A$ is replaced by one of mass $qm$ kg, and the beam is on the point of tilting about $C$.

(b) Find $q$. [4]

(c) State how the assumption that the rod is uniform was used. [1]

[12 marks]

Part (a) — Find $p$ [7 marks] · 3 steps

Distances from $A$: $C$ at $0.6$, CoM at $1$ (uniform), $D$ at $1.8$, $B$ at $2$. From $C$: CoM at $0.4$ right, $D$ at $1.2$ right.
STEP 1/3 M1

Strategy: the beam is in equilibrium, so vertical forces sum to zero. Two ropes pull up; gravity (beam + particle) pulls down. Combine with the ratio $T_C = 4T_D$ to express $T_D$ in terms of known quantities.

Formula (vertical equilibrium): $T_C + T_D = (\text{total weight})$.

Total downward weight = beam weight + particle weight:

$\;\;\text{Weight} = mg + pmg = mg(1 + p)$

So:

$\;\;T_C + T_D = mg(1 + p)$

Use the given ratio $T_C = 4T_D$:

$\;\;4T_D + T_D = mg(1 + p)$

$\;\;5T_D = mg(1 + p)$

$$\boxed{\;T_D = \dfrac{mg(1+p)}{5}\;}$$

Why both ropes lift: with the particle hanging at $A$ (left of $C$), the system would tip ACW about $C$ if only $T_C$ held it \u2014 so both ropes must pull up. Their reactions add to balance the downward weight.
$T_D = mg(1+p)/5$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $T_D = mg(1+p)/5$.
STEP 2/3 M1 A1 A1 A1

Strategy: taking moments about $C$ removes $T_C$ from the equation (zero moment arm). That leaves one equation in $T_D$ and $p$. Combine with the result from STEP 1 to solve for $p$.

Formula (moments balance): $\Sigma\text{CW} = \Sigma\text{ACW}$ about the pivot.

Distances from $C$ (using midpoint = uniform beam): CoM at $0.4$ m right, $D$ at $1.2$ m right, $A$ at $0.6$ m left. Moments:

$\;\;\bullet$ Beam weight $mg$ down at $0.4$ m right \u2192 CW moment $= 0.4mg$

$\;\;\bullet$ Rope $T_D$ up at $1.2$ m right \u2192 ACW moment $= 1.2T_D$

$\;\;\bullet$ Particle $pmg$ down at $0.6$ m left \u2192 ACW moment $= 0.6pmg$

Set CW = ACW:

$\;\;0.4mg = 1.2T_D + 0.6pmg$

Substitute $T_D = mg(1+p)/5$ from STEP 1:

$\;\;0.4mg = 1.2 \cdot \dfrac{mg(1+p)}{5} + 0.6pmg$

Divide every term by $mg$:

$\;\;0.4 = 0.24(1 + p) + 0.6p$

Expand:

$\;\;0.4 = 0.24 + 0.24p + 0.6p$

$\;\;0.4 = 0.24 + 0.84p$

$\;\;0.84p = 0.16$

$\;\;p = \dfrac{0.16}{0.84} = \dfrac{4}{21}$

$$\boxed{\;p = \dfrac{4}{21}\;}$$

Pivot trick: taking moments about $C$ kills the $T_C$ term (its moment arm is zero), so the unknown $T_C$ disappears \u2014 ONE equation, ONE unknown ratio.
$\boxed{\,p = 4/21\,}$
↳ from STEP 2:From STEP 2: $p = 4/21$.
STEP 3/3 A1 A1

Strategy: verify the answer by back-substituting into the equilibrium equation and checking the magnitudes are physically sensible (positive, ratio $4:1$).

$p = 4/21 \approx 0.19$ \u2014 a small particle (only $\sim$19% of beam mass), which is plausible given the rope at $C$ takes 4$\times$ the load of the rope at $D$.

Check $T_D$:

$\;\;T_D = \dfrac{mg(1 + 4/21)}{5} = \dfrac{mg \cdot 25/21}{5} = \dfrac{5mg}{21}$

Then $T_C = 4T_D$:

$\;\;T_C = 4 \cdot \dfrac{5mg}{21} = \dfrac{20mg}{21}$

Total upward: $T_C + T_D = \dfrac{25mg}{21}$.

Total downward: $mg + pmg = mg(1 + 4/21) = \dfrac{25mg}{21}$ ✓

$$\boxed{\;p = \dfrac{4}{21}\;\text{ verified}\;}$$

Sanity rule: equilibrium requires $\Sigma F_{\text{up}} = \Sigma F_{\text{down}}$. Always re-substitute and confirm both sums match \u2014 a strong check against arithmetic slips.
$p = 4/21$ verified

Part (b) — Find $q$ [4 marks] · 1 step

On point of tilting about $C$ → $T_D = 0$ (rope at $D$ goes slack).
STEP 1/1 M1 A1 A1 A1

Strategy: on the point of tilting about $C$, the rope at $D$ goes slack ($T_D = 0$). Take moments about $C$ \u2014 with $T_D$ and $T_C$ both gone (zero arm or zero force), only beam weight and particle weight contribute.

Formula (moments balance about $C$): $\Sigma\text{CW} = \Sigma\text{ACW}$.

Forces about $C$ (with $T_D = 0$):

$\;\;\bullet$ Beam weight $mg$ down at $0.4$ m right of $C$ \u2192 CW moment $= 0.4mg$

$\;\;\bullet$ Particle $qmg$ down at $0.6$ m left of $C$ \u2192 ACW moment $= 0.6qmg$

Balance:

$\;\;0.6\,qmg = 0.4\,mg$

Divide both sides by $mg$:

$\;\;0.6\,q = 0.4$

Divide by $0.6$:

$\;\;q = \dfrac{0.4}{0.6} = \dfrac{2}{3}$

$$\boxed{\;q = \dfrac{2}{3}\;}$$

Tilting rule: on point of tilting about $C$ \u2192 $T_D = 0$ (rope at $D$ goes slack). The single moment equation is enough \u2014 no need for vertical equilibrium unless asked for $T_C$. Don\u2019t accidentally include $T_D$.
$\boxed{\,q = 2/3\,}$

Part (c) — Use of "uniform" [1 marks] · 1 step

From (a)/(b): beam weight $mg$ was placed at $0.4$ m right of $C$ (midpoint of beam, $1$ m from $A$) when taking moments — question asks how the “uniform” assumption was used.
STEP 1/1 B1

The beam being uniform means the centre of mass lies at the geometric centre (midpoint), i.e. $1$ m from $A$. We used this to place the weight $mg$ at $0.4$ m right of $C$.

"Uniform" → weight at midpoint of beam.
