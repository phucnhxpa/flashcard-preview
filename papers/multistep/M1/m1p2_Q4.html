Q4 — Forces $P$, $Q$ at $150°$; resultant $\sqrt{129}$ → find $X$ and angle

Question

Two horizontal forces act on a particle at the same point. Force $P$ has magnitude $X$ N and force $Q$ has magnitude $5\sqrt{3}$ N. The angle between $P$ and $Q$ is $150°$. The resultant has magnitude $\sqrt{129}$ N.

(i) Find $X$. [4]

(ii) Find the angle (to the nearest degree) between $Q$ and the resultant. [4]

[8 marks]

Part (i) — Find $X$ [4 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2 M1 A1

Strategy: two forces at an angle → use the parallelogram law. The resultant is the diagonal, and its magnitude is given by cosine rule with $+2PQ\cos\theta$ (angle BETWEEN the vectors, not the supplementary triangle angle).

Formula (cosine rule, vector addition):

$$R^2 = P^2 + Q^2 + 2PQ\cos\theta$$

where $\theta$ is the angle between the vectors as drawn from the same point.

Substitute $R = \sqrt{129}$, $P = X$, $Q = 5\sqrt{3}$, $\theta = 150°$:

Term-by-term:

• $P^2 = X^2$

• $Q^2 = (5\sqrt{3})^2 = 25 \cdot 3 = 75$

• $2PQ = 2 \cdot X \cdot 5\sqrt{3} = 10\sqrt{3}\,X$

$$129 = X^2 + 75 + 10\sqrt{3}\,X \cdot \cos 150°$$

Use $\cos 150° = -\tfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$:

$$129 = X^2 + 75 + 10\sqrt{3}\,X \cdot \left(-\tfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\right)$$

Simplify $10\sqrt{3} \cdot \tfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2} = \tfrac{10 \cdot 3}{2} = 15$:

$$129 = X^2 + 75 - 15X$$

$$\boxed{\;X^2 - 15X + 75 = 129\;}$$

Sign convention: for vector addition ($+2PQ\cos\theta$); for the triangle form ($-2bc\cos A$). They use supplementary angles — don’t mix them up. Why parallelogram form? When forces are added tail-to-tail, the resultant is the diagonal; the angle BETWEEN them needs $+2PQ\cos\theta$ (not $-$).
$X^2 - 15X + 75 = 129$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $X^2 - 15X + 75 = 129$.
STEP 2/2 M1 A1

Strategy: rearrange the equation from STEP 1 to standard quadratic form, then factorise (or use the formula). Reject any negative root since $X$ is a magnitude.

From STEP 1: $X^2 - 15X + 75 = 129$. Move 129:

$$X^2 - 15X + 75 - 129 = 0$$

$$X^2 - 15X - 54 = 0$$

Factorise: need two numbers with product $-54$, sum $-15$. Try $-18$ and $+3$: $(-18)(+3) = -54$ ✓, $(-18) + (+3) = -15$ ✓.

$$(X - 18)(X + 3) = 0$$

$$X = 18 \quad\text{or}\quad X = -3$$

Magnitude $> 0$ → reject $X = -3$.

$$\boxed{\;X = 18\ \text{N}\;}$$

Sense check: with $X = 18$, $R^2 = 18^2 + 75 - 15(18) = 324 + 75 - 270 = 129$ ✓ (matches). Quadratic formula alternative: $X = \tfrac{15 \pm \sqrt{225 + 216}}{2} = \tfrac{15 \pm 21}{2}$ → $18$ or $-3$.
$\boxed{\,X = 18\ \text{N}\,}$

Part (ii) — Angle between $Q$ and resultant [4 marks] · 1 step

From (i): $X = 18$. Triangle of forces: sides $X = 18$, $Q = 5\sqrt{3}$, $R = \sqrt{129}$.
STEP 1/1 M1 A1 M1 A1

Strategy: the three forces form a closed triangle (sides $P$, $Q$, $R$). The angle between $Q$ and the resultant $R$ is opposite to side $P$. Apply sine rule.

Triangle setup: in the force triangle, when adding $Q$ and $P$ tail-to-head, the closing side is $R$ (the resultant). The angle at the tail of $Q$ (between $Q$ and $R$) is opposite to side $P = 18$. The angle in the triangle opposite $R$ is $180° - 150° = 30°$.

Formula (sine rule):

$$\frac{\sin\beta}{P} = \frac{\sin(180° - 150°)}{R} = \frac{\sin 30°}{R}$$

(Note: the supplementary angle $30°$ inside the triangle, but $\sin 30° = \sin 150° = 1/2$.)

Substitute $P = 18$, $R = \sqrt{129}$:

$$\sin\beta = \frac{18 \cdot (1/2)}{\sqrt{129}} = \frac{9}{\sqrt{129}}$$

Compute: $\sqrt{129} \approx 11.36$, so $\sin\beta \approx 0.792$.

$$\beta = \arcsin(0.792) \approx 52.4°$$

$$\boxed{\;\beta \approx 52°\;\text{(to nearest degree)}\;}$$

In the parallelogram, the diagonal $R$ and side $Q$ form a triangle with $P$. The interior angle at $P$’s vertex (between $Q$ and $R$) has $P$ as its opposite side. Sense check: $\beta \approx 52° < 150°$ ✓; the angle between $Q$ and $R$ should be smaller than the angle between $P$ and $Q$ (since $R$ is "between" them).
$\boxed{\,\beta \approx 52°\,}$ (nearest degree)
