Q8 — Robots $R$ and $S$ on a square floor; find closest approach

Question

A robot $R$ moves with constant velocity. At $t = 0$, $R$ is at $(-2\mathbf{i} + \mathbf{j})$ m; at $t = 11$ s, $R$ is at $(9\mathbf{i} + 23\mathbf{j})$ m. A second robot $S$ moves with velocity $(-\mathbf{i} - \mathbf{j})$ m/s. At $t = 0$, $S$ is at the corner $C = (25\mathbf{i} + 25\mathbf{j})$ m of a square floor of side $50$ m centred at the origin.

(a) Find $\mathbf{r}(t)$ for $R$. [3]

(b) Find $\mathbf{s}(t)$ for $S$. [1]

(c) Show $\overrightarrow{SR}(t) = (2t - 27)\mathbf{i} + (3t - 24)\mathbf{j}$ m. [2]

(d) Find the value of $t$ when the distance between $R$ and $S$ is minimum. [3]

[9 marks]

Part (a) — $\mathbf{r}(t)$ [3 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 M1 A1 A1

Strategy: $R$ has constant velocity, so $\mathbf{v}_R$ is the displacement between the two given times divided by the elapsed time. Then write $\mathbf{r}(t)$ as initial position plus $\mathbf{v}_R \cdot t$.

Formula (constant velocity): $\mathbf{v} = \dfrac{\mathbf{r}(t_2) - \mathbf{r}(t_1)}{t_2 - t_1}$.

Compute the displacement vector ($t_1 = 0$, $t_2 = 11$):

$\;\;\mathbf{r}(11) - \mathbf{r}(0) = (9\mathbf{i} + 23\mathbf{j}) - (-2\mathbf{i} + \mathbf{j})$

$\;\;= 11\mathbf{i} + 22\mathbf{j}$

Divide by elapsed time $11$ s:

$\;\;\mathbf{v}_R = \dfrac{11\mathbf{i} + 22\mathbf{j}}{11} = \mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j}$ m/s

Apply $\mathbf{r}(t) = \mathbf{r}(0) + \mathbf{v}_R\,t$:

$\;\;\mathbf{r}(t) = (-2\mathbf{i} + \mathbf{j}) + (\mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j})t$

Collect components:

$$\boxed{\;\mathbf{r}(t) = (-2 + t)\mathbf{i} + (1 + 2t)\mathbf{j}\;}$$

Sense check: at $t = 11$, $\mathbf{r}(11) = 9\mathbf{i} + 23\mathbf{j}$ ✓ \u2014 matches the given position, confirming the velocity is correct.
$\mathbf{r}(t) = (-2+t)\mathbf{i} + (1+2t)\mathbf{j}$

Part (b) — $\mathbf{s}(t)$ [1 marks] · 1 step

From question: $S$ starts at $C = 25\mathbf{i} + 25\mathbf{j}$ m at $t=0$, with constant velocity $\mathbf{v}_S = -\mathbf{i} - \mathbf{j}$ m/s.
STEP 1/1 B1

Strategy: $S$ also has constant velocity. Apply the same position formula: initial position + velocity × time.

Formula: $\mathbf{s}(t) = \mathbf{s}(0) + \mathbf{v}_S\,t$.

Substitute $\mathbf{s}(0) = 25\mathbf{i} + 25\mathbf{j}$ and $\mathbf{v}_S = -\mathbf{i} - \mathbf{j}$:

$\;\;\mathbf{s}(t) = (25\mathbf{i} + 25\mathbf{j}) + (-\mathbf{i} - \mathbf{j})t$

Collect components:

$$\boxed{\;\mathbf{s}(t) = (25 - t)\mathbf{i} + (25 - t)\mathbf{j}\;}$$

Geometric note: both components are $(25 - t)$, so $S$ moves along the line $y = x$ from $(25, 25)$ towards the origin \u2014 a $-45°$ diagonal trajectory.
$\mathbf{s}(t) = (25-t)\mathbf{i} + (25-t)\mathbf{j}$

Part (c) — Show $\overrightarrow{SR}$ [2 marks] · 1 step

From (a): $\mathbf{r}(t) = (-2+t)\mathbf{i} + (1+2t)\mathbf{j}$. From (b): $\mathbf{s}(t) = (25-t)\mathbf{i} + (25-t)\mathbf{j}$.
STEP 1/1 M1 A1

Strategy: $\overrightarrow{SR}$ is the displacement from $S$ to $R$, computed as $\mathbf{r} - \mathbf{s}$. Subtract component-wise.

Formula (vector subtraction): $\overrightarrow{SR}(t) = \mathbf{r}(t) - \mathbf{s}(t)$.

$\mathbf{i}$-component: $(-2 + t) - (25 - t)$

$\;\;= -2 + t - 25 + t = 2t - 27$

$\mathbf{j}$-component: $(1 + 2t) - (25 - t)$

$\;\;= 1 + 2t - 25 + t = 3t - 24$

Combine:

$$\boxed{\;\overrightarrow{SR}(t) = (2t - 27)\mathbf{i} + (3t - 24)\mathbf{j}\;}\quad\blacksquare$$

Sign trap: when subtracting $(25 - t)$, both inner terms flip sign \u2014 the $-t$ becomes $+t$. Easy slip is to write $-2 + t - 25 - t = -27$ (losing the $+2t$). Always distribute the minus sign carefully.
$\overrightarrow{SR} = (2t-27)\mathbf{i} + (3t-24)\mathbf{j}$ ✓

Part (d) — Minimum distance [3 marks] · 1 step

From (c): $\overrightarrow{SR}(t) = (2t-27)\mathbf{i} + (3t-24)\mathbf{j}$ m.
STEP 1/1 M1 A1 A1

Strategy: the distance $|\overrightarrow{SR}|$ is minimised when $|\overrightarrow{SR}|^2$ is minimised (squaring is monotonic for non-negative values). Differentiate $|\overrightarrow{SR}|^2$ with respect to $t$ and set to zero.

Formula (chain rule): $\dfrac{d}{dt}(at + b)^2 = 2a(at + b)$.

Define:

$\;\;f(t) = |\overrightarrow{SR}|^2 = (2t - 27)^2 + (3t - 24)^2$

Differentiate term by term:

$\;\;\dfrac{d}{dt}(2t - 27)^2 = 2 \cdot 2 \cdot (2t - 27) = 4(2t - 27)$

$\;\;\dfrac{d}{dt}(3t - 24)^2 = 2 \cdot 3 \cdot (3t - 24) = 6(3t - 24)$

Sum:

$\;\;f'(t) = 4(2t - 27) + 6(3t - 24)$

Expand:

$\;\;f'(t) = 8t - 108 + 18t - 144$

$\;\;f'(t) = 26t - 252$

Set $f'(t) = 0$:

$\;\;26t = 252$

$\;\;t = \dfrac{252}{26} = \dfrac{126}{13}$

$\;\;t \approx 9.69$ s

$$\boxed{\;t = \dfrac{126}{13} \approx 9.69\ \text{s}\;}$$

Phuc\u2019s slip (per redo): wrote "$12t$" when differentiating $(3t-24)^2$ \u2014 forgot to multiply by the inner derivative $3$ properly. The chain-rule output must be $2 \cdot 3 \cdot (3t - 24) = 6(3t - 24) = 18t - 144$, not $12t$. Always write $2a(at+b)$ in full before expanding.
$\boxed{\,t = \dfrac{126}{13} \approx 9.69\ \text{s}\,}$
