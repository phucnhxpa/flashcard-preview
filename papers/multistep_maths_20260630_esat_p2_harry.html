[ESAT Q1] A biased six-sided die has outcomes 1–5 each with probability P, and outcome 6 with probability q. What is the correct expression for q, and why is it NOT (1 − P)?

Back
Trap: writing Pr(6) = 1 − P subtracts only ONE of the five equal-probability outcomes.

Fix: Probabilities sum to 1, so 5P + q = 1, giving q = 1 − 5P. There are FIVE outcomes each at probability P.

[ESAT Q1] A fair die and the biased die (q = 1 − 5P) are rolled together. What is Pr(both show 6), and what operation do you use?
click to flip
Back
Trap: ADDING the probabilities (treating "and" as "or").

Fix: Independent events ⇒ multiply. "AND" = multiply, "OR" (mutually exclusive) = add.
Pr(both sixes) = (1/6)·(1 − 5P) = (1 − 5P)/6.

click to flip
Back
Trap: calling 360°/n the INTERIOR angle.

Fix: 360°/n is the exterior angle. Interior = 180° − exterior = 180° − 72° = 108°.
Harry's trick: 360 ÷ 5 = (360 ÷ 10) × 2 = 72.

Back
Trap: treating 70.2 as the THIRD TERM a₃, not the SUM of the first three terms S₃.

Fix: 70.2 is S₃, so use the S_n formula: a(1 − r³)/(1 − r) = 70.2. With S∞ = a/(1 − r) = 75:

[P2 Q4] What identity collapses a(1 − r³)/(1 − r) into a(1 + r + r²), and why is it useful here?
click to flip
Back
Difference of two cubes: a³ − b³ = (a − b)(a² + ab + b²).
Special case: 1 − r³ = (1 − r)(1 + r + r²), so a(1 − r³)/(1 − r) = a(1 + r + r²).
This is why S_n with n = 3 simplifies cleanly and lets you divide by (1 − r) safely.

[P2 Q5] f(x) = 3x³ + ax² − 10x + b. Given (3x − 4) is a factor and f(2) = 8, state BOTH theorem applications precisely. What slip did Phuc make?
click to flip
Back
Trap: confusing factor theorem with remainder theorem — both say "substitute c into f", the only difference is whether the result is 0.

Fix:
• Factor theorem: (3x − 4) is a factor ⇔ f(4/3) = 0.
• Remainder theorem: remainder on dividing by (x − 2) is f(2) = 8.
Solve simultaneously ⇒ a = −1, b = 8.

[P2 Q9 Proof] Prove: if n is odd, then n³ is odd (hence 3n³ is odd). What TWO notation slips did Phuc make?
click to flip
Back
Trap 1: wrote n = 2n + 1 — a letter clash (n on both sides).
Trap 2: squared instead of cubed.

Fix: Write n = 2k + 1 with k ∈ ℤ, then cube:
n³ = (2k+1)³ = 8k³ + 12k² + 6k + 1 = 2(4k³ + 6k² + 3k) + 1, which is odd. So 3n³ is odd.
