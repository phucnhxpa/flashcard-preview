How to count solutions / extrapolate: # solutions of f(x)=g(x) = # times the two graphs cross. Count crossings in one repeating unit (one period, or one π-branch for tan vs a flat line), then × number of whole units, then adjust the leftover end piece. Watch the branch that dips below the axis near a tan asymptote — that extra crossing is the classic trap.

Memory hooks

sin & cos are the same wave 90° apart: cos x = sin(x + 90°). tan is the odd one out.
sin & tan pass through the origin (0,0); cos starts at the top (0,1).
Only tan has asymptotes, infinite range, and period 180° not 360°.
Zeros of tan = zeros of sin (need sin x = 0). Asymptotes of tan = zeros of cos. "Where cos is 0, tan blows up."
