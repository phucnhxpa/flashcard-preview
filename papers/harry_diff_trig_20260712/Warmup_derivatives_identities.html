Rule · where is the variable?
If the variable is in the power (not the base), the power rule does not apply. Differentiating leaves \(a^{x}\) unchanged and brings down \(\ln a\):\[ \frac{d}{dx}\bigl(a^{x}\bigr)=a^{x}\ln a, \qquad \frac{d}{dx}\bigl(a^{kx}\bigr)=k\,a^{kx}\ln a. \]
Rule · natural log of a function
“Differentiate the argument, and that goes on the top.” \[ \frac{d}{dx}\bigl(\ln f(x)\bigr)=\frac{f'(x)}{f(x)}\quad (f(x)>0), \qquad \frac{d}{dx}(e^{x})=e^{x},\quad \frac{d}{dx}(e^{kx})=k\,e^{kx}. \]

Reciprocal identities
\[ \tan A=\frac{\sin A}{\cos A},\quad \operatorname{cosec} A=\frac{1}{\sin A},\quad \sec A=\frac{1}{\cos A},\quad \cot A=\frac{1}{\tan A}. \]Harry's hook: “cosec is one over sine … the third letter” — the third letter of cosec matches \(\sin\), of sec matches \(\cos\).
Pythagorean identities
\[ \sin^{2}A+\cos^{2}A=1,\quad 1+\tan^{2}A=\sec^{2}A,\quad 1+\cot^{2}A=\operatorname{cosec}^{2}A. \]
Double-angle identities
\[ \sin 2A=2\sin A\cos A, \]\[ \cos 2A=\cos^{2}A-\sin^{2}A=1-2\sin^{2}A=2\cos^{2}A-1. \]
How the three \(\cos 2A\) forms connect (understanding only)
Start from \(\cos 2A=\cos^2A-\sin^2A\) (cosine first). Replace \(\cos^2A=1-\sin^2A\Rightarrow 1-2\sin^2A\); replace \(\sin^2A=1-\cos^2A\Rightarrow 2\cos^2A-1\).
Sense check
“Memorise these three forms rather than deriving them during the exam” — deriving costs time; this is prime flashcard material.
Phuc's slips (recorded): For \(1+\tan^2A\) Phuc answered \(\cot^2A\); Harry corrected it to \(\sec^2A\). For \(\cos 2A\) he first reached for \(\sin(A+B)\), then wrote it in the wrong order as \(\sin^2A-\cos^2A\) and \(\cos^2A-2\sin^2A\). Order matters: it is \(\cos^2A-\sin^2A\), cosine first.
