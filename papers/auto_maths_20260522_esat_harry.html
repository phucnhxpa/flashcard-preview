[Q1] A square of side $m$ has a semicircle (diameter = full side $m$) cut out. Remaining area = 100 cm². What is the correct equation for the remaining area, and what is the common trap?
click to flip
Back
Trap: writing $m^2 - \pi r^2 = 100$ — forgetting the $\frac{1}{2}$ in the semicircle area formula.

Fix: Semicircle area = $\frac{1}{2}\pi r^2$ with $r = m/2$:
$$m^2 - \frac{1}{2}\pi\left(\frac{m}{2}\right)^2 = 100$$
Forgetting the $\frac{1}{2}$ produces $4-\pi$ in the denominator instead of $8-\pi$.

[Q1] After setting up $m^2 - \frac{\pi m^2}{8} = 100$, how do you solve for $m$ and simplify to match the answer option $20\sqrt{\frac{2}{8-\pi}}$?
click to flip
Back
Factorise before expanding: $m^2\left(1 - \frac{\pi}{8}\right) = m^2 \cdot \frac{8-\pi}{8} = 100$
$$m^2 = \frac{800}{8-\pi} \Rightarrow m = \frac{\sqrt{800}}{\sqrt{8-\pi}}$$
$\sqrt{800} = \sqrt{400\times 2} = 20\sqrt{2}$
$$m = \frac{20\sqrt{2}}{\sqrt{8-\pi}} = 20\sqrt{\frac{2}{8-\pi}}$$
Answer: A

[Q1] Why should you factorise $m^2$ out immediately rather than expanding and regrouping? What ESAT technique does this demonstrate?
click to flip
Back
Technique: Factorise before expanding.
Factor $m^2(1 - \pi/8) = 100$ keeps the algebra compact and directly isolates $m^2$. Expanding first creates more terms to regroup — slower and more error-prone.

Also: read the options first — all options have $m$ (not $r$), so express $r = m/2$ to target the answer form.

[Q2] Right triangle PQR, right angle at Q. PQ = 20, RQ = 10. Perpendicular from Q to hypotenuse PR has length $x$. Find PR and the scale factor for similar triangles.
click to flip
Back
Pythagoras: $PR^2 = 20^2 + 10^2 = 500 \Rightarrow PR = 10\sqrt{5}$

Similar triangles: Dropping an altitude from the right angle to the hypotenuse creates two triangles similar to the original.
Scale factor: $\frac{PR}{PQ} = \frac{10\sqrt{5}}{20} = \frac{\sqrt{5}}{2}$, so sub-triangle sides = original $\div \sqrt{5}$.

[Q2] The altitude $x = 20/\sqrt{5}$ is numerically correct but not listed. What technique transforms it to match option E ($4\sqrt{5}$)?
click to flip
Back
Rationalise the denominator:
$$\frac{20}{\sqrt{5}} \cdot \frac{\sqrt{5}}{\sqrt{5}} = \frac{20\sqrt{5}}{5} = 4\sqrt{5}$$

ESAT answer options have surds in the numerator, not the denominator. Always rationalise at the end before scanning options.

Alternative (faster): Two-area trick: $\frac{1}{2}\cdot 20\cdot 10 = \frac{1}{2}\cdot 10\sqrt{5}\cdot x \Rightarrow x = \frac{200}{10\sqrt{5}} = \frac{20}{\sqrt{5}} = 4\sqrt{5}$.

[Q3] Unit cube. Find the length from a vertex to the midpoint of the opposite face. What is the two-step 3D Pythagoras method?
click to flip
Back
Vertex $(0,0,0)$ → midpoint of opposite face $(1, \frac{1}{2}, \frac{1}{2})$.

Step 1: Base diagonal to point directly below: $x = \sqrt{1^2 + (\frac{1}{2})^2} = \sqrt{\frac{5}{4}} = \frac{\sqrt{5}}{2}$

Step 2: Add vertical: $y = \sqrt{x^2 + (\frac{1}{2})^2} = \sqrt{\frac{5}{4} + \frac{1}{4}} = \sqrt{\frac{6}{4}} = \frac{\sqrt{6}}{2}$

Trap: $\sqrt{3}$ is the body diagonal (corner-to-corner), NOT vertex-to-face-midpoint.

[Q3] Why is $\sqrt{3}$ (the body diagonal) a distractor, and how does $\sqrt{6}/2$ differ from $\sqrt{3}/2$?
click to flip
Back
$\sqrt{3} \approx 1.732$ = corner-to-corner diagonal ($\sqrt{1^2+1^2+1^2}$).
$\sqrt{6}/2 \approx 1.225$ = vertex-to-face-midpoint (two-step Pythagoras).
$\sqrt{3}/2 \approx 0.866$ = completely different value.

Trap: Confusing these. The two-step method gives $\sqrt{6}/2$, confirmed with green checkmark. Never apply 3D Pythagoras once when the target is a face midpoint — use two successive 2D steps.

[Q4] Q:R = 5:2 and R:S = 3:10. Find Q:S in simplest form. What method do you use, and what was Phuc's error?
click to flip
Back
Method: Align vertically, make R the same in both (LCM of 2 and 3 = 6).
$$Q:R = 5:2 \xrightarrow{\times 3} 15:6$$
$$R:S = 3:10 \xrightarrow{\times 2} 6:20$$
$$Q:R:S = 15:6:20 \Rightarrow Q:S = 15:20 = 3:4$$

Trap: Phuc multiplied R (→6) but left S = 10 instead of 20, getting 15:10 = 3:2. Always apply the multiplier to BOTH numbers.

[Q5] At 9:45, what is the angle between the hour and minute hands? What is the key insight about the hour hand?
click to flip
Back
Key insight: The hour hand moves continuously — it does NOT sit at 9 just because it is "9-something".

Minute hand at 45 min = 270° from 12 (at the 9-position).
Hour hand: $9\times 30° + \frac{45}{60}\times 30° = 270° + 22.5° = 292.5°$
$$\text{Angle} = 292.5° - 270° = \boxed{22.5°}$$
Answer: D
10
Front
[Q5] Why is the answer NOT 0°? And why not 7.5°? What trap does the clock angle question exploit?
click to flip
Back
Trap 1 (→0°): Assuming the hour hand sits exactly at 9. It does not — it moves $\frac{3}{4}$ of the way from 9 to 10.

Trap 2 (→7.5°): Using $\frac{1}{4}$ hour instead of $\frac{3}{4}$ hour. At 45 min past = $\frac{3}{4}$ hour, the hour hand moves $\frac{3}{4}\times 30° = 22.5°$ past 9, NOT $\frac{1}{4}\times 30° = 7.5°$.

Formula: $|30H - 5.5M| = |30(9) - 5.5(45)| = |270 - 247.5| = 22.5°$

[Q6] Mean age of 20 members is 28. Mean increases by 2 when two new members join. What is the mean age of the two new members?
click to flip
Back
Step 1: $\frac{\Sigma_1}{20} = 28 \Rightarrow \Sigma_1 = 560$
Step 2: New mean = $28 + 2 = 30$; new count = 22
$\frac{\Sigma_2}{22} = 30 \Rightarrow \Sigma_2 = 660$
Step 3: Sum added = $660 - 560 = 100$
Step 4: Mean of 2 new members = $\frac{100}{2} = \mathbf{50}$ years

Answer: E

[Q6] What happens if you forget the denominator changes from 20 to 22 when new members join? What distractor does this produce?
click to flip
Back
Trap: Using $\frac{\Sigma}{20} = 30$ instead of $\frac{\Sigma}{22} = 30$.
This gives $\Sigma = 600$, extra = $600 - 560 = 40$, mean = $\frac{40}{2} = 20$ years.

20 years is option A — a deliberate distractor designed to catch exactly this error.

Fix: After adding 2 members, the denominator is 22, not 20. Always update $n$.

Back

[Formula] State the key formulas: semicircle area, altitude of right triangle, 3D Pythagoras (two-step), clock angle, rationalise, mean.
click to flip
Back
• Semicircle area: $\frac{1}{2}\pi r^2$
• Altitude: $h = \frac{ab}{c}$ (legs $a,b$; hyp $c$)
• 3D two-step: $d_1 = \sqrt{a^2+b^2}$, then $d_2 = \sqrt{d_1^2+c^2}$
• Clock angle: $|30H - 5.5M|$
• Rationalise: $\frac{a}{\sqrt{b}} = \frac{a\sqrt{b}}{b}$
• Mean: $\bar{x} = \frac{\Sigma x}{n}$, so $\Sigma = n\bar{x}$
• Surd split: $\sqrt{a/b} = \sqrt{a}/\sqrt{b}$
