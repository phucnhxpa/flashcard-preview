Q1 · Functions \(f\) and \(g\) (range, inverse, composite)

Part (a) · State the range of \(f\)[1] · not worked in lesson
Hide working
Standard approach (independent practice)
\(x^{2}+3\ge 3\) for all real \(x\), and \(\ln\) is increasing, so \(f(x)\ge\ln 3\). Hence the range is \(f(x)\ge\ln 3\).
Sense check
The minimum of \(x^{2}+3\) is \(3\) at \(x=0\); \(\ln\) has no upper bound, so the range is unbounded above.
Common trap: writing the range as "\(f(x)>\ln 3\)" (strict). The value \(\ln 3\) is attained at \(x=0\), so the inequality is \(\ge\).
Part (b) · Find \(g^{-1}\)[3] · not worked in lesson
Hide working
Standard approach (independent practice)
Set \(y=\dfrac{3+5x}{x+2}\), make \(x\) the subject: \(y(x+2)=3+5x \Rightarrow x(y-5)=3-2y\).
Result
\( g^{-1}(x)=\dfrac{3-2x}{x-5} \) (with \(x\neq 5\)).
Sense check
Swapping \(x\leftrightarrow y\) form is equivalent; check \(g^{-1}(g(0))=0\).
Part (c) · Find \(fg(0)\)[2] · not worked in lesson
Carry-inUses \(g\) then \(f\): \(g(0)=\dfrac{3+0}{0+2}\), then \(f\) of that.
Hide working
Standard approach (independent practice)
\(g(0)=\dfrac{3}{2}\); then \(fg(0)=f\!\left(\tfrac{3}{2}\right)=\ln\!\left(\tfrac{9}{4}+3\right)=\ln\!\left(\tfrac{21}{4}\right).\)
Sense check
Composite order \(fg\) means "do \(g\) first"; \(\tfrac{21}{4}=5.25>1\) so the logarithm is positive.
Part (d) · Exact \(a\) with \(g(e^{2a})=f(\sqrt{e^{4}-3})\)[3] · not worked in lesson
Carry-inRight-hand side: \(f\!\left(\sqrt{e^{4}-3}\right)=\ln\!\left((e^{4}-3)+3\right)=\ln(e^{4})=4.\)
Hide working
Standard approach (independent practice)
Simplify the right side to \(4\) (above). Then solve \(g(e^{2a})=\dfrac{3+5e^{2a}}{e^{2a}+2}=4\).
Result
\(3+5e^{2a}=4(e^{2a}+2)\Rightarrow e^{2a}=5\Rightarrow a=\tfrac{1}{2}\ln 5.\)
Sense check
\(a=\tfrac12\ln5\) is exact (no decimals), as the command "exact value" requires.
