Law of reflection: normal, angles and construction

Question

A ray strikes a plane mirror at 35° to the normal. Construct the reflected ray and state the law.

Lesson-grounded retrieval and exam explanation

Complete method3 steps

STEP 1/3

Draw the reference line first

At the point of incidence, draw the normal: an imaginary line at 90° to the mirror surface. The normal is always the reference for i and r. The board diagram explicitly labelled the incident ray “in”, reflected ray “out”, and the normal as always perpendicular.

STEP 2/3

Apply the law

The angle of incidence i is the angle between incoming ray and normal. The angle of reflection r is between outgoing ray and normal. The law is i = r. Here i = 35°, so draw the reflected ray symmetrically with r = 35°.

STEP 3/3

Avoid the common measurement error

Do not measure from the mirror. A ray 35° to the normal is 55° to the surface, but i is still 35°. Sense-check the sketch: the two rays should be mirror images about the normal, with arrows showing approach and departure.

Answer to retrieve: Draw the normal at 90° to the mirror. The reflected ray is 35° to the normal on the other side: i = r.
