Q10 ֲ· Nuclear Fission ג€” Balancing Mass and Atomic Number

Question

A plutonium-239 nucleus absorbs a neutron and undergoes fission, producing xenon and zirconium. The equation has some non-zero integers replaced by $w, x, y, z$:

$$^{1}_{0}\text{n} + ^{239}_{94}\text{Pu} \longrightarrow ^{w}_{54}\text{Xe} + ^{y}_{x}\text{Zr} + z\,^{1}_{0}\text{n}$$

Which equation is correct?

A. $w+y=240$ ֲ ֲ  B. $z = 240-(w+y)$ ֲ ֲ  C. $x = 40-z$
D. $94 = 54+x+1$ ֲ ֲ  E. $240 = 54+x$ ֲ ֲ  F. $94 = w+y+1$

[1 mark]

Solution 4 steps

STEP 1/4

Strategy. Apply conservation of mass number (top numbers): sum on the left = sum on the right.

Mass number balance

$$1 + 239 = w + y + z$$

Simplify

$$w + y + z = 240$$
Key fact. A free neutron is $^{1}_{0}$n: it carries mass number 1 but atomic number 0. So the incoming neutron counts in the $A$ balance but contributes nothing to the $Z$ balance. Treat the unknowns as algebra and write down the two balance equations.
Mass number: $w + y + z = 240$.
STEP 2/4

Strategy. Apply conservation of atomic number (bottom numbers): sum on the left = sum on the right. The $z$ free neutrons carry atomic number 0, so $z$ does not appear here.

Atomic number balance

$$0 + 94 = 54 + x + 0$$

Solve for $x$

$$x = 94 - 54 = 40$$
Phuc's mistake (board). Phuc momentarily wanted to include $z$ in the atomic-number balance. Tom's correction: $z$ counts free neutrons, and a neutron's atomic number is $0$, so it drops out of the bottom-row balance entirely. The atomic-number equation is just $94 = 54 + x$, giving $x = 40$.
Atomic number: $x = 40$.
STEP 3/4

Strategy. Run down the options, eliminating any that use a variable in the wrong row or drop a term.

From the mass-number law: $w + y + z = 240$, which rearranges to $z = 240 - (w+y)$. This is exactly option B.

Why the others fail:

ג€¢ C ($x = 40-z$): wrong ג€” $x = 40$ outright; $z$ has nothing to do with $x$ ("miss variables").
ג€¢ D ($94 = 54+x+1$) and E ($240 = 54+x$): both force $x \ne 40$, contradicting the atomic-number balance.
ג€¢ F ($94 = w+y+1$): wrong ג€” it drops $z$ and mixes mass numbers ($w, y$) into an atomic-number statement.

Technique. The correct answer is often a rearrangement of a balance law rather than the law in its most obvious form ג€” here $z = 240-(w+y)$ rather than $w+y+z=240$.
Option B matches the rearranged mass-number balance.
STEP 4/4

Strategy. Confirm: $z = 240 - (w+y)$ is a direct rearrangement of the mass-number conservation law $w + y + z = 240$.

Option A ($w+y = 240$) would be correct only if $z = 0$, but the problem states $z$ is a non-zero integer, so $w + y \ne 240$.

Sanity check. Option B is the only one that is a valid algebraic rearrangement of a conservation law and does not contradict $x = 40$. It correctly isolates $z$ as the "leftover" neutrons accounting for the difference between the initial total nucleon number ($240$) and the total already in the daughter nuclei ($w + y$).
$\boxed{z = 240 - (w+y)}$ ג€” Answer B
