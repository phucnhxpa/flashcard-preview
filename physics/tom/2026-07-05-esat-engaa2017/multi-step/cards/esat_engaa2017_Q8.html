Q8 · PET Scanner — Gamma-Ray Path Difference

Question

In a medical scanner (Positron Emission Tomography, PET) a source inside the patient emits pairs of gamma-rays simultaneously in opposite directions towards two detectors. The detectors are $3.0$ m apart. When the source is at $Q$, half-way between the detectors, the two gamma-rays arrive at the same time. In a scan the gamma-rays arrive with a time difference of $4.0 \times 10^{-10}$ s. The gamma-rays travel at $3.0 \times 10^{8}$ m/s.

How far from $Q$ is the source?

A. 6.0 mm   B. 12 mm   C. 24 mm   D. 6.0 cm   E. 12 cm   F. 24 cm

[1 mark]

Solution 3 steps

STEP 1/3

Strategy. Both rays travel at the same speed, so the extra distance one covers is simply speed times the time delay. Use $d = v \times t$.

Formula (distance = speed × time)

$$\Delta x = v \times t = (3.0 \times 10^{8}) \times (4.0 \times 10^{-10})$$

Evaluate

$$\Delta x = 12 \times 10^{-2} \text{ m} = 12 \text{ cm}$$
Analogy (two cars). Picture two cars both driving at the same speed, $20$ m/s. If one arrives $20$ s before the other, the difference in the distances they covered is $v \times t = 20 \times 20 = 400$ m. Exactly the same logic turns the gamma-ray time delay into a path difference: same speed, different arrival times $\Rightarrow$ difference in distance $= v \times \Delta t$.
Path difference: $\Delta x = 12$ cm.
STEP 2/3

Strategy. The key geometry: shifting the source by $x$ from the midpoint makes one path $x$ longer and the other $x$ shorter, so the path difference is $2x$.

Path difference geometry

$$\Delta x = (d + x) - (d - x) = 2x$$
Analogy (grid-paper demo). On grid paper, put the source at the midpoint: each path is $15$ squares, so the difference is $0$. Now slide the source $2$ squares towards one detector: that path becomes $13$, the other becomes $17$. The path difference is $17 - 13 = 4$ squares — twice the $2$-square shift. This is the "$2x$" relationship made concrete: move the source by $x$, change the path difference by $2x$.
Path difference $= 2x \Rightarrow x = \Delta x / 2$.
STEP 3/3

Strategy. Solve for $x$: the displacement of the source from the midpoint is half the path difference.

Substitute and evaluate

$$x = \frac{\Delta x}{2} = \frac{12}{2} = 6.0 \text{ cm}$$
Phuc's mistake (board). Phuc computed $\Delta x = 12$ cm and was ready to give $12$ cm as the answer. Tom's correction: $12$ cm is the path difference between the two rays, not the displacement of the source. Because shifting the source by $x$ makes one path longer and the other shorter, the path difference is $2x$ — so the displacement is half of $12$ cm, i.e. $6.0$ cm.

Sense check. The $3.0$ m detector separation is a distractor — it never enters the calculation. All you need is the speed and the time difference to get the path difference, then halve it. Recognising that the detector gap is irrelevant is exactly the "decide which information matters" skill Tom is training.

$\boxed{x = 6.0 \text{ cm}}$ — Answer D
