Q01 · Decay chain: one alpha and two beta-minus emissions

Lesson worked problem

A parent nucleus must finish with its mass number reduced by 4 while its proton number is unchanged. Which sequence from the lesson achieves this, and how can you verify it?

Source status: Reconstructed only from the compiled lesson guide’s worked example. Exact original option wording and mark allocation were not supplied, so none is claimed here.

Full method3 steps · marks not specified

STEP 1/3

Track the alpha emission

An alpha particle is $^{4}_{2}\mathrm{He}$. Emitting it changes the nucleus by $\Delta A=-4$ and $\Delta Z=-2$. Write these two changes separately before considering any later emissions.

Why this works: This is the only emission in the lesson pattern that changes the mass number.
Check / transfer: After the alpha alone, the mass target is met but the proton number is two too low.
STEP 2/3

Add the beta-minus emissions

A beta-minus particle is $^{0}_{-1}e$. Each beta-minus emission leaves $A$ unchanged and increases the daughter nucleus proton number by 1. Two emissions therefore give $\Delta A=0$ and $\Delta Z=+2$.

Why this works: Nucleon number is unchanged because a neutron becomes a proton; the emitted electron balances charge in the nuclear equation.
Check / transfer: The two beta emissions exactly repair the alpha emission’s $-2$ change in $Z$.
STEP 3/3

Combine and check

Add the changes: $$\Delta A=-4+0=-4,\qquad \Delta Z=-2+2=0.$$ Therefore the required sequence is $\boxed{1\alpha+2\beta^-}$.

Why this works: ESAT decay equations are checked by balancing total mass number and total proton number on both sides.
Check / transfer: Three beta-minus emissions cannot work: they give $\Delta A=0$ and $\Delta Z=+3$.
Recognition trigger: Track $A$ and $Z$ independently, then add every emission’s changes.
