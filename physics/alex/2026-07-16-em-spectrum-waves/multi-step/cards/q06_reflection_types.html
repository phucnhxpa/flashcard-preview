Q06 · Reflection types and Alex’s periscope drawing

Source: Loom recording (full video analysis) + transcript.

Question

Compare specular and diffuse reflection, then reconstruct the two-mirror periscope ray diagram using Alex’s drawing method.

Full tutor method3 steps

STEP 1/3Compare smooth and rough surfaces

Compare smooth and rough surfaces

Specular reflection: parallel incident rays strike a smooth surface. Its local normals are aligned, so the reflected rays remain parallel and travel in the same direction, giving an ordered clear image.

Diffuse reflection: parallel rays strike a rough surface. Alex drew a different normal at each impact point, perpendicular to the local tangent; the reflected rays scattered in different directions.

Check: every diffuse ray still obeys $$i=r$$ relative to its own local normal.
STEP 2/3Plan the periscope backwards from the eye

Plan the periscope backwards from the eye

Goal: draw a route that lets an observer see an object over a wall. Alex’s explicit drawing tip was to start at the observer’s eye and work backwards to the object.

Draw the eye by the lower opening, the object beyond the wall by the upper opening, and two parallel plane mirrors at about $$45^\circ$$. From the eye, sketch backwards to the lower mirror, vertically up to the upper mirror, then horizontally out to the object.

Why: working backwards fixes the required destination and makes the two reflection points easier to place.
STEP 3/3Correct the ray direction and verify both reflections

Correct the ray direction and verify both reflections

After planning backwards, put arrowheads in the true direction of light travel: object → upper mirror → lower mirror → eye. At the upper mirror, the horizontal ray is turned down the tube; at the lower mirror, it is turned horizontally into the eye.

Imagine or draw a normal at each mirror and check equal angles on both sides. This is the tutor’s periscope correction: the construction begins from the eye for planning, but the final arrows must still point from the object to the eye.

Final drawing: object, wall/tube, two parallel 45° mirrors, one continuous two-reflection path, and correctly directed arrowheads.
