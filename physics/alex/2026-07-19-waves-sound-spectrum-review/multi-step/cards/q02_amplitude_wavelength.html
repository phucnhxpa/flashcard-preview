Q02 · Read amplitude, then calculate wavelength

Source: Loom recording (full video analysis) + transcript.

Question

(a) A transverse-wave diagram labels J at a crest, K from equilibrium to a crest, and L across one wavelength. Which letter shows amplitude?

(e) A sound wave has frequency 6600 Hz and speed 330 m/s. Calculate its wavelength using $\lambda=v/f$ and choose the unit from kg, m and N.

Full tutor method3 steps

STEP 1/3Identify the equilibrium-to-crest distance

Identify the equilibrium-to-crest distance

Phuc chose K, and Alex confirmed it. Amplitude is the maximum displacement from the equilibrium position. The crest is a point, not a distance, and the horizontal distance from one matching point to the next is wavelength.

Diagram check: if a label spans crest to trough, that is twice the amplitude; if it spans crest to crest, it is one wavelength.
Answer (a): K.
STEP 2/3Write and rearrange the wave equation

Write and rearrange the wave equation

Start from $v=f\lambda$. The question already supplies the rearranged form, but the physical route is:

$$\lambda=\frac{v}{f}.$$

This is sensible because metres per second divided by per second leaves metres. Put speed in the numerator and frequency in the denominator.

Why: each of the $f$ cycles per second advances the pattern by one wavelength, giving $v=f\lambda$.
STEP 3/3Substitute, select the unit, and check

Substitute, select the unit, and check

Phuc wrote the correct setup:

$$\lambda=\frac{330}{6600}=0.050\,\text{m}.$$

Alex pointed to the matching solution. From kg, m and N, choose m because wavelength is a distance.

Sense check: $0.050$ m is 5.0 cm. A high-frequency sound at 330 m/s should have a short wavelength.
Final answer: $\boxed{0.050\,\text{m}}$.
