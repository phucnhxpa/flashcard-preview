30-second method recall

  1. SUVAT requires constant acceleration in one dimension.
  2. Choose a positive direction and list signs before selecting an equation.
  3. For a time interval, use endpoint subtraction.
  4. The nth second is n−1 to n.
  5. For time above a height, check two physical crossings and subtract later minus earlier.

Jargon you should be able to explain

SUVAT

Meaning
A set of equations for one-dimensional motion with constant acceleration.

Avoid
Do not use them unchanged when acceleration varies.

Displacement

Meaning
Signed change in position, measured in metres.

Avoid
It is not always the same as total distance.

Direct proportionality

Meaning
A relationship y=kx; its graph is a straight line through the origin.

Avoid
A straight line with non-zero intercept is not direct proportion.

Endpoint subtraction

Meaning
For an interval, calculate s(t₂)−s(t₁).

Avoid
It is not merely putting Δt into a formula with the original initial velocity.

nth second

Meaning
The interval from t=n−1 to t=n.

Avoid
The fourth second is not the whole interval from t=0 to t=4.

Positive direction

Meaning
A stated choice that fixes signs. With up positive, a=−g.

Avoid
Gravity does not become zero at maximum height.

Crossing times

Meaning
The two times a projectile reaches a chosen height.

Avoid
Labels t₁ and t₂ are arbitrary; use later minus earlier.

Maximum height

Meaning
For vertical projection, hmax=u²/(2g) under the stated model.

Avoid
A height target is not a given value to substitute.

Braking-distance scaling

Meaning
At fixed deceleration magnitude, braking distance is proportional to initial speed squared.

Avoid
It is not proportional to speed itself.

Unit conversion

Meaning
Convert km h⁻¹ to m s⁻¹ by dividing by 3.6.

Avoid
Do not square a km h⁻¹ value before converting.
