Q13(b) · Road-train length 3 marks

Source status: Officially controlled Pearson homework item.

Original question

A car accelerates uniformly at 3.7 m s⁻² as it passes a stationary road train. The initial speed of the car is 30 m s⁻¹ and it reaches the speed limit of 130 km h⁻¹ as it passes the front of the cab. Calculate the length of the road train.

Full method3 steps

STEP 1/3
STEP 1/3Convert before squaring

Use one consistent unit system before substituting into SUVAT. Convert the final speed: 130 km h⁻¹ = $130×1000/3600=130/3.6=36.111…$ m s⁻¹.

Why this works: Squaring a speed in km h⁻¹ while using acceleration in m s⁻² would mix units and make the displacement meaningless.
STEP 2/3
STEP 2/3Choose the no-time equation

The required quantity is the distance travelled while passing the stationary road train, which is its length. Time is not asked for, so use $v²=u²+2as$.

Check: Since v>u and a>0, the numerator and the resulting length must both be positive.
STEP 3/3
STEP 3/3Keep precision until the end

Substitute the unrounded converted speed: $s=[(36.111…)²-30²]/[2(3.7)]$.

Why this works: Premature rounding is amplified by the square and can shift the final answer.
Check: At about 30–36 m s⁻¹, travelling roughly 55 m during this acceleration is physically plausible.
Recall answer: 54.596… m ≈54.6 m; official result 55 m.
