When is distance equal to the magnitude of displacement? (Q001)

Answer

Only when the object travels directly along a straight line from start to finish without reversing direction.

Record 02

If an object moves away and returns to the start, what are its displacement and distance? (Q002)

Answer

Displacement = 0 (final position equals initial). Distance > 0 (twice the outward path length). Displacement depends only on initial and final position, not ground covered.

Record 03

On an a-t graph, what is the condition for an object starting at u = -5 m/s to reverse direction? (Q003)

Answer

The cumulative signed acceleration area must reach +5 m/s to bring v to 0, and exceed it to become positive. Positive acceleration alone is not enough; it first reduces the negative velocity.

How do you find total distance from a v-t graph where velocity never goes below zero? (Q004 context)

Answer

Calculate the total area under the graph (e.g., triangle + rectangle or trapezium). Since v ≥ 0, signed area equals distance. Units: (m/s)(s) = m.

How do you find the maximum positive velocity on a curved s-t graph? (Q006)

Answer

Find the point with the steepest positive tangent gradient. Do not use average velocity (s/t) from the origin, as that only equals instantaneous velocity in special constant-velocity cases.

Record 07

What are the forces and acceleration at terminal velocity for a falling object? (Q007)

Answer

Drag equals weight. Resultant force (ΣF) = 0. Acceleration = 0. Velocity is constant but non-zero. (Trap: do not say 'all forces are zero' or 'drag is zero').

A water-ring accelerometer is in a car. The s-t graph has a constant negative slope. What does the water surface look like? (Q008)

Answer

Level (horizontal). Constant negative slope means constant negative velocity, so acceleration is zero. Accelerometers respond to acceleration, not velocity.

Answer

Initial constant +g (free fall). Large negative acceleration when parachute opens (upward force). Acceleration then tends back to zero as a new lower terminal speed is reached.

Record 13

What do the gradient and area of a velocity-time (v-t) graph represent?

Answer

Gradient = acceleration (a = dv/dt). Area = signed displacement (Δs = ∫v dt). Absolute area gives total distance.

Record 14

What do the gradient and area of an acceleration-time (a-t) graph represent?

Answer

Gradient = jerk (rate of change of acceleration, not normally required). Area = change in velocity (Δv = ∫a dt). To get v(t), add initial velocity u.

Record 17

How do you calculate total distance vs net displacement from a v-t graph that crosses the time axis?

Answer

Distance = sum of the absolute magnitudes of all areas (cannot decrease). Net displacement = sum of the signed areas (cancellation is allowed).

Record 19

Why does constant acceleration produce a parabola on an s-t graph?

Answer

s = s₀ + ut + ½at². The t² term makes displacement quadratic in time. Differentiating gives linear v-t and horizontal a-t. If u=0, the parabola starts with a horizontal tangent.

Record 20

What is the ideal discontinuity caveat for instantaneous velocity jumps on a v-t graph?

Answer

An instantaneous vertical jump implies an ideal impulse (infinite acceleration over zero time). In reality, contact time is finite and acceleration is very large but finite. In an ideal graph, it is represented by an impulse/distribution, not an ordinary measurable infinite acceleration (derived caveat).

Record 21

What is the reliable segment-by-segment algorithm for converting motion graphs?

Answer

1) Write sign convention. 2) Mark breakpoints. 3) Read slope first (s-t→v, v-t→a). 4) Read area second (v-t→Δs, a-t→Δv). 5) Use boundary conditions (v=0 → horizontal s-t tangent). 6) Check signs. 7) Flag ideal jumps. 8) Check physical story.
