Record 01

When is distance equal to the magnitude of displacement? (Q001)

Answer

Distance equals the magnitude of displacement only when the object travels directly along a straight line from start to finish without reversing direction.

Q001displacementdistance
Record 02

What are the displacement and distance if an object moves away and then returns to the start? (Q002)

Answer

Displacement = 0 (final position equals initial position), but distance > 0 (twice the outward path length).

Q002displacementdistance
Record 03

What does the area under an acceleration-time (a-t) graph give, and how do you find actual velocity? (Q003)

Answer

The area gives the *change* in velocity (Δv). To find actual velocity, add the initial velocity: v(t) = u + ∫a dt.

Record 04

If an object starts with u = -5 m/s and experiences positive acceleration, what is the condition for it to reverse direction? (Q003)

Answer

The cumulative signed acceleration area must reach +5 m/s to bring v to 0, and exceed it to become positive. Positive acceleration alone is not enough.
