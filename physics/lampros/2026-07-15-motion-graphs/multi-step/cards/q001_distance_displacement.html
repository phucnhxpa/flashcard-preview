Q001 · Distance versus displacement

Back to deckOpen full-method PNG
Source status: Recording-derived concept check; solved. Not an official exam item.
Recall promptWhen is distance equal to the magnitude of displacement?
Concept sketch: derived from the graph method on this card.

Reveal the method

STEP 1/3Define both quantities
Displacement is the signed vector from initial to final position; distance is total path length.
STEP 2/3Test the direction condition
A straight route alone is insufficient: an out-and-back straight journey has non-zero distance but zero displacement.
STEP 3/3State the secure condition
Require a direct path and no reversal. Then the path length equals the magnitude of the start-to-finish displacement.
Recall answer: Only for direct one-direction travel from start to finish without reversing.
Diagnostic / redo: If you wrote “straight line” only, redo an out-and-back sketch and label final position.
