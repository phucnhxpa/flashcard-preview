Q005 · Bounce graph: apex versus contact

Recall promptOn the displayed bouncing-ball v–t graph, where is the maximum height after the first bounce? What does A represent?
Concept sketch: derived from the graph method on this card.

Reveal the method

STEP 1/3Trace the physical sequence
Release: v=0; falling speed grows; contact reverses the velocity; after leaving the floor the ball rises and slows.
STEP 2/3Use the turning-point rule
At a maximum height, instantaneous v=0 after the upward flight. The labelled point is C in the given convention.
STEP 3/3Separate the other zero
A is a zero-velocity crossing while the ball deforms during contact. It is not a free-flight apex.
Recall answer: C is the first post-bounce apex. A is v=0 during floor contact, not that apex.
Diagnostic / redo: If every v=0 point became “maximum height”, state the physical event either side of the point.
