Recall promptFor a falling object whose v–t curve levels off, which statement is secure at release and at terminal velocity?
Concept sketch: derived from the graph method on this card.

Reveal the method

STEP 1/3Read the gradient
A curve that levels off has decreasing acceleration; a horizontal v–t tangent means a=0.
STEP 2/3Analyse release
At v=0, drag is zero, so weight is unbalanced and acceleration is g downward.
STEP 3/3Analyse terminal state
At terminal velocity, upward drag balances downward weight. Net force and acceleration are zero, while speed can be non-zero.
Recall answer: At release a≈g because drag=0. At terminal velocity, ΣF=0 because drag equals weight; neither force is zero.
Diagnostic / redo: If you wrote “drag is zero at terminal velocity”, draw the two opposing force arrows.
