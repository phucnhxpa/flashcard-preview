Q010 · Convert s–t to v–t and a–t

Original question

Question 1 — Describe the motion of a person with displacement-time graph as shown below. Sketch the velocity-time and acceleration-time graphs corresponding to this displacement-time graph.

Full method3 steps

STEP 1/3
STEP 1/3Translate each segment
s–t gradient gives v: straight positive slope → constant positive v; plateau → v=0.
STEP 2/3
STEP 2/3Use the parabola rule carefully
A stated/known parabolic s–t segment has linearly changing v. Its horizontal initial tangent means the final v–t segment begins at v=0, then descends negative.
STEP 3/3
STEP 3/3State the idealisation
The positive-v to rest join is an ideal discontinuity. An exact a–t representation needs an impulse/undefined-at-the-instant caveat; do not draw a finite ordinary acceleration there.
Recall answer: Recall answer: v–t: constant positive, then 0, then a straight line starting at v=0 and decreasing negative. a–t: 0 within the first two open intervals, then constant negative.
Diagnostic / redo: Diagnostic / redo: If the final v–t line starts positive, return to the horizontal tangent at the start of the final s–t parabola.
