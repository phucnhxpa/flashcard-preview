30-second graph dictionary

  • s–t gradient = v. A horizontal s–t line means rest.
  • v–t gradient = a; signed v–t area = displacement. Use absolute areas for distance.
  • a–t area = Δv; recover velocity by adding the initial velocity.
  • Velocity and acceleration with the same sign: speeding up. Opposite signs: slowing down.
  • Read axes, units, signs, initial condition and modelling words before calculation.

Jargon to explain accurately

Displacement

Meaning
Signed change in position from start to finish.

Avoid
It can be zero after a non-zero journey.

Distance

Meaning
Total path length travelled.

Avoid
It is never reduced by a return journey.

Signed area

Meaning
Area below a time axis counts as negative for displacement or Δv.

Avoid
Distance from v–t uses absolute areas instead.

Tangent gradient

Meaning
The instantaneous slope of a curve. On s–t it is velocity.

Avoid
s/t is generally an average, not the tangent gradient.

Turning point

Meaning
A point where instantaneous velocity is zero.

Avoid
Its physical meaning depends on the surrounding motion.

Terminal velocity

Meaning
Constant velocity reached when net force is zero.

Avoid
Drag and weight are equal, not both zero.

Accelerometer

Meaning
A device responding to acceleration.

Avoid
It does not directly report the sign of velocity.

Ideal discontinuity

Meaning
An instantaneous jump in a modelled quantity.

Avoid
It needs an impulse/undefined-at-the-instant caveat, not a finite ordinary value.

Parabolic s–t

Meaning
For uniform acceleration, displacement has quadratic time dependence.

Avoid
Infer this only when stated/known, not from casual curve appearance.

Transcript-supported / derived

Meaning
A conclusion grounded in spoken lesson content and standard graph relations where later board visuals are unavailable.

Avoid
It is not a claim about unrecoverable visual geometry.
