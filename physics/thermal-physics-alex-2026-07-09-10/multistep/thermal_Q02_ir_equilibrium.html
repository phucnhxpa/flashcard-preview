02. Infrared balance and thermal equilibrium

Infrared · energy balanceAlex lessons · 9–10 July 2026Progressive worked solution
Exact question / setup
All objects emit and absorb infrared radiation. For each case, state the inequality between infrared power absorbed and emitted and predict the temperature change: (a) a cooling object; (b) a heating object; (c) an object at thermal equilibrium. State the limiting temperature at which thermal emission would cease in the lesson model.
Use net energy transfer, not the mere presence of emission. Suggested total: 5 marks.
9 July board: hotter objects emit more; cooling means emission exceeds absorption; heating means absorption exceeds emission.
Part (a) · cooling[1 mark]
STEP 1/1[1]

Strategy

Compare outgoing and incoming infrared power.

Full working

\[P_{\mathrm{emit}}>P_{\mathrm{absorb}}\] Net energy leaves the object, so its internal energy and temperature decrease.

Sense check: A cooling object still absorbs infrared; emission is simply greater.

Part (b) · heating[1 mark]
STEP 1/1[1]

Strategy

Reverse the energy imbalance.

Full working

\[P_{\mathrm{absorb}}>P_{\mathrm{emit}}\] Net energy enters the object, so its internal energy and temperature increase.

Sense check: A heating object still emits infrared; absorption is greater.

Part (c) · equilibrium and limit[3 marks]
STEP 1/2[2]

Strategy

State equilibrium generally, then its radiative special case.

Full working

At thermal equilibrium there is no net thermal-energy transfer and the temperature is constant. If radiation is the only route, \(P_{\mathrm{emit}}=P_{\mathrm{absorb}}\).

Sense check: Equal emission and absorption is the radiative-balance case; equilibrium is broader than radiation alone.

STEP 2/2[1]

Strategy

Recall the theoretical limit used in the lesson.

Full working

At \(0\,\mathrm{K}\) (absolute zero), thermal particle motion is at its minimum and the lesson model treats thermal infrared emission as ceasing.

Sense check: \(0\,\mathrm{K}\), not \(0\,^{\circ}\mathrm{C}\).

Final answer
Cooling: \(P_{emit}>P_{abs}\). Heating: \(P_{abs}>P_{emit}\). Equilibrium: no net thermal transfer; for radiation-only balance, \(P_{emit}=P_{abs}\). Lesson limit: \(0\,\mathrm{K}\).
Physics-tightening note: Thermal equilibrium means no net thermal-energy transfer. Equality of emitted and absorbed radiation is the relevant special case when radiation is the only exchange route. Ordinary thermal infrared from a warm object is a broad spectrum produced by thermally excited charge motion and molecular/lattice modes; it is not generally caused by orbital electrons dropping between discrete atomic levels.
