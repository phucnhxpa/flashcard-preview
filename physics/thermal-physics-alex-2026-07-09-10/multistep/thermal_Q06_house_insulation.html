06. House insulation: separate all three mechanisms

Insulation · conduction · convection · radiationAlex lessons · 9–10 July 2026Progressive worked solution
Exact question / setup
“Add labels to the house below to suggest methods of insulation, and explain which of the three methods of heat transfer they help to reduce.” Cover: double glazing, cavity-wall/loft insulation, a draught excluder, and foil behind a radiator.
For every feature, name the mechanism and the physical reason. Suggested total: 8 marks.
10 July completed house-insulation board with glazing, trapped-air insulation, draught exclusion and foil reflection.
Trapped gas and fibre insulation[5 marks]
STEP 1/3[2]

Strategy

Explain double glazing precisely.

Full working

Two panes trap a narrow layer of air or low-conductivity gas such as argon. Sparse gas particles reduce conduction; a suitably narrow sealed gap also suppresses bulk circulation.

Sense check: The key is a sealed, narrow gas layer—not simply “more glass.”

STEP 2/3[2]

Strategy

Give the two roles of loft/cavity insulation.

Full working

Fibres trap many small air pockets. Air conducts poorly, reducing conduction; immobilised pockets cannot circulate, reducing convection.

Sense check: “Trapped air” earns two distinct mechanism links.

STEP 3/3[1]

Strategy

State the ideal limit without confusing it with common construction.

Full working

A vacuum has no particles, so neither conduction through a medium nor convection can occur; practical walls/windows more often use trapped gas.

Sense check: A vacuum is ideal in principle but not the default cavity fill.

Airflow and radiation[3 marks]
STEP 1/2[1]

Strategy

Identify the clue in a draught.

Full working

A draught excluder blocks the movement and replacement of whole volumes of air, so it reduces convection.

Sense check: Flowing air signals convection, not conduction.

STEP 2/2[2]

Strategy

Explain the foil layer.

Full working

A shiny foil layer is a good reflector and poor absorber/emitter of infrared; behind a radiator it reflects infrared back into the room.

Sense check: Radiation is controlled by surface properties, not by trapped air alone.

Physics-tightening note: Conduction through a gas occurs through random molecular motion and collisions. “Passing vibrations” is the solid-lattice model and should not be used for air or argon.
Final answer
Trapped gas lowers conduction; small trapped pockets stop convection; draught exclusion blocks convective airflow; shiny foil reflects infrared. Each feature must be linked to the correct transfer mechanism.
Source-backed trap and prevention rule

Phuc first called a draught “conduction.” Alex corrected it to convection because whole volumes of air move and are replaced. Phuc also needed the second trapped-air role: immobilised pockets stop a convection current as well as reducing conduction.
