07. Copper SHC: detect the worksheet typo

Specific heat capacity · data validationAlex lessons · 9–10 July 2026Progressive worked solution
Exact question / setup
The worksheet states: “A copper block has a specific heat capacity of \(3850\,\mathrm{J\,kg^{-1}\,K^{-1}}\). It has a mass of \(0.5\,\mathrm{kg}\) and is heated by \(3\,\mathrm{K}\). How much energy is transferred?” (a) Calculate using the printed value. (b) The accepted copper value is about \(385\,\mathrm{J\,kg^{-1}\,K^{-1}}\); identify the factor-of-ten worksheet typo and calculate the physics-correct result.
Preserve the exact board wording, but distinguish transcription arithmetic from physically correct data. Suggested total: 6 marks.
10 July worksheet: printed 3850, while the handwritten setup shows 385. Accepted copper SHC is approximately 385 J kg⁻¹ K⁻¹.
Part (a) · exact printed worksheet[3 marks]
STEP 1/2[1]

Strategy

Write the symbolic formula before substituting.

Full working

Formula: \[\Delta E=mc\Delta\theta.\]

Sense check: Energy is the requested unknown, not temperature.

STEP 2/2[2]

Strategy

Substitute the printed value exactly.

Full working

\[\Delta E=(0.5)(3850)(3)=5775\,\mathrm{J}.\] Strictly to the 1 s.f. precision of \(0.5\) and \(3\), this is \(6\times10^3\,\mathrm{J}\).

Sense check: The arithmetic is correct for the printed—but physically implausible—value.

Part (b) · physics correction[3 marks]
STEP 1/2[1]

Strategy

Compare the worksheet datum with accepted copper data.

Full working

\(3850\,\mathrm{J\,kg^{-1}\,K^{-1}}\) is ten times too large; copper is approximately \(385\,\mathrm{J\,kg^{-1}\,K^{-1}}\).

Sense check: The handwritten 385 is physically plausible; the printed sheet likely added a zero.

STEP 2/2[2]

Strategy

Recalculate with the corrected datum.

Full working

\[\Delta E=(0.5)(385)(3)=577.5\,\mathrm{J}\approx 6\times10^2\,\mathrm{J}\quad(1\,\mathrm{s.f.}).\]

Sense check: Halving a kilogram and raising it 3 K should require hundreds, not several thousands, of joules for copper.

Final answer
Printed-value arithmetic: \(5775\,\mathrm{J}\) (\(\approx6\times10^3\,\mathrm{J}\), 1 s.f.). Physics-correct copper result: \(577.5\,\mathrm{J}\) (\(\approx6\times10^2\,\mathrm{J}\), 1 s.f.).
Source-backed trap and prevention rule

The screenshot proves a discrepancy: the printed prompt says 3850 while handwriting shows 385, but it does not establish authorship. Accepted copper data show that 3850 is a factor of ten too large and 385 is physically correct, so the printed slide almost certainly gained an extra zero. Do not claim the handwriting “dropped a zero”; quote the printed datum, then give the corrected calculation.
