08. Avocado Q3: corrected specific heat capacity

SHC · rearrangement · arithmeticAlex lessons · 9–10 July 2026Progressive worked solution
Exact question / setup
“What is the specific heat capacity of an avocado? An average avocado has a mass of \(0.215\,\mathrm{kg}\) and it takes \(1294\,\mathrm{J}\) for it to be cooled down by \(2\,\mathrm{K}\).”
Use the magnitude of the temperature change. The calculator value is 3009.302325… J kg⁻¹ K⁻¹; report \(3.01\times10^3\,\mathrm{J\,kg^{-1}\,K^{-1}}\) to 3 s.f. when the printed 2 K is treated as an exact classroom value. The circled 2500 is incorrect. Suggested total: 4 marks.
10 July board Q3/Q4: the visible substitution is 1294/(0.215×2), but the circled 2500 is inconsistent with it.
Question 3 · calculate \(c\)[4 marks]
STEP 1/4[1]

Strategy

Start with the SHC equation.

Full working

Formula: \[\Delta E=mc\Delta\theta.\]

Sense check: The energy magnitude is 1294 J even though the avocado cools.

STEP 2/4[1]

Strategy

Rearrange before entering numbers.

Full working

\[c=\frac{\Delta E}{m\Delta\theta}.\]

Sense check: The unit follows as \(\mathrm{J}/(\mathrm{kg\,K})\).

STEP 3/4[1]

Strategy

Substitute with brackets around the entire denominator.

Full working

\[c=\frac{1294}{(0.215)(2)}=\frac{1294}{0.430}.\]

Sense check: The denominator is \(0.430\,\mathrm{kg\,K}\), not 0.5.

STEP 4/4[1]

Strategy

Calculate, retain the unrounded value, then report.

Full working

\[c=3009.302325\ldots\,\mathrm{J\,kg^{-1}\,K^{-1}}.\] Treating the printed \(2\,\mathrm{K}\) as an exact/simple classroom value, report \(\boxed{3.01\times10^3\,\mathrm{J\,kg^{-1}\,K^{-1}}}\) to 3 s.f. If \(2\,\mathrm{K}\) were measured to only 1 s.f., strict least-significant-figures reporting would instead be \(3\times10^3\,\mathrm{J\,kg^{-1}\,K^{-1}}\).

Sense check: An avocado is water-rich, so a value of order \(10^3\,\mathrm{J\,kg^{-1}\,K^{-1}}\) is plausible.

Final answer
\(\boxed{c=3.01\times10^3\,\mathrm{J\,kg^{-1}\,K^{-1}}}\) to 3 s.f. under the classroom-exact \(2\,\mathrm{K}\) convention (calculator value \(3009.302325\ldots\)).
Physics-tightening note: For a cooling interval, the signed temperature change may be negative in an energy-change convention, but an energy-magnitude question uses positive magnitudes to obtain a positive material constant. Specific heat capacity cannot be negative. State the rounding convention explicitly: 3009 is 4 s.f.; 3009.3 is 5 s.f.; \(3.01\times10^3\) is the corrected guide’s 3 s.f. report when \(2\,\mathrm{K}\) is treated as exact/simple classroom data.
