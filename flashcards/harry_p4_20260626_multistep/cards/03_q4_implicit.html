Standalone question

The curve C has equation 4x^2 + y^2 − 2xy = 24x. (a) Find dy/dx in terms of x and y. (b) P(a,b) lies on C, has gradient 2, with a>0 and b>0. Find a and b.

Concept visual - supports the stated method; it does not add a different question or solution.

Multi-step working

Step 1 - Differentiate (a)

\(8x+2y\frac{dy}{dx}-2(x\frac{dy}{dx}+y)=24.\)

Step 2 - Collect dy/dx

\((2y-2x)\frac{dy}{dx}=24-8x+2y\Rightarrow\frac{dy}{dx}=\frac{12-4x+y}{y-x}.\)

Step 3 - Use the gradient at P

\(2=\frac{12-4a+b}{b-a}\Rightarrow 2a+b=12\Rightarrow b=12-2a.\)

Step 4 - Use P on C

\(4a^2+b^2-2ab=24a.\)

Step 5 - Solve and filter

\(a^2-8a+12=0\Rightarrow(a-2)(a-6)=0;\quad (a,b)=(2,8),(6,0);\quad b>0\Rightarrow(2,8).\)

Recall answer: dy/dx = (12−4x+y)/(y−x), and P=(2,8).
