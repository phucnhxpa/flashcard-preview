Standalone question

The final guide identifies P4 Q8 as dV/dt=12te^(−t), with V(0)=6. Solve for V in terms of t, then decide whether the vessel capacity 20 is reached.

Concept visual - supports the stated method; it does not add a different question or solution.

Multi-step working

Step 1 - Integrate

\(V=\int12te^{-t}dt=-12(t+1)e^{-t}+C.\)

Step 2 - Use the initial value

\(6=V(0)=-12+C\Rightarrow C=18.\)

Step 3 - Write the model

\(V=18-12te^{-t}-12e^{-t}.\)

Step 4 - Compare with capacity

\(V=18-12(t+1)e^{-t}<18<20\quad(t\ge0).\)

Recall answer: V=18−12te^(−t)−12e^(−t). The vessel never reaches capacity 20; it stays below 18 for t≥0.
