Packet Q7 / Official WME01 Q7 — lift and two boxes

Question

From the paper. A simple lift operates by means of a vertical cable which is attached to the top of the lift. The lift has mass \(m\). A box Q is placed on the floor of the lift. A box P is placed directly on top of box Q, as shown in Figure 4. The cable is modelled as being light and inextensible and air resistance is modelled as being negligible. The tension in the cable is \(\dfrac{42mg}{5}\). The lift and its contents move vertically upwards with acceleration \(\dfrac{2g}{5}\). Using the model, (a) find, in terms of \(m\), the combined mass of boxes P and Q. During the motion of the lift, the force exerted on box P by box Q is \(\dfrac{14mg}{5}\). Using the model, (b) find, in terms of \(m\), the mass of box P.

Part (a) · combined mass [OFFICIAL MS REFERENCE ONLY]4 marks

Carry-in / given: Standard method: the combined mass follows from the total weight / contact forces.
STEP 1/1

Teaching visual — official-reference system choice.

Sense check, exam trigger and stopping point: verify that the lift and each box share the stated acceleration direction; when you see connected lift bodies, draw separate force diagrams. This item was not discussed, so no further lesson-derived solution is shown.

Strategy, status and formula first. Packet Q7 / official Q7 was NOT DISCUSSED, so this is OFFICIAL MS REFERENCE ONLY and must not be described as live Student working. Let \(M=p+q\) be the combined mass of boxes P and Q. Treat the lift and both boxes as one system: internal contact forces cancel, leaving cable tension upward and total weight downward. Newton’s second law is \(\sum F=Ma\); choose upward positive because the stated acceleration is upward.

Authorised working. The whole moving system has mass \(m+M\), cable tension \(42mg/5\), acceleration \(2g/5\), and weight \((m+M)g\). Therefore \(\dfrac{42mg}{5}-(m+M)g=(m+M)\dfrac{2g}{5}\). Divide by \(g\), then multiply by five: \(42m-5(m+M)=2(m+M)\). Hence \(42m=7(m+M)\), so \(m+M=6m\) and \(M=5m\). The requested quantity is only the boxes’ combined mass, not the lift-plus-boxes total. The exact official answer, and no claimed lesson answer, is \(p+q=5m\).

OFFICIAL MS REFERENCE ONLY: combined mass of P and Q \(=5m\).
Hard sign logic, check-back and transfer. Tension is positive and weight negative because of the upward axis; the acceleration term uses the mass of the entire chosen system. Substitution gives tension \(8.4mg\), total weight \(6mg\), and resultant \(2.4mg=(6m)(0.4g)\), so the equation checks. A common neutral trap is using \(M\) rather than \(m+M\) in \(Ma\): that silently excludes the lift while retaining its cable tension. Another is setting resultant force to zero merely because forces are vertical; the system accelerates. Transfer trigger: for a lift with several contents, circle the chosen system, total every included mass once, omit internal contacts, and let the stated acceleration determine whether the force equation balances or has a nonzero resultant.

Part (b) · mass of P [OFFICIAL MS REFERENCE ONLY]3 marks

Carry-in / given: Carry-in: combined mass \(=5m\); solve the contact-force equations for P’s mass.
STEP 1/1

Strategy and definition first. This part also remains OFFICIAL MS REFERENCE ONLY because Q7 was not discussed. Isolate box P, the upper box, since the source gives the force exerted on P by Q. For P alone, the contact force \(14mg/5\) acts upward and weight \(pg\) acts downward. With upward positive and acceleration \(2g/5\), Newton’s second law is “upward contact minus weight equals mass times upward acceleration.” This force-direction statement must precede substitution.

Complete authorised working. Write \(\dfrac{14mg}{5}-pg=p\left(\dfrac{2g}{5}\right)\). Divide by \(g\) and multiply by five: \(14m-5p=2p\). Therefore \(14m=7p\), giving \(p=2m\). The carry-in \(p+q=5m\) is compatible and would imply \(q=3m\), but Q7(b) asks only for P, so \(q\) is a check rather than an additional requested answer. The official scheme authorises \(2m\); no live derivation occurred.

OFFICIAL MS REFERENCE ONLY: mass of P \(=2m\).

Check-back. If \(p=2m\), P’s weight is \(2mg\), the upward contact is \(2.8mg\), and the resultant is \(0.8mg\). Meanwhile \(pa=(2m)(2g/5)=0.8mg\), so magnitude, sign and dimensions agree. Also \(p=2m<5m=p+q\), as an individual box must be lighter than the positive combined mass.

Neutral trap diagnosis and transfer trigger. Applying the cable tension to P fails because the cable pulls the lift, not the upper box. Reversing the contact force fails Newton’s third-law labelling: “force exerted on P by Q” is upward on P; its equal-and-opposite partner acts downward on Q. Using combined mass \(5m\) in P’s \(Ma\) term changes the chosen system midway. On any stacked-body lift problem, trigger a fresh free-body diagram for the named object, translate “on A by B” literally, keep the same positive axis as earlier, and use only that object’s mass on the right-hand side.

STEP 1/1

Source boundary before recall. State “Packet Q7 / official WME01 Q7: NOT DISCUSSED.” The official equations are useful reference material, but they are not evidence of Student solving, Tutor diagnosis, or a lesson correction. This boundary matters especially because the final values are simple: remembering \(5m\) and \(2m\) must not be allowed to erase the modelling decisions that earn the method marks.

Formula-first map. Use \(\sum F=Ma\), with upward positive and \(a=2g/5\). For part (a), define \(M=p+q\) and select lift plus both boxes, total mass \(m+M\). The external-force equation is \(42mg/5-(m+M)g=(m+M)2g/5\). For part (b), redraw P alone: \(14mg/5-pg=p(2g/5)\). The right-hand mass changes because the system boundary changes.

Authorised chain and answers. Part (a): multiply the equation by \(5/g\) to obtain \(42m-5(m+M)=2(m+M)\), so \(42m=7(m+M)\), \(m+M=6m\), and \(M=p+q=5m\). Part (b): \(14m-5p=2p\), so \(7p=14m\) and \(p=2m\). These exact answers belong only to the OFFICIAL MS REFERENCE ONLY layer.

Failure diagnosis. “Vertical” does not mean equilibrium: the given upward acceleration requires a positive upward resultant. Contact forces are internal only when the selected system contains both objects at that contact; they must appear when P alone is isolated. Cable tension belongs to the full lift system, not directly to P. Finally, do not use \(5m\) as the total mass in part (a): it is the result for the boxes alone.

Check-back. With total system mass \(6m\), \(42mg/5-6mg=12mg/5=(6m)(2g/5)\). With \(p=2m\), \(14mg/5-2mg=4mg/5=(2m)(2g/5)\). Both resultants point upward, matching the acceleration, and \(q=5m-2m=3m>0\) provides a consistency check without changing the requested answer.

Transfer trigger and labels. When a lift question supplies cable tension, acceleration, or a contact force, first write the object set beside the diagram. Sum masses only inside that set, suppress only contacts internal to it, and use the same directional convention throughout.
