Q9 — Curve: Tangent, Normal, Differentiation & Area

Question

A curve $C$ has equation $y = f(x)$. (a) Find a value. (b) Show a result. (c)(i) Find $x$-coordinate. (c)(ii) Find equation of the normal. (d) Find the area between the curve and the normal.

[14 marks]

Solution 4 steps

STEP 1/4

Parts (a)-(b) — Finding values from the curve equation:

Substitute given coordinates into $y = f(x)$ to find unknown constants.

Or differentiate and evaluate at specific points.

Phuc's issue: "I didn't know how to do questions A and B." These typically involve straightforward substitution — don't overthink.
Find constants by substituting known points into $y = f(x)$
↳ from STEP 1:Constants from parts (a)-(b)
STEP 2/4

Part (c)(i) — Find the $x$-coordinate of a specific point:

Solve an equation (e.g., setting two expressions equal, or finding where a condition holds).

$x$-coordinate found — carry to next step
↳ from STEP 2:$x = a$ from (c)(i)
STEP 3/4

Part (c)(ii) — Equation of the normal line (6-step pipeline):

Step 1: From (c)(i), we have $x = a$.

Step 2: Find $y = f(a)$ by substituting into curve equation.

Step 3: Differentiate: $f'(x) = \frac{dy}{dx}$

Step 4: Gradient of tangent: $m_T = f'(a) = 8$

Step 5: Gradient of normal (negative reciprocal): $m_N = -\frac{1}{m_T} = -\frac{1}{8}$

Step 6: Normal line: $y - f(a) = -\frac{1}{8}(x - a)$

Critical distinction: $\frac{dy}{dx}$ gives the gradient (a number), NOT the equation of the line. The line requires point-gradient form: $y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$.
$m_N = -\frac{1}{8}$; Normal: $y - f(a) = -\frac{1}{8}(x - a)$
↳ from STEP 3:Normal line equation from (c)(ii)
STEP 4/4

Part (d) — Area between curve and normal:

$$\text{Area} = \int_a^b [\text{upper function} - \text{lower function}]\,dx$$

Method:

1. Find intersection points of curve and normal (these are the limits).

2. Determine which function is "upper" by testing a sample point.

3. Integrate the difference.

Exam checklist for curve questions:
• Always rewrite fractions/roots as powers: $\sqrt{x} = x^{1/2}$, $\frac{1}{x^2} = x^{-2}$
• Power rule: $\int x^n\,dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} + C$ for $n \neq -1$
• Tangent gradient = $f'(a)$. Normal gradient = $-1/f'(a)$. These are numbers.
Area $= \int_a^b [f(x) - \text{normal line}]\,dx$
