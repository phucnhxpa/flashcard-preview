Q5 — Sector $BDE$ + triangles $ABE$, $BCD$ (garden $ABCDEA$)

Question

The garden $ABCDEA$ is made up of triangle $ABE$, sector $BDE$ (centred at $B$, radius $6$ m), and right-angled triangle $BCD$.

Given: $AB = 10.8$ m, $AE = 12.2$ m, $\angle BCD = \tfrac{\pi}{2}$, $\angle EBD = 1.3$ rad, and $A,B,C$ are collinear.

(a) Area of sector $BDE$, in m². [2]

(b) Angle $ABE$ in radians to 2 d.p. [2]

(c) Total area of garden, to 3 s.f. [3]

[7 marks]

Part (a) — Sector area [2 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 M1 A1

Strategy: sector area depends only on the radius and the angle subtended at the centre. Both are given here, so plug straight into the booklet formula. Make sure the angle is in radians (it is — $1.3$ rad).

Formula (sector area, in booklet):

$$A = \tfrac{1}{2}\, r^2\, \theta\quad(\theta\text{ in radians})$$

Substitute $r = 6$ m, $\theta = 1.3$ rad:

$\;\;A_{\text{sector}} = \tfrac{1}{2}(6)^2(1.3)$

$\;\;\phantom{A_{\text{sector}}} = \tfrac{1}{2}(36)(1.3)$

$\;\;\phantom{A_{\text{sector}}} = 18 \cdot 1.3$

$\;\;\phantom{A_{\text{sector}}} = 23.4\ \text{m}^2$

$$\boxed{\;A_{\text{sector}} = 23.4\ \text{m}^2\;}$$

Critical: $\theta$ MUST be in radians for $A = \tfrac{1}{2}r^2\theta$. If your calculator is in degrees you'll get $\tfrac{1}{2}(36)(0.0227) \approx 0.408$ — clearly wrong for a 6 m radius garden. Convert if needed: $\theta_{\text{rad}} = \theta_{\text{deg}} \times \dfrac{\pi}{180}$.
$A_{\text{sector}} = \boxed{23.4\ \text{m}^2}$

Part (b) — Angle $ABE$ [2 marks] · 1 step

From (a): sector $BDE$ has $r = 6$, $\angle DBE = 1.3$ rad, area $= \tfrac{1}{2}r^2\theta = \boxed{23.4\ \text{m}^2}$. Now use the right angle at $B$ inside the rectangle $ABCD$.
STEP 1/1 M1 A1

Strategy: there are three angles at $B$ on the upper side of the line $AC$: $\angle ABE$, $\angle EBD$, and $\angle DBC$. Because $A, B, C$ are collinear, those three angles together form a straight line at $B$ — they sum to $\pi$ rad.

Angles-on-a-line equation:

$\;\;\angle ABE + \angle EBD + \angle DBC = \pi$

Substitute known values: $\angle EBD = 1.3$ rad (given) and $\angle DBC = \tfrac{\pi}{2}$ (from the right-angle configuration of triangle $BCD$):

$\;\;\angle ABE + 1.3 + \tfrac{\pi}{2} = \pi$

Subtract $1.3$ and $\tfrac{\pi}{2}$:

$\;\;\angle ABE = \pi - \tfrac{\pi}{2} - 1.3 = \tfrac{\pi}{2} - 1.3$

Numerically:

$\;\;\angle ABE \approx 1.5708 - 1.3 = 0.2708\ldots$ rad

$$\boxed{\;\angle ABE \approx 0.27\ \text{rad (2 d.p.)}\;}$$

How to spot the trick: when a problem says "X, Y, Z collinear" with rays going up from the middle vertex, the angles at the middle vertex sum to $\pi$ rad ($180°$). Here the angles are $\angle ABE$, $\angle EBD = 1.3$, $\angle DBC = \tfrac{\pi}{2}$. Don't round early: keep the unrounded value $\tfrac{\pi}{2} - 1.3$ for use in part (c) — rounding to $0.27$ then squaring/sin-ing it introduces 3rd-significant-figure drift.
$\angle ABE \approx 0.27$ rad

Part (c) — Total area [3 marks] · 3 steps

From (a)/(b): sector area $= 23.4$ m²; $\angle ABE = \tfrac{\pi}{2}-1.3 \approx 0.27$ rad. Now find the two triangle areas and sum.
STEP 1/3 M1

Strategy: in $\triangle ABE$ we know two sides ($AB = 10.8$ m and $BE = 6$ m, since $BE$ is a radius) plus the included angle $\angle ABE \approx 0.27$ rad. That's the SAS configuration ⇒ use the area formula $\tfrac{1}{2}ab\sin C$.

Formula (triangle area, SAS, in booklet):

$$A = \tfrac{1}{2}\, ab\, \sin C$$

where $a, b$ are two sides and $C$ is the angle BETWEEN them.

Substitute $a = AB = 10.8$, $b = BE = 6$, included angle $\angle ABE = 0.27$ rad:

$\;\;A_{ABE} = \tfrac{1}{2}(10.8)(6)\sin(0.27)$

Compute $\sin(0.27) \approx 0.2667$ (RAD mode):

$\;\;A_{ABE} \approx \tfrac{1}{2}(10.8)(6)(0.2667)$

$\;\;\phantom{A_{ABE}} = \tfrac{1}{2}(64.8)(0.2667)$

$\;\;\phantom{A_{ABE}} \approx 8.64\ \text{m}^2$

$$\boxed{\;A_{ABE} \approx 8.64\ \text{m}^2\;}$$

Phuc said: "I forget the cosine and sine rule." The three triangle tools to keep straight: sine rule (angle–opposite-side pair), cosine rule (two sides + included angle ⇒ third side, OR three sides ⇒ any angle), and area $= \tfrac{1}{2}ab\sin C$ (two sides + included angle ⇒ area only). Here we want AREA with two sides + included angle ⇒ pick $\tfrac{1}{2}ab\sin C$.
$A_{ABE} \approx 8.64$ m²
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $A_{ABE} \approx 8.64$ m²
STEP 2/3 M1

Strategy: $\triangle BCD$ has a right angle at $C$ and we know the hypotenuse $BD = 6$ m (a radius). Use SOH-CAH-TOA with $\angle DBC$ to find the two legs, then use legs to compute area.

SOH-CAH-TOA in a right triangle with angle $\theta$ and hypotenuse $h$:

$\;\;\text{adjacent} = h\cos\theta$ (CAH)

$\;\;\text{opposite} = h\sin\theta$ (SOH)

Identify pieces: right angle at $C$ ⇒ side opposite (which is $BD$, the radius) is the hypotenuse $h = 6$. The angle is $\angle DBC = 1.3$ rad. $BC$ is adjacent to this angle; $CD$ is opposite.

$\;\;BC = 6\cos(1.3) \approx 6 \times 0.2675 \approx 1.606$ m

$\;\;CD = 6\sin(1.3) \approx 6 \times 0.9636 \approx 5.780$ m

Area of right triangle ($= \tfrac{1}{2} \cdot$ leg $\cdot$ leg):

$\;\;A_{BCD} = \tfrac{1}{2}(BC)(CD)$

$\;\;\phantom{A_{BCD}} \approx \tfrac{1}{2}(1.606)(5.780)$

$\;\;\phantom{A_{BCD}} \approx 4.64\ \text{m}^2$

$$\boxed{\;A_{BCD} \approx 4.64\ \text{m}^2\;}$$

Identify hypotenuse first: it's always opposite the right angle. Here the right angle is at $C$, so the side opposite $C$ is $BD$ — that's the hypotenuse. The two legs ($BC$ and $CD$) are the sides ADJACENT to the right angle. Calculator MUST be in RAD mode for $\sin(1.3)$ and $\cos(1.3)$ — otherwise you'll get $\sin(1.3°) \approx 0.0227$ and totally wrong legs.
$A_{BCD} \approx 4.64$ m²
↳ from STEP 2:From STEP 2: $A_{BCD} \approx 4.64$ m²
STEP 3/3 A1

Strategy: the garden $ABCDEA$ is the union of three non-overlapping regions (triangle $ABE$, sector $BDE$, triangle $BCD$). Total area $=$ sum of the parts.

Sum:

$\;\;\text{Total} = A_{ABE} + A_{\text{sector}} + A_{BCD}$

Plug in:

$\;\;\phantom{\text{Total}} \approx 8.64 + 23.4 + 4.64$

$\;\;\phantom{\text{Total}} = 36.68\ldots\ \text{m}^2$

Round to 3 s.f.:

$$\boxed{\;\text{Total area} \approx 36.7\ \text{m}^2\;}$$

Check the magnitudes: the sector dominates ($23.4$ m², large angle $1.3$ rad over the full radius squared); $\triangle ABE$ is moderate ($8.64$ m², modest sides but narrow $0.27$ rad angle); $\triangle BCD$ is smallest ($4.64$ m², small legs from a single radius split). Ratios make sense given the geometry. Final precision: the question asks for 3 s.f., so $36.7$ — keeping more digits ($36.68$) and then rounding only at the end avoids drift.
Total area $\approx 36.7$ m² (3 s.f.)
