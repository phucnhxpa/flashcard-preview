Q8 — Triangle Area in Composite Shape (Sine/Cosine Rule)

Question

A composite shape consists of a right triangle, a sector, and a general triangle $ABC$. Find the area of each part and the total area using the cosine rule and sine area formula.

[10 marks]

Solution 4 steps

STEP 1/4

Setup — Identify the composite shape:

The shape consists of: a sector, a right triangle, and a general (non-right) triangle $ABC$.

What to notice: Triangle $ABC$ is NOT right-angled, so you need the sine/cosine rule.

Phuc's recurring weakness (5th paper!): "Review formulas for triangles, there are only two formulas." Actually three tools: (1) Area $= \frac{1}{2}ab\sin C$, (2) Cosine rule, (3) Sine rule.
Plan: sector area → right triangle area → cosine rule for missing side → sine area formula for $\triangle ABC$ → sum all
↳ from STEP 1:Identified 3 sub-shapes
STEP 2/4

Parts (a)-(c) — Sector and right triangle:

Sector area: $A_{\text{sector}} = \frac{1}{2}r^2\theta$ (with $\theta$ in radians!)

Right triangle area: $A = \frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{height}$

Find angle: Use given information from the shape geometry.

Sector + right triangle areas computed from given measurements
↳ from STEP 2:Angle from part (b), side $BC$ from shape
STEP 3/4

Part (d), Step 1 — Use Cosine Rule to find missing side $AC$:

In triangle $ABC$, we know: two sides ($BC$, plus info from earlier parts) and one angle.

$$a^2 = b^2 + c^2 - 2bc\cos A$$

Phuc's insight: "We need two sides next to the angle, so we need $BC$ and $AC$. We only have $BC$, so we need to find $AC$ using the cosine rule."

Missing side $AC$ found via cosine rule
STEP 4/4

Part (d), Step 2 — Sine rule for area, then total:

With two sides and the included angle:

$$A_{\triangle ABC} = \frac{1}{2} \cdot BC \cdot AC \cdot \sin(\angle BCA)$$

Total area:

$$A_{\text{total}} = A_{\text{sector}} + A_{\text{right triangle}} + A_{\triangle ABC}$$

Triangle toolkit (MUST MEMORISE):
• $A = \frac{1}{2}ab\sin C$ — need 2 sides + included angle
• $a^2 = b^2 + c^2 - 2bc\cos A$ — cosine rule
• $\frac{a}{\sin A} = \frac{b}{\sin B}$ — sine rule
Decision tree: Missing a side? → cosine/sine rule FIRST, THEN area formula.
$A_{\text{total}} = A_{\text{sector}} + A_{\text{right \triangle}} + A_{\triangle ABC}$
