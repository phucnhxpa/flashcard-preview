STEP 1/1

Strategy and formula selection. A negative index means the finite positive-integer binomial theorem is not the right tool. Use the general binomial series, supplied in the formula booklet, in the form \[(1+z)^n=1+nz+\frac{n(n-1)}{2!}z^2+\frac{n(n-1)(n-2)}{3!}z^3+\cdots,\qquad |z|<1.\] The bracket must begin with \(1\), so factor the constant before substituting. The lesson reached exactly \[(2+5x)^{-2}=\bigl(2(1+\tfrac52x)\bigr)^{-2}=2^{-2}(1+\tfrac52x)^{-2}=\frac14(1+\tfrac52x)^{-2}.\] Phuc recognised the \(5/2\) factor after Harry prompted the normalisation. STEP 1/2

Formula-first substitution and full coefficient derivation. In the general series take \(n=-2\) and \(z=\tfrac52x\). Compute each coefficient explicitly rather than memorising a pattern: \[1+nz=1-2z,\] \[\frac{n(n-1)}{2}z^2=\frac{(-2)(-3)}2z^2=3z^2,\] \[\frac{n(n-1)(n-2)}6z^3=\frac{(-2)(-3)(-4)}6z^3=-4z^3.\] Therefore \[(1+z)^{-2}=1-2z+3z^2-4z^3+\cdots.\] Substitute \(z=\tfrac52x\) and retain the outside \(1/4\): \[(2+5x)^{-2}=\frac14\left[1-5x+3\left(\frac{25}{4}x^2\right)-4\left(\frac{125}{8}x^3\right)\right]+\cdots.\] Distributing \(1/4\) gives \[\boxed{\frac14-\frac54x+\frac{75}{16}x^2-\frac{125}{8}x^3+\cdots}.\]

Diagnosis and sign check. Each factor in the cubic coefficient matters: the coefficient inside is \(-4\), and \((5x/2)^3=125x^3/8\); multiplying by the outside quarter yields \(-125x^3/8\), not \(-125x^3/2\). The alternating signs follow because \(z>0\) for positive \(x\) while \(n=-2\). Differentiate the simpler identity \((1+z)^{-1}=1-z+z^2-z^3+\cdots\) with respect to \(z\), then negate, to recover \((1+z)^{-2}=1-2z+3z^2-4z^3+\cdots\); this independently checks the coefficient pattern.
