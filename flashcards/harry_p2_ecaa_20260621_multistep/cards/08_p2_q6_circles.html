Standalone question

(a) A(-2,18), B(14,6) are ends of a diameter of C1; find its equation (5). (b) C2 is centred at the origin and touches C1; find the possible equations of C2 (4).

Concept visual - supports the stated method; it does not add a different question or solution.

Multi-step working

Step 1 - (a) Centre and radius from the diameter

\(\text{centre}=\left(\tfrac{-2+14}{2},\tfrac{18+6}{2}\right)=(6,12);\quad r=\tfrac12|AB|=\tfrac12\sqrt{16^2+(-12)^2}=\tfrac12\sqrt{400}=10.\)

Step 2 - (a) Write C1

\((x-6)^2+(y-12)^2=100.\)

Step 3 - (b) Distance from the origin to the C1 centre

\(\sqrt{6^2+12^2}=\sqrt{180}=6\sqrt5.\)

Step 4 - (b) Two touching circles (inner and outer)

\(x^2+y^2=(6\sqrt5-10)^2=280-120\sqrt5\quad\text{or}\quad x^2+y^2=(6\sqrt5+10)^2=280+120\sqrt5.\)

Recall answer: C1: (x-6)^2 + (y-12)^2 = 100; C2: x^2 + y^2 = 280 +/- 120 sqrt(5). Draw C1 and both touching circles first; each radius is the centre-distance minus-or-plus the radius of C1.
