P2 Q4 — division, factors, proof and decreasing interval

Question

Let $f(x)=4x^3+13x^2-10x+8$.

(a) Divide by $x-2$; find the quotient and remainder. (4)

(b)(i) Show that $x+4$ is a factor. (ii) Hence prove that $f(x)=0$ has only one real solution. (5)

(c) Find the interval on which $f$ is decreasing. (3)

Lesson status: (a) and (b)(i) partial; (b)(ii) live repair; (c) original correct.

[12 marks]

Part (a)[4 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2

Polynomial division by $x-2$ gives quotient $4x^2+21x+32$.

STEP 2/2

The remainder is $f(2)=72$.

Target: quotient $4x^2+21x+32$; remainder $72$.
The submitted division was correct, but the remainder was copied as 32 instead of 72.

Part (b)[5 marks] · 3 steps

Carry-in: from Part (b)(i): $f(-4)=0$, hence $x+4$ is a factor.
STEP 1/3

$$f(-4)=-256+208+40+8=0.$$

Therefore $x+4$ is a factor.

STEP 2/3

Using the known factor,

$$f(x)=(x+4)(4x^2-3x+2).$$

STEP 3/3

For the quadratic factor,

$$\Delta=(-3)^2-4(4)(2)=-23<0.$$

It has no real roots, so the cubic has exactly one real solution.

Target: the only real solution is $x=-4$.
