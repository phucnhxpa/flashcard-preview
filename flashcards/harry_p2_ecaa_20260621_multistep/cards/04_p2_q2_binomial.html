The first four terms of (1+px)^10 in ascending powers of x are 1 + 15x + qx^2 + rx^3. Find the values of p, q and r (6).

Concept visual - supports the stated method; it does not add a different question or solution.

Multi-step working

Step 1 - Expand with the supplied theorem

\((1+px)^{10}=1+10px+45p^2x^2+120p^3x^3+\cdots\quad(C(10,2)=45,\ C(10,3)=120).\)

Step 2 - Compare the x-term

\(10p=15\ \Rightarrow\ p=\tfrac32.\)

Step 3 - Find q and r

\(q=45p^2=45\cdot\tfrac94=\tfrac{405}{4}=101.25,\qquad r=120p^3=120\cdot\tfrac{27}{8}=405.\)
