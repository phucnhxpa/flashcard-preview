Standalone question

A square with vertices at (+/-1, +/-1) is rotated 90 degrees clockwise about the origin, then reflected in the line y=x. Which single transformation returns the labelled square to its original position? Official answer: B, reflection in the y-axis.

Concept visual - supports the stated method; it does not add a different question or solution.

Multi-step working

Step 1 - Label and apply the two moves (lesson method)

\(P(1,1),\ Q(-1,1),\ R(-1,-1),\ S(1,-1);\quad \text{rotate }90^\circ\text{ cw: }(x,y)\mapsto(y,-x);\quad \text{reflect }y=x:(x,y)\mapsto(y,x).\)

Step 2 - Build the vertex table

\(\text{After both moves every point has }x\mapsto -x\text{ while }y\text{ is unchanged}.\)

Step 3 - Conclude from the vertices

\(\text{Net effect}=\text{reflection in the }y\text{-axis; it is its own inverse, so answer }\mathbf{B}.\)

Step 4 - Derived verification check - NOT a recorded lesson method

\(\begin{pmatrix}0&1\1&0\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix}0&1\-1&0\end{pmatrix}=\begin{pmatrix}-1&0\0&1\end{pmatrix}=\text{reflection in the }y\text{-axis (matrix check only)}.\)

Recall answer: Reflection in the y-axis, answer B. Track every vertex (the lesson method); the matrix composition is a Derived verification check, not the recorded lesson method - under exam conditions show the vertex table.
