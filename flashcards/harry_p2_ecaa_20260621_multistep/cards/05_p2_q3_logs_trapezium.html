Standalone question

(a) y = 3 x 2^(-x) passes through P(k, 300000); use logs to find k (2). (b)(i) Trapezium-rule estimate from six tabulated y-values over x = -0.5 to 7 (3). (b)(ii) Use (i) to estimate a stated sum of two integrals (2).

Concept visual - supports the stated method; it does not add a different question or solution.

Multi-step working

Step 1 - (a) Take logs, change of base

\(300000=3\cdot2^{-k}\Rightarrow 2^{-k}=100000\Rightarrow k=-\frac{\log 100000}{\log 2}\approx-16.61.\)

Step 2 - (b)(i) Count the gaps, not the values

\(\text{Six values}\Rightarrow\text{FIVE strips};\quad h=\frac{7-(-0.5)}{5}=1.5.\)

Step 3 - (b)(i) Apply the trapezium rule

\(\text{est}=\tfrac{h}{2}\bigl[y_0+y_5+2(y_1+y_2+y_3+y_4)\bigr]\approx 6.6255\to 6.63.\)

Step 4 - (b)(ii) Follow-through

\(\tfrac13+\tfrac13(6.6255)=4.417\to 4.42.\)

Recall answer: k is approximately -16.61; trapezium estimate is approximately 6.63; follow-through is approximately 4.42. The single mis-count (six ordinates give five strips) propagated through both (b)(i) and (b)(ii).
