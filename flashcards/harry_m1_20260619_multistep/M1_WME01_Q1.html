Packet Q2 / Official WME01 Q1 — train speed–time graph

From the paper. A train accelerates from rest to \(20\ \mathrm{m\,s^{-1}}\), runs at that speed for \(180\ \mathrm{s}\), then decelerates to rest. The magnitude of the acceleration is twice the magnitude of the deceleration; total distance is \(4.8\ \mathrm{km}\). Sketch the graph, find \(T\), and find the initial acceleration.

Part (a) · the sketch3
Carry-in / given: Acceleration phase lasts \(T\); because \(|a|=2|d|\), the deceleration phase lasts \(2T\).
Algebra-only: no spatial, graphical, or statistical diagram is needed for this reasoning.

Place the peak at \(20\ \mathrm{m\,s^{-1}}\); read base markers from the two ramp durations.

Base markers: \(T,\ T+180,\ 3T+180\).

Trapezium peaking at \(20\ \mathrm{m\,s^{-1}}\) with base \(0\to 3T+180\).

Sense check: the constant phase runs to \(T+180\), not to \(180\) from the origin.

Trap: assuming both ramp times are equal. The acceleration is twice the deceleration, so the deceleration phase is twice as long.
Part (b) · find T5
Carry-in / given: Carry-in: total distance \(=4800\ \mathrm{m}\) (convert \(4.8\ \mathrm{km}\) first).

Area of the trapezium = distance; use \(A=\tfrac12(a+b)h\).

Formula: \(A=\tfrac12(a+b)h\), parallel sides \(a=180,\ b=3T+180\), height \(h=20\).

\(4800=\tfrac12\big(180+(3T+180)\big)(20)=(3T+360)(10)\).

\(480=3T+360\ \Rightarrow\ 3T=120\ \Rightarrow\ T=40\ \mathrm{s}\).

\(T=40\ \mathrm{s}\).

Sense check: \(3(40)+180=300\ \mathrm{s}\) total time; area is positive.

Trap: using the gradient instead of the area; equating km directly to a metre-based area without converting \(4.8\ \mathrm{km}=4800\ \mathrm{m}\). (Peak label is \(20\ \mathrm{m\,s^{-1}}\); do not cite a mis-drawn value such as “30”.)
Part (c) · initial acceleration2
Carry-in / given: Carry-in: peak speed \(20\ \mathrm{m\,s^{-1}}\), acceleration phase lasts \(T=40\ \mathrm{s}\).
Algebra-only: no spatial, graphical, or statistical diagram is needed for this reasoning.

Gradient of the first segment = acceleration.

Formula: \(a=\dfrac{\Delta v}{\Delta t}\).

\(a=\dfrac{20}{T}=\dfrac{20}{40}=0.5\ \mathrm{m\,s^{-2}}\).

\(a=0.5\ \mathrm{m\,s^{-2}}\).

Sense check: positive, since the train speeds up from rest.

Trap: reading the gradient off the wrong segment (e.g. the deceleration phase).
