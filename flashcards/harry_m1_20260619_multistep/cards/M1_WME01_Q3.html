Packet Q3 / Official WME01 Q3 — constant-acceleration vectors

Question

From the paper. A particle moves with constant acceleration; its position is \(\mathbf{r}(t)\). (a) Find the speed at \(t=2\). (b) Find the angle the velocity makes with \(\mathbf{i}\) at \(t=2\). (c) Find \(T\) for which the direction is \(2\mathbf{i}-3\mathbf{j}\).

[10 marks]

Lesson status: REVIEWED ONLY / DEFERRED · Source class: official WME01/01 Jan 2023 (packet Q3)

Part (a) · speed at t = 2 [MS reference]status boundary

Carry-in / given: Standard method: \(\mathbf{v}(t)=\dfrac{d\mathbf{r}}{dt}\); speed \(=\lvert\mathbf{v}\rvert\).
STEP 1/1

Differentiate position to get velocity; speed is the magnitude of the velocity vector.

Formula: speed \(=\sqrt{v_x^2+v_y^2}\).

OFFICIAL MS REFERENCE ONLY: speed at \(t=2\) is \(\sqrt{45}=3\sqrt5\approx6.7\ \mathrm{m\,s^{-1}}\).

\(3\sqrt5\approx6.7\ \mathrm{m\,s^{-1}}\).
Trap: forgetting that speed uses both components (not just one).

Part (b) · angle with i at t = 2 [MS reference]status boundary

Carry-in / given: Carry-in: the velocity components at \(t=2\) from part (a).
STEP 1/1

The direction angle uses \(\tan^{-1}(v_y/v_x)\), quadrant-correct.

Formula: \(\theta=\tan^{-1}\!\big(\tfrac{v_y}{v_x}\big)\) (reference to \(\mathbf{i}\)).

OFFICIAL MS REFERENCE ONLY: angle \(=27^\circ\) (or \(333^\circ\) per the stated convention).

\(27^\circ\) (or \(333^\circ\)).
Trap: measuring the angle from \(\mathbf{j}\) instead of \(\mathbf{i}\); dropping the quadrant statement.

Part (c) · T for direction 2i − 3j [MS reference]status boundary

Carry-in / given: Carry-in: the velocity expression \(\mathbf{v}(t)\) from the paper.
STEP 1/1

Set the velocity direction equal to \(2\mathbf{i}-3\mathbf{j}\) (i.e. \(v_y/v_x=-3/2\)) and solve for \(T\).

OFFICIAL MS REFERENCE ONLY: \(T=3.2\).

\(T=3.2\).

Sense check: keep the exact ratio until the final value.

Trap: rounding before solving for \(T\).
