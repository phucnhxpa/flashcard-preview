Packet Q3 / Official WME01 Q3 — constant-acceleration vectors

Question

From the official paper. A particle P is moving with constant acceleration \((-4\mathbf i+\mathbf j)\ \mathrm{m\,s^{-2}}\). At time \(t=0\), P has velocity \((14\mathbf i-5\mathbf j)\ \mathrm{m\,s^{-1}}\). (a) Find the speed of P at time \(t=2\) seconds. [3 marks] (b) Find the size of the angle between the direction of \(\mathbf i\) and the direction of motion of P at time \(t=2\) seconds. [3 marks] At time \(t=T\) seconds, P is moving in the direction of vector \((2\mathbf i-3\mathbf j)\). (c) Find the value of \(T\). [4 marks]

[Total: 10 marks]

Part (a) · speed at t = 2 [MS reference]3 marks · official reference only

Carry-in / given: \(\mathbf a=-4\mathbf i+\mathbf j\), \(\mathbf u=14\mathbf i-5\mathbf j\), and \(\mathbf v=\mathbf u+\mathbf at\).
STEP 1/1

Exam trigger and stopping point: when you see constant-acceleration vectors, write one SUVAT equation per component. The lesson review remains deferred, so no further live solution is claimed; the completed derivation below is explicitly official-reference working.

Source boundary and correct route. The lesson only reviewed this question—the Tutor said the earlier work was “pretty good”—and no subpart was re-worked live. Everything numerical below is therefore OFFICIAL MS REFERENCE ONLY, not a claim about live Student work. The official data give acceleration \(\mathbf a=-4\mathbf i+\mathbf j\) and initial velocity \(\mathbf u=14\mathbf i-5\mathbf j\), so use \(\mathbf v=\mathbf u+\mathbf a t\), not differentiation of a nonexistent position vector.

OFFICIAL MS REFERENCE ONLY — full derivation. At \(t=2\), \[\mathbf v(2)=(14\mathbf i-5\mathbf j)+2(-4\mathbf i+\mathbf j)=6\mathbf i-3\mathbf j\ \mathrm{m\,s^{-1}}.\] Speed is the magnitude of velocity, not either component and not their sum: \[|\mathbf v(2)|=\sqrt{6^2+(-3)^2}=\sqrt{36+9}=\sqrt{45}=3\sqrt5\ \mathrm{m\,s^{-1}}.\] Numerically this is \(6.708\ldots\ \mathrm{m\,s^{-1}}\), so \(6.7\ \mathrm{m\,s^{-1}}\) is a suitable rounded form.

Checks and mark-route awareness. The first method mark comes from forming velocity at \(t=2\); the second comes from applying Pythagoras to those components; the accuracy mark requires the correct speed from the correct vector. Squaring \(3\sqrt5\) returns \(45\), exactly the component-square sum. The signs also fit the motion: after two seconds the horizontal component remains positive and the vertical component remains negative.

OFFICIAL MS REFERENCE ONLY: \(\boxed{3\sqrt5\ \mathrm{m\,s^{-1}}}\) (approximately \(6.7\ \mathrm{m\,s^{-1}}\)). REVIEWED ONLY / DEFERRED in the lesson.
Trap and transfer trigger. Do not differentiate unless a position function is actually supplied. Here the trigger is “constant acceleration plus initial velocity”, so write \(\mathbf v=\mathbf u+\mathbf a t\), substitute the time, then take the magnitude. Retain the negative component inside the square; its square is positive. This preserves both the mechanics model and the REVIEWED/DEFERRED attribution boundary.

Part (b) · angle with i at t = 2 [MS reference]3 marks · official reference only

Carry-in / given: From official-reference part (a), \(\mathbf v(2)=6\mathbf i-3\mathbf j\ \mathrm{m\,s^{-1}}\).
STEP 1/1

OFFICIAL MS REFERENCE ONLY — not produced live. The vector \(6\mathbf i-3\mathbf j\) lies below the positive \(\mathbf i\)-direction because its \(\mathbf i\)-component is positive and its \(\mathbf j\)-component is negative. Let \(\alpha\) be the acute size of the angle between the positive \(\mathbf i\)-direction and the direction of motion. Component geometry gives \[\tan\alpha=\frac{|{-3}|}{6}=\frac12.\]

Therefore \[\alpha=\tan^{-1}\!\left(\frac12\right)=26.565\ldots^\circ,\] which rounds to \(27^\circ\). If direction is instead reported as a standard anticlockwise bearing from positive \(\mathbf i\), the same below-axis vector has angle \(360^\circ-26.565\ldots^\circ=333.435\ldots^\circ\), accepted as \(333^\circ\). These are two conventions describing one vector, not two possible quadrants.

Check and interpretation. Since \(\tan 26.565\ldots^\circ=0.5\), the vertical magnitude is half the horizontal magnitude, matching \(|-3|/6\). An answer of \(-27^\circ\) does not answer the command “size”; \(63^\circ\) would be the complementary angle with \(\mathbf j\), not with \(\mathbf i\). The sign sketch must come before the inverse tangent because a calculator’s principal output alone does not identify orientation.

OFFICIAL MS REFERENCE ONLY: \(\boxed{27^\circ}\), equivalently \(333^\circ\) under the full-turn convention. Lesson work remains REVIEWED ONLY / DEFERRED.
Transfer trigger. For “angle with \(\mathbf i\)”, carry the actual velocity components, inspect their signs, form vertical-over-horizontal, and then state the requested angle convention. Never use speed in place of a signed component: magnitude contains no quadrant information.

Part (c) · T for direction 2i − 3j [MS reference]4 marks · official reference only

Carry-in / given: \(\mathbf v(T)=(14-4T)\mathbf i+(-5+T)\mathbf j\); target direction \(2\mathbf i-3\mathbf j\).
STEP 1/1

OFFICIAL MS REFERENCE ONLY — full set-up. At time \(T\), constant acceleration gives \[\mathbf v(T)=(14-4T)\mathbf i+(-5+T)\mathbf j.\] “Moving in the direction of \(2\mathbf i-3\mathbf j\)” means the velocity components have the same ratio; it does not mean the velocity equals that particular vector. Hence \[\frac{14-4T}{-5+T}=\frac{2}{-3}.\]

Cross-multiply carefully: \[-3(14-4T)=2(-5+T),\] so \[-42+12T=-10+2T,\qquad 10T=32,\qquad T=3.2.\] The ratio method earns the direction marks because it eliminates the unknown positive scale factor. An equivalent route is \(\mathbf v(T)=\lambda(2\mathbf i-3\mathbf j)\) and solving the two component equations for \(T\) and \(\lambda\).

Orientation check. Substitution gives \[\mathbf v(3.2)=1.2\mathbf i-1.8\mathbf j=0.6(2\mathbf i-3\mathbf j).\] The scale factor \(0.6\) is positive, so the velocity is in the stated direction, not merely parallel in the opposite orientation. Also \((-1.8)/(1.2)=-3/2\), confirming the ratio exactly. Setting the components equal to \(2\) and \(-3\) would wrongly impose a particular speed.

OFFICIAL MS REFERENCE ONLY: \(\boxed{T=3.2\ \mathrm s}\). No live completion is claimed; lesson status remains REVIEWED ONLY / DEFERRED.
Transfer trigger. Build \(\mathbf v(T)=\mathbf u+\mathbf aT\), equate component ratios or introduce a scale factor, solve, and then check that the multiplier is positive. This final sign check distinguishes “in the direction of” from anti-parallel motion.
