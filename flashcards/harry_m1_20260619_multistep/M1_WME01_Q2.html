Packet Q1 / Official WME01 Q2 — direct collision and impulse

From the paper. Two particles collide directly along a line. Particle A has mass \(3m\) and particle B has mass \(m\); each approaches the other at \(1.5\ \mathrm{m\,s^{-1}}\). Find the speeds after impact, then the magnitude of the impulse on B.

Part (a) · speeds after impact5
Carry-in / given: Take A’s initial direction as positive; label post-collision speeds \(v_A=x\), \(v_B=x+1\).

Apply conservation of linear momentum along the line of impact using signed velocities.

Formula: \(\sum mu=\sum mv\).

\((3m)(1.5)+m(-1.5)=3mx+m(x+1)\).

\(4.5-1.5=3x+x+1\ \Rightarrow\ 3=4x+1\ \Rightarrow\ x=\tfrac12\).

\(v_A=\tfrac12\ \mathrm{m\,s^{-1}}=0.5\ \mathrm{m\,s^{-1}}\), \(v_B=x+1=\tfrac32\ \mathrm{m\,s^{-1}}=1.5\ \mathrm{m\,s^{-1}}\).

\(v_A=0.5\ \mathrm{m\,s^{-1}},\ v_B=1.5\ \mathrm{m\,s^{-1}}\).

Sense check: both speeds are positive, consistent with the chosen direction.

Trap: dropping B’s negative initial sign; two unlabelled numbers do not say which is A and which is B — label them. (Earlier-guide claim of a live slip to \(x=2.75,\ v_B=3.75\) is removed: the recording shows only the correct values.)
Part (b) · magnitude of impulse on B3
Carry-in / given: Carry-in: \(v_B=1.5\ \mathrm{m\,s^{-1}}\) after, \(u_B=-1.5\ \mathrm{m\,s^{-1}}\) before.
Algebra-only: no spatial, graphical, or statistical diagram is needed for this reasoning.

Impulse on B is B’s change of momentum; “magnitude” means a positive quantity.

Formula: \(I_B=m(v_B-u_B)\).

\(I_B=m\!\left(\tfrac32-(-1.5)\right)=m\!\left(\tfrac32+\tfrac32\right)=3m\ \mathrm{N\,s}\).

\(\lvert I_B\rvert=3m\ \mathrm{N\,s}\).

Sense check: magnitude is positive; the modulus bars are dropped in the final answer.

Trap: taking impulse on the wrong particle, or leaving the modulus bars / a sign. The question asks for the impulse on B.
