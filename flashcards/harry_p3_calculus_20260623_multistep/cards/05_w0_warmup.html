Standalone question

The curve y = x^2 + ax + b meets the line y = x + 1 at x = 2 and x = 4. Find a and b.

Concept visual - supports the stated method; it does not add a different question or solution.

Multi-step working

Step 1 - Equate at meeting points

\(x^2+ax+b=x+1.\)

Step 2 - Substitute x=2

\(4+2a+b=3\ \Rightarrow\ 2a+b=-1.\)

Step 3 - Substitute x=4

\(16+4a+b=5\ \Rightarrow\ 4a+b=-11.\)

Step 4 - Subtract

\(2a=-10\ \Rightarrow\ a=-5,\ b=9.\)

Recall answer: a = -5, b = 9. No official mark total (paper unresolved).
