Q2 — Complex Numbers: Cubic with Given Root

Question

$f(z) = 4z^3 + pz^2 + qz + 108$, where $z = -3$ is a root. (a) Find $p$. (b) Solve $f(z) = 0$ completely. (c) Modulus of each root. (d) Argand diagram.

[10 marks]

Solution 4 steps

STEP 1/4

Step 1 — Factor theorem to find $p$:

$f(z) = 4z^3 + pz^2 + qz + 108$, given $z = -3$ is a root.

$f(-3) = 0$:

$4(-27) + 9p - 3q + 108 = 0$

$-108 + 9p - 3q + 108 = 0 \Rightarrow 9p - 3q = 0 \Rightarrow q = 3p$

(Need a second condition from the question to find both $p$ and $q$.)

$q = 3p$ (need second equation to solve)
↳ from STEP 1:$p$ and $q$ found
STEP 2/4

Step 2 — Polynomial long division:

Divide $f(z)$ by $(z + 3)$ to get a quadratic $Q(z)$.

Must show working for full marks — calculator can check but not replace.

Phuc's question: "I don't need to do factor stuff, right?" Fuat: You CAN check from calculator, but must show polynomial long division.
Quadratic factor $Q(z)$ obtained
↳ from STEP 2:Quadratic $Q(z)$
STEP 3/4

Step 3 — Solve the quadratic:

Use quadratic formula on $Q(z)$. If discriminant $< 0$, roots are complex conjugates $a \pm bi$.

Key rule: For polynomials with real coefficients, complex roots always come in conjugate pairs.

All three roots found: one real ($z = -3$), two complex conjugates
STEP 4/4

Step 4 — Modulus and Argand diagram:

For $z = a + bi$: $|z| = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2}$

Both conjugate roots have the same modulus.

Argand diagram: Real root on $x$-axis. Complex conjugates symmetric about $x$-axis.

Argand rules: $x$-axis = real, $y$-axis = imaginary. Label each point. Conjugate pair = symmetric about $x$-axis.
