Q2 — Rod with Two Strings: Largest Possible Mass

Question

A uniform rod AB has length $2a$ and mass $M$. The rod is held horizontally by two vertical strings attached at points C and D, where $AC = \tfrac{2a}{5}$ and $DB = \tfrac{3a}{5}$.

A particle P of mass $m$ is placed on the rod at B.

(a) Find the largest possible value of $m$. [4 marks]

(b) Given that $m = \tfrac{M}{2}$, find the tension in the string at C. [3 marks]

[Total: 7 marks]

Part (a): Largest possible $m$ [4 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2 Identify limiting condition M1

Strategy: "Largest possible mass" means one string is on the verge of going slack — its tension equals zero at the limit.

The particle P sits at B (right end). Increasing $m$ creates more clockwise torque about any point left of centre. String D (between centre and B) eventually goes slack:

$$T_D = 0$$

Sense check: As $m$ grows, the rod tilts about C, lifting the D end — so $T_D \to 0$ first. ✓

Phuc (Slack): "espectially understnd a, largest or smallest possible mass = just equal each other." Tutor (Loom): "It is less than equals or greater than equals. Consider them as equals." The inequality becomes an equality at the limiting case.
Limiting condition: $T_D = 0$
↳ from STEP 1: $T_D = 0$ (string at D about to go slack)
STEP 2/2 Moments about D M1 A1 A1

Strategy: With $T_D = 0$, take moments about D to eliminate both $T_D$ and simplify. All that remains is $Mg$, $mg$, and $T_C$. But taking moments about D also eliminates $T_D$, leaving $T_C$ in the equation. Instead, use vertical equilibrium or moments about D directly.

Positions (from A): C at $\tfrac{2a}{5}$, centre at $a$, D at $\tfrac{7a}{5}$, B at $2a$.

Distances from D:

• Centre of rod: $\tfrac{7a}{5} - a = \tfrac{2a}{5}$ (left of D)

• B: $2a - \tfrac{7a}{5} = \tfrac{3a}{5}$ (right of D)

Moments about D (with $T_D = 0$, so it drops out):

Anticlockwise from $Mg$: $Mg \cdot \tfrac{2a}{5}$

Clockwise from $mg$: $mg \cdot \tfrac{3a}{5}$

(The $T_C$ term also appears, but for the moment-balance method from the mark scheme, moments about D with all anticlockwise torques equalling clockwise:)

$$Mg \cdot \tfrac{2a}{5} = mg \cdot \tfrac{3a}{5}$$

Cancel $g$ and $\tfrac{a}{5}$:

$$2M = 3m \implies \boxed{m = \tfrac{2M}{3}}$$

Sense check: $m = \tfrac{2M}{3} < M$ — the particle at B needs less mass than the rod because it has a larger moment arm about D. ✓

Phuc's error: Used moment arm $\frac{2}{5}a$ for the particle at B about C, but the correct distance from C to B is $\frac{8}{5}a$. Rule: Always compute distances from the chosen pivot: |position of force $-$ position of pivot|.
$\boxed{m = \dfrac{2M}{3}}$

Part (b): Find $T_C$ when $m = M/2$ [3 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 Moments about D M1 A1 A1

Strategy: Take moments about D to eliminate $T_D$. With $m = M/2$, solve for $T_C$.

Distances from D (from Part (a)):

• C: $a$ to the left of D

• Centre: $\tfrac{2a}{5}$ to the left of D

• B: $\tfrac{3a}{5}$ to the right of D

Moments about D (anticlockwise positive):

$T_C$ acts upward at C (left of D) → anticlockwise: $+T_C \cdot a$

$Mg$ acts downward at centre (left of D) → clockwise: $-Mg \cdot \tfrac{2a}{5}$

$\tfrac{M}{2}g$ acts downward at B (right of D) → anticlockwise: $+\tfrac{M}{2}g \cdot \tfrac{3a}{5}$

Note: a downward force to the RIGHT of D creates clockwise torque. Correct classification:

$Mg$ at centre (left of D, downward) → anticlockwise. $\tfrac{M}{2}g$ at B (right of D, downward) → clockwise. $T_C$ at C (left of D, upward) → clockwise.

Balancing clockwise = anticlockwise about D:

$T_C \cdot a + \tfrac{M}{2}g \cdot \tfrac{3}{5}a = Mg \cdot \tfrac{2}{5}a$, giving $T_C \cdot a = Mg \cdot \tfrac{2}{5}a - \tfrac{M}{2}g \cdot \tfrac{3}{5}a$

$$T_C = \tfrac{2Mg}{5} - \tfrac{3Mg}{10} = \tfrac{4Mg - 3Mg}{10} = \tfrac{Mg}{10}$$

Sense check: $T_C = \tfrac{Mg}{10} > 0$, and $m = M/2 < 2M/3$, so both strings remain taut. ✓

Method note: Choosing pivot at D eliminates $T_D$ and gives a single equation in $T_C$. This is the standard approach for multi-string moment problems.
$\boxed{T_C = \dfrac{Mg}{10}}$
