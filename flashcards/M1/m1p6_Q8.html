Q8 — Ships: Position Vectors & Bearing

Question

Ship A has velocity $(3\mathbf{i} + 12\mathbf{j})$ km/h. Ship B has velocity $(p\mathbf{i} + q\mathbf{j})$ km/h.

At noon: A is at position $(-9\mathbf{i} + 6\mathbf{j})$ km, B is at position $(16\mathbf{i} + 6\mathbf{j})$ km.

At time $t$ hours after noon, $\overrightarrow{AB} = [(25 - 12t)\mathbf{i} - 9t\mathbf{j}]$ km.

(a) Find the speed of A. [1 mark]

(b) Find the values of $p$ and $q$. [4 marks]

(c) Find the bearing of B from A when the ships are 15 km apart. [7 marks]

[Total: 12 marks]

Part (a): Speed of A [1 mark] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 Magnitude of velocity B1

Strategy: Speed = magnitude of velocity vector.

$$\text{Speed of A} = |3\mathbf{i} + 12\mathbf{j}| = \sqrt{3^2 + 12^2} = \sqrt{9 + 144} = \sqrt{153}$$ $$= \sqrt{9 \times 17} = 3\sqrt{17} \approx 12.4 \text{ km/h}$$

Sense check: A reasonable ship speed. ✓

$\boxed{3\sqrt{17} \approx 12.4 \text{ km/h}}$

Part (b): Find $p$ and $q$ [4 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 Compare coefficients of $\overrightarrow{AB}$ M1 A1 A1 A1

Strategy: Write position vectors for A and B at time $t$, compute $\overrightarrow{AB} = \mathbf{r}_B - \mathbf{r}_A$, then compare with the given expression.

Position vectors:

$$\mathbf{r}_A = (-9 + 3t)\mathbf{i} + (6 + 12t)\mathbf{j}$$ $$\mathbf{r}_B = (16 + pt)\mathbf{i} + (6 + qt)\mathbf{j}$$

Compute $\overrightarrow{AB}$:

$$\overrightarrow{AB} = \mathbf{r}_B - \mathbf{r}_A = [25 + (p-3)t]\mathbf{i} + [(q-12)t]\mathbf{j}$$

Compare with given: $\overrightarrow{AB} = (25 - 12t)\mathbf{i} - 9t\mathbf{j}$

Coefficient of $t$ in $\mathbf{i}$: $p - 3 = -12 \implies \boxed{p = -9}$

Coefficient of $t$ in $\mathbf{j}$: $q - 12 = -9 \implies \boxed{q = 3}$

Sense check: Constant term: $25 = 16 - (-9) = 25$ ✓. Ship B moves at $(-9\mathbf{i} + 3\mathbf{j})$, heading west and slightly north. ✓

Phuc's error (Loom): Set up $\overrightarrow{AB}$ but dropped the $\mathbf{j}$-component, failing to compare both coefficient pairs. Tutor: "AB, B minus A. So in reality..." — the tutor had to guide through completing the coefficient comparison for both components.
$\boxed{p = -9, \quad q = 3}$

Part (c): Bearing when 15 km apart [7 marks] · 3 steps

↳ from Part (b): $\overrightarrow{AB} = (25 - 12t)\mathbf{i} - 9t\mathbf{j}$
STEP 1/3 Set $|\overrightarrow{AB}| = 15$ and solve for $t$ M1 A1 M1

Strategy: Set $|\overrightarrow{AB}|^2 = 225$ and solve the resulting quadratic in $t$.

$$|\overrightarrow{AB}|^2 = (25 - 12t)^2 + (9t)^2 = 225$$

Expand:

$$625 - 600t + 144t^2 + 81t^2 = 225$$ $$225t^2 - 600t + 400 = 0$$

Divide by 25:

$$9t^2 - 24t + 16 = 0$$ $$(3t - 4)^2 = 0$$ $$t = \tfrac{4}{3} \text{ hours}$$

Sense check: The discriminant is zero — the ships are exactly 15 km apart at one unique instant. This means 15 km is the minimum distance. ✓

Key insight: A perfect square means the distance of 15 km is achieved at exactly one moment — this is the closest approach distance. The quadratic has a repeated root.
$t = \tfrac{4}{3}$ hours (1 hour 20 minutes after noon)
↳ from STEP 1: $t = 4/3$ hours
STEP 2/3 Find $\overrightarrow{AB}$ at $t = 4/3$ A1

Strategy: Substitute $t = 4/3$ into $\overrightarrow{AB}$ to get the displacement vector from A to B.

$$\overrightarrow{AB} = \left(25 - 12 \times \tfrac{4}{3}\right)\mathbf{i} - 9 \times \tfrac{4}{3}\;\mathbf{j}$$ $$= (25 - 16)\mathbf{i} - 12\mathbf{j}$$ $$= 9\mathbf{i} - 12\mathbf{j}$$

Meaning: B is 9 km east and 12 km south of A.

Verification: $|\overrightarrow{AB}| = \sqrt{81 + 144} = \sqrt{225} = 15$ ✓

$\overrightarrow{AB} = 9\mathbf{i} - 12\mathbf{j}$ (B is 9 E, 12 S of A)
↳ from STEP 2: $\overrightarrow{AB} = 9\mathbf{i} - 12\mathbf{j}$ (9 E, 12 S)
STEP 3/3 Convert to bearing M1 A1 A1

Strategy: Convert the displacement vector to a bearing. Bearings are measured clockwise from North ($+\mathbf{j}$).

$\overrightarrow{AB} = 9\mathbf{i} - 12\mathbf{j}$: this points east ($+\mathbf{i}$) and south ($-\mathbf{j}$), placing B in the SE quadrant from A.

Angle from South toward East:

$$\alpha = \arctan\left(\frac{9}{12}\right) = \arctan\left(\frac{3}{4}\right) \approx 36.87°$$

This is the angle east of south. The bearing (clockwise from North) is:

$$\text{Bearing} = 180° - 36.87° + 36.87° = 180° + 36.87°$$

Correction: the vector points to the SE quadrant. Starting from North and going clockwise:

North → East = 90°. Then from East toward South by $\arctan(12/9) = \arctan(4/3) \approx 53.13°$.

So bearing = $90° + 53.13° = 143.13°$.

Alternatively: $\tan\theta = \frac{9}{12} = \frac{3}{4}$, where $\theta$ is measured from South toward East. Bearing = $180° - \theta = 180° - 36.87° = 143.13°$.

Rounding to nearest degree: $\boxed{143°}$

$$\theta = \arctan\left(\frac{9}{12}\right) = \arctan(0.75) \approx 36.87°$$

This is $36.87°$ west of north. Bearing = $360° - 36.87° \approx 323°$.

$$\boxed{\text{Bearing} \approx 323°}$$
