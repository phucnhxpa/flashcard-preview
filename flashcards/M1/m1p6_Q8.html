Q8 — Ships: Position Vectors & Bearing

Question

Ship A velocity $(3\mathbf{i}+12\mathbf{j})$; ship B velocity $(p\mathbf{i}+q\mathbf{j})$. At noon: A at $(-9\mathbf{i}+6\mathbf{j})$, B at $(16\mathbf{i}+6\mathbf{j})$. Given $\overrightarrow{AB} = [(25-12t)\mathbf{i} - 9t\mathbf{j}]$. Find $p$, $q$, and bearing when 15 km apart.

[12 marks]

Solution 4 steps

STEP 1/4

Part (a) — Speed of ship A:

Ship A velocity $(3\mathbf{i}+12\mathbf{j})$.

$$\text{Speed} = |3\mathbf{i}+12\mathbf{j}| = \sqrt{9+144} = \sqrt{153} = 3\sqrt{17}$$

Speed of A $= \boxed{3\sqrt{17} \approx 12.4 \text{ km/h}}$
↳ from STEP 1:Position vectors at time $t$
STEP 2/4

Part (b) — Find $p$ and $q$ by coefficient comparison:

$\mathbf{r}_A = (-9+3t)\mathbf{i} + (6+12t)\mathbf{j}$

$\mathbf{r}_B = (16+pt)\mathbf{i} + (6+qt)\mathbf{j}$

$\overrightarrow{AB} = \mathbf{r}_B - \mathbf{r}_A = [25+(p-3)t]\mathbf{i} + [(q-12)t]\mathbf{j}$

Given: $\overrightarrow{AB} = [(25-12t)\mathbf{i} - 9t\mathbf{j}]$

Compare coefficients: $p - 3 = -12$, $q - 12 = -9$

Phuc's error: Set up $\overrightarrow{AB}$ but dropped the $\mathbf{j}$-component. Always compare BOTH components!
$\boxed{p = -9}$, $\boxed{q = 3}$
↳ from STEP 2:$p = -9, q = 3$
STEP 3/4

Part (c) — When ships are 15 km apart:

$|\overrightarrow{AB}|^2 = (25-12t)^2 + (9t)^2 = 225$

$625 - 600t + 144t^2 + 81t^2 = 225$

$225t^2 - 600t + 400 = 0$

$(15t - 20)^2 = 0 \Rightarrow (3t - 4)^2 = 0$

$t = \frac{4}{3}$ hours (exactly one instant — discriminant = 0)
↳ from STEP 3:$\overrightarrow{BA} = -9\mathbf{i} + 12\mathbf{j}$
STEP 4/4

Part (c) continued — Bearing:

At $t = 4/3$: $\overrightarrow{AB} = 9\mathbf{i} - 12\mathbf{j}$

$\overrightarrow{BA} = -9\mathbf{i} + 12\mathbf{j}$ (9 km W, 12 km N)

$\theta = \arctan(9/12) \approx 36.87°$ west of north

Bearing $= 360° - 36.87°$

Bearing rule: Bearings are measured clockwise from North. If the direction is west of north, bearing $= 360° - \theta$. Discriminant $= 0$ means ships reach that distance at exactly one instant (tangent condition).
Bearing $\approx \boxed{323°}$
