Q5 — Vertical Projectile (Speed Thresholds)

Question

Ball projected up at 29.4 m/s from A, 19.6 m above ground. (a) Distance while speed $< 14.7$ m/s. (b) Time with speed $> 29.4$ m/s. (c) Speed-time graph.

[10 marks]

Solution 3 steps

STEP 1/3

Part (a) — Distance while speed $< 14.7$ m/s:

Ball projected up at 29.4 m/s from A, 19.6 m above ground.

Find height where speed = 14.7: $v^2 = u^2 - 2gs$

$$14.7^2 = 29.4^2 - 2(9.8)h \Rightarrow h = 33.075 \text{ m}$$

Max height: $\frac{29.4^2}{2 \times 9.8} = 44.1$ m

Speed $< 14.7$ between $h = 33.075$ and $h = 44.1$ (up and back down).

Distance $= 2(44.1 - 33.075) = \boxed{22.05 \text{ m}}$
↳ from STEP 1:Speed = 29.4 at $t = 6$ s (back at A)
STEP 2/3

Part (b) — Time with speed $> 29.4$ m/s:

Key insight: Speed exceeds initial speed ONLY when ball falls below launch point A.

Time to return to A: $t_1 = \frac{2 \times 29.4}{9.8} = 6$ s.

From A to ground (19.6 m below): $19.6 = 29.4t + \frac{1}{2}(9.8)t^2$

Solve: $t \approx 0.6$ s.

Phuc's confusion: Wrote "29.4" with no working. Speed $>$ initial speed is only possible after passing A on the way down — the ball gains extra speed from the 19.6 m drop.
Time with speed $> 29.4$ m/s: $\approx \boxed{0.6 \text{ s}}$
STEP 3/3

Part (c) — Speed-time graph:

Shape: V-shape starting at $v = 29.4$, drops to $0$ at peak ($t = 3$ s), rises back to $29.4$ at $t = 6$ s, then continues to $\sim 33.3$ m/s at ground.

Speed at ground: $v = \sqrt{29.4^2 + 2(9.8)(19.6)} \approx 33.3$ m/s.

Speed vs velocity: Speed is always positive (absolute value of velocity). The graph is V-shaped, not a straight line crossing zero.
V-shape: $29.4 \to 0 \to 29.4 \to 33.3$ m/s
