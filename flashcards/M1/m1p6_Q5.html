Q5 — Vertical Projectile: Speed Thresholds

Question

A small ball is projected vertically upwards with speed 29.4 m/s from a point A, which is 19.6 m above horizontal ground. The ball moves freely under gravity until it hits the ground. The ball does not rebound.

(a) Find the distance travelled by the ball while its speed is less than 14.7 m/s. [4 marks]

(b) Find the time for which the speed of the ball is greater than 29.4 m/s. [5 marks]

(c) Sketch a speed–time graph for the motion. [3 marks]

[Total: 12 marks]

Part (a): Distance while speed $< 14.7$ m/s [4 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2 Find heights where speed = 14.7 M1 A1

Strategy: Use $v^2 = u^2 - 2gs$ (upward positive) to find the height above A where the speed equals 14.7 m/s. The ball passes through this speed twice — once going up, once coming down.

SUVAT: $v^2 = u^2 - 2gs$ with $u = 29.4$, $|v| = 14.7$, $g = 9.8$:

$$14.7^2 = 29.4^2 - 2(9.8)s$$ $$216.09 = 864.36 - 19.6s$$ $$19.6s = 648.27$$ $$s = 33.075 \text{ m above A}$$

Maximum height above A:

$$s_{\max} = \frac{u^2}{2g} = \frac{29.4^2}{19.6} = \frac{864.36}{19.6} = 44.1 \text{ m}$$

Sense check: The speed-threshold height (33.075 m) is below the peak (44.1 m). ✓

Speed = 14.7 at height 33.075 m; peak at 44.1 m above A
↳ from STEP 1: Speed = 14.7 at 33.075 m; peak at 44.1 m
STEP 2/2 Calculate total distance M1 A1

Strategy: The ball has speed $< 14.7$ m/s between heights 33.075 m and 44.1 m above A. It traverses this range twice (up and down).

Distance in one direction:

$$44.1 - 33.075 = 11.025 \text{ m}$$

Total distance (up + down through this zone):

$$2 \times 11.025 = \boxed{22.05 \text{ m}}$$

Sense check: The ball decelerates from 14.7 to 0 over 11.025 m, then accelerates back to 14.7 over the same distance. Total = 22.05 m. ✓

Key concept: "Distance travelled while speed is less than X" means identifying the zone where speed is below the threshold, then counting the distance twice if the ball passes through the zone in both directions (up and back).
$\boxed{22.05 \text{ m}}$

Part (b): Time with speed $> 29.4$ m/s [5 marks] · 3 steps

STEP 1/3 Identify when speed exceeds 29.4 M1

Strategy: The ball is launched at 29.4 m/s upward. During the upward journey and return to A, the speed is always $\leq 29.4$ m/s (it decelerates, stops, then re-accelerates back to launch speed at A). Speed only exceeds 29.4 m/s after the ball passes below A on the way down.

Key insight: Speed $> 29.4$ m/s is only possible when the ball falls below the launch point A, because it has gained additional kinetic energy from the extra 19.6 m drop to the ground.

Sense check: At A on the way down, the ball has the same speed as launch (29.4 m/s) by energy conservation. Below A, it goes faster. ✓

STEP 2/3 Time to return to A M1 A1

Strategy: Find the time from launch until the ball returns to A (displacement = 0). The ball goes up and comes back.

Using $s = ut - \tfrac{1}{2}gt^2$ with $s = 0$ (back at A):

$$0 = 29.4t - 4.9t^2 = t(29.4 - 4.9t)$$ $$t = 0 \text{ (launch)} \quad \text{or} \quad t = \frac{29.4}{4.9} = 6 \text{ s}$$

Part (c): Speed–time graph [3 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 Sketch the speed–time graph B1 B1 B1

Strategy: Speed = |velocity|. The velocity is linear ($v = u - gt$), so the speed graph is a V-shape reflected at the points where velocity passes through zero.

Key features:
