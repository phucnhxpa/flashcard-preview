Q4 — Lift / Elevator Problem (N2L + N3L)

Question

A lift of mass 250 kg is raised vertically by a cable with tension 3616 N. Two people (Alan and Bhavana) stand inside. The floor exerts forces of 565 N on Alan and 226 N on Bhavana. The lift accelerates upward at constant acceleration.

(a) Find the acceleration of the lift. [3 marks]

(b) Find the mass of Alan. [3 marks]

[Total: 6 marks]

Part (a): Find acceleration [3 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 N2L on lift only (with N3L) M1 A1 A1

Strategy: Apply $F = ma$ to the lift alone. By Newton's Third Law, the passengers push DOWN on the lift floor with the same forces the floor exerts UP on them (565 N and 226 N).

Forces on the lift (positive upward):

• Cable tension: $+3616$ N (upward)

• Weight of lift: $-250g = -250 \times 9.8 = -2450$ N

• Reaction from Alan (N3L): $-565$ N (Alan pushes down on floor)

• Reaction from Bhavana (N3L): $-226$ N (Bhavana pushes down on floor)

N2L on the lift:

$$3616 - 2450 - 565 - 226 = 250a$$ $$375 = 250a$$ $$\boxed{a = 1.5 \text{ m/s}^2}$$

Sense check: Positive acceleration confirms the lift accelerates upward, consistent with cable tension exceeding total downward forces. ✓

Tutor (Loom): "It is better to consider this one as the single object... the lift. We need to show that [passengers push down on the lift by N3L]." The 565 N and 226 N act DOWN on the lift because these are the N3L pairs of the floor's upward push on the passengers.
$\boxed{a = 1.5 \text{ m/s}^2}$

Part (b): Find Alan's mass [3 marks] · 1 step

↳ from Part (a): $a = 1.5$ m/s²
STEP 1/1 N2L on Alan alone M1 A1 A1

Strategy: Isolate Alan as a separate particle. Apply $F = ma$ vertically with the floor reaction (565 N upward) and his weight (downward).

Forces on Alan (positive upward):

• Floor reaction: $+565$ N

• Weight: $-mg$

N2L on Alan:

$$565 - mg = ma$$ $$565 = m(g + a) = m(9.8 + 1.5) = 11.3m$$ $$m = \frac{565}{11.3}$$ $$\boxed{m = 50 \text{ kg}}$$

Sense check: 50 kg is a reasonable human mass. The apparent weight (565 N) exceeds $mg = 490$ N because the lift accelerates upward. ✓
