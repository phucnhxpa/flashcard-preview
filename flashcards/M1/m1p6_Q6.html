Q6 — Direction Vector as Force Constraint

Question

Particle (0.5 kg) at rest. Forces $\mathbf{F}_1 = (-3\mathbf{i}+2\mathbf{j})$ and $\mathbf{F}_2 = (p\mathbf{i}+q\mathbf{j})$. Motion along $(\mathbf{i} - 2\mathbf{j})$. (a) Show $2p + q - 4 = 0$. (b) Given $p = 5$, find speed at $t = 4$.

[8 marks]

Solution 3 steps

STEP 1/3

Part (a) — Direction vector as constraint:

Particle (0.5 kg) at rest. Forces $\mathbf{F}_1 = (-3\mathbf{i}+2\mathbf{j})$ N and $\mathbf{F}_2 = (p\mathbf{i}+q\mathbf{j})$ N.

Moves in direction $(\mathbf{i} - 2\mathbf{j})$.

Method: Resultant $\mathbf{R} = (p-3)\mathbf{i} + (q+2)\mathbf{j}$ must be parallel to $(\mathbf{i} - 2\mathbf{j})$.

$$\frac{q+2}{p-3} = \frac{-2}{1}$$

$$q + 2 = -2(p-3) = 6 - 2p$$

Phuc's insight: "The direction vector is the clue — we use that direction vector as the information to find the unknowns."
Error: Wrote "$2p + 9 - q = 0$" instead of "$2p + q - 4 = 0$" (handwriting confusion 4/9).
$\boxed{2p + q - 4 = 0}$
↳ from STEP 1:$p = 5, q = -6$
STEP 2/3

Part (b) — Find velocity components:

Given $p = 5$: $q = 4 - 2(5) = -6$

$\mathbf{R} = (5-3)\mathbf{i} + (-6+2)\mathbf{j} = 2\mathbf{i} - 4\mathbf{j}$

$\mathbf{a} = \frac{\mathbf{R}}{m} = \frac{2\mathbf{i} - 4\mathbf{j}}{0.5} = 4\mathbf{i} - 8\mathbf{j}$ m/s²

$\mathbf{a} = 4\mathbf{i} - 8\mathbf{j}$ m/s²
↳ from STEP 2:$\mathbf{a} = 4\mathbf{i} - 8\mathbf{j}$
STEP 3/3

Find speed at $t = 4$:

$\mathbf{v}(4) = \mathbf{u} + \mathbf{a}t = 0 + 4(4\mathbf{i} - 8\mathbf{j}) = 16\mathbf{i} - 32\mathbf{j}$

Speed $= |\mathbf{v}| = \sqrt{16^2 + 32^2} = \sqrt{256 + 1024} = \sqrt{1280}$

Direction constraint rule: "Moves in direction $(a\mathbf{i} + b\mathbf{j})$" means $\mathbf{R} = \lambda(a\mathbf{i} + b\mathbf{j})$ for $\lambda > 0$. Set $R_j/R_i = b/a$.
Speed $= \boxed{16\sqrt{5} \approx 35.8 \text{ m/s}}$
