Q6 — Direction Vector as Force Constraint

Question

A particle A of mass 0.5 kg is initially at rest. Two forces act on A:

$\mathbf{F}_1 = (-3\mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j})$ N and $\mathbf{F}_2 = (p\mathbf{i} + q\mathbf{j})$ N.

(a) Given that A moves in the direction of $(\mathbf{i} - 2\mathbf{j})$, show that $2p + q - 4 = 0$. [3 marks]

(b) Given also that $p = 5$, find the speed of A at $t = 4$ s. [4 marks]

[Total: 7 marks]

Part (a): Show $2p + q - 4 = 0$ [3 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 Direction constraint on resultant M1 A1 A1

Strategy: "Moves in the direction of $(\mathbf{i} - 2\mathbf{j})$" means the resultant force $\mathbf{R}$ is parallel to $(\mathbf{i} - 2\mathbf{j})$. This gives a ratio constraint on the components of $\mathbf{R}$.

Resultant force:

$$\mathbf{R} = \mathbf{F}_1 + \mathbf{F}_2 = (-3 + p)\mathbf{i} + (2 + q)\mathbf{j}$$

Direction constraint: $\mathbf{R} = \lambda(\mathbf{i} - 2\mathbf{j})$ for some $\lambda > 0$. The ratio of $\mathbf{j}$-component to $\mathbf{i}$-component must equal $-2/1$:

$$\frac{2 + q}{p - 3} = \frac{-2}{1} = -2$$ $$2 + q = -2(p - 3) = -2p + 6$$ $$2p + q + 2 - 6 = 0$$ $$\boxed{2p + q - 4 = 0} \quad \checkmark$$

Sense check: The equation is linear in $p$ and $q$, representing a line of valid $(p, q)$ pairs. ✓

Part (b): Speed at $t = 4$ given $p = 5$ [4 marks] · 2 steps

↳ from Part (a): $2p + q - 4 = 0$
STEP 1/2 Find acceleration vector M1 A1

Strategy: Substitute $p = 5$ into the constraint to find $q$, then compute $\mathbf{a} = \mathbf{R}/m$.

From $2(5) + q - 4 = 0$: $q = 4 - 10 = -6$.

Resultant force:

$$\mathbf{R} = (-3 + 5)\mathbf{i} + (2 + (-6))\mathbf{j} = 2\mathbf{i} - 4\mathbf{j}$$

Acceleration ($m = 0.5$ kg):

$$\mathbf{a} = \frac{\mathbf{R}}{m} = \frac{2\mathbf{i} - 4\mathbf{j}}{0.5} = 4\mathbf{i} - 8\mathbf{j} \text{ m/s}^2$$

Sense check: $\mathbf{a}$ is in the direction of $(\mathbf{i} - 2\mathbf{j})$ as expected. ✓

$\mathbf{a} = 4\mathbf{i} - 8\mathbf{j}$ m/s², $q = -6$
↳ from STEP 1: $\mathbf{a} = 4\mathbf{i} - 8\mathbf{j}$ m/s²
STEP 2/2 Find speed at $t = 4$ M1 A1

Strategy: A starts from rest, so $\mathbf{v} = \mathbf{a}t$. Find the magnitude at $t = 4$.

Velocity at $t = 4$:

$$\mathbf{v} = 4(4\mathbf{i} - 8\mathbf{j}) = 16\mathbf{i} - 32\mathbf{j}$$

Speed:

$$|\mathbf{v}| = \sqrt{16^2 + 32^2} = \sqrt{256 + 1024} = \sqrt{1280}$$ $$= \sqrt{256 \times 5} = 16\sqrt{5} \approx 35.8 \text{ m/s}$$ $$\boxed{|\mathbf{v}| = 16\sqrt{5} \approx 35.8 \text{ m/s}}$$

Sense check: Acceleration magnitude = $\sqrt{16 + 64} = \sqrt{80} \approx 8.94$ m/s². After 4 s from rest: speed $\approx 8.94 \times 4 = 35.8$ m/s. ✓
