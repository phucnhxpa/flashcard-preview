S1 Q1 — regression and correlation

Source boundary: [Official reference] WST01/01 October 2025 values as reproduced in the final guide.
Status: [Lesson evidence] [Completed with prompting].
Standalone guide-derived question wording

Eight horses have heights $h$ (cm) and weights $w$ (kg). Given $\sum h=1160$, $\sum w=4160$, $\sum hw=604135$, $\sum w^2=2166600$, $S_{hh}=286$ and $n=8$: calculate $S_{hw}$ and $S_{ww}$; find $r$; obtain the regression line of $w$ on $h$; interpret its gradient; estimate for $h=153$ and $h=170$; and classify the reliability of these estimates.

Visual boundary: algebra-only; no diagram is needed for this regression/correlation calculation.

Part (a) — deviation sums

3 marks · 2 step(s)

Formula

Use $S_{hw}=\sum hw-\dfrac{\sum h\sum w}{n}$ and $S_{ww}=\sum w^2-\dfrac{(\sum w)^2}{n}$.

$$S_{hw}=604135-\frac{1160\times4160}{8}=935.$$

Sense check: the cross-deviation sum is positive, matching positive association.

Second deviation sum

$$S_{ww}=2166600-\frac{4160^2}{8}=3400.$$

$S_{hw}=935$, $S_{ww}=3400$.

Part (b) — correlation

2 marks · 1 step(s)
Carry-in (visible before masking): $S_{hw}=935$, $S_{hh}=286$, $S_{ww}=3400$.

Formula then substitute

$$r=\frac{S_{hw}}{\sqrt{S_{hh}S_{ww}}}=\frac{935}{\sqrt{286\times3400}}=0.948176\ldots$$

$r=\mathbf{0.948}$ (3 s.f., awrt).

Sense check: $r$ is dimensionless and lies between −1 and 1.

Revision-guide trap: copy the printed $S_{hh}=286$ exactly and give this inexact decimal to 3 significant figures.

Parts (c)–(f) — line, meaning and reliability

6 marks · 2 step(s)
Carry-in (visible before masking): $S_{hw}=935$, $S_{hh}=286$, $\bar h=145$, $\bar w=520$.

Regression line

$$b=\frac{935}{286}=3.2692\ldots\approx3.27,\qquad a=520-3.27(145)=45.96\ldots\approx46.0.$$

$w=46.0+3.27h$.

Interpret and use

On average, each 1 cm increase in height is associated with a 3.27 kg increase in weight.

$h=153$: $w=46.0+3.27(153)=546.31\Rightarrow\boxed{546\text{ kg}}$.

$h=170$: $w=601.9\Rightarrow\boxed{602\text{ kg}}$.

Sense check: 153 is interpolation (more reliable); 170 is extrapolation (less reliable).
