S1 Q8 — tree diagram and Bayes-style reversal

Source boundary: [Official reference] WST01/01 Q8 forward-tree probabilities and results as reproduced in the final guide.
Status: [Lesson evidence] [Completed with prompting].
Standalone guide-derived question wording

A person travels by car with probability $1/2$, bicycle with probability $1/6$, or walking with probability $1/3$. Conditional late probabilities are $1/5$, $1/10$ and $1/20$ respectively. Find $P(L)$, then $P(\mathrm{car}\mid L)$, and give the reversed tree branches conditional on $L$ and $L\prime$.

Part (a) — total late probability

1 mark · 1 step(s)

Add complete path products

$$P(L)=\frac12\cdot\frac15+\frac16\cdot\frac1{10}+\frac13\cdot\frac1{20}=\frac1{10}+\frac1{60}+\frac1{60}.$$

$P(L)=\frac{2}{15}$.

Sense check: sum path probabilities, not the separate late branch labels.

Part (b) — conditional transport given late

2 marks · 1 step(s)
Carry-in (visible before masking): $P(L)=2/15$ and $P(\mathrm{car}\cap L)=\frac12\cdot\frac15=1/10$.

Bayes formula

$$P(\mathrm{car}\mid L)=\frac{P(\mathrm{car})P(L\mid\mathrm{car})}{P(L)}=\frac{1/10}{2/15}=\frac34.$$

$P(\mathrm{car}\mid L)=\frac34$.

Sense check: the numerator is the joint car-and-late probability.

Part (c) — reversed tree

4 marks · 2 step(s)
Carry-in (visible before masking): $P(L)=2/15$, so $P(L\prime)=13/15$. Given late: car $3/4$, bicycle $1/8$, walk $1/8$.

Not-late branches

$$P(\mathrm{car}\mid L\prime)=\frac{(1/2)(4/5)}{13/15}=\frac6{13}.$$

$$P(\mathrm{bike}\mid L\prime)=\frac{(1/6)(9/10)}{13/15}=\frac9{52},\quad P(\mathrm{walk}\mid L\prime)=\frac{(1/3)(19/20)}{13/15}=\frac{19}{52}.$$

Fan check

Given $L$: $\frac34+\frac18+\frac18=1$.

Given $L\prime$: $\frac6{13}+\frac9{52}+\frac{19}{52}=\frac{24+9+19}{52}=1$.

Both reversed-tree fans sum to 1.

Sense check: each set of child branches must exhaust its parent condition.
