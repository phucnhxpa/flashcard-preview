NSAA specimen Q17 — proportional change

Source boundary: [Official reference] Cambridge NSAA Section 1 specimen, Part A Q17; historical ESAT-predecessor practice, not a current ESAT question.
Status: [Lesson evidence] [Completed with prompting].
Standalone guide-derived question wording

A quantity is defined by $M=\dfrac{(x+y)^2z}{P}Q$. Both $x$ and $y$ increase by 50%; $z$ decreases by 20%; $P$ is doubled; $Q$ is unchanged. Find the overall change in $M$.

Visual boundary: algebra-only; no diagram is needed for this proportional-change calculation.

Whole question

Option C · 2 step(s)

Strategy

Replace each quantity by its scale factor in the unchanged expression. Factor the common $1.5$ from $x+y$ before squaring.

$(x+y)\mapsto\frac32(x+y)$, so $(x+y)^2\mapsto\left(\frac32\right)^2(x+y)^2=\frac94(x+y)^2$.

Sense check: increasing both terms in the bracket makes this factor greater than 1.

Combine the remaining factors

$z\mapsto\frac45z$, while doubling $P$ puts a factor $\frac12$ in the quotient; $Q$ has factor 1.

$$\frac{M\prime}{M}=\frac94\times\frac45\times\frac12=\frac9{10}.$$

$M$ becomes 90% of its old value: a 10% decrease, option C.

Revision-guide trap: a doubled denominator contributes $\frac12$; do not send the 2 to the numerator.
