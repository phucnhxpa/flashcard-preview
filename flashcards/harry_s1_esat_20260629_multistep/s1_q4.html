S1 Q4 — discrete distribution, CDF and variance

Source boundary: [Official reference] WST01/01 Q4 distribution and results as reproduced in the final guide.
Status: [Lesson evidence] [Completed with prompting]. This is Q4, not legacy Q3 numbering.
Standalone guide-derived question wording

A discrete random variable $X$ has values $-1,0,1,2,3$ with probabilities $0.05,0.10,a,b,0.35$, and $E(X)=1.8$. Find $a$ and $b$; give $F(x)=P(X\le x)$ at the listed values; find $P(2X<1)$; and find $\mathrm{Var}(5-3X)$.

Part (a) — find a and b

3 marks · 2 step(s)

Total probability and mean

$$0.05+0.10+a+b+0.35=1\Rightarrow a+b=0.5.$$

$$-0.05+a+2b+1.05=1.8\Rightarrow a+2b=0.8.$$

Sense check: both unknown probabilities remain non-negative.

Solve simultaneously

Subtract the first equation from the second: $b=0.3$, then $a=0.2$.

$a=0.20$, $b=0.30$.

Parts (b)–(c) — CDF and threshold

3 marks · 2 step(s)
Carry-in (visible before masking): $P(X=x)$ for $x=-1,0,1,2,3$ is $0.05,0.10,0.20,0.30,0.35$.

Build cumulative totals

$$F(-1)=0.05,\ F(0)=0.15,\ F(1)=0.35,\ F(2)=0.65,\ F(3)=1.$$

Sense check: a CDF never decreases and ends at 1.

Convert the inequality

$2X<1\Leftrightarrow X<0.5$. Since $X$ is discrete on integers, this means $X\le0$.

$P(2X<1)=F(0)=0.15$.

Part (d) — variance transformation

4 marks · 2 step(s)
Carry-in (visible before masking): $E(X)=1.8$ and PMF $0.05,0.10,0.20,0.30,0.35$ at $x=-1,0,1,2,3$.

Formula first

$$E(X^2)=\sum x^2P(X=x)=0.05+0.2+1.2+3.15=4.6.$$

$$\mathrm{Var}(X)=E(X^2)-[E(X)]^2=4.6-1.8^2=1.36.$$

Sense check: do not divide by the five listed values.

Transform the variance

$$\mathrm{Var}(5-3X)=(-3)^2\mathrm{Var}(X)=9(1.36)=12.24.$$

$\mathrm{Var}(5-3X)=\mathbf{12.2}$ (3 s.f.).

Revision-guide trap: subtract $\mu^2$, not $\mu$; a constant shift does not alter variance.
