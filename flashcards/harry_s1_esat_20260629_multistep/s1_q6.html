S1 Q6 — chain Venn, independence and conditional probability

Source boundary: [Official reference] WST01/01 Q6 regions and results as reproduced in the final guide.
Status: [Lesson evidence] [Completed with prompting]. Q6(b) is recorded as a tutor teaching moment, not a learner-error claim.
Standalone guide-derived question wording

A chain Venn diagram has $A$ overlapping $B$, $B$ overlapping $C$, and no $A\cap C$ or triple-overlap region. Its regions are: A-only 0.36, $A\cap B$ 0.24, B-only $x$, $B\cap C$ 0.09, C-only $y$, and outside $z$. Events A and B are independent, and $P(B\mid C\prime)=31/75$. Find $x,z,y$, $P(B)$, and evaluate $P((B\cup C)\prime\cap A)$ and $P((A\cup B)\cap C\prime)$.

Part (a) — independence

3 marks · 1 step(s)

Formula then substitute

$$P(A\cap B)=P(A)P(B).$$ Here $P(A)=0.36+0.24=0.60$ and $P(B)=0.24+x+0.09=0.33+x$.

$$0.24=0.60(0.33+x)\Rightarrow0.4=0.33+x.$$

$x=0.07$ and $P(B)=0.40$.

Sense check: all B regions total $0.24+0.07+0.09=0.40$.

Part (b) — conditional probability

2 marks · 1 step(s)
Carry-in (visible before masking): $x=0.07$. Thus $B\cap C\prime$ contains 0.24 and 0.07, so $P(B\cap C\prime)=0.31$.

Condition on all of C′

$C\prime$ contains A-only, $A\cap B$, B-only and the outside: $P(C\prime)=0.36+0.24+0.07+z=0.67+z$.

$$\frac{0.31}{0.67+z}=\frac{31}{75}\Rightarrow0.67+z=0.75.$$

$z=0.08$.

Sense check: the given $31/75$ is used as the conditional probability; $z$ belongs in its denominator.

Status boundary: the guide describes this setup correction as a tutor teaching moment, not a learner mistake.

Part (c) — complete the probability total

2 marks · 1 step(s)
Carry-in (visible before masking): $x=0.07$, $z=0.08$; known regions are 0.36, 0.24, 0.07, 0.09 and 0.08.

Subtract known regions

$$y=1-0.36-0.24-0.07-0.09-0.08=0.16.$$

$y=0.16$.

Sense check: all six regions now sum to 1.

Part (d) — read regions literally

2 marks · 2 step(s)
Carry-in (visible before masking): Chain geometry: A and C do not overlap; all of $A\cup B$ lies outside C.

First expression

$(B\cup C)\prime\cap A$ is the A-only region.

$P((B\cup C)\prime\cap A)=0.36$.

Second expression

$(A\cup B)\cap C\prime$ contains A-only, $A\cap B$ and B-only.

$$0.36+0.24+0.07=\boxed{0.67}.$$

Sense check: “$\cap$” means both conditions at once.
