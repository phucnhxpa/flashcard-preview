S1 Q2 — box plot, outlier and skew

Source boundary: [Official reference] WST01/01 Q2 values as reproduced in the final guide.
Status: [Lesson evidence] [Completed with prompting].
Standalone guide-derived question wording

For 35 Club A rugby-player weights, $Q_1=95$, median $=105$, $Q_3=112$. The question supplies the rule: an outlier is more than $1.5\times\mathrm{IQR}$ beyond a quartile. Show whether 139 is the only outlier; state the box-plot landmarks; and interpret Club B positive skew using $Q_3-Q_2>Q_2-Q_1$.

Part (a) — IQR

2 marks · 1 step(s)

Find the middle-half spread

$$\mathrm{IQR}=Q_3-Q_1=112-95=17.$$

IQR $=17$ kg.

Sense check: the median 105 lies between Q1 and Q3.

Part (b) — supplied outlier rule

3 marks · 1 step(s)
Carry-in (visible before masking): $Q_1=95$, $Q_3=112$, IQR $=17$.

Apply both fences

$$1.5\times17=25.5,$$ $$\text{lower}=95-25.5=69.5,\quad\text{upper}=112+25.5=137.5.$$

Since $139>137.5$, it lies beyond the upper fence.

139 is the only outlier.

Sense check: use the supplied $1.5\times$IQR rule, not mean $\pm2s$.

Revision-guide wording target: state “only outlier” unambiguously when that is what the question asks.

Parts (c)–(d) — plot landmarks and skew context

6 marks · 2 step(s)
Carry-in (visible before masking): Lower fence 69.5; upper fence 137.5; 139 is plotted separately as the outlier.

Box-plot coordinates

Lower whisker 72; $Q_1=95$; median 105; $Q_3=112$; upper whisker 127; plotted outlier 139.

Sense check: the five-number structure is ordered from 72 to 127, with 139 outside.

Interpret Club B

$Q_3-Q_2>Q_2-Q_1$, so Club B has positive skew.

Club B’s weights are more varied above the median than below it.
