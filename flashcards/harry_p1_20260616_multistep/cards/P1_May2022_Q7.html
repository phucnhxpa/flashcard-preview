Question 7 — Differentiation: find A, then f(x)

9 marksBack to deck
Source class: Reviewed live (differentiation for A, then recover f). The official QP/MS control wording, marks and values. Lesson-specific correction points follow the passed 16 June guide.
Official question

Given \(f'(x)=2x^{-1/2}+Ax^{-2}+3\) with \(f''(4)=0\), find \(A\). Then given \(f(12)=8\sqrt3\), find \(f(x)\). (9)

Part (a)

Carry-in / given: No carry-in: differentiate \(f'(x)\) once more to get \(f''(x)\).
Strategy

Use fractional/negative power rules; set \(f''(4)=0\) and solve for \(A\).

Full working

Step 1 — write powers explicitly. \(f'(x)=2x^{-1/2}+Ax^{-2}+3\). To differentiate, multiply by the current power and subtract 1.

Step 2 — differentiate each term. \(\frac{d}{dx}(2x^{-1/2})=2(-\tfrac12)x^{-3/2}=-x^{-3/2}\). \(\frac{d}{dx}(Ax^{-2})=-2Ax^{-3}\). The constant 3 differentiates to 0. So

\[f''(x)=-x^{-3/2}-2Ax^{-3}.\]

Step 3 — use the condition \(f''(4)=0\). Substitute \(x=4\): \(4^{-3/2}=(4^{1/2})^{-3}=2^{-3}=\tfrac18\), and \(4^{-3}=2^{-6}=\tfrac1{64}\).

\[-4^{-3/2}-2A\cdot4^{-3}=0\ \Rightarrow\ -\tfrac18-\tfrac{A}{32}=0\ \Rightarrow\ A=-4.\]

Step 4 — method matters. Even a correct \(A=-4\) scores 0 if obtained by invalid working (e.g. dropped fractional powers). The justification above is what earns the mark.

Step 5 — exam technique. When differentiating a power with a fractional or negative index, write the index explicitly before applying the rule: \(x^{-1/2}\) becomes \(-\tfrac12x^{-3/2}\), never \(x^{1/2}\). A very common slip is to forget that the coefficient multiplies the new power too, so \(2x^{-1/2}\) differentiates to \(2(-\tfrac12)x^{-3/2}=-x^{-3/2}\), where the 2 and the \(-\tfrac12\) cancel. For negative powers, \(\frac{d}{dx}(Ax^{-2})=-2Ax^{-3}\); keep the minus sign attached to the 2. Finally, evaluate powers of 4 carefully: \(4^{-3/2}\) means \((4^{1/2})^{-3}=2^{-3}=\tfrac18\), not \(-8\). Mistakes at this arithmetic stage are the usual reason a correct method still yields the wrong \(A\).

Revision focus. Reaching \(A=-4\) by invalid working (scores 0); or a dropped fractional power.

Part (b)

Carry-in / given: Carry-in: \(A=-4\), so \(f'(x)=2x^{-1/2}-4x^{-2}+3\), and \(f(12)=8\sqrt3\).
Strategy

Integrate term by term, add \(c\), then use \(f(12)=8\sqrt3\) to fix \(c\).

Full working

Step 1 — integrate \(f'(x)\). Raise each power by one and divide: \(\int 2x^{-1/2}=2\cdot\frac{x^{1/2}}{1/2}=4x^{1/2}=4\sqrt x\). \(\int -4x^{-2}=-4\cdot\frac{x^{-1}}{-1}=4x^{-1}=\frac4x\). \(\int 3=3x\). Plus the constant \(c\).

\[f(x)=4x^{1/2}+4x^{-1}+3x+c=4\sqrt x+\frac4x+3x+c.\]

Step 2 — use \(f(12)=8\sqrt3\) to find \(c\). \(\sqrt{12}=2\sqrt3\), so \(4\sqrt{12}=8\sqrt3\). Also \(\frac4{12}=\frac13\).

\[f(12)=4\sqrt{12}+\frac{4}{12}+36+c=8\sqrt3+\tfrac13+36+c=8\sqrt3\ \Rightarrow\ c=-\left(\tfrac13+36\right)=-\tfrac{109}{3}.\]

Step 3 — keep \(c\) exact. Do not round \(-\tfrac{109}{3}\approx-36.33\); the exact fraction is required. The final function is

\[f(x)=4\sqrt x+\frac4x+3x-\frac{109}{3}.\]

Step 4 — exam technique. After integrating, the constant \(c\) is fixed by the one given point, so substitute that point the moment you have the integrated form — do not delay it to the end where a slip is easy. Work with exact surds (\(\sqrt{12}=2\sqrt3\)) rather than decimals; here \(4\sqrt{12}=8\sqrt3\), which cancels the \(8\sqrt3\) already present and leaves only the rational part to absorb into \(c\). A frequent error is to drop a power while integrating (for example writing \(x^{-1/2}\) as \(x^{1/2}\) instead of \(2x^{1/2}\)) or to round the constant; either loses the final mark. Always finish by stating \(f(x)\) in full, with \(c\) already substituted, exactly as the question asks.

Revision focus. Dropping a power gave the wrong constant; rounding \(-\tfrac{109}{3}\) to a decimal.
Final answers
\(A=-4\); \(f(x)=4\sqrt x+\frac4x+3x-\frac{109}{3}\).
