Q7 — Differentiation: find A, then f(x)

Question

The curve \(C\) has equation \(y=f(x)\), \(x>0\). Given that \(f'(x)=\frac{2}{\sqrt{x}}+\frac{A}{x^2}+3\), where \(A\) is a constant, and \(f''(x)=0\) when \(x=4\): (a) find \(A\). Given also that \(f(x)=8\sqrt3\) when \(x=12\): (b) find \(f(x)\), giving each term in simplest form. (9)

[9 marks]

Lesson status: Reviewed live (differentiation for A, then recover f). The official QP/MS control wording, marks and values.

Part (a)[4 marks] · 1 steps

Carry-in: differentiate \(f'(x)\) once more to get \(f''(x)\).
STEP 1/1

Strategy and why this applies. Use fractional/negative power rules; set \(f''(4)=0\) and solve for \(A\).

Step 1 — write powers explicitly. \(f'(x)=2x^{-1/2}+Ax^{-2}+3\). To differentiate, multiply by the current power and subtract 1.

Step 2 — differentiate each term. \(\frac{d}{dx}(2x^{-1/2})=2(-\tfrac12)x^{-3/2}=-x^{-3/2}\). \(\frac{d}{dx}(Ax^{-2})=-2Ax^{-3}\). The constant 3 differentiates to 0. So

\[f''(x)=-x^{-3/2}-2Ax^{-3}.\]

Step 3 — use the condition \(f''(4)=0\). Substitute \(x=4\): \(4^{-3/2}=(4^{1/2})^{-3}=2^{-3}=\tfrac18\), and \(4^{-3}=2^{-6}=\tfrac1{64}\).

\[-4^{-3/2}-2A\cdot4^{-3}=0\ \Rightarrow\ -\tfrac18-\tfrac{A}{32}=0\ \Rightarrow\ A=-4.\]

Step 4 — method matters. Even a correct \(A=-4\) scores 0 if obtained by invalid working (e.g. dropped fractional powers). The justification above is what earns the mark.

Step 5 — transfer trigger and exam technique. When differentiating a power with a fractional or negative index, write the index explicitly before applying the rule: \(x^{-1/2}\) becomes \(-\tfrac12x^{-3/2}\), never \(x^{1/2}\). A very common slip is to forget that the coefficient multiplies the new power too, so \(2x^{-1/2}\) differentiates to \(2(-\tfrac12)x^{-3/2}=-x^{-3/2}\), where the 2 and the \(-\tfrac12\) cancel. For negative powers, \(\frac{d}{dx}(Ax^{-2})=-2Ax^{-3}\); keep the minus sign attached to the 2. Finally, evaluate powers of 4 carefully: \(4^{-3/2}\) means \((4^{1/2})^{-3}=2^{-3}=\tfrac18\), not \(-8\). Mistakes at this arithmetic stage are the usual reason a correct method still yields the wrong \(A\).

Evidence-grounded lesson diagnosis — what went wrong. Reaching \(A=-4\) by invalid working (scores 0); or a dropped fractional power.

Part (b)[5 marks] · 1 steps

Carry-in: \(A=-4\), so \(f'(x)=2x^{-1/2}-4x^{-2}+3\), and \(f(12)=8\sqrt3\).
STEP 1/1

Strategy and why this applies. Integrate term by term, add \(c\), then use \(f(12)=8\sqrt3\) to fix \(c\).

Step 1 — integrate \(f'(x)\). Raise each power by one and divide: \(\int 2x^{-1/2}=2\cdot\frac{x^{1/2}}{1/2}=4x^{1/2}=4\sqrt x\). \(\int -4x^{-2}=-4\cdot\frac{x^{-1}}{-1}=4x^{-1}=\frac4x\). \(\int 3=3x\). Plus the constant \(c\).

\[f(x)=4x^{1/2}+4x^{-1}+3x+c=4\sqrt x+\frac4x+3x+c.\]

Step 2 — use \(f(12)=8\sqrt3\) to find \(c\). \(\sqrt{12}=2\sqrt3\), so \(4\sqrt{12}=8\sqrt3\). Also \(\frac4{12}=\frac13\).

\[f(12)=4\sqrt{12}+\frac{4}{12}+36+c=8\sqrt3+\tfrac13+36+c=8\sqrt3\ \Rightarrow\ c=-\left(\tfrac13+36\right)=-\tfrac{109}{3}.\]

Step 3 — keep \(c\) exact. Do not round \(-\tfrac{109}{3}\approx-36.33\); the exact fraction is required. The final function is

\[f(x)=4\sqrt x+\frac4x+3x-\frac{109}{3}.\]

Step 4 — transfer trigger and exam technique. After integrating, the constant \(c\) is fixed by the one given point, so substitute that point the moment you have the integrated form — do not delay it to the end where a slip is easy. Work with exact surds (\(\sqrt{12}=2\sqrt3\)) rather than decimals; here \(4\sqrt{12}=8\sqrt3\), which cancels the \(8\sqrt3\) already present and leaves only the rational part to absorb into \(c\). A frequent error is to drop a power while integrating (for example writing \(x^{-1/2}\) as \(x^{1/2}\) instead of \(2x^{1/2}\)) or to round the constant; either loses the final mark. Always finish by stating \(f(x)\) in full, with \(c\) already substituted, exactly as the question asks.

Sense check. Differentiating the recovered function reproduces the supplied derivative, and substituting x=12 reproduces the supplied value. On another paper, a value of f fixes the integration constant only after integration.

Common trap / why the rejected route fails. Dropping a power gave the wrong constant; rounding \(-\tfrac{109}{3}\) to a decimal.

Final reference answer

Official answer: \(A=-4\); \(f(x)=4\sqrt x+\frac4x+3x-\frac{109}{3}\).
