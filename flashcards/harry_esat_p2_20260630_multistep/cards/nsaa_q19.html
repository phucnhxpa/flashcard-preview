NSAA S1 Part A Q19 — biased dice

Status boundary. Deferred / not completed live. The target answer below is official-reference only.
Full standalone question wording and marks

Two six-sided dice, each numbered 1 to 6, are rolled. One die is fair; the other is biased: it lands on 1 to 5 with equal probability, and on 6 with a different probability. The probability of a total of 12 is $1/18$. Find the probability of a total of 2. Options: A $1/72$; B $1/45$; C $1/36$; D $1/18$; E $1/9$.

Part (a) [1 mark]
Carry-in / given: No earlier part: use the given total-12 event.
Step 1 — setup

Strategy. Set $q=P(6_{\rm biased})$.

Full working. Formula: $P(12)=P(6_{\rm fair})P(6_{\rm biased})$.
Substitute: $\frac16q=\frac1{18}$, so $q=\frac13$.
Final check: total 12 means six on both dice, not a sum of 10 or more.

Sense check. Use the stated conditions and keep the part label visible.

Step 2 — conclusion

Strategy. Each of biased outcomes 1–5 has probability $(1-q)/5=2/15$.

Full working. Formula: $P(2)=P(1_{\rm fair})P(1_{\rm biased})$.
Substitute: $\frac16\cdot\frac2{15}=\frac2{90}$.
Final check: $P(2)=\boxed{1/45}$, option B.
