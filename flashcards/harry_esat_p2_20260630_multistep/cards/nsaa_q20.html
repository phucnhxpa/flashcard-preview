NSAA S1 Part A Q20 — regular-pentagon bearing

Status boundary. Deferred / not completed live. The final bearing below is official-reference only.
Full standalone question wording and marks

A cross-country track is a regular pentagon. Competitors run clockwise. On the third leg they run on a bearing of $110^\circ$. On what bearing do they run for the first leg? Options: A $034^\circ$; B $038^\circ$; C $106^\circ$; D $178^\circ$; E $182^\circ$; F $244^\circ$; G $322^\circ$; H $326^\circ$.

Part (a) [1 mark]
Carry-in / given: No earlier part: the third-leg heading is $110^\circ$.
Step 1 — setup

Strategy. Find the turn at each vertex.

Full working. Formula: exterior angle of a regular pentagon $=360^\circ/5$.
Substitute: $360^\circ/5=72^\circ$.
Final check: $72^\circ$ is an exterior turn; $108^\circ$ is the interior angle.

Sense check. Use the stated conditions and keep the part label visible.

Revision-guide trap. Revision-guide trap: a bearing is measured clockwise from north, not from a page axis.
Step 2 — conclusion

Strategy. Carry-in: third-leg bearing $110^\circ$ and each clockwise turn $72^\circ$.

Full working. Formula: first leg is two turns before third leg.
Substitute: $110^\circ-2(72^\circ)=-34^\circ$.
Final check: add $360^\circ$: $\boxed{326^\circ}$, option H.

Sense check. The final result must satisfy the question’s stated condition.

Revision-guide trap. Revision-guide trap: a bearing is measured clockwise from north, not from a page axis.
