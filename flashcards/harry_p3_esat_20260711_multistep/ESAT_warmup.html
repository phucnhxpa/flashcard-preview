ESAT warm-up · sphere = 10 closed cylinders

Problem (as read on the board). The surface area of a solid sphere of radius \(R\) equals the total surface area of 10 solid closed cylinders of radius \(r\) and height \(h\), with \(h=4R\). Find \(R\) in terms of \(r\).

5 marksSolved on boardlesson-recording reconstruction
Derive the surface-area equation2 marks
Carry-in: \(h=4R\); a closed cylinder has two circular ends and one curved side.

Separate the sphere from one complete cylinder before multiplying by 10.

Formula: sphere SA \(=4\pi R^2\); closed-cylinder SA \(=2\pi r^2+2\pi rh\).

Substitute: \(4\pi R^2=10(2\pi r^2+2\pi r h)\).

Then use: \(h=4R\), so \(4\pi R^2=10(2\pi r^2+8\pi rR)=20\pi r^2+80\pi rR\).

Sense check: both sides are areas, so every term has squared length units.

Phuc's recorded slip: the first setup used only one circular end. A closed cylinder contributes two \(\pi r^2\) ends.
Solve for the physical radius3 marks
Carry-in: From the setup: \(4\pi R^2=20\pi r^2+80\pi rR\).

Make a quadratic in \(R\), then retain the positive root because a radius is positive.

Divide by \(4\pi\): \(R^2=5r^2+20rR\), hence \(R^2-20rR-5r^2=0\).

Formula: \(R=\frac{-b\pm\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}\) with \(a=1,b=-20r,c=-5r^2\).

\(R=\frac{20r\pm\sqrt{400r^2+20r^2}}2=\frac{20r\pm2r\sqrt{105}}2=r(10\pm\sqrt{105})\).

Final check: \(10-\sqrt{105}<0\), so it cannot be a radius.

\(\boxed{R=r(10+\sqrt{105})}\)
Phuc's recorded slip: \(\pi r^2h\) is cylinder volume, not surface area. Also retain the square in \(4\pi R^2\).
