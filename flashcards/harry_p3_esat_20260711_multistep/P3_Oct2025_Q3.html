P3 October 2025 · Q3 — linearise, exponentiate, differentiate

From the paper. The share price £\(V\) is monitored; a graph of \(\log_{10}V\) against \(t\) (years) is a straight line through \((0,2)\) and \((5,2.25)\). (a) find \(\log_{10}V=mt+c\); (b) write as \(V=ab^t\) (exact \(a\), \(b\) to 3 s.f.); (c) when \(t=T\) the rate of increase is £50/yr — find \(T\) to the nearest integer. Solutions relying entirely on calculator technology are not acceptable.

9 marksSolvedofficial-paper text in passed guide
Part (a) · equation of the line2 marks
Carry-in: Vertical variable is \(\log_{10}V\); points \((0,2)\), \((5,2.25)\).

Read “\(\log_{10}V\) against \(t\)” as y against x, then find gradient and intercept.

\(m=\dfrac{2.25-2}{5-0}=0.05\), and \(c=2\).

\(\log_{10}V-2=0.05(t-0)\).

\(\boxed{\log_{10}V=0.05t+2}\)

Sense check: at \(t=5\), the model returns \(2.25\).

Phuc's recorded slip: the vertical axis was initially named \(V\), not \(\log_{10}V\).
Part (b) · undo the logarithm3 marks
Carry-in: \(\log_{10}V=0.05t+2\); requested form \(V=ab^t\).

Raise 10 to both sides and split the exponent before identifying \(a\) and \(b\).

\(V=10^{0.05t+2}=10^2\cdot10^{0.05t}=100(10^{0.05})^t\).

Thus \(a=100\) exactly and \(b=10^{0.05}=1.12201\ldots=1.12\) to 3 s.f.

Sense check: at \(t=0\), this gives \(V=100\), consistent with \(\log_{10}V=2\).

\(\boxed{V=100(10^{0.05})^t;\ a=100,\ b=1.12\text{ (3 s.f.)}}\)
Harry's recorded correction: rewrite \(10^{0.05t}=(10^{0.05})^t\); do not leave the exponent in a form that hides \(b^t\).
Part (c) · rate of increase4 marks
Carry-in: Use the exact model \(V=100\cdot10^{0.05t}\), not the rounded base \(1.12\).

“Rate” means differentiate. Keep the exact base throughout and set the derivative to positive 50.

Formula: \(\dfrac{d}{dt}a^{kt}=a^{kt}k\ln a\).

\(\dfrac{dV}{dt}=100\cdot10^{0.05t}(0.05\ln10)\).

Set \(100\cdot10^{0.05T}(0.05\ln10)=50\), giving \(10^{0.05T}=\dfrac{10}{\ln10}\).

\(T=\dfrac{\log_{10}(10/\ln10)}{0.05}=12.755686\ldots\).

Sense check: only the final requested integer is rounded.

\(\boxed{T=13}\)
Phuc's recorded slip: “arithmetic series” was suggested for a rate. Harry's cue was: rate \(\Rightarrow\) differentiate; an increase means \(+50\), not \(-50\).

Source class: lesson-taught prior-paper review. Arithmetic uses the passed-guide correction: differentiating rounded \(1.12^t\) is not an exact method.

Formula discrimination. Surface area is not volume: a closed cylinder needs two circular ends and a curved side. A logarithm model must be converted before identifying an exponential base. In a quotient, differentiate both numerator and denominator before simplifying. In an R-form question, use coefficient matching before choosing an inverse trigonometric function.
