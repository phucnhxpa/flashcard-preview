Part (a) · establish a root2 marks
Carry-in: \(f(x)=\dfrac{2x^2+3x-4}{e^x}-\dfrac1{x^2}\), on the whole interval \([1,2]\).

Show a sign change at the stated endpoints, then state continuity on the closed interval.

\(f(1)=\dfrac1e-1=-0.6321\ldots<0\).

\(f(2)=\dfrac{10}{e^2}-\dfrac14=1.1033\ldots>0\).

\(f\) is continuous on \([1,2]\) because neither denominator is zero there.

Conclusion: by continuity and the sign change, \(f(x)=0\) has a root \(\alpha\in[1,2]\).

Sense check: the plotted curve crosses the horizontal axis between the labelled endpoint signs.

Part (b) · reach the cube-root form2 marks
Carry-in: Start from \(f(x)=0\); target \(x=\sqrt[3]{\dfrac{e^x+4x^2}{2x+3}}\).

Work towards the target denominator \(2x+3\) by exposing a factor of \(x^3\).

\(\dfrac{2x^2+3x-4}{e^x}=\dfrac1{x^2}\Rightarrow x^2(2x^2+3x-4)=e^x\).

\(2x^4+3x^3=e^x+4x^2\Rightarrow x^3(2x+3)=e^x+4x^2\).

\(x^3=\dfrac{e^x+4x^2}{2x+3}\Rightarrow x=\sqrt[3]{\dfrac{e^x+4x^2}{2x+3}}\).

Sense check: cubing the target reproduces the isolated \(x^3\) equation.
