Question 8 — Trig equation and greenhouse model

12 marksone-question recall cardMathJax SVG
Official question

In this question you must show all stages of your working. Solutions relying entirely on calculator technology are not acceptable. Use the equation of the model to answer parts (a) to (c).

(i) Solve, for \(0<x\le\pi\), \(5\sin x\tan x+13=\cos x\), giving your answer in radians to 3 significant figures. (5)
(ii) \(H=10+12\sin(kt+18)^\circ,\ 0\le t<24\), where \(H\) is temperature in °C and \(t\) is hours after midnight. Given \(H=20\) at 6 am and \(0<k<20\): (a) find all possible \(k\), 2 d.p. (4). Given further \(0<k<10\): (b) find the maximum temperature (1); (c) find the time of day of this maximum, nearest minute (2).

Source class: Lesson-taught question / mode, units and rounding fixes. The official QP/MS controls wording, marks and values; lesson-specific correction points follow the passed 10 July guide.

Part (i)

5 marksfinalised steps
Carry-in / given: No carry-in: the stated interval is in radians.
Strategy

Replace \(\tan x\), clear the denominator and solve the quadratic in \(\cos x\).

Full working

Formula. \(\tan x=\sin x/\cos x\), \(\sin^2x=1-\cos^2x\).

\[5\sin^2x+13\cos x=\cos^2x\Rightarrow6\cos^2x-13\cos x-5=0.\]\[(3\cos x+1)(2\cos x-5)=0\Rightarrow\cos x=-\frac13.\]\[x=\arccos(-1/3)=1.91\text{ rad (3 s.f.)}.\]

Sense check. \(\cos x=-1/3\) lies in quadrant II of \((0,\pi]\).

Revision focus. The correct value is 1.91 rad to 3 s.f.; the conflicting 0.927 figure was a video-ledger error.

Part (ii)(a)

4 marksfinalised steps
Carry-in / given: Carry-in: at 6 am, \(t=6\); the sine-model angle has a degree sign.
Strategy

Set \(H=20\), solve the two degree-angle sine solutions, then isolate \(k\).

Full working
\[20=10+12\sin(6k+18)^\circ\Rightarrow\sin(6k+18)^\circ=\frac56.\]\[6k+18=56.4^\circ\ \text{or}\ 123.6^\circ.\]\[k=6.41\ \text{or}\ 17.59\quad(2\text{ d.p.}).\]

Sense check. The degree symbol means the calculator must be in degree mode for this part.

Revision focus. Phuc used radians; the \((\cdots)^\circ\) in the model determines the correct calculator mode.

Part (ii)(b)

1 markfinalised steps
Carry-in / given: Carry-in: \(0<k<10\), so use \(k=6.41\).
Strategy

Use the maximum value of sine.

Full working

Maximum. \(\sin\theta\le1\).

\[H_{\max}=10+12(1)=22\,^\circ\mathrm C.\]

Sense check. It is a temperature, so include the unit °C.

Revision focus. The number alone is incomplete: the answer needs the temperature unit.

Part (ii)(c)

2 marksfinalised steps
Carry-in / given: Carry-in: \(k=6.41\) and the maximum occurs when the sine angle is \(90^\circ\).
Strategy

Solve for the elapsed time and convert the decimal hour to minutes.

Full working
\[6.41t+18=90\Rightarrow t=11.23\ldots\text{ h}.\]\[0.23\ldots\times60\approx14\text{ min}.\]

Final format. \(11{:}14\).

Sense check. The question asks for time of day, not decimal hours.

Revision focus. Use the found \(k=6.41\); do not replace it with an unrelated decimal.
Final answers
\(x=1.91\) rad; \(k=6.41,17.59\); maximum \(22\,^\circ\mathrm C\); time \(11{:}14\).
