ESAT Recall Sheet

ESAT Maths (30 May) — Functions & Proportional Reasoning Recall Sheet

The whole lesson in one mental model. This lesson is about comparing sizes without a calculator: how powers and roots reorder on \(0<x<1\), how inverse functions reflect across \(y=x\), how proportionality turns a percentage change into a clean fraction, and fast fraction/volume manipulation (the “burger method”, cancel-early). The recurring skill is turn a numeric question into an exact-fraction or ordering argument, then read off the answer.

1. Jargon glossary

Term Student-friendly meaning Exact meaning Symbols / units What it is NOT Lesson example Phuc / common trap
Behaviour on \(0<x<1\) Between 0 and 1, “bigger powers make smaller numbers”. For \(0<x<1\): \(x^2<x<\sqrt{x}<1<\tfrac1{\sqrt{x}}<\tfrac1x\). dimensionless. NOT the same ordering as \(x>1\) (it reverses). Comparing \(\tfrac1x\) vs \(\tfrac1{\sqrt x}\). Assuming “square is bigger” (true only for \(x>1\)).
Square root as inverse of squaring \(\sqrt{\,}\) undoes \(x^2\). \(\sqrt{x}\) is the inverse of \(x^2\) on \(x\ge0\); its graph is \(y=x^2\) reflected in \(y=x\). — NOT a two-branch curve here — positive branch only. Reflecting \(y=x^2\) to get \(y=\sqrt x\). Including the negative root for a length/size.
Inverse function (graphically) Swap \(x\) and \(y\) / mirror in the 45° line. \(f^{-1}\) reflects \(f\) across \(y=x\); domains/ranges swap. — NOT the reciprocal \(1/f\). \(y=\sqrt x\) as the mirror of \(y=x^2\). Confusing inverse with reciprocal.
Reciprocal \(1/x\) One over the number. The multiplicative inverse; on \(0<x<1\) it is \(>1\) and large. dimensionless. NOT the inverse function. At \(x=\tfrac14\), \(\tfrac1x=4\). Treating \(1/x\) and \(1/\sqrt x\) as similar in size.
Direct / inverse proportion “Scales with” / “scales against”. \(P\propto Q^n \Rightarrow P=kQ^n\); inverse when \(n<0\). \(k\) constant. NOT equality — there’s a hidden constant \(k\). \(P\propto 1/Q^2\). Dropping \(k\), or mis-signing the power.
Percentage change via multiplier Turn “+40%” into “×1.4”. A \(p\%\) increase is multiplication by \((1+p/100)\); substitute into the proportion. dimensionless. NOT “add 40% of the formula”. \(Q\to1.4Q\) in \(P\propto1/Q^2\). Reporting the new value when asked for the change.
Burger method (÷ fractions) Outer-over-inner trick for stacked fractions. For \(\dfrac{a/b}{c}\), “outer × outer / inner × inner”: \(\dfrac{a}{bc}\); for \(\dfrac{a}{b/c}=\dfrac{ac}{b}\). — NOT a new rule — it’s “multiply by the reciprocal” organised. Simplifying a compound fraction fast. Inverting the wrong part of the stack.
Cancel early Simplify before multiplying out. Divide common factors before expanding to keep numbers small. — NOT skipping steps — it’s reordering them. Sphere:cylinder ratio cancels to \(2:3\). Expanding first, then fighting big numbers.

4. Exam-technique recall

Cue Action
“Which is largest/smallest” on \(0<x<1\) recall the ordering chain; confirm with \(x=\tfrac14\)
Percentage change in a proportional quantity convert to a multiplier, work in exact fractions (e.g. \(25/49\)), then subtract from 1 for the change
Ball-in-tube / nested solids set the linking dimension (e.g. \(h=2r\)) and cancel \(\pi r^3\) early
Stacked fractions burger method; don’t expand brackets before introducing fractions
Multiple-choice forms match the exact simplified form (fraction/surd) of the option
ESAT vs A-level time do the quick ones first; A-level foundations > ESAT-only prep (Harry’s steer)

5. Big picture

  • On \(0<x<1\) the size ordering reverses — that single fact answers a whole class of ESAT questions.
  • Proportionality questions become trivial once you switch to a multiplier in exact fractions; decimals invite slips.
  • [Phuc trap] \(\left(\tfrac12\right)^2=\tfrac14\), not \(1\) — square the numerator and denominator.
  • Reflexes: order it, fraction it, cancel early.
