1.1 Binomial expansion (Q1)

Term Student-friendly meaning Exact mathematical meaning Symbols/conditions What it is NOT Lesson example Phuc/common trap
Binomial expansion Multiply out a bracket to a power, term by term \((1+u)^n=1+nu+\tfrac{n(n-1)}{2!}u^2+\tfrac{n(n-1)(n-2)}{3!}u^3+\cdots\) Real \(n\); converges for \(\lvert u\rvert<1\) if \(n\notin\mathbb{Z}^{\ge0}\) (here \(n=9\), terminates) Not just “\(1+nu\)”; not the same as factorising Q1(a): \(\left(1-\tfrac{x}{6}\right)^9=1-\tfrac32x+x^2-\tfrac{7}{18}x^3+\cdots\) “\(+1x^2\)” written where just “\(x^2\)” is wanted (MS accepts \(1x^2\))
“First four terms, ascending powers” Give the four lowest-power terms, smallest power first Terms in \(x^0,x^1,x^2,x^3\) in that order, simplified Must be simplified; keep order low→high Not the whole expansion; not descending powers Q1(a), 3 marks Writing an unnecessary coefficient on \(x^2\)
“Coefficient of \(x^m\)” The number multiplying \(x^m\) after everything is collected Sum of every product that lands on \(x^m\) Collect all cross-terms reaching \(x^m\); you may skip terms you don’t need Not a single term — often a sum of contributions Q1(b): coeff of \(x^3\) in \((10x+3)\left(1-\tfrac{x}{6}\right)^9=\tfrac{53}{6}\) Missed the \(10x\times x^2\) product; took only \(3\times x^3\) → lost the final mark
Reconstruction note — — — — Gemini also reports a written “\(\tfrac38x^2\)” third term [Gemini — source-limited] contradicts “calc correct”; not a confirmed loss

1.2 Arithmetic sequences & series (Q2)

Term Student-friendly meaning Exact mathematical meaning Symbols/conditions What it is NOT Lesson example Phuc/common trap
Arithmetic sequence Add the same amount each step \(u_n=a+(n-1)d\) \(a=\) first term, \(d=\) common difference, \(n\in\mathbb{Z}^{+}\) Not geometric (that multiplies) Q2: 6th term \(a+5d=2\) Confusing the term formula with the sum formula
Common difference \(d\) The fixed gap between consecutive terms \(d=u_{n+1}-u_n\) (constant) Can be negative or (here) positive Not a ratio Q2(a): \(d=20\) —
Sum of \(n\) terms \(S_n\) Add up the first \(n\) terms \(S_n=\tfrac n2\big(2a+(n-1)d\big)=\tfrac n2(a+l)\) \(l=\) last term; \(n\) a positive integer Not the \(n\)th term \(u_n\) Q2(a): \(S_{10}=-80\Rightarrow2a+9d=-16\) → \(a=-98,\,d=20\) Mixing up \(S_n\) and \(u_n\)
“Smallest \(n\) such that \(S_n>k\)” The first whole number of terms making the sum exceed \(k\) Solve \(S_n>k\) (a quadratic in \(n\)), take smallest integer above the positive root Reject negative/irrelevant root; test integers each side Not “solve \(S_n=k\)” — it’s an inequality Q2(b): \(5n^2-54n-4000>0\Rightarrow n=35\) Arithmetic slip forming/simplifying the quadratic → lost the accuracy mark (method marks banked)

1.3 Geometric sequences & exponential models (Q10)

Term Student-friendly meaning Exact mathematical meaning Symbols/conditions What it is NOT Lesson example Phuc/common trap
Geometric sequence Multiply by the same factor each step \(u_n=ar^{\,n-1}\) \(a=\) first term, \(r=\) common ratio (\(r\ne0\)) Not arithmetic (that adds \(d\)) Q10 dormice: \(\times1.03\) each year Using \(n-1\) vs \(t\) indexing wrongly (see below)
Exponential growth model “Grows \(r\%\) a year” → repeated multiply value after \(t\) yr \(=(\text{start})\times\left(1+\tfrac{r}{100}\right)^t\) \(t\) measured from the stated start (\(t=0\) = start) Not \(\times(1+\tfrac{r}{100})^{\,t-1}\) when \(t\) is “years after start” Q10(a): \(2000\times1.03^{6}=2390\) (3 s.f.) Wrote \(2000\times1.03^{7-1}\) — “\(n\) vs \(t\)” confusion; also rounded to 3 d.p. not 3 s.f.
Fitting \(N=ab^{t}\) Find the start \(a\) and ratio \(b\) from two data points Two equations → \(b^{\,\Delta t}=\tfrac{N_2}{N_1}\), then back-substitute for \(a\) Give \(a,b\) to stated s.f.; \(b<1\) = decay Not \(N=A+B(1.2)^t\) (that’s not what the paper asks) Q10(b) [Setup only / MS-only]: \(b=0.98,\ a\approx4000\) Recording only reached the set-up (\(t=4,N=3690\))
“When are they equal?” Find the time both models give the same number Solve \(\text{model}_1=\text{model}_2\) for \(t\) Take logs; answer to stated precision Not a guess — solve algebraically Q10(c) [Not reached / MS-only]: \(T=13.9\) (1 d.p.) Recording ended before this part

1.4 Logarithms (Q3)

Term Student-friendly meaning Exact mathematical meaning Symbols/conditions What it is NOT Lesson example Phuc/common trap
Logarithm “What power gives this number?” \(\log_b X=c\iff b^{\,c}=X\) \(b>0,\ b\ne1,\ X>0\) Not multiplication; \(\log(X+Y)\ne\log X+\log Y\) Q3(i): base-2 logs —
Power law A power inside comes out front \(p\log_b X=\log_b X^{p}\) \(X>0\) Not \((\log_b X)^p\) \(2\log_2(2-x)=\log_2(2-x)^2\) —
Product/quotient laws Add logs = multiply; subtract = divide \(\log_b X+\log_b Y=\log_b XY\); \(\log_b X-\log_b Y=\log_b\tfrac XY\) Same base; args \(>0\) Not \(\log_b(X+Y)\) \(4+\log_2(x+10)=\log_2\!\big(16(x+10)\big)\) —
Change of base Rewrite a log in another base \(\log_b X=\dfrac{\log_a X}{\log_a b}\) Any valid base \(a\) — Q3(ii): \(\log_{\sqrt a}(a^{6})=\tfrac{6}{1/2}=12\) QP text-extraction blurred the base; MS answer \(=12\) confirms base \(\sqrt a\)
Domain / extraneous root Some algebra answers break the “must be positive” rule — throw them out Every log argument must stay \(>0\); reject roots that violate it Check after solving Not “keep both roots” Q3(i): \(x^2-20x-156=0\Rightarrow x=-6\) or \(26\); reject \(26\) (needs \(2-x>0\)) → \(x=-6\) Praised — Phuc correctly rejected the extraneous root

1.5 Factor & remainder theorems, polynomials (Q4)

Term Student-friendly meaning Exact mathematical meaning Symbols/conditions What it is NOT Lesson example Phuc/common trap
Remainder theorem To get the remainder, just plug the root in Dividing \(f(x)\) by \((x-a)\) leaves remainder \(f(a)\) From \(f(x)=(x-a)q(x)+r\Rightarrow f(a)=r\) Not long division (that’s the slow way) Q4(a): \(f(2)=0+21=21\) Started long division / asked “what is the remainder theorem?” — Harry taught it from scratch
Factor theorem If it’s a factor, plugging the root gives zero \((px-q)\) a factor \(\iff f\!\left(\tfrac qp\right)=0\) \(r=0\) special case of remainder theorem Not “remainder \(\ne0\)” Q4(b): \((2x-1)\) factor \(\Rightarrow f(\tfrac12)=0\Rightarrow k=11\) —
Command word “hence” Use what you just proved Reuse the previous result (\(k=11\) and the known factor) — Not “start over” Q4(c): use \(k=11\) + factor \((2x-1)\) —
Fully factorise Break into factors that can’t go further \(2x^3+x^2+x-1=(2x-1)(x^2+x+1)\) Quadratic factor may be irreducible Not “leave a quadratic you could split” — but do check first Q4(c)(i) Tried to factorise \(x^2+x+1\) further and got stuck
Discriminant Quick test for how many real roots / whether it factorises \(\Delta=b^2-4ac\): \(>0\) two, \(=0\) one, \(<0\) none; perfect square ⇒ factorises over rationals Real \(a,b,c\) Not the roots themselves Q4(c)(ii): \(x^2+x+1\) has \(\Delta=1-4=-3<0\) Must state \(b^2-4ac=-3<0\) as the reason — MS gives A0 without it

1.6 Circle geometry (Q7)

Term Student-friendly meaning Exact mathematical meaning Symbols/conditions What it is NOT Lesson example Phuc/common trap
Completing the square (circle) Turn the messy equation into centre–radius form \(x^2+y^2+2gx+2fy+c=0\to(x+g)^2+(y+f)^2=g^2+f^2-c\) RHS \(=r^2>0\) Not the general quadratic-formula CTS Q7(a): \(x^2+y^2+8x-10y=29\to(x+4)^2+(y-5)^2=70\) —
Centre & radius Where the circle sits and how big Centre \((-g,-f)\); radius \(r=\sqrt{g^2+f^2-c}\) \(r>0\); radius is a length Radius is not \(r^2\) Q7(a): centre \((-4,5)\), radius \(\sqrt{70}\) —
Exact value Leave it as a surd, no rounding e.g. \(\sqrt{70}\), not \(8.366\) “Exact” ⇒ no decimals Not a decimal approximation Q7(a): radius \(=\sqrt{70}\) Wrote both \(\sqrt{70}\) and \(8.366\) — “exact = no decimals allowed”
Distance between centres How far apart the two middles are \(d=\sqrt{(x_2-x_1)^2+(y_2-y_1)^2}\) Straight-line (Pythagoras) Not the difference of radii Q7(b): \(d=\sqrt{9^2+13^2}=\sqrt{250}=15.81\) —
“Neither touch nor intersect” The circles miss each other completely Separated outside: \(d>r_1+r_2\); or one inside other: \(d<\lvert r_1-r_2\rvert\) Compare \(d\) with \(r_1+r_2\) (and \(\lvert r_1-r_2\rvert\)) Not “just say they don’t meet” — show the numbers Q7(b) [DR]: sum of radii \(\sqrt{70}+\sqrt{52}=15.58<15.81=d\) ⇒ separated Knew the idea but stopped — didn’t compute both and state the conclusion in words
Video-ledger conflict — — — — Gemini “reconstructed” \(C_1\) radius \(=5\) Corrected: official radius \(\sqrt{70}\) (QP/MS)

1.7 Trigonometry & trig models (Q8)

Term Student-friendly meaning Exact mathematical meaning Symbols/conditions What it is NOT Lesson example Phuc/common trap
Pythagorean identity Turn \(\sin^2\) into \(\cos^2\) (or back) \(\sin^2x+\cos^2x=1\) All \(x\) Not \(\sin x+\cos x=1\) Q8(i): \(5(1-\cos^2x)+13\cos x=\cos^2x\) —
\(\tan\) identity \(\tan\) is \(\sin\) over \(\cos\) \(\tan x=\dfrac{\sin x}{\cos x}\) \(\cos x\ne0\) — Q8(i): clears to \(6\cos^2x-13\cos x-5=0\) —
Solving in an interval Find every solution in the allowed range Get the principal value, then all others in range (e.g. \(\sin\): \(\theta,\ 180^\circ-\theta\); \(\cos\): \(\pm\theta\)) Respect the stated interval Not “just the first answer” Q8(i): \(\cos x=-\tfrac13\) in \(0<x\le\pi\Rightarrow x=1.91\) rad; Q8(ii)(a): \(\sin(6k+18)^\circ=\tfrac56\Rightarrow 56.4^\circ,123.6^\circ\) —
Radians vs degrees Two angle units — the calculator must match the question \(\pi\text{ rad}=180^\circ\) Set mode per question A \((\ldots)^\circ\) symbol ⇒ degrees, not radians Q8(i) radians; Q8(ii) model in degrees Calculator left in radians for the degree model → wrong \(\sin^{-1}(5/6)\)

1.8 Differentiation, integration & trapezium rule (Q6, Q9)

Term Student-friendly meaning Exact mathematical meaning Symbols/conditions What it is NOT Lesson example Phuc/common trap
Stationary point Where the curve momentarily flattens Point where \(\dfrac{dy}{dx}=0\) Solve derivative \(=0\); may be several Not an intercept Q9(a): \(\tfrac{dy}{dx}=x^{1/2}(12-5x)=0\Rightarrow x=0\) or \(\tfrac{12}{5}\); \(P\) at \(x=\tfrac{12}{5}\) Found \(y\) too and treated \(x=0\) as a candidate — only the \(x\)-coordinate of \(P\) is asked; \(x=0\) is the intercept
Power rule (differentiate) Bring the power down, drop it by one \(\dfrac{d}{dx}x^n=nx^{n-1}\) Any real \(n\) — \(8x^{3/2}-2x^{5/2}\to12x^{1/2}-5x^{3/2}\) —
Power rule (integrate) Raise the power, divide by the new power \(\displaystyle\int x^n\,dx=\dfrac{x^{n+1}}{n+1}+c\) \(n\ne-1\) Not “divide by the old power” Q9(b): \(\int(8x^{3/2}-2x^{5/2})dx=\tfrac{16}{5}x^{5/2}-\tfrac47x^{7/2}\) Slipped integrating \(x^{5/2}\) (got \(\tfrac27x^{7/2}\)); correct is \(\tfrac47x^{7/2}\)
Signed area Area below the axis counts as negative Definite integral is negative below the \(x\)-axis For an area below, prepend a leading minus Not a modulus — Harry prefers a leading \(-\) Q9(b): \(R_2\) below axis ⇒ leading minus, then \(R_1=R_2\Rightarrow k^{5/2}\!\big(\tfrac{16}{5}-\tfrac47k\big)=0\Rightarrow k=\tfrac{28}{5}=5.6\) Unsure of \(R_1\)’s upper limit and \(R_2\)’s sign
Integration variable Keep \(x\) as the thing you integrate; a limit is a different letter In \(\int_a^k f(x)\,dx\), \(x\) is the variable, \(k\) is the (constant) upper limit Don’t substitute the limit letter into the integrand The upper limit \(k\) is not a replacement for \(x\) inside \(f\) Q9(b): keep \(x\); \(k\) is the end-point, reject \(k=0\) from the diagram (\(k>4\)) Reportedly wrote \(k\) in place of \(x\) inside the integrand — [Gemini — source-limited]
Trapezium rule Estimate area with straight-line strips \(\displaystyle\int\approx\tfrac h2\big[y_0+y_n+2(y_1+\cdots+y_{n-1})\big]\) Equal strip width \(h\); use all tabulated \(y\) Not the exact integral Q6(b) [Blank/deferred; MS-only]: \(h=0.3\Rightarrow\approx2.34\) (2 d.p.) Left blank in the exam — this is the homework question
Increasing exponential sketch \(a^x+4\) climbs and flattens to a line Curve \(y=a^x+4\) (\(a>1\)): through \((0,5)\), asymptote \(y=4\) Label intercept and asymptote Not a straight line Q6(a) [Blank/deferred; MS-only]: intercept \((0,5)\), asymptote \(y=4\) Blank in the exam
