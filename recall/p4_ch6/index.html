P4 Ch 6 — Integration (All Techniques)

The whole chapter in one mental model. Integration in P4 = (a) applications of definite integration: area under a parametric curve $A=\int g(t)\,f'(t)\,dt$, volume of revolution $V=\pi\int y^2\,dx$. (b) Three core techniques for the antiderivative when standard rules don't fit: substitution (reverse chain rule), by parts (reverse product rule, $\int u\,dv=uv-\int v\,du$), partial fractions (rational functions → simpler logs and powers). (c) First-order separable differential equations $\dfrac{dy}{dx}=f(x)g(y)$ → separate, integrate each side; particular solution from a boundary condition. (d) Modelling real-world situations with these DEs.

1. Jargon glossary

Term Meaning Where
Definite integral $\int_a^b f(x)\,dx$ — the signed area between $f$ and the $x$-axis from $a$ to $b$. Evaluated by FTC: $F(b)-F(a)$. 6.1
Indefinite integral $\int f(x)\,dx=F(x)+c$ — a family of antiderivatives. The constant $c$ is essential. 6.3
Area under a parametric curve When $x=f(t),\ y=g(t)$, area $=\int_a^b g(t)\,f'(t)\,dt$. The $f'(t)\,dt$ replaces $dx$. 6.1
Volume of revolution about $x$-axis $V=\pi\int_a^b y^2\,dx$. Spin a curve $y=f(x)$ around the $x$-axis; each thin strip of width $dx$ becomes a disc of area $\pi y^2$. 6.2
Volume of revolution (parametric) $V=\pi\int_a^b [g(t)]^2\,f'(t)\,dt$. Same idea with parametric $y=g(t)$ and $dx=f'(t)\,dt$. 6.2
Integration by substitution Reverse of chain rule. Set $u=h(x)$, replace $dx=\dfrac{du}{h'(x)}$, change all $x$'s to $u$'s. For definite integrals, change the limits too. 6.3
Integration by parts Reverse of product rule. $\int u\dfrac{dv}{dx}\,dx=uv-\int v\dfrac{du}{dx}\,dx$. Choose $u$ to simplify when differentiated. 6.4
LIATE / ILATE rule Choose $u$ in this priority order: Log, Inverse trig, Algebraic ($x^n$), Trig, Exponential. (P4: typically logs first, then $x^n$, then $e^x/\sin x/\cos x$ as $\dfrac{dv}{dx}$.) 6.4
Partial-fraction integration Decompose $\dfrac{P(x)}{Q(x)}$ into simple pieces; each piece $\dfrac{A}{ax+b}$ integrates to $\dfrac{A}{a}\ln|ax+b|$, each $\dfrac{A}{(ax+b)^k}$ to $\dfrac{-A}{a(k-1)(ax+b)^{k-1}}$ for $k\ge 2$. 6.5
Separable differential equation $\dfrac{dy}{dx}=f(x)g(y)$. Separate: $\dfrac{1}{g(y)}\,dy=f(x)\,dx$. Integrate each side. 6.6
General solution The full family $y=F(x,c)$ with arbitrary constant $c$ — represents many curves. 6.6
Particular solution One specific curve from the family, pinned by a boundary / initial condition $(x_0,y_0)$. 6.6
Boundary / initial condition A given pair $(x_0,y_0)$ that fixes the constant $c$ (or $k$ if you wrote $+\ln k$). 6.6 Ex 17
Modelling Translating "rate ∝ ..." into a DE, then solving. Common: exponential growth/decay, Newton cooling. 6.7

4. Substitution recipe (Section 6.3)

Step Action
1 Spot the structure: $\int f(g(x))g'(x)\,dx$ form, OR a hint substitution given
2 Set $u=g(x)$ (or whatever the question gives)
3 Compute $\dfrac{du}{dx}=g'(x)$ → $du=g'(x)\,dx$, so $dx=\dfrac{du}{g'(x)}$
4 Replace every $x$-term: convert $f(g(x))$ to $f(u)$, replace $dx$, and rewrite any leftover $x$ in terms of $u$
5 Integrate w.r.t. $u$
6 Indefinite: substitute back $u=g(x)$. Definite: change limits at step 2 (table $x\mid u$) — no need to back-substitute

Common substitution hints:

Integrand pattern Try
$f(\text{linear}) \cdot $ (linear) $u=$ linear
$\sqrt{ax+b}$ $u^2=ax+b$ (rational power becomes integer)
$f(\sin x)\cos x$ $u=\sin x$
$f(\cos x)\sin x$ $u=\cos x$
$f(\tan x)\sec^2 x$ $u=\tan x$
$f(e^x)e^x$ $u=e^x$
$\sqrt{a^2-x^2}$ $x=a\sin u$ (trig substitution)

5. Integration by parts (Section 6.4)

$$\int u\,\dfrac{dv}{dx}\,dx=uv-\int v\,\dfrac{du}{dx}\,dx.$$

Choosing $u$ — by integrand type Pick $u=$ $\dfrac{dv}{dx}=$
$x^n\sin x$, $x^n\cos x$ $x^n$ (algebraic) trig piece
$x^n e^{kx}$ $x^n$ $e^{kx}$
$x^n\ln x$ $\ln x$ $x^n$
Pure $\ln x$ $u=\ln x$, $\dfrac{dv}{dx}=1$ gives $\int \ln x\,dx=x\ln x-x+c$
$e^x\sin x$, $e^x\cos x$ Either; do parts twice, then solve algebraically (loop) —

LIATE order (priority for $u$): Logs > Inverse trig > Algebraic ($x^n$) > Trig > Exponential. The thing higher on the list goes as $u$.

Repeated parts: for $x^n e^x$ etc., apply parts $n$ times.

6. Partial-fraction integration (Section 6.5)

Form Integral
$\dfrac{A}{ax+b}$ $\dfrac{A}{a}\ln\lvert ax+b\rvert+c$
$\dfrac{A}{(ax+b)^k},\ k\ge 2$ $\dfrac{-A}{a(k-1)(ax+b)^{k-1}}+c$
$\dfrac{P(x)}{Q(x)}$ proper, $Q$ factorisable Decompose → integrate each piece
$\dfrac{P(x)}{Q(x)}$ improper Polynomial-divide first → polynomial part + proper part

Procedure:

  1. Check proper / improper.
  2. Factorise denominator (use factor theorem if needed).
  3. Decompose into partial fractions (Ch 2 method).
  4. Integrate piece-by-piece using the table above.
  5. Combine logs using $\ln a-\ln b=\ln(a/b)$, $n\ln a=\ln a^n$ if asked.

7. Separable DE recipe (Section 6.6)

Step Action
1 Rearrange to $\dfrac{dy}{dx}=f(x)g(y)$
2 Separate: $\dfrac{1}{g(y)}\,dy=f(x)\,dx$
3 Integrate each side separately, both producing integrals
4 Add one constant of integration ($+c$ or $+\ln k$ for log-friendly form)
5 If asked: rearrange to $y=h(x)$ (exponentiate, etc.)
6 If boundary condition given: substitute $(x_0,y_0)$ to find $c$ (or $k$). State the particular solution.

Tip: when both sides give logs, write $+\ln k$ instead of $+c$ to absorb sign and combine into $\ln$ form: $\ln\lvert y\rvert=\ln\lvert f(x)\rvert+\ln k\Rightarrow y=k f(x)$.

8. Modelling differential equations (Section 6.7)

Phrase DE
Population growth ∝ population $\dfrac{dP}{dt}=kP$ → $P=A e^{kt}$
Radioactive decay ∝ amount $\dfrac{dN}{dt}=-kN$ → $N=A e^{-kt}$
Newton's cooling $\dfrac{d\theta}{dt}=-k(\theta-\theta_0)$ → $\theta=\theta_0+A e^{-kt}$
Logistic-like (P3 only briefly here) $\dfrac{dP}{dt}=kP(M-P)$ — separate, partial-fraction integrate
Limitations of exponential model Real populations don't grow forever; food/space cap it — model breaks for large $t$

10. Decision flow — pick the technique

Spot this Do this
$\int f(g(x))g'(x)\,dx$ shape, OR substitution given Substitution
Product of two functions, one easy to integrate, one easy to differentiate Integration by parts
$\ln x$ in integrand Parts with $u=\ln x$
$e^x\sin x$ or $e^x\cos x$ Parts twice; solve algebraic loop
Rational function of $x$ Partial fractions (after polynomial division if improper)
$\sqrt{ax+b}$ alone $u^2=ax+b$
$\sqrt{a^2-x^2}$ Trig sub $x=a\sin\theta$
Definite integral Change limits OR integrate then sub limits
Curve given parametrically, area $\int g(t)f'(t)\,dt$
Curve given parametrically, volume of revolution $\pi\int [g(t)]^2 f'(t)\,dt$
$\dfrac{dy}{dx}=$ product of $x$-stuff and $y$-stuff Separable DE
$\dfrac{dy}{dx}=$ sum/difference of $x$ and $y$ — not separable Beyond P4 (use FP3)
Boundary condition given Find $c$ after general solution

11. Common exam pitfalls

Pitfall Fix
Forgetting $+c$ in indefinite integrals Always add the constant
Forgetting to change limits in substitution Build a table $x\mid u$, sub limits at step 2
Sign error in $\int e^{-kx}\,dx=-\dfrac{1}{k}e^{-kx}+c$ Watch the negative
$\int\dfrac{1}{(ax+b)^k}\,dx$ written as $\ln$ instead of power Only $k=1$ gives log; $k\ge 2$ gives a power
Forgetting modulus in $\ln\lvert\dots\rvert$ Always include $\lvert\ \rvert$ unless context is positive
Choosing $u$ wrong in parts (you get a worse integral) LIATE rule. If integrand has $\ln x$, ALWAYS $u=\ln x$
Forgetting $dx=f'(t)\,dt$ in parametric volume $V=\pi\int [g(t)]^2 f'(t)\,dt$, not $\pi\int [g(t)]^2\,dt$
Forgetting to square $y$ in volume of revolution $\pi\int y^2\,dx$, not $\pi\int y\,dx$
Not factorising denominator before partial-fraction E.g. $1-x^2=(1-x)(1+x)$
Improper rational function: forgetting to divide first Always check degrees
In separable DE: integrating both sides but forgetting one constant Add one $+c$ at the end (or $+\ln k$ for log style)
Using boundary condition before integrating Integrate first, get general, then sub boundary
In modelling: writing $+kP$ for decay Decay = $-kP$
Volume formula about $y$-axis instead of $x$-axis $V_x$-axis $=\pi\int y^2\,dx$; $V_y$-axis $=\pi\int x^2\,dy$

12. Big picture (30-second mental model)

P4 integration is techniques + applications + DEs:

  1. Standard antiderivatives — memorise the lookup table.
  2. Substitution — reverse chain rule. Most common: hint-given $u$, or spot $f(g(x))g'(x)$.
  3. Parts — reverse product rule. LIATE for choosing $u$.
  4. Partial fractions — reverse "common denominator". Each piece integrates to a log or power.
  5. Applications — $A=\int g(t)f'(t)\,dt$ for parametric area; $V=\pi\int y^2\,dx$ for volume.
  6. Separable DEs — split $f(x)$ from $g(y)$, integrate each side, add $+c$, apply boundary.
  7. Modelling — translate "rate ∝ X" into DE, then solve.

Eight techniques, one mindset: find the structure, pick the tool.
