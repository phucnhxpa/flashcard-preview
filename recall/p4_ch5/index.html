P4 Ch 5 — Differentiation (Parametric, Implicit, Rates)

The whole chapter in one mental model. Three flavours of differentiation beyond the standard $\dfrac{dy}{dx}$ machinery: (1) Parametric — when $x=p(t),\ y=q(t)$, use $\dfrac{dy}{dx}=\dfrac{dy/dt}{dx/dt}$. (2) Implicit — when $x$ and $y$ are tangled in a single equation $F(x,y)=0$, differentiate both sides with respect to $x$, treating $y$ as a function of $x$ via the chain rule: $\dfrac{d}{dx}f(y)=f'(y)\dfrac{dy}{dx}$. Solve for $\dfrac{dy}{dx}$. (3) Rates of change / connected rates — chain rule between time-derivatives of related quantities: $\dfrac{dA}{dt}=\dfrac{dA}{dr}\cdot\dfrac{dr}{dt}$. Differential equations come from translating "rate ∝ X" into $\dfrac{d?}{dt}=k\cdot X$ (with sign).

1. Jargon glossary

Term Meaning Where
Parametric differentiation Find $\dfrac{dy}{dx}$ when both $x$ and $y$ are functions of a parameter $t$. Two separate derivatives, divided. Result usually still in $t$. 5.1
Chain rule for parametric $\dfrac{dy}{dx}=\dfrac{dy/dt}{dx/dt}$. From $\dfrac{dy}{dt}=\dfrac{dy}{dx}\cdot\dfrac{dx}{dt}$, divide. 5.1
Tangent line At point $P=(x_0,y_0)$, the line $y-y_0=m(x-x_0)$ where $m=\dfrac{dy}{dx}\big\rvert_P$ is the gradient at $P$. 5.1 Ex 2
Normal line Perpendicular to tangent at $P$. Gradient $-1/m$. Equation $y-y_0=-\dfrac{1}{m}(x-x_0)$. 5.1 Ex 2
Implicit equation An equation $F(x,y)=0$ where $y$ is not isolated. Examples: $x^2+xy+y^3=2$, $\sin(x+y)=2x$. May or may not be solvable for $y$. 5.2
Implicit differentiation Differentiate both sides of $F(x,y)=0$ w.r.t. $x$, treating $y$ as a function of $x$. Use chain rule on every $y$-term: $\dfrac{d}{dx}y^n=ny^{n-1}\dfrac{dy}{dx}$. 5.2
Product rule on $xy$ $\dfrac{d}{dx}(xy)=x\dfrac{dy}{dx}+y$. Critical move in implicit differentiation when an $xy$ term appears. 5.2
Stationary / zero-gradient point Where $\dfrac{dy}{dx}=0$. In parametric/implicit problems, set the numerator of $\dfrac{dy}{dx}$ to $0$. 5.1 Q4, 5.2 Q6
Vertical tangent Where $\dfrac{dx}{dy}=0$ (or $\dfrac{dy}{dx}\to\infty$). Set denominator to $0$. 5.1 challenge
Connected rates of change Two or more quantities related by an equation; their time-derivatives are linked via chain rule. 5.3
Differential equation An equation involving a derivative, e.g. $\dfrac{dN}{dt}=-kN$. Models real-world processes (decay, cooling, growth). 5.3
Constant of proportionality $k$ The fixed positive constant in "rate ∝ X". The sign in front of $k$ encodes whether the quantity is growing ($+k$) or shrinking ($-k$). 5.3 Ex 8–10
Newton's law of cooling $\dfrac{d\theta}{dt}=-k(\theta-\theta_0)$ — temperature drop is proportional to excess over surroundings $\theta_0$. 5.3 Ex 9
Radioactive decay $\dfrac{dN}{dt}=-kN$ — exponential decay of particles, since rate ∝ amount remaining. 5.3 Ex 8

5. Tangent / normal procedure (parametric or implicit)

Step Action
1 Find $\dfrac{dy}{dx}$ (parametric: $\dfrac{dy/dt}{dx/dt}$; implicit: differentiate, solve for $\dfrac{dy}{dx}$)
2 Substitute the given parameter $t_0$ (or coords $(x_0,y_0)$) to get gradient $m$
3 Find the point: in parametric, sub $t_0$ into $x(t),y(t)$; in implicit, the point is given
4 Tangent: $y-y_0=m(x-x_0)$. Normal: $y-y_0=-\dfrac{1}{m}(x-x_0)$
5 Simplify to required form (e.g. $ax+by+c=0$ with integer/exact constants)

6. Worked archetypes

# Setup Key step
1 $x=t^3+t,\ y=t^2+1,\ t=2$ $\dfrac{dy/dt}{dx/dt}=\dfrac{2t}{3t^2+1}=\dfrac{4}{13}$
2 $x=3\sin\theta,\ y=5\cos\theta,\ \theta=\pi/6$. Find normal $\dfrac{dy}{dx}=\dfrac{-5\sin\theta}{3\cos\theta}=-\dfrac{5}{3\sqrt3}$. Normal grad $=\dfrac{3\sqrt3}{5}$
3 $x^3+x+y^3+3y=6$ — implicit $3x^2+1+3y^2\dfrac{dy}{dx}+3\dfrac{dy}{dx}=0\Rightarrow\dfrac{dy}{dx}=-\dfrac{3x^2+1}{3(1+y^2)}$
4 $4xy^2+\dfrac{6x^2}{y}=10$ at $(1,1)$ Differentiate, sub $(1,1)$: $8\dfrac{dy}{dx}+4+12-6\dfrac{dy}{dx}=0\Rightarrow\dfrac{dy}{dx}=-8$
5 $e^{2x}\ln y=x+y-2$ at $(1,1)$ $e^{2x}\cdot\dfrac{1}{y}\dfrac{dy}{dx}+2e^{2x}\ln y=1+\dfrac{dy}{dx}$. Sub: $e^2\dfrac{dy}{dx}=1+\dfrac{dy}{dx}\Rightarrow\dfrac{dy}{dx}=\dfrac{1}{e^2-1}$
6 Circle area $A=\pi r^2$, $\dfrac{dr}{dt}=5$. Find $\dfrac{dA}{dt}$ at $r=3$ $\dfrac{dA}{dt}=\dfrac{dA}{dr}\cdot\dfrac{dr}{dt}=2\pi r\cdot 5=30\pi$
7 Hemisphere $V=\tfrac{2}{3}\pi r^3,\ S=3\pi r^2$, $\dfrac{dV}{dt}=6$. Find $\dfrac{dS}{dt}$ $\dfrac{dS}{dt}=\dfrac{dS}{dr}\cdot\dfrac{dr}{dV}\cdot\dfrac{dV}{dt}=6\pi r\cdot\dfrac{1}{2\pi r^2}\cdot 6=\dfrac{18}{r}$
8 "Rate of decay $\propto$ amount remaining" $\dfrac{dN}{dt}=-kN$
9 "Newton's cooling: rate of temp loss $\propto$ excess over surroundings $\theta_0$" $\dfrac{d\theta}{dt}=-k(\theta-\theta_0)$
10 Snowman head: $\dfrac{dV}{dt}=-kA$ where $V=\tfrac43\pi R^3,\ A=4\pi R^2$ $4\pi R^2\dfrac{dR}{dt}=-k\cdot 4\pi R^2\Rightarrow\dfrac{dR}{dt}=-k$ — radius shrinks at constant rate

7. Differential equations from word problems — translation table

Word phrase Maths
"Rate of change of $X$ w.r.t. time" $\dfrac{dX}{dt}$
"$\dfrac{dX}{dt}$ is proportional to $Y$" $\dfrac{dX}{dt}=kY$

"Inversely proportional to" $\dfrac{dX}{dt}=\dfrac{k}{Y}$
"Rate at which water leaks out" $\dfrac{dV}{dt}=-(\dots)$

9. Common exam pitfalls

Pitfall Fix
Forgetting to apply chain rule to $y$-terms in implicit Every $y$-term gets $\dfrac{dy}{dx}$ multiplier
Forgetting product rule on $xy,\ x^2y,\ xy^2,\ etc.$ Two pieces: differentiate $x$ part, then $y$ part with $\dfrac{dy}{dx}$
Sign error in normal: using $+1/m$ instead of $-1/m$ Normal is perpendicular: $m_{\text{normal}}=-\dfrac{1}{m_{\text{tangent}}}$
For parametric, calculating $\dfrac{dy}{dx}=\dfrac{dx/dt}{dy/dt}$ (inverted) Top is $dy/dt$, bottom is $dx/dt$
Substituting $(x_0,y_0)$ before rearranging $\dfrac{dy}{dx}$ — fine and often easier; but make sure each term is differentiated first Sub-after-differentiate is the rule of thumb
Forgetting the minus in decay/cooling DE "Loss"/"decay" ⇒ $-k$ in front
Using $\dfrac{dV}{dt}=\dfrac{dV}{dr}$ alone Always include the chain: $\cdot\dfrac{dr}{dt}$
Forgetting units in DE Track them; helps spot $-k$ vs $+k$ errors
In $a^x$: writing derivative as $a^x$ instead of $a^x\ln a$ $\dfrac{d}{dx}a^x=a^x\ln a$
Stationary points: solving $\dfrac{dy}{dx}=0$ ignoring excluded $t$ values Check parameter domain

10. Big picture (30-second mental model)

Differentiation generalises:

  1. Parametric: $x,y$ both depend on $t$ → $\dfrac{dy}{dx}=\dfrac{dy/dt}{dx/dt}$. Used when the curve is "flowing" along a parameter.
  2. Implicit: $F(x,y)=0$ → differentiate term-by-term, treat $y=y(x)$, use chain rule on every $y$-term, then solve algebraically for $\dfrac{dy}{dx}$.
  3. Rates of change: chain rule connects time-derivatives of related quantities. The differential equation is built from "rate ∝ ..." with appropriate sign.

Three modes, one chain rule — that's the chapter.
