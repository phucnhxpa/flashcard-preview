ESAT Recall Sheet

ESAT Maths (22 May) — Geometry & Problem-Solving Recall Sheet

The whole lesson in one mental model. The ESAT rewards speed under constraint: 27 multiple-choice questions in 40 minutes (~90 s each), no calculator, no formula sheet, only the final answer is marked. So you win by (1) having every mensuration formula memorised, (2) reducing variables to match the answer options, and (3) recognising a small set of geometry archetypes — areas with cut-outs, similar triangles, 3D Pythagoras, ratios, and continuously-moving clock hands.

1. Jargon glossary

Term Student-friendly meaning Exact meaning Symbols / units What it is NOT Lesson example Phuc / common trap
Semicircle area Half a circle’s area. Area enclosed by a diameter and half the circumference \(=\tfrac12\pi r^2\). \(r\) = radius (length); area in length². NOT \(\pi r^2\) (that’s a full circle). Square minus a semicircular cut-out \(= m^2-\tfrac12\pi r^2\). [Phuc trap] wrote \(\pi r^2\) for the cut-out (forgot the \(\tfrac12\)) — doubles the removed area.
Reduce variables to match options Use the multiple-choice answers to decide what to solve for. If options are in terms of one variable, eliminate the others early so your algebra targets that variable’s form. — NOT a licence to guess; it shapes the algebra, you still derive. Side of square expressed as \(20\sqrt{2/(8-\pi)}\) to match an option. Solving for the wrong variable, then having no matching option.
Similar triangles Same shape, scaled — equal angles, proportional sides. Triangles with equal corresponding angles; corresponding side ratios are all equal. ratio (dimensionless). NOT merely “both right-angled”; need equal angles. Perpendicular from hypotenuse splits a right triangle into two similar to the original. Pairing the wrong corresponding sides in the ratio.
Altitude on hypotenuse The height dropped from the right angle to the longest side. The line from the right-angle vertex perpendicular to the hypotenuse; length \(h=\dfrac{\text{leg}_1\cdot\text{leg}_2}{\text{hypotenuse}}\). length. NOT a median (which goes to the midpoint). \(h=\dfrac{20\cdot10}{10\sqrt5}=\dfrac{20}{\sqrt5}=4\sqrt5\). Forgetting to rationalise the surd to match an option.
Rationalising the denominator Removing the root from the bottom of a fraction. Multiply top and bottom by the surd so the denominator becomes rational. — NOT changing the value — it’s an equivalent form. \(\dfrac{20}{\sqrt5}\cdot\dfrac{\sqrt5}{\sqrt5}=\dfrac{20\sqrt5}{5}=4\sqrt5\). Leaving \(20/\sqrt5\) when options are rationalised.
3D Pythagoras Pythagoras done twice to get a diagonal through a solid. Find a base diagonal, then combine with the vertical to get the space diagonal: apply \(a^2+b^2=c^2\) in two stages. length. NOT a single application of \(a^2+b^2=c^2\). Vertex to midpoint of opposite face of a unit cube \(=\tfrac{\sqrt6}{2}\). Mixing up which lengths form the right angle in the second triangle.
Ratio (combine via common letter) Linking two ratios that share a quantity. To merge \(A:B\) and \(B:C\), scale so the shared term \(B\) matches, then read \(A:C\). dimensionless. NOT just stacking numbers — scale both parts. \(Q:R\) and \(R:S \to Q:S = 3:4\). [Phuc trap] doubled only \(R\) (3→6) but left \(S\), getting 3:2 instead of 3:4.
Clock angle The angle between the hour and minute hands. The hour hand moves continuously: at \(H{:}M\) it sits \(30H+0.5M\) degrees from 12; minute hand at \(6M\). degrees. NOT “hour hand parked on the hour number”. 9:45 → hour hand ¾ of the way to 10. [Phuc trap] assumed the hand stays on 9.
Mean (with a new member) Average; total ÷ count, updated when someone joins. \(\bar x=\dfrac{\sum x}{n}\); with a new member recompute the total then divide by the new \(n\). same units as the data. NOT the average of the two averages. Group mean age recomputed after a member joins. Averaging averages instead of using totals.

2. Formula / case recall — mensuration (memorise: ESAT gives NO formula sheet)

Quantity / case Formula Symbol meanings Units When to use Rearrangements Trap Mini worked example
Circle area \(A=\pi r^2\) \(r\) radius length² round regions \(r=\sqrt{A/\pi}\) using \(d\) instead of \(r\) \(r=3\Rightarrow A=9\pi\)
Semicircle area \(A=\tfrac12\pi r^2\) \(r\) radius length² half-disc cut-outs — dropping the \(\tfrac12\) \(r=2\Rightarrow A=2\pi\)
Circle circumference \(C=2\pi r=\pi d\) \(d\) diameter length arc/perimeter parts \(r=C/2\pi\) mixing \(C\) and \(A\) \(r=5\Rightarrow C=10\pi\)
Triangle area \(A=\tfrac12 bh\) \(b\) base, \(h\) ⟂ height length² any triangle \(h=2A/b\) using a slant side as \(h\) \(b=6,h=4\Rightarrow A=12\)
Pythagoras (2D) \(a^2+b^2=c^2\) \(c\) hypotenuse length right triangles \(a=\sqrt{c^2-b^2}\) calling a leg the hypotenuse \(3,4\Rightarrow c=5\)
Altitude on hypotenuse \(h=\dfrac{ab}{c}\) \(a,b\) legs, \(c\) hyp length ⟂ from right angle \(c=\dfrac{ab}{h}\) not rationalising \(\dfrac{20\cdot10}{10\sqrt5}=4\sqrt5\)

4. Exam-technique recall (ESAT-specific)

Cue Action
27 Q / 40 min ~90 s each — triage: bank the quick ones, skip-and-return on fiddly ones
No calculator / no formula sheet every formula above is memorised; do surds/fractions by hand
Only final answer marked working earns nothing — but neat working still prevents slips
Answer options are surds rationalise to match the listed form
Geometry wording draw a diagram (rough is fine); look for right angles & similar triangles
Messy algebra forming factor out the common term; reduce variables to the option’s form

5. Big picture

  • The ESAT is “GCSE/early-A-level content at speed”. Fluency with mensuration + surds is the whole game.
  • Three reflexes win marks: draw it, factor early, rationalise to match.
  • Your two evidenced slips here — ½ on the semicircle and scaling only one part of a ratio — are both “rushing the routine step”; slow down on the mechanical move.
