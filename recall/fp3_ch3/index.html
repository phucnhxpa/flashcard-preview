FP3 Ch 3 — Differentiation

The whole chapter in one mental model. Three families of new derivatives to memorise: (i) hyperbolic functions — derivatives are almost trig but with $\cosh\to+\sinh$ (no minus), (ii) inverse hyperbolic — derivatives are square-root reciprocals of $x^2\pm 1$, and (iii) inverse trig — derivatives are $1/\sqrt{1-x^2}$ or $1/(1+x^2)$. Once you know these, every problem is product/quotient/chain rule with the standard derivatives plugged in. Many "show that" questions involve squaring, cross-multiplying, or differentiating implicit identities derived from these.

1. Jargon glossary

Term Meaning Where
Hyperbolic derivative pattern $(\sinh x)'=\cosh x$, $(\cosh x)'=\sinh x$ — the same as trig BUT both with positive signs (no minus on $\cosh$). The reciprocals $\operatorname{sech},\operatorname{cosech},\coth$ all derive negative signs (as in trig). Mnemonic: hyperbolic = "trig minus the minuses on $\sin/\cos$ pair". 3.1
Inverse-hyperbolic derivative The derivative of an inverse hyperbolic function is a square-root reciprocal: $(\operatorname{arsinh}x)'=\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{x^2+1}}$, $(\operatorname{arcosh}x)'=\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{x^2-1}}$, $(\operatorname{artanh}x)'=\dfrac{1}{1-x^2}$. The denominator structure mirrors the inverse-trig pattern but with the sign inside the radical flipped. 3.2
Inverse-trig derivative $(\arcsin x)'=\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}$, $(\arccos x)'=-\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}$, $(\arctan x)'=\dfrac{1}{1+x^2}$. Domain restrictions: $\arcsin/\arccos$ need $\lvert x\rvert<1$; $\arctan$ defined everywhere. 3.3
Implicit differentiation derivation The standard way to prove an inverse-function derivative: write $y=\arcsin x$, take $\sin y=x$, differentiate both sides w.r.t. $x$ to get $\cos y\dfrac{\mathrm dy}{\mathrm dx}=1$, solve, then convert $\cos y$ back to $\sqrt{1-x^2}$ using the Pythagorean identity. 3.3 Ex 7
Chain rule $\dfrac{\mathrm d}{\mathrm dx}f(g(x))=f'(g(x))\cdot g'(x)$. E.g. $\dfrac{\mathrm d}{\mathrm dx}\cosh 3x=3\sinh 3x$. 3.1
Product rule $(uv)'=u'v+uv'$. Used for things like $x^2\cosh 4x$ or $x\operatorname{arcosh}3x$. 3.1 Ex 3
Quotient rule $\left(\dfrac{u}{v}\right)'=\dfrac{u'v-uv'}{v^2}$. Often easier to rewrite as $u\cdot v^{-1}$ and use product+chain. 3.1 Q9
"Show that" pattern Many exam questions: differentiate, square, multiply, then substitute back. Goal is usually to derive an identity like $(x^2-1)y'^2=4y$ from $y=(\operatorname{arcosh}x)^2$. 3.2 Ex 6
Stationary value A point where $\dfrac{\mathrm dy}{\mathrm dx}=0$. For $y=12\cosh x-\sinh x$, set $12\sinh x-\cosh x=0$ → $\tanh x=1/12$ → $x=\operatorname{artanh}(1/12)$. Ex 3A Q18
Tangent at a hyperbola point Differentiating parametrically with $x=a\cosh q,\ y=b\sinh q$ gives $\dfrac{\mathrm dy}{\mathrm dx}=\dfrac{b\cosh q}{a\sinh q}$, hence tangent $bx\cosh q-ay\sinh q=ab$ (uses $\cosh^2-\sinh^2=1$). Ex 3A Q20
Domain caveat (arcosh) $\operatorname{arcosh}x$ defined only $x\ge 1$; its derivative formula $\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{x^2-1}}$ blows up at $x=1$. 3.2
Domain caveat (artanh) $\operatorname{artanh}x$ defined only $\lvert x\rvert<1$; derivative $\dfrac{1}{1-x^2}$ blows up at $x=\pm 1$. 3.2
Sign on $\arccos$ The derivative is negative: $(\arccos x)'=-(\arcsin x)'$. Easy to forget. Memorise: $\arccos x=\tfrac{\pi}{2}-\arcsin x$, so derivatives differ only in sign. 3.3

2. Hyperbolic-function derivatives (Section 3.1)

Function Derivative Sign vs trig analogue Trick / note
$\sinh x$ $\cosh x$ same as $\sin\to\cos$ Derive from $\sinh x=\tfrac{e^x-e^{-x}}{2}$
$\cosh x$ $+\sinh x$ opposite to $\cos\to-\sin$ The "no-minus" rule for hyperbolic
$\tanh x$ $\operatorname{sech}^2 x$ same as $\tan\to\sec^2$ Both share the squared-secant pattern
$\coth x$ $-\operatorname{cosech}^2 x$ same as $\cot\to-\operatorname{cosec}^2$ Both retain the negative
$\operatorname{sech}x$ $-\operatorname{sech}x\tanh x$ same as $\sec\to\sec\tan$ but negative "Sech derivative = neg sech tanh"
$\operatorname{cosech}x$ $-\operatorname{cosech}x\coth x$ same as $\operatorname{cosec}\to-\operatorname{cosec}\cot$ "Cosech derivative = neg cosech coth"

With argument $kx$ (chain rule): $\dfrac{\mathrm d}{\mathrm dx}\sinh(kx)=k\cosh(kx)$, $\dfrac{\mathrm d}{\mathrm dx}\cosh(kx)=k\sinh(kx)$, etc.

3. Inverse-hyperbolic derivatives (Section 3.2)

Function Derivative Domain
$\operatorname{arsinh}x$ $\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{x^2+1}}$ all $x\in\mathbb R$
$\operatorname{arcosh}x$ $\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{x^2-1}}$ $x>1$
$\operatorname{artanh}x$ $\dfrac{1}{1-x^2}$ $\lvert x\rvert<1$
$\operatorname{arcoth}x$ $\dfrac{1}{1-x^2}$ $\lvert x\rvert>1$
$\operatorname{arsech}x$ $-\dfrac{1}{x\sqrt{1-x^2}}$ $0
$\operatorname{arcosech}x$ $-\dfrac{1}{\lvert x\rvert\sqrt{x^2+1}}$ $x\ne 0$

Memory pattern: $\operatorname{arsinh}\to\sqrt{x^2+1}$ (plus inside), $\operatorname{arcosh}\to\sqrt{x^2-1}$ (minus). Compare inverse trig: $\arcsin\to\sqrt{1-x^2}$ (subtract from 1), $\arctan\to(1+x^2)$.

4. Inverse-trig derivatives (Section 3.3)

Function Derivative Domain
$\arcsin x$ $\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}$ $\lvert x\rvert<1$
$\arccos x$ $-\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}$ $\lvert x\rvert<1$
$\arctan x$ $\dfrac{1}{1+x^2}$ all $x\in\mathbb R$
$\operatorname{arccot}x$ $-\dfrac{1}{1+x^2}$ all $x\in\mathbb R$
$\operatorname{arcsec}x$ $\dfrac{1}{\lvert x\rvert\sqrt{x^2-1}}$ $\lvert x\rvert>1$
$\operatorname{arccosec}x$ $-\dfrac{1}{\lvert x\rvert\sqrt{x^2-1}}$ $\lvert x\rvert>1$

With chain rule: $\dfrac{\mathrm d}{\mathrm dx}\arcsin(kx)=\dfrac{k}{\sqrt{1-k^2x^2}}$; $\dfrac{\mathrm d}{\mathrm dx}\arctan(kx)=\dfrac{k}{1+k^2x^2}$.

Family $\sin/\sinh$ $\cos/\cosh$ $\tan/\tanh$

Hyperbolic direct $\cosh x$ $+\sinh x$ $\operatorname{sech}^2 x$

6. Worked-out archetype examples

# Problem Method Answer
1 Differentiate $\cosh 3x$ Chain rule $3\sinh 3x$
2 Differentiate $x^2\cosh 4x$ Product + chain $2x\cosh 4x+4x^2\sinh 4x$
3 $y=A\cosh 3x+B\sinh 3x$, show $y''=9y$ Differentiate twice; coefficients re-emerge $y''=9A\cosh 3x+9B\sinh 3x=9y$
4 $\dfrac{\mathrm d}{\mathrm dx}\ln(\cosh x)$ Chain on $\ln$ $\dfrac{\sinh x}{\cosh x}=\tanh x$
5 Differentiate $x\operatorname{arcosh}x$ Product rule + standard formula $\operatorname{arcosh}x+\dfrac{x}{\sqrt{x^2-1}}$
6 $y=(\operatorname{arcosh}x)^2$, show $(x^2-1)(y')^2=4y$ $y'=\dfrac{2\operatorname{arcosh}x}{\sqrt{x^2-1}}$, square, multiply by $x^2-1$ matches
7 Differentiate $\arcsin x^2$ Chain: $\sin y=x^2$, $\cos y\,y'=2x$, $\cos y=\sqrt{1-x^4}$ $\dfrac{2x}{\sqrt{1-x^4}}$
8 $y=\arctan\!\left(\dfrac{1-x}{1+x}\right)$, find $y'$ Implicit diff + algebra simplification $-\dfrac{1}{1+x^2}$
9 Stationary points of $y=12\cosh x-\sinh x$ $y'=12\sinh x-\cosh x=0$ → $\tanh x=1/12$ $x=\operatorname{artanh}(1/12)$
10 Derive $\arcsin x$'s derivative from scratch $\sin y=x$, $\cos y\,y'=1$, $\cos y=\sqrt{1-x^2}$ $y'=\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}$

7. "Show that" identity machine

Many FP3 questions look like "given $y=f(x)$, show that $g(x,y,y',y'')=0$." Procedure:

Step Action
1 Differentiate $y$ once → $y'$
2 If asked for $y''$ later, differentiate again — use product/chain rule
3 Multiply both sides by the appropriate factor (often $\sqrt{1\pm x^2}$ or $1\pm x^2$) to clear radicals
4 Square both sides to remove residual square roots
5 Substitute the original $y$-relation to eliminate the inverse function
6 Rearrange to match the target identity

Worked example (3.2 Ex 6): $y=(\operatorname{arcosh}x)^2 \Rightarrow y'=\dfrac{2\operatorname{arcosh}x}{\sqrt{x^2-1}} \Rightarrow \sqrt{x^2-1}\,y'=2\operatorname{arcosh}x \Rightarrow (x^2-1)(y')^2=4(\operatorname{arcosh}x)^2=4y$.

8. Common pitfalls

Pitfall Fix
Writing $(\cosh x)'=-\sinh x$ (trig habit) Hyperbolic: $(\cosh x)'=+\sinh x$ — no minus
$(\arccos x)'=+\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}$ (forgot the minus) $\arccos$ has a leading minus
Confusing $(\operatorname{arsinh}x)'$ with $(\arcsin x)'$ — both look similar Inverse hyperbolic has plus under root: $\sqrt{x^2+1}$. Inverse trig has minus from 1: $\sqrt{1-x^2}$
Forgetting chain rule on $\sinh(kx)$, $\cosh(kx)$ Always multiply by inner derivative $k$
$(\operatorname{arcosh}x)'$ at $x=1$ Domain $x>1$; derivative undefined at $x=1$
$(\operatorname{artanh}x)'$ at $x=\pm 1$ Domain $\lvert x\rvert<1$; derivative undefined at boundary
In implicit diff: forgetting $\dfrac{\mathrm dy}{\mathrm dx}$ when differentiating $y$-terms Treat $y$ as function of $x$; derivatives of $y^n$ require $\dfrac{\mathrm dy}{\mathrm dx}$
In "show that" problems: stopping after first derivative Often need 2nd derivative or to square / multiply both sides
Trying to use trig identities on hyperbolic functions Use $\cosh^2-\sinh^2=1$ (note minus), not $\cosh^2+\sinh^2=1$
Not simplifying after using chain rule E.g. $\dfrac{\mathrm d}{\mathrm dx}\arctan(\dfrac{1-x}{1+x})$ simplifies to $-\dfrac{1}{1+x^2}$

9. Big picture (the 30-second mental model)

This chapter is a derivative lookup table plus the standard rules:

  1. Hyperbolic direct: derivative pattern is "trig but no minuses on the $\sinh$/$\cosh$ pair". Reciprocals (sech, cosech, coth) keep their negative signs.
  2. Inverse hyperbolic: derivatives are square-root-reciprocals of $x^2\pm 1$ (or $1-x^2$). Memorise three core formulas: $\operatorname{arsinh}\to\sqrt{x^2+1}$, $\operatorname{arcosh}\to\sqrt{x^2-1}$, $\operatorname{artanh}\to 1-x^2$.
  3. Inverse trig: derivatives are square-root-reciprocals of $1-x^2$ (for $\arcsin,\arccos$) or $1+x^2$ (for $\arctan$). $\arccos$ has a minus.
  4. All derivations: use implicit differentiation on $\sin y=x$ etc., applying $\sin^2+\cos^2=1$ or $\cosh^2-\sinh^2=1$ to convert back into $x$.
  5. Application machinery: chain, product, quotient rules; then "show-that" problems often require differentiating, multiplying through, squaring, and substituting.

Three derivative families × three calculus rules — that's the chapter.
