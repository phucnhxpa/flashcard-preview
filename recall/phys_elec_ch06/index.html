The whole topic in one mental model. Two conservation laws run every circuit. Charge is conserved → Kirchhoff's First Law (KCL): at *every* junction $\sum I_{in} = \sum I_{out}$ — this holds in *any* circuit, series or parallel, always, because charge can never pile up or vanish at a point. Energy is conserved → Kirchhoff's Second Law (KVL): around *any* closed loop $\sum \varepsilon = \sum IR$ — the e.m.f. given to each coulomb equals the total p.d. it drops on that single trip round one loop. From these two laws everything else falls out: in series, current is the same and voltages add, so $R_T = \sum R_i$ (resistance *increases*); in parallel, voltage is the same and currents add, so $\frac{1}{R_T} = \sum \frac{1}{R_i}$ (resistance *decreases*, always below the smallest branch). For combination networks: collapse each parallel sub-circuit first, then add series values. The single golden rule for proofs (Alex, 52:53): *start from the quantity that adds up* — series → voltage adds; parallel → current adds.

5. Combination-network method (two-step golden rule)

  • Step 1 — collapse the parallel section first. Compute the parallel sub-circuit's $R_p$ using $\frac{1}{R_p} = \sum \frac{1}{R_i}$ (or product-over-sum for two). *Never* apply a single formula to the whole network at once (Phuc's 55:22 error).
  • Step 2 — add series resistances. $R_T = R_{series} + R_p$.
  • Step 3 — total current from $I_T = \varepsilon / R_T$.
  • Step 4 — series p.d. across each series element: $V = I_T R$ (same current).
  • Step 5 — parallel-section p.d. by KVL: $V_p = \varepsilon - V_{series}$.
  • Step 6 — branch currents in the parallel section: $I_i = V_p / R_i$.
  • Always check both laws: KCL ($\sum I_{branch} = I_T$) and KVL ($\sum V = \varepsilon$).

Worked instance (Example 1, Figure 4): $12.0$ V; $R_1 = 8.0\ \Omega$ series with $R_2 = 3.0\ \Omega \parallel R_3 = 6.0\ \Omega$. $R_{23} = 2.0\ \Omega \Rightarrow R_T = 10.0\ \Omega \Rightarrow I_T = 1.2$ A. $V_1 = 1.2 \times 8.0 = 9.6$ V; $V_{23} = 12.0 - 9.6 = 2.4$ V; $I_2 = 2.4/3.0 = 0.80$ A, $I_3 = 2.4/6.0 = 0.40$ A. Check KCL: $0.80 + 0.40 = 1.20$ A ✓; KVL: $9.6 + 2.4 = 12.0$ V ✓.

Mark-scheme keywords

  • KCL: "consequence of conservation of charge"; "sum of currents into a junction equals the sum out".
  • KVL: "consequence of conservation of energy"; "sum of e.m.f.s equals the sum of p.d.s around a loop".
  • Series derivation: must show $V_T = V_1 + V_2$, then $IR_T = IR_1 + IR_2$, then cancel $I$.
  • Parallel derivation: must show $I_T = I_1 + I_2$, then $V/R_T = V/R_1 + V/R_2$, then cancel $V$.
  • Always show working — "no ecf if no working" is standard.
