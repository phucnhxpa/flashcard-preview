0.2 The four boundaries this pack keeps explicit

Because these are the exact places marks (and provenance) leak, each has its own flagged treatment and is called out inline wherever it applies:

  1. \(f(x)>0\) — validity condition on \(\dfrac{d}{dx}\ln f(x)=\dfrac{f'(x)}{f(x)}\) and on dropping the modulus in \(\ln|f(x)|\). [S]-derived (a mathematical validity condition; all lesson examples satisfy it), not a verbatim tutor statement. See §2 and §6.
  2. Radians — the derivative formulae and the \(R\)-form angle assume \(x\)/\(\alpha\) in radians; Q4(b) explicitly wanted \(\alpha\) in radians to 3 s.f. The radian assumption on the derivative formulae is [S]; the Q4 radian demand is [T] (from the board). See §7.2.
  3. Phase / quadrant — in the \(R\)-form, \(\tan\alpha=\tfrac{b}{a}\) alone does not fix \(\alpha\); the quadrant is fixed by the signs of \(a,b\) (equivalently \(\alpha=\operatorname{atan2}(b,a)\)). [S]-derived. Here \(a,b>0\Rightarrow\) first-quadrant \(\alpha\). See §5.
  4. Tutor-vs-official derived boundary — every [S] row is a specification expansion added for consolidation, not something Harry said. The tutor drilled the formulae from memory; the conditions and general rules around them are the official layer. This boundary is the spine of §0 and §8.

1. Jargon glossary

The canonical 7-column recall table. Definitions are 30–80 words. Tier tags: [T] tutor, [S] spec, [E] email.

Term Student-friendly meaning Exact mathematical meaning Symbols / conditions What it is NOT Lesson example Phuc / common trap
Power rule [T][S] To differentiate a variable raised to a fixed number, drop the power to the front and knock one off it. \(\dfrac{d}{dx}(x^{n})=n\,x^{n-1}\). The variable is in the base, the constant is the exponent. \(n\) constant; variable in the base. Not the \(a^x\) rule. If the variable is in the power, this rule does not apply. \(\tfrac{d}{dx}(x^3)=3x^2\), \(\tfrac{d}{dx}(x^4)=4x^3\) (basics check). Phuc trap: first wrote \(\tfrac{d}{dx}(a^x)=x\,a^{x-1}\) — power rule mis-applied to an exponential. Harry: check where the variable is.
Exponential-base rule \(a^{x}\) [T][S] Differentiating a number to a variable power leaves the number-power alone and pulls down a natural log of the base. \(\dfrac{d}{dx}(a^{x})=a^{x}\ln a\). Variable in the power; base \(a\) constant. \(a>0\) constant [S]. Chain form: \(\tfrac{d}{dx}(a^{kx})=k\,a^{kx}\ln a\). Not \(x\,a^{x-1}\) (power rule) and the factor is \(\ln a\), not \(\ln(kx)\). \(\tfrac{d}{dx}(3^{x})=3^{x}\ln3\); \(\tfrac{d}{dx}(3^{2x})=2\cdot3^{2x}\ln3\); \(\tfrac{d}{dt}(9^t)=9^t\ln9=2\cdot9^t\ln3\). Phuc trap: the \(x\,a^{x-1}\) slip above. Harry: “\(a\) is a constant, \(x\) is a variable” — variable in the power \(\Rightarrow a^x\ln a\).
\(\ln f(x)\) derivative (chain) [T][S] Differentiate a natural log by putting the derivative of the inside on top and keeping the inside on the bottom. \(\dfrac{d}{dx}\ln f(x)=\dfrac{f'(x)}{f(x)}\). Requires \(f(x)>0\) so \(\ln f(x)\) is real [S] (validity condition; all lesson examples satisfy it). Not just \(\tfrac1{f(x)}\) — you must include \(f'(x)\) on top. \(\tfrac{d}{dx}\ln(x^2+3x+5)=\dfrac{2x+3}{x^2+3x+5}\); for \(\ln(kx)\) the \(k\) cancels: \(\tfrac{k}{kx}=\tfrac1x\). Phuc trap: wrote \(\tfrac{1}{3x}\) for \(\tfrac{d}{dx}\ln 3x\) (dropped \(f'=3\)). Harry: “differentiate the argument, and that goes on the top.”
Constant multiple / neatness [T] A constant just rides along; by convention write it at the front. \(\tfrac{d}{dx}(c\,g(x))=c\,g'(x)\). Harry’s convention: constants at the front. \(c\) constant. Not a term you differentiate; it does not gain a power or a log. \(\tfrac{d}{dx}(5\ln x)=\tfrac5x\); \(\tfrac{d}{dx}(5\cdot4^x)=5\cdot4^x\ln4\). Common trap: Harry (05:47): “we tend to put these constants at the front … not wrong the other way, but it just looks a little odd.”
Reciprocal trig functions [T][S] The “co-” partners: cosec, sec, cot are 1 over sin, cos, tan. \(\cosec A=\tfrac1{\sin A}\), \(\sec A=\tfrac1{\cos A}\), \(\cot A=\tfrac1{\tan A}=\tfrac{\cos A}{\sin A}\). Undefined where the denominator is \(0\) [S] (\(\sin A=0\), \(\cos A=0\), resp.). \(\sec\) is not \(1/\sin\). Use the third letter: cosec\(\to\sin\), sec\(\to\cos\). Recall drill: Phuc gave all four correctly. Common trap: pairing \(\sec\) with \(\sin\). Harry’s hook: “cosec is one over sine … the third letter, remember?”
Standard trig derivatives [T][S] The six memorised derivatives, with an alternating sign pattern. \(\sin\!\to\!\cos\), \(\cos\!\to\!-\sin\), \(\tan\!\to\!\sec^2\), \(\cosec\!\to\!-\cosec\cot\), \(\sec\!\to\!\sec\tan\), \(\cot\!\to\!-\cosec^2\). Assume radians [S]. Order \(\sin,\cos,\tan,\cosec,\sec,\cot\) gives signs \(+\,-\,+\,-\,+\,-\). Not reprinted in the exam formula booklet — you must know them. Board table; \(\tan\!\to\!\sec^2\) links to \(1+\tan^2=\sec^2\). Phuc trap: wrote \(\cos x\) with no sign, and “\(\pm\)” for \(\tfrac{d}{dx}\cos x\): “I usually confuse about the plus minus.” Fix: the \(+-+-+-\) pattern.
Pythagorean identities [T][S] The “1 = sin² + cos²” family and its two reciprocal cousins. \(\sin^2A+\cos^2A=1\); \(1+\tan^2A=\sec^2A\); \(1+\cot^2A=\cosec^2A\). The two cousins come from dividing the master identity by \(\cos^2A\) / \(\sin^2A\) [S]. \(1+\tan^2A\) is not \(\cot^2A\). Recall drill C5–C7; feeds \(4\cos^2x\) in Q4(a). Phuc trap: answered \(\cot^2A\) for \(1+\tan^2A\). Harry: it is \(\sec^2A\) (same pair as \(\tfrac{d}{dx}\tan x=\sec^2x\)).
Double-angle identities [T][S] Formulas for \(\sin\)/\(\cos\) of \(2A\) in terms of \(A\); memorise the three \(\cos2A\) forms. \(\sin2A=2\sin A\cos A\); \(\cos2A=\cos^2A-\sin^2A=1-2\sin^2A=2\cos^2A-1\). Order in \(\cos2A\): cosine first. Derivations are [S] (for understanding). \(\cos2A\) is not \(\sin^2A-\cos^2A\) and not \(\cos^2A-2\sin^2A\). Q4(a): \(2\cos^2A-1\) rearranges to \(2\cos^2A=\cos2A+1\). Phuc trap: reached for \(\sin(A+B)\), then wrote wrong order/forms. Harry: “memorise these three forms rather than deriving them during the exam” → flashcards.
\(R\)-form (harmonic / auxiliary angle) [T][S] Rewrite “\(a\sin+b\cos\)” as one shifted sine wave of amplitude \(R\). \(a\sin\theta+b\cos\theta=R\sin(\theta+\alpha)\); match \(R\cos\alpha=a\), \(R\sin\alpha=b\). \(R>0\); \(R=\sqrt{a^2+b^2}\) exact; \(\alpha\) fixed by the signs of \(a,b\) (quadrant), not by \(\tan\alpha\) alone [S]; radians here. \(R\) is not a decimal when “exact” is asked. \(\tan\alpha=\tfrac{b}{a}\) alone does not fix \(\alpha\). Q4(b): \(4\sin2x+2\cos2x=2\sqrt5\sin(2x+\alpha)\), \(\alpha=\arctan\tfrac12\approx0.464\). Phuc trap: wrote \(R=4.472\). Harry: “we want the exact value of \(R\) … no decimals allowed” \(\Rightarrow R=2\sqrt5\).
Exact value [T][S] Leave surds / fractions / \(\pi\) — no rounded decimals. A value with no rounding: \(\sqrt{20}=2\sqrt5\), \(\arctan\tfrac12\), \(2\sqrt5-1\) kept symbolic. Applies whenever the command word says “exact”. Not a decimal (\(R=4.472\) is not exact). \(R=2\sqrt5\); \(f_{\max}=2\sqrt5-1\). Phuc trap: decimal \(R=4.472\). Harry: “Exact, no decimals allowed.”
Significant figures ≠ decimal places [T] “3 s.f.” counts from the first non-zero digit; “3 d.p.” counts after the point. \(3.694\) to 3 s.f. is \(3.69\) (not \(3.694\), which is 4 s.f. / 3 d.p.). Round the final answer to the demanded accuracy. s.f. is not d.p. Q4(c)(ii): \(3.694\to3.69\) (3 s.f.). Phuc trap: quoted \(3.694\). Harry: 3 s.f. \(\neq\) 3 d.p.
Whole-angle periodicity [T][S] When you solve \(\sin(\text{angle})=1\), add the \(2\pi\) to the angle, before dividing to get \(x\). \(\sin\theta=1\Rightarrow\theta=\tfrac\pi2+2n\pi\) [S]; the \(2\pi\) belongs to \(\theta=2x+\alpha\), not to \(x\). Solve for the whole angle first, then rearrange for \(x\). The \(x\)-values are not \(2\pi\) apart after you divide by 2. Q4(c)(ii): \(2x+\alpha=\tfrac\pi2,\tfrac{5\pi}2\Rightarrow x\approx0.553,\,3.69\). Phuc trap (headline): added \(2\pi\) to \(x\). Harry: “these ones, when you’ve rearranged, are not two pi apart.”
Modulus function [T][S] A “V”-shaped graph; the vertex is the corner. \(f(x)=a|x-h|+k\) has vertex \((h,k)\); split into two linear branches to solve algebraically. Piecewise: \(2|x-5|+10=2x\) for \(x\ge5\), \(=-2x+20\) for \(x<5\). Not smooth at the vertex; “calculator solutions not acceptable” on Q1(b). Q1: vertex \(P=(5,10)\); solve \(2|x-5|+10>6x\Rightarrow x<\tfrac52\). Common trap: solving the wrong branch — sketch first to pick the branch, then do algebra.
Function transformation [T][S] \(af(x-h)\): inside shifts \(x\), outside scales \(y\). \(y=3f(x-2)\): \(x\to x+2\) (right 2), \(y\to3y\) (stretch \(\times3\)) applied to each point. Inside operations act on \(x\) (opposite sense); outside on \(y\). The stretch factor multiplies \(y\), not \(x\). Q1(c): \(P=(5,10)\to P'=(7,30)\). Common trap: mixing which coordinate moves — inside \(\Rightarrow x\), outside \(\Rightarrow y\).
Algebraic (polynomial) division [T][S] Divide top by bottom to split a fraction into a polynomial plus a remainder term. \(\dfrac{2x^2-5x+8}{x-2}=2x-1+\dfrac{6}{x-2}\) (quotient \(2x-1\), remainder \(6\)). Remainder sits over the divisor: \(\tfrac{6}{x-2}\), not \((x-2)(2x-1)+6\) over \((x-2)\). Not the same as factorising; there is a leftover \(\tfrac{C}{x-2}\). Q2(a): \(A=2,B=-1,C=6\); also by substitution/identity. Phuc trap: \(-5x-(-4x)=-9x\). Harry: “always bracket the term you subtract” \(\Rightarrow-x\).
\(\int \tfrac1x\) / \(\int\tfrac{f'}{f}\) (log integral) [T][S] Integrating “derivative over function” gives a natural log. \(\int\tfrac1x\,dx=\ln|x|+c\); \(\int\tfrac{f'(x)}{f(x)}\,dx=\ln|f(x)|+c\) (reverse of the \(\ln f(x)\) derivative). Keep the modulus; drop it only where the argument is known positive (\(f(x)>0\)) [S]. Not a power-rule integral; there is no \(\tfrac{x^0}{0}\). Q2(b): \(\int\tfrac{6}{x-2}=6\ln|x-2|\); on \([4,8]\), \(x-2>0\). Common trap: forgetting the modulus, or the log law \(\ln6-\ln2=\ln\tfrac62=\ln3\).
Command word “sketch” vs “plot” [T] A sketch needs shape + key points; a plot needs an accurate scale. “Sketch”: show shape and label intercepts as coordinate pairs. “Plot”: accurate scaled graph. Label intercepts as pairs, e.g. \((0,6)\), \((2,0)\). A sketch is not graph paper to scale. Q3 (discussed only): Phuc’s \(\log_{10}y\)-vs-\(\log_{10}x\) sketch “pretty much fine”. Common trap: giving un-labelled intercepts; use coordinate pairs.
“Invent-and-carry” exam tactic [T] If you’re stuck on an early part, make up a plausible value and carry it forward. Assume a value for a missing early result so later parts still earn method marks (carry-forward). Later parts marked on method, not on the (wrong) carried value. Not a licence to guess the final answer; it rescues method marks. Harry stressed it 3× (Q1). Common trap: leaving later parts blank because an early part failed.
Forgetting curve / spaced practice [E] We forget over time; spacing and revisiting beats cramming. Space practice out; revisit learned material regularly to combat the forgetting curve. Email guidance, not video teaching. Not a one-and-done: re-doing seen questions is valid consolidation, though less diagnostic than an unseen paper. Email attachments show a forgetting-curve diagram. Common trap (per Harry): assuming re-done questions are worthless — “there is a place for both.”

\(u(x)\,v(x)\) \(u'v+uv'\) Product rule [S] — not drilled this lesson; P3 spec 4.2. Two functions multiplied. Both terms; don’t differentiate factor-by-factor separately. [S]
\(\dfrac{u(x)}{v(x)}\) \(\dfrac{u'v-uv'}{v^{2}}\) Quotient rule [S] — not drilled this lesson; spec 4.2. Minus sign, order matters. Top-over-bottom function. Keep the minus in the numerator; \(v^2\) underneath. [S]

\(\cos2A=2\cos^{2}A-1\) Sub \(\sin^2A=1-\cos^2A\) [S]. Rearranges to \(2\cos^2A=\cos2A+1\). Used in Q4(a) for \(4\cos^2x\). — [T]

[T] Harry’s rule: “memorising these three forms rather than deriving them during the exam” — deriving costs time; “the kind of thing that is really helpful for flashcards.” The three-step derivation (start \(\cos^2A-\sin^2A\) \(\to\) sub \(\cos^2A=1-\sin^2A\) \(\to\) \(1-2\sin^2A\); sub \(\sin^2A=1-\cos^2A\) \(\to\) \(2\cos^2A-1\)) is kept [S] for understanding only.

5.2 Q4(b) — to \(R\sin(2x+\alpha)+c\), \(R>0\), \(0<\alpha<\tfrac\pi2\) · Solved

Method / line Conditions & meaning Trap Src
Expand \(R\sin(2x+\alpha)=R\cos\alpha\sin2x+R\sin\alpha\cos2x\) Compound-angle identity [S]. Match to \(4\sin2x+2\cos2x\). [T][S]
Match: \(R\cos\alpha=4\), \(R\sin\alpha=2\) Equate \(\sin2x\), \(\cos2x\) coefficients. Line up which is \(a\), which is \(b\). [T]
\(R\) (square-and-add): \(R^2=4^2+2^2=20\Rightarrow R=2\sqrt5\) \(R^2(\cos^2\alpha+\sin^2\alpha)=a^2+b^2\); exact surd. Phuc trap: \(R=4.472\) (decimal). Harry: “Exact, no decimals allowed.” [T][S]

Part Working Trap Src

6.2 Q2 — algebraic division, then integration · \(g(x)=\dfrac{2x^2-5x+8}{x-2}\)

Part / method Working Trap Src
(a) M1 long division · Solved \(2x^2-5x+8=(x-2)(2x-1)+6\Rightarrow g=2x-1+\tfrac{6}{x-2}\); \(A=2,B=-1,C=6\). Phuc trap: \(-5x-(-4x)=-9x\). Harry: “always bracket the term you subtract” \(\Rightarrow-x\). Remainder \(6\) sits over \((x-2)\) alone. [T]
