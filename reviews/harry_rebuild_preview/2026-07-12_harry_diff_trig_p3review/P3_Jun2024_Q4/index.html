Pearson Edexcel IAL P3 · WMA13/01 · June 2024 · Q4

P3 June 2024 Q4 — double angle and R-form

Question

$$f(x)=8\sin x\cos x+4\cos^2x-3$$

(a) Write $f(x)$ in the form $$a\sin2x+b\cos2x+c$$ where $a$, $b$ and $c$ are integers to be found.   (3)

(b) Use the answer to part (a) to write $f(x)$ in the form $$R\sin(2x+\alpha)+c$$ where $R>0$ and $0<\alpha<\dfrac{\pi}{2}$. Give the exact value of $R$ and give the value of $\alpha$ in radians to 3 significant figures.   (3)

(c) Hence, or otherwise,

  (i) state the maximum value of $f(x)$

  (ii) find the second smallest positive value of $x$ at which a maximum value of $f(x)$ occurs. Give your answer to 3 significant figures.   (3)

[9 marks: (a) 3, (b) 3, (c) 3]

Part (a)[3 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1Use the matching double-angle identities3 marks

Use $2\sin x\cos x=\sin2x$ and $2\cos^2x=1+\cos2x$:

$$8\sin x\cos x=4\sin2x,$$

$$4\cos^2x=2(1+\cos2x)=2+2\cos2x.$$

Therefore

$f(x)=4\sin2x+2\cos2x-1$, so $a=4$, $b=2$, $c=-1$.

Part (b)[3 marks] · 1 step

Carry-in from (a): $f(x)=4\sin2x+2\cos2x-1$.
STEP 1/1Match coefficients in R-form3 marks

Expand $R\sin(2x+\alpha)$:

$$R\sin2x\cos\alpha+R\cos2x\sin\alpha.$$

Hence $R\cos\alpha=4$ and $R\sin\alpha=2$. Therefore

$$R=\sqrt{4^2+2^2}=2\sqrt5,$$

$$\tan\alpha=\frac24=\frac12\Rightarrow\alpha=0.464\text{ rad }(3\text{ s.f.}).$$

$f(x)=2\sqrt5\sin(2x+0.464)-1$ (with $\alpha=\tan^{-1}(1/2)$ before rounding).

Part (c)[3 marks] · 2 steps

Carry-in from (b): $f(x)=2\sqrt5\sin(2x+\alpha)-1$, where $\alpha=\tan^{-1}(1/2)$.
STEP 1/2State the maximum1 mark

Since $-1\le\sin(2x+\alpha)\le1$, the maximum occurs when the sine is $1$.

Maximum $f(x)=2\sqrt5-1$.
STEP 2/2Solve for the second positive maximum2 marks

Use the exact $\alpha=\tan^{-1}(1/2)$:

$$2x+\alpha=\frac\pi2+2k\pi.$$

$$x=\frac\pi4-\frac\alpha2+k\pi.$$

For $k=0$, the smallest positive value is approximately $0.554$. For $k=1$:

$$x=\frac\pi4-\frac12\tan^{-1}(1/2)+\pi=3.69517\ldots$$

The second smallest positive value is $x=3.70$ radians (3 s.f.).
Check. Consecutive maxima are $\pi$ apart because the sine argument is $2x$.
