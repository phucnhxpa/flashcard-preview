Warm-up — derivatives and trigonometric identities

Question

A. Differentiate: $a^x$, $a^{kx}$, $3^x$, $x^3$, $\ln(3x)$, $\ln(x^2+3x+5)$, $3^{2x}$, $2^{3x}$, $5\ln x$, $e^x$, $5\ln(2x)$, $9^t$ with respect to $t$, $5\cdot4^x$, $x^4$, $\ln(6x)$, $6\ln x$, $3e^{2x}$ and $e^{-x}$.

B. Recall the derivatives of: $\sin x$, $\cos x$, $\tan x$, $\operatorname{cosec}x$, $\sec x$ and $\cot x$, including every sign.

C. Recall: $\tan A$, $\operatorname{cosec}A$, $\sec A$, $\cot A$ in reciprocal/quotient form; the three Pythagorean identities; $\sin2A$; and all three equivalent forms of $\cos2A$.

Part A · Differentiation[no marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1Apply the power, exponential and logarithmic rules

Rules first. $\frac{d}{dx}x^n=nx^{n-1}$, $\frac{d}{dx}a^{u}=a^u\ln(a)u'$, $\frac{d}{dx}e^u=e^uu'$, and $\frac{d}{dx}\ln u=u'/u$.

Therefore:

$a^x$$a^x\ln a$$a^{kx}$$ka^{kx}\ln a$
$3^x$$3^x\ln3$$x^3$$3x^2$
$\ln(3x)$$1/x$$\ln(x^2+3x+5)$$(2x+3)/(x^2+3x+5)$
$3^{2x}$$2(3^{2x})\ln3$$2^{3x}$$3(2^{3x})\ln2$
$5\ln x$$5/x$$e^x$$e^x$
$5\ln(2x)$$5/x$$9^t$$9^t\ln9$
$5\cdot4^x$$5(4^x)\ln4$$x^4$$4x^3$
$\ln(6x)$$1/x$$6\ln x$$6/x$
$3e^{2x}$$6e^{2x}$$e^{-x}$$-e^{-x}$
Check. Constant factors inside $\ln(kx)$ cancel: $(kx)'/(kx)=1/x$.

Part B · Trigonometric derivatives[no marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1Recall the signs with reciprocal identities

$$ (\sin x)'=\cos x,\quad (\cos x)'=-\sin x,\quad (\tan x)'=\sec^2x. $$

$$ (\operatorname{cosec}x)'=-\operatorname{cosec}x\cot x,\quad (\sec x)'=\sec x\tan x,\quad (\cot x)'=-\operatorname{cosec}^2x. $$

Sign check. The derivatives of $\cos x$, $\operatorname{cosec}x$ and $\cot x$ are negative; the derivative of $\sin x$ is exactly $\cos x$.

Part C · Identities[no marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1Rebuild identities from definitions

$\tan A=\sin A/\cos A$, $\operatorname{cosec}A=1/\sin A$, $\sec A=1/\cos A$, $\cot A=\cos A/\sin A$.

$$\sin^2A+\cos^2A=1,\quad 1+\tan^2A=\sec^2A,\quad 1+\cot^2A=\operatorname{cosec}^2A.$$

$$\sin2A=2\sin A\cos A,$$

$$\cos2A=\cos^2A-\sin^2A=2\cos^2A-1=1-2\sin^2A.$$

The last two forms follow by replacing either $\sin^2A$ or $\cos^2A$ using $\sin^2A+\cos^2A=1$.
