WMA13/01 October 2025 Q1 — functions

Question

In this question you must show all stages of your working. Solutions relying entirely on calculator technology are not acceptable.

The functions $f$ and $g$ are defined by $$f(x)=\ln(x^2+3),\qquad x\in\mathbb R,$$ $$g(x)=\frac{3+5x}{x+2},\qquad x\in\mathbb R,\ x>-2.$$

(a) State the range of $f$. (1)

(b) Find $g^{-1}$. (3)

(c) Find $fg(0)$. (2)

(d) Find the exact value of $a$ for which $$g(e^{2a})=f\!\left(\sqrt{e^4-3}\right).$$ (3)

Not covered in the lesson — official-reference reconstruction below.

[9 marks: (a) 1, (b) 3, (c) 2, (d) 3]

Part (a) — official-reference reconstruction[1 mark] · 1 step

STEP 1/1

Strategy. Find the least possible value of the expression inside the logarithm, then use the fact that $\ln x$ is strictly increasing.

For every real $x$, $x^2\ge0$, so

$$x^2+3\ge3.$$

Equality occurs at $x=0$, so the lower endpoint is attained. Also $x^2+3$ is unbounded above as $|x|\to\infty$. Since the natural logarithm is increasing on positive inputs,

$$\ln(x^2+3)\ge\ln3,$$

with no upper bound. Hence

Range of $f$: $\boxed{[\ln3,\infty)}$.
Check. $f(0)=\ln3$ confirms inclusion of the left endpoint; $f(x)\to\infty$ as $|x|\to\infty$.

Part (b) — official-reference reconstruction[3 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2

Strategy. Set $y=g(x)$, solve for $x$, then transfer the original range of $g$ to the domain of $g^{-1}$.

$$y=\frac{3+5x}{x+2}$$ $$y(x+2)=3+5x$$ $$yx+2y=3+5x$$ $$x(y-5)=3-2y$$ $$x=\frac{3-2y}{y-5}=\frac{2y-3}{5-y}.$$

Rename $y$ as the inverse input:

$\boxed{g^{-1}(x)=\dfrac{2x-3}{5-x}}$.
STEP 2/2

Domain and range. Rewrite the original function as

$$g(x)=5-\frac7{x+2}.$$

Because the source domain is $x>-2$, the denominator $x+2$ is positive, so $7/(x+2)>0$ and therefore $g(x)<5$. As $x\to-2^+$, $g(x)\to-\infty$; as $x\to\infty$, $g(x)\to5$ from below. Thus the range of $g$ is $(-\infty,5)$, which becomes the inverse domain. The inverse range is the original domain $(-2,\infty)$.

Domain: $\boxed{x<5}$; range: $\boxed{g^{-1}(x)>-2}$.
Composition check. Substituting $u=(2x-3)/(5-x)$ into $g(u)$ simplifies to $x$, for $x<5$.

Part (c) — official-reference reconstruction[2 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1

Strategy. In Edexcel composition notation, $fg(0)=f(g(0))$: evaluate the inner function first.

$$g(0)=\frac{3+5(0)}{0+2}=\frac32.$$

Now apply $f$:

$$fg(0)=f\!\left(\frac32\right)=\ln\!\left(\left(\frac32\right)^2+3\right)$$ $$=\ln\!\left(\frac94+\frac{12}4\right)=\ln\!\left(\frac{21}4\right).$$
$\boxed{fg(0)=\ln(21/4)}$.
Wrong-route diagnosis. $fg$ denotes composition here, not multiplication, and reversing the order would calculate a different expression.
Check. $g(0)=3/2$ is real and lies in the domain of $f$, which is all real numbers.

Part (d) — official-reference reconstruction[3 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1

Strategy. Simplify the right-hand side exactly, let the positive exponential quantity remain symbolic, and solve the resulting rational equation before taking logarithms.

First,

$$f\!\left(\sqrt{e^4-3}\right)=\ln\!\left((\sqrt{e^4-3})^2+3\right)$$ $$=\ln(e^4-3+3)=\ln(e^4)=4.$$

Therefore $g(e^{2a})=4$. Substitute into $g$:

$$\frac{3+5e^{2a}}{e^{2a}+2}=4.$$ $$3+5e^{2a}=4e^{2a}+8$$ $$e^{2a}=5.$$

Taking natural logarithms gives $2a=\ln5$, so

$\boxed{a=\dfrac12\ln5}$.
Check. With $e^{2a}=5$, $g(5)=(3+25)/(5+2)=4$, exactly matching the simplified right-hand side.
