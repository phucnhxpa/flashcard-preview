Part (b)[4 marks] · 3 steps

Carry-in from the printed question/part (a): $y=\sqrt{4x-7}$, $P(8,5)$, and $l:5x+2y-50=0$.
STEP 1/3setup

The curve meets the $x$-axis when $\sqrt{4x-7}=0$, so $4x-7=0$ and $x=7/4$. The curve reaches $P$ at $x=8$.

The normal is $5x+2y-50=0$, or $y=25-\tfrac52x$. It meets the $x$-axis at $x=10$.

Thus $R$ is the area under the curve from $7/4$ to $8$, plus the triangular area under the normal from $8$ to $10$.

The lesson discussed only the reverse-chain-rule integral. The complete official-reference reconstruction must add the second piece; omitting it is the key common trap.
STEP 2/3M1 A1

Use the formula $\int u^{1/2}\,du=\tfrac23u^{3/2}$. With $u=4x-7$, $du=4\,dx$, so

$$\int(4x-7)^{1/2}dx=\frac14\cdot\frac23(4x-7)^{3/2}=\frac{(4x-7)^{3/2}}6.$$

Apply the curve bounds:

$$\left[\frac{(4x-7)^{3/2}}6\right]_{7/4}^{8}=\frac{25^{3/2}}6-0=\frac{125}{6}.$$
Differentiate the antiderivative: $\tfrac16\cdot\tfrac32(4x-7)^{1/2}\cdot4=(4x-7)^{1/2}$.
STEP 3/3dM1 A1

The region under the normal from $x=8$ to $x=10$ is a triangle with base $10-8=2$ and height $5$:

$$A_{\triangle}=\frac12(2)(5)=5.$$

Therefore

$$A_R=\frac{125}{6}+5=\frac{125}{6}+\frac{30}{6}=\frac{155}{6}.$$
Exact area of $R$: $\dfrac{155}{6}$ square units
The answer is larger than the curve-only area $125/6$ by exactly the visible positive triangle area $5$.
