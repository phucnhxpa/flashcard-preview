ESAT Maths 1 Q5 — area in a unit square

Question

$WXYZ$ is a square of side length 1.

$WM:MX=1:2$
$XN:NY=3:1$
$YP:PZ=4:1$

What is the area of triangle $MNP$?

A$\frac13$B$\frac25$
C$\frac9{20}$D$\frac1{30}$
E$\frac{19}{60}$F$\frac{23}{60}$

Question 5Multiple choice · 3 steps

STEP 1/3

Each ratio gives parts of a complete side of length $1$:

$$WM=\frac13,\quad MX=\frac23;\qquad XN=\frac34,\quad NY=\frac14;$$ $$YP=\frac45,\quad PZ=\frac15.$$
For $1:2$, the whole contains $1+2=3$ equal parts, so $WM=1/3$, not $1/2$. The same “part over total parts” rule applies to each side.
STEP 2/3

The square has area $1$. Outside $\triangle MNP$ are triangle $XMN$, triangle $NYP$, and trapezium $WMPZ$.

$$A_{XMN}=\frac12\left(\frac23\right)\left(\frac34\right)=\frac14,$$$$A_{NYP}=\frac12\left(\frac14\right)\left(\frac45\right)=\frac1{10}.$$

The left and right parallel sides of trapezium $WMPZ$ are $WM=1/3$ and $PZ=1/5$, separated by distance $1$:

$$A_{WMPZ}=\frac12\left(\frac13+\frac15\right)(1)=\frac4{15}.$$
STEP 3/3

Therefore

$$A_{MNP}=1-\left(\frac14+\frac1{10}+\frac4{15}\right).$$

With denominator $60$:

$$A_{MNP}=\frac{60-15-6-16}{60}=\frac{23}{60}.$$
Answer: F, $\dfrac{23}{60}$
Coordinate shoelace using $M=(0,1/3)$, $N=(3/4,1)$, $P=(1,1/5)$ also gives $23/60$, independently checking the subtraction.
