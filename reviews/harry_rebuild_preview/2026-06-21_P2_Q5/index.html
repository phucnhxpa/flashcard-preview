WMA12 October 2025 Q5 — trigonometric equations

Question

In this question you must show all stages of your working.
Solutions relying entirely on calculator technology are not acceptable.

(i) Solve, for \(0<\theta\leq360^\circ\), the equation

\[4\tan\theta+5\sin\theta=0\]

giving any non-exact answers to one decimal place. (5)

(ii) Solve, for \(0<x<\pi\), the equation

\[\frac{\sin x}{\cos x}+\frac{\cos x}{\sin x}=\frac{5}{\cos x}\]

giving the answers, in radians, to 3 significant figures. (4)

[9 marks: (i) 5, (ii) 4]

Part (i)[5 marks] · 3 steps

1Rewrite and factorise without cancelling
M1, dM1

Strategy. Express tangent using sine and cosine, then factor. This is essential because the product produces two branches; cancelling \(\sin\theta\) would silently discard the branch \(\sin\theta=0\).

\[4\tan\theta+5\sin\theta=0\]

\[4\frac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta}+5\sin\theta=0\]

The original equation already requires \(\cos\theta\ne0\). Multiplying by \(\cos\theta\) is therefore valid at every admissible solution:

\[4\sin\theta+5\sin\theta\cos\theta=0\]

\[\sin\theta\bigl(4+5\cos\theta\bigr)=0\]

Common trap. Do not divide by \(\sin\theta\). Division would assume \(\sin\theta\ne0\) and lose the endpoint solution \(360^\circ\) as well as \(180^\circ\).
Branches: \(\sin\theta=0\) or \(\cos\theta=-\frac45\).
2Solve both branches and apply the degree domain
A1, A1, B1

For \(\sin\theta=0\), the integer multiples of \(180^\circ\) in \(0<\theta\leq360^\circ\) are \(180^\circ\) and \(360^\circ\). The lower endpoint \(0^\circ\) is excluded; the upper endpoint \(360^\circ\) is included.

For \(\cos\theta=-\frac45\), cosine is negative in quadrants II and III. Let the acute reference angle be

\[\alpha=\cos^{-1}\!\left(\frac45\right)=36.869897\ldots^\circ.\]

Hence

\[\theta=180^\circ-\alpha=143.130102\ldots^\circ,\qquad \theta=180^\circ+\alpha=216.869897\ldots^\circ.\]

Only the non-exact values are rounded to one decimal place, as instructed.

Quadrant check. Both angles lie where cosine is negative; neither lies at \(90^\circ\) or \(270^\circ\), where the original tangent would be undefined.
\(\theta=143.1^\circ,\ 180^\circ,\ 216.9^\circ,\ 360^\circ\).
3Substitute to check every final value
Check

For \(180^\circ\) and \(360^\circ\), both \(\sin\theta\) and \(\tan\theta\) are zero, so \(4\tan\theta+5\sin\theta=0\) exactly.

For either unrounded cosine-branch angle, \(\cos\theta=-\frac45\), while \(\sin\theta=\frac35\) in quadrant II and \(\sin\theta=-\frac35\) in quadrant III. Thus

\[\tan\theta=\frac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta}=-\frac54\sin\theta\]

and direct substitution gives

\[4\tan\theta+5\sin\theta=4\left(-\frac54\sin\theta\right)+5\sin\theta=0.\]

All four values satisfy the equation and the domain. The check uses the unrounded angles, so small calculator residuals caused only by entering \(143.1^\circ\) or \(216.9^\circ\) do not create a false concern.

Transfer. Whenever a trigonometric equation factorises, solve every factor before filtering by the stated interval; never cancel a factor that might be zero.

Part (ii)[4 marks] · 3 steps

1Clear denominators on the admissible domain
M1, A1

Strategy. Multiply by the common denominator \(\sin x\cos x\), then use \(\sin^2x+\cos^2x=1\). The original fractions require both \(\sin x\ne0\) and \(\cos x\ne0\), so this multiplication is reversible for every admissible solution.

\[\left(\frac{\sin x}{\cos x}+\frac{\cos x}{\sin x}\right)\sin x\cos x=\frac5{\cos x}\sin x\cos x\]

\[\sin^2x+\cos^2x=5\sin x\]

\[1=5\sin x\]

\[\sin x=\frac15.\]

Common trap. Multiplying only selected terms, or replacing \(\sin x+\cos x\) by \(1\), is invalid. It is the sum of the squares that equals \(1\).
Reduced equation: \(5\sin x=1\).
2Select both radian solutions and treat excluded points
dM1, A1

On \(0<x<\pi\), sine is positive in quadrants I and II. The first solution is

\[x_1=\sin^{-1}\!\left(\frac15\right)=0.2013579208\ldots.\]

The second-quadrant solution is

\[x_2=\pi-x_1=2.940234733\ldots.\]

To 3 significant figures, in radians, these are \(0.201\) and \(2.94\). Neither endpoint \(0\) nor \(\pi\) is included; both would also make the original denominator \(\sin x\) zero. The interior point \(\frac\pi2\) makes \(\cos x=0\), so the original equation is undefined there, but it is not produced by \(\sin x=\frac15\).

Domain check. \(0<0.201357\ldots<\pi\) and \(0<2.940234\ldots<\pi\); both have non-zero sine and cosine.
\(x=0.201,\ 2.94\text{ radians}\) to 3 significant figures.
3Substitute the final values back into the original form
Check

For either unrounded solution, \(\sin x=\frac15\) and \(\cos x\ne0\). Combining the left-hand side over its common denominator gives

\[\frac{\sin x}{\cos x}+\frac{\cos x}{\sin x}=\frac{\sin^2x+\cos^2x}{\sin x\cos x}=\frac1{(1/5)\cos x}=\frac5{\cos x},\]

which is exactly the right-hand side. This checks both the quadrant-I value, where cosine is positive, and the quadrant-II value, where cosine is negative.

Numerically, the unrounded values are \(x=0.2013579208\ldots\) and \(x=2.940234733\ldots\); substitution produces equality before rounding. The displayed 3-significant-figure values are presentation values, not values to feed back into the equation for an exact check.

Transfer. When clearing trigonometric denominators, record where every denominator is non-zero first, solve the simplified equation, and then test the survivors in the original equation.
