P2 January 2025 Q2 — binomial expansion

Question

(a) Find the first 4 terms, in ascending powers of $x$, of the binomial expansion of

$$(2-5x)^8$$

giving each term in simplest form. (4)

This expansion is to be used to find an approximation for $2.05^8$.

(b) State the value of $x$ that should be used.

(There is no need to carry out this calculation.) (1)

Status: corrected in review. The lesson evidence records an exponent-copy slip in part (a) and an over-reading of the command in part (b).

[5 marks: (a) 4, (b) 1]

Part (a)[4 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1

Strategy. Treat the expression as $(a+b)^n$ with $a=2$, $b=-5x$ and the printed exponent $n=8$. Generate terms for $r=0,1,2,3$ only, because four ascending powers means stopping after $x^3$. Simplify every numerical coefficient and preserve the alternating signs.

Why this method applies. The binomial theorem is designed for a positive integer exponent and produces powers $x^r$ in ascending order when $r$ increases. Each term combines a binomial coefficient, a descending power of 2 and a rising power of $-5x$. Copying $8$ accurately is structural: it determines all coefficients and the degree.

Formula or definition first. Use $T_{r+1}=\binom8r2^{8-r}(-5x)^r$ for $r=0,1,2,3$. The signs alternate because $(-5x)^r$ is negative for odd $r$ and positive for even $r$.

Full intermediate derivation. For $r=0$: $\binom802^8=256$. For $r=1$: $\binom812^7(-5x)=8\cdot128\cdot(-5x)=-5120x$. For $r=2$: $\binom822^6(25x^2)=28\cdot64\cdot25x^2=44800x^2$. For $r=3$: $\binom832^5(-125x^3)=56\cdot32\cdot(-125)x^3=-224000x^3$. Thus $\boxed{256-5120x+44800x^2-224000x^3+\cdots}$.

Difficult transition. The most fragile transition is coefficient arithmetic. Keep the three factors visible before multiplying, especially $\binom83=56$, $2^5=32$ and $(-5)^3=-125$. This preserves sign and exposes which factor was copied wrongly if a coefficient disagrees.

Evidence-grounded diagnosis / why the rejected route fails. The recording directly supports that the exponent 8 was copied as 2 on the first line. That route fails globally: it changes the coefficient row, gives only three terms, and cannot approximate an eighth power. The correction is to re-read the original expression before any expansion.

Check back / sense check. The constant term must be $2^8=256$. Signs should alternate $+,-,+,-$, and the powers should be $x^0,x^1,x^2,x^3$. Differentiating the first two terms at $x=0$ also gives slope $-5120$, agreeing with $8(2)^7(-5)$.

Exam trigger / transfer trigger. When asked for the first $k$ binomial terms, write a term template with $r=0$ through $k-1$, then build a coefficient table before simplifying. Circle the exponent in the question and run constant-term, sign-pattern and power-order checks.

Part (b)[1 mark] · 1 step

Carry-in: Part (a) prepared the expansion of $(2-5x)^8$; the target base is $2.05$.
STEP 1/1

Strategy. Match the base of the prepared expansion to the base of the requested numerical power. Solve the simple linear equation $2-5x=2.05$ and stop, because the command is ‘State the value of $x$’ and explicitly says no approximation calculation is needed.

Why this method applies. The expansion represents the function $(2-5x)^8$. To use it for $2.05^8$, the bracket itself—not the exponent or a coefficient—must equal 2.05. This structural matching selects the input $x$ for which the two eighth powers are identical.

Formula or definition first. Set $2-5x=2.05$. Then $-5x=2.05-2=0.05$, so $x=0.05/(-5)$.

Full intermediate derivation. Divide: $x=-0.01$. Substitution verifies the representation: $2-5(-0.01)=2+0.05=2.05$. Therefore the required statement is $\boxed{x=-0.01}$. No numerical evaluation of the truncated polynomial belongs in the answer.

Difficult transition. The negative sign is not optional. Since the target base 2.05 is greater than 2, the term $-5x$ must be positive, forcing $x<0$. A positive $0.01$ would produce $2-0.05=1.95$, approximating the wrong power.

Evidence-grounded diagnosis / why the rejected route fails. The lesson evidence says the learner over-read the instruction and started treating it as a full approximation task. That route wastes time and risks arithmetic errors. The printed parenthetical sentence authorises an immediate stop after finding $x$.

Check back / sense check. Insert $-0.01$ into the base and recover exactly 2.05. Its small magnitude also makes successive powers $x^r$ rapidly smaller, which explains why the first four terms can form a useful local approximation if the calculation were requested.

Exam trigger / transfer trigger. For binomial approximation questions, first solve ‘prepared base = target base’. Then obey the command boundary: ‘state’ requires the substitution only; ‘hence estimate’ requires evaluation. Use the sign of the base change as a fast check on $x$.
