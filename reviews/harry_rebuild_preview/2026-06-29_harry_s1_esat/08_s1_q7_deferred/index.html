WST01 October 2025 Q7 — normal distribution

Question

The wingspans, $W$ mm, of Speckled Wood butterflies are normally distributed, with $W\sim N(51,1.7^2)$.

A Speckled Wood butterfly is selected at random.

(a) Use standardisation to find the probability that this butterfly has a wingspan between 48.45 mm and 54.40 mm (3)

Given that this butterfly has a wingspan between 48.45 mm and 54.40 mm,

(b) find the probability that the wingspan of this butterfly is greater than 53.55 mm
You must show your working. (3)

The wingspans, $X$ mm, of Large White butterflies are also normally distributed, with $X\sim N(\mu,\sigma^2)$.

Given that $P(X\lt64.58)=0.30$ and $P(X\gt69.46)=0.10$

(c) calculate the value of $\mu$ and the value of $\sigma$
You must show your working. (4)

Not covered in the lesson — official-reference reconstruction below.

[10 marks: (a)3, (b)3, (c)4]

Part (a)[3 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2Official-reference reconstruction

Standardise with mean 51 and standard deviation 1.7 (not variance $1.7^2$): $$z_L=\frac{48.45-51}{1.7}=-1.5,\qquad z_U=\frac{54.40-51}{1.7}=2.$$

STEP 2/2Official-reference reconstruction

Therefore $$P(48.45\lt W\lt54.40)=\Phi(2)-\Phi(-1.5)=0.9772-0.0668=0.9104.$$ Hence $\boxed{0.910\text{ (3 s.f.)}}$.

Check. The interval runs from 1.5 standard deviations below the mean to 2 above it, so a probability a little above 0.9 is plausible.

Part (b)[3 marks] · 2 steps

Carry-in: From part (a), $P(48.45\lt W\lt54.40)=0.910442\ldots$.
STEP 1/2Official-reference reconstruction

The condition restricts the sample space to $48.45\lt W\lt54.40$. Intersecting it with $W\gt53.55$ leaves $53.55\lt W\lt54.40$. Since $$(53.55-51)/1.7=1.5,$$ the numerator is $$P(1.5\lt Z\lt2)=0.9772-0.9332=0.0440.$$

STEP 2/2Official-reference reconstruction

Apply conditional probability: $$\frac{P(53.55\lt W\lt54.40)}{P(48.45\lt W\lt54.40)}=\frac{0.044057\ldots}{0.910442\ldots}=0.048390\ldots.$$ Thus $\boxed{0.0484}$.

Check. The numerator interval is contained in the denominator interval, so the ratio must lie between 0 and 1.

Part (c)[4 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2Official-reference reconstruction

Convert both statements to left-tail standard scores. From $P(X\lt64.58)=0.30$, $z_{0.30}=-0.524400\ldots$. From $P(X\gt69.46)=0.10$, $P(X\lt69.46)=0.90$, so $z_{0.90}=1.281552\ldots$.

Hence $$\frac{64.58-\mu}{\sigma}=-0.524400,\qquad \frac{69.46-\mu}{\sigma}=1.281552.$$

STEP 2/2Official-reference reconstruction

Write $64.58=\mu-0.524400\sigma$ and $69.46=\mu+1.281552\sigma$. Subtracting gives $$4.88=(1.805952)\sigma,$$ so $$\sigma=2.70218\ldots\approx\boxed{2.70\text{ mm}}.$$ Then $$\mu=64.58+0.524400(2.70218\ldots)=65.9970\ldots\approx\boxed{66.0\text{ mm}}.$$

Check. $64.58\lt\mu\lt69.46$ and $\sigma\gt0$, consistent with the 30th and 90th percentiles.
