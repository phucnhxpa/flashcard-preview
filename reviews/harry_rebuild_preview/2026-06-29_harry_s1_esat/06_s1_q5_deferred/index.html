WST01 October 2025 Q5 — grouped data and coding

Question

The weights, $x$ grams, of 300 medium-sized apples are summarised in the table below.

WeightFrequency
$105\leq x\lt115$28
$115\leq x\lt125$42
$125\leq x\lt130$75
$130\leq x\lt140$63
$140\leq x\lt150$52
$150\leq x\lt170$40

A histogram is drawn (not shown) to represent these data. The bar that represents the class $125\leq x\lt130$ is 1.8 cm wide and 12 cm high.

(a) Calculate the width and the height of the bar that represents the class $150\leq x\lt170$ (3)

(b) Use linear interpolation to estimate the median of $x$
Give your answer to one decimal place. (2)

The weights, $w$ grams, of 300 large apples are coded using $y=(w-200)/2$. These coded data are summarised by $\sum y=6000$ and $\sum y^2=150000$.

(c) Calculate

    (i) the mean of $w$ (2)
    (ii) the variance of $w$ (3)

Not covered in the lesson — official-reference reconstruction below.

[10 marks: (a)3, (b)2, (c)(i)2, (c)(ii)3]

Part (a)[3 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2Official-reference reconstruction

Strategy. Calibrate horizontal width from class width, then use histogram area (or frequency density) for height. The given class has data width $5$ represented by $1.8$ cm, so the horizontal scale is $1.8/5$ cm per gram.

The target class has width $170-150=20$, hence its drawn width is $$20\times\frac{1.8}{5}=\boxed{7.2\text{ cm}}.$$

Do not make height proportional to frequency alone: unequal class widths require frequency density.
STEP 2/2Official-reference reconstruction

The given bar has drawn area $1.8\times12=21.6\text{ cm}^2$ for frequency 75. Therefore the target frequency 40 has area $$21.6\times\frac{40}{75}=11.52\text{ cm}^2.$$ Its height is $$\frac{11.52}{7.2}=\boxed{1.6\text{ cm}}.$$

Check. Densities are $75/5=15$ and $40/20=2$; the height ratio $2/15$ gives $12(2/15)=1.6$.

Part (b)[2 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2Official-reference reconstruction

Strategy. Form cumulative frequencies and locate the median rank $300/2=150$.

Upper boundary115125130140150170
Cumulative frequency2870145208260300

The 150th value is in $130\leq x\lt140$, with lower boundary 130, previous cumulative frequency 145, class frequency 63 and width 10.

STEP 2/2Official-reference reconstruction

Linear interpolation gives $$\tilde x=130+\frac{150-145}{63}\times10=130.79365\ldots$$ so $$\boxed{\tilde x=130.8\text{ g}}\quad\text{(1 d.p.)}.$$

Check. Only $5/63$ of the class is traversed, so the estimate should be just above 130, as obtained. The official scheme also accepts 130.9 if the $(n+1)/2$ convention is used.

Part (c)(i)[2 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2Official-reference reconstruction

From $y=(w-200)/2$, rearrange to $w=2y+200$. First compute the coded mean: $$\bar y=\frac{\sum y}{300}=\frac{6000}{300}=20.$$

STEP 2/2Official-reference reconstruction

Transform the mean linearly: $$\bar w=2\bar y+200=2(20)+200=\boxed{240\text{ g}}.$$

Check. Substitution into the coding gives $(240-200)/2=20$, recovering the coded mean.

Part (c)(ii)[3 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2Official-reference reconstruction

Use the coded second moment before transforming: $$\operatorname{Var}(Y)=\frac{\sum y^2}{300}-\bar y^2=\frac{150000}{300}-20^2=500-400=100.$$

The shift 200 does not affect variance; the scale factor 2 is squared.
STEP 2/2Official-reference reconstruction

Since $W=2Y+200$, $$\operatorname{Var}(W)=2^2\operatorname{Var}(Y)=4(100)=\boxed{400\text{ g}^2}.$$

Check. Variance is non-negative and carries squared units. Using $2\operatorname{Var}(Y)+200$ would incorrectly mix variance and location.
