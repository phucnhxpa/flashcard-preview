Q2
Worked solution

Part (a): Simplify $\frac{3^{1.5}}{3^{0.5}}$ into the form $3^a$.

Thought Process:
We need to simplify this expression involving powers of the same base. The fundamental law of indices for division, $a^m / a^n = a^{m-n}$, is directly applicable here.

Working:
The given expression is $\frac{3^{1.5}}{3^{0.5}}$.
Apply the quotient rule for indices, which states that when dividing powers with the same base, you subtract the exponents:
$$ \frac{a^m}{a^n} = a^{m-n} $$
Substituting the values from our expression:
$$ \frac{3^{1.5}}{3^{0.5}} = 3^{1.5 - 0.5} $$
Perform the subtraction of the exponents:
$$ 1.5 - 0.5 = 1 $$
So, the expression simplifies to $3^1$.
Comparing this to the form $3^a$, we can identify the value of $a$.

Answer: $a = 1$

Part (b): Simplify $\frac{(2x^{3/2})^2}{2x^4}$.

Thought Process:
This problem requires simplifying an algebraic fraction involving powers. We should first simplify the numerator by applying the power rule $(ab)^n = a^n b^n$ and $(x^m)^n = x^{mn}$. Then, we'll simplify the entire fraction by dividing coefficients and subtracting powers of $x$ using the quotient rule.

Working:
The given expression is $\frac{(2x^{3/2})^2}{2x^4}$.
First, expand the numerator $(2x^{3/2})^2$. Remember that the exponent '2' applies to both the coefficient '2' and the variable term $x^{3/2}$:
$$ (2x^{3/2})^2 = 2^2 \times (x^{3/2})^2 $$
Calculate $2^2$:
$$ 2^2 = 4 $$
Apply the power of a power rule $(a^m)^n = a^{mn}$ to $(x^{3/2})^2$:
$$ (x^{3/2})^2 = x^{(3/2) \times 2} = x^3 $$
So, the numerator simplifies to $4x^3$.
Now substitute this back into the original fraction:
$$ \frac{4x^3}{2x^4} $$
Next, simplify the fraction. Divide the numerical coefficients and apply the quotient rule for the variable terms.
Divide coefficients:
$$ \frac{4}{2} = 2 $$
Apply the quotient rule $\frac{a^m}{a^n} = a^{m-n}$ to the $x$ terms:
$$ \frac{x^3}{x^4} = x^{3-4} = x^{-1} $$
Combine these results:
$$ 2 \times x^{-1} = 2x^{-1} $$
This can also be written as $\frac{2}{x}$.

Answer: $2x^{-1}$ (or $\frac{2}{x}$)

Key techniques

  • power of a power rule
  • quotient rule for indices
  • distribute power to product

Common pitfalls

  • Incorrectly applying fractional or negative indices (e.g., misinterpreting $\sqrt{x}$ as $x^{-2}$ or $x^2$).
  • Forgetting to apply the exponent to all terms inside a bracket, e.g., in $(2x^{3/2})^2$, forgetting to square the '2'.
  • Errors in arithmetic when combining exponents (e.g., $1.5 - 0.5$ or $3-4$).
