Worked solution

Let's break down this question step by step.

Part (a): Deduce values of $x$ for which $f(x) > 0$

Thought Process: We are given $f(x)$ in factored form: $f(x) = (3x - 2)^2 (x - 4)$. To find where $f(x) > 0$, we need to analyze the sign of each factor. The term $(3x - 2)^2$ is a square, so it will always be greater than or equal to zero. It's only zero when $3x - 2 = 0$, i.e., $x = 2/3$. For $f(x)$ to be strictly positive, neither factor can be zero and the product must be positive.

Working:
We have $f(x) = (3x - 2)^2 (x - 4)$.
Since $(3x - 2)^2 \ge 0$ for all real $x$, for $f(x) > 0$, we must have:


  1. $(3x - 2)^2 \ne 0 \implies x \ne 2/3$

  2. $(x - 4) > 0$

From condition 2, $x - 4 > 0 \implies x > 4$.
This condition automatically satisfies $x \ne 2/3$ (since $4 > 2/3$).

Answer: $x > 4$

Part (b): Expand $f(x)$ to the form $ax^3 + bx^2 + cx + d$

Thought Process: This is a standard algebraic expansion. We should first expand the squared term $(3x - 2)^2$ and then multiply the result by $(x - 4)$. Be careful with signs and collecting like terms.

Working:
First, expand $(3x - 2)^2$:
$$(3x - 2)^2 = (3x)^2 - 2(3x)(2) + (-2)^2 = 9x^2 - 12x + 4$$

Now, multiply this by $(x - 4)$:
$$f(x) = (9x^2 - 12x + 4)(x - 4)$$
$$f(x) = 9x^2(x - 4) - 12x(x - 4) + 4(x - 4)$$
$$f(x) = (9x^3 - 36x^2) - (12x^2 - 48x) + (4x - 16)$$
$$f(x) = 9x^3 - 36x^2 - 12x^2 + 48x + 4x - 16$$
$$f(x) = 9x^3 - (36 + 12)x^2 + (48 + 4)x - 16$$
$$f(x) = 9x^3 - 48x^2 + 52x - 16$$

Comparing this to $ax^3 + bx^2 + cx + d$, we find $a = 9$, $b = -48$, $c = 52$, $d = -16$.

Answer: $f(x) = 9x^3 - 48x^2 + 52x - 16$

Part (c): Find the length of line PQ

Thought Process: The problem states that line $l$ passes through the y-intercept of C and is parallel to the x-axis. This means $l$ is a horizontal line. First, we need to find the y-intercept of C by setting $x=0$ in $f(x)$. This will give us the equation of line $l$. Then, to find where $l$ cuts C again, we set $f(x)$ equal to the y-intercept value and solve for $x$. We expect a cubic equation, which we already know one root of (the y-intercept), so we can factor it to find the other roots, which correspond to points P and Q. The length PQ will be the difference between the x-coordinates of P and Q.

Working:


  1. Find the y-intercept of C:


Set $x = 0$ in the original function $f(x) = (3x - 2)^2 (x - 4)$ or the expanded form from part (b).
Using $f(x) = 9x^3 - 48x^2 + 52x - 16$:
$f(0) = 9(0)^3 - 48(0)^2 + 52(0) - 16 = -16$.
So, the y-intercept is $(0, -16)$.

  1. Determine the equation of line $l$:
Since $l$ passes through $(0, -16)$ and is parallel to the x-axis, its equation is $y = -16$.
  1. Find the intersection points of $C$ and $l$:
Set $f(x) = -16$: $$9x^3 - 48x^2 + 52x - 16 = -16$$ Add 16 to both sides: $$9x^3 - 48x^2 + 52x = 0$$
  1. Solve the cubic equation:
Factor out $x$: $$x(9x^2 - 48x + 52) = 0$$ This gives one solution $x = 0$ (which is the y-intercept, let's call it point O as in the diagram). The other two solutions for $x$ correspond to points P and Q, and are found by solving the quadratic equation: $$9x^2 - 48x + 52 = 0$$ Using the quadratic formula $x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$ with $a=9$, $b=-48$, $c=52$: $$x = \frac{-(-48) \pm \sqrt{(-48)^2 - 4(9)(52)}}{2(9)}$$ $$x = \frac{48 \pm \sqrt{2304 - 1872}}{18}$$ $$x = \frac{48 \pm \sqrt{432}}{18}$$
  1. Simplify the surd $\sqrt{432}$:
We look for the largest perfect square factor of 432. $432 = 2 \times 216 = 2 \times 2 \times 108 = 2^3 \times 54 = 2^3 \times 2 \times 27 = 2^4 \times 3^3 = 16 \times 27 = 144 \times 3$. So, $\sqrt{432} = \sqrt{144 \times 3} = \sqrt{144} \times \sqrt{3} = 12\sqrt{3}$.
  1. Substitute the simplified surd back into the quadratic formula:
$$x = \frac{48 \pm 12\sqrt{3}}{18}$$ Divide all terms by the common factor of 6: $$x = \frac{8 \pm 2\sqrt{3}}{3}$$ These are the x-coordinates of points P and Q. Let $x_P = \frac{8 - 2\sqrt{3}}{3}$ and $x_Q = \frac{8 + 2\sqrt{3}}{3}$.
  1. Calculate the length of PQ:
Since the line $l$ is horizontal, the length PQ is simply the absolute difference between the x-coordinates of P and Q. $$PQ = |x_Q - x_P|$$ $$PQ = \left| \frac{8 + 2\sqrt{3}}{3} - \frac{8 - 2\sqrt{3}}{3} \right|$$ $$PQ = \left| \frac{(8 + 2\sqrt{3}) - (8 - 2\sqrt{3})}{3} \right|$$ $$PQ = \left| \frac{8 + 2\sqrt{3} - 8 + 2\sqrt{3}}{3} \right|$$ $$PQ = \left| \frac{4\sqrt{3}}{3} \right|$$ $$PQ = \frac{4\sqrt{3}}{3}$$ This is in the form $k\sqrt{3}$, where $k = \frac{4}{3}$.
Answer: $\text{Length of PQ} = \frac{4\sqrt{3}}{3}$

Key techniques

  • analyse sign of factored polynomial
  • expand algebraic expressions
  • solve cubic equation by factoring
  • apply quadratic formula to find exact roots
  • simplify surds for exact length

Common pitfalls

  • Misinterpreting $f(x) > 0$: Forgetting that $(3x-2)^2$ is always non-negative, and thus only $x-4 > 0$ matters (excluding $x=2/3$).
  • Algebraic errors in expansion: Especially with negative signs or incorrect squaring of binomials (e.g., $(3x-2)^2 \ne 9x^2 + 4$).
  • Errors in quadratic formula or surd simplification: Forgetting to simplify $\sqrt{432}$ completely or incorrectly dividing terms in the fraction after using the quadratic formula.
