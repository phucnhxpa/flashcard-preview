Worked solution

Thought Process:

Part (a) asks us to express the quadratic function $f(x) = 2x^2 + 8x - 1$ in the form $a(x+p)^2+q$. This is the standard "completed square" form. The strategy is to first factor out the coefficient of $x^2$ from the $x^2$ and $x$ terms, then complete the square for the expression remaining inside the bracket, and finally distribute the factored-out coefficient back and simplify the constant term.

Part (b) then asks for the coordinates of the minimum point. Once the function is in the completed square form $a(x+p)^2+q$, we know that the term $(x+p)^2$ is always non-negative. Since $a=2$ (which is positive), the parabola opens upwards, meaning there is a minimum point. This minimum occurs when $(x+p)^2$ is at its smallest possible value, which is 0. This happens when $x+p=0$, or $x=-p$. At this point, the value of $f(x)$ will be $a(0)+q = q$. So, the minimum point will have coordinates $(-p, q)$.

Working:

(a) Express $f(x)$ in the form $a(x+p)^2+q$

Given the function $f(x) = 2x^2 + 8x - 1$.

Step 1: Factor out the coefficient of $x^2$ (which is 2) from the terms involving $x^2$ and $x$.
$$f(x) = 2(x^2 + 4x) - 1$$

Step 2: Complete the square for the expression inside the bracket, $x^2 + 4x$. To do this, we take half of the coefficient of $x$ (which is $4/2 = 2$), square it ($2^2 = 4$), and then add and subtract this value inside the bracket.
$$f(x) = 2(x^2 + 4x + 4 - 4) - 1$$

Step 3: Group the first three terms inside the bracket as a perfect square trinomial.
$$f(x) = 2((x+2)^2 - 4) - 1$$

Step 4: Distribute the factored-out coefficient (2) back into the bracket.
$$f(x) = 2(x+2)^2 - 2(4) - 1$$
$$f(x) = 2(x+2)^2 - 8 - 1$$

Step 5: Combine the constant terms.
$$f(x) = 2(x+2)^2 - 9$$

Comparing this to the form $a(x+p)^2+q$, we have $a=2$, $p=2$, and $q=-9$.

(b) Hence, write down the coordinates of the minimum point of $f(x)$

From part (a), we have $f(x) = 2(x+2)^2 - 9$.

Since the coefficient $a=2$ is positive, the parabola opens upwards, and the function has a minimum value.
The term $(x+2)^2$ is always non-negative, i.e., $(x+2)^2 \ge 0$.
The minimum value of $(x+2)^2$ is 0.
This minimum occurs when $x+2 = 0$, which means $x = -2$.

Substitute $x=-2$ back into the completed square form of $f(x)$ to find the minimum value:
$$f(-2) = 2(-2+2)^2 - 9$$
$$f(-2) = 2(0)^2 - 9$$
$$f(-2) = 0 - 9$$
$$f(-2) = -9$$

Thus, the minimum point has coordinates $(-2, -9)$.

Answer:
(a) $f(x) = 2(x+2)^2 - 9$
(b) Minimum point: $(-2, -9)

Key techniques

  • factorizing leading coefficient
  • adding and subtracting square of half coefficient
  • identifying vertex from completed square form

Common pitfalls

  • Forgetting to factor out the coefficient of $x^2$ before completing the square, or making an error when distributing it back.
  • Sign errors, especially with the constant term when completing the square (e.g., $-(c/2)^2$ term).
  • Incorrectly identifying the x-coordinate of the minimum point (e.g., stating $x=p$ instead of $x=-p$).
