Worked solution

Part (a): Express f(x) in the form $a - b(x + c)^2$

Thought Process: We need to rewrite the given quadratic function $f(x) = 3 + 12x - 2x^2$ into the specified form $a - b(x + c)^2$. This is a standard process called 'completing the square'. The key steps are to first rearrange the quadratic into standard descending power form ($Ax^2 + Bx + C$), then factor out the coefficient of $x^2$ (which is -2) from the $x^2$ and $x$ terms. After that, we complete the square for the resulting quadratic expression inside the bracket. Finally, we expand the factored term back and combine with the constant term to match the target form.

Working:
Start with the given function:
$$f(x) = 3 + 12x - 2x^2$$
Rearrange into standard form:
$$f(x) = -2x^2 + 12x + 3$$
Factor out the coefficient of $x^2$ from the $x^2$ and $x$ terms:
$$f(x) = -2(x^2 - 6x) + 3$$
Now, we complete the square for the expression inside the bracket, $x^2 - 6x$. We know that $(x-c)^2 = x^2 - 2cx + c^2$. Comparing $x^2 - 6x$ with $x^2 - 2cx$, we see that $2c = 6$, so $c=3$. Therefore, $x^2 - 6x$ can be written as $(x-3)^2 - 3^2$, which simplifies to $(x-3)^2 - 9$.
Substitute this completed square expression back into the equation for $f(x)$:
$$f(x) = -2((x-3)^2 - 9) + 3$$
Distribute the -2 across the terms inside the square bracket:
$$f(x) = -2(x-3)^2 + (-2)(-9) + 3$$
$$f(x) = -2(x-3)^2 + 18 + 3$$
Combine the constant terms:
$$f(x) = 21 - 2(x-3)^2$$
Comparing this to the form $a - b(x + c)^2$, we can identify the values:
$a = 21$
$b = 2$
$c = -3$

Answer: $a = 21, b = 2, c = -3$

Part (b): Find the area of the triangle PQR

Thought Process: The problem asks for the area of triangle PQR, where P and Q are x-intercepts of the curve $y = f(x) - 7$, and R is the y-intercept of the same curve. To solve this, we need to:


  1. Find the coordinates of the y-intercept R by setting $x=0$ in the equation $y = f(x) - 7$.

  2. Find the coordinates of the x-intercepts P and Q by setting $y=0$ in the equation $y = f(x) - 7$ and solving for $x$. Using the completed square form of $f(x)$ from part (a) is often the most efficient way to solve for $x$.

  3. Once P, Q, and R are found, we can calculate the area of the triangle using the formula $ rac{1}{2} imes ext{base} imes ext{height}$. The base will be the distance between P and Q along the x-axis, and the height will be the absolute value of the y-coordinate of R.

Working:
The equation of the curve is $y = f(x) - 7$.

1. Find the y-intercept R:
To find the y-intercept, set $x = 0$ in the equation $y = f(x) - 7$. It's easiest to use the original form of $f(x) = 3 + 12x - 2x^2$ for this step:
$$y = (3 + 12(0) - 2(0)^2) - 7$$
$$y = (3 + 0 - 0) - 7$$
$$y = 3 - 7$$
$$y = -4$$
So, the point R is $(0, -4)$.

2. Find the x-intercepts P and Q:
To find the x-intercepts, set $y = 0$ in the equation $y = f(x) - 7$. This means we need to solve $f(x) - 7 = 0$. Using the completed square form $f(x) = 21 - 2(x-3)^2$ from part (a) is efficient:
$$21 - 2(x-3)^2 - 7 = 0$$
Combine the constant terms:
$$14 - 2(x-3)^2 = 0$$
Rearrange the equation to isolate the squared term:
$$2(x-3)^2 = 14$$
Divide by 2:
$$(x-3)^2 = 7$$
Take the square root of both sides, remembering both positive and negative roots:
$$x - 3 = \pm\sqrt{7}$$
Solve for $x$:
$$x = 3 \pm \sqrt{7}$$
So, the x-intercepts are $P = (3 - \sqrt{7}, 0)$ and $Q = (3 + \sqrt{7}, 0)$ (the order of P and Q doesn't affect the area calculation).

3. Calculate the area of triangle PQR:
The base of the triangle, PQ, lies along the x-axis. The length of the base is the distance between the x-coordinates of Q and P:
$$ ext{Base PQ} = (3 + \sqrt{7}) - (3 - \sqrt{7})$$
$$ ext{Base PQ} = 3 + \sqrt{7} - 3 + \sqrt{7}$$
$$ ext{Base PQ} = 2\sqrt{7}$$
The height of the triangle is the perpendicular distance from the x-axis to the point R. This is the absolute value of the y-coordinate of R.
$$ ext{Height} = |-4| = 4$$
Now, calculate the area of the triangle using the formula $ rac{1}{2} imes ext{base} imes ext{height}$:
$$ ext{Area} = rac{1}{2} imes (2\sqrt{7}) imes 4$$
$$ ext{Area} = 4\sqrt{7}$$
The question asks for the answer in the form $m\sqrt{n}$, where $m$ and $n$ are integers. Our result $4\sqrt{7}$ fits this form with $m=4$ and $n=7$.

Answer: $4\sqrt{7}$

Key techniques

  • factor out coefficient of x^2
  • solve quadratic equation by completing the square
  • find x-intercepts by setting y=0
  • find y-intercept by setting x=0
  • calculate triangle area (1/2 base x height)

Common pitfalls

  • Sign errors when factoring out a negative coefficient (e.g., in part (a), $ -2(x^2 - 6x) $ not $ -2(x^2 + 6x) $).
  • Mistakes with the constant term when completing the square, especially when a coefficient for $x^2$ is factored out (e.g., remembering to multiply the $ -9 $ by $ -2 $).
  • Incorrectly identifying the base and height for the triangle area calculation. The base is the distance between x-intercepts, and the height is the absolute y-coordinate of the y-intercept.
