Worked solution

Thought Process

We are asked to solve a quadratic inequality. The general approach for these types of problems is to first find the 'critical values' by treating the inequality as an equality. Then, we use a sketch of the quadratic graph to determine which regions satisfy the original inequality. A positive coefficient of $x^2$ means the parabola opens upwards.

Working

We need to solve the inequality $2x^2 + 5x - 3 > 0$.

Step 1: Find the critical values by solving the corresponding equality.
$$2x^2 + 5x - 3 = 0$$
We can factorise the quadratic expression. We look for two numbers that multiply to $2 \times -3 = -6$ and add to $5$. These numbers are $6$ and $-1$.
$$2x^2 + 6x - x - 3 = 0$$
Factor by grouping:
$$2x(x + 3) - 1(x + 3) = 0$$
$$(2x - 1)(x + 3) = 0$$
This gives us the critical values:
$$2x - 1 = 0 \implies x = \frac{1}{2}$$
$$x + 3 = 0 \implies x = -3$$
So, the critical values are $x = -3$ and $x = \frac{1}{2}$.

Step 2: Sketch the graph of $y = 2x^2 + 5x - 3$.
Since the coefficient of $x^2$ is $2$ (which is positive), the parabola opens upwards. It intersects the x-axis at the critical values $x = -3$ and $x = \frac{1}{2}$.

Step 3: Determine the regions satisfying the inequality.
The inequality is $2x^2 + 5x - 3 > 0$. This means we are looking for the values of $x$ for which the graph of $y = 2x^2 + 5x - 3$ is above the x-axis.
From our sketch, the parabola is above the x-axis when $x$ is to the left of $-3$ or to the right of $\frac{1}{2}$.

Therefore, the solution to the inequality is $x < -3$ or $x > \frac{1}{2}$.

Answer: $x
< -3$ or $x > \frac{1}{2}$

Key techniques

  • factorise quadratic
  • find critical values
  • sketch quadratic graph
  • interpret graph for inequality

Common pitfalls

  • Incorrectly factorising the quadratic expression, leading to wrong critical values.
  • Mixing up the inequality signs for the final answer (e.g., giving $-3 < x < 1/2$ when the question asks for $ > 0$).
  • Not providing two separate inequalities for the solution (e.g., $x<-3$ and $x>1/2$), sometimes incorrectly combined into a single interval or using an 'and' instead of 'or'.
