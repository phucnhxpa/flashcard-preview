Calculate areas of a sector and triangles, and an angle in a composite garden shape.

Worked solution

THOUGHT PROCESS:

This question requires calculating areas and angles within a composite shape, specifically a circular sector and two triangles. All calculations must use radians consistently.

Part (a): Area of a Circular Sector. We use the standard formula $A = \frac{1}{2}r^2\theta$. We are given the radius $r=6$ and angle $\theta=1.3$ radians.

Part (b): Angle in Triangle ABE using the Cosine Rule. We are given three side lengths of triangle ABE (6, 10.8, 12.2). To find angle ABE, we apply the Cosine Rule: $c^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab \cos C$. We identify the side $c=12.2$ as being opposite angle ABE, and then rearrange the formula to solve for $\cos C$ and find the angle.

Part (c): Total Area. This part requires summing the areas of three components: the sector calculated in part (a), the area of triangle ABE, and the area of triangle BCD. For triangle ABE, we use the formula $A = \frac{1}{2}ab \sin C$, using sides 6 and 10.8 and the included angle ABE found in part (b). For triangle BCD, the problem implies sides of length 6 and 6. The included angle $\text{CBD}$ is found by inferring a common geometric arrangement where the angle $1.3$ (from the sector), angle ABE, and angle CBD are adjacent angles that together sum to $\pi$ radians (a straight line). Thus, $\text{CBD} = \pi - 1.3 - \text{ABE}$. We then calculate $A_{BCD}$ and sum all three areas to get the total.

WORKING:

Part (a): Calculate the area of the sector.
Using $r=6$ and $\theta=1.3$ radians:
$A_{sector} = \frac{1}{2}(6)^2(1.3) = \frac{1}{2}(36)(1.3) = 18 \times 1.3 = 23.4 \text{ m}^2$

Part (b): Calculate angle ABE using the Cosine Rule.
In triangle ABE, let the sides be $a=6$, $b=10.8$, and the side opposite angle ABE be $c=12.2$.
Applying the Cosine Rule, $c^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab \cos C$:
$$(12.2)^2 = (6)^2 + (10.8)^2 - 2(6)(10.8) \cos(\text{ABE})$$
$$148.84 = 36 + 116.64 - 129.6 \cos(\text{ABE})$$
$$148.84 = 152.64 - 129.6 \cos(\text{ABE})$$
Rearranging to solve for $\cos(\text{ABE})$:
$$129.6 \cos(\text{ABE}) = 152.64 - 148.84$$
$$129.6 \cos(\text{ABE}) = 3.8$$
$$\text{ABE} = \arccos\left(\frac{3.8}{129.6}\right) \approx 1.54131 \text{ radians}$$
Rounding to 3 significant figures:
$$\text{ABE} \approx 1.54 \text{ rad}$$

Part (c): Calculate the total area.

  1. Area of Triangle ABE:
Using sides $a=6$, $b=10.8$, and included angle $\text{ABE} \approx 1.54131$ radians: $A_{ABE} = \frac{1}{2}(6)(10.8) \sin(1.54131) \approx 32.383 \text{ m}^2$
  1. Area of Triangle BCD:
Assume sides $BC=6$ and $BD=6$. Calculate the included angle $\text{CBD}$: $$\text{CBD} = \pi - 1.3 - \text{ABE}$$ Using the unrounded value for ABE: $$\text{CBD} = \pi - 1.3 - 1.54131 \approx 0.30028 \text{ radians}$$ Now calculate $A_{BCD}$: $A_{BCD} = \frac{1}{2}(6)(6) \sin(0.30028) \approx 5.083 \text{ m}^2$
  1. Total Area:
Sum the three areas: $A_{total} = A_{sector} + A_{ABE} + A_{BCD}$ $A_{total} = 23.4 + 32.383 + 5.083 \approx 60.866 \text{ m}^2$

Rounding to 3 significant figures:

Answer: $60.9 \text{ m}^2$

Key techniques

  • apply formula for area of sector in radians
  • apply cosine rule to find an angle given three sides
  • apply formula for area of triangle given two sides and included angle
  • use angle properties (angles on a straight line) in radians
  • combine areas of composite shapes

Common pitfalls

  • Using degrees instead of radians for angle calculations or mixing units within the same problem.
  • Incorrectly applying the Cosine Rule, especially when identifying which side is opposite the unknown angle.
  • Premature rounding of intermediate results, which can lead to inaccuracies in the final answer.
