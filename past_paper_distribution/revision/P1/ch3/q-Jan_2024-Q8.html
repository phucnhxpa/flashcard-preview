Worked solution

Thought Process:
We recognize this as a problem involving calculations with sectors of circles, specifically relating to arc length and area, using radians. The challenge lies in correctly interpreting the "platform" shape, which, based on the mark scheme, is composed of two concentric sectors (one minor, one major) and their connecting radial segments. We must use the given angle and arc length to find radii, then apply standard formulae for arc length and sector area, paying attention to which angle (minor or major) applies to which part of the shape. Precision with intermediate values is crucial for "show that" questions and for final accuracy.

Working:

(a) Show that $AO = 7.03$ m (3 marks)

Thought Process:
First, we need to find the radius of the outer sector. We are given the arc length of the outer arc ($S_{DC} = 9$ m) and the angle it subtends at the centre ($\theta = 0.8$ rad). We can use the formula $S = r\theta$. Once we have the outer radius ($OD$), the problem statement (as implied by the mark scheme) will provide a relationship between $OD$ and $AO$ (e.g., $AO = \frac{5}{8} OD$), which we will use to calculate $AO$.

Working:
Let $OD$ be the radius of the outer circle.
Given arc length $DC = 9$ m and angle $\angle DOC = 0.8$ radians.
Using the formula for arc length, $S = r\theta$:
$$9 = OD \times 0.8$$
$$OD = \frac{9}{0.8} = 11.25 \text{ m}$$
The problem implies that $AO$ is $\frac{5}{8}$ of $OD$.
$$AO = \frac{5}{8} \times 11.25$$
$$AO = \frac{56.25}{8} = 7.03125 \text{ m}$$
Rounding to 3 significant figures, we get:
$$AO = 7.03 \text{ m}$$
This confirms the required value.

(b) Find the perimeter of the platform (3 marks)

Thought Process:
The perimeter of the platform consists of three main parts:


  1. The outer arc ($DC$). Its length is given as 9 m.

  2. The inner arc ($AEB$). The mark scheme indicates this arc uses the *major* angle ($2\pi - 0.8$ radians) and the inner radius ($AO = 7.03125$ m). We will use $S=r\theta$ for this.

  3. The two straight radial edges ($DA$ and $CB$). Each length is the difference between the outer and inner radii ($OD - AO$).

We will calculate each part and sum them.

Working:
Radius of outer arc, $OD = 11.25$ m.
Radius of inner arc, $AO = 7.03125$ m (using the unrounded value for precision).

  1. Outer arc length $DC = 9$ m (given).
  1. Inner arc length $AEB$:
The angle for this arc is the major angle, $(2\pi - 0.8)$ radians. $$S_{AEB} = AO \times (2\pi - 0.8)$$ $$S_{AEB} = 7.03125 \times (2\pi - 0.8)$$ $$S_{AEB} \approx 7.03125 \times (6.283185... - 0.8)$$ $$S_{AEB} \approx 7.03125 \times 5.483185...$$ $$S_{AEB} \approx 38.5501 \text{ m}$$
  1. Lengths of the two straight edges $DA$ and $CB$:
$$DA = CB = OD - AO$$ $$DA = CB = 11.25 - 7.03125$$ $$DA = CB = 4.21875 \text{ m}$$

Now, sum these parts to find the total perimeter:
$$P = S_{DC} + S_{AEB} + DA + CB$$
$$P = 9 + 38.5501... + 4.21875 + 4.21875$$
$$P = 9 + 38.5501... + 8.4375$$
$$P = 55.9876... \text{ m}$$
Rounding to one decimal place as 'awrt 56.0 m' is specified in the mark scheme:
$$P \approx 56.0 \text{ m}$$

Answer: The perimeter of the platform is approximately $56.0$ m.

(c) Find the area of the platform (3 marks)

Thought Process:
Based on the mark scheme, the area of the platform is the sum of two sector areas:


  1. The area of the outer minor sector ($ODC$), with radius $OD = 11.25$ m and angle $0.8$ radians.

  2. The area of the inner major sector ($OAEB$), with radius $AO = 7.03125$ m and angle $(2\pi - 0.8)$ radians.


We will use the formula for the area of a sector, $A = \frac{1}{2}r^2\theta$, for each part and then add them.

Working:
Radius of outer sector, $OD = 11.25$ m.
Radius of inner sector, $AO = 7.03125$ m.

  1. Area of outer sector $ODC$:
$$A_{ODC} = \frac{1}{2} (OD)^2 \times \angle DOC$$ $$A_{ODC} = \frac{1}{2} (11.25)^2 \times 0.8$$ $$A_{ODC} = \frac{1}{2} (126.5625) \times 0.8$$ $$A_{ODC} = 50.625 \text{ m}^2$$
  1. Area of inner sector $OAEB$:
The angle for this sector is the major angle, $(2\pi - 0.8)$ radians. $$A_{OAEB} = \frac{1}{2} (AO)^2 \times (2\pi - 0.8)$$ $$A_{OAEB} = \frac{1}{2} (7.03125)^2 \times (2\pi - 0.8)$$ $$A_{OAEB} \approx \frac{1}{2} (49.438476...) \times 5.483185...$$ $$A_{OAEB} \approx 135.445... \text{ m}^2$$

Now, sum these areas to find the total area of the platform:
$$A_{platform} = A_{ODC} + A_{OAEB}$$
$$A_{platform} = 50.625 + 135.445...$$
$$A_{platform} = 186.07... \text{ m}^2$$
Rounding to 3 significant figures as 'awrt 186 m$^2$' is specified in the mark scheme:
$$A_{platform} \approx 186 \text{ m}^2$$

Answer: The area of the platform is approximately $186$ m$^2$.

Key techniques

  • Apply arc length formula ($S=r\theta$)
  • Apply sector area formula ($A=\frac{1}{2}r^2\theta$)
  • Calculate angle in major/minor sector
  • Sum components for perimeter/area

Common pitfalls

  • Incorrectly identifying major vs. minor angle for relevant parts of the shape.
  • Premature rounding of intermediate values, leading to inaccuracy in 'show that' questions or final answers.
  • Misinterpreting the components of the platform's perimeter or area based on the specific question wording/diagram (e.g., adding sector areas vs. subtracting them for an annulus segment).
