Worked solution

This question requires calculations of angles, areas, and perimeters of a logo composed of a triangle and a major circular sector. All sector-related calculations must use angles in radians.

Part (a): Find the angle YXZ

Thought process: To find an angle in triangle XYZ (sides XY=4, XZ=6, YZ=9), use the cosine rule. Let angle YXZ be $\theta$.

Working:
$$YZ^2 = XY^2 + XZ^2 - 2(XY)(XZ) \cos \theta$$
$$9^2 = 4^2 + 6^2 - 2(4)(6) \cos \theta$$
$$81 = 16 + 36 - 48 \cos \theta$$
$$81 = 52 - 48 \cos \theta$$
$$48 \cos \theta = -29$$
$$\cos \theta = -\frac{29}{48}$$
$$\theta = \arccos\left(-\frac{29}{48}\right) \approx 2.22170 \text{ radians}$$
Rounding to 3 s.f.: $\theta = 2.22 \text{ radians}$.

Answer: The angle YXZ is $2.22$ radians.

Part (b): Find the area of the major sector

Thought process: The major sector has radius $r=4$ cm. Its angle, $\alpha$, is the reflex angle $2\pi - \theta$. The area formula is $A = \frac{1}{2}r^2\alpha$.

Working:
Minor sector angle $\theta = 2.2217$ radians.
Major sector angle $\alpha = 2\pi - 2.2217 \approx 4.061485 \text{ radians}$.
$$A_{major} = \frac{1}{2}(4^2)(4.061485)$$
$$A_{major} = 8 \times 4.061485 = 32.49188$$
Rounding to 3 s.f.: $A_{major} = 32.5 \text{ cm}^2$.

Answer: The area of the major sector is $32.5 \text{ cm}^2$.

Part (c): Find the total area of the logo

Thought process: The total area is the sum of triangle XYZ and the major sector area. For triangle XYZ, use $A = \frac{1}{2}ab\sin C$ with sides XY=4, XZ=6 and included angle $\theta$.

Working:
Area of triangle XYZ $A_{triangle} = \frac{1}{2}(4)(6)\sin(2.2217)$$
$$A_{triangle} = 12 \sin(2.2217) \approx 9.5604 \text{ cm}^2$$
Total area $A_{logo} = A_{triangle} + A_{major}$
$$A_{logo} = 9.5604 + 32.49188 = 42.05228$$
Rounding to 3 s.f.: $A_{logo} = 42.1 \text{ cm}^2$.

Answer: The total area of the logo is $42.1 \text{ cm}^2$.

Part (d): Find the perimeter of the logo

Thought process: The perimeter comprises YZ, the segment ZW, and the major arc WY. ZW is found by $XZ - XW$ (where XW is radius). Major arc length is $L = r\alpha$.

Working:
Length YZ = 9 cm.
Length ZW = $6 - 4 = 2$ cm.
Major arc length $L_{major} = 4 \times 4.061485 = 16.24594 \text{ cm}$.
Perimeter $P_{logo} = YZ + ZW + L_{major}$
$$P_{logo} = 9 + 2 + 16.24594 = 27.24594$$
Rounding to 3 s.f.: $P_{logo} = 27.2 \text{ cm}$.

Answer: The perimeter of the logo is $27.2 \text{ cm}$.

Key techniques

  • apply cosine rule to find angle
  • calculate area of major sector (radians)
  • calculate arc length of major sector (radians)
  • calculate triangle area using 1/2ab sinC
  • combine lengths and areas for composite figures

Common pitfalls

  • Mixing degrees and radians: Always ensure your calculator is in radian mode for sector/arc length formulas and when using sine/cosine of angles expressed in radians.
  • Using the minor sector angle instead of the major sector angle for calculations involving the major sector's area or arc length.
  • Incorrectly identifying perimeter components: Only the exterior boundary of the shape counts. Do not include internal lines like XY or XW in the perimeter.
