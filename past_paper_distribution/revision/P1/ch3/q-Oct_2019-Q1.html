Question 1 continued
Worked solution

Part (a): Find the exact value of $r$

Thought Process:
We are given the area of a sector ($A = 15 \text{ cm}^2$) and the angle in radians ($\theta = 1.25 \text{ rad}$). We need to find the radius ($r$). We recognize that the formula for the area of a sector in radians is $A = \frac{1}{2}r^2\theta$. Our strategy is to substitute the given values into this formula and then solve for $r$.

Working:


  1. Recall the formula for the area of a sector:


$$A = \frac{1}{2}r^2\theta$$

  1. Substitute the given values for $A$ and $\theta$:


$$15 = \frac{1}{2}r^2(1.25)$$

  1. Multiply both sides by 2 to clear the fraction:


$$30 = r^2(1.25)$$

  1. Divide by 1.25 to isolate $r^2$:


$$r^2 = \frac{30}{1.25}$$
$$r^2 = 24$$

  1. Take the square root of both sides. Since $r$ is a length, it must be positive:


$$r = \sqrt{24}$$

  1. Simplify the surd $\sqrt{24}$. We look for the largest perfect square factor of 24, which is 4 ($24 = 4 \times 6$):


$$r = \sqrt{4 \times 6}$$
$$r = \sqrt{4} \times \sqrt{6}$$
$$r = 2\sqrt{6}$$

Answer: $r = 2\sqrt{6}$

Part (b): Find the exact length of the perimeter of the sector

Thought Process:
The perimeter of a sector consists of two radii and the arc length. We have already found the radius $r = 2\sqrt{6}$ from part (a) and we are given the angle $\theta = 1.25 \text{ rad}$. We recognize that the formula for the arc length of a sector in radians is $s = r\theta$. Our strategy is to first calculate the arc length and then add it to the two radii to find the total perimeter.

Working:


  1. Recall the formula for the arc length of a sector:


$$s = r\theta$$

  1. Substitute the values for $r$ and $\theta$:


$$s = (2\sqrt{6})(1.25)$$

  1. Calculate the arc length:


$$s = 2.5\sqrt{6}$$

  1. The perimeter $P$ of the sector is the sum of the two radii and the arc length:


$$P = r + r + s$$
$$P = 2r + s$$

  1. Substitute the values for $r$ and $s$:


$$P = 2(2\sqrt{6}) + 2.5\sqrt{6}$$
$$P = 4\sqrt{6} + 2.5\sqrt{6}$$

  1. Combine the terms with $\sqrt{6}$:


$$P = (4 + 2.5)\sqrt{6}$$
$$P = 6.5\sqrt{6}$$

  1. Write the answer in simplest form, converting the decimal to a fraction:


$$P = \frac{13}{2}\sqrt{6}$$

Answer: $P = \frac{13}{2}\sqrt{6}$

Key techniques

  • apply sector area formula
  • apply arc length formula
  • simplify surds
  • algebraic manipulation

Common pitfalls

  • Confusing the area formula $A = \frac{1}{2}r^2\theta$ with the arc length formula $s = r\theta$ (or vice-versa).
  • Forgetting to add both radii when calculating the perimeter of the sector, only adding one radius and the arc length.
  • Errors in simplifying surds, e.g., not simplifying $\sqrt{24}$ to $2\sqrt{6}$ or making arithmetic mistakes with surds (e.g., $2\sqrt{6} \times 1.25 \neq 2.5\sqrt{150}$).
