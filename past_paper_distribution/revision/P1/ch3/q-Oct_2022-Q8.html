Worked solution

Thought Process:
We recognize this as a multi-part trigonometry problem involving a circular sector and an associated triangle. The question progresses logically from basic sector properties (area, perimeter) to triangle properties within the sector (area, chord length) and finally to a separate triangle formed using the chord, requiring the Sine Rule. We must be careful with radian measure for angles and choose the correct formula for each part.

(a) A sector OAB has centre O, radius 6m and angle $AOB = \frac{2\pi}{3}$ radians.

(a) (i) Find the area of the sector OAB.


  • Thought Process: The area of a sector is given by the formula $A = \frac{1}{2}r^2\theta$. We are given the radius $r=6$m and the angle $\theta = \frac{2\pi}{3}$ radians.

  • Working:


$$A = \frac{1}{2}r^2\theta$$
$$A = \frac{1}{2}(6)^2\left(\frac{2\pi}{3}\right)$$
$$A = \frac{1}{2}(36)\left(\frac{2\pi}{3}\right)$$
$$A = 18\left(\frac{2\pi}{3}\right)$$
$$A = 12\pi \text{ m}^2$$

(a) (ii) Find the perimeter of the sector OAB.


  • Thought Process: The perimeter of a sector consists of two radii and the arc length. The arc length is given by $L = r\theta$.

  • Working:


First, find the arc length:
$$L = r\theta$$
$$L = 6\left(\frac{2\pi}{3}\right)$$
$$L = 4\pi \text{ m}$$
Then, calculate the perimeter:
$$\text{Perimeter} = 2r + L$$
$$\text{Perimeter} = 2(6) + 4\pi$$
$$\text{Perimeter} = 12 + 4\pi \text{ m}$$

(b) Find the area of triangle OAB.


  • Thought Process: The area of a triangle when two sides and the included angle are known is given by $A = \frac{1}{2}ab\sin C$. Here, the sides are $OA=OB=r=6$ and the included angle is $\angle AOB = \frac{2\pi}{3}$.

  • Working:


$$A_{\triangle OAB} = \frac{1}{2}r^2\sin\theta$$
$$A_{\triangle OAB} = \frac{1}{2}(6)^2\sin\left(\frac{2\pi}{3}\right)$$
$$A_{\triangle OAB} = \frac{1}{2}(36)\left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\right)$$
$$A_{\triangle OAB} = 18\left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\right)$$
$$A_{\triangle OAB} = 9\sqrt{3} \text{ m}^2$$

(c) Find the length of the chord AB.


  • Thought Process: To find the length of the chord AB, we can use the Cosine Rule in triangle OAB, as we know two sides ($OA=OB=6$) and the included angle ($\angle AOB = \frac{2\pi}{3}$). The Cosine Rule states $c^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab\cos C$.

  • Working:


$$AB^2 = OA^2 + OB^2 - 2(OA)(OB)\cos(\angle AOB)$$
$$AB^2 = 6^2 + 6^2 - 2(6)(6)\cos\left(\frac{2\pi}{3}\right)$$
$$AB^2 = 36 + 36 - 72\left(-\frac{1}{2}\right)$$
$$AB^2 = 72 + 36$$
$$AB^2 = 108$$
$$AB = \sqrt{108}$$
$$AB = \sqrt{36 \times 3}$$
$$AB = 6\sqrt{3} \text{ m}$$

(d) A point C is such that triangle ABC has $AC=8$m and $BC=6$m. Given that $\angle ACB = \frac{\pi}{6}$ radians, find $\angle BAC$ in radians to 3 significant figures.


  • Thought Process: We have a triangle ABC where we know one side length from part (c) ($AB=6\sqrt{3}$), two other side lengths given ($AC=8$, $BC=6$), and one angle ($\angle ACB = \frac{\pi}{6}$). We need to find $\angle BAC$. This is a direct application of the Sine Rule, which states $\frac{\sin A}{a} = \frac{\sin B}{b} = \frac{\sin C}{c}$.

  • Working:


From part (c), we know $AB = 6\sqrt{3}$.
Given $AC = 8$ and $BC = 6$, and $\angle ACB = \frac{\pi}{6}$.
Applying the Sine Rule to $\triangle ABC$ to find $\angle BAC$:
$$\frac{\sin(\angle BAC)}{BC} = \frac{\sin(\angle ACB)}{AB}$$
$$\frac{\sin(\angle BAC)}{6} = \frac{\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{6}\right)}{6\sqrt{3}}$$
We know $\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{6}\right) = \frac{1}{2}$.
$$\frac{\sin(\angle BAC)}{6} = \frac{\frac{1}{2}}{6\sqrt{3}}$$
$$\sin(\angle BAC) = 6 \times \frac{\frac{1}{2}}{6\sqrt{3}}$$
$$\sin(\angle BAC) = \frac{3}{6\sqrt{3}}$$
$$\sin(\angle BAC) = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{3}}$$
$$\angle BAC = \arcsin\left(\frac{1}{2\sqrt{3}}\right)$$
Using a calculator (ensure radian mode):
$$\angle BAC \approx \arcsin(0.288675...)$$
$$\angle BAC \approx 0.29259 \dots \text{ radians}$$
Rounding to 3 significant figures:
$$\angle BAC \approx 0.293 \text{ radians}$$

Answer:
(a)(i) $12\pi \text{ m}^2$
(a)(ii) $12 + 4\pi \text{ m}$
(b) $9\sqrt{3} \text{ m}^2$
(c) $6\sqrt{3} \text{ m}$
(d) $0.293 \text{ radians}$

Key techniques

  • calculate sector area using radians
  • calculate arc length and perimeter of sector
  • calculate triangle area (1/2ab sinC)
  • apply cosine rule to find a side
  • apply sine rule to find an angle

Common pitfalls

  • Confusing radians and degrees, especially when using a calculator or exact values for standard angles.
  • Incorrectly applying the Sine Rule (e.g., matching the wrong angle with its opposite side).
  • Forgetting to include the two radii when calculating the perimeter of a sector.
  • Making arithmetic errors with fractions or surds, especially when dealing with exact trigonometric values.
