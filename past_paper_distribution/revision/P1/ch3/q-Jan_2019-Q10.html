10.	A sector AOB, of a circle centre O, has radius r cm and angle θ radians.
	
Given that the area of the sector is 6 cm2 and that the perimeter of the sector is 10 cm, 
	
(a)	 show that
3θ 2 − 13θ + 12 = 0
(4)
	
(b)	Hence find possible values of r and θ.

Worked solution

Part (a)

Thought Process:
The problem provides information about a sector of a circle: its area and its perimeter. We need to use the standard formulae for the area and perimeter of a sector in radians, relating the radius ($r$) and the angle ($ heta$). Once we have these two equations, our goal is to eliminate the radius $r$ to derive the given quadratic equation in terms of $ heta$ only.

Working:
Let the radius of the sector be $r$ cm and the angle be $ heta$ radians.

  1. Formulate equations from given information:
The area of a sector is given by $A = rac{1}{2}r^2 heta$. The perimeter of a sector is given by $P = r heta + 2r$ (arc length plus two radii).

Given that the area $A = 6$ cm$^2$ and the perimeter $P = 10$ cm, we can write the two equations:
$$6 = rac{1}{2}r^2 heta \quad ext{(Equation 1)}$$
$$10 = r heta + 2r \quad ext{(Equation 2)}$$

  1. Rearrange Equation 2 to express $r$ in terms of $ heta$:
We aim to eliminate $r$. It's often easier to isolate a variable that isn't squared. From Equation 2: $$10 = r( heta + 2)$$ $$r = rac{10}{ heta + 2} \quad ext{(Equation 3)}$$
  1. Substitute Equation 3 into Equation 1:
Now substitute the expression for $r$ from Equation 3 into Equation 1 to eliminate $r$ and get an equation purely in $ heta$: $$6 = rac{1}{2}\left( rac{10}{ heta + 2} ight)^2 heta$$ $$12 = \left( rac{100}{( heta + 2)^2} ight) heta$$ $$12 = rac{100 heta}{( heta + 2)^2}$$
  1. Simplify and rearrange to match the target equation:
Multiply both sides by $( heta + 2)^2$: $$12( heta + 2)^2 = 100 heta$$ Expand $( heta + 2)^2$: $$12( heta^2 + 4 heta + 4) = 100 heta$$ Divide both sides by 4 to simplify the coefficients: $$3( heta^2 + 4 heta + 4) = 25 heta$$ Distribute the 3: $$3 heta^2 + 12 heta + 12 = 25 heta$$ Move all terms to one side to form a quadratic equation: $$3 heta^2 + 12 heta - 25 heta + 12 = 0$$ $$3 heta^2 - 13 heta + 12 = 0$$ This matches the required equation.

Part (b)

Thought Process:
Now that we have the quadratic equation for $ heta$, we need to solve it to find the possible values of $ heta$. Once we have these values, we will substitute each back into an earlier equation (preferably Equation 3 from Part (a)) to find the corresponding values of $r$. We must present the pairs of $(r, heta)$ clearly.

Working:


  1. Solve the quadratic equation for $ heta$:


We have the equation $3 heta^2 - 13 heta + 12 = 0$.
We can solve this by factorisation or using the quadratic formula. By factorisation:
$$(3 heta - 4)( heta - 3) = 0$$
This gives two possible values for $ heta$:
$$3 heta - 4 = 0 \implies heta = rac{4}{3}$$
$$ heta - 3 = 0 \implies heta = 3$$

  1. Find the corresponding values of $r$ for each $ heta$:
Using Equation 3: $r = rac{10}{ heta + 2}$.

Case 1: $ heta = rac{4}{3}$
$$r = rac{10}{ rac{4}{3} + 2} = rac{10}{ rac{4}{3} + rac{6}{3}} = rac{10}{ rac{10}{3}} = 10 imes rac{3}{10} = 3$$

Case 2: $ heta = 3$
$$r = rac{10}{3 + 2} = rac{10}{5} = 2$$

Both pairs of $(r, heta)$ are valid since $r > 0$ and $ heta > 0$.

Answer:
Possible values of $r$ and $ heta$ are:
$(r=3, heta= rac{4}{3})$ and $(r=2, heta=3)$.

Key techniques

  • recall sector formulas (radians)
  • formulate and solve simultaneous equations
  • algebraic manipulation to derive target equation
  • factorise or use quadratic formula
  • substitute values to find remaining variables

Common pitfalls

  • Incorrectly recalling or applying the formulae for sector area or perimeter in radians.
  • Making algebraic errors when rearranging equations or substituting one into another, particularly when expanding brackets like $( heta+2)^2$.
  • Failing to fully solve the quadratic equation or incorrectly pairing the values of $r$ and $ heta$ (e.g., mixing up which $r$ goes with which $ heta$).
  • Not checking the validity of solutions (e.g. radius and angle must be positive).
