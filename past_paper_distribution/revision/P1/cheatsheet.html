7× 6-9m
Given a graph of $y=f(x)$ or its equation, you are asked to sketch or describe a transformed graph like $y=af(x+b)+c$. You might also need to find transformation parameters from a diagram.

A: Transform Key Points/Features: Always a reliable method, especially when the original graph has clear turning points, intercepts, or asymptotes.
$f(x)+a$: Vertical translation by vector $\begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ a \end{pmatrix}$ (MEMORIZE)
$f(x+a)$: Horizontal translation by vector $\begin{pmatrix} -a \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}$ (MEMORIZE)
$af(x)$: Vertical stretch, scale factor $a$, parallel to the y-axis (MEMORIZE)
$f(ax)$: Horizontal stretch, scale factor $1/a$, parallel to the x-axis (MEMORIZE)
$-f(x)$: Reflection in the x-axis (MEMORIZE)
$f(-x)$: Reflection in the y-axis (MEMORIZE)
  • Confusing $f(x+a)$ as a shift to the right. It is a shift to the LEFT by 'a'.
  • Confusing $f(ax)$ as a stretch by factor 'a'. It is a stretch by factor $1/a$.
  • Incorrectly transforming asymptotes. Vertical asymptotes $x=k$ are affected by horizontal transformations. Horizontal asymptotes $y=c$ are affected by vertical transformations.
  • Applying transformations in the wrong order.

A: Substitution Method: Standard method for all 'disguised quadratic' problems.
$a^{m+n} = a^m a^n$ (MEMORIZE)

A: Applying Index Laws: For expressions involving integer, fractional, or negative powers.
B: Rationalising the Denominator: When a fraction has a surd in the denominator, especially of the form $a \pm \sqrt{b}$ or $\sqrt{a} \pm \sqrt{b}$.
$a^m \times a^n = a^{m+n}$ (MEMORIZE)
$(a^m)^n = a^{mn}$ (MEMORIZE)
$a^{m/n} = \sqrt[n]{a^m}$ (MEMORIZE)
$(a+b)(a-b) = a^2-b^2$ (Difference of Squares) (MEMORIZE)
$\sqrt{ab} = \sqrt{a}\sqrt{b}$ (MEMORIZE)
  • Incorrectly applying index laws, e.g., $(x^2)^3 = x^5$ instead of $x^6$.
  • Forgetting to apply a power to the numerical coefficient, e.g., $(2x^3)^2 = 2x^6$ instead of $4x^6$.
  • Errors in expanding brackets when rationalising, especially with signs, e.g., $(3-\sqrt{2})^2 = 9-2$ instead of $9 - 6\sqrt{2} + 2$.
  • Not fully simplifying surds, e.g., leaving $\sqrt{12}$ instead of writing $2\sqrt{3}$.

A: Substitute and solve simultaneously: Always — this is the standard fit-from-data method.
Substitute pair $(t_1, h_1)$: $h_1 = a + b/t_1$

Decomposition Method: This is the standard method for all composite shape problems.
$s = r\theta$ (Arc Length, $\theta$ in radians) (formula booklet)
$A = \frac{1}{2}r^2\theta$ (Area of Sector, $\theta$ in radians) (formula booklet)
$A = \frac{1}{2}ab\sin C$ (Area of Triangle) (formula booklet)
$a^2 = b^2 + c^2 - 2bc\cos A$ (Cosine Rule) (formula booklet)
$\frac{a}{\sin A} = \frac{b}{\sin B} = \frac{c}{\sin C}$ (Sine Rule) (formula booklet)
Area of Segment = Area of Sector - Area of Triangle = $\frac{1}{2}r^2(\theta - \sin \theta)$ (MEMORIZE)
  • Using degrees instead of radians for sector/arc formulas (CHECK CALCULATOR MODE!).
  • Prematurely rounding intermediate values, which leads to inaccuracies in the final answer.
  • Forgetting to add straight-line lengths when calculating the perimeter of a shape that includes arcs.
  • Using the minor sector angle when the major sector is required (e.g. using $\theta$ instead of $2\pi - \theta$).

A: Parameter Analysis: When given an equation $y = A f(Bx+C) + D$ and asked to find features or sketch the graph.
B: Graphical Intersection Method: When asked for the 'number of solutions' to an equation like `trig(x) = linear(x)`.
For $y = A \sin(B\theta) + D$ or $y = A \cos(B\theta) + D$:
Amplitude = $|A|$ (MEMORIZE)
Period = $\frac{2\pi}{B}$ (radians) or $\frac{360^\circ}{B}$ (degrees) (MEMORIZE)
Principal Axis (Vertical Shift) is the line $y = D$ (MEMORIZE)
Range = $[D - |A|, D + |A|]$ (MEMORIZE)
  • Incorrectly calculating the period (e.g., using $2\pi \times B$ instead of $2\pi / B$).
  • Confusing amplitude (A) with the maximum value (D+A).
  • For $A < 0$, forgetting the graph is reflected in the principal axis (e.g., a negative cosine graph starts at a minimum).
  • Inaccurately sketching the tangent graph, especially missing or misplacing asymptotes.

Rule Selection Method: Standard approach for all non-right-angled triangle problems.
$\frac{a}{\sin A} = \frac{b}{\sin B} = \frac{c}{\sin C}$ (Sine Rule) (formula booklet)
$a^2 = b^2 + c^2 - 2bc\cos A$ (Cosine Rule) (formula booklet)
$A = \frac{1}{2}ab\sin C$ (Area of Triangle) (formula booklet)
Ambiguous case: If $\sin \theta = k$, then potential solutions are $\theta_1 = \arcsin(k)$ and $\theta_2 = 180^\circ - \theta_1$ (MEMORIZE)
  • Forgetting to check for the ambiguous case (obtuse angle) when using the Sine Rule to find an angle.
  • Applying the Cosine Rule with the wrong angle (it must be the angle *between* the two known sides).
  • Mixing up side-angle pairings in the Sine Rule (e.g., `a` with `sin B`).
  • Calculator in the wrong mode (degrees vs. radians).

Identity, Substitute, Solve, CAST: For any equation with a mix of sin and cos, or with a squared trig term.
$\sin^2 x + \cos^2 x \equiv 1$ (MEMORIZE)
$\tan x \equiv \frac{\sin x}{\cos x}$ (MEMORIZE)
Quadratic Formula: $x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}$ (formula booklet)

  • Dividing by a trig function (e.g., `sin x`) which could be zero, thus losing potential solutions.

Finding Equations of Tangents and Normals
11× 7-11m
You are given a curve's equation, $y=f(x)$, and a point $P$ (or just its x-coordinate). You're asked to find the equation of the line that is either the tangent or the normal to the curve at point $P$

The Standard 5-Step Method: Always. This is the universal procedure for these questions.
$\frac{d}{dx}(ax^n) = anx^{n-1}$ (MEMORIZE)
$m_{normal} = -\frac{1}{m_{tangent}}$ (MEMORIZE)
$y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$ (formula booklet)
  • Incorrectly converting roots/fractions to index form, e.g., $\frac{1}{x\sqrt{x}} = x^{-3/2}$, not $x^{-1.5}$ or other variants.
  • Confusing the normal and tangent gradients. A common mistake is using $m_T$ when asked for the normal.
  • Substituting the x-coordinate into the original function $y=f(x)$ to find the gradient, instead of into the derivative $\frac{dy}{dx}$.
  • Algebraic errors when rearranging the final line equation into the required form, especially with fractions and signs.

The Limit Definition Method: Only when explicitly asked to use 'first principles'.
$f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h}$ (formula booklet)

A: Differentiate twice + solve: When asked to find f''(x) and then solve f''(x) = 0 or f''(x) > 0.
