  • Forgetting that (g(x))² generates cross-terms when g(x) itself is a series
  • Sign errors when composing alternating series (e.g. ln(1+x) substituted into another alternating series)

A: Direct substitution using x = r cos θ, y = r sin θ: Converting a specific point or finding tangent coordinates
B: Use r² = x² + y² and r cos θ = x, r sin θ = y for curve conversion: Converting the entire polar equation to Cartesian form
$x = r\cos\theta,\quad y = r\sin\theta$ (MEMORIZE)
$r^2 = x^2 + y^2$ (MEMORIZE)
$\tan\theta = \frac{y}{x}$ (MEMORIZE)
$\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{dy/d\theta}{dx/d\theta} = \frac{\frac{dr}{d\theta}\sin\theta + r\cos\theta}{\frac{dr}{d\theta}\cos\theta - r\sin\theta}$ (MEMORIZE — or derive from product rule)

  • Not substituting the correct θ value when evaluating the gradient
  • Leaving the answer in polar form when Cartesian is requested
