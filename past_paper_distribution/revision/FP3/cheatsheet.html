Reduction formula derivation and recursive evaluation
18× 7-12m
You are given an integral $I_n = \int f(x)^n \, dx$ (or a definite version) and asked to show that $I_n$ satisfies a recurrence relation such as $nI_n = (n-1)I_{n-2} + \text{boundary terms}$. Then use

A: Integration by parts with strategic splitting: Almost always the primary method; split $f(x)^n$ into $f(x)^{n-k} \cdot g(x)$ where $g(x)$ is directly integrable
B: Substitution before IBP (for integrands like $x^n \cos(x^2)$): When the integrand contains a composite function like $\cos(x^2)$ or $(x^3+1)^{1/2}$ that suggests an inner substitution
$\text{IBP: } \int u\,dv = uv - \int v\,du$ (formula booklet)
$\sin^2 x = 1 - \cos^2 x$ (MEMORIZE)
$\tan^2 x = \sec^2 x - 1$ (MEMORIZE)
$\cosh^2 \theta - \sinh^2 \theta = 1$ (formula booklet)
$I_n(\sec): nI_n = \sec^{n-2}x\tan x + (n-2)I_{n-2}$ — derive, don't memorize
$I_n(\sin^n x, 0 \text{ to } \pi/2): nI_n = (n-1)I_{n-2}$ — derive, don't memorize
  • Forgetting that after IBP the original integral $I_n$ reappears — you MUST collect like terms and solve for $I_n$
  • Failing to replace $\cos^2 x$ with $1 - \sin^2 x$ (or $\tan^2 x$ with $\sec^2 x - 1$) to split the remaining integral into $I_n$ and $I_{n-k}$
  • Dropping the boundary term $[uv]$ or evaluating it incorrectly — always check at BOTH limits
  • Using wrong base case: $I_0 = \pi/2$ vs $I_1 = 1$ depends on whether $n$ is even or odd
  • Algebraic error in the coefficient when integrating to find $v$ (e.g. missing $2/9$ factor)
