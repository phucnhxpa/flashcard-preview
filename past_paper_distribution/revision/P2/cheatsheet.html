Finding and Using the Equation of a Circle

A: Completing the Square: When given the general form of the circle's equation ($x^2 + y^2 + 2gx + 2fy + c = 0$).
B: Using Midpoint and Distance Formulae: When given the coordinates of two points that form a diameter.
Standard form: $(x-a)^2 + (y-b)^2 = r^2$ for centre $(a, b)$ and radius $r$. (formula booklet)
General form: $x^2 + y^2 + 2gx + 2fy + c = 0$ for centre $(-g, -f)$ and radius $\sqrt{g^2+f^2-c}$. (MEMORIZE)
Midpoint: $M = \left(\frac{x_1+x_2}{2}, \frac{y_1+y_2}{2}\right)$ (formula booklet)
Distance: $d = \sqrt{(x_2-x_1)^2 + (y_2-y_1)^2}$ (formula booklet)
  • Sign errors identifying the centre, e.g., centre of $(x+3)^2 + (y-2)^2 = 16$ is $(-3, 2)$, not $(3, -2)$.
  • Forgetting to square the radius for the equation, or forgetting to square root the constant term to find the radius.
  • Arithmetic errors when completing the square, especially with the constant terms.
  • Forgetting that $r^2$ must be positive for a real circle to exist.

A: Geometric Property (Perpendicular Radius): This is the most direct and reliable method for circles.
B: Implicit Differentiation: A powerful calculus-based alternative, and good practice for general curves.
Perpendicular gradients: $m_1 m_2 = -1$ (MEMORIZE)

  • Errors in calculating the negative reciprocal (e.g., forgetting the sign change or inverting incorrectly).
  • Mistakes applying the chain rule during implicit differentiation.

A: Direct Application of Formula: This is the standard and universal method for all binomial expansion questions in P2.
$(a+b)^n = a^n + \binom{n}{1}a^{n-1}b + \binom{n}{2}a^{n-2}b^2 + ... + \binom{n}{r}a^{n-r}b^r + ... + b^n$ (formula booklet)

A: Formulate and Solve (Non-Linear) Equations: The standard method for all GP problems.
