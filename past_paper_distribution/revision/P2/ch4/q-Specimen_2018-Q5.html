Q5
Worked solution

This is a multi-part question testing your understanding of both arithmetic and geometric series formulas and your ability to solve simultaneous equations.

Part (a): Find the value of $a$ and the value of $d$ for the arithmetic series.

Thought Process:
We are given information about the sum of the first 4 terms and the sum of the first 8 terms of an arithmetic series. We need to find the first term ($a$) and the common difference ($d$). The standard formula for the sum of the first $n$ terms of an arithmetic series is $S_n = \frac{n}{2}(2a + (n-1)d)$. We will use this formula twice to create two simultaneous equations in terms of $a$ and $d$, and then solve them.

Working:
For an arithmetic series, the sum of the first $n$ terms is given by:
$$S_n = \frac{n}{2}(2a + (n-1)d)$$

We are given $S_4 = 50$. Substituting $n=4$:
$$S_4 = \frac{4}{2}(2a + (4-1)d)$$
$$50 = 2(2a + 3d)$$
$$50 = 4a + 6d$$
Dividing by 2 to simplify, we get our first equation:
$$\qquad 25 = 2a + 3d \quad \text{(Equation 1)}$$

We are also given $S_8 = 196$. Substituting $n=8$:
$$S_8 = \frac{8}{2}(2a + (8-1)d)$$
$$196 = 4(2a + 7d)$$
Dividing by 4 to simplify, we get our second equation:
$$\qquad 49 = 2a + 7d \quad \text{(Equation 2)}$$

Now we have a system of two linear simultaneous equations:


  1. $2a + 3d = 25$

  2. $2a + 7d = 49$

To solve these, we can subtract Equation 1 from Equation 2:
$$(2a + 7d) - (2a + 3d) = 49 - 25$$
$$4d = 24$$
$$d = \frac{24}{4}$$
$$d = 6$$

Now substitute the value of $d=6$ back into Equation 1 to find $a$:
$$2a + 3(6) = 25$$
$$2a + 18 = 25$$
$$2a = 25 - 18$$
$$2a = 7$$
$$a = \frac{7}{2}$$
$$a = 3.5$$

Part (b): Find the value of $r$ for the geometric series.

Thought Process:
We are told the first term of the geometric series is the same as the arithmetic series' first term (so $a=3.5$). We are given the sum to infinity, $S_\infty = 40$. The formula for the sum to infinity of a geometric series is $S_\infty = \frac{a}{1-r}$, provided $|r|<1$. We can substitute the known values and solve for $r$.

Working:
For a geometric series, the sum to infinity is given by:
$$S_\infty = \frac{a}{1-r} \quad \text{for } |r|<1$$

From part (a), we found $a = 3.5$. We are given $S_\infty = 40$. Substitute these values into the formula:
$$40 = \frac{3.5}{1-r}$$

Multiply both sides by $(1-r)$:
$$40(1-r) = 3.5$$

Divide both sides by 40:
$$1-r = \frac{3.5}{40}$$
$$1-r = \frac{35}{400}$$
$$1-r = \frac{7}{80}$$

Now, solve for $r$:
$$r = 1 - \frac{7}{80}$$
$$r = \frac{80}{80} - \frac{7}{80}$$
$$r = \frac{73}{80}$$

We should check that $|r|<1$: $$\left|\frac{73}{80}\right| < 1$$ which is true, so the sum to infinity exists.

Part (c): Find the sum of the first 5 terms of this geometric series.

Thought Process:
We need to find the sum of the first 5 terms, $S_5$, of the geometric series. We already have $a=3.5$ and $r=73/80$ from previous parts. The formula for the sum of the first $n$ terms of a geometric series is $S_n = \frac{a(1-r^n)}{1-r}$ (or $S_n = \frac{a(r^n-1)}{r-1}$, using the first form is often easier when $r<1$). We will substitute the values and calculate.

Working:
For a geometric series, the sum of the first $n$ terms is given by:
$$S_n = \frac{a(1-r^n)}{1-r}$$

We have $a = 3.5$, $r = \frac{73}{80}$, and we want to find $S_5$ (so $n=5$):
$$S_5 = \frac{3.5\left(1 - \left(\frac{73}{80}\right)^5\right)}{1 - \frac{73}{80}}$$

We know $1 - \frac{73}{80} = \frac{7}{80}$ from part (b). Substitute this in:
$$S_5 = \frac{3.5\left(1 - \left(\frac{73}{80}\right)^5\right)}{\frac{7}{80}}$$

To simplify the fraction, remember that dividing by a fraction is the same as multiplying by its reciprocal:
$$S_5 = 3.5 \times \frac{80}{7} \left(1 - \left(\frac{73}{80}\right)^5\right)$$
$$S_5 = \frac{3.5 \times 80}{7} \left(1 - \left(\frac{73}{80}\right)^5\right)$$
$$S_5 = \frac{280}{7} \left(1 - \left(\frac{73}{80}\right)^5\right)$$
$$S_5 = 40 \left(1 - \left(\frac{73}{80}\right)^5\right)$$

Using a calculator for the numerical value:
$$\left(\frac{73}{80}\right)^5 \approx (0.9125)^5 \approx 0.638519$$
$$S_5 = 40 (1 - 0.638519...)$$
$$S_5 = 40 (0.361480...)$$
$$S_5 \approx 14.4592...$$
Rounding to 3 significant figures:
$$S_5 \approx 14.5$$

Answer:
(a) $a = 3.5$, $d = 6$
(b) $r = \frac{73}{80}$
(c) $S_5 = 14.5$ (3 s.f.)

Key techniques

  • formulate simultaneous equations
  • solve simultaneous linear equations
  • apply AP sum formula
  • apply GP sum to infinity formula
  • apply GP sum to n terms formula

Common pitfalls

  • Mistaking arithmetic progression formulas for geometric progression formulas, or vice versa, especially for sum to n terms.
  • Making algebraic errors when solving simultaneous equations or rearranging the sum to infinity formula.
  • Forgetting to check the condition $|r|<1$ when using the sum to infinity formula (though it's usually satisfied in these problems, it's good practice).
  • Prematurely rounding values during intermediate steps, which can lead to inaccuracies in the final answer. Keep full calculator precision until the very end.
