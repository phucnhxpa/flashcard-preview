Worked solution

Let's break down this problem step by step.

Part (a): Show that $r$ satisfies $3r^2 - 4r - 4 = 0$ [3 marks]

Thought Process:
We know the $n$-th term of a geometric sequence is $u_n = ar^{n-1}$ where $a = u_1$. We have two conditions involving $a$ and $r$, so we can eliminate $a$ to obtain an equation in $r$ alone.

Working:


  1. Express terms: $u_2 = ar$, $u_3 = ar^2$, $u_4 = ar^3$.

  1. From the given conditions:
$$ar + ar^2 = 6 \quad \Rightarrow \quad a r(1+r) = 6 \tag{1}$$ $$ar^3 = 8 \tag{2}$$
  1. Divide equation (2) by equation (1) to eliminate $a$:
$$\frac{ar^3}{ar(1+r)} = \frac{8}{6}$$ $$\frac{r^2}{1+r} = \frac{4}{3}$$
  1. Cross-multiply:
$$3r^2 = 4(1+r)$$ $$3r^2 = 4 + 4r$$ $$3r^2 - 4r - 4 = 0 \qquad \square$$

Part (b): Find $u_1$ [3 marks]

Thought Process:
Solve the quadratic for $r$. Since the sequence has a sum to infinity, we need $|r| < 1$. This will select the correct root. Then substitute back to find $a = u_1$.

Working:


  1. Factorise $3r^2 - 4r - 4 = 0$:


$$(3r + 2)(r - 2) = 0$$
$$r = -\frac{2}{3} \quad \text{or} \quad r = 2$$

  1. For a sum to infinity to exist, $|r| < 1$. Since $|2| \not< 1$, we reject $r = 2$.
$$r = -\frac{2}{3}$$
  1. Substitute into $ar^3 = 8$:
$$a\left(-\frac{2}{3}\right)^3 = 8$$ $$a\left(-\frac{8}{27}\right) = 8$$ $$a = 8 \times \left(-\frac{27}{8}\right) = -27$$
Answer: $u_1 = -27$

Part (c): Find $S_\infty$ [2 marks]

Thought Process:
Use the sum to infinity formula $S_\infty = \frac{a}{1-r}$ with $a = -27$ and $r = -\frac{2}{3}$.

Working:
$$S_\infty = \frac{-27}{1 - \left(-\frac{2}{3}\right)} = \frac{-27}{1 + \frac{2}{3}} = \frac{-27}{\frac{5}{3}} = -27 \times \frac{3}{5} = -\frac{81}{5}$$

Answer: $S_\infty = -\frac{81}{5}$ (or $-16.2$)

Key techniques

  • define geometric sequence terms
  • formulate and solve simultaneous equations
  • solve quadratic equations by factoring or formula
  • apply condition for sum to infinity
  • calculate sum to infinity

Common pitfalls

  • Incorrectly forming equations from sequence terms (e.g., confusing $u_n = ar^{n-1}$ with $ar^n$)
  • Failing to apply the $|r| < 1$ condition for sum to infinity, leading to choosing $r = 2$ instead of $r = -\frac{2}{3}$
  • Sign errors when cubing $r = -\frac{2}{3}$ — forgetting the negative gives $a = 27$ instead of $a = -27$
  • Algebraic errors in $S_\infty$: forgetting that $1 - (-\frac{2}{3}) = \frac{5}{3}$, not $\frac{1}{3}$
