A past-paper question (UNIT P2, CHAPTER 5 = "Exponentials and logarithms", session Jun 2022, Q4, total 6 marks)

*Thought Process:*
We are given two simultaneous equations. One is linear ($a-b=8$) and the other involves logarithms ($\log_4 a + \log_4 b = 3$). Our strategy will be to first simplify the logarithmic equation using logarithm rules. Then, we will use substitution from the linear equation into the simplified logarithmic equation to form a single equation in one variable, which we anticipate will be a quadratic. Finally, we'll solve this quadratic for one variable, then find the other, ensuring both $a$ and $b$ are positive constants and expressed in fully simplified surd form.

*Working:*
Let the given equations be:


  1. $a - b = 8$

  2. $\log_4 a + \log_4 b = 3$

*Step 1: Simplify the logarithmic equation.*
We recognize that the sum of logarithms with the same base can be combined into the logarithm of a product using the rule $\log_c x + \log_c y = \log_c (xy)$.
Applying this rule to equation (2):
$$ \log_4 (ab) = 3 $$
Now, we convert this logarithmic equation into its equivalent index form. The definition $\log_c N = P \iff N = c^P$ allows us to rewrite this as:
$$ ab = 4^3 $$
$$ ab = 64 \quad (3) $$

*Step 2: Use substitution to form a quadratic equation.*
From equation (1), we can easily express $a$ in terms of $b$:
$$ a = 8 + b \quad (4) $$
Substitute this expression for $a$ from equation (4) into equation (3):
$$ (8 + b)b = 64 $$
$$ 8b + b^2 = 64 $$
Rearrange this into the standard quadratic form $Ab^2 + Bb + C = 0$:
$$ b^2 + 8b - 64 = 0 $$

*Step 3: Solve the quadratic equation for $b$.*
Since the question asks for answers in surd form, and the coefficients are not straightforward for factoring, we will use the quadratic formula $x = \frac{-B \pm \sqrt{B^2-4AC}}{2A}$. Here, $A=1$, $B=8$, and $C=-64$.
$$ b = \frac{-8 \pm \sqrt{8^2 - 4(1)(-64)}}{2(1)} $$
$$ b = \frac{-8 \pm \sqrt{64 + 256}}{2} $$
$$ b = \frac{-8 \pm \sqrt{320}}{2} $$
Now, we need to simplify the surd $\sqrt{320}$. We look for the largest perfect square factor of 320.
$320 = 64 \times 5$.
So, $\sqrt{320} = \sqrt{64 \times 5} = \sqrt{64} \times \sqrt{5} = 8\sqrt{5}$.
Substitute this simplified surd back into the expression for $b$:
$$ b = \frac{-8 \pm 8\sqrt{5}}{2} $$
$$ b = -4 \pm 4\sqrt{5} $$

*Step 4: Check for valid solutions and find the corresponding $a$ values.*
We are given that $a$ and $b$ must be positive constants. Logarithms are only defined for positive arguments.
Consider the two possible values for $b$:


  1. $b = -4 + 4\sqrt{5}$


We know that $\sqrt{5} \approx 2.236$. So, $4\sqrt{5} \approx 8.944$. Therefore, $b = -4 + 8.944 = 4.944 > 0$. This is a valid solution for $b$.

  1. $b = -4 - 4\sqrt{5}$


This value is clearly negative ($b \approx -4 - 8.944 = -12.944$). Since $b$ must be positive, we reject this solution.

So, we take the valid solution for $b$: $b = -4 + 4\sqrt{5}$.
Now, substitute this value of $b$ back into equation (4) to find $a$:
$$ a = 8 + b $$
$$ a = 8 + (-4 + 4\sqrt{5}) $$
$$ a = 8 - 4 + 4\sqrt{5} $$
$$ a = 4 + 4\sqrt{5} $$
Since $4+4\sqrt{5}$ is positive (as $4\sqrt{5} > 0$), this is a valid solution for $a$.

*Step 5: Present the answers in fully simplified surd form.*
$a = 4 + 4\sqrt{5}$
$b = -4 + 4\sqrt{5}$

Answer: $a = 4 + 4\sqrt{5}$, $b = -4 + 4\sqrt{5}$

Key techniques

  • Apply logarithm product rule: $\log x + \log y = \log(xy)$
  • Convert logarithmic form to index form: $\log_c N = P \iff N = c^P$
  • Substitute to form a quadratic equation
  • Solve quadratic equation using the quadratic formula
  • Simplify surds

Common pitfalls

  • Incorrectly applying logarithm rules, e.g., $\log a + \log b \neq \log(a+b)$.
  • Making algebraic errors when substituting or solving the quadratic equation.
  • Forgetting to check the domain restriction that $a$ and $b$ must be positive, leading to an extraneous solution.
  • Not simplifying the surds in the final answer to their simplest form.
