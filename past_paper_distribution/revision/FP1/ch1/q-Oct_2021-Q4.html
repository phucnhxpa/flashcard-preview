Worked solution

This question tests your understanding of complex roots of polynomials with real coefficients, polynomial factorization, and graphical representation of complex numbers.

Part (a): Write down another complex root.

Thought Process: We are given a polynomial $f(z) = 2z^3 - z^2 + az + b$, where $a$ and $b$ are integers. This is crucial because it means all coefficients (2, -1, $a$, $b$) are real numbers. The Conjugate Root Theorem states that if a complex number $z_1 = x + yi$ is a root of a polynomial equation with real coefficients, then its complex conjugate $\bar{z_1} = x - yi$ must also be a root.

Working:
Given root: $z_1 = -1 - 3i$.
Since the polynomial $f(z)$ has real coefficients, the complex conjugate of $z_1$ must also be a root.
The complex conjugate of $-1 - 3i$ is $-1 + 3i$.

Answer (a): The other complex root is $-1 + 3i$.

Part (b): Determine the value of $a$ and the value of $b$.

Thought Process: We now have two roots: $z_1 = -1 - 3i$ and $z_2 = -1 + 3i$. Since $f(z)$ is a cubic polynomial (degree 3), it must have three roots. As complex roots of real polynomials always come in conjugate pairs, and we already have one pair, the third root, $z_3$, must be a real number.

We can form a quadratic factor from the complex conjugate pair. If $z_1$ and $z_2$ are roots, then $(z-z_1)(z-z_2)$ is a factor of $f(z)$. We will then multiply this quadratic factor by a linear factor $(kz-m)$ to form the cubic $f(z)$ and compare coefficients to find $a$ and $b$.

Working:
The two complex conjugate roots are $z_1 = -1 - 3i$ and $z_2 = -1 + 3i$.
A quadratic factor of $f(z)$ is given by:
$$(z - (-1 - 3i))(z - (-1 + 3i))$$
$$= (z + 1 + 3i)(z + 1 - 3i)$$
This expression is in the form $(A + B)(A - B)$, where $A = (z+1)$ and $B = 3i$.
$$= (z+1)^2 - (3i)^2$$
$$= (z^2 + 2z + 1) - (9i^2)$$
Recall that $i^2 = -1$. So, $9i^2 = 9(-1) = -9$.
$$= z^2 + 2z + 1 - (-9)$$
$$= z^2 + 2z + 1 + 9$$
$$= z^2 + 2z + 10$$

So, $(z^2 + 2z + 10)$ is a factor of $f(z)$. Since $f(z)$ is a cubic, the remaining factor must be linear, which we can denote as $(kz - m)$.
Therefore, $f(z) = (z^2 + 2z + 10)(kz - m)$.

We are given $f(z) = 2z^3 - z^2 + az + b$.
Comparing the coefficient of $z^3$ in both expressions:
From $(z^2 + 2z + 10)(kz - m)$, the $z^3$ term is $z^2 \times kz = kz^3$.
From the given $f(z)$, the $z^3$ term is $2z^3$.
So, $kz^3 = 2z^3 \implies k = 2$.

Now we have $f(z) = (z^2 + 2z + 10)(2z - m)$.
Expanding this expression:
$$(z^2 + 2z + 10)(2z - m) = 2z(z^2 + 2z + 10) - m(z^2 + 2z + 10)$$
$$= 2z^3 + 4z^2 + 20z - mz^2 - 2mz - 10m$$
Collect terms by powers of $z$:
$$= 2z^3 + (4-m)z^2 + (20-2m)z - 10m$$

Now, we compare the coefficients of this expanded form with $f(z) = 2z^3 - z^2 + az + b$.

Comparing coefficients of $z^2$:
$4 - m = -1$
$m = 4 + 1$
$m = 5$

With $k=2$ and $m=5$, the third factor is $(2z - 5)$. This means the third root is $z_3 = \frac{5}{2}$.

Finally, we determine $a$ and $b$ by comparing the remaining coefficients:
Comparing coefficients of $z$:
$a = 20 - 2m$
Substitute $m=5$:
$a = 20 - 2(5)$
$a = 20 - 10$
$a = 10$

Comparing the constant terms:
$b = -10m$
Substitute $m=5$:
$b = -10(5)$
$b = -50$

Answer (b): $a = 10$, $b = -50$.

Part (c): Show all the roots on an Argand diagram.

Thought Process: An Argand diagram is a graphical representation of complex numbers. A complex number $x + yi$ is plotted as a point $(x, y)$ in the complex plane, where the horizontal axis represents the real part (Re(z)) and the vertical axis represents the imaginary part (Im(z)). We have found all three roots: $z_1 = -1 - 3i$, $z_2 = -1 + 3i$, and $z_3 = 2.5$ (from $2z-5=0$).

Working:
The three roots of the equation $f(z) = 0$ are:
$z_1 = -1 - 3i$
$z_2 = -1 + 3i$
$z_3 = \frac{5}{2} = 2.5$

To show these on an Argand diagram:


  • Plot $z_1 = -1 - 3i$ as the point with coordinates $(-1, -3)$.

  • Plot $z_2 = -1 + 3i$ as the point with coordinates $(-1, 3)$.

  • Plot $z_3 = 2.5$ (which is $2.5 + 0i$) as the point with coordinates $(2.5, 0)$ on the real axis.

Diagram Description:
Draw a set of coordinate axes. Label the horizontal axis "Re(z)" and the vertical axis "Im(z)". Ensure your axes are scaled appropriately to fit all points.


  • Mark a point for $z_1$ at $(-1, -3)$.

  • Mark a point for $z_2$ at $(-1, 3)$.

  • Mark a point for $z_3$ at $(2.5, 0)$.

Answer: See diagram description above.

Key techniques

  • Identify complex conjugate roots
  • Form quadratic factor from conjugate pair
  • Expand and compare polynomial coefficients
  • Plot complex numbers on Argand diagram

Common pitfalls

  • Forgetting or misapplying the Conjugate Root Theorem (e.g., if coefficients were not real, the theorem wouldn't apply, but this is rare in FP1).
  • Algebraic errors during the expansion of factors or comparison of coefficients, especially sign errors. Double-check your expansion.
  • Incorrectly plotting points on the Argand diagram (e.g., swapping real and imaginary parts, or misjudging coordinates, particularly for the real root).
