Combined mean and standard deviation from two groups
1× 6-8m (part of a larger question)

A: Recover sums and combine: Standard and only reliable method for combining groups
$\Sigma x = n\bar{x}$ (MEMORIZE)
$\Sigma x^2 = n(\sigma^2 + \bar{x}^2)$ — rearranged from $\sigma^2 = \frac{\Sigma x^2}{n} - \bar{x}^2$ (MEMORIZE)
$\bar{x}_c = \frac{\Sigma x_1 + \Sigma x_2}{n_1 + n_2}$ (MEMORIZE)
$\sigma_c = \sqrt{\frac{\Sigma x_1^2 + \Sigma x_2^2}{n_1 + n_2} - \bar{x}_c^2}$ (MEMORIZE)
  • Averaging the two means or the two s.d.s directly — this is WRONG unless n₁ = n₂ (and even then wrong for s.d.)
  • Forgetting to add x̄² when recovering Σx²: writing Σx² = nσ² instead of n(σ² + x̄²)
  • Using sample variance (n−1) from one formula and population variance (n) from another — stay consistent with S1 convention (n divisor)

Calculating PMCC from summary statistics
14× 3-5m
You are given summary statistics such as Σx, Σy, Σx², Σy², Σxy and n. You must compute Sxx, Syy, Sxy and then the product moment correlation coefficient r.
Jan 2023 Q6Jan 2024 Q2Jun 2022 Q2Jun 2024 Q4Oct 2024 Q2

A: Direct formula using summary statistics: Always — this is the standard and expected method when summary statistics are provided
B: Recover Sxy from given regression coefficient b: When the regression line is already given and you need to find r (e.g. Jun 2021)
$S_{xx} = \sum x^2 - \frac{(\sum x)^2}{n}$ (formula booklet)
$S_{yy} = \sum y^2 - \frac{(\sum y)^2}{n}$ (formula booklet)
$S_{xy} = \sum xy - \frac{(\sum x)(\sum y)}{n}$ (formula booklet)
$r = \frac{S_{xy}}{\sqrt{S_{xx} \cdot S_{yy}}}$ (formula booklet)
  • Forgetting to divide (Σx)² by n — writing Sxx = Σx² − (Σx)² instead of Σx² − (Σx)²/n
  • Mixing up Sxx and Syy or Sxy in the formula
  • Arithmetic errors with large squared sums — always double-check on the calculator
  • Giving r to insufficient decimal places (typically 3 s.f. or 4 d.p. required)
