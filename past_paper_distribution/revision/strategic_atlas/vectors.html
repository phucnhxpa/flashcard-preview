Cross Product Normal

2 moves · 10 examples
Cross two in-plane (or direction) vectors to obtain a perpendicular normal
$\;\mathbf{u} = \begin{pmatrix}1\\0\\2\end{pmatrix},\quad \mathbf{v} = \begin{pmatrix}0\\1\\1\end{pmatrix}\;$
$\;\mathbf{n} = \mathbf{u} \times \mathbf{v} = \begin{vmatrix}\mathbf{i}&\mathbf{j}&\mathbf{k}\\1&0&2\\0&1&1\end{vmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix}-2\\-1\\1\end{pmatrix}\;$
9q cross product normal
Use half the cross product magnitude for triangle area in 3D
$\;\overrightarrow{AB} = (1,0,2),\quad \overrightarrow{AC} = (0,1,1)\;$
$\;\overrightarrow{AB} \times \overrightarrow{AC} = (-2,-1,1),\quad \text{Area} = \tfrac{1}{2}\sqrt{4+1+1} = \tfrac{\sqrt{6}}{2}\;$
1q cross product normal

Parametric Substitution

2 moves · 7 examples
Substitute parametric line into plane equation to find intersection point
$\;\mathbf{r} = (1,0,0) + t(1,1,1),\quad x + y + z = 6\;$
$\;(1+t)+(t)+(t)=6 \Rightarrow 1+3t=6 \Rightarrow t=\tfrac{5}{3} \Rightarrow \mathbf{r}=\bigl(\tfrac{8}{3},\,\tfrac{5}{3},\,\tfrac{5}{3}\bigr)\;$
6q parametric substitution
Parametrise symmetric line form to substitute into plane equation

$\;(1+2t)+(2+t)+(0-t)=5 \Rightarrow 2t+3=5 \Rightarrow t=1 \Rightarrow (3,\,3,\,-1)\;$
1q parametric substitution

Dot Product Angle

2 moves · 6 examples
Apply dot product formula with modulus to find angle between directions in 3D
$\;\mathbf{u} = \begin{pmatrix}1\\2\\2\end{pmatrix},\quad \mathbf{v} = \begin{pmatrix}2\\1\\-2\end{pmatrix}\;$
$\;\cos\theta = \dfrac{|\mathbf{u}\cdot\mathbf{v}|}{|\mathbf{u}||\mathbf{v}|} = \dfrac{|2+2-4|}{3\cdot3} = 0 \Rightarrow \theta = 90^\circ\;$
5q dot product angle

$\;\mathbf{n_1} = (1,0,1),\quad \mathbf{n_2} = (0,1,-1)\;$
$\;\cos\theta = \dfrac{|\mathbf{n_1}\cdot\mathbf{n_2}|}{|\mathbf{n_1}||\mathbf{n_2}|} = \dfrac{|-1|}{\sqrt{2}\cdot\sqrt{2}} = \dfrac{1}{2} \Rightarrow \theta = 60^\circ\;$
1q dot product angle

$\;\text{(i)}\ 1+\lambda=2\mu,\quad \text{(ii)}\ 2\lambda=1+\mu \Rightarrow \lambda=1,\,\mu=1;\quad \text{(iii)}\ 0+1=1+0\ \checkmark \Rightarrow \text{intersection at } (2,2,1)\;$
