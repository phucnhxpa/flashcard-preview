Perpendicularity Condition

4 moves · 30 examples
Take negative reciprocal of tangent gradient to obtain normal gradient
$\;m_T = 3\;$
$\;m_N = -\dfrac{1}{m_T} = -\dfrac{1}{3}\;$
26q perpendicularity condition
Apply m₁m₂ = −1 to obtain the perpendicular gradient
$\;m_1 = \dfrac{3}{2}\;$
$\;m_2 = \dfrac{-1}{m_1} = -\dfrac{2}{3}\;$
2q perpendicularity condition
Apply m₁·m₂ = −1 and cancel shared parametric factors
$\;m_{PQ} = \dfrac{2}{p+q},\quad m_{OS} = \dfrac{p+q}{2}\cdot(-pq),\quad m_{PQ}\cdot m_{OS} = -1\;$
$\;\dfrac{2}{p+q}\cdot\dfrac{-(p+q)pq}{2} = -1 \Rightarrow pq = 1\;$
1q perpendicularity condition
Use negative reciprocal of gradient for perpendicular line
$\;m = 3,\quad P = (1,\,2)\;$
$\;m_\perp = -\tfrac{1}{3},\quad y - 2 = -\tfrac{1}{3}(x - 1)\;$
1q perpendicularity condition

Parametric Elimination

5 moves · 11 examples
Eliminate the parameter to obtain a Cartesian equation
$\;x = t^2,\quad y = 2t\;$
$\;t = \dfrac{y}{2} \Rightarrow x = \left(\dfrac{y}{2}\right)^2 = \dfrac{y^2}{4}\;$
4q parametric elimination
Eliminate the parameter θ via the Pythagorean identity to obtain a Cartesian locus
$\;x = 3\cos\theta,\quad y = 2\sin\theta\;$
$\;\cos^2\theta + \sin^2\theta = 1 \Rightarrow \dfrac{x^2}{9} + \dfrac{y^2}{4} = 1\;$
3q parametric elimination
Isolate the parameter from each line, then equate to eliminate it
$\;\ell_1:\, y = tx,\quad \ell_2:\, ty = x - 1\;$
$\;t = \dfrac{y}{x} = \dfrac{x-1}{y} \Rightarrow y^2 = x^2 - x\;$
2q parametric elimination
Compute x²+y² to bridge parametric point to (x²+y²)²-form locus
$\;x = \dfrac{4\cos\theta}{1+\cos^2\theta},\quad y = \dfrac{4\sin\theta}{1+\cos^2\theta},\quad D = 1+\cos^2\theta\;$
$\;x^2+y^2 = \dfrac{16}{D^2}\cdot 1 = \dfrac{16}{D},\quad 4x^2+4y^2\cos^2\!\theta = \dfrac{16\cdot 4}{D^2} \Rightarrow (x^2+y^2)^2 = 4x^2+4y^2\;$
1q parametric elimination
Substitute parametric coordinates directly into the Cartesian equation of the other curve
$\;x = t^2,\ y = 2t,\quad xy = 8\;$
$\;(t^2)(2t) = 8 \Rightarrow t^3 = 4 \Rightarrow t = \sqrt[3]{4}\;$
1q parametric elimination
