Substitution Bridge

12 moves · 14 examples · 4 sub-groups

Mechanics of Substitution

5 moves
Procedural moves that build the bridge between variables: differentiating the substitution to get dx in terms of du, converting limits, and rewriting leftover x-factors via inversion or rearrangement.
Differentiate the given substitution to express dx in terms of du
$\;\int 2x\,e^{x^2}\,dx,\quad u = x^2\;$
$\;\dfrac{du}{dx} = 2x \Rightarrow dx = \dfrac{du}{2x}\;$
1q substitution bridge
Convert x-limits to u-limits immediately on substitution
$\;\int_0^1 2x\,(x^2+1)^3\,dx,\quad u=x^2+1\;$
$\;x=0\Rightarrow u=1,\; x=1\Rightarrow u=2:\quad \int_1^2 u^3\,du = \left[\tfrac{u^4}{4}\right]_1^2 = \tfrac{15}{4}\;$
1q substitution bridge
Rearrange the substitution equation to rewrite leftover integrand pieces in u
$\;\int \dfrac{x}{(1+x)^2}\,dx,\quad u=1+x\;$
$\;x = u-1,\; dx = du \Rightarrow \int \dfrac{u-1}{u^2}\,du\;$
1q substitution bridge
Re-express x, dx, and all radical parts in the new variable u
$\;\int \frac{1}{1+\sqrt{x}}\,dx,\quad u = 1+\sqrt{x}\;$
$\;\sqrt{x}=u-1,\ x=(u-1)^2,\ dx=2(u-1)\,du \Rightarrow \int \frac{2(u-1)}{u}\,du\;$
1q substitution bridge
Invert the substitution to rewrite orphan x-factors in u
$\;\int x\sqrt{2x+1}\,dx,\quad u=2x+1\;$
$\;x=\tfrac{u-1}{2},\ dx=\tfrac{1}{2}du \Rightarrow \tfrac{1}{4}\int (u-1)\,u^{1/2}\,du = \tfrac{1}{4}\int \bigl(u^{3/2}-u^{1/2}\bigr)du\;$
1q substitution bridge

Chain-Rule Absorption

3 moves
Recognising that the integrand is a composite function whose inner function's derivative already appears as a factor, so u = inner function absorbs everything cleanly.
Substitute u equal to the inner expression to absorb its derivative
$\;\int_0^2 \frac{x}{(x+1)^2}\,dx\;$
$\;u = x+1,\; x = u-1,\; \int_1^3 \frac{u-1}{u^2}\,du = \int_1^3 \left(\frac{1}{u} - \frac{1}{u^2}\right)du\;$
2q substitution bridge
Use 'hence' to reverse part (a)'s derivative as an antiderivative
$\;\text{(a)}\ \dfrac{d}{dx}\bigl[(1+x^2)^{1/2}\bigr] = \dfrac{x}{\sqrt{1+x^2}};\quad \text{(b)}\ \int \dfrac{3x}{\sqrt{1+x^2}}\,dx\;$
$\;\int \dfrac{3x}{\sqrt{1+x^2}}\,dx = 3\,(1+x^2)^{1/2} + C\;$
1q substitution bridge
Substitute u = inner function to absorb its derivative from the numerator
$\;\int \dfrac{\cos x}{(1+\sin x)^2}\,dx\;$
$\;u = 1+\sin x,\; du = \cos x\,dx \Rightarrow \int u^{-2}\,du = -\dfrac{1}{1+\sin x}+C\;$
1q substitution bridge

$\;u=x^2+1,\; du=2x\,dx \Rightarrow \int u^3\,du = \tfrac{u^4}{4}+C = \tfrac{(x^2+1)^4}{4}+C\;$
2q substitution bridge

$\;\int \dfrac{x}{\sqrt{x+1}}\,dx,\quad u=\sqrt{x+1}\;$

Recognising When and How to Set Up IBP

2 moves

$\;\int \ln x\,dx = \int \ln x \cdot 1\,dx\;$
