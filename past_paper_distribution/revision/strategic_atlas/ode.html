Integrating Factor Method

3 moves · 10 examples
Construct integrating factor μ = e^{∫P dx} and multiply through to collapse LHS into d/dx[μy]
$\;\dfrac{dy}{dx} + \dfrac{2}{x}\,y = x\;$
$\;\mu = e^{\int 2/x\, dx} = x^{2},\quad \dfrac{d}{dx}\bigl[x^{2}\,y\bigr] = x^{3}\;$
4q integrating factor method
Form integrating factor μ = e^{∫P(x)dx} to collapse LHS into exact derivative
$\;\dfrac{dy}{dx} + \dfrac{2}{x}\,y = x^3\;$
$\;\mu = e^{\int 2/x\,dx} = e^{2\ln x} = x^2\;$
3q integrating factor method
Apply integrating factor e^{∫P(x)dx} to solve first-order linear ODE
$\;\dfrac{dy}{dx} + 3y = e^{-x}\;$
$\;\mu = e^{3x},\quad \dfrac{d}{dx}\!\left[e^{3x}y\right] = e^{2x} \Rightarrow e^{3x}y = \tfrac{1}{2}e^{2x} + C\;$
3q integrating factor method

$\;y_p = \lambda e^{2x}\ \text{overlaps CF} \Rightarrow y_p = \lambda x e^{2x}\;$
6q undetermined coefficients
Substitute trial PI into ODE and equate coefficients to determine constants
$\;y'' - 3y' + 2y = 4e^{5x},\quad y_{PI} = ae^{5x}\;$
$\;25a - 15a + 2a = 4 \Rightarrow 12a = 4 \Rightarrow a = \tfrac{1}{3}\;$
2q undetermined coefficients
Include both sin and cos in PI trial when forcing is a single trig term
$\;y'' + 3y' + 2y = \cos x\;$
$\;y_{PI} = a\cos x + b\sin x \Rightarrow (a+3b)\cos x + (-3a+b)\sin x = \cos x\;$

Separation Of Variables

4 moves · 9 examples · 2 sub-groups

Direct Separation of a Factorable ODE

3 moves
The right-hand side already visibly factors as f(y)·g(x), so the solver immediately moves all y-terms to one side and x-terms to the other and integrates.
Separate variables to reduce a first-order ODE to two independent integrals
$\;\dfrac{dy}{dx} = xy\;$
$\;\dfrac{1}{y}\,dy = x\,dx \Rightarrow \ln|y| = \dfrac{x^2}{2} + C\;$
5q separation of variables
Separate variables to split a product-form ODE into two independent integrals
$\;\dfrac{dy}{dx} = xy\;$
$\;\dfrac{1}{y}\,dy = x\,dx \Rightarrow \ln|y| = \tfrac{1}{2}x^2 + C\;$
2q separation of variables
Separate variables to isolate y and x on opposite sides
$\;\dfrac{dy}{dx} = \dfrac{x}{y}\;$
$\;y\,dy = x\,dx \Rightarrow \dfrac{y^2}{2} = \dfrac{x^2}{2} + C\;$
1q separation of variables

Trigonometric Rewrite to Enable Separation

1 moves
A trigonometric identity (e.g. tan y = sin y / cos y) must be applied first to reveal the multiplicative f(y)·g(x) structure before variables can be separated.
Rewrite tan y as sin y / cos y to separate variables
$\;\dfrac{dy}{dx} = \dfrac{\tan y}{1+x}\;$
$\;\dfrac{\cos y}{\sin y}\,dy = \dfrac{dx}{1+x}\;$
1q separation of variables

Auxiliary Equation Method

5 moves · 9 examples · 2 sub-groups

Exponential Substitution to Derive Auxiliary Equation

2 moves
These moves focus on the specific algebraic act of substituting y = e^{mx} into the ODE to convert the differential equation into a polynomial equation in m.
Substitute y = e^{mx} to form the auxiliary polynomial equation
$\;y'' - 3y' + 2y = 0\;$
$\;y = e^{mx} \Rightarrow (m^2 - 3m + 2)e^{mx} = 0 \Rightarrow m^2 - 3m + 2 = 0\;$
2q auxiliary equation method
Substitute y = e^(mx) to form the auxiliary equation
$\;y'' - 3y' + 2y = 0,\quad y = e^{mx}\;$
$\;(m^2 - 3m + 2)e^{mx} = 0 \Rightarrow m^2 - 3m + 2 = 0\;$
1q auxiliary equation method

Form Auxiliary Equation and Classify Root Type

3 moves
These moves encompass the full pipeline from forming the auxiliary equation through to selecting the complementary function form based on whether roots are real-distinct, repeated, or complex conjugate.
Form auxiliary equation to convert constant-coefficient ODE into algebra
$\;y'' + 5y' + 6y = 0\;$
$\;m^2 + 5m + 6 = 0 \Rightarrow (m+2)(m+3)=0 \Rightarrow y = Ae^{-2x} + Be^{-3x}\;$
4q auxiliary equation method
Form auxiliary equation and select CF form from root type
$\;y'' - 5y' + 6y = 0\;$
$\;m^2 - 5m + 6 = 0 \Rightarrow (m-2)(m-3)=0 \Rightarrow y = Ae^{2x} + Be^{3x}\;$
1q auxiliary equation method
Form auxiliary equation to find complementary function
$\;y'' - 5y' + 6y = 0\;$
$\;m^2 - 5m + 6 = 0 \Rightarrow (m-2)(m-3)=0 \Rightarrow y = Ae^{2x} + Be^{3x}\;$
1q auxiliary equation method
