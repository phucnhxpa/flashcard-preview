Algebra & Functions

Divide by cosh to isolate tanh, then apply artanh logarithmic form

Family: Ratio To Inverse Hyperbolic Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

Fires when an equation has reduced to the form a·cosh x = b·sinh x, or more generally any linear combination collapsing to a constant ratio of cosh and sinh. Cue phrases include 'give your answer in exact/logarithmic form' and the absence of a constant term that would force an exponential quadratic. The presence of both cosh x and sinh x with no stray constants is the signal that a single tanh evaluation will close the problem.

Motivation — Why this works

Since tanh x = sinh x / cosh x, dividing by cosh x converts the equation into tanh x = b/a, a one-step inverse hyperbolic. Edexcel's formula booklet gives artanh y = (1/2)ln((1+y)/(1-y)) for |y|<1, which delivers the exact logarithmic answer directly. This is shorter and cleaner than the e^x-substitution route, which requires forming and solving a quadratic in e^x before taking logs.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. I notice the equation is a·cosh x = b·sinh x with no loose constant — the ratio cosh:sinh is fixed.
  2. I rule out the e^x-substitution route: it works but produces a quadratic in e^{2x} when a simpler division will do.
  3. I commit to dividing by cosh x because that produces tanh x = b/a, a single artanh evaluation.
  4. I check that |b/a| < 1 so artanh is defined (otherwise no real solution exists).
  5. I apply x = (1/2)ln((1+y)/(1-y)) and simplify the internal fraction before taking the log.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. From a·cosh x = b·sinh x, divide both sides by cosh x to obtain tanh x = a/b (or b/a, depending on arrangement).
  2. Verify |y| < 1 so that artanh y is real.
  3. Apply x = (1/2) ln((1+y)/(1-y)).
  4. Combine the numerator and denominator into single fractions and simplify the ratio.
  5. Cancel common factors inside the logarithm and present in lowest terms.

Worked Micro-Example

Solve 5 cosh x = 17 sinh x exactly. Divide by cosh x: tanh x = 5/17. Then x = \tfrac{1}{2}\ln\!\left(\dfrac{1+5/17}{1-5/17}\right) = \tfrac{1}{2}\ln\!\left(\dfrac{22}{12}\right) = \tfrac{1}{2}\ln\!\left(\dfrac{11}{6}\right).

Variants — Same move, different surface

Equation reduces to cosh x = k with k ≥ 1
Use arcosh x = ln(x + √(x²−1)); note two solutions ±arcosh k if context allows.
Equation reduces to sinh x = k
Use arsinh x = ln(x + √(x²+1)); valid for all real k, unique real solution.
Equation gives a·sinh x = b·cosh x with |a|>|b|
Dividing yields tanh x = b/a with |b/a|<1; same artanh formula applies.
Reduces to coth x = k with |k|>1
Rewrite as tanh x = 1/k and apply the standard artanh formula rather than invoking arcoth.
Exponential-quadratic route required (presence of constant term)
Substitute u = e^x, multiply through by e^x, solve quadratic, then take ln of positive root(s).

Common Misreadings

  • Treating the equation like sinh x = k and reaching for arsinh instead of recognising the cosh/sinh ratio.
  • Forgetting the (1/2) factor and writing x = ln((1+y)/(1-y)).
  • Dividing by sinh x to get coth x = a/b and then stalling because arcoth is not in the standard formula list.

Pitfalls

  • Not checking |y| < 1 before applying artanh — if |y| ≥ 1 the equation has no real solution.
  • Failing to simplify (1+y)/(1-y) into lowest terms (e.g. leaving 22/12 instead of 11/6).
  • Sign slip when y is negative: writing (1−y)/(1+y) by mistake.

A-level Scope Notes

Within Edexcel IAL Further Pure: hyperbolic functions, the identity tanh = sinh/cosh, and the logarithmic forms of artanh, arsinh, arcosh are all in the formula booklet, making this the expected exact-form route.
