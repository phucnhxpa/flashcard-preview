Strategic Atlas / Integration / Area Sign Correction
Integration

Take absolute value of negative definite integral to report geometric area

Family: Area Sign Correction Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

Fires when a definite integral $\int_a^b y\,dx$ has been evaluated and produces a negative number, but the question asks for 'the area of region R' (a geometric, non-negative quantity). Also fires when inspection shows the curve lies below the x-axis on all or part of $[a,b]$. Phrasings include 'find the exact area', 'show the area is', or 'the shaded region R'. Distinct from problems asking for 'the value of the integral', which keep the sign.

Motivation — Why this works

A definite integral measures signed area: portions of the curve below the x-axis contribute negative values. Geometric area is unsigned, so a negative integral result must be reflected to its magnitude. Taking $|\int_a^b y\,dx|$ gives the correct area whenever the region lies entirely on one side of the axis, or when the mark scheme treats R as a single region whose magnitude is required. The principle is simply $\text{Area} = \int_a^b |y|\,dx$, which collapses to $|\int_a^b y\,dx|$ when $y$ does not change sign on $[a,b]$.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. I notice the integral evaluated to a negative number, but the question asked for an area.
  2. I rule out the possibility I made an arithmetic slip by re-checking the antiderivative and substitution at the limits.
  3. I commit to an area-sign correction: the negativity is geometric, not computational — the curve must lie below the x-axis on (most of) the interval.
  4. I quickly test the sign of $y$ at a sample point in $[a,b]$ to confirm the curve is below (or to detect a crossing).
  5. If sign is consistent, I report Area $= |\text{integral}|$; if there is a crossing, I consider whether the question expects the rigorous split $\int|y|\,dx$ or just $|\int y\,dx|$.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Evaluate the definite integral $\int_a^b y\,dx$ to a numerical/exact value.
  2. If the result is negative, check the sign of $y$ on $[a,b]$ by sampling or solving $y=0$.
  3. If $y \le 0$ throughout $[a,b]$ (or the question accepts net magnitude), report Area $= \big|\int_a^b y\,dx\big|$.
  4. If $y$ changes sign at $c\in(a,b)$ and rigour is required, write Area $= \big|\int_a^c y\,dx\big| + \big|\int_c^b y\,dx\big|$.
  5. State the final area as a positive exact value.

Worked Micro-Example

Find the area between $y = x^2 - 4$ and the x-axis on $[0,2]$. Compute $\int_0^2 (x^2-4)\,dx = \big[\tfrac{x^3}{3} - 4x\big]_0^2 = \tfrac{8}{3} - 8 = -\tfrac{16}{3}$. Since $y\le 0$ on $[0,2]$, Area $= \big|-\tfrac{16}{3}\big| = \tfrac{16}{3}$.

Variants — Same move, different surface

Curve entirely below the x-axis on $[a,b]$
Area $= -\int_a^b y\,dx$ directly; equivalent to taking the absolute value.
Curve crosses x-axis at $c\in(a,b)$
Split: Area $= \big|\int_a^c y\,dx\big| + \big|\int_c^b y\,dx\big|$ rather than $|\int_a^b y\,dx|$.
Area between two curves with $f(x) < g(x)$ swapping
Use $\int_a^b |f(x)-g(x)|\,dx$, splitting at intersections.
Area bounded by curve and y-axis using $\int x\,dy$
Same sign correction applied: take $|\int_c^d x\,dy|$ when integrating with respect to $y$.

Common Misreadings

  • Leaving the answer as a negative number, treating the signed integral as the area.
  • Assuming the curve is entirely above or below the axis without checking sign at a sample point.
  • Confusing 'value of the integral' (signed) with 'area of region' (unsigned).

Pitfalls

  • Taking $|\int_a^b y\,dx|$ when the curve crosses the axis: positive and negative pieces partially cancel and the magnitude understates the true geometric area.
  • Forgetting to check whether the question wants the integral's value or the area.
  • Sign errors when substituting limits into the antiderivative, which can fake a negative result that should have been positive.

A-level Scope Notes

Standard Edexcel IAL Pure (C2/P2 area under a curve, C4/P4 area between curves and parametric area). The split-at-root version is expected when a question explicitly highlights crossings or shaded sub-regions.
