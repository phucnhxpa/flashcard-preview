Complex Numbers

Reduce substituted modulus equation to Cartesian circle or line

Family: Modulus To Cartesian Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

After substituting z = g(w) into a locus |z - α| = r (or similar), the w-plane equation appears as |f(w)| = k|h(w)| or |w - α| = k|w - β|. Fires when the question asks for the image of a circle/line under a Möbius-type map, or asks to describe the locus of w geometrically. Also triggers when a modulus-of-fraction expression must be turned into a named shape (circle with centre/radius, or perpendicular bisector).

Motivation — Why this works

The identity |A/B| = |A|/|B| lets us split a modulus of a fraction into a ratio, converting the substituted equation into the Apollonius form |w - p| = k|w - q|. When k = 1 this is the perpendicular bisector of pq (a line); when k ≠ 1 it is an Apollonius circle. Squaring both sides removes the square roots inherent in |·|, and writing w = u + iv yields a polynomial equation in u, v whose u² + v² coefficient determines whether the locus is a circle (nonzero) or a line (zero).

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. I notice the substituted equation has a single fraction inside a modulus equal to a constant, so the natural first move is to clear the denominator using |A/B| = |A|/|B|.
  2. I rule out trying to interpret the equation geometrically straight away, because the constants α, β in |w - α| = k|w - β| are not yet visible until I tidy the algebra.
  3. I commit to the Cartesian route: write w = u + iv, square both sides, and let the algebra decide whether u² + v² survives (circle) or cancels (line).
  4. The first concrete operation is to combine the substituted expression into one fraction (P + Qw)/(R + Sw), then separate moduli to get |P + Qw| = r|R + Sw|.
  5. Inside the move I watch the coefficient of u² + v² after squaring: if nonzero I divide through to make it 1 and complete the square; if zero I just rearrange to ax + by + c = 0.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Combine the substituted expression into a single fraction: |(P + Qw)/(R + Sw)| = r.
  2. Apply |A/B| = |A|/|B| to get |P + Qw| = r|R + Sw|.
  3. Substitute w = u + iv so each side becomes √(real² + imag²).
  4. Square both sides to eliminate the square roots.
  5. Expand and collect terms in u², v², u, v, and constants.
  6. If u² + v² coefficient ≠ 0: divide through, complete the square, read off centre and radius.
  7. If u² + v² terms cancel: rearrange to linear form au + bv + c = 0 and identify the line.

Worked Micro-Example

Given |2w - 1| = |w + 1|, square both sides: (2u-1)² + (2v)² = (u+1)² + v². Expand: 4u² - 4u + 1 + 4v² = u² + 2u + 1 + v². Simplify: 3u² + 3v² - 6u = 0, so u² + v² - 2u = 0, giving (u-1)² + v² = 1 — a circle centre (1, 0), radius 1.

Variants — Same move, different surface

Substituted equation has form |w - α| = |w - β| (k = 1)
u² + v² terms cancel automatically; result is the perpendicular bisector of αβ — solve directly as a linear equation in u, v.
Original locus is arg(z - a) = θ rather than |z - a| = r
After substitution, use arg(A/B) = arg A - arg B; equate to θ and take tangents to obtain a linear (half-line) Cartesian equation in u, v.
Original locus is a line Re(z) = c or Im(z) = c
Substitute z = g(w), then take real or imaginary part of the resulting expression in u, v; typically yields a circle through the image of ∞.
Modulus inequality |z - α| < r instead of equality
Same algebraic reduction but keep the inequality through squaring (valid since both sides ≥ 0); final answer is the interior/exterior of the circle or a half-plane.

Common Misreadings

  • Treating |P + Qw| = r|R + Sw| as P + Qw = r(R + Sw), dropping the moduli entirely.
  • Assuming the image must be a circle and forcing completion of the square even when u² + v² terms have cancelled to give a line.
  • Confusing the Apollonius circle centre with the midpoint of α and β (it is not the midpoint unless k = 1, which gives a line not a circle).

Pitfalls

  • Algebraic errors expanding |P + Q(u+iv)|² — especially missing the cross-term 2(real·imag) contributions.
  • Forgetting to divide through by the common coefficient of u² and v² before completing the square.
  • Mis-squaring r|R + Sw| as r|R + Sw|² instead of r²|R + Sw|².
  • Stopping at an unsimplified Cartesian equation without identifying centre/radius or line equation as the question demands.

A-level Scope Notes

Fully within Edexcel IAL Further Pure scope: loci in the Argand diagram, Apollonius circles, and Möbius-type transformations w = (az+b)/(cz+d) with images being circles or straight lines.
