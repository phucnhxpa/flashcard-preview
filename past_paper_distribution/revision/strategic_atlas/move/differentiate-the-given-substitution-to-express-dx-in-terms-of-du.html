Strategic Atlas / Integration / Substitution Bridge
Integration

Differentiate the given substitution to express dx in terms of du

Family: Substitution Bridge Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

Fires whenever a question prescribes a substitution (e.g. 'use the substitution u = ...') for an integral. The very first task, before rewriting the integrand or limits, is to build the bridge between dx and du. Cues include explicit phrasing 'using u = ...', 'by means of the substitution', or seeing a composite expression in the integrand that matches the proposed u. Also fires when you choose your own u and need to swap differentials.

Motivation — Why this works

An integral in x cannot be evaluated in u-world until every x-piece — including the differential dx — has been replaced. Implicitly differentiating the given relation u = f(x) gives du/dx = f'(x), which rearranges to dx = du/f'(x). This is the mechanical bridge that licenses the change of variable, and the factor f'(x) it introduces is usually exactly what cancels a matching factor already in the integrand, which is why the substitution was chosen.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. I notice the substitution is given, so the choice of u is fixed — my job is to convert dx, not to invent a strategy.
  2. I rule out trying to substitute piecemeal (replacing only some x's with u) because the differential must be converted too, or the integral is meaningless.
  3. I commit to differentiating u with respect to x, rewriting any surds or roots as fractional powers so the power rule applies cleanly.
  4. After getting du/dx, I rearrange algebraically to isolate dx = (something) du, keeping that 'something' in a form that will visibly cancel with factors in the original integrand.
  5. I scan the integrand for the factor f'(x) that has just appeared — its presence confirms the substitution will simplify, and I plan the cancellation.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Rewrite u in a form ready to differentiate (e.g. \sqrt{x} as x^{1/2}).
  2. Differentiate implicitly: compute du/dx = f'(x).
  3. Rearrange to isolate dx: dx = du / f'(x), or equivalently dx = (1/f'(x)) du.
  4. Keep the expression in a form that exposes cancellation with the integrand (don't prematurely simplify away a useful factor).
  5. Hold this dx-expression ready to substitute alongside the rewritten integrand.

Worked Micro-Example

Given \int \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}(1+\sqrt{x})}\,dx with u = 1+\sqrt{x}. Differentiate: \frac{du}{dx} = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}}, so dx = 2\sqrt{x}\,du. Substituting, the \sqrt{x} cancels: \int \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}\cdot u}\cdot 2\sqrt{x}\,du = \int \frac{2}{u}\,du = 2\ln|u| + C.

Variants — Same move, different surface

Trigonometric substitution u = 1 + \sin x
Differentiate to get du = \cos x\,dx, so dx = du/\cos x; look for a \cos x in the integrand to cancel.
Exponential substitution u = e^x + 1
du = e^x\,dx gives dx = du/e^x; the e^x factor in the integrand cancels directly.
Logarithmic substitution u = \ln x
du = dx/x gives dx = x\,du, and the 1/x already in the integrand cancels with the x.
Polynomial-inside-bracket u = x^2 + 1
du = 2x\,dx gives dx = du/(2x); the 2x (or x with a constant adjustment) must be present in the integrand.
Inverse-trig style u = \arctan x
du = dx/(1+x^2) gives dx = (1+x^2)\,du, used when 1/(1+x^2) appears as a factor.

Common Misreadings

  • Treating du/dx as a literal fraction without justification — in A-level work, the rearrangement dx = du/(du/dx) is accepted as shorthand for the chain rule and should be written carefully.
  • Thinking the substitution alone replaces x everywhere and forgetting that dx is a separate object that must also be converted.
  • Believing du and dx are interchangeable, leading to the integral being left with mixed differentials.

Pitfalls

  • Sign or coefficient errors when differentiating fractional powers, especially dropping the 1/2 in \frac{d}{dx}(x^{1/2}).
  • Simplifying dx = 2\sqrt{x}\,du back into x-form too early, hiding the cancellation that motivates the substitution.
  • Forgetting to also transform the limits when the integral is definite (a separate but linked move).
  • Algebraic slips when inverting du/dx, e.g. writing dx = 2\sqrt{x} when it should be dx = 2\sqrt{x}\,du.

A-level Scope Notes

Fully within Edexcel IAL Pure Mathematics (C4/P4 integration by substitution). Uses only standard differentiation rules and treats du/dx rearrangement as accepted A-level notation.
