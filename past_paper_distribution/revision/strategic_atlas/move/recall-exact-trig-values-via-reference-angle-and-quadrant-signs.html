Trigonometry

Recall exact trig values via reference angle and quadrant signs

Family: Exact Value Recall Frequency: 2 questions

Trigger — When to use

Fires whenever a trig function is evaluated at a 'special' angle — either a standard angle (π/6, π/4, π/3) or one related to a standard angle by π ± α, 2π − α, or −α (e.g. 2π/3, 3π/4, 5π/6, 7π/6). The cue is usually: substitute a specific x-value into a trig expression, find a y-intercept, or evaluate sin/cos/tan inside a chord/area formula. The expected answer is in EXACT form (surds, fractions of π) rather than a decimal — often signalled by 'give your answer in exact form' or a follow-up calculation that must remain exact.

Motivation — Why this works

The unit-circle exact values for π/6, π/4, π/3 are memorised, and any angle in [0, 2π] that shares a reference angle with one of these inherits the same magnitude — only the sign changes according to the quadrant (CAST rule). Using exact values preserves precision, keeps surd forms intact for downstream calculations, and is required for full marks on Edexcel papers where decimal approximations are penalised. The underlying principle is the symmetry of sine and cosine about the axes, encoded in identities like sin(π − α) = sin α and cos(π − α) = −cos α.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. I notice the angle inside the trig function is a 'nice' fraction of π — either standard (π/4 here) or a simple multiple like 2π/3, 5π/6.
  2. I rule out reaching for a calculator: the question asks for exact form, and a decimal would forfeit marks; I also rule out trying to expand via compound-angle identities unless the angle truly demands it.
  3. I commit to the reference-angle method because every angle in [0, 2π] reduces to one of π/6, π/4, π/3 (or 0, π/2) up to a sign.
  4. Bridge: I rewrite the angle as π ± α or 2π − α (or recognise it directly as standard), identify the reference angle α, and locate the quadrant.
  5. Tactical decision: apply CAST — sine positive in Q1/Q2, cosine positive in Q1/Q4, tangent positive in Q1/Q3 — to attach the correct sign to the standard magnitude.
  6. Finally I substitute the exact surd back into the surrounding expression and simplify, keeping √2, √3 in surd form (rationalising denominators if needed).

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Identify the angle θ and write it as π ± α, 2π − α, or recognise it as a standard angle directly; let α be the reference angle.
  2. Determine the quadrant of θ (Q1: 0 < θ < π/2; Q2: π/2 < θ < π; Q3: π < θ < 3π/2; Q4: 3π/2 < θ < 2π).
  3. Recall the standard exact value: sin(π/6)=1/2, sin(π/4)=√2/2, sin(π/3)=√3/2 (and cos values mirror these).
  4. Apply CAST to attach the correct sign: e.g. cos(2π/3) = −cos(π/3) = −1/2; sin(2π/3) = +sin(π/3) = √3/2.
  5. Substitute the exact value into the original expression and simplify, leaving the answer in surd/fractional form.

Worked Micro-Example

Evaluate $y = 3\cos(x + \pi/4)$ at $x = 0$. Substitute: $y = 3\cos(\pi/4)$. Recall $\cos(\pi/4) = \tfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$. Hence $y = 3 \cdot \tfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2} = \tfrac{3\sqrt{2}}{2}$. Similarly $\cos(2\pi/3) = -\cos(\pi/3) = -\tfrac{1}{2}$ since $2\pi/3$ is in Q2.

Variants — Same move, different surface

Angle of the form π ± α (Q2 or Q3)
Reference angle is α; sin keeps sign in Q2, flips in Q3; cos flips in both.
Angle of the form 2π − α or −α (Q4)
Reference angle is α; cos keeps sign, sin flips (sin is odd, cos is even).
Surd denominator like 1/√2 appears
Rationalise by multiplying top and bottom by √2 to get √2/2 — Edexcel accepts either form but √2/2 is cleaner.
Tangent at a special angle
Use tan = sin/cos with the same reference-angle/quadrant method; tan(π/3)=√3, tan(π/6)=√3/3, tan(π/4)=1.

Common Misreadings

  • Treating 2π/3 (or any Q2 angle) as if it were in Q1 and forgetting the sign change on cosine.
  • Confusing cos(π/4) with cos(π/3): writing 1/2 instead of √2/2 (or vice versa).
  • Reading the angle in degrees when it is in radians (calculator mode error).

Pitfalls

  • Negating sine in Q2 — only cosine and tangent flip there.
  • Leaving the answer as 1/√2 without rationalising to √2/2 when the mark scheme expects rationalised surds.
  • Premature decimal conversion that propagates rounding error into a later exact-form calculation.

A-level Scope Notes

Fully within Edexcel IAL Pure Maths (P2/P3). The exact values for π/6, π/4, π/3 are NOT given in the formula booklet — they must be memorised. The reference-angle/quadrant logic is the standard approach taught for trig of any angle; avoid invoking complex-exponential definitions, which are out of scope.
