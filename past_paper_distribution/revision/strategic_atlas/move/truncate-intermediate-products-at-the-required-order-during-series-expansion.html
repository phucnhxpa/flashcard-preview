Truncate intermediate products at the required order during series expansion

Family: Order Truncation Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger â€” When to use

The question states 'up to and including the term in x^n', 'first four non-zero terms', or 'in ascending powers of x as far as the term in x^n'. Intermediate work involves multiplying two binomial/Maclaurin expansions, squaring a series, or substituting one series into another. Cues include products like (1+ax)^p(1+bx)^q expanded together, or composite forms like ln(1+f(x)) where f(x) is itself a series. Whenever the natural algebra would generate x^{n+1}, x^{n+2}, â€¦ terms, this move applies.

Motivation â€” Why this works

Each cross-product in a series multiplication contributes to a specific power of x, and any contribution above x^n cannot affect the requested answer. Discarding such terms the moment they arise keeps expressions short and reduces arithmetic error. The underlying principle is that O(x^{n+1}) is closed under addition and under multiplication by bounded series, so truncated arithmetic is exact up to the stated order. This mirrors how the binomial/Maclaurin remainder behaves and is the standard convention in IAL series work.

  2. Expand each individual series only as far as x^n, appending '+ â€¦' or '+ O(x^{n+1})'.

Worked Micro-Example

Find $(1+2x)^{1/2}(1-x)^{-1}$ up to $x^2$. $(1+2x)^{1/2} = 1 + x - \tfrac{1}{2}x^2 + \dots$ $(1-x)^{-1} = 1 + x + x^2 + \dots$ Multiplying and keeping only terms with power $\le 2$: $1 + (1+1)x + (-\tfrac{1}{2}+1+1)x^2 = 1 + 2x + \tfrac{3}{2}x^2$.

Variants â€” Same move, different surface

Squaring a single series, e.g. $(1+x-x^2+\dots)^2$ to order $x^3$
Only pair terms whose degree-sum â‰¤ 3; the original series itself need only be carried to $x^3$.
Composite expansion such as $\ln(1+f(x))$ where $f(x) = 2x - x^2 + \dots$, to order $x^3$
Since $f = O(x)$, $f^2 = O(x^2)$ and $f^3 = O(x^3)$, so keep $f$ to $x^3$, $f^2$ to $x^3$, and $f^3$ only its leading $x^3$ term.
Maclaurin series of products like $e^x \sin x$ to order $x^4$
Take each standard series to $x^4$, then convolve degree-by-degree, discarding any $x^k$ with $k>4$.
Approximation questions ending '...hence find an approximation for f(0.1)'
Same truncation logic for the symbolic step; afterwards substitute the numerical value into the truncated polynomial only.

Common Misreadings

  • Reading 'first four terms' as 'first four non-zero terms' (or vice versa) and truncating at the wrong place.
  • Assuming truncation means dropping the highest term currently visible, rather than dropping everything strictly above x^n.
  • Thinking each factor must be expanded to the same depth as the target n, when in fact composite/inner series may need fewer or more terms depending on their lowest power.

Pitfalls

  • Truncating an inner series too early so that a needed cross-product at exactly x^n is lost (e.g. dropping the x^2 term of u when u^2 still contributes at x^2 via 2Â·1Â·u).
  • Forgetting that a constant term in one factor multiplies every term of the other, so the other factor must be carried fully to x^n.
  • Dropping the boundary term x^n itself by mistake when collecting.
  • Mixing up validity range of the binomial expansion with truncation order â€” these are independent issues.
