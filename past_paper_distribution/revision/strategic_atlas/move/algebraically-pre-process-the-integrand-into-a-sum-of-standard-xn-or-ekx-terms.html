Integration

Algebraically pre-process the integrand into a sum of standard x^n or e^{kx} terms

Family: Algebraic Preparation Frequency: 2 questions

Trigger вҖ” When to use

The integrand is presented in a compact form that hides its true structure: a single fraction p(x)/(ax^k) with a polynomial numerator over a monomial denominator, or an expression like (f(x))^2 where f contains an exponential e^{g(x)}. Equivalently: 'before integrating, can I split or expand this into separate terms each matching a standard rule?'

Motivation вҖ” Why this works

The power rule вҲ«x^n dx = x^{n+1}/(n+1) (n вү  вҲ’1) and вҲ«e^{kx} dx = e^{kx}/k only apply to clean monomial or exponential forms вҖ” not to fractions or squared products as written. Splitting a fraction term-by-term, or applying the index law (e^a)^2 = e^{2a}, converts the integrand into a SUM of such standard forms, each integrable directly or via a tractable IBP. This is almost always the cheapest first step: it sidesteps substitution and partial fractions entirely, or it cleans up an integrand so the heavier technique (IBP, reduction) becomes routine.

Thought Process вҖ” The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Look at the integrand and ask: does it match a standard form (x^n, e^{kx}, sin/cos, 1/x) directly? If not, can simple algebra get it there?
  2. Rule out heavier tools: substitution is unwarranted if there is no inner-function/derivative pairing; partial fractions need a factored polynomial denominator (not a single x^k); trig identities are irrelevant here.
  3. Spot the structural giveaway вҖ” either a single denominator dividing every numerator term, or a squared expression that can be expanded using index laws like (e^{a})^2 = e^{2a} and (cx)^2 = cВІxВІ.
  4. Commit to the move because it strictly simplifies and never introduces new variables or constants of integration to track.
  5. Execute the algebra carefully: divide each numerator term separately, OR square each factor separately, watching signs and constants.
  6. Re-read the new integrand and confirm each term is now a standard form before reaching for the integration rule.

Mechanic вҖ” Concrete operations

  1. Identify the algebraic obstacle: a fraction p(x)/(ax^k), or a power (cf(x))^n where f involves e^{g(x)}.
  2. If a fraction: split as ОЈ (numerator term)/(ax^k), then simplify each quotient using x^a/x^b = x^{aвҲ’b} to obtain a sum of Оұx^n terms.
