Algebra & Functions

Multiply through by the denominator to match the show-that target form

Family: Target Form Manipulation Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

Fires in 'show that' problems where the working has produced an equation containing a 1/t (or 1/m) factor, while the prescribed target form has t (or m) multiplying one of the variables. Typical surface: after substituting a parametric gradient into point-gradient form, you obtain y − 2at = (1/t)(x − at²) but must reach ty = x + at². The mismatch between a denominator on one side and a coefficient on the other side of the target is the cue. Equivalent phrasings: 'clear the fraction', 'scale the equation by t'.

Motivation — Why this works

The target form is a linear combination of the variables with specific coefficients, and any equivalent equation reaches it by a single scalar multiplication followed by collecting like terms. Multiplying both sides by t simultaneously eliminates the 1/t and produces the ty coefficient demanded by the target, so two cosmetic obligations are discharged in one step. This is more reliable than expanding the right-hand side first, because every term then has to be re-multiplied later anyway. The underlying principle is that 'show that' answers are unique up to algebraic equivalence, so choosing the multiplier that matches the target minimises rearrangement.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notice the current equation has a 1/t on the right while the target has ty (not y) on the left — the denominator and the missing factor are both t.
  2. Rule out expanding (1/t)(x − at²) first: that would split into x/t − at, leaving fractions to clear later and inviting sign slips.
  3. Commit to multiplying both sides by t because it kills the 1/t and creates the ty term in one stroke.
  4. Bridge: distribute t carefully across the left side y − 2at, getting ty − 2at².
  5. Tactically, after multiplying, only a single rearrangement (moving −at² across) is needed to land on ty = x + at².

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Identify the multiplier that matches the target: here, t, because the target has ty and the working has 1/t.
  2. Multiply both sides of y − 2at = (1/t)(x − at²) by t.
  3. Distribute on the left: ty − 2at² = x − at².
  4. Add 2at² to both sides: ty = x − at² + 2at² = x + at².
  5. Confirm the result matches the prescribed target form exactly.

Worked Micro-Example

Given $y - 2at = \tfrac{1}{t}(x - at^2)$, multiply both sides by $t$: $ty - 2at^2 = x - at^2$. Adding $2at^2$ to both sides gives $ty = x + at^2$, the required form.

Variants — Same move, different surface

Tangent to a rectangular hyperbola xy = c² at (ct, c/t), target form x + t²y = 2ct.
After point-gradient substitution you get y − c/t = −(1/t²)(x − ct); multiply through by t² to clear the denominator and produce the t²y term.
Normal to a parabola at (at², 2at), target form y + tx = 2at + at³.
The gradient is −t (no fraction), so no multiplication is needed — recognise that the move only fires when a denominator is present.
Chord/tangent on an ellipse with parametric point (a cosθ, b sinθ).
Multiply through by sinθ or cosθ as dictated by which trig term appears in the target's coefficients.
Show-that equations involving 1/m gradients in coordinate geometry.
Multiply by m to convert 1/m factors into the required my term.

Common Misreadings

  • Reading the 1/t as a constant and trying to absorb it into a, rather than seeing it as the multiplier signal.
  • Thinking the move is 'cross-multiply' (a two-sided fraction operation) when actually only one side has the fraction.
  • Assuming you must first expand brackets before scaling, doubling the work.

Pitfalls

  • Forgetting to multiply every term on the left by t — a common slip is writing ty − 2at instead of ty − 2at².
  • Sign error when moving −at² across to combine with +2at²·... resulting in −at² instead of +at².
  • Multiplying only one side, breaking equality.
  • Cancelling t prematurely on the right before distributing on the left.

A-level Scope Notes

Standard technique in Edexcel IAL Further Pure coordinate geometry (parabola, rectangular hyperbola, ellipse parametric tangent/normal proofs). No content beyond A-level scope is invoked.
