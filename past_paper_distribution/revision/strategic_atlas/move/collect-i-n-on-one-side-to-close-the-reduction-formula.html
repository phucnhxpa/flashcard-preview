Algebra & Functions

Collect I_n on one side to close the reduction formula

Family: Recurrence Isolation Frequency: 2 questions

Trigger — When to use

Fires during a reduction-formula derivation (typically after integration by parts and a Pythagorean/identity substitution such as cos²x = 1 − sin²x or sec²x = 1 + tan²x) when the working produces an equation in which the target integral I_n appears on BOTH sides, e.g. I_n = (n−1)I_{n−2} − (n−1)I_n. The visual cue is a 'circular-looking' equation where I_n is multiplied by different coefficients on the left and right. Equivalently: any time you see the unknown integral on both sides of an equals sign with non-cancelling coefficients, this move is the resolution. This is the hallmark step that converts an implicit identity into an explicit recurrence.

Motivation — Why this works

The move works because I_n can be treated as a single algebraic unknown: collecting like terms turns an implicit relation into an explicit formula linking I_n to I_{n−2}. The underlying principle is just linear algebra — solve a I_n = b I_{n−2} + c I_n for I_n by gathering coefficients. Without this step the integration-by-parts work is wasted: the formula remains circular and cannot be used to recursively evaluate I_0, I_1, I_2, … . Completing the move yields the clean two-term recurrence required by the question (and by the mark scheme).

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. I notice I_n appears on the left and a multiple of I_n (e.g. −(n−1)I_n) appears on the right after the identity substitution.
  2. I rule out trying further integration by parts or another substitution — that would just reintroduce I_n; the equation is already closed, just not yet solved.
  3. I commit to treating I_n as an algebraic unknown, exactly as I would solve 'x = a − bx' for x: gather all I_n terms on one side.
  4. Concretely, I add (n−1)I_n to both sides so that the right-hand side contains only I_{n−2}.
  5. I factor the coefficient on the left carefully: 1·I_n + (n−1)·I_n = n·I_n (the leading 1 is easy to forget).
  6. Finally, I divide by n to present the answer in the standard I_n = … form expected by the mark scheme.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Start from I_n = (n−1)I_{n−2} − (n−1)I_n.
  2. Add (n−1)I_n to both sides: I_n + (n−1)I_n = (n−1)I_{n−2}.
  3. Combine like terms on the left: nI_n = (n−1)I_{n−2}.
  4. Divide both sides by n: I_n = ((n−1)/n) I_{n−2}.

Worked Micro-Example

After IBP and using $\cos^2 x = 1 - \sin^2 x$ on $I_n = \int_0^{\pi/2} \sin^n x\,dx$, one obtains $I_n = (n-1)I_{n-2} - (n-1)I_n$. Collecting: $nI_n = (n-1)I_{n-2}$, hence $I_n = \dfrac{n-1}{n} I_{n-2}$.

Variants — Same move, different surface

Reduction formula with a non-unit coefficient on the left, e.g. after IBP you get 2I_n = … − 3I_n
Same logic: add 3I_n to both sides giving 5I_n = …, then divide by 5. The leading coefficient is no longer 1 but the collection step is identical.
tan^n x or sec^n x reductions using 1 + tan²x = sec²x
The identity substitution produces I_n + I_{n−2} (additive, not subtractive); collect to get (something)I_n = … then isolate. Same algebraic move, different sign pattern.
Reduction formulae involving an extra constant term, e.g. I_n = A − (n−1)I_n
Still collect I_n: nI_n = A, so I_n = A/n. The presence of a constant A doesn't change the isolation step.

Common Misreadings

  • Thinking the equation is 'circular' / a dead end and abandoning the derivation, when in fact the repeated I_n is precisely what makes a reduction formula solvable.
  • Mistaking the equation for an identity in n and trying to equate coefficients, rather than solving for I_n algebraically.

Pitfalls

  • Arithmetic slip: writing 1 + (n−1) = n−1 instead of n — forgetting the coefficient of the first I_n is 1.
  • Sign error when moving −(n−1)I_n across the equals sign (it becomes +(n−1)I_n).
  • Leaving the answer as nI_n = (n−1)I_{n−2} when the mark scheme demands the explicit I_n = ((n−1)/n) I_{n−2} form.

A-level Scope Notes

Standard Edexcel IAL Further Pure 2/3 reduction-formula technique; no formula booklet entry is invoked — this is a pure algebraic manipulation. Stay within deriving and applying recurrences for ∫sin^n x dx, ∫cos^n x dx, ∫tan^n x dx, ∫x^n e^x dx and similar; do not stray into solving general linear difference equations, which is out of scope.
