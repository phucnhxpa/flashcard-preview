Trigonometry

Convert a·cosθ + b·sinθ to R-form by expanding and matching coefficients

Family: Harmonic Form Conversion Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

The expression is a linear combination a·cosθ ± b·sinθ with constant coefficients, and the question explicitly asks for it in the form R cos(θ ± α) or R sin(θ ± α) with R > 0 and α acute. Typical phrasings include 'express in the form…', 'hence find the maximum value', or 'solve a·cosθ + b·sinθ = k'. The cue is a two-term sinusoid in a single angle θ that needs collapsing into one trig term.

Motivation — Why this works

The compound-angle identity R cos(θ + α) = R cosα·cosθ − R sinα·sinθ is itself a linear combination of cosθ and sinθ. So any a·cosθ + b·sinθ can be matched to it by choosing R and α to satisfy two simultaneous equations. Squaring and adding the equations gives R² = a² + b² (Pythagoras), and dividing gives tanα = (sinθ-coefficient ratio). This single-term form is what makes amplitude, max/min, and equation-solving immediate.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notice the expression is a·cosθ + b·sinθ — a linear combination in a single variable θ — and that the question prescribes a target form R cos(θ + α).
  2. Rule out direct factoring or t-substitution: the prescribed form is a hint that the compound-angle expansion is the intended route.
  3. Commit to expanding the target form via the addition formula, because matching coefficients reduces the problem to two linear equations in R cosα and R sinα.
  4. Bridge: write R cos(θ + α) = R cosα·cosθ − R sinα·sinθ and line up with the original expression term-by-term, being careful with signs.
  5. Tactically, square-and-add to get R, then divide to get tanα; finally check the quadrant of α from the signs of R cosα and R sinα.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Expand the target form: R cos(θ + α) = R cosα·cosθ − R sinα·sinθ.
  2. Match the cosθ coefficient: R cosα = a.
  3. Match the sinθ coefficient, tracking signs: −R sinα = b, so R sinα = −b (or +b if original had −b·sinθ).
  4. Square and add: R² = (R cosα)² + (R sinα)² = a² + b²; take R = √(a² + b²) (positive root).
  5. Divide: tanα = (R sinα)/(R cosα), giving α = arctan(…).
  6. Check the quadrant of α using the signs of R cosα and R sinα; state α to required accuracy in the requested unit (radians or degrees).

Worked Micro-Example

Express $5\cos\theta - 4\sin\theta$ in the form $R\cos(\theta + \alpha)$. Expand: $R\cos(\theta+\alpha) = R\cos\alpha\cos\theta - R\sin\alpha\sin\theta$. Match: $R\cos\alpha = 5$, $R\sin\alpha = 4$. Then $R = \sqrt{5^2 + 4^2} = \sqrt{41}$ and $\tan\alpha = \tfrac{4}{5}$, so $\alpha \approx 0.6747$ rad. Hence $5\cos\theta - 4\sin\theta = \sqrt{41}\cos(\theta + 0.6747)$.

Variants — Same move, different surface

Target form R sin(θ + α) with a·sinθ + b·cosθ
Expand R sin(θ + α) = R sinθ cosα + R cosθ sinα; match R cosα = a (sinθ coefficient) and R sinα = b (cosθ coefficient).
Target form R cos(θ − α) with a·cosθ + b·sinθ (both positive)
Expand R cos(θ − α) = R cosα cosθ + R sinα sinθ, so R cosα = a and R sinα = b — keeps α acute and positive.
Target form R sin(θ − α) with a·sinθ − b·cosθ
Expand R sin(θ − α) = R sinθ cosα − R cosθ sinα; match R cosα = a and R sinα = b.
Follow-up: solve a·cosθ + b·sinθ = k
Convert to R cos(θ + α) = k, then θ + α = ±arccos(k/R) + 2πn; subtract α and select roots in the required interval.
Follow-up: max/min of a·cosθ + b·sinθ
Once in R-form, max = R and min = −R, attained when the inner trig equals ±1.

Common Misreadings

  • Reading −b·sinθ as giving R sinα = −b instead of +b, missing the minus sign already inside the expansion of cos(θ + α).
  • Choosing the wrong target-form template (e.g. expanding cos(θ − α) when the question says cos(θ + α)).
  • Treating α as freely signed rather than acute/positive as the question typically demands.

Pitfalls

  • Forgetting to verify the quadrant of α: tanα alone is ambiguous; the signs of R cosα and R sinα fix it.
  • Mixing radians and degrees — always match the unit specified in the question.
  • Leaving R as ±√(a²+b²); R is defined positive, so take the positive root only.
  • Rounding α too early and propagating the error into a subsequent equation-solving part.

A-level Scope Notes

Standard Edexcel IAL P3/P4 (Pure) content: harmonic form using compound-angle identities, with α typically acute and R positive. Used for amplitude, max/min, and solving a·cosθ + b·sinθ = k on a given interval.
