Trigonometry

Multiply through by cos θ to clear sec θ and form sin 2θ

Family: Strategic Multiplication Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

Fires when an equation contains the pair 2 sin θ on one side and sec θ (or 1/cos θ) on the other, while the target form is expressed in 2θ functions such as sin 2θ, cot 2θ, or cosec 2θ. The cue is the simultaneous presence of a single-angle factor 2 sin θ and a reciprocal of cos θ, hinting at the double-angle identity sin 2θ = 2 sin θ cos θ. Also triggers when the question explicitly asks you to recast a θ/2θ-mixed equation into a pure 2θ equation.

Motivation — Why this works

Multiplying both sides by cos θ does two useful things at once: it cancels the cos θ in the denominator of sec θ (turning that side into a constant or pure 2θ expression), and it converts 2 sin θ on the other side into 2 sin θ cos θ = sin 2θ. This single move replaces a θ/2θ-mixed equation with a clean equation in 2θ only, which is exactly what is needed before applying identities like 1 + cot² 2θ = cosec² 2θ. It is valid because sec θ already restricts the domain to cos θ ≠ 0, so no solutions are lost.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notice the structural mismatch: left side has 2 sin θ multiplied by a 2θ-expression, right side has sec θ — but the target form is purely in 2θ.
  2. Rule out converting cot 2θ back to θ via double-angle formulas — this is algebraically heavy and goes the wrong direction.
  3. Commit to a move that promotes θ to 2θ rather than demoting 2θ to θ, since the target is in 2θ.
  4. Spot that multiplying by cos θ simultaneously kills sec θ and turns 2 sin θ into sin 2θ via the double-angle identity — a single operation achieves both conversions.
  5. Confirm validity: sec θ in the original already forces cos θ ≠ 0, so multiplying by cos θ introduces no spurious roots.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Rewrite reciprocal/quotient functions explicitly: sec θ = 1/cos θ, cot 2θ = cos 2θ / sin 2θ.
  2. Multiply both sides of the equation by cos θ.
  3. On the side with sec θ, cos θ · (1/cos θ) cancels, leaving the constant or remaining 2θ-terms.
  4. On the side with 2 sin θ, group as (2 sin θ cos θ) · (rest) and replace 2 sin θ cos θ by sin 2θ.
  5. Resulting equation is purely in 2θ; proceed with Pythagorean identities such as 1 + cot² 2θ = cosec² 2θ.

Worked Micro-Example

Solve $2\sin\theta\,(3\cot^2 2\theta - 7) = 13\sec\theta$.\n\nMultiply both sides by $\cos\theta$: $2\sin\theta\cos\theta\,(3\cot^2 2\theta - 7) = 13$.\n\nUse $2\sin\theta\cos\theta = \sin 2\theta$: $\sin 2\theta\,(3\cot^2 2\theta - 7) = 13$.\n\nNow expand $\sin 2\theta \cdot \cot^2 2\theta = \cos^2 2\theta / \sin 2\theta$ and use $1+\cot^2 2\theta = \csc^2 2\theta$ to get a quadratic in $\csc 2\theta$.

Variants — Same move, different surface

Equation contains 2 cos θ on one side and cosec θ = 1/sin θ on the other.
Multiply through by sin θ; the 2 cos θ becomes 2 sin θ cos θ = sin 2θ and the cosec θ collapses.
Equation has sin θ ± cos θ structure with a sec θ or cosec θ factor and a 2θ target.
Multiply by cos θ (or sin θ) to clear the reciprocal, then use sin 2θ or cos 2θ = cos²θ − sin²θ to recast in 2θ.
Equation mixes tan θ with a constant and a 2θ-expression.
Multiply through by cos θ to convert tan θ into sin θ, then look for sin 2θ or cos 2θ groupings.

Common Misreadings

  • Trying to expand cot 2θ in terms of θ via cot 2θ = (cos²θ − sin²θ)/(2 sin θ cos θ), which produces a much messier equation.
  • Multiplying by sin² 2θ first to clear the cot² 2θ fraction; this clears denominators but leaves the θ vs 2θ mismatch unresolved.
  • Treating sec θ as if it were sec 2θ and trying to pair it directly with cot 2θ.

Pitfalls

  • Forgetting to state or check that cos θ ≠ 0 (already guaranteed by sec θ existing, but worth noting).
  • Mis-grouping: writing 2 sin θ · cos θ · cot² 2θ but failing to identify the 2 sin θ cos θ block as sin 2θ before simplifying.
  • Dividing by sin 2θ later without checking sin 2θ ≠ 0, which can lose valid solutions.

A-level Scope Notes

Uses only the double-angle identity sin 2θ = 2 sin θ cos θ and reciprocal/Pythagorean identities, all standard in Edexcel IAL P3/P4 trigonometry.
