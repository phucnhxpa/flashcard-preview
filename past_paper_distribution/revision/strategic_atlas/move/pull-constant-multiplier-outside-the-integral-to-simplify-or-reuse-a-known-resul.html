Strategic Atlas / Integration / Constant Factor Extraction
Integration

Pull constant multiplier outside the integral to simplify or reuse a known result

Family: Constant Factor Extraction Frequency: 2 questions

Trigger — When to use

Fires whenever the integrand contains a numerical (or otherwise x-independent) multiplicative factor — for example ∫ ½ log₁₀x dx, ∫ 2xeˣ dx, ∫ 3 sin x dx. Two distinct surface cues: (i) the new integral is a scalar multiple of one whose value is already known (e.g. 'using your answer to part (a)'); (ii) a previous step (such as differentiating x² in IBP) has produced a coefficient that will be carried through several more lines of working. In either case the constant should be extracted before any further technique (trapezium rule, IBP, substitution) is applied.

Motivation — Why this works

The linearity of integration, ∫ k·f(x) dx = k·∫ f(x) dx, is the underlying identity. Extracting the constant either (a) reduces the new integral to a scalar multiple of an integral already evaluated, removing the need for fresh computation, or (b) keeps the inner integrand as small as possible, so subsequent IBP/substitution work involves cleaner coefficients and fewer places to make arithmetic slips. The constant is reattached only once, at the very end.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notice that the integrand factorises as (constant) × (function of x), e.g. ½ · log₁₀x or 2 · xeˣ.
  2. Rule out fresh techniques: there is no need to redo a trapezium estimate or restart IBP from scratch when the only difference from a prior integral is a numerical scaling.
  3. Consider absorbing the constant into u or f(x) for the next step — reject this because it produces messier derivatives and obscures the link to the known result.
  4. Commit to linearity: ∫ k·f(x) dx = k·∫ f(x) dx, with k pulled outside the integral sign immediately.
  5. Decide where to reattach k: typically at the final substitution stage, multiplying the value of the inner integral by k once and only once.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Identify the constant factor k in the integrand k·f(x).
  2. Rewrite ∫ₐᵇ k·f(x) dx = k · ∫ₐᵇ f(x) dx.
  3. Evaluate (or quote) the inner integral ∫ₐᵇ f(x) dx using the appropriate method or a previously stated answer.
  4. Multiply the result by k to obtain the final value.

Worked Micro-Example

Given $\int_2^{14} \log_{10} x\,dx \approx 10.10$ from part (a), evaluate $\int_2^{14} \tfrac12 \log_{10} x\,dx$. Extract the constant: $\int_2^{14} \tfrac12 \log_{10} x\,dx = \tfrac12 \int_2^{14} \log_{10} x\,dx \approx \tfrac12 \times 10.10 = 5.05.$

Variants — Same move, different surface

New integral is a scalar multiple of one estimated numerically (e.g. by trapezium rule) in an earlier part
Pull the scalar outside and multiply it into the quoted estimate — do not redo the trapezium calculation.
Repeated IBP where differentiating xⁿ produces a coefficient n
Extract the coefficient before the next IBP round so the inner u, dv have smaller numbers; reattach the coefficient at the back-substitution stage.
Integrand of the form c·f(x) with c a surd, π, or e (any x-independent expression)
Same linearity rule applies — c need not be rational, only independent of x.

Common Misreadings

  • Treating the constant as part of the function and starting a fresh trapezium-rule or IBP computation, ignoring the link to a previous part.
  • Absorbing the constant into u for the next IBP (e.g. setting u = 2x), which is valid but produces messier derivatives downstream.

Pitfalls

  • Forgetting to reattach the extracted constant at the end (e.g. writing I = e − 1 instead of I = e − 2(1)).
  • Pulling out something that depends on x (only genuine constants, i.e. quantities independent of the integration variable, may be extracted).

A-level Scope Notes

This is the linearity rule for integrals, used routinely throughout Edexcel IAL P2/P3/P4 and is not separately listed in the formula booklet — it is assumed knowledge. Avoid extending the idea to pulling functions of x outside an integral, which is invalid.
