Algebra & Functions

Multiply tangent/normal equation by t² to clear all fractional powers of t at once

Family: Strategic Multiplication Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

Fires when a tangent or normal equation to a parametric curve (e.g. rectangular hyperbola xy = c² with point (ct, c/t), or parabola y² = 4ax with point (at², 2at)) is written in point-gradient form and contains terms with denominators t and t². The target 'show that' form is fraction-free with integer-looking coefficients, e.g. t²y + x = 2ct or t²y + x = 10t. Cue phrases: 'show that the tangent has equation …', 'prove the tangent at P is …'. The presence of mixed denominators 1/t and 1/t² is the decisive signal.

Motivation — Why this works

The lowest common denominator of 1/t and 1/t² is t², so a single multiplication by t² simultaneously clears every fraction without case-splitting or term-by-term rearrangement. This is faster and less error-prone than expanding the bracket first and then clearing denominators piecemeal. It also lands the equation directly in the symmetric form the syllabus uses for parametric tangents/normals, making the final rearrangement to the required 'show that' form a one-line job.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notices the point-gradient equation contains 1/t on the LHS and 1/t² multiplying the bracket on the RHS.
  2. Rules out expanding the bracket first — that scatters t² across multiple terms and forces multiple fraction-clearing steps later.
  3. Commits to multiplying through by t² because it is the LCD of every fractional term present, so one stroke removes all denominators.
  4. Bridges to the operation by writing the equation with the bracket still intact, then multiplying both sides (every term) by t².
  5. Inside the move, watches the sign on -(X - 5t) when distributing, and groups X and t²Y on the same side to match the target form.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Write the point-gradient form: Y - 5/t = -(1/t²)(X - 5t).
  2. Multiply every term on both sides by t²: t²Y - 5t = -(X - 5t).
  3. Distribute the negative on the RHS: t²Y - 5t = -X + 5t.
  4. Collect X and Y on one side, constants on the other: t²Y + X = 10t.
  5. Compare with the requested 'show that' form to confirm match.

Worked Micro-Example

Tangent to xy = 25 at P(5t, 5/t) has gradient -1/t². Point-gradient: $y - \tfrac{5}{t} = -\tfrac{1}{t^2}(x - 5t)$. Multiply by $t^2$: $t^2 y - 5t = -(x - 5t) = -x + 5t$. Hence $t^2 y + x = 10t$, as required.

Variants — Same move, different surface

Tangent to parabola y² = 4ax at (at², 2at), gradient 1/t
Only one fractional power (1/t) is present, so multiply through by t (not t²) to clear it; target form ty = x + at².
Normal to rectangular hyperbola xy = c² at (ct, c/t), gradient t²
No fractions on the gradient side, but the y-coordinate c/t still introduces 1/t; multiply by t to clear, yielding t³x - ty + c - c t⁴ = 0 style form.
Tangent to ellipse in parametric form (a cos θ, b sin θ)
No t-powers in denominators, so this multiplication move does not apply; instead divide through by ab to reach x cos θ /a + y sin θ /b = 1.

Common Misreadings

  • Treating the equation as already simplified and trying to rearrange without clearing denominators, leading to fractional final answers.
  • Multiplying only the bracketed RHS by t² while forgetting the lone 5/t term on the LHS.
  • Choosing to multiply by t instead of t², which clears 1/t² only partially.

Pitfalls

  • Forgetting to multiply every term — especially the constant 5/t on the LHS — by t².
  • Sign errors when distributing the negative across -(X - 5t).
  • Writing t·t² = t² instead of t³ if the gradient also carries an extra t factor in a normal-line variant.

A-level Scope Notes

Standard for Edexcel IAL Pure/Further Pure parametric tangent and normal proofs on rectangular hyperbolas (xy = c²) and parabolas (y² = 4ax); requires only point-gradient form and basic algebraic manipulation.
