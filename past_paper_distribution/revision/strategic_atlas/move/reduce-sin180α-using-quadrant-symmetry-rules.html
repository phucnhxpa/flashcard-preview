Trigonometry

Reduce sin(180°±α) using quadrant symmetry rules

Family: Identity Selection Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

An expression contains sin(180° − α), sin(180° + α), or sin(α − 180°) and a value sin α = p (or similar) is already known. The question wants compound-angle terms collapsed to ±sin α so a numerical substitution can finish the job. Cue phrases include 'given sin α = …, find …' with 180° appearing inside the sine. Trigger fires whenever the sum/difference involves a quadrantal angle (180°, 360°, 90°) rather than two general angles.

Motivation — Why this works

On the unit circle, adding or subtracting 180° reflects a point through the origin, so the sine value either keeps its magnitude with the same sign (Q2: 180° − α) or flips sign (Q3: 180° + α). These symmetry results — equivalent to a CAST-diagram reading — replace the full addition formula with a one-line ±sin α answer. This is faster and avoids sign-handling errors that arise from expanding sin(A ± B) with A = 180°, where cos 180° = −1 and sin 180° = 0 must be tracked.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notice that one of the angles in the sine is exactly 180° (a quadrantal angle), not a general second angle.
  2. Rule out the addition formula sin(A ± B) = sin A cos B ± cos A sin B as overkill — it works but multiplies the chances of a sign slip.
  3. Commit to the symmetry/CAST route because each target reduces to ±sin α directly.
  4. Bridge: for each term, identify the quadrant of the resulting angle (Q2 for 180° − α, Q3 for 180° + α) to fix the sign of sine.
  5. Tactically, rewrite sin(α − 180°) as −sin(180° − α) using the odd property sin(−θ) = −sin θ before applying the symmetry rule.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Identify each sine term containing 180° ± α or α − 180°.
  2. Apply sin(180° − α) = sin α (Q2, sine positive, reference angle α).
  3. Apply sin(180° + α) = −sin α (Q3, sine negative, reference angle α).
  4. For sin(α − 180°), use sin(α − 180°) = −sin(180° − α) = −sin α.
  5. Substitute sin α = p into the simplified expression to obtain the numerical answer.

Worked Micro-Example

Given $\sin\alpha = p$, simplify $2\sin(180°-\alpha) + \sin(180°+\alpha) - \sin(\alpha-180°)$.\n$\sin(180°-\alpha)=\sin\alpha=p$, $\sin(180°+\alpha)=-\sin\alpha=-p$, $\sin(\alpha-180°)=-\sin\alpha=-p$.\nExpression $= 2p + (-p) - (-p) = 2p$.

Variants — Same move, different surface

Cosine with 180° ± α
Use cos(180° − α) = −cos α and cos(180° + α) = −cos α (both Q2 and Q3 give negative cosine).
Either trig with 360° ± α
sin(360° − α) = −sin α, cos(360° − α) = cos α (Q4, sine negative, cosine positive).
Co-function with 90° ± α
Switch function: sin(90° − α) = cos α, cos(90° − α) = sin α, sin(90° + α) = cos α.
Tangent with 180° ± α
tan has period 180°, so tan(180° ± α) = ±tan α directly.
Negative-angle inputs sin(−α)
Use the odd property sin(−α) = −sin α (and even property cos(−α) = cos α) before applying any 180° rule.

Common Misreadings

  • Treating the move as requiring the full addition formula sin(A + B) = sin A cos B + cos A sin B with A = 180°, when symmetry gives the answer in one line.
  • Conflating sin(α − 180°) with sin(180° − α) and missing the sign reversal coming from the odd property.
  • Assuming the same sign rule applies to cosine that applies to sine (it does not — cos is negative in both Q2 and Q3).

Pitfalls

  • Assigning a positive sign to sin(180° + α) because Q3 was misread as having positive sine.
  • Forgetting that sin is odd, so sin(α − 180°) = −sin(180° − α), not +sin(180° − α).
  • Mixing up reference-angle magnitude with the signed sine value when reading the CAST diagram.

A-level Scope Notes

Fully within Edexcel IAL Pure Mathematics (P2/P3) trigonometry: unit-circle symmetries, CAST diagram, and odd/even properties of sine and cosine, with no recourse to identities outside the syllabus.
