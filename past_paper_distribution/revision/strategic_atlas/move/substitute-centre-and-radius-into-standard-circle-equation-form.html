Coordinate Geometry

Substitute centre and radius into standard circle equation form

Family: Circle Equation Assembly Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

Fires when a question asks for 'an equation of the circle C' (or 'hence find an equation of C') after the centre (a,b) and radius r have been determined in earlier parts or are otherwise known. Cue words include 'hence', 'write down', or simply 'find an equation' once both pieces of data are in hand. The default target is the standard form (x−a)² + (y−b)² = r² unless the question specifies the expanded general form.

Motivation — Why this works

The standard form (x−a)² + (y−b)² = r² is the direct algebraic encoding of the locus definition: every point (x,y) at distance r from (a,b). Once centre and radius are known, no further geometry is needed — assembly is pure substitution. Leaving the equation in standard form preserves the geometric data and is normally accepted unless an expanded form is explicitly demanded.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notice that both centre (a,b) and radius r are already available from earlier work, so no new geometry is required.
  2. Rule out re-deriving via distance formula or general form x²+y²+2gx+2fy+c=0 — that would ignore the 'hence' instruction and waste time.
  3. Commit to the standard template (x−a)² + (y−b)² = r² because it slots the known data in directly.
  4. Bridge to operation: write the template, then substitute a, b, r carefully, watching signs.
  5. Tactical decision: simplify x−(−a) to x+a, square the radius on the RHS, and stop — do not expand unless asked.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Write the template: (x − a)² + (y − b)² = r².
  2. Substitute the known centre coordinates a and b, using brackets to manage signs.
  3. Substitute r² (the square of the radius) on the RHS, not r itself.
  4. Simplify any double negatives, e.g. x − (−7) = x + 7.
  5. Leave in standard form unless the question asks for the expanded general form.

Worked Micro-Example

Centre (−7, 9), radius 5. Template: $(x-a)^2+(y-b)^2=r^2$. Substitute: $(x-(-7))^2+(y-9)^2=5^2$. Simplify: $(x+7)^2+(y-9)^2=25$.

Variants — Same move, different surface

Centre has positive coordinates, e.g. (3, 4), radius √10
Direct substitution: (x−3)² + (y−4)² = 10; note r² = 10, not √10.
Question demands general form x²+y²+2gx+2fy+c=0
Expand the standard form, collect like terms, and move the constant to the LHS.
Centre at origin (0,0)
Equation collapses to x² + y² = r².
Radius given as a length squared (e.g. r² = 50 found from distance²)
Use r² directly on the RHS without taking a square root then re-squaring.

Common Misreadings

  • Treating 'hence' as optional and re-deriving the equation from scratch using a point and the distance formula.
  • Assuming the expanded general form is required when the standard form suffices.
  • Confusing the centre's coordinates with their negatives inside the brackets.

Pitfalls

  • Writing (x − 7)² instead of (x + 7)² when the centre's x-coordinate is −7.
  • Putting r on the RHS instead of r² (e.g. writing 5 instead of 25).
  • Dropping brackets and writing x − −7² which mis-squares the sign.
  • Expanding unnecessarily and then making algebraic errors that lose the clean standard form.

A-level Scope Notes

Fully within Edexcel IAL Pure Mathematics (P2 coordinate geometry of the circle). The standard form (x−a)² + (y−b)² = r² and its expanded equivalent are both core syllabus content.
