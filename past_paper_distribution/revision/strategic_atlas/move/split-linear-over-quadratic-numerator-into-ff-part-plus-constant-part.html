Integration

Split linear-over-quadratic numerator into f'/f part plus constant part

Family: Numerator Decomposition Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

Fires when integrating a fraction of the form (Ax+B)/(x²+c) where the numerator is linear and the denominator is an irreducible quadratic (or quadratic+constant). The numerator is neither purely a multiple of the denominator's derivative nor purely a constant — it is a mixture of both. Cue phrases: 'linear over quadratic with no real roots', 'denominator does not factorise', 'numerator has both an x-term and a constant'. Especially common after partial fractions produce a residue of the form (Ax+B)/(x²+c).

Motivation — Why this works

The derivative of x²+c is 2x, so any multiple of x in the numerator generates a logarithm via the f'/f pattern, while any pure constant in the numerator generates an arctan. Splitting the numerator into 'derivative-of-denominator part' + 'constant part' decomposes the integral into two standard forms that can each be written down by recognition. The split is exact (just algebra), so no information is lost, and it avoids forcing a substitution that would not handle the constant residue.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. I notice the integrand is (linear)/(quadratic with no real roots), e.g. (−2x+2)/(x²+1), so neither ln nor arctan alone will cover it.
  2. I rule out a single substitution u = x²+1 because du = 2x dx cannot absorb the constant +2 in the numerator; I also rule out partial fractions because the denominator is irreducible.
  3. I commit to splitting the numerator: extract the multiple of f'(x)=2x that matches the x-coefficient, and leave the remaining constant as a separate fraction.
  4. Concretely I rewrite (−2x+2)/(x²+1) as −2x/(x²+1) + 2/(x²+1), matching the f'/f template and the arctan template respectively.
  5. I check the scaling: the f'/f piece needs the numerator to be exactly the derivative (up to a constant factor I can pull out), and the arctan piece needs the denominator in the form x²+a² so I know whether to divide by a.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Compute f'(x) where f(x) is the denominator (here f' = 2x).
  2. Write the numerator Ax+B as (A/2)·(2x) + B, so the integrand becomes (A/2)·f'(x)/f(x) + B/f(x).
  3. Integrate the first piece using ∫f'/f dx = ln|f(x)|, giving (A/2)ln|x²+c|.
  4. Integrate the second piece using ∫1/(x²+a²) dx = (1/a)arctan(x/a), with a=√c.
  5. Combine and add the constant of integration C.

Worked Micro-Example

Evaluate $\int \dfrac{-2x+2}{x^{2}+1}\,dx$. Split: $\int \dfrac{-2x}{x^{2}+1}\,dx + \int \dfrac{2}{x^{2}+1}\,dx$. The first is $-\ln(x^{2}+1)$ (f'/f pattern); the second is $2\arctan x$. Hence the integral equals $-\ln(x^{2}+1) + 2\arctan x + C$.

Variants — Same move, different surface

Denominator x²+a² with a≠1, e.g. ∫(3x+5)/(x²+4) dx
Split into (3/2)·2x/(x²+4) + 5/(x²+4); first piece gives (3/2)ln(x²+4), second gives (5/2)arctan(x/2) — remember the 1/a factor.
Residue from partial fractions of the form (Ax+B)/(x²+px+q) with irreducible quadratic
Complete the square on the denominator first to get (x+p/2)² + k, then split numerator into a multiple of d/dx[(x+p/2)²+k] = 2(x+p/2) plus a constant; substitute u = x+p/2 if helpful.
Numerator already proportional to f'(x), e.g. ∫6x/(x²+1) dx
No split needed — directly recognise f'/f with a constant multiple, giving 3ln(x²+1).
Numerator is purely a constant, e.g. ∫5/(x²+9) dx
Skip the split; apply arctan formula directly: (5/3)arctan(x/3).

Common Misreadings

  • Treating the whole fraction with a single u = x²+1 substitution, ignoring that the constant in the numerator does not fit du = 2x dx.
  • Trying partial fractions on x²+1 (which is irreducible over the reals) and getting stuck.
  • Reading −2x+2 as a single 'linear factor' to be cancelled with the denominator.

Pitfalls

  • Mis-scaling the f'/f piece: forgetting to divide by 2 when matching Ax to (A/2)·2x.
  • Forgetting the 1/a factor in ∫1/(x²+a²) dx = (1/a)arctan(x/a) when a≠1.
  • Dropping the absolute value or sign when integrating f'/f (though x²+c>0 makes |·| unnecessary here).
  • Omitting the constant of integration after combining two integrals.

A-level Scope Notes

Fully within Edexcel IAL Pure/Further Pure: standard results ∫f'/f dx = ln|f| and ∫1/(x²+a²) dx = (1/a)arctan(x/a) are both on the formula sheet, and this split routinely appears after partial fractions or in Further Pure integration questions.
