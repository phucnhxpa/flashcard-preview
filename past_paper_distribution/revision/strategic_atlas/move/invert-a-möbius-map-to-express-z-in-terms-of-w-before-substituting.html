Complex Numbers

Invert a Möbius map to express z in terms of w before substituting

Family: Transformation Inversion Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

Fires whenever a transformation w = f(z) is given (typically a Möbius map like w = (az+b)/(cz+d)) and you must find the image in the w-plane of a locus already known in z (e.g. |z+1| = 2, arg(z) = π/4, or a line). Direct substitution of w = f(z) into a z-condition is awkward because z still appears; you need a clean expression for z in terms of w. The cue is: 'locus given in z, transformation given as w = f(z), image required'.

Motivation — Why this works

A Möbius map is bijective on ℂ ∪ {∞}, so it has an explicit inverse z = g(w) which is itself a Möbius map. Once z is written purely in terms of w, the z-locus equation becomes a condition in w alone, which can then be simplified to a recognisable line or circle. This converts a geometric image-finding problem into routine algebraic substitution, the standard FP2/FP3 technique.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notice: I have a condition on z (e.g. |z+1| = 2) and a relation w = (z+1)/(z-1); I want a condition on w alone.
  2. Rule out: parametrising the z-circle as z = -1 + 2e^{iθ} and tracking w(θ) — feasible but messy and not the expected method.
  3. Commit: since the map is Möbius, it inverts cleanly, so solving for z in terms of w gives a single rational expression I can substitute.
  4. Bridge: cross-multiply w(z-1) = z+1, then collect z-terms onto one side.
  5. Tactical: keep the inverted form z = (w+1)/(w-1) unsimplified for substitution; only simplify after substituting into the modulus or Cartesian condition.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Start from w = (z+1)/(z-1) and multiply both sides by (z-1): w(z-1) = z+1.
  2. Expand: wz - w = z + 1.
  3. Group z-terms: wz - z = w + 1, i.e. z(w-1) = w+1.
  4. Solve: z = (w+1)/(w-1).
  5. Substitute this expression for z into the given z-locus and simplify to obtain the w-locus.

Worked Micro-Example

Given w = (z+1)/(z-1) and the z-locus |z+1| = 2. Invert: z = (w+1)/(w-1). Then z+1 = (w+1)/(w-1) + 1 = (2w)/(w-1), so |2w/(w-1)| = 2 gives |w| = |w-1|. This is the perpendicular bisector of 0 and 1, i.e. the line Re(w) = 1/2.

Variants — Same move, different surface

w = 1/z
Inversion is trivial: z = 1/w; substitute directly into the z-locus.
w = (az+b)/(cz+d) general Möbius
Cross-multiply and collect z-terms to get z = (b - dw)/(cw - a).
w = z + k or w = kz (translation/dilation)
Inversion is a one-step rearrangement z = w - k or z = w/k; locus shape is preserved up to that shift/scale.
w-locus given, z-locus required
No inversion needed — substitute w = f(z) directly into the w-condition.
w = z² (non-Möbius, outside Möbius family but related)
Inversion gives z = ±√w with branch care; rarely appears at A-level in image-of-locus form.

Common Misreadings

  • Trying to substitute w = (z+1)/(z-1) directly into a z-locus and ending with a mixed equation in both z and w.
  • Mixing the Cartesian (x,y) form of the z-locus with the complex variable w instead of working in pure complex form.
  • Believing the image of a circle must be a circle and skipping the substitution — under Möbius maps, circles can map to lines when the locus passes through the pole z = 1.

Pitfalls

  • Sign errors when collecting z-terms: wz - w = z + 1 should give z(w-1) = w+1, not z(w+1) = w-1.
  • Forgetting to exclude the pole of the inverse (e.g. w = 1 here) from the image set.
  • Simplifying z = (w+1)/(w-1) prematurely or substituting only part of it into |z+1|, leading to algebraic chaos.

A-level Scope Notes

Standard Edexcel IAL Further Pure technique for Möbius transformations: invert algebraically, substitute, then identify the resulting w-locus as a line or circle using |w - a| = |w - b| or |w - c| = r.
