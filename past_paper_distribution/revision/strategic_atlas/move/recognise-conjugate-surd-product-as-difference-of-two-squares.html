Recognise conjugate surd product as difference of two squares

Family: Difference Of Squares Recognition Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

Fires whenever a product of two bracketed expressions appears in the form (a − b)(a + b), where a and b may be surds, integers, or mixed surd–integer terms. Common cues: two brackets that differ only in a single sign, conjugate surd pairs like (√n − k)(√n + k), or a denominator of the form √a ± b waiting to be rationalised. Also fires when a question defines p and q as conjugate surds and later asks for pq, p² − q², or an area/length involving their product.

Motivation — Why this works

The identity (a − b)(a + b) = a² − b² collapses the cross terms, eliminating any surd that appears symmetrically in both brackets. When a is a surd and b is rational (or vice versa), the result is fully rational, removing surds from the calculation entirely. Spotting this avoids a four-term FOIL expansion and the associated sign/surd errors. Examination questions deliberately engineer conjugate pairs so that this shortcut produces a clean exact answer.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notice the two brackets are identical except for the sign between their terms — a conjugate pair signal.
  2. Rule out brute-force FOIL expansion: it works but wastes time and invites sign errors with surds.
  3. Commit to the (a − b)(a + b) = a² − b² template because it kills the cross terms automatically.
  4. Identify a and b explicitly: here a = √5, b = 1, so the product becomes (√5)² − 1².
  5. Evaluate (√5)² = 5 carefully (squaring removes the radical, it does not double the radicand) to get 5 − 1 = 4.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Match the product to the template (a − b)(a + b) by reading off a and b.
  2. Write the product as a² − b² without expanding term by term.
  3. Compute a² (squaring a surd √n gives n) and b² separately.
  4. Subtract to get a rational number; substitute back into the larger expression.
  5. Simplify the final expression (e.g. multiply by remaining factors) to obtain the clean exact form.

Worked Micro-Example

Given $p = \sqrt{5} - 1$ and $q = \sqrt{5} + 1$, find $pq$ and hence the area $\tfrac{1}{2}pq\sin\tfrac{\pi}{3}$. \[ pq = (\sqrt{5} - 1)(\sqrt{5} + 1) = (\sqrt{5})^2 - 1^2 = 5 - 1 = 4. \] \[ \text{Area} = \tfrac{1}{2} \times 4 \times \tfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2} = \sqrt{3}. \]

Variants — Same move, different surface

Rationalising a surd denominator such as $\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{5}-1}$
Multiply numerator and denominator by the conjugate $\sqrt{5}+1$; the denominator becomes $(\sqrt{5})^2 - 1^2 = 4$.
Factorising expressions like $x^2 - 7$ over surds
Write as $(x - \sqrt{7})(x + \sqrt{7})$ — the same identity used in reverse.
Complex numbers in Further Pure: $(a + bi)(a - bi)$
Gives $a^2 + b^2$ since $i^2 = -1$; used to rationalise complex denominators.
Simplifying $\sin^2\theta - \cos^2\theta$ style identities written as products
Apply $(\sin\theta-\cos\theta)(\sin\theta+\cos\theta)$ recognition in trig manipulation.
Binomial-like products $(2\sqrt{3} - 5)(2\sqrt{3} + 5)$
Square each term carefully: $(2\sqrt{3})^2 = 12$, giving $12 - 25 = -13$.

Common Misreadings

  • Treating the brackets as unrelated and launching into full FOIL expansion, missing the conjugate structure.
  • Assuming any pair of brackets with surds collapses this way, even when the signs don't match the (a − b)(a + b) template.
  • Confusing the conjugate-product identity with $(a+b)^2 = a^2 + 2ab + b^2$ and including a cross term.

Pitfalls

  • Writing $(\sqrt{5})^2 = \sqrt{25}$ and leaving it, or worse $(\sqrt{5})^2 = \sqrt{10}$ — squaring a surd $\sqrt{n}$ gives $n$, not $\sqrt{n^2}$ unsimplified or $\sqrt{2n}$.
  • Sign slip when b is negative: $(a - (-b))(a + (-b)) = a^2 - b^2$ still, but mis-bracketing gives wrong sign.
  • Forgetting to square the coefficient when $a = k\sqrt{n}$: $(k\sqrt{n})^2 = k^2 n$, not $k\sqrt{n^2}$.

A-level Scope Notes

Fully within Edexcel IAL P1 surds and indices; reappears in P3/P4 when rationalising and in FP1 with complex conjugates. No content beyond standard A-level algebra is required.
