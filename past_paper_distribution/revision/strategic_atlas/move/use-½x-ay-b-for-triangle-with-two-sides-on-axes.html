Coordinate Geometry

Use ½|x_A||y_B| for triangle with two sides on axes

Family: Geometric Structure Recognition Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

Fires whenever a triangle has one vertex at the origin O and the other two vertices on the coordinate axes (one on the x-axis, one on the y-axis). Phrasings include 'A is on the x-axis, B is on the y-axis', 'tangent meets the axes at A and B', or 'curve cuts the axes at P and Q'. The right angle at O is automatic, so the area collapses to half the product of the two intercepts. Recognise it before reaching for shoelace, determinant, or integration methods.

Motivation — Why this works

Because OA lies along the x-axis and OB along the y-axis, the angle AOB is exactly 90°, so OA and OB serve directly as base and perpendicular height. The area is therefore ½|x_A||y_B| with no trigonometry or vector setup required. In 'show independent of parameter' tasks the examiner usually engineers the intercepts so that the parameter appears as √k in one factor and 1/√k in the other, guaranteeing cancellation. Recognising the structure turns the problem into a single multiplication where the cancellation is visible.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notice that one named vertex is the origin and the other two sit on the two coordinate axes — a right angle at O is forced.
  2. Rule out shoelace, vector cross product, or integration: those would work but add unnecessary algebra and sign tracking.
  3. Commit to area = ½ × |x-intercept| × |y-intercept| because the perpendicular sides are already aligned with the axes.
  4. Bridge: read off x_A and y_B from the previous part (often tangent/normal axis intercepts) and substitute directly.
  5. Tactically watch for matched √-factors or reciprocal parameter terms that will cancel, confirming the 'independent of parameter' conclusion.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Identify the intercepts: A = (x_A, 0) on the x-axis and B = (0, y_B) on the y-axis.
  2. Check signs/positivity of x_A and y_B from given domain (e.g. a > 0, k > 0) so absolute values can be dropped.
  3. Write Area = ½ |x_A| |y_B|.
  4. Substitute the expressions for x_A and y_B from the earlier part of the question.
  5. Simplify, observing any cancellation of parameters (e.g. √k · 1/√k = 1).
  6. State the conclusion explicitly, e.g. 'the area is 2a², which does not involve k, hence is independent of k.'

Worked Micro-Example

The tangent to $xy = a^2$ at $P(a\sqrt{k},\, a/\sqrt{k})$ meets the axes at $A = (2a\sqrt{k},\, 0)$ and $B = (0,\, 2a/\sqrt{k})$. Then $\text{Area}(OAB) = \tfrac{1}{2}\cdot 2a\sqrt{k}\cdot \tfrac{2a}{\sqrt{k}} = 2a^2$. Since no $k$ remains, the area is independent of $k$.

Variants — Same move, different surface

Tangent to ellipse $x^2/a^2 + y^2/b^2 = 1$ at $(a\cos\theta, b\sin\theta)$ meeting the axes
Intercepts become $(a/\cos\theta, 0)$ and $(0, b/\sin\theta)$; area is $\tfrac{ab}{|\sin 2\theta|}$, minimised at $\theta = \pi/4$.
Tangent to rectangular hyperbola $xy = c^2$ at $(ct, c/t)$
Intercepts $(2ct, 0)$ and $(0, 2c/t)$; area $= 2c^2$, independent of $t$.
Normal (rather than tangent) cutting the axes
Same ½|x_A||y_B| formula; only the intercept expressions change because the gradient is the negative reciprocal.
Straight line $x/p + y/q = 1$ in intercept form
Intercepts read off directly as $p$ and $q$; area with axes is $\tfrac{1}{2}|pq|$.

Common Misreadings

  • Applying the shoelace/determinant formula even though two sides already lie on the axes, multiplying chances of sign error.
  • Trying to integrate the curve between intercepts to get the triangle area, confusing the region under the curve with the triangle OAB.
  • Treating O as an arbitrary point and computing |OA|, |OB| via distance formula rather than just reading off the coordinates.

Pitfalls

  • Omitting the explicit conclusion 'independent of k' after the cancellation — the final mark is for stating it, not just obtaining a k-free expression.
  • Dropping the factor of ½, giving the rectangle area instead of the triangle area.
  • Forgetting absolute values when intercepts could be negative (e.g. parameter ranges allowing $\cos\theta < 0$).

A-level Scope Notes

Fully within Edexcel IAL Pure / Further Pure: uses only intercept reading, basic area of a right-angled triangle, and surd cancellation, all standard at C1–C4 / FP1 level.
