Algebra & Functions

Rewrite target as combination of elementary symmetric functions to substitute Vieta values

Family: Symmetric Function Substitution Frequency: 3 questions

Trigger — When to use

The question asks for the value of, or asks you to form a new equation from, an expression that is symmetric in two (or more) roots/unknowns — e.g. α² + β², α/β + β/α, 1/α + 1/β, (p+q)² − 3pq — but you only know the elementary symmetric functions s = α+β and t = αβ (from Vieta's, or from a given constraint). The cue is: the target is symmetric, but it is NOT itself s or t. Equivalently: 'I'm being asked for a power sum or a rational symmetric expression in roots whose individual values are unknown or messy (e.g. complex, surd).' The student must NOT try to solve for the individual roots.

Motivation — Why this works

Every symmetric polynomial in α, β can be written in terms of the elementary symmetric polynomials s = α+β and t = αβ (Fundamental Theorem of Symmetric Polynomials, used implicitly at A-level). Standard identities — α² + β² = s² − 2t, α³ + β³ = s³ − 3st, 1/α + 1/β = s/t, α/β + β/α = (s² − 2t)/t — convert the target into a rational expression in s and t, which Vieta's formulas then evaluate immediately. This sidesteps the impossibility (or messiness) of finding α and β individually and is the engine behind every 'roots of a quadratic' transfer question in FP1.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notice the target expression is symmetric in the roots/unknowns: swapping α ↔ β leaves it unchanged. This is the structural flag.
  2. Check whether the target is already s or t — it isn't. So I need a reduction identity to bridge it back to s and t.
  3. Rule out solving for individual roots: either there's only one equation in two unknowns (so impossible), or the roots are complex/awkward, making symmetric reduction the expected and cleaner route.
  4. Commit to expanding/manipulating the target until every term is expressible in s and t. Common identities: (α+β)² = α² + 2αβ + β²; α² + β² = s² − 2t; α³ + β³ = s³ − 3st; 1/α + 1/β = s/t; (2/α)(2/β) = 4/t.
  5. If the target is a rational expression like α/β + β/α, first put over a common denominator αβ = t to expose α² + β² in the numerator, then apply s² − 2t.
  6. Substitute the Vieta values for s and t (with attention to signs, especially when the leading coefficient ≠ 1) and simplify.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Identify s = α + β and t = αβ from Vieta's (or from the given constraint).
  2. Algebraically rewrite the target so all root-terms are grouped as α+β, αβ, α²+β², α³+β³, etc.
  3. Apply the appropriate symmetric-function identity (e.g. α² + β² = (α+β)² − 2αβ) to eliminate non-elementary pieces.
  4. Substitute the numerical values of s and t.
  5. Simplify the resulting rational expression, clearing compound fractions if needed.

Worked Micro-Example

Given $\alpha+\beta = 5/2$ and $\alpha\beta = 2$, find $\frac{2}{\alpha} + \frac{2}{\beta}$. Combine: $\frac{2}{\alpha}+\frac{2}{\beta} = \frac{2(\alpha+\beta)}{\alpha\beta} = \frac{2(5/2)}{2} = \frac{5}{2}$.

Variants — Same move, different surface

Target involves α² + β² (e.g. α/β + β/α, or sum of squares of new roots)
Use α² + β² = (α+β)² − 2αβ; if target is α/β + β/α, first write as (α² + β²)/(αβ).
Target involves α³ + β³ (e.g. cubes of roots, or (p+q)³-type targets)
Use α³ + β³ = (α+β)³ − 3αβ(α+β); same reduction principle, one level up.
Forming a new quadratic whose roots are f(α), f(β) (e.g. 2/α and 2/β, or α+1, β+1)
Compute new sum S' and new product P' as rational functions of s and t, then write x² − S'x + P' = 0.
Three roots α, β, γ of a cubic
Use α² + β² + γ² = (α+β+γ)² − 2(αβ+βγ+γα); the elementary symmetric functions are now three quantities from Vieta's.
Target is given directly (no Vieta) — e.g. constraint p² + q² − pq = −2, find (p+q)² − 3pq
Expand the target and recognise it equals the LHS of the constraint exactly; no further substitution needed.

Common Misreadings

  • Computing 2/α + 2/β as 2/(α+β), confusing addition of fractions with reciprocal of a sum.
  • Trying to solve a single equation like p² + q² − pq = −2 for explicit p and q (two unknowns, one equation — impossible) instead of recognising the target is itself a symmetric expression.
  • Misreading the target, e.g. (p+q)² − 3pq read as (p−q)² − 3pq or (p+q)² − 3(pq)².

Pitfalls

  • Forgetting the factor of 2 in α² + β² = (α+β)² − 2αβ (writing −αβ instead of −2αβ).
  • Sign errors when αβ is negative: e.g. −2(−12/A) = +24/A — easy to drop the sign change.
  • Algebraic slip when expanding (α+β)²: writing α² + β² instead of α² + 2αβ + β².
  • When the leading coefficient of the quadratic ≠ 1, getting Vieta's signs wrong: for ax² + bx + c, s = −b/a and t = c/a.
  • Failing to clear compound fractions cleanly when substituting s and t into a rational target.

A-level Scope Notes

Fully within Edexcel IAL Further Pure 1 (FP1) 'Roots of quadratic equations' and standard P1/P2 algebraic manipulation. Vieta's formulas for quadratics (and FP1 cubics) are in scope; the general Fundamental Theorem of Symmetric Polynomials and Newton's identities are NOT examinable — students should rely on the specific identities α²+β² = s²−2t and α³+β³ = s³−3st rather than a general framework.
