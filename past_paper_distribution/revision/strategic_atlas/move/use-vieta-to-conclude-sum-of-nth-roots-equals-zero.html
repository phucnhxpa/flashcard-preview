Complex Numbers

Use Vieta to conclude sum of nth roots equals zero

Family: Vieta Polynomial Root Link Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

A 'show that' or 'find' part asks for the sum of all n roots of an equation of the form z^n = w (equivalently z^n - w = 0). The polynomial is monic with no z^{n-1} term. Phrasings include 'show that z_1 + z_2 + ... + z_n = 0', or summing trig forms like Σ cis(θ_k). Also fires when a question asks about the centroid of the roots on an Argand diagram.

Motivation — Why this works

For a monic polynomial z^n + a_{n-1}z^{n-1} + ... + a_0 = 0, Vieta gives Σz_k = -a_{n-1}. In z^n - w = 0 the coefficient of z^{n-1} is zero, so the roots sum to zero. Geometrically the n roots are equally spaced on a circle centred at the origin, so their centroid (and hence sum) is the origin. This avoids summing n trigonometric expressions by hand.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notice the question asks for a sum of all roots — manual addition of n cis terms would be slow.
  2. Rule out direct trigonometric summation; that route is error-prone and not the intended elegant argument.
  3. Commit to Vieta because the underlying equation z^n - w = 0 is a monic polynomial with a missing z^{n-1} term.
  4. Bridge: rewrite the given equation in standard polynomial form and read off the coefficient of z^{n-1}.
  5. Tactically, if the equation is shifted, e.g. (z-a)^n = w, substitute u = z - a first so the missing-coefficient argument applies cleanly.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Rewrite the equation as z^n - w = 0.
  2. Confirm it is monic and identify the coefficient of z^{n-1}, which is 0.
  3. Apply Vieta: Σz_k = -(coefficient of z^{n-1})/(leading coefficient) = 0.
  4. State the conclusion: z_1 + z_2 + ... + z_n = 0.
  5. Optionally support with the geometric remark that the roots are vertices of a regular n-gon centred at 0.

Worked Micro-Example

Solve z^5 = 32 and show Σz_k = 0. The roots are z_k = 2 cis(2πk/5) for k = 0,1,2,3,4. The polynomial z^5 - 32 = 0 has no z^4 term, so by Vieta Σz_k = -0 = 0. Hence 2 Σ_{k=0}^{4} cis(2πk/5) = 0.

Variants — Same move, different surface

Shifted equation (z - a)^n = w
Let u = z - a so u^n = w gives Σu_k = 0; therefore Σz_k = Σ(u_k + a) = na.
Equation z^n = w with non-zero w
The argument is unchanged — the value of w only affects a_0, not a_{n-1}, so Σz_k = 0 regardless.
Sum of products of pairs of roots
Higher Vieta: Σ_{i<j} z_i z_j = a_{n-2}, which is also 0 for z^n - w = 0 when n ≥ 3.
Sum of squares of roots
Use (Σz_k)^2 - 2Σ_{i<j}z_iz_j = 0 - 0 = 0 for n ≥ 3.

Common Misreadings

  • Thinking Vieta only applies to real-coefficient polynomials — it applies to any monic polynomial over C.
  • Believing the value of w on the right-hand side affects the sum of roots — it only affects the constant term.
  • Mistaking the geometric centroid argument as separate from Vieta when they are the same statement.

Pitfalls

  • Forgetting the sign in Vieta: sum of roots = -a_{n-1}/a_n, not +a_{n-1}.
  • Failing to make the polynomial monic before reading off coefficients.
  • Trying to sum cis(2πk/n) directly with trig identities and making algebra slips.
  • Not substituting u = z - a in shifted equations before applying the result.

A-level Scope Notes

Vieta's formulas for sums and products of roots are explicitly in Edexcel IAL Further Pure (FP1/FP2), and nth roots of complex numbers are core complex-number content; this move sits squarely within scope.
