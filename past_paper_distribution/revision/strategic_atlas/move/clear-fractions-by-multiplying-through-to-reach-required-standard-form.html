Coordinate Geometry

Clear fractions by multiplying through to reach required standard form

Family: Form Conversion Frequency: 3 questions

Trigger — When to use

The current equation is mathematically correct but cosmetically wrong for the question's specified output format. Typical cues: (i) the question demands a line in the form ax + by + c = 0 with a, b, c integers, yet your point-gradient form y − y₁ = m(x − x₁) contains a fractional gradient m = p/q; (ii) a conic equation like 4x² − y² = 4 or 9x² + 4y² = 36 is given with non-unit RHS, blocking direct read-off of a and b in the standard hyperbola/ellipse form. In each case the fix is the same — multiply (or divide) through by a single constant to clear fractions or normalise the RHS to 1.

Motivation — Why this works

Multiplying or dividing every term by a well-chosen constant rescales the equation without changing its solution set, but reshapes it into the canonical form the next step (or the mark scheme) requires. For lines, multiplying by the denominator of the fractional gradient eliminates fractions in one stroke and lets you collect terms into ax + by + c = 0. For central conics, dividing by the RHS produces x²/a² ± y²/b² = 1, from which a, b, asymptotes, and foci can be read directly. This is a formatting/standardisation move — small in content, but failing to do it costs the final accuracy mark or blocks every subsequent calculation.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. I notice the equation is correct in content but not in the form the question or the next step requires — there is a fraction, or the RHS is not 1.
  2. I rule out completing the square (no quadratic-in-one-variable mismatch), substitution (no awkward variable), or re-deriving the equation — none of these are needed since the equation itself is right.
  3. I commit to a single rescaling: multiply both sides by the denominator (for a fractional gradient) or divide both sides by the constant RHS (for a conic), because this is the minimal operation that fixes the form.
  4. I identify the exact constant: for y − y₁ = (p/q)(x − x₁) it is q; for Ax² + By² = K it is K (giving x²/(K/A) + y²/(K/B) = 1).
  5. I apply the multiplication to EVERY term on both sides (a common slip is to miss the lone constant), then rearrange/collect onto one side and check the result has no common factor and matches the demanded template.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Identify the offending feature: a fractional coefficient p/q, or a non-unit RHS K.
  2. Multiply (or divide) every term on both sides by q (or by K).
  3. Expand any brackets, watching signs carefully.
  4. Collect all terms onto one side (for ax + by + c = 0) or leave normalised (for x²/a² ± y²/b² = 1).
  5. Check: integer coefficients with no common factor, or RHS = 1 with denominators a², b² visible. Verify a leading sign convention if required.

Worked Micro-Example

Tangent at (2,3) with gradient −5/8: $y - 3 = -\tfrac{5}{8}(x-2)$. Multiply both sides by 8: $8y - 24 = -5(x-2) = -5x + 10$. Rearrange: $5x + 8y - 34 = 0$ ✓ (integer coefficients, no common factor).

Variants — Same move, different surface

Fractional gradient p/q in a line equation y − y₁ = (p/q)(x − x₁)
Multiply both sides by q, expand, then collect to ax + by + c = 0.
Integer gradient — no fraction to clear
Skip the multiplication; expand and rearrange directly into ax + by + c = 0.
Hyperbola Ax² − By² = K (e.g. 4x² − y² = 4)
Divide through by K to get x²/(K/A) − y²/(K/B) = 1; read off a², b²; use c² = a² + b² for foci.
Ellipse Ax² + By² = K (e.g. 9x² + 4y² = 36)
Divide by K to obtain x²/a² + y²/b² = 1; use c² = |a² − b²| for foci.
Circle in expanded form x² + y² + Dx + Ey + F = 0
Division alone is insufficient — complete the square in x and y to reach (x − h)² + (y − k)² = r².

Common Misreadings

  • Treating the coefficient of x² in 4x² − y² = 4 as a², giving a = 2 instead of dividing first to find a² = 1.
  • Reading the question's 'integer coefficients' as a hint to round, rather than as a directive to clear fractions exactly.

Pitfalls

  • Forgetting to multiply EVERY term (e.g. missing the constant on the LHS when multiplying y − 3 = …).
  • Sign error when expanding −p(x − x₁) — getting −px − p·x₁ instead of −px + p·x₁.
  • Leaving the answer in point-gradient form, losing the final presentation mark.
  • Using the ellipse focus formula c² = a² − b² for a hyperbola, where the correct relation is c² = a² + b².

A-level Scope Notes

Fully within Edexcel IAL P1/P2 (straight lines) and FP1/FP3 (conics). Standard forms x²/a² ± y²/b² = 1 and the focus relations c² = a² − b² (ellipse) and c² = a² + b² (hyperbola) appear in the IAL Further Maths formula booklet. Avoid generalising to rotated conics with an xy term — those are out of scope.
