Negate inequality to restore positive leading coefficient

Family: Inequality Orientation Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger ŌĆö When to use

Fires when a quadratic (or higher polynomial) inequality has a negative leading coefficient, e.g. $-0.4t^2 + 8t - 16.8 > 0$ or $-x^2 + 5x - 6 \geq 0$. Common after rearranging two expressions ($P_A > P_B$) or after expanding a bracket with a leading minus sign. The cue is awkwardness in reading the sign diagram of a downward-opening parabola.

Motivation ŌĆö Why this works

An upward-opening parabola ($a>0$) has the canonical sign pattern: positive outside its roots, negative strictly between them. Multiplying both sides by $-1$ (and flipping the inequality) converts the problem to this standard orientation, so the student can directly apply the 'between roots / outside roots' rule without mentally inverting it. The operation is an equivalence ŌĆö the solution set is unchanged ŌĆö because multiplying by a strictly negative constant is a reversible, order-reversing bijection on $\mathbb{R}$.

Thought Process ŌĆö The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notice the leading coefficient is negative, so the parabola opens downward and the 'positive region' is between the roots ŌĆö opposite to the default mental model.
  2. Rule out trying to read the sign chart directly with $a<0$: it works but invites slip-ups, especially when combined with other manipulations.
  3. Commit to multiplying through by $-1$ because it is a single clean operation that normalises orientation while preserving the solution set.
  4. Bridge: rewrite every term with its sign flipped, then immediately flip the inequality symbol ($>$ becomes $<$, $\geq$ becomes $\leq$).
  5. Optionally divide further by the (now positive) leading coefficient to simplify, e.g. divide $0.4t^2 - 8t + 16.8 < 0$ by $0.4$ to get $t^2 - 20t + 42 < 0$.

Mechanic ŌĆö Concrete operations

  1. Identify the negative leading coefficient on the polynomial side of the inequality.
  2. Multiply every term on both sides by $-1$.
  3. Reverse the inequality symbol ($>$ Ōåö $<$, $\geq$ Ōåö $\leq$).
  4. (Optional) Divide through by the new positive leading coefficient to simplify.
  5. Solve the standard upward-opening quadratic inequality: 'between roots' for $<0$, 'outside roots' for $>0$.

Worked Micro-Example

Solve $-0.4t^2 + 8t - 16.8 > 0$.\\ Multiply by $-1$ and reverse: $0.4t^2 - 8t + 16.8 < 0$.\\ Divide by $0.4$: $t^2 - 20t + 42 < 0$.\\ Roots: $t = 10 \pm \sqrt{58}$.\\ Solution: $10-\sqrt{58} < t < 10+\sqrt{58}$.

Variants ŌĆö Same move, different surface

Linear inequality with negative coefficient, e.g. $-2x + 5 \geq 1$
Multiply by $-1$ and flip $\geq$ to $\leq$, giving $2x - 5 \leq -1$.
Dividing both sides by a negative constant, e.g. $-3x > 12$
Divide by $-3$ and flip the inequality: $x < -4$.
Cubic inequality with negative leading term, e.g. $-x^3 + x < 0$
Multiply by $-1$, flip sign: $x^3 - x > 0$, then factorise and use a sign table.
Rational inequality where numerator has been negated
Negate numerator and flip inequality only if the denominator's sign is unchanged; otherwise handle by case analysis on the denominator.

Common Misreadings

  • Forgetting to reverse the inequality, ending up with the complementary (exterior) interval as the answer.
  • Reversing the inequality but only flipping signs on one side of the inequality.
  • Confusing this with multiplying by a variable expression, which is not a safe equivalence because the sign of the variable is unknown.

Pitfalls

  • Double-flipping by accident if a later step also divides by a negative quantity.
  • Flipping signs of some terms but not others (e.g. forgetting the constant term).
  • Applying the 'flip' rule when multiplying by a positive number out of habit.

A-level Scope Notes

Standard manipulation in Edexcel IAL Pure Maths (P1/P2) inequality work; underpins quadratic, cubic, and modulus inequality solving throughout the syllabus.
