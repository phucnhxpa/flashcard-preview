Trigonometry

Pre-compute exact trig values once before substituting into Taylor coefficients

Family: Evaluate Then Substitute Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

Fires when a Taylor/Maclaurin expansion about a specific angle (commonly π/6, π/4, π/3) requires evaluating several derivatives whose formulas all share the same trig building blocks (e.g. sec a, tan a, sin a, cos a). Cue phrases: 'expand about x = π/3 up to the term in (x−π/3)⁴', 'find the Taylor series', or any problem where f, f′, f″, f‴ at a fixed angle are all polynomials in the same trig values. Especially relevant when derivatives of tan, sec, or products thereof appear repeatedly.

Motivation — Why this works

Each derivative is a polynomial in a small set of trig values at a single angle, so those values are constants for the whole problem. Computing them once (and their needed powers) converts every coefficient evaluation into integer/surd arithmetic, eliminating repeated exact-value recall. This both speeds the work and isolates a single point of failure for the exact values rather than repeating the recall four times.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notice the Taylor series needs f^(k)(a) for k = 0,1,2,3,4 at one fixed angle a.
  2. Rule out re-deriving each exact trig value at every step — wasteful and error-prone.
  3. Commit to listing the trig primitives (sec a, tan a, plus the powers actually appearing: sec², sec⁴) in a small table.
  4. Bridge: derive sec a and tan a from the 30-60-90 (or 45-45-90) triangle, then square/fourth-power to get the rest.
  5. Tactically, keep √3 and 2 symbolic — never decimalise — and substitute directly into each derivative formula.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Identify the fixed angle a and the trig primitives appearing in f, f′, f″, f‴, f⁽⁴⁾.
  2. Compute exact values from the standard triangle: e.g. at a = π/3, sin a = √3/2, cos a = 1/2, so tan a = √3, sec a = 2.
  3. Pre-compute the higher powers used in the derivative formulas: sec²a = 4, sec⁴a = 16.
  4. Substitute these constants into each f^(k)(a) expression and simplify to integer×surd form.
  5. Assemble the Taylor polynomial f(a) + f′(a)(x−a) + f″(a)(x−a)²/2! + … using the tabulated values.

Worked Micro-Example

Expand f(x) = tan²x about x = π/3 to order (x−π/3)². Pre-compute: tan(π/3)=√3, sec(π/3)=2, sec²(π/3)=4. Then f(π/3)=(√3)²=3; f′(x)=2tan x sec²x gives f′(π/3)=2·√3·4=8√3; f″(x)=6sec⁴x − 4sec²x gives f″(π/3)=6·16 − 4·4 = 80. Hence f(x) ≈ 3 + 8√3(x−π/3) + 40(x−π/3)².

Variants — Same move, different surface

Taylor expansion of sec x or sec²x about π/4
Pre-compute sec(π/4)=√2, tan(π/4)=1, then sec²=2, sec⁴=4; all derivative coefficients become small integer combinations of √2.
Maclaurin series (a = 0) for products of sin, cos, tan
The primitives collapse to sin 0 = 0, cos 0 = 1, tan 0 = 0; pre-tabulating them lets many derivative terms be eliminated by inspection.
Taylor expansion about π/6 of expressions involving cot or csc
Pre-compute cot(π/6)=√3, csc(π/6)=2, csc²=4; reuse exactly as for the sec/tan case.
Small-angle approximation problems requiring f(a+h) for fixed a
Same idea: tabulate the trig primitives at a once, then substitute into the linear/quadratic approximation formula.

Common Misreadings

  • Treating sec⁴a as 4·sec a or as (sec a)·4 instead of (sec a)⁴.
  • Reading 'expansion about π/3' as a Maclaurin series and evaluating derivatives at 0 instead of π/3.
  • Assuming the Taylor coefficients should be decimals because the question gives a numeric angle, then losing exact form.

Pitfalls

  • Confusing sec(π/3)=2 with sec(π/6)=2/√3 — the 30° vs 60° mix-up.
  • Forgetting the factorial denominators k! when assembling the Taylor polynomial after substituting f^(k)(a).
  • Dropping a factor when expanding sec²a · tan a etc.; keep √3 and 2 symbolic throughout.
  • Sign slips when the derivative formula has a subtraction, e.g. 6sec⁴a − 4sec²a.

A-level Scope Notes

Fully within Edexcel IAL Further Pure: Taylor/Maclaurin series and exact trig values at π/6, π/4, π/3 are core content; no results outside the standard triangle values are required.
