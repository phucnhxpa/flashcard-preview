Differential Equations

Apply boundary conditions to full CF+PI to fix constants

Family: Boundary Value Determination Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

The general solution y = CF + PI has been written with two arbitrary constants A and B still free, and the question supplies two conditions — typically y(x₀) = k₁ and y'(x₀) = k₂, or values at two distinct points. Phrases like 'given that y = … and dy/dx = … when x = …', 'find the particular solution', or 'hence solve the differential equation subject to…' all signal this move. The move fires only after both CF and PI are in hand; applying it earlier loses the PI's contribution.

Motivation — Why this works

The CF+PI form parametrises a two-parameter family of solutions, and a unique member is selected by imposing as many independent conditions as there are constants. Substituting each condition produces a linear equation in A and B, and the resulting 2×2 system is guaranteed solvable when the conditions are independent. Crucially, the PI contributes to both y and y' at x₀, so it must be included before substitution. Differentiating term-by-term gives the second equation needed to pin down both constants.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notice that the assembled general solution still has two free constants A and B, and the question provides exactly two numerical conditions — a determinacy match.
  2. Rule out applying the conditions to the CF alone (a frequent mistake): the PI shifts both y and dy/dx at x₀, so the full y = CF + PI must be used.
  3. Commit to forming a 2×2 linear system in A and B by substituting each condition into the appropriate expression (y itself or dy/dx).
  4. Bridge: differentiate y = CF + PI carefully (product rule for terms like (A+Bx)e^{mx}, chain rule for e^{mx}) to obtain dy/dx in terms of A, B, x.
  5. Tactically, substitute x = x₀ first to simplify exponentials/trigs to clean numbers (often 1 or 0), then solve by elimination or substitution and write the particular solution.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Write the full general solution y = y_CF + y_PI with constants A, B explicit.
  2. Differentiate to obtain dy/dx in terms of A, B, x.
  3. Substitute the first condition (value of y at x₀) to form equation (1) in A and B.
  4. Substitute the second condition (value of dy/dx at x₀, or y at a second point) to form equation (2).
  5. Solve the simultaneous linear system for A and B.
  6. Substitute the values back to state the particular solution explicitly.

Worked Micro-Example

Given y = (A + Bx)e^{2x} + x + 1 with y(0) = 2 and y'(0) = 5. Then dy/dx = Be^{2x} + 2(A+Bx)e^{2x} + 1. At x = 0: y(0) = A + 1 = 2 ⇒ A = 1; y'(0) = B + 2A + 1 = 5 ⇒ B = 5 - 2 - 1 = 2. So y = (1 + 2x)e^{2x} + x + 1.

Variants — Same move, different surface

Boundary conditions at two distinct x-values, e.g. y(0) = 1 and y(1) = 0
Skip differentiating; substitute each x-value into y itself to form the two equations.
Second-order ODE with complex-root CF y = e^{αx}(A cos βx + B sin βx) + PI
Differentiate using product rule across the trig factor; conditions at x = 0 typically isolate A immediately, then B from y'(0).
Coupled/substitution problems where y is recovered from a substituted variable u
Apply conditions in the original variable y after back-substituting, not to u directly, since the conditions are stated for y.
Condition involves y''(x₀) (rare in IAL)
Differentiate twice; use the ODE itself if it gives a quicker relation between y'' and lower-order terms at x₀.

Common Misreadings

  • Treating the CF constants as if they live only in the CF, and applying y(x₀) to y_CF alone before adding PI.
  • Reading 'general solution' as the final answer when the question actually asks for the particular solution satisfying the conditions.
  • Confusing dy/dx = k with y = k at the given x-value when the prime notation is missed.

Pitfalls

  • Differentiation slips on (A + Bx)e^{mx} — forgetting either the product rule or the chain factor m.
  • Sign errors when sin(0) = 0 and cos(0) = 1 collapse terms — dropping the wrong term.
  • Arithmetic mistakes when solving the 2×2 system, especially with negative exponents or fractions.
  • Forgetting to write out the final particular solution after finding A and B.

A-level Scope Notes

Standard FP2/FP3-level technique for second-order linear ODEs with constant coefficients in Edexcel IAL Further Maths; conditions are always given in a form that yields a solvable linear system in A and B.
