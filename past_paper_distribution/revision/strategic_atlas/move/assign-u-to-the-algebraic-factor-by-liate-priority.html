Integration

Assign u to the algebraic factor by LIATE priority

Family: Liate Assignment Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

Fires inside an integration-by-parts problem where the integrand is a product of two distinct function types (e.g. polynomial × exponential, polynomial × trig, polynomial × ln). The student must decide which factor becomes u and which becomes dv/dx. Cues include: 'integrate ∫x²e^{2x}dx', 'find ∫x ln x dx', or any product where one factor simplifies under differentiation while the other does not. LIATE (Logarithmic, Inverse trig, Algebraic, Trig, Exponential) gives the priority order for u.

Motivation — Why this works

Integration by parts trades ∫u dv for uv − ∫v du, so progress requires that ∫v du be easier than the original. Choosing u from a class that simplifies under differentiation (logs vanish to 1/x; polynomials drop in degree) shrinks the residual integral. Choosing dv from a class that integrates without growing in complexity (exponentials and trig functions reproduce themselves) keeps v manageable. LIATE encodes this: earlier letters differentiate to something simpler, later letters integrate without harm.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. I notice the integrand is a product of two function-type classes — say x² (algebraic) and e^{2x} (exponential).
  2. I rule out u = e^{2x}: differentiating it just rescales the exponential while dv = x²dx integrates to x³/3, raising the polynomial degree — strictly worse.
  3. I commit to u = x² because it differentiates to 2x, lowering the polynomial degree, while dv = e^{2x}dx integrates cleanly to ½e^{2x}.
  4. Bridge: write down u, du/dx, dv/dx, v in a small table before applying ∫u dv = uv − ∫v du.
  5. Tactical decision: if the polynomial degree is ≥ 2, anticipate that IBP will need to be applied repeatedly, each time keeping the algebraic factor as u.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Identify the two factor-types in the integrand and rank them by LIATE.
  2. Assign u = the higher-priority factor (earlier letter in LIATE); set dv/dx = the remaining factor.
  3. Compute du/dx by differentiation and v by integration (handling any chain-rule constant such as ½ for e^{2x}).
  4. Apply ∫u (dv/dx) dx = uv − ∫v (du/dx) dx.
  5. If the new integral is still a product of two types, re-apply LIATE to it — keeping the algebraic factor as u in each round.

Worked Micro-Example

Evaluate ∫x²e^{2x}dx. LIATE: Algebraic before Exponential, so u = x², dv/dx = e^{2x}. Then du/dx = 2x, v = ½e^{2x}. So ∫x²e^{2x}dx = ½x²e^{2x} − ∫x e^{2x}dx. Apply IBP again with u = x, dv/dx = e^{2x}: ∫x e^{2x}dx = ½x e^{2x} − ¼e^{2x} + C. Combine: ½x²e^{2x} − ½x e^{2x} + ¼e^{2x} + C.

Variants — Same move, different surface

∫x ln x dx (algebraic × logarithmic)
Logarithmic beats Algebraic, so u = ln x, dv/dx = x. Then du/dx = 1/x and the residual integral ∫(x²/2)(1/x)dx = ∫x/2 dx becomes elementary.
∫x cos x dx (algebraic × trigonometric)
Algebraic beats Trig, so u = x, dv/dx = cos x. Polynomial degree drops to 0 after one application.
∫ln x dx (logarithm alone)
Treat as ln x · 1: set u = ln x, dv/dx = 1, so v = x. LIATE still chooses the log as u.
∫e^x sin x dx (exponential × trig, both self-regenerating)
LIATE gives no genuine simplification; instead apply IBP twice and solve the resulting linear equation in I.

Common Misreadings

  • Picking u = e^{2x} because exponentials 'differentiate easily' — the gain is illusory because dv = x²dx integrates to a higher-degree polynomial.
  • Treating LIATE as a hard rule rather than a heuristic; e.g. forcing u = ln x in ∫(ln x)/x dx where the substitution u = ln x (not IBP) is faster.
  • Forgetting that '1' counts as algebraic, so ∫ln x dx still falls under LIATE with dv/dx = 1.

Pitfalls

  • On the second round of IBP, swapping the assignment (taking u = e^{2x} for the reduced integral), which undoes the degree reduction achieved in round one.
  • Forgetting the ½ chain-rule factor when integrating e^{2x} to get v = ½e^{2x}.
  • Sign errors in the uv − ∫v du formula, especially when v carries a fractional constant.

A-level Scope Notes

Edexcel IAL Pure 3/4 introduces integration by parts with the LIATE heuristic on products of polynomial, exponential, trigonometric, and logarithmic factors; the self-regenerating ∫e^x sin x dx case appears in Further Pure.
