Integration

Recognise residual integral as lower-index I_k to close reduction recurrence

Family: Pattern Recognition Recursion Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

Fires at the final stage of deriving a reduction formula, immediately after integration by parts (or a trig identity substitution) has produced a leftover integral. The cue is that this leftover integral has the same integrand template as the original I_n but with the power/index shifted (typically to n−1 or n−2). Phrases like 'hence show that I_n = ...' or 'deduce a reduction formula' signal that this identification is the closing step.

Motivation — Why this works

A reduction formula's purpose is to express I_n in terms of I_{n-1} or I_{n-2}, so the residual integral must be relabelled rather than evaluated. By matching the residual against the given definition of I_k, the student converts an unevaluable integral into algebra and obtains a closed recurrence. This works because IBP on x^n e^{-x}, sin^n x, cos^n x, etc. is specifically engineered so the leftover integral has the same shape with reduced index.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. I notice the post-IBP residual ∫_0^1 x^{n-1}e^{-x}dx has exactly the integrand pattern of the defined family I_k = ∫_0^1 x^k e^{-x}dx.
  2. I rule out attempting another IBP or substitution — the integral is not meant to be evaluated; doing so would loop indefinitely.
  3. I commit to pattern-matching: the exponent on x is n−1, so this is I_{n-1} by definition.
  4. I substitute the label I_{n-1} back into the line emerging from IBP, replacing the integral symbol entirely.
  5. I tidy signs and constants from the boundary term to present the recurrence in the exact form requested (e.g. I_n = nI_{n-1} − e^{-1}).

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Write down the definition of I_k as stated in the question for reference.
  2. Compare the residual integral's integrand and limits to this definition.
  3. Identify the shifted index (here k = n−1) and replace the integral by I_{n-1}.
  4. Combine with the boundary term from IBP to assemble I_n = nI_{n-1} − e^{-1}.
  5. State the recurrence clearly as the proved reduction formula.

Worked Micro-Example

Given I_n = ∫_0^1 x^n e^{-x}dx, IBP with u=x^n, dv=e^{-x}dx gives I_n = [−x^n e^{-x}]_0^1 + n∫_0^1 x^{n-1}e^{-x}dx = −e^{-1} + n∫_0^1 x^{n-1}e^{-x}dx. The remaining integral matches the definition with index n−1, so it equals I_{n-1}. Hence I_n = nI_{n-1} − e^{-1}.

Variants — Same move, different surface

Trig reduction, e.g. I_n = ∫ sin^n x dx
After IBP the residual integral has sin^{n-2}x (one cos^2 = 1−sin^2 step), so identify it as I_{n-2}, not I_{n-1}.
Residual integral equals I_n itself (e.g. some sec^n x cases)
Treat as a linear equation in I_n: collect like terms on one side and divide to isolate I_n.
Two-parameter family I_{m,n} = ∫ x^m (ln x)^n dx
Identify the residual as I_{m,n-1} (or I_{m-1,n}) depending on which factor was differentiated in IBP.
Definite integral with nonstandard limits
Match both the integrand and the limits to the given I_k definition before relabelling.

Common Misreadings

  • Trying to evaluate the residual integral directly or applying IBP again, not realising it is meant to be relabelled.
  • Mismatching the index (writing I_n instead of I_{n-1}) by miscounting the power reduction from IBP.
  • Forgetting that a constant multiplier (like the n out front) belongs to the recurrence coefficient, not inside I_{n-1}.

Pitfalls

  • Equating the residual with I_{n-1} when the limits of integration differ from those in the I_k definition.
  • Ignoring an extra constant or sign factor produced by IBP, leading to a recurrence with the wrong coefficient.
  • Dropping the boundary term [uv] from IBP entirely once the integral has been relabelled.

A-level Scope Notes

Standard Edexcel IAL Further Pure reduction-formula technique (FP2/FP3 style); uses only IBP, basic trig identities, and direct definitional matching.
