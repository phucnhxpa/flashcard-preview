Complex Numbers

Read |w - a| = |w - b| as the perpendicular bisector of segment ab

Family: Geometric Interpretation Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

Fires whenever a locus or image equation reduces to |w - a| = |w - b| with equal coefficients on both sides. Typical surface forms include |w| = |w - 1|, |z - 2| = |z + 3i|, or |w - (1+i)| = |w - (3-i)|. Also appears as the end-stage of a Möbius image computation when the original circle passes through the pole, forcing a straight-line image. Any equality of two unweighted moduli of linear expressions in the same variable should cue this reading.

Motivation — Why this works

By definition, |w - a| is the distance from the point w to the point a in the Argand diagram. Setting two such distances equal characterises the set of points equidistant from a and b, which is exactly the perpendicular bisector of the segment ab. This geometric reading bypasses Cartesian expansion and yields the line equation directly via midpoint and perpendicularity. It also aligns with the Möbius fact that a circle through the pole maps to a straight line, so recognising the bisector form confirms the expected geometry.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notice the equation has the shape |w - a| = |w - b| with no scalar multiplier on either side — a pure equidistance statement.
  2. Rule out Apollonius circle: that requires |w - a| = k|w - b| with k ≠ 1; here k = 1, so the locus is a line, not a circle.
  3. Commit to the geometric reading because the locus is the set of points equidistant from a and b — this is the perpendicular bisector of segment ab by definition.
  4. Bridge to operation: compute the midpoint m = (a+b)/2 and identify the direction of ab, since the bisector passes through m perpendicular to ab.
  5. Tactically, if a and b lie on the real axis (or on a line parallel to an axis), write the answer as Re(w) = const or Im(w) = const directly; otherwise give the line through m perpendicular to b - a.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Identify a and b from |w - a| = |w - b|.
  2. Compute midpoint m = (a + b)/2.
  3. Determine the direction of segment ab (vector b - a) and take the perpendicular direction.
  4. If a, b are real, write Re(w) = Re(m); if purely imaginary, write Im(w) = Im(m); otherwise give the line through m perpendicular to b - a.
  5. State the locus and, if needed, sketch it on the Argand diagram.

Worked Micro-Example

Solve $|w| = |w - 1|$. Here $a = 0$, $b = 1$, both real, midpoint $m = \tfrac{1}{2}$. The locus is the perpendicular bisector of the segment from $0$ to $1$, i.e. the vertical line $\operatorname{Re}(w) = \tfrac{1}{2}$. Writing $w = u + iv$, the locus is $u = \tfrac{1}{2}$, $v \in \mathbb{R}$.

Variants — Same move, different surface

|w - a| = k|w - b| with k ≠ 1
Locus becomes an Apollonius circle, not a line; square and rearrange to circle form |w - c| = r.
|z - (1+i)| = |z - (3 - i)|
Midpoint is 2, direction b - a = 2 - 2i; perpendicular bisector passes through 2 with gradient 1 in the (x,y)-plane, giving y = x - 2.
|z - 2i| = |z + 2i|
a = 2i, b = -2i are purely imaginary; bisector is the real axis Im(z) = 0.
Möbius image of a circle through the pole
Image equation often simplifies to |w - a| = |w - b|, confirming the circle-to-line result; read off the line directly.

Common Misreadings

  • Treating |w - a| = |w - b| as a circle (e.g. mis-recalling it as Apollonius) instead of a straight line.
  • Converting immediately to Cartesian and expanding squares, missing the one-line geometric answer.
  • Confusing the locus with the segment ab itself rather than its perpendicular bisector.

Pitfalls

  • Using a or b as the point on the line instead of the midpoint (e.g. writing Re(w) = 1 instead of Re(w) = 1/2).
  • Getting the perpendicular direction wrong when a, b are not axis-aligned — forgetting to take the negative reciprocal of the gradient of ab.
  • Dropping the equality and writing an inequality, turning the locus into a half-plane unintentionally.

A-level Scope Notes

Perpendicular bisector loci of the form |z - a| = |z - b| are explicit Edexcel IAL Further Pure content (Argand diagrams and loci), and the Möbius circle-to-line corollary is standard in FP3.
