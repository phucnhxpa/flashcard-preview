Strategic Atlas / Integration / Integration By Parts
Integration

Assign u to the polynomial factor in polynomial×exponential integrals via LIATE/LIPET

Family: Integration By Parts Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

The integrand is a product of a polynomial xⁿ and an exponential e^{kx} (or similar pairing of two function classes that cannot be combined by substitution). Cues include phrases like 'find ∫x²e^{3x} dx' or any ∫(polynomial)(exponential) dx where direct substitution fails. The two factors come from different LIATE/LIPET categories (Logarithmic, Inverse-trig, Polynomial, Trigonometric, Exponential), signalling integration by parts.

Motivation — Why this works

Integration by parts converts ∫u(dv/dx)dx into uv − ∫v(du/dx)dx, so progress depends on whether differentiating u and integrating dv/dx makes the new integral simpler. A polynomial differentiates to a lower-degree polynomial (and eventually to 0), while an exponential is invariant under both differentiation and integration up to a constant. Hence choosing u = polynomial guarantees the integral simplifies on each pass, while choosing u = exponential leaves a higher-degree polynomial multiplying the exponential — strictly worse. LIATE/LIPET encodes this priority: L > I > A(P) > T > E for the role of u.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notice the integrand is a product of two different function classes (here x² and e^{3x}) that cannot be merged by a single substitution.
  2. Rule out u-substitution: t = 3x leaves x² intact; t = x² leaves e^{3x} tangled — neither collapses the product.
  3. Commit to integration by parts since it is the standard tool for products of distinct LIATE classes.
  4. Bridge: apply LIATE/LIPET — Polynomial outranks Exponential for u, so set u = x² and dv/dx = e^{3x}.
  5. Tactically anticipate that differentiating x² twice will eliminate the polynomial factor, so this single assignment will need to be repeated (parts applied twice) — confirming the choice is the one that terminates.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Identify the two LIATE classes present (Polynomial and Exponential).
  2. By LIATE, pick u = x² (the polynomial); set dv/dx = e^{3x}.
  3. Differentiate: du/dx = 2x.
  4. Integrate dv/dx: v = ∫e^{3x} dx = (1/3)e^{3x}.
  5. Apply ∫u(dv/dx)dx = uv − ∫v(du/dx)dx: I = (1/3)x²e^{3x} − ∫(1/3)e^{3x}·2x dx = (1/3)x²e^{3x} − (2/3)∫xe^{3x} dx.
  6. Recognise the residual ∫xe^{3x} dx is a smaller instance of the same template — apply parts again with u = x.

Worked Micro-Example

Find ∫x²e^{3x} dx. Take u = x², dv/dx = e^{3x}, so du/dx = 2x and v = (1/3)e^{3x}. Then ∫x²e^{3x} dx = (1/3)x²e^{3x} − (2/3)∫xe^{3x} dx. Apply parts again to ∫xe^{3x} dx with u = x: this gives (1/3)xe^{3x} − (1/9)e^{3x}. Combining: ∫x²e^{3x} dx = (1/3)x²e^{3x} − (2/9)xe^{3x} + (2/27)e^{3x} + C.

Variants — Same move, different surface

∫x sin(2x) dx (polynomial × trigonometric)
By LIATE, P beats T, so u = x, dv/dx = sin(2x); v = −(1/2)cos(2x). One application of parts suffices.
∫x ln x dx (polynomial × logarithmic)
L beats P in LIATE, so reverse the heuristic: u = ln x, dv/dx = x. Differentiating ln x gives 1/x, eliminating the logarithm.
∫x² cos x dx (higher-degree polynomial × trig)
u = x² again; parts must be applied twice, with sign-tracking on the trig factor (cos → sin → −cos).
Definite integral ∫₀¹ x e^{2x} dx
Same LIATE choice u = x, dv/dx = e^{2x}; evaluate the boundary term [uv]₀¹ before integrating the residual.

Common Misreadings

  • Choosing u = e^{3x} because 'exponentials are easy to differentiate' — but then v = (1/3)x³ and the residual ∫(1/3)x³·3e^{3x} dx is worse than the original.
  • Treating LIATE as a rigid law rather than a heuristic — failing to notice that for ∫x ln x dx the logarithm (L) takes precedence over the polynomial (P).
  • Confusing this with a u-substitution and trying to set u = x²e^{3x} or u = 3x.

Pitfalls

  • Forgetting the 1/3 from integrating e^{3x}: v = (1/3)e^{3x}, not e^{3x}.
  • Dropping the minus sign in uv − ∫v(du/dx)dx, especially when chaining a second application of parts.
  • Mishandling the constant 2 from du/dx = 2x — it must be carried into the residual integral as (2/3)∫xe^{3x} dx.
  • Stopping after one application when the polynomial still has degree ≥ 1; parts must be repeated until the polynomial is exhausted.

A-level Scope Notes

Fully within Edexcel IAL Pure Mathematics (C4/P3) integration by parts; LIATE/LIPET is the standard heuristic taught for choosing u. Repeated application for x²e^{kx} is examinable.
