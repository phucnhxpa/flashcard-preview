Trigonometry

Negate amplitude to reflect cosine graph across x-axis

Family: Reflection By Negation Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

Fires when fitting an equation $y = A\cos x$ or $y = A\sin x$ to a sketched graph and a key point has the wrong sign — typically a maximum where the standard curve gives a minimum (e.g. point $(180°, +5)$ on a cosine-shaped curve). Recognise it when the magnitude matches but the sign of $y$ is reversed at $x = 0°, 90°, 180°,$ or $270°$. Also triggered when a graph 'starts at its minimum' rather than its maximum at $x = 0°$.

Motivation — Why this works

Multiplying a trig function by $-1$ reflects its graph in the x-axis, swapping all maxima with minima while leaving the period and zeros untouched. Since $\cos(180°) = -1$, the function $y = -5\cos x$ evaluates to $+5$ at $x = 180°$, converting the minimum of $5\cos x$ into a maximum. This is the simplest correction available — it preserves the cosine shape and avoids introducing a phase shift, which is the conventional Edexcel form.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notice the magnitude of the target $y$-value matches the amplitude, but the sign is wrong at a quarter-period landmark.
  2. Rule out $y = A\sin x$ if the landmark is at $x = 0°$ or $180°$ (where sine is zero), and rule out positive-amplitude cosine since it gives the wrong sign.
  3. Commit to a reflection: the cosine shape is correct, only its orientation needs flipping.
  4. Bridge to the operation by writing $y = -A\cos x$ and testing the landmark point.
  5. Confirm at one further point (e.g. $x = 0°$ or $x = 90°$) that the negated form matches the rest of the graph, not just the single landmark.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Identify the amplitude $A$ from the maximum/minimum values on the graph.
  2. Test $y = A\cos x$ at the landmark $x$-value and observe the sign mismatch.
  3. Negate the coefficient: write $y = -A\cos x$.
  4. Verify $-A\cos x$ at the landmark gives the required $y$-value.
  5. Cross-check at a second landmark (e.g. $x = 0°$) to confirm the reflection is consistent across the whole curve.

Worked Micro-Example

Graph passes through $(0°, -5)$ and $(180°, +5)$ with amplitude $5$. Test $y = 5\cos x$: at $x = 0°$, $y = 5$ — wrong sign. Negate: $y = -5\cos x$. Check: at $x = 0°$, $y = -5(1) = -5$ ✓; at $x = 180°$, $y = -5(-1) = +5$ ✓. So $y = -5\cos x$.

Variants — Same move, different surface

Sine graph reflected (starts falling at origin instead of rising)
Use $y = -A\sin x$; verify at $x = 90°$ that $y = -A$ instead of $+A$.
Reflected cosine with vertical shift, e.g. graph oscillates between $1$ and $9$ with max at $x = 180°$
Use $y = -4\cos x + 5$: amplitude $4$, midline $5$, negated to place max at $x = 180°$.
Reflected cosine with altered period, e.g. max at $x = 90°$
Combine negation with a horizontal scale: $y = -A\cos(2x)$ places max at $x = 90°$.
Phase-shift alternative
$y = A\cos(x - 180°)$ or $y = A\cos(x + 180°)$ achieves the same reflection, but negative amplitude is the standard Edexcel answer.

Common Misreadings

  • Assuming amplitude must be positive and forcing a sine function with a phase shift, overcomplicating the answer.
  • Confusing $y = -5\cos x$ with $y = 5\cos(-x)$; since cosine is even, $\cos(-x) = \cos x$, so the latter produces no change.
  • Misreading the reflection as a vertical translation when both the maximum and minimum values are negated.

Pitfalls

  • Writing the amplitude as $5$ instead of $-5$ in the final answer, losing the reflection.
  • Forgetting to verify at a second point, so a sign error at the original landmark goes undetected.
  • Combining negation with an unnecessary phase shift, producing a correct but non-canonical equation.

A-level Scope Notes

Sits squarely within Edexcel IAL P2 trig graph-fitting: amplitude, period, and reflections of $\sin x$ and $\cos x$. No identities beyond evaluating $\sin$ and $\cos$ at $0°, 90°, 180°, 270°$ are required.
