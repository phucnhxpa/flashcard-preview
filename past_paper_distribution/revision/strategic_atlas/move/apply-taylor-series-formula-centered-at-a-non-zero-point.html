Sequences & Series

Apply Taylor series formula centered at a non-zero point

Family: Taylor Template Application Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

The question explicitly asks for an expansion 'in ascending powers of (x − a)' for some a ≠ 0, or 'the Taylor series of f about x = a'. Phrasings like 'expand near x = π/3', 'approximate f near a', or 'Taylor polynomial of degree n at a' all fire this move. Distinguish from Maclaurin cues, which say 'in ascending powers of x' or 'about x = 0'. Once derivative values f(a), f′(a), f″(a), … have been computed, the move is the formula assembly itself.

Motivation — Why this works

The Taylor expansion f(x) = Σ f⁽ⁿ⁾(a)/n! · (x−a)ⁿ is the local polynomial that matches f and all its derivatives at x = a. Dividing each derivative value by n! and attaching (x−a)ⁿ guarantees the kth derivative of the polynomial at a equals f⁽ᵏ⁾(a). Maclaurin is the special case a = 0; shifting the centre simply replaces x with (x − a) in the basis. The construction is mechanical once the derivative values at a are in hand.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notice the phrase 'in ascending powers of (x − a)' — this fixes the centre and rules out Maclaurin.
  2. Rule out trying to expand standard series (e.g. for tan²x) directly, since those are centred at 0; here the basis must be (x − π/3)ⁿ.
  3. Commit to the Taylor template once derivative values at a are computed, since the formula is now a substitution exercise.
  4. Decide the truncation order from the highest derivative computed (here up to f‴ gives terms up to (x − π/3)³).
  5. Simplify each coefficient f⁽ⁿ⁾(a)/n! carefully, keeping surds exact, and append '+ …' to mark truncation.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Write the Taylor template: f(x) = f(a) + f′(a)(x−a) + f″(a)/2!·(x−a)² + f‴(a)/3!·(x−a)³ + …
  2. Substitute the precomputed values f(a), f′(a), f″(a), f‴(a) (here 3, 8√3, 80, 352√3).
  3. Divide each by the corresponding n!: coefficients become 3, 8√3, 40, 176√3/3.
  4. Assemble: 3 + 8√3(x − π/3) + 40(x − π/3)² + (176√3/3)(x − π/3)³ + …
  5. Sanity-check by setting x = a: the expansion collapses to f(a) = 3 = tan²(π/3) ✓.

Worked Micro-Example

Expand $f(x) = \ln x$ about $x = 1$ to order 3. Compute $f(1)=0,\ f'(1)=1,\ f''(1)=-1,\ f'''(1)=2$. Apply the Taylor template: $\ln x = 0 + 1\cdot(x-1) + \tfrac{-1}{2!}(x-1)^2 + \tfrac{2}{3!}(x-1)^3 + \dots = (x-1) - \tfrac{1}{2}(x-1)^2 + \tfrac{1}{3}(x-1)^3 + \dots$

Variants — Same move, different surface

Expanding sin x or cos x about x = π/4 or x = π/2
Derivative values cycle through ±sin a, ±cos a; substitute these into the same template with (x − a) as the basis.
Expanding e^x about x = 1
All derivatives equal e at a = 1, giving e·Σ (x−1)ⁿ/n!; the constant e factors out.
Expanding f(x) about a negative centre, e.g. x = −π/4
Use basis (x − (−π/4)) = (x + π/4); be careful with the sign in (x − a).
Solution of an ODE about x = a using Taylor series
Derivatives f⁽ⁿ⁾(a) are obtained by repeatedly differentiating the ODE and evaluating at a, then substituted into the same Taylor template.
Approximating f(a + h) numerically
Replace (x − a) by h in the expansion to get f(a + h) ≈ f(a) + f′(a)h + f″(a)h²/2! + …

Common Misreadings

  • Using the Maclaurin formula (centre 0) out of habit, producing powers of x instead of (x − a).
  • Treating 'ascending powers of (x − π/3)' as 'ascending powers of x' after a substitution and forgetting to convert back.
  • Forgetting to include '+ …' to indicate the series continues beyond the computed terms.

Pitfalls

  • Dividing by n instead of n! (e.g. using 3 instead of 6 for the cubic term).
  • Sign errors when the centre is negative, e.g. writing (x − π/4) instead of (x + π/4) for a = −π/4.
  • Mixing exact and decimal forms of surds, losing marks for an inexact final answer.
  • Computing derivatives of the wrong function (e.g. tan x instead of tan²x) before substituting into the template.

A-level Scope Notes

Taylor series at a non-zero centre is part of Edexcel IAL Further Pure (FP2/FP3). Convergence/remainder analysis is not required; the focus is formula application up to a stated order.
