Trigonometry

Choose the sin-only (or cos-only) form of cos 2θ to match the target variable

Family: Identity Selection Frequency: 2 questions

Trigger — When to use

The equation or expression contains a multiple-angle term such as cos 2θ alongside a single-angle term in just one of sin θ or cos θ (e.g. cos 2x = sin x, or a denominator like 1 − 2sin θ − 4sin²θ written purely in sin θ). The other side — or the target form — uses only one trig function, so the multiple-angle term must collapse into that same function. Phrased differently: 'I see cos 2θ but the rest of the problem is purely in sin θ (or purely in cos θ)' — that mismatch is the cue.

Motivation — Why this works

cos 2θ has three equivalent forms (cos²θ − sin²θ, 2cos²θ − 1, 1 − 2sin²θ), and choosing the one that matches the dominant single-angle variable produces a single-variable polynomial equation in one step. This avoids invoking the Pythagorean identity sin²θ + cos²θ = 1 as a second substitution. Mathematically it converts a transcendental-looking equation into an algebraic one (quadratic, cubic, etc.) in u = sin θ or u = cos θ, which can then be factorised or solved by the quadratic formula. The same logic extends to sin 3θ = 3sin θ − 4sin³θ and cos 3θ = 4cos³θ − 3cos θ when triple angles are present.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notice the mismatch: the multiple-angle term (cos 2θ, sin 3θ, …) lives alongside single-angle terms in just one of sin θ or cos θ.
  2. Recall that cos 2θ has three forms; mentally list them: cos²θ − sin²θ, 2cos²θ − 1, 1 − 2sin²θ.
  3. Rule out the form cos²θ − sin²θ (leaves both functions) and rule out the form that introduces the wrong variable (e.g. 2cos²θ − 1 when the rest is in sin θ) — these would force an extra Pythagorean step and risk sign errors.
  4. Commit to the form that matches: 1 − 2sin²θ when the rest is in sin θ, or 2cos²θ − 1 when the rest is in cos θ.
  5. If a triple angle is also present, pair the choice with sin 3θ = 3sin θ − 4sin³θ (sin-only) or cos 3θ = 4cos³θ − 3cos θ (cos-only) to keep the whole expression in one variable.
  6. Substitute, then collect into a polynomial in u = sin θ (or cos θ) and proceed by factorisation or the quadratic formula.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Identify the target variable (sin θ or cos θ) by inspecting the side of the equation already in single-angle form.
  2. Replace cos 2θ with 1 − 2sin²θ (sin-target) or 2cos²θ − 1 (cos-target).
  3. If sin 3θ or cos 3θ appears, replace using sin 3θ = 3sin θ − 4sin³θ or cos 3θ = 4cos³θ − 3cos θ, distributing any leading minus sign carefully.
  4. Move all terms to one side and collect like powers of u = sin θ (or cos θ).
  5. Factorise the resulting polynomial in u and solve each factor for θ in the required range.

Worked Micro-Example

Solve $\cos 2x = \sin x$. RHS is in $\sin x$, so substitute $\cos 2x = 1 - 2\sin^2 x$: $1 - 2\sin^2 x = \sin x \;\Rightarrow\; 2\sin^2 x + \sin x - 1 = 0 \;\Rightarrow\; (2\sin x - 1)(\sin x + 1) = 0$, giving $\sin x = \tfrac12$ or $\sin x = -1$.

Variants — Same move, different surface

Equation purely in cos θ, e.g. cos 2θ = cos θ
Use cos 2θ = 2cos²θ − 1 to obtain a quadratic in cos θ.
Triple-angle term present alongside double-angle, both with a sin-only target
Pair cos 2θ = 1 − 2sin²θ with sin 3θ = 3sin θ − 4sin³θ to get a cubic in sin θ.
Equation of the form sin 2θ = (function of cos θ)
Expand sin 2θ = 2 sin θ cos θ and factor out cos θ — analogous 'match the variable' logic.
Integration: ∫ cos²x dx or ∫ sin²x dx
Use cos 2x = 2cos²x − 1 or 1 − 2sin²x respectively to linearise the integrand.

Common Misreadings

  • Reaching for cos 2θ = cos²θ − sin²θ by default, then getting stuck with both sin and cos and needing an extra Pythagorean substitution.
  • Choosing 2cos²θ − 1 when the equation is in sin θ (or vice versa), introducing the wrong variable and doubling the work.

Pitfalls

  • Sign error when subtracting a triple-angle expansion: failing to distribute the minus sign across all terms of 3sin θ − 4sin³θ (the −4sin³θ becoming +4sin³θ).
  • Dropping a factor when factorising the resulting polynomial — losing a valid solution branch.
  • Forgetting to check that solutions of u = sin θ satisfy |u| ≤ 1 before solving for θ.

A-level Scope Notes

Fully within Edexcel IAL P3 trigonometric identities and equations. The double-angle forms of cos 2θ and the triple-angle expansions of sin 3θ, cos 3θ are in the IAL formula booklet. Avoid invoking Chebyshev polynomials or general n-tuple-angle formulas — stay with the listed identities.
