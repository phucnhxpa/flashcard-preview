Trigonometry

Select R sin(x − α) form to match +sin, −cos sign pattern

Family: Identity Selection Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

Fires when an expression of the form a sin x + b cos x must be rewritten as a single harmonic R-form, and the coefficients have the specific sign pattern a > 0, b < 0 (e.g. 8 sin x − 15 cos x). Cues include phrases like 'express in the form R sin(x − α)', or solving/optimising a sin x − b cos x with both a, b > 0. Also fires whenever the student wants R > 0 and α acute (0 < α < π/2) without sign juggling.

Motivation — Why this works

The expansion R sin(x − α) = R cos α · sin x − R sin α · cos x naturally produces a +sin x and −cos x term when R > 0 and α is acute. Matching coefficients then yields R cos α = a and R sin α = b with both right-hand sides positive, guaranteeing α lies in the first quadrant. Any other R-form would force a negative R sin α or R cos α, breaking the convention that R > 0 and α is acute and inviting sign errors.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notice the linear combination a sin x + b cos x with a > 0 and b < 0 — the sign asymmetry is the key cue.
  2. Rule out R cos(x − α) and R cos(x + α): these expand with a +cos x term, so matching forces a negative coefficient onto sin α or cos α.
  3. Commit to R sin(x − α) because its expansion R cos α · sin x − R sin α · cos x mirrors the +sin, −cos pattern exactly with positive R, α.
  4. Bridge: write R sin(x − α) = R cos α sin x − R sin α cos x and equate coefficients to a and b in absolute value.
  5. Tactically, compute R = √(a² + b²) and tan α = b/a (using the magnitudes), then verify α is acute and check the result by substituting a test value of x.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Expand R sin(x − α) = R cos α · sin x − R sin α · cos x.
  2. Equate coefficients: R cos α = a (the sin x coefficient) and R sin α = |b| (the magnitude of the cos x coefficient).
  3. Compute R = √(a² + b²).
  4. Compute α = arctan(|b|/a), confirming 0 < α < π/2.
  5. Write the final form R sin(x − α) and sanity-check by expanding or testing one value of x.

Worked Micro-Example

Express $8\sin x - 15\cos x$ as $R\sin(x-\alpha)$ with $R>0$, $0<\alpha<\pi/2$. Expanding: $R\sin(x-\alpha)=R\cos\alpha\sin x - R\sin\alpha\cos x$, so $R\cos\alpha=8$, $R\sin\alpha=15$. Then $R=\sqrt{8^2+15^2}=17$ and $\tan\alpha=15/8$, giving $\alpha=\arctan(15/8)\approx 1.0808$. Hence $8\sin x-15\cos x = 17\sin(x-\arctan(15/8))$.

Variants — Same move, different surface

a sin x + b cos x with a, b > 0
Choose R sin(x + α) so the expansion R cos α sin x + R sin α cos x matches both positive coefficients.
a cos x + b sin x with a, b > 0
Choose R cos(x − α) so the expansion R cos α cos x + R sin α sin x matches both positive coefficients.
a cos x − b sin x with a, b > 0
Choose R cos(x + α) so the expansion R cos α cos x − R sin α sin x matches the +cos, −sin pattern.
Both coefficients negative, e.g. −a sin x − b cos x
Factor out −1 first to obtain a standard sign pattern, then apply the appropriate R-form and reattach the sign.

Common Misreadings

  • Defaulting to R cos(x − α) regardless of sign pattern, then being forced to allow negative R sin α.
  • Reading 'R sin(x − α)' as automatically meaning a − b sin x form rather than checking the actual expansion.

Pitfalls

  • Dropping the minus sign in the expansion of sin(x − α) and swapping which coefficient equals R sin α versus R cos α.
  • Computing tan α = a/|b| instead of |b|/a, i.e. inverting the ratio.
  • Reporting α in degrees when the question (or later use in calculus) requires radians, or vice versa.

A-level Scope Notes

Standard Edexcel IAL P3/P4 harmonic form technique; conventions R > 0 and 0 < α < π/2 are exactly those used in the specification.
