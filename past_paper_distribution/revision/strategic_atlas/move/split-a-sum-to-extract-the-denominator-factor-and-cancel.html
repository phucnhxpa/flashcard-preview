Trigonometry

Split a sum to extract the denominator factor and cancel

Family: Strategic Factorisation Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

Fires when proving a fractional trig identity whose denominator is a simple binomial like (sinθ − cosθ), (sinθ + cosθ), or (1 ± cosθ), and the numerator (after compound-angle or double-angle expansion) is a sum of products in which only some terms visibly carry the denominator. The cue is: 'one chunk already factors, the other almost does'. Equivalent phrasings: 'force the denominator to appear', 'rewrite a sum as (target factor) + remainder'. Especially common after expanding sin 3θ, cos 3θ via sin(2θ+θ), cos(2θ+θ).

Motivation — Why this works

Rational trig identities are proved by exhibiting the denominator as an explicit factor of the numerator so it can cancel. If the numerator splits as A·(sinθ+cosθ) + B·(sinθ−cosθ), one writes (sinθ+cosθ) = (sinθ−cosθ) + 2cosθ to convert the awkward group into 'denominator-factor + leftover'. The leftover is then handled separately, often collapsing via a double-angle identity. This is just the algebraic identity u = (u−v) + v applied to trig binomials, but it is the key unlocking step.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. I notice the denominator is (sinθ − cosθ) and my expanded numerator splits into one piece already carrying (sinθ − cosθ) and another piece carrying (sinθ + cosθ).
  2. I rule out polynomial long division and brute substitution t = tan(θ/2): both work but obscure the structure and waste time.
  3. I commit to forcing (sinθ − cosθ) to appear inside the second piece, because the partial factorisation already in front of me is a strong hint that the whole numerator is meant to factor.
  4. Bridge: I rewrite sinθ + cosθ = (sinθ − cosθ) + 2cosθ, distribute, and regroup so (sinθ − cosθ) becomes a common factor of two of the three resulting terms.
  5. Tactically I keep the leftover term (here 2 sin 2θ cosθ) aside, anticipating that after cancellation it will combine with sin²θ + cos²θ = 1 and a double-angle simplification to give the RHS.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Expand sin 3θ − cos 3θ via sin(2θ+θ) and cos(2θ+θ) to get sin 2θ(sinθ + cosθ) + cos 2θ(sinθ − cosθ).
  2. Write sinθ + cosθ = (sinθ − cosθ) + 2cosθ to introduce the denominator factor.
  3. Substitute and distribute: sin 2θ·(sinθ − cosθ) + 2 sin 2θ·cosθ + cos 2θ·(sinθ − cosθ).
  4. Group the two terms carrying (sinθ − cosθ): (sinθ − cosθ)(sin 2θ + cos 2θ) + 2 sin 2θ cosθ.
  5. Divide by (sinθ − cosθ): LHS = sin 2θ + cos 2θ + 2 sin 2θ cosθ/(sinθ − cosθ).
  6. Simplify the residual fraction (using 2 sin 2θ cosθ = sin 2θ·2cosθ and combining with the cancelled piece) to reach 2 + sin 2θ.

Worked Micro-Example

Prove $(\sin 3\theta - \cos 3\theta)/(\sin\theta - \cos\theta) = 2 + \sin 2\theta$. Expanding: numerator $= \sin 2\theta(\sin\theta+\cos\theta) + \cos 2\theta(\sin\theta-\cos\theta)$. Write $\sin\theta+\cos\theta = (\sin\theta-\cos\theta) + 2\cos\theta$, giving numerator $= (\sin\theta-\cos\theta)(\sin 2\theta + \cos 2\theta) + 2\sin 2\theta\cos\theta$. Dividing and simplifying $2\sin 2\theta\cos\theta/(\sin\theta-\cos\theta)$ together with $\cos 2\theta = -(\sin\theta-\cos\theta)(\sin\theta+\cos\theta)$ yields $2 + \sin 2\theta$.

Variants — Same move, different surface

Denominator (sinθ + cosθ) with numerator sin 3θ + cos 3θ
Split sinθ − cosθ = (sinθ + cosθ) − 2cosθ; same extract-and-cancel logic with reversed sign on the leftover.
Denominator (1 − cosθ) in half-angle proofs
Rewrite numerator using sin²θ = (1−cosθ)(1+cosθ) so (1−cosθ) becomes an explicit factor.
Denominator (1 + sinθ) with numerator involving cos²θ
Use cos²θ = (1−sinθ)(1+sinθ) to expose the factor directly.
Triple-angle route via sin³θ − cos³θ
Use difference-of-cubes a³−b³ = (a−b)(a²+ab+b²) so (sinθ−cosθ) factors immediately, then convert sin²θ+sinθcosθ+cos²θ = 1 + ½sin 2θ.

Common Misreadings

  • Treating the identity as needing R sin(θ+α) form because two trig functions of θ appear — this move is algebraic factorisation, not harmonic form.
  • Expanding both sides and trying to match term-by-term, missing that the denominator must be cancelled structurally.
  • Believing the split sinθ+cosθ = (sinθ−cosθ) + 2cosθ is a trig identity rather than a trivial algebraic rearrangement.

Pitfalls

  • Forgetting to multiply sin 2θ across both parts after the split, so the (sinθ−cosθ) factor is incomplete.
  • Sign error when invoking cos 2θ = −(sinθ−cosθ)(sinθ+cosθ); the minus sign is essential.
  • Cancelling (sinθ−cosθ) without first checking it is a factor of every numerator term — partial cancellation is invalid.
  • Stopping at sin 2θ + cos 2θ + (residual fraction) and not finishing the simplification of the residual.

A-level Scope Notes

Uses only compound-angle, double-angle, and Pythagorean identities from Edexcel IAL P3/P4 (and reinforced in FP1 trig identities). No content beyond the standard A-level trig identity toolkit is required.
