Binomial Expansion

State validity of binomial expansion via |u| < 1

Family: Validity Condition Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

Fires whenever a question asks for the 'range of values of $x$ for which the expansion is valid' (or equivalent phrasing) after a generalised binomial expansion $(1+u)^n$ with $n$ not a non-negative integer. Cues include the words 'valid', 'convergent', or 'state the range'. Also fires implicitly when an approximation must be justified for a particular substitution. The bracket is typically of the form $(1 + ax)^n$ or $(1 - ax)^n$ requiring conversion of $|u|<1$ into a bound on $x$.

Motivation — Why this works

The generalised binomial series $(1+u)^n = \sum \binom{n}{k} u^k$ for non-positive-integer $n$ is a power series with radius of convergence $1$, so it converges only for $|u| < 1$. When $u$ is a function of $x$ (e.g. $u = \frac{2x}{5}$), the convergence requirement transfers directly to $x$ via $|u(x)| < 1$. Edexcel mark schemes require a strict inequality and expect the bound on $x$ to reflect the inverse of the coefficient inside $u$.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notice the expansion was generalised (fractional/negative index), so a validity statement is required, not optional.
  2. Rule out $|x|<1$ as the answer — that would only apply if $u = x$ exactly; here $u$ is scaled.
  3. Commit to using $|u| < 1$ where $u$ is whatever sits inside the bracket alongside the $1$.
  4. Identify $u$ explicitly from the original bracket (e.g. $u = \frac{2x}{5}$) and write $|u| < 1$.
  5. Solve for $x$ by isolating it, choosing whether to present the answer as a modulus inequality or as a double inequality.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Identify $u$ from the bracket $(1 + u)^n$ used in the expansion.
  2. Write the convergence requirement $|u| < 1$.
  3. Substitute $u$ in terms of $x$ and rearrange to isolate $|x|$.
  4. State the final range as $|x| < k$ or equivalently $-k < x < k$.

Worked Micro-Example

Given the expansion of $\left(1 + \tfrac{2x}{5}\right)^{-3}$, validity requires $\left|\tfrac{2x}{5}\right| < 1$. Hence $|2x| < 5$, so $|x| < \tfrac{5}{2}$, i.e. $-\tfrac{5}{2} < x < \tfrac{5}{2}$.

Variants — Same move, different surface

Bracket $(1 - \tfrac{3x}{4})^n$ with non-integer $n$
Sign inside $u$ is irrelevant to $|u|<1$; condition becomes $\left|\tfrac{3x}{4}\right| < 1 \Rightarrow |x| < \tfrac{4}{3}$.
Bracket of form $(a + bx)^n$ not yet in standard form
First factor out $a^n$ to obtain $\left(1 + \tfrac{bx}{a}\right)^n$, then apply $\left|\tfrac{bx}{a}\right| < 1$.
Product/quotient of two binomial expansions
Take the intersection of the individual validity ranges; the smaller bound on $|x|$ wins.
Expansion in powers of $\tfrac{1}{x}$ for large $x$
After factoring out $x$, condition $\left|\tfrac{c}{x}\right| < 1$ gives $|x| > c$ rather than $|x| < c$.

Common Misreadings

  • Treating $u$ as $x$ and stating $|x| < 1$ regardless of the coefficient inside the bracket.
  • Reading the coefficient directly as the bound, e.g. writing $|x| < \tfrac{2}{5}$ instead of $|x| < \tfrac{5}{2}$.
  • Quoting only a one-sided bound such as $x < \tfrac{5}{2}$ and omitting the negative end.

Pitfalls

  • Using $\leq$ instead of $<$ — the endpoints are not included.
  • Forgetting to invert the coefficient when solving $|bx/a| < 1$ for $x$.
  • Failing to factor out $a$ first when the bracket is $(a+bx)^n$, leading to an incorrect $u$.
  • When two expansions are multiplied, quoting only one of the two ranges instead of the intersection.

A-level Scope Notes

Fully within Edexcel IAL P3/P4 generalised binomial expansion; only strict inequalities $|u|<1$ are examined and no formal radius-of-convergence proof is required.
