Coordinate Geometry

Take negative reciprocal of tangent gradient to obtain normal gradient

Family: Perpendicularity Condition Frequency: 26 questions

Trigger — When to use

The question explicitly asks for the equation of the NORMAL to a curve at a given point (or asks where the normal meets an axis/another line). Differentiation only delivers the tangent gradient m_T = dy/dx, so a perpendicularity conversion is required before writing the line equation. Watch for keywords like 'normal at P', 'perpendicular to the curve at', or 'line through P perpendicular to the tangent'. The same trigger appears whenever a tangent gradient is known but the line actually needed is perpendicular to it (e.g. radius vs tangent in circle geometry).

Motivation — Why this works

Two non-vertical lines are perpendicular iff the product of their gradients is −1, i.e. m₁·m₂ = −1. Therefore m_N = −1/m_T, which is the single algebraic bridge from the calculus output (tangent gradient) to the geometric object requested (normal line). Without this step, the student would unwittingly write the tangent equation instead of the normal. The operation is purely algebraic — flip the fraction AND change its sign — and avoids any need to reason about angles directly.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Read the question carefully and notice the word 'normal' (not 'tangent') — this is the decisive cue that dy/dx alone is not the gradient of the line I will write down.
  2. Rule out using m_T directly in y − y₀ = m(x − x₀); that would give the tangent line, the wrong object.
  3. Recall the perpendicularity identity m₁m₂ = −1, valid for any two non-vertical perpendicular lines, and rearrange it as m_N = −1/m_T.
  4. Evaluate dy/dx at the given x-coordinate first to obtain a concrete numerical m_T, then apply the negative reciprocal — flip the fraction AND negate the sign in one combined step.
  5. Check the sign carefully: if m_T is already negative, the double negative gives a positive m_N (e.g. m_T = −2 ⇒ m_N = +1/2).
  6. Handle the special case m_T = 0 separately: the normal is vertical, with equation x = x₀, since −1/0 is undefined.
  7. Substitute m_N together with the point (x₀, y₀) into the point-gradient form to write the normal's equation.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Compute m_T = dy/dx evaluated at the given point.
  2. Apply the perpendicularity condition: m_T · m_N = −1.
  3. Solve for the normal gradient: m_N = −1/m_T (flip the fraction and change its sign).
  4. Simplify carefully, watching the sign — if m_T = a/b then m_N = −b/a.
  5. Substitute m_N and the point (x₀, y₀) into y − y₀ = m_N(x − x₀) to obtain the normal's equation.

Worked Micro-Example

At P(2, −3) on a curve, suppose dy/dx = −2 so $m_T = -2$. Then $m_N = -\dfrac{1}{m_T} = -\dfrac{1}{-2} = \dfrac{1}{2}$. The normal is $y - (-3) = \tfrac{1}{2}(x - 2)$, i.e. $y = \tfrac{1}{2}x - 4$. Setting $x = 0$ gives the y-intercept $B = (0, -4)$.

Variants — Same move, different surface

Tangent gradient is an integer k (e.g. m_T = −5)
m_N = −1/k directly; e.g. m_T = −5 gives m_N = 1/5 (mind the double negative).
Tangent gradient is a fraction a/b (e.g. m_T = 797/8)
m_N = −b/a; flip the fraction and negate, e.g. m_T = 797/8 ⇒ m_N = −8/797.
Horizontal tangent (m_T = 0)
Formula is undefined; the normal is vertical with equation x = x₀.
Vertical tangent (dy/dx undefined, dx/dy = 0)
Normal is horizontal with equation y = y₀.
Circle geometry — radius and tangent
The radius from centre to point of tangency is the normal direction; tangent gradient is the negative reciprocal of the radius gradient.

Common Misreadings

  • Confusing 'normal' with 'tangent' and using m_T directly in the line equation — the single most common error on normal-line questions.
  • Negating without reciprocating (writing m_N = −m_T, e.g. 5 instead of 1/5).
  • Reciprocating without negating (writing m_N = 1/m_T, dropping the minus sign).

Pitfalls

  • Sign error from double negative: writing −1/(−5) = −1/5 instead of +1/5.
  • Inverting incorrectly for fractional m_T: writing −a/b instead of −b/a (e.g. −797/8 instead of −8/797).
  • Forgetting to evaluate dy/dx at the specific x-coordinate first — applying the negative reciprocal to the symbolic derivative rather than the numerical gradient.
  • Applying the formula blindly when m_T = 0, producing division by zero rather than recognising the vertical normal.

A-level Scope Notes

Fully within Edexcel IAL Pure Mathematics (P1/P2/P3/P4). The perpendicularity condition m₁m₂ = −1 is assumed knowledge and not in the formula booklet. Students should not generalise to 3D (where perpendicularity uses dot products of direction vectors, not gradients).
