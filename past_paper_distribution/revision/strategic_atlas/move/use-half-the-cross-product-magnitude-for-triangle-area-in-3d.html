Vectors

Use half the cross product magnitude for triangle area in 3D

Family: Cross Product Normal Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

The question gives three points $A$, $B$, $C$ in 3D (or two vectors from a common vertex) and asks for the area of the triangle they form. Phrasings include 'find the area of triangle ABC', 'area of the triangle with vertices...', or follow-up parts that need a normal vector to a plane through three points. It also fires whenever the area is needed as a stepping stone to a tetrahedron volume via the scalar triple product.

Motivation — Why this works

By definition $|\mathbf{u} \times \mathbf{v}| = |\mathbf{u}||\mathbf{v}|\sin\theta$, which is the area of the parallelogram spanned by $\mathbf{u}$ and $\mathbf{v}$; the triangle is exactly half of this parallelogram. Computing $\overrightarrow{AB} \times \overrightarrow{AC}$ packages the $\sin\theta$ factor into a single determinant, bypassing the two-step route via $\cos\theta$ from the dot product. The resulting cross product vector is also a normal to the plane $ABC$, so it is often reusable in later parts (plane equation, scalar triple product, distance from a point).

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notice that three 3D points are given and an area is requested — coordinate-plane projection or Heron's formula are awkward in 3D.
  2. Rule out $\tfrac12 ab\sin\theta$ via the dot product (two stages: $\cos\theta$, then $\sin\theta$) and Heron's (three side lengths plus a square root chain).
  3. Commit to $\tfrac12|\overrightarrow{AB} \times \overrightarrow{AC}|$: one determinant, one magnitude, and the intermediate vector doubles as a plane normal for any follow-up.
  4. Bridge: pick a common vertex (usually $A$) and write the two displacement vectors $\overrightarrow{AB}$ and $\overrightarrow{AC}$ component-wise.
  5. Tactically, expand the $3\times3$ determinant along the top row, watching the alternating sign on the $\mathbf{j}$-cofactor, then compute $\sqrt{x^2+y^2+z^2}$ and halve.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Form $\overrightarrow{AB} = B - A$ and $\overrightarrow{AC} = C - A$.
  2. Set up $\overrightarrow{AB} \times \overrightarrow{AC} = \begin{vmatrix} \mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\ a_1 & a_2 & a_3 \\ c_1 & c_2 & c_3 \end{vmatrix}$.
  3. $\mathbf{i}$-component: $a_2 c_3 - a_3 c_2$.
  4. $\mathbf{j}$-component: $-(a_1 c_3 - a_3 c_1)$ — keep the leading minus sign.
  5. $\mathbf{k}$-component: $a_1 c_2 - a_2 c_1$.
  6. Compute $|\overrightarrow{AB} \times \overrightarrow{AC}| = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2 + z^2}$.
  7. Area of triangle $= \tfrac12|\overrightarrow{AB} \times \overrightarrow{AC}|$.

Worked Micro-Example

Let $A=(1,2,3)$, $B=(5,-2,2)$, $C=(4,3,4)$. Then $\overrightarrow{AB}=(4,-4,-1)$, $\overrightarrow{AC}=(3,1,1)$. Cross product: $\mathbf{i}((-4)(1)-(-1)(1))-\mathbf{j}((4)(1)-(-1)(3))+\mathbf{k}((4)(1)-(-4)(3)) = (-3,-7,16)$. Magnitude $=\sqrt{9+49+256}=\sqrt{314}$, so area $=\tfrac12\sqrt{314}$.

Variants — Same move, different surface

Parallelogram with adjacent sides $\overrightarrow{AB}$, $\overrightarrow{AD}$
Drop the $\tfrac12$: area $=|\overrightarrow{AB}\times\overrightarrow{AD}|$.
Triangle in 2D with vertices $(x_i,y_i)$
Embed in 3D with $z=0$, or use the shoelace form $\tfrac12|x_1(y_2-y_3)+x_2(y_3-y_1)+x_3(y_1-y_2)|$.
Tetrahedron volume from four points
Reuse the same $\overrightarrow{AB}\times\overrightarrow{AC}$ inside a scalar triple product: $V=\tfrac16|\overrightarrow{AD}\cdot(\overrightarrow{AB}\times\overrightarrow{AC})|$.
Need a normal to the plane through $A,B,C$
Take the cross product without halving or taking magnitude; use it directly as $\mathbf{n}$ in the plane equation $\mathbf{n}\cdot\mathbf{r}=\mathbf{n}\cdot\mathbf{a}$.

Common Misreadings

  • Forgetting the $\tfrac12$ and reporting the parallelogram area as the triangle area.
  • Using $\overrightarrow{AB}$ and $\overrightarrow{BC}$ (not from a common vertex), which still gives the right magnitude but is a frequent source of sign/component slips.
  • Treating $|\overrightarrow{AB}\times\overrightarrow{AC}|$ as $|\overrightarrow{AB}||\overrightarrow{AC}|$, ignoring the $\sin\theta$ structure.

Pitfalls

  • Dropping the negative sign on the $\mathbf{j}$-cofactor when expanding the determinant — the most common cross-product error.
  • Sign mistakes inside the $2\times2$ minors, especially with products like $(-1)(-3)$ or $(-4)(1)$.
  • Computing $\sqrt{x+y+z}$ instead of $\sqrt{x^2+y^2+z^2}$ for the magnitude.
  • Forgetting to halve at the end, or halving twice (once on the vector, once on the magnitude).

A-level Scope Notes

Fully within Edexcel IAL Further Pure: cross product, its magnitude as parallelogram area, and triangle area as half of that are standard FP3 vector content; the same cross product feeds plane equations and scalar triple products.
