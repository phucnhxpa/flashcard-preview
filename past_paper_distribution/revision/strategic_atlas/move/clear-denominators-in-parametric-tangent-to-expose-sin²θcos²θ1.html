Trigonometry

Clear denominators in parametric tangent to expose sin²θ+cos²θ=1

Family: Identity Selection Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

Fires when deriving the tangent (or normal) to a curve given parametrically — typically an ellipse P(acosθ, bsinθ) or similar — and the point-slope form contains sinθ in a denominator. After clearing the fraction, the equation has matching coefficients of sin²θ and cos²θ on one side, with the variable terms (in x and y) on the other. The target form has a constant RHS, signalling that the θ-dependence must collapse.

Motivation — Why this works

The Pythagorean identity sin²θ + cos²θ = 1 is the only mechanism in A-level scope that converts a symmetric combination of sin²θ and cos²θ with equal coefficients into a constant. Parametric tangent equations on conics are engineered so that after fraction-clearing, exactly this symmetric pair appears, allowing the identity to deliver the standard constant RHS. Recognising this in advance lets the student multiply strategically rather than expand blindly.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notices the target tangent form has a constant RHS, but the point-slope expression carries cosθ and sinθ throughout — so θ must cancel somewhere.
  2. Rules out expanding and collecting in x, y first (that leaves messy θ-coefficients); also rules out dividing further, since denominators already obstruct.
  3. Commits to clearing the sinθ denominator by multiplying through, anticipating that this will produce sin²θ and cos²θ terms with equal coefficients.
  4. Bridges to the first operation: multiply both sides by the denominator (e.g. 3sinθ), then group constants on one side and x, y terms on the other.
  5. Tactically checks that the sin²θ and cos²θ coefficients match before invoking sin²θ + cos²θ = 1; if they don’t match, suspects an algebra error.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Write the tangent in point-slope form using dy/dx from parametric differentiation.
  2. Multiply both sides by the denominator of the gradient to clear the fraction.
  3. Move all x- and y-terms to the LHS and all θ-only terms to the RHS.
  4. Group the RHS as k(sin²θ + cos²θ) where k is the common coefficient.
  5. Apply sin²θ + cos²θ = 1 to reduce the RHS to the constant k.

Worked Micro-Example

Ellipse $x=3\cos\theta$, $y=2\sin\theta$ at $P$. Then $\dfrac{dy}{dx}=-\dfrac{2\cos\theta}{3\sin\theta}$, so $y-2\sin\theta=-\dfrac{2\cos\theta}{3\sin\theta}(x-3\cos\theta)$. Multiply by $3\sin\theta$: $3y\sin\theta-6\sin^2\theta=-2x\cos\theta+6\cos^2\theta$. Rearrange: $2x\cos\theta+3y\sin\theta=6(\sin^2\theta+\cos^2\theta)=6$.

Variants — Same move, different surface

Standard ellipse $x^2/a^2+y^2/b^2=1$ with $P(a\cos\theta,b\sin\theta)$
Same multiply-and-collapse yields $\frac{x\cos\theta}{a}+\frac{y\sin\theta}{b}=1$.
Hyperbola with $P(a\sec\theta,b\tan\theta)$
Clear $\tan\theta$ denominator; RHS collapses via $\sec^2\theta-\tan^2\theta=1$ rather than the Pythagorean form.
Rectangular hyperbola $xy=c^2$ with $P(ct,c/t)$
No trig identity needed; clearance instead exposes a $t^2$/$1$ structure that simplifies to a constant directly.
Normal (not tangent) to a parametric ellipse
Same clearance idea, but the resulting RHS combines $\sin\theta\cos\theta(a^2-b^2)$ — no Pythagorean collapse; expect a θ-dependent RHS.

Common Misreadings

  • Treating the sin²θ and cos²θ terms as needing numerical evaluation rather than identity collapse.
  • Believing the identity only applies when sin²θ and cos²θ are isolated, not when they appear with a common coefficient.
  • Confusing the tangent derivation with the normal, where no Pythagorean collapse occurs.

Pitfalls

  • Failing to group sin²θ and cos²θ together because they sit on opposite sides of the equation after expansion.
  • Sign errors when transposing $-2x\cos\theta$ or $-6\sin^2\theta$ across the equals sign.
  • Multiplying only one side by $3\sin\theta$, breaking the equation.
  • Mismatched coefficients of sin²θ and cos²θ (often from arithmetic slips) which prevent the identity from applying cleanly.

A-level Scope Notes

Fully within Edexcel IAL P3/P4 (parametric differentiation, tangents to parametric curves) and Further Pure (conics). Uses only the Pythagorean identity sin²θ+cos²θ=1, standard at AS level.
