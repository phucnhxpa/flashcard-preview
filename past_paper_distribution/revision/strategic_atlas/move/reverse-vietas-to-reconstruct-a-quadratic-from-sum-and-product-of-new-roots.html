Reverse Vieta's to reconstruct a quadratic from sum and product of new roots

Family: Reverse Vieta'S Frequency: 5 questions

Trigger — When to use

The question asks to 'form/find a quadratic equation whose roots are …' where the new roots are expressions in α and β (e.g. α+1/β, α/β + β/α, (α±β)², α²+β² etc.). After computing the sum S' and product P' of these new roots (typically using known α+β and αβ from the original equation), the next step is to assemble the equation. Equivalent phrasings include 'write down a quadratic with roots…', 'show that the equation with roots … is …', or 'find an equation with integer coefficients whose roots are …'.

Motivation — Why this works

Vieta's formulas are reversible: any monic quadratic with roots R₁, R₂ is x² − (R₁+R₂)x + R₁R₂ = 0. Building the equation directly from S' and P' avoids ever needing to find the new roots individually or to expand (x−R₁)(x−R₂) by hand, which keeps signs and fractions under control. This is the standard FP1 endpoint for 'new equation from roots' questions and mirrors, in reverse, the very first move used to extract α+β and αβ from the original equation.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. I notice the question wants a new quadratic, not the new roots themselves, so I focus on producing coefficients rather than solving anything.
  2. I rule out substituting the new-root expression into the original equation (messy) and rule out forming (x−R₁)(x−R₂) explicitly (sign-error prone, especially with fractions or negatives).
  3. I commit to reverse Vieta's because I already have, or can quickly compute, S' = sum of new roots and P' = product of new roots from α+β and αβ.
  4. Bridge to operation: I write the template x² − S'x + P' = 0 and substitute my values, being careful that the coefficient of x is −S' (not +S').
  5. Tactical decision: if S' or P' is fractional, I multiply through by the LCM of denominators to obtain integer coefficients; I then check the integer coefficients share no common factor, since exam mark schemes expect the simplest integer form.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Compute S' = R₁ + R₂ and P' = R₁ · R₂ in terms of α+β and αβ, then evaluate numerically.
  2. Write the monic quadratic: x² − S'x + P' = 0.
  3. If S' or P' is a fraction, multiply through by the LCM of denominators to clear fractions.
  4. Check the resulting integer coefficients are coprime; divide out any common factor.
  5. State the final equation, e.g. ax² + bx + c = 0.

Worked Micro-Example

Suppose α+β = 3 and αβ = 2, and the new roots are α/β and β/α. Then S' = (α²+β²)/(αβ) = ((α+β)²−2αβ)/(αβ) = (9−4)/2 = 5/2 and P' = 1. Reverse Vieta's gives x² − (5/2)x + 1 = 0; multiplying by 2 yields 2x² − 5x + 2 = 0.

Variants — Same move, different surface

Fractional S' or P' (e.g. S' = 28/25, P' = 2/5)
Multiply the whole equation by the LCM of denominators to obtain integer coefficients (e.g. 25x² − 28x + 10 = 0).
Question demands a specific leading coefficient (e.g. 'with leading coefficient 2')
Multiply x² − S'x + P' = 0 through by the required leading coefficient: 2x² − 2S'x + 2P' = 0.
Three new roots (FP1 cubic case)
Use x³ − (Σ new roots)x² + (Σ pairwise products)x − (triple product) = 0; same reverse-Vieta logic, extended via elementary symmetric polynomials σ₁, σ₂, σ₃.
New roots given symbolically (e.g. α²+1, β²+1)
Express S' and P' symbolically in terms of α+β and αβ first, then substitute numerical values before assembling x² − S'x + P' = 0.

Common Misreadings

  • Trying to substitute the new-root expression into the original equation instead of building a fresh equation from S' and P'.
  • Treating the question as asking for the new roots themselves rather than a quadratic equation.
  • Confusing reverse Vieta's with simply factoring (x−R₁)(x−R₂), which invites sign errors when roots are negative or fractional.

Pitfalls

  • Sign error: writing x² + S'x + P' = 0 instead of x² − S'x + P' = 0 — the coefficient of x is minus the sum.
  • Mishandling a negative P' (e.g. writing x² − S'x − |P'| as x² − S'x + |P'|).
  • Leaving fractional coefficients in the final answer when the mark scheme expects integer coefficients.
  • Forgetting to divide out a common factor from the integer coefficients, leaving a non-simplified form.

A-level Scope Notes

Fully within Edexcel IAL Further Pure 1 (FP1), 'Roots of quadratic equations'. Vieta's formulas α+β = −b/a and αβ = c/a are standard FP1 content (not in the formula booklet — must be memorised). Avoid invoking general symmetric-function theory or Newton's identities beyond α²+β², α³+β³ — those identities are derived in-line from (α+β) and αβ, not quoted.
