Trigger — When to use

Fires in 'form a new quadratic whose roots are...' problems where the two new roots are reciprocal-paired in α and β, e.g. (kα/β, kβ/α), (α/β, β/α), or (α·f(β), β·f(α)) with symmetric f. The give-away is that multiplying the two new roots causes every α and β to cancel, leaving a pure constant. Often the question asks for both the sum-coefficient and the product-coefficient of the new quadratic, and only the sum is genuinely hard.

Motivation — Why this works

Vieta's relations say the product of the new roots equals (constant term)/(leading coeff) of the new quadratic. When the new roots are reciprocal-structured, their product is independent of α and β, so this coefficient is fixed by inspection without using the original sum/product values at all. The structural symmetry — each root is the 'flip' of the other in α↔β — guarantees full cancellation. Recognising this saves substituting messy values of α+β or αβ into a product expression.

Thought Process — Step-by-step reasoning

  1. I notice the two new roots are mirror images under α↔β, e.g. 3α/β and 3β/α.
  2. I rule out brute-force expansion via α+β and αβ for the product part — that machinery is needed for the sum, not here.
  3. I commit because multiplying the roots will cancel α and β completely, giving a constant.
  4. Bridge: I write the product directly: (3α/β)(3β/α) = 9αβ/(αβ) = 9.
  5. Tactically, I still use Vieta on the original quadratic for the sum (the hard part), but the product coefficient drops out for free.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Identify the two new roots and check if they are reciprocal-paired in α, β.
  2. Multiply them symbolically; cancel matching α and β factors to obtain a constant c.
  3. Write Vieta's product for the new quadratic: (constant term)/(leading coeff) = c.
  4. Solve for the unknown coefficient directly.

Worked Micro-Example

Let α, β be roots of 4x² − 5x + B = 0. Form the quadratic with roots 3α/β and 3β/α. Product of new roots = (3α/β)(3β/α) = 9. By Vieta on the new quadratic (with leading coefficient 4 preserved), B/4 = 9, so B = 36 — no need to compute α+β or αβ.

Variants — Same move, different surface

New roots α/β and β/α
Product = 1 directly; constant term equals leading coefficient.
New roots kα/β and kβ/α
Product = k², independent of α,β.
New roots 1/α and 1/β
Product = 1/(αβ); not a pure constant — needs Vieta value, but still telescopes nicely.
New roots α+k and β+k
No cancellation: product = αβ + k(α+β) + k²; must use both Vieta relations.
New roots α² /β and β²/α
Product = αβ — collapses to a single Vieta quantity, not a constant, but still a one-step read-off.

Common Misreadings

  • Confusing 'product of roots' with 'sum of roots' and trying to compute (3α/β)+(3β/α) = 3(α²+β²)/(αβ) instead.
  • Assuming the leading coefficient of the new quadratic must be 1, missing that the question may keep the original leading coefficient.
  • Believing α and β must be found explicitly before any coefficient can be determined.

Pitfalls

  • Forgetting to match the leading coefficient of the new quadratic when applying Vieta (e.g. setting product = B instead of B/4).
  • Cancelling α/β incorrectly when α or β could be zero — check the original quadratic has nonzero constant term.
  • Substituting numeric Vieta values into the product unnecessarily and making arithmetic slips.

A-level Scope Notes

Sits squarely in Edexcel IAL Further Pure 1 'roots of polynomial equations' — forming new quadratics from symmetric functions of α, β using Vieta's formulas.
