Algebra & Functions

Use Vieta's product-of-roots to pin down a repeated real root

Family: Vieta'S Formulas Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

A monic polynomial of known degree has its constant term given, some roots already identified (often a complex conjugate pair from an earlier part), and one or more remaining roots — possibly repeated — to determine. The unknown coefficients in the middle of the polynomial block direct long division. The phrase 'repeated root' or 'double root' alongside a known constant term is the strongest cue. Whenever the unknown root enters the product cleanly (e.g. as r or r²), Vieta's product relation collapses the problem to a single equation.

Motivation — Why this works

For a monic polynomial x^n + ... + c = 0, Vieta gives the product of all roots as (−1)^n · c. The constant term is therefore a direct algebraic fingerprint of the roots, independent of the intermediate coefficients. If a subset of roots has known product (e.g. a conjugate pair contributes |z|²), the remaining factor of the product isolates the unknown root. This bypasses the need to recover any other coefficient and avoids polynomial division.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notices that the constant term is given explicitly while several middle coefficients are unknown.
  2. Rules out polynomial long division: it requires knowing the divisor coefficients, which depend on data not yet available.
  3. Commits to Vieta because the product of roots is determined purely by the constant term and the leading coefficient.
  4. Bridges by computing the product of the already-known roots (here the conjugate pair gives |4+3i|² = 25).
  5. Sets up product_known × r^k = ±(constant), solves for r, then applies the sign/positivity constraint to pick the correct root.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Write Vieta's product-of-roots: for monic x^n + ... + c, product = (−1)^n c.
  2. List all n roots, including multiplicity (a repeated root r appears as r·r).
  3. Compute the product of the known roots; for a conjugate pair a±bi this is a²+b².
  4. Form a single equation in the unknown root(s) and solve.
  5. Apply any stated constraint ('positive', 'real', 'integer') to select the correct value.

Worked Micro-Example

Solve $x^4 + Ax^3 + Bx^2 + Cx + 225 = 0$ given $4+3i$ is a root and the remaining real root is repeated and positive. Roots: $4+3i,\ 4-3i,\ r,\ r$. Product $= 225$, so $(4+3i)(4-3i)\cdot r^2 = 25r^2 = 225$. Thus $r^2 = 9$, and positivity gives $r = 3$.

Variants — Same move, different surface

Monic cubic x³ + px² + qx + d = 0 with one known real root and a repeated unknown root
Product of roots = −d (sign flips for odd degree); set known_root · r² = −d.
Coefficient of x^{n−1} is given instead of the constant term
Use Vieta's sum-of-roots: sum = −(coefficient of x^{n−1}); known_sum + 2r = −A gives r directly.
Non-monic polynomial a·x^n + ... + c = 0
Product of roots = (−1)^n c/a; divide through by leading coefficient before applying Vieta.
Quartic with two distinct unknown real roots r and s rather than a repeated root
Vieta alone gives one equation in two unknowns; combine with sum-of-roots or another Vieta relation to solve simultaneously.

Common Misreadings

  • Reading 'repeated root' as a single extra root instead of two equal roots, leading to r¹ instead of r² in the product.
  • Taking the product of roots as −225 for a quartic; the sign rule (−1)^n flips only for odd degree.
  • Assuming the conjugate pair contributes 4²−(3i)² = 25 by accident rather than via |z|² = a²+b².

Pitfalls

  • Reporting r = ±3 without invoking the 'positive' constraint to discard r = −3.
  • Forgetting to divide by the leading coefficient if the polynomial is not monic.
  • Mixing up product-of-roots and sum-of-roots formulas under time pressure.

A-level Scope Notes

Vieta's relations for quadratics, cubics and quartics with real or complex conjugate roots are standard in Edexcel IAL Further Pure (FP1/FP2) and complement the conjugate-root theorem used in Core Pure.
