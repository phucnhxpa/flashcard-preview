Algebra & Functions

Decompose modulus graph via chain of transformations from y = |x|

Family: Transformation Sequencing Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

Fires when a function of the form $y = a|x - p| + q$ or $y = q - a|x - p|$ is presented and a sketch (or key features: vertex, intercepts, gradients) is required, often with a parameter such as $k$. Common cues are 'sketch the graph of $y = k - 2|x|$', 'state the coordinates of the vertex in terms of $k$', or follow-up parts asking how many solutions an equation has. Also fires when comparing the graph to the parent $y = |x|$ is faster than piecewise analysis.

Motivation — Why this works

Every modulus function of this form is built from $y = |x|$ by a composition of stretches, reflections, and translations, and these transformations act predictably on the V-shape. Decomposing the formula in the order (horizontal shift, stretch, reflection, vertical shift) immediately fixes the vertex, the gradient of each arm, and the orientation (V or inverted V). This avoids casework on $x \ge 0$ vs $x < 0$ and gives a sketch that is structurally correct, which is essential when later parts use the graph to count intersections with a line $y = mx + c$.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notice the expression contains $|x|$ (or $|x - p|$) wrapped by multiplicative and additive constants — this is a transformed parent graph, not a new shape to plot pointwise.
  2. Rule out plotting via tables of values or solving piecewise inequalities: both are slower and more error-prone for a sketch worth a few marks.
  3. Commit to the transformation chain because the vertex, slopes, and orientation drop out directly, and intercepts are then a one-line calculation.
  4. Bridge: read the formula outwards from $|x|$ — identify in order any inside shift, the multiplier on $|\cdot|$ (stretch and possibly reflection), and the outside constant (vertical shift).
  5. Tactically, apply the inside shift first, then the stretch, then the reflection (if the coefficient is negative), then the vertical translation; track the vertex through each step rather than redrawing.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Identify the parent: $y = |x|$ (V-shape, vertex $(0,0)$, gradients $\pm 1$).
  2. Apply any horizontal shift inside the modulus: $|x - p|$ moves the vertex to $(p, 0)$.
  3. Apply the vertical stretch by $|a|$: gradients become $\pm |a|$.
  4. If the coefficient of $|\cdot|$ is negative, reflect in the $x$-axis to get an inverted V.
  5. Apply the vertical shift: add $q$ (or $k$) so the vertex moves to $(p, q)$.
  6. Compute axis intercepts from the final equation to label the sketch.

Worked Micro-Example

Sketch $y = k - 2|x|$ for $k > 0$. Start from $y = |x|$; stretch vertically by $2$ to get $y = 2|x|$ (gradients $\pm 2$); reflect in the $x$-axis to get $y = -2|x|$ (inverted V, vertex $(0,0)$); translate up by $k$ to get $y = k - 2|x|$ with vertex $(0, k)$. Setting $y = 0$ gives $|x| = k/2$, so $x$-intercepts are $\left(\pm \tfrac{k}{2}, 0\right)$.

Variants — Same move, different surface

$y = |x - a| + b$ with $a, b > 0$
No reflection or stretch; translate the V-shape right by $a$ and up by $b$, vertex at $(a, b)$, gradients $\pm 1$.
$y = |2x - k|$
Factor inside as $2|x - k/2|$: horizontal translation by $k/2$ then vertical stretch by $2$, giving vertex $(k/2, 0)$ and gradients $\pm 2$.
$y = a - |x - p|$
Translate right by $p$, reflect in $x$-axis, then translate up by $a$, giving an inverted V with vertex $(p, a)$ and gradients $\pm 1$.
$y = |\,f(x)\,|$ where $f$ is linear, e.g. $y = |3x + 6|$
Rewrite as $3|x + 2|$: vertex at $(-2, 0)$, gradients $\pm 3$; reflection of the parent in the $x$-axis is implicit only on the part where $f(x) < 0$.

Common Misreadings

  • Reading $y = k - 2|x|$ as $y = |k - 2x|$, conflating an external subtraction with subtraction inside the modulus.
  • Treating the coefficient $2$ in $|2x - k|$ as a horizontal effect only and not factoring it out, leading to a wrong vertex.
  • Assuming $k$ must be positive and drawing the graph above the $x$-axis without considering sign of $k$ when the question allows it.

Pitfalls

  • Forgetting the reflection when the coefficient of $|\cdot|$ is negative, drawing a V opening upward with vertex $(0, k)$.
  • Placing $x$-intercepts at $\pm k$ instead of $\pm k/2$ by ignoring the factor of $2$.
  • Applying transformations in the wrong order (e.g. translating before stretching when the stretch is on the modulus argument) and shifting the vertex incorrectly.
  • Labelling only the vertex and omitting intercepts (or vice versa), losing presentation marks on a 'sketch showing key features' question.
