Differential Equations

Select CF form by classifying auxiliary equation roots

Family: Cf Structure Selection Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

Fires when solving a homogeneous linear second-order ODE with constant coefficients ay'' + by' + cy = 0, immediately after forming and solving the auxiliary equation am^2 + bm + c = 0. The student has two root values (or a repeated root) and must commit to one of three CF templates. Cues include phrases like 'find the complementary function', 'general solution of the homogeneous equation', or being mid-way through a particular-integral problem.

Motivation — Why this works

A second-order linear homogeneous ODE has a 2-dimensional solution space, so the CF must contain two linearly independent solutions with two arbitrary constants. The discriminant of the auxiliary equation determines which pair of independent solutions exists: distinct real roots give two independent exponentials; a repeated root forces an xe^{mx} term to supply a second independent solution; complex conjugate roots p ± qi combine via Euler's formula into e^{px}(A cos qx + B sin qx). Choosing the template that matches the root type guarantees the Wronskian is non-zero and the general solution is complete.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. I notice I have solved the auxiliary equation and obtained specific root values — e.g. m = 1 and m = 3/2.
  2. I check whether the roots are real-and-distinct, real-and-repeated, or complex conjugates by inspecting the discriminant or the values themselves.
  3. Since the two roots are different real numbers, I rule out the (A + Bx)e^{mx} form and the e^{px}(A cos qx + B sin qx) form.
  4. I commit to the distinct-real template y_CF = Ae^{m_1 x} + Be^{m_2 x}, knowing this gives two independent exponentials.
  5. I substitute the actual root values carefully, keeping fractional exponents in surd/fraction form rather than decimals, and label A, B as arbitrary constants.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Solve the auxiliary equation am^2 + bm + c = 0 for m.
  2. Classify the roots: distinct real, repeated real, or complex conjugate p ± qi.
  3. Select the matching CF template:
  4. • distinct real m_1, m_2: y_CF = Ae^{m_1 x} + Be^{m_2 x}
  5. • repeated root m: y_CF = (A + Bx)e^{mx}
  6. • complex p ± qi: y_CF = e^{px}(A cos qx + B sin qx)
  7. Substitute the specific root values into the chosen template with two arbitrary constants A, B.

Worked Micro-Example

Solve 2y'' - 5y' + 3y = 0. Auxiliary equation: 2m^2 - 5m + 3 = 0, giving (2m-3)(m-1) = 0, so m = 1 or m = 3/2. Roots are real and distinct, so y_CF = Ae^{x} + Be^{(3/2)x}.

Variants — Same move, different surface

Repeated real root, e.g. m^2 - 4m + 4 = 0 giving m = 2 (double).
Use y_CF = (A + Bx)e^{2x} to provide a second independent solution.
Complex conjugate roots, e.g. m^2 + 2m + 5 = 0 giving m = -1 ± 2i.
Use y_CF = e^{-x}(A cos 2x + B sin 2x).
Pure imaginary roots, e.g. m^2 + 9 = 0 giving m = ±3i.
p = 0, so y_CF = A cos 3x + B sin 3x (no exponential factor).
Roots with one zero, e.g. m^2 - 3m = 0 giving m = 0, 3.
y_CF = A + Be^{3x}, since e^{0·x} = 1.

Common Misreadings

  • Treating two close roots (e.g. m = 1 and m = 1.5) as if they were repeated.
  • Reading complex roots p ± qi but forgetting the e^{px} envelope when p ≠ 0.
  • Confusing the auxiliary equation's roots with values of y, leading to writing y = m_1 x + m_2 instead of exponentials.

Pitfalls

  • Misidentifying distinct roots as repeated and writing (A + Bx)e^{mx} incorrectly.
  • Dropping the x in the Bx e^{mx} term for the repeated case, leaving only one independent solution.
  • Omitting the e^{px} factor in the complex case when the real part p is non-zero.
  • Converting fractional roots like 3/2 to decimals (1.5), risking arithmetic slips when applying initial conditions.

A-level Scope Notes

Fully within Edexcel IAL Further Pure scope: second-order linear ODEs with constant coefficients, where the three auxiliary-root cases are the standard syllabus content for the complementary function.
