Trigonometry

Read turning points and intercepts directly from a labelled trig graph

Family: Graph Feature Reading Frequency: 2 questions

Trigger — When to use

A trigonometric curve y = a sin(bx + c) + d or a cos(...) + d is shown with specific points labelled (typically called P, Q, R) — these are usually maxima, minima, or x-intercepts. The x-axis is marked in multiples or fractions of π (e.g. ticks at −π, 0, π, 2π) and the y-axis shows specific numerical bounds. The question asks 'state the coordinates of P (and Q)' as a low-mark opener, often before asking you to determine the constants a, b, c, d. Equivalent phrasings: 'write down the coordinates of the maximum/minimum point', 'find the coordinates of the point where the curve meets the x-axis'.

Motivation — Why this works

Trig curves oscillate predictably between max = (vertical shift) + |amplitude| and min = (vertical shift) − |amplitude|, with intercepts and turning points landing symmetrically between the labelled π-ticks. Recognising peaks, troughs, and zero-crossings from the graph's shape lets you extract exact coordinates with no algebra — using only the symmetry/periodicity of sin and cos. This is the entry point for every subsequent parameter-identification step (finding a, d from max/min and b, c from period and phase), so getting it right is structurally essential.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notice the graph is a periodic wave with labelled points and an x-axis scaled in multiples of π — this signals a 'read off' question, not a calculation.
  2. Rule out solving f'(x) = 0 or setting up equations; the graph already displays the features visually, and the labelled marks are placed precisely at exam-friendly locations (turning points or intercepts).
  3. Commit to direct reading because special features of standard sin/cos transforms always sit either AT a labelled tick or at the MIDPOINT between two consecutive labelled ticks (by symmetry of the wave).
  4. Classify each labelled point: is it a peak (highest visible y), a trough (lowest visible y), or a zero-crossing (curve meets x-axis)? Use the curve's shape near the point to decide.
  5. For the x-coordinate, identify the two nearest labelled x-ticks bracketing the point and take their midpoint if it sits between them, or read the tick value directly if it sits on one. For the y-coordinate, read off the labelled max/min from the y-axis (for turning points) or write 0 (for x-intercepts).
  6. Sanity check: the spacing between consecutive max/min should equal half a period, and a max and the next min should be exactly π/b apart — confirms the readings are mutually consistent.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. For a maximum point P: read y_P = top of the wave from y-axis; locate x_P as either a labelled tick or the midpoint of the two ticks bracketing the peak. Write P = (x_P, y_P).
  2. For a minimum point: same procedure with y = bottom of the wave. E.g. ticks at −π and 0 bracketing a trough at y = −4 gives min = (−π/2, −4).
  3. For an x-intercept Q: set y_Q = 0; locate x_Q as the midpoint of the two labelled ticks bracketing the crossing. E.g. crossing between π and 2π gives Q = (3π/2, 0).
  4. Always write coordinates in the order (x, y) with x in radians (as a multiple/fraction of π), never degrees.

Worked Micro-Example

A graph of y = a\cos(bx) + d shows a peak at the labelled tick x = 0 with height 12, and a trough between ticks x = π and x = 2π with depth −2. Reading directly: maximum = (0, 12); minimum sits at midpoint x = \tfrac{3\pi}{2}, so min = \left(\tfrac{3\pi}{2}, -2\right). No algebra needed.

Variants — Same move, different surface

Labelled point is an x-intercept rather than a turning point
y-coordinate is 0 by definition; still locate x by midpoint-between-ticks reasoning.
Graph is a horizontal/vertical translation y = a sin(bx + c) + d with phase shift
Turning points are shifted by −c/b from the standard positions; use the labelled ticks AS GIVEN — do not assume max sits at x = 0.
Negative amplitude (reflected curve, e.g. y = −3 sin x)
Swap the visual roles: the peak of the displayed curve is still the maximum y-value, but it occurs where the un-reflected sin would have a minimum.

Common Misreadings

  • Treating the x-axis as degrees and writing (−90, −4) instead of (−π/2, −4).
  • Confusing the maximum and minimum, especially on a reflected curve.
  • Assuming a labelled point must lie ON a tick when it actually lies at the midpoint between ticks (or vice versa).

Pitfalls

  • Writing coordinates as (y, x) instead of (x, y).
  • Misreading the y-axis scale when it is non-uniform or only partially labelled.
  • When multiple cycles are shown, picking the wrong peak — always match to the labelled letter (P, Q) on the diagram.
  • Forgetting to express x as a multiple of π (e.g. writing 1.57 instead of π/2).

A-level Scope Notes

Fully within Edexcel IAL P2/P3 trigonometry (graphs of sin, cos, tan and their transformations y = a f(bx + c) + d). No formula booklet entry is needed — this is purely graph reading. Avoid invoking calculus (setting derivative to zero) since the question is designed to be answered by inspection.
