Strategic Atlas / Differentiation / Power Rule Evaluation
Differentiation

Differentiate polynomial and evaluate to find tangent gradient at a point

Family: Power Rule Evaluation Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

Fires when a question asks for the gradient (or slope) of the tangent to a polynomial curve y = f(x) at a specified point, or asks for f'(a) directly. Phrasings include 'find the gradient of the curve at P', 'gradient of the tangent at x = a', or 'rate of change at the point'. The function is a sum of power terms with no product/quotient/chain structure. Often appears as part (a) of a question that later asks for first-principles verification.

Motivation — Why this works

By definition, the gradient of the tangent to y = f(x) at x = a is f'(a). For polynomials, the power rule d/dx(x^n) = n·x^{n-1} applied term-by-term gives the derivative directly, bypassing the limit definition. Linearity of differentiation means coefficients pass through and constants vanish. This is the canonical efficient route whenever first principles is not explicitly demanded.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notice the keyword 'gradient of the tangent' (or equivalent) and identify it as a request for f'(a).
  2. Rule out first principles unless the question explicitly asks 'from first principles' or 'using the definition of the derivative' — here power rule is faster.
  3. Commit to term-by-term power rule because the function is a polynomial with no composite, product, or quotient structure.
  4. Differentiate each term to obtain the gradient function dy/dx, keeping it as an expression in x.
  5. Substitute the x-coordinate of the given point (not the y-coordinate) to produce a single numerical gradient.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Identify the function y = f(x) and the x-coordinate a of the given point.
  2. Apply the power rule term-by-term: d/dx(c·x^n) = c·n·x^{n-1}; constants differentiate to 0.
  3. Write dy/dx as a simplified expression in x.
  4. Substitute x = a into dy/dx.
  5. Simplify to a single numerical value and state it as the gradient.

Worked Micro-Example

Find the gradient of y = x^2 + 3x - 2 at P(3, 16).\n dy/dx = 2x + 3.\n At x = 3: dy/dx = 2(3) + 3 = 9.\n Gradient of the tangent at P is 9.

Variants — Same move, different surface

Cubic or higher polynomial, e.g. y = 2x^3 - 5x + 1 at x = 2
Same procedure; dy/dx = 6x^2 - 5, then substitute x = 2 to get 19.
Negative or fractional powers, e.g. y = x^{1/2} + x^{-1}
Rewrite in index form first, then apply power rule: dy/dx = (1/2)x^{-1/2} - x^{-2}.
Point given by y-value only
Solve f(x) = y_0 for x first, choose the relevant root, then evaluate f'(x) there.
Question asks for the gradient of the normal
Compute f'(a) as usual, then take -1/f'(a).
Question asks for the equation of the tangent
After finding m = f'(a), use y - y_0 = m(x - a) with the given point.

Common Misreadings

  • Reading 'gradient of the tangent' as 'gradient of the normal' and reporting -1/f'(a).
  • Substituting the y-coordinate into dy/dx instead of the x-coordinate.
  • Treating the request as requiring first principles when only the answer is needed.
  • Stopping at dy/dx and reporting the gradient function rather than a number.

Pitfalls

  • Forgetting that the constant term differentiates to 0 (or differentiating it to the constant itself).
  • Index errors: e.g. d/dx(x^{-1}) written as -x^{-2} mis-signed, or fractional powers reduced incorrectly.
  • Coefficient errors when applying the power rule to terms like 3x (forgetting it differentiates to 3, not 3x).
  • Arithmetic slips at the substitution stage.

A-level Scope Notes

Fully within Edexcel IAL P1/C1 scope: power rule for polynomials and rational powers, with evaluation at a point to give a tangent gradient. No use of product, quotient, or chain rule is required for the basic form.
