Strategic Atlas / Differential Equations / Bernoulli Reduction
Differential Equations

Divide Bernoulli ODE by y^n to expose substitution terms

Family: Bernoulli Reduction Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

Fires immediately after recognising a Bernoulli ODE of the form dy/dx + P(x)y = Q(x)y^n (with n ≠ 0,1) and choosing the substitution v = y^{1-n}. Cue phrases: 'reduce to linear form', 'use the substitution v = y^{-2}', or seeing a y^3 (or other y^n) on the right while dy/dx and a linear y term sit on the left. The move triggers when you need y^{-n} dy/dx and y^{1-n} to materialise so v and dv/dx can replace them cleanly.

Motivation — Why this works

If v = y^{1-n}, then dv/dx = (1-n) y^{-n} dy/dx, so the only way to make this expression appear in the ODE is to divide every term by y^n. Doing so simultaneously turns the linear y term into y^{1-n} = v and the y^n term on the right into a constant (in y), eliminating y entirely except through v. This is structurally cleaner than substituting y = v^{1/(1-n)} directly, which forces fractional-power chain-rule expressions. The division is the algebraic bridge that linearises the ODE in one step.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notice the ODE matches the Bernoulli template dy/dx + P(x)y = Q(x)y^n, so a substitution will linearise it.
  2. Rule out direct substitution y = v^{1/(1-n)} because the chain rule produces awkward fractional powers of v.
  3. Commit to the standard route: pick v = y^{1-n} and arrange for y^{-n} dy/dx to appear, since dv/dx is proportional to that.
  4. Bridge: divide the entire ODE by y^n so each term is expressible in v and dv/dx.
  5. Tactically, after dividing, replace y^{-n} dy/dx using dv/dx = (1-n) y^{-n} dy/dx, replace y^{1-n} by v, then scale to obtain dv/dx + \tilde P(x) v = \tilde Q(x).

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Identify n in the y^n term and set v = y^{1-n}; compute dv/dx = (1-n) y^{-n} dy/dx.
  2. Divide every term of the ODE by y^n.
  3. Replace y^{-n} dy/dx by dv/dx /(1-n).
  4. Replace y^{1-n} by v.
  5. Multiply through by (1-n) to clear the fraction and obtain the linear ODE dv/dx + (1-n)P(x) v = (1-n)Q(x).
  6. Proceed with integrating factor on the resulting linear ODE.

Worked Micro-Example

Solve dy/dx + (6/x)y = (e^x/x) y^3. Set v = y^{-2}, so dv/dx = -2 y^{-3} dy/dx. Divide by y^3: y^{-3} dy/dx + (6/x) y^{-2} = e^x/x. Substitute: -\tfrac12 dv/dx + (6/x) v = e^x/x. Multiply by -2: dv/dx - (12/x) v = -2e^x/x, a linear ODE ready for integrating factor x^{-12}.

Variants — Same move, different surface

Bernoulli with n = 2, e.g. dy/dx + P(x)y = Q(x)y^2
Set v = y^{-1}; divide by y^2; then dv/dx = -y^{-2} dy/dx, so multiply by -1 after substitution.
Bernoulli with n = 1/2, e.g. dy/dx + P(x)y = Q(x)\sqrt{y}
Set v = y^{1/2}; divide by y^{1/2}; dv/dx = \tfrac12 y^{-1/2} dy/dx, so multiply by 2 after substitution.
Bernoulli with negative n, e.g. dy/dx + P(x)y = Q(x)y^{-1}
Set v = y^2; divide by y^{-1} (i.e. multiply by y); dv/dx = 2y dy/dx, then divide by 2 after substitution.
Coefficient on dy/dx not equal to 1, e.g. a(x) dy/dx + P(x)y = Q(x)y^n
First divide through by a(x) to put the ODE in standard form, then divide by y^n as usual.

Common Misreadings

  • Treating the ODE as separable because of the y^n factor and trying to separate variables instead of recognising Bernoulli structure.
  • Choosing v = y^n instead of v = y^{1-n}, which fails to linearise.
  • Believing the substitution itself does the work, without realising the division by y^n is the step that produces the substitutable terms.

Pitfalls

  • Forgetting to divide ALL terms by y^n, especially the dy/dx term.
  • Sign or factor error when multiplying through by (1-n) to clear the coefficient on dv/dx.
  • Arithmetic slip in scaling coefficients, e.g. (6/x) × (-2) = -12/x, not -6/x.
  • Mis-applying the chain rule: writing dv/dx = (1-n) y^{1-n} dy/dx instead of (1-n) y^{-n} dy/dx.

A-level Scope Notes

Bernoulli reduction via a given substitution v = y^{1-n} is standard in Edexcel IAL Further Pure (FP2/FP3) ODE work; the substitution is typically supplied in the question, and only first-order Bernoulli forms with simple P(x), Q(x) appear.
