Coordinate Geometry

Match y² = 4ax to read parabola focus directly

Family: Standard Form Matching Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

Fires whenever a parabola is given in the form y² = kx (or x² = ky) and the question asks for the focus, directrix, vertex, or parametric coordinates. Cues include phrases like 'find the focus of the parabola', 'state the coordinates of the focus S', or 'write down the directrix'. Also fires implicitly when setting up parametric points P(at², 2at) on the curve. The presence of a bare y² = (number)·x form is the strongest cue.

Motivation — Why this works

The standard parabola y² = 4ax has focus (a, 0) and directrix x = -a as a memorised result derived from the focus-directrix definition. Matching the given equation to this template extracts a in one step, after which every focal/parametric quantity is a direct substitution. This avoids re-deriving from |PS| = perpendicular distance to directrix and keeps work compact and error-free.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notice the equation is y² = (constant)·x with no shifts — a standard right-opening parabola.
  2. Rule out completing the square or translation work: no (x - h) or (y - k) terms appear, so vertex is at the origin.
  3. Commit to template-matching against y² = 4ax because the question asks for a focal feature, which is a tabulated result.
  4. Bridge: equate the coefficient of x to 4a and solve for a in one line.
  5. Tactically, once a is known, write down focus (a, 0), directrix x = -a, and parametric point (at², 2at) as needed — all flow from the same a.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Identify the standard form: y² = 4ax (right-opening) with vertex at origin.
  2. Equate coefficients: 4a = (given coefficient of x), solve for a.
  3. State focus S = (a, 0).
  4. If required, state directrix x = -a and parametric form (at², 2at).

Worked Micro-Example

Find the focus of $y^2 = 20x$. Compare with $y^2 = 4ax$: $4a = 20 \Rightarrow a = 5$. Hence focus $S = (5, 0)$, directrix $x = -5$, and a general point on the curve is $P(5t^2, 10t)$.

Variants — Same move, different surface

Parabola x² = 4ay (opens upward)
Focus is at (0, a), directrix y = -a, parametric point (2at, at²).
Parabola y² = -4ax (opens leftward)
Focus at (-a, 0), directrix x = a; treat a as positive after matching the magnitude.
Rectangular hyperbola xy = c²
Match xy = c² to read off c; parametric point is (ct, c/t). Focus is not in A-level scope here.
Ellipse x²/a² + y²/b² = 1
Read a, b directly; foci at (±ae, 0) with b² = a²(1 - e²).

Common Misreadings

  • Treating the coefficient of x as a itself rather than as 4a.
  • Misidentifying y² = kx as x² = ky and placing the focus on the wrong axis.
  • Assuming the vertex is not at the origin when no shift is present.

Pitfalls

  • Writing a = 20 from 4a = 20 instead of a = 5.
  • Placing the focus at (0, 5) instead of (5, 0) — confusing the axis of symmetry.
  • Forgetting the sign of a for left/down-opening parabolas, leading to a wrong-side directrix.

A-level Scope Notes

Fully within Edexcel IAL Further Pure: parabola y² = 4ax with focus (a, 0), directrix x = -a, and parametric form (at², 2at) are standard results. No conic rotation or translation beyond the syllabus is needed.
