Trigonometry

Decompose sector perimeter into two radii plus arc length

Family: Geometric Decomposition Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

The question asks for the 'perimeter' (or 'total length of boundary') of a circular sector defined by radius r and angle θ in radians. Cues include a pie-slice diagram, phrases like 'find the perimeter of sector OAB', or being given r and θ and asked for a length that is not simply the arc. Distinguish carefully from questions asking only for arc length s = rθ or for area (½r²θ).

Motivation — Why this works

A sector's boundary has three pieces: two straight radii (each length r) and one curved arc (length s = rθ when θ is in radians). The formula s = rθ only measures the curved part, so the full perimeter is P = 2r + rθ = r(2 + θ). This decomposition reduces a geometric figure to a single algebraic expression once r and θ are known.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notice the figure is a sector and the requested quantity is perimeter — i.e. total boundary length, not area and not just the arc.
  2. Rule out using P = rθ alone (that's arc length) and rule out using degree-based formulas since the radian formula s = rθ is cleaner here.
  3. Commit to P = 2r + s because the boundary genuinely consists of two radii plus one arc — three separate edges.
  4. Bridge to computation: identify r and θ (ensure θ is in radians), compute s = rθ, then add 2r.
  5. Tactically, factor as P = r(2 + θ) when r is messy (e.g. a surd) so the surd is handled once, and combine like surd terms carefully at the end.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Confirm θ is in radians; convert from degrees if necessary using θ_rad = θ_deg × π/180.
  2. Compute the arc length s = rθ.
  3. Write the perimeter formula P = 2r + s, or equivalently P = r(2 + θ).
  4. Substitute the values of r and θ and simplify, keeping surds exact where required.
  5. Combine like terms (e.g. surd coefficients) to give a single simplified expression.

Worked Micro-Example

Sector with $r = 2\sqrt{6}$ and $\theta = 1.25$ rad. Arc: $s = r\theta = 2\sqrt{6} \times 1.25 = 2.5\sqrt{6}$. Perimeter: $P = 2r + s = 4\sqrt{6} + 2.5\sqrt{6} = 6.5\sqrt{6}$. Equivalently $P = r(2+\theta) = 2\sqrt{6}(3.25) = 6.5\sqrt{6}$.

Variants — Same move, different surface

Perimeter of a circular segment (region between chord and arc)
Boundary is arc + chord, so P = rθ + 2r sin(θ/2); the two radii are replaced by one chord.
Perimeter of an annular sector (between two concentric arcs of radii R and r)
P = (R − r) + (R − r) + Rθ + rθ = 2(R − r) + (R + r)θ — two straight edges plus two arcs.
Angle given in degrees
Either convert to radians first or use s = (θ/360) × 2πr, then still add 2r for the perimeter.
Composite figure (e.g. sector attached to a triangle)
Identify which radii are interior (shared) edges and exclude them from the outer perimeter.

Common Misreadings

  • Reading 'perimeter of sector' as 'arc length' and reporting only s = rθ.
  • Treating the sector as a full circle and using 2πr.
  • Confusing perimeter with area and computing ½r²θ instead.
  • Adding three radii (or only one) rather than exactly two.

Pitfalls

  • Using θ in degrees inside s = rθ without converting to radians.
  • Arithmetic slips when combining surd terms with decimal coefficients, e.g. 4√6 + 2.5√6.
  • Dropping the surd during multiplication (e.g. computing 2.5 × 2 = 5 instead of 2.5 × 2√6 = 5√6).
  • Forgetting to factor or simplify, leaving an unsimplified answer like 4√6 + 2.5√6.

A-level Scope Notes

This is core Edexcel IAL Pure (P2) sector geometry: arc length s = rθ and perimeter P = r(2 + θ) with θ in radians. No calculus or beyond-syllabus tools are required.
