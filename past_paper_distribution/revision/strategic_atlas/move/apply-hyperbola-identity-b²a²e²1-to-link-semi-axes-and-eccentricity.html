Coordinate Geometry

Apply hyperbola identity b²=a²(e²−1) to link semi-axes and eccentricity

Family: Parameter Identity Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

Fires when a hyperbola is presented in standard form x²/a² − y²/b² = 1 (or its y-transverse counterpart) and the question requires a relationship between a, b, and the eccentricity e. Typical cues are 'show that e² = …', 'find the eccentricity', or being told the foci/directrix and one semi-axis. Also fires when later parts of a multi-part question need e expressed in terms of a single unknown semi-axis to act as a parameter bridge.

Motivation — Why this works

The identity b² = a²(e² − 1) is the defining metric relation for a hyperbola, derived from the focus–directrix definition with c² = a² + b² and e = c/a. Because it ties all three constants in one equation, knowing any two immediately yields the third, and rearrangement gives e² as a rational function of a (or a² as a function of e). This makes it the standard 'currency converter' between geometric data (foci, directrices, asymptote slopes) and algebraic parameters.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notice the equation is in standard hyperbola form, so b² can be read directly from the y² denominator.
  2. Rule out ellipse identity b² = a²(1 − e²); the minus sign on the y² term flags hyperbola, so e > 1 and the sign flips.
  3. Commit to b² = a²(e² − 1) because it is the unique A-level identity linking a, b, e for this conic.
  4. Bridge: substitute the known b² and isolate e² (or a²) depending on which is unknown.
  5. Tactically keep the result as a single rational expression, e.g. e² = (a² + b²)/a², so it can be reused cleanly in later parts.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Identify a² and b² from the standard form x²/a² − y²/b² = 1.
  2. Write the identity b² = a²(e² − 1).
  3. Substitute the known value of b² (or a²).
  4. Rearrange to isolate the required quantity, typically e² = 1 + b²/a² = (a² + b²)/a².
  5. Sanity check: confirm e² > 1 (hyperbola) before passing the expression to the next part.

Worked Micro-Example

Given $\dfrac{x^2}{a^2} - \dfrac{y^2}{25} = 1$, express $e^2$ in terms of $a$. Using $b^2 = a^2(e^2-1)$ with $b^2 = 25$: $25 = a^2(e^2-1)$, so $e^2 - 1 = \dfrac{25}{a^2}$, giving $e^2 = \dfrac{a^2+25}{a^2}$. Check: $e^2 > 1$ for all real $a \neq 0$, consistent with a hyperbola.

Variants — Same move, different surface

Ellipse x²/a² + y²/b² = 1 with b < a
Use b² = a²(1 − e²), so e² = (a² − b²)/a² and 0 < e < 1.
Hyperbola with transverse axis along y: y²/a² − x²/b² = 1
Same identity b² = a²(e² − 1) but a is now the y-semi-axis; foci lie on the y-axis at (0, ±ae).
Foci/directrix data given instead of b
Use c = ae and directrix x = a/e, then recover b² via b² = a²(e² − 1) = c² − a².
Asymptote slope ±m given
For x-transverse hyperbola, m = b/a, so e² = 1 + m².

Common Misreadings

  • Treating the conic as an ellipse because b² < a² and applying b² = a²(1 − e²).
  • Reading the larger denominator as b² rather than a² when the equation is written y²/… − x²/… = 1.
  • Assuming e is given numerically when only the identity is needed symbolically.

Pitfalls

  • Sign error: writing e² − 1 = b²/a² as 1 − e² = b²/a², producing e < 1.
  • Forgetting to square e when substituting later (mixing e and e²).
  • Dropping the constraint e > 1, which can hide extraneous roots in subsequent parts.

A-level Scope Notes

Fully within Edexcel IAL Further Pure scope (Further Pure conics): standard-form hyperbola, eccentricity, foci and directrices, with c² = a² + b² and e = c/a.
