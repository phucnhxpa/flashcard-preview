Coordinate Geometry

Complete the square on x and y groups to extract circle centre and radius

Motivation вҖ” Why this works

Completing the square rewrites xВІ + bx as (x + b/2)ВІ вҲ’ (b/2)ВІ, converting the general equation into the standard form (xвҲ’a)ВІ + (yвҲ’b)ВІ = rВІ. This isolates the geometric data вҖ” centre (a, b) and radius r вҖ” which are not directly visible in the expanded equation. The underlying identity is the perfect-square identity (x + p)ВІ = xВІ + 2px + pВІ, applied independently to the x-group and y-group because there is no cross term. It is the canonical bridge between algebraic and geometric representations of a circle and is essential before applying any distance, tangency, or intersection condition.

Thought Process вҖ” The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notice the equation has xВІ and yВІ with equal (usually unit) coefficients and no xy term вҖ” this is a circle in general form.
  2. Notice the centre and radius are hidden inside the linear x and y terms; they cannot be read off until the equation is in standard form.
  3. Rule out the quadratic formula (gives x in terms of y, useless here) and rule out using the direct formula centre = (вҲ’D/2, вҲ’E/2), rВІ = DВІ/4 + EВІ/4 вҲ’ F because (i) mark schemes typically expect to see the completed-square form and (ii) it is more transparent and reusable for later parts of the question.
  4. Commit to completing the square because it simultaneously delivers the centre, the radiusВІ, and a clean equation for any subsequent geometric work (tangents, intersections, distance from a point).
  5. Tactically: group x-terms together and y-terms together, move any constant to the RHS, complete each square independently, and remember to subtract the (b/2)ВІ constants on the LHS or, equivalently, add them to the RHS.
  6. If the leading coefficients are not 1 (e.g. 2xВІ + 2yВІ + вҖҰ), divide through first; if the equation contains an unknown k, carry it symbolically into rВІ = вҖҰ вҲ’ k for use in a later condition.

Mechanic вҖ” Concrete operations

  1. Rearrange into the form (xВІ + Dx) + (yВІ + Ey) = вҲ’F (move the constant to the RHS).
  2. Complete the x-square: xВІ + Dx = (x + D/2)ВІ вҲ’ (D/2)ВІ.
  3. Complete the y-square: yВІ + Ey = (y + E/2)ВІ вҲ’ (E/2)ВІ.
  4. Substitute back and move the subtracted constants to the RHS: (x + D/2)ВІ + (y + E/2)ВІ = (D/2)ВІ + (E/2)ВІ вҲ’ F.
  5. Read off centre (вҲ’D/2, вҲ’E/2) and radius r = вҲҡ((D/2)ВІ + (E/2)ВІ вҲ’ F), simplifying any surd to its exact form (e.g. вҲҡ27 = 3вҲҡ3).

Worked Micro-Example

Given $x^2 + y^2 + 6x - 4y - 14 = 0$: group as $(x^2+6x)+(y^2-4y)=14$. Complete the squares: $(x+3)^2 - 9 + (y-2)^2 - 4 = 14$, so $(x+3)^2 + (y-2)^2 = 27$. Hence centre $(-3, 2)$ and radius $\sqrt{27} = 3\sqrt{3}$.

Variants вҖ” Same move, different surface

Common Misreadings

  • Treating the equation as two separate quadratics to solve via the quadratic formula instead of recognising it as a single circle equation.
  • Assuming the centre is (D, E) read straight off the linear coefficients, ignoring the halving and sign flip.
  • Mistaking an equation with an xy term or unequal xВІ and yВІ coefficients for a circle (it is then an ellipse or rotated conic, outside this move's scope).

Pitfalls

  • Sign errors when reading the centre: (x + 3)ВІ gives centre x-coordinate вҲ’3, not +3; (y вҲ’ 2)ВІ gives +2.
  • Forgetting to subtract the (D/2)ВІ and (E/2)ВІ constants on the LHS (or equivalently add them to the RHS) after completing each square.
  • Leaving the radius as вҲҡ27 instead of simplifying to 3вҲҡ3 when 'exact' form is required.
  • Forgetting to divide through first when the leading coefficients on xВІ and yВІ are not 1.
  • Computing r when only rВІ was needed (or vice versa) вҖ” particularly when carrying an unknown k symbolically.
