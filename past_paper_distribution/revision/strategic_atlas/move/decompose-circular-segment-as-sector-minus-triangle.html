Trigonometry

Decompose circular segment as sector minus triangle

Family: Circular Segment Decomposition Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

Fires when a region is bounded by a chord and an arc of a circle — i.e. a circular segment. Cues include phrases like 'region bounded by arc AB and chord AB', diagrams showing a 'lens-like' or 'D-shaped' piece of a disc, or a question giving radius and a central angle and asking for an enclosed area that is not the full sector. Equivalent phrasings: 'shaded region between chord and arc', 'area cut off by a chord'. The move applies whenever both $r$ and the subtended angle $\theta$ (in radians) are known or computable.

Motivation — Why this works

The Edexcel formula sheet provides $\tfrac{1}{2}r^2\theta$ for sector area and $\tfrac{1}{2}ab\sin C$ for triangle area, but no direct segment formula. A segment is exactly the sector swept by the chord's endpoints minus the isosceles triangle joining those endpoints to the centre. Both sub-regions share the same $r$ and $\theta$, so the decomposition introduces no new unknowns and yields the compact exact form $\tfrac{1}{2}r^2(\theta - \sin\theta)$. This is far cleaner than any integration approach at A-level.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notice the region is bounded by one straight edge (a chord) and one curved edge (an arc of a single circle) — this is the signature of a segment.
  2. Rule out direct formula lookup (no segment formula is given) and rule out integration (over-engineered and not expected at this level).
  3. Commit to expressing the segment as (sector with the same chord endpoints) − (triangle with vertices at the two chord endpoints and the centre).
  4. Identify the radius $r$ and the central angle $\theta$ subtended by the chord; ensure $\theta$ is in radians.
  5. Apply $A_{\text{seg}} = \tfrac{1}{2}r^2\theta - \tfrac{1}{2}r^2\sin\theta = \tfrac{1}{2}r^2(\theta - \sin\theta)$, keeping symbolic form until the final numerical step.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Identify radius $r$ and the central angle $\theta$ (in radians) subtended at the centre by the chord.
  2. Compute sector area $A_{\text{sect}} = \tfrac{1}{2}r^2\theta$.
  3. Compute triangle area $A_{\triangle} = \tfrac{1}{2}r^2\sin\theta$.
  4. Subtract: $A_{\text{seg}} = A_{\text{sect}} - A_{\triangle} = \tfrac{1}{2}r^2(\theta - \sin\theta)$.
  5. Substitute numerical values at the end and round only once to the requested precision.

Worked Micro-Example

A chord $BD$ of a circle of radius $3$ m subtends an angle of $\theta = \pi - 1.4$ at the centre $O$. Then $A_{\text{seg}} = \tfrac{1}{2}(3)^2\bigl((\pi-1.4) - \sin(\pi-1.4)\bigr) = 4.5\,(1.7416 - 0.9860) \approx 3.40\ \text{m}^2.$

Variants — Same move, different surface

Minor segment with $\theta < \pi$
Standard form $\tfrac{1}{2}r^2(\theta - \sin\theta)$ applies directly; segment is small.
Major segment (the larger piece on the other side of the chord)
Either use angle $2\pi - \theta$ in the same formula, or compute (full circle area $\pi r^2$) − (minor segment area).
Only the chord length $\ell$ and radius $r$ are given
First recover $\theta$ via $\ell = 2r\sin(\theta/2)$, then apply the segment formula.
Only arc length $s$ and radius $r$ are given
Use $\theta = s/r$, then apply $\tfrac{1}{2}r^2(\theta - \sin\theta)$.
Composite region: segment plus a triangle or rectangle
Compute the segment piece by this decomposition and add/subtract the polygonal pieces separately.

Common Misreadings

  • Treating the region as a sector and forgetting to subtract the triangle.
  • Treating the region as a triangle only, ignoring the curved boundary.
  • Computing semicircle minus two sectors — works but introduces extra rounding and unnecessary steps.
  • Misidentifying which angle is the central angle (e.g. using an inscribed angle from the diagram).

Pitfalls

  • Using degrees instead of radians in $\tfrac{1}{2}r^2\theta$ (the sector formula requires radians).
  • Calculator left in degree mode when evaluating $\sin\theta$ — gives a wrong triangle area.
  • Rounding $\theta$, sector area, and triangle area separately before subtracting, causing loss of precision in a small difference.
  • Confusing the angle subtended by the chord with its supplement, especially when the diagram uses $\pi - \alpha$ style expressions.

A-level Scope Notes

Fully within Edexcel IAL Pure Mathematics: uses only the standard radian sector formula $\tfrac{1}{2}r^2\theta$ and triangle area $\tfrac{1}{2}ab\sin C$, both on the formula sheet. No calculus or non-standard identities required.
