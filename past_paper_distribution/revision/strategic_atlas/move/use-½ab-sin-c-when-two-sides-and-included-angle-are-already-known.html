Vectors

Use ½ab sin C when two sides and included angle are already known

Family: Area Formula Selection Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

Fires when a triangle's area is required in a 3D vectors problem and earlier parts have already produced two side lengths meeting at a known included angle (typically from a prior dot-product or cosine calculation). Cues include phrases like 'Hence find the area of triangle...' immediately after a part that found |PA|, |PE|, and the angle APE. Also fires whenever the question structure has clearly built towards reusing earlier numerical results rather than restarting from coordinates.

Motivation — Why this works

The scalar formula Area = ½|a||b| sin C is equivalent to ½|a × b| but avoids the overhead of forming displacement vectors and evaluating a 3×3 determinant. When the magnitudes and angle are already in hand, substituting them directly is the shortest route and matches the 'hence' structure examiners expect. It also reuses earlier marks-bearing work, reducing arithmetic risk.

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notice that the previous parts of the question have produced two side lengths meeting at a vertex plus the angle between them.
  2. Rule out the cross-product route ½|PE × PA|: it would require recomputing displacement vectors and a determinant, duplicating earlier work.
  3. Commit to ½ab sin C because all three ingredients (|a|, |b|, sin C) are immediately available from prior parts.
  4. Identify which two sides bound the included angle so the correct magnitudes are paired with the correct sin θ.
  5. Substitute the stored values, evaluate sin θ (often via sin θ = √(1 − cos²θ) if only cos θ was found), and multiply through with the ½ factor.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Identify the vertex of the triangle at which the known angle sits and the two sides meeting there.
  2. Quote |PA| and |PE| from earlier parts.
  3. Quote the angle θ (or compute sin θ from cos θ via sin θ = √(1 − cos²θ)).
  4. Apply Area = ½ |PA| |PE| sin θ.
  5. Simplify to a clean exact or decimal value.

Worked Micro-Example

Given |PA| = 3, |PE| = 2√2, and cos(APE) = 1/√2 from a prior dot-product part, then sin(APE) = 1/√2. Hence Area of △APE = ½ · 3 · 2√2 · (1/√2) = ½ · 3 · 2 = 3.

Variants — Same move, different surface

Triangle given only by three position vectors with no prior angle work
Form two edge vectors and use Area = ½|a × b| instead.
Parallelogram area instead of triangle
Drop the ½ factor: Area = |a||b| sin θ or |a × b|.
Only cos θ has been computed in a prior part
Convert via sin θ = √(1 − cos²θ), taking the positive root since 0 < θ < π.
Tetrahedron volume rather than triangle area
Switch to ⅙|a · (b × c)| using scalar triple product (Further Maths).

Common Misreadings

  • Pairing the angle with sides that do not actually meet at that vertex.
  • Treating an angle between direction vectors as the triangle's interior angle without checking orientation.
  • Using ½ × base × height when no perpendicular height has been established.

Pitfalls

  • Forgetting the ½ factor and reporting a parallelogram-sized answer.
  • Using cos θ in place of sin θ when only cos θ was previously computed.
  • Taking sin θ negative; in a triangle 0 < θ < π so sin θ > 0.
  • Citing the supplementary angle in working — numerically harmless since sin(π − θ) = sin θ, but may cost reasoning marks.

A-level Scope Notes

Fully within Edexcel IAL Pure/Further Pure: combines the ½ab sin C formula from Pure trigonometry with magnitudes and angles obtained via the scalar product in 3D vectors.
