Algebra & Functions

Extract pq before p+q when Vieta's gives reciprocal-root data

Family: Vieta'S Inversion Frequency: 1 questions

Trigger — When to use

Fires when a quadratic is stated to have roots that are functions of p and q (most commonly 1/p and 1/q), and the question asks for p+q and pq. Cue phrases: 'the equation ... has roots 1/p and 1/q', or any restatement where Vieta's outputs are reciprocals/transforms of the desired symmetric functions. Also fires when forming a new quadratic whose roots relate to the original by reciprocation.

Motivation — Why this works

Vieta's gives sum = -b/a and product = c/a of whatever the stated roots are. When those roots are 1/p and 1/q, the product relation 1/(pq) = c/a directly inverts to pq, and the sum relation (p+q)/(pq) = -b/a then yields p+q by a single multiplication. This bypasses solving the quadratic explicitly (which would introduce surds) and keeps values rational, exploiting the identity 1/p + 1/q = (p+q)/(pq).

Thought Process — The step-by-step reasoning

  1. Notice the roots of the given equation are 1/p and 1/q, not p and q — Vieta's outputs are reciprocal-flavoured, not the targets.
  2. Rule out solving the quadratic for 1/p, 1/q explicitly and reciprocating: that path produces surds and clutters any follow-up part.
  3. Commit to the algebraic bridge: 1/p + 1/q = (p+q)/(pq) and (1/p)(1/q) = 1/(pq), so the product relation alone fixes pq.
  4. First operation: write Vieta's for the given equation cleanly with correct signs, isolating 1/(pq) and (p+q)/(pq).
  5. Tactical order: invert the product equation to get pq, then substitute pq into the sum equation to get p+q — never the reverse.

Mechanic — Concrete operations

  1. Identify the stated roots (e.g. 1/p, 1/q) and write Vieta's: sum = -b/a, product = c/a.
  2. Set 1/(pq) = c/a and invert to obtain pq = a/c.
  3. Set (p+q)/(pq) = -b/a and multiply by pq to obtain p+q = (-b/a)(a/c) = -b/c.
  4. State p+q and pq as the clean rational answers; sanity-check signs against the original coefficients.

Worked Micro-Example

The equation $5x^2 - 4x + 2 = 0$ has roots $1/p$ and $1/q$. Vieta's: $1/p + 1/q = 4/5$ and $(1/p)(1/q) = 2/5$. Product first: $1/(pq) = 2/5 \Rightarrow pq = 5/2$. Sum next: $(p+q)/(pq) = 4/5 \Rightarrow p+q = (4/5)(5/2) = 2$.

Variants — Same move, different surface

Given equation has roots $p^2$ and $q^2$
Vieta's yields $p^2+q^2$ and $p^2q^2$; take $pq = \pm\sqrt{p^2q^2}$ (sign from context) then $(p+q)^2 = p^2+q^2 + 2pq$ to recover $p+q$.
Given equation has roots $p+k$ and $q+k$ for constant $k$
Sum gives $p+q+2k$, product gives $pq + k(p+q) + k^2$; subtract $2k$ from sum first, then back-substitute into product.
Forming a new quadratic with roots $1/p, 1/q$ from one with roots $p, q$
Use sum of new roots = (p+q)/(pq) and product = 1/(pq); equivalently reverse the coefficient order of the original.
Roots are $\alpha p$ and $\alpha q$ for constant $\alpha$
Divide the Vieta sum by $\alpha$ and the product by $\alpha^2$ to recover $p+q$ and $pq$.

Common Misreadings

  • Treating p and q themselves as the roots of the given equation rather than 1/p and 1/q — reverses the entire problem.
  • Reading 'roots 1/p and 1/q' as 'roots p and q after dividing the equation through' and rescaling coefficients incorrectly.

Pitfalls

  • Sign error on sum of roots: sum = -b/a, not b/a; with $b=-4$ the sum is $+4/5$.
  • Attempting p+q before pq — the sum relation $(p+q)/(pq) = -b/a$ is unusable without pq in hand.
  • Forgetting to invert: writing pq = c/a instead of pq = a/c when the roots are reciprocals.
  • Dropping the $\pm$ when a variant requires a square root (e.g. roots $p^2, q^2$ case).

A-level Scope Notes

Standard Edexcel IAL Further Pure 1/2 content on roots of quadratics and Vieta's relations; uses only symmetric-function identities like $1/p+1/q=(p+q)/(pq)$ and $(p+q)^2 = p^2+q^2+2pq$.
