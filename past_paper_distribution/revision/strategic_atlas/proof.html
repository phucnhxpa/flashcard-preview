Induction Conclusion

1 moves · 3 examples
Close induction with a complete formal conclusion to secure the final A-mark
$\;\text{Base case }(n=1)\checkmark,\quad \text{Inductive step }(n=k \Rightarrow n=k+1)\checkmark\;$
$\;\text{True for }n=1;\; n=k \Rightarrow n=k+1;\; \therefore \text{ true for all } n\in\mathbb{Z}^{+} \text{ by induction.}\;$
3q induction conclusion

Factor Extraction

1 moves · 3 examples
Extract the target divisor as an explicit factor to close a divisibility/parity proof
$\;6n + 4,\quad n \in \mathbb{Z}\;$
$\;6n + 4 = 2(3n + 2) = 2k,\quad k = 3n+2 \in \mathbb{Z} \Rightarrow 2 \mid (6n+4)\;$
3q factor extraction

Recurrence Induction Bridge

1 moves · 2 examples
Substitute the assumed closed form into the recurrence to bridge k to k+1
$\;u_{k+1} = 2u_k + 1,\quad u_k = 2^k - 1\;$
$\;u_{k+1} = 2(2^k - 1) + 1 = 2^{k+1} - 2 + 1 = 2^{k+1} - 1\;$
2q recurrence induction bridge

$\;0 < 2y < \dfrac{\pi}{2} \Rightarrow \sin(2y) > 0 \Rightarrow \sin(2y) = +\sqrt{\dfrac{x}{6}}\;$
2q sign analysis

$\;f(k{+}1)=4(9M-6k+1)+6k+5=36M-18k+9=9(4M-2k+1)\;$

$\;5^{k+2} - 1 = 5 \cdot 5^{k+1} - 1 = 5(4M+1) - 1 = 20M + 4 = 4(5M+1)\;$

Induction Decomposition

1 moves · 2 examples
Decompose the (k+1) case to expose the inductive hypothesis
$\;f(k) = 3^{k+1} - 2k - 3,\quad 4 \mid f(k)\;$
$\;f(k+1) - 3f(k) = (3^{k+2}-2k-5) - 3(3^{k+1}-2k-3) = 4k+4 = 4(k+1) \Rightarrow f(k+1)=3f(k)+4(k+1)\;$
2q induction decomposition

Reverse And Add

1 moves · 1 examples
Write series forwards and backwards then add to pair terms
$\;S_n = a + (a+d) + \dots + [a+(n-1)d]\quad(1);\quad S_n = [a+(n-1)d] + \dots + a\quad(2)\;$
$\;(1)+(2):\; 2S_n = \underbrace{[2a+(n-1)d] + \cdots + [2a+(n-1)d]}_{n} = n[2a+(n-1)d] \Rightarrow S_n = \tfrac{n}{2}[2a+(n-1)d]\;$
1q reverse and add

Staggered Subtraction

1 moves · 1 examples
Multiply sum by common ratio then subtract to telescope
$\;S = 1 + r + r^2 + \cdots + r^{n-1}\;$
$\;rS - S = r^n - 1 \Rightarrow S = \dfrac{r^n - 1}{r - 1}\;$
1q staggered subtraction

Domain Restriction

$\;a,\, b \in \mathbb{Z}^+,\quad a = 6 - 2b\;$
$\;a > 0 \Rightarrow 6-2b>0 \Rightarrow b<3,\; b\geq 1 \Rightarrow b\in\{1,2\}\;$
1q domain restriction

Perfect Square Anchoring

$\;k > 0,\quad k + \dfrac{4}{k} \geq 4\;$
$\;(k-2)^2 \geq 0 \Rightarrow k^2 - 4k + 4 \geq 0 \Rightarrow k - 4 + \dfrac{4}{k} \geq 0 \Rightarrow k + \dfrac{4}{k} \geq 4\;$
1q perfect square anchoring

Induction Separation

1 moves · 1 examples
Split sum to k+1 into sum to k plus new term
$\;\text{Assume } \sum_{r=1}^{k} r = \tfrac{1}{2}k(k+1)\;$
$\;\sum_{r=1}^{k+1} r = \tfrac{1}{2}k(k+1) + (k+1) = \tfrac{1}{2}(k+1)(k+2)\;$
1q induction separation

$\;\dfrac{k(k+1)}{2} + (k+1) = \dfrac{(k+1)(k+2)}{2}\;$

Exponential Definition Proof

1 moves · 1 examples
Expand RHS via exponential definitions to verify hyperbolic identity
$\;\cosh A\cosh B + \sinh A\sinh B\;$
$\;\tfrac{1}{4}(e^A+e^{-A})(e^B+e^{-B}) + \tfrac{1}{4}(e^A-e^{-A})(e^B-e^{-B}) = \tfrac{e^{A+B}+e^{-(A+B)}}{2} = \cosh(A+B)\;$
1q exponential definition proof

Recurrence Substitution

1 moves · 1 examples
Drive the inductive step through the recurrence, not the closed form
$\;u_{k+2} = 5u_{k+1} - 6u_k,\quad u_k = 3\cdot2^k - 2\cdot3^k,\quad u_{k+1} = 3\cdot2^{k+1} - 2\cdot3^{k+1}\;$
$\;5(3\cdot2^{k+1} - 2\cdot3^{k+1}) - 6(3\cdot2^k - 2\cdot3^k) = 2^k(30-18) - 3^k(30-18) = 12\cdot2^k - 12\cdot3^k = 3\cdot2^{k+2} - 2\cdot3^{k+2}\;$
1q recurrence substitution
