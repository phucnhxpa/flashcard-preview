3.	 A car is moving at a constant speed of 25 m s–1 along a straight horizontal road.
	
The car is modelled as a particle.
	
At time t = 0, the car is at the point A and the driver sees a road sign 48 m ahead.
	
Let t seconds be the time that elapses after the car passes A.
	
In a first model, the car is assumed to decelerate uniformly at 6 m s–2 from A until the car 
reaches the road sign.
	
(a)	 Use this first model to find the speed of the car as it reaches the sign.
(2)
	
The road sign indicates that the speed limit immediately after the sign is 13 m s–1.
	
In a second model, the car is assumed to decelerate uniformly at 6 m s–2 from A until it 
reaches a speed of 13 m s–1. The car then maintains this speed until it reaches the road sign.
	
(b)	Use this second model to find the value of t at which the car reaches the sign.
(4)
	
In a third model, the car is assumed to move with constant speed 25 m s–1 from A until 
time t = 0.2, the car then decelerates uniformly at 6 m s–2 until it reaches a speed of 13 m s–1. 
The car then maintains this speed until it reaches the road sign.
	
(c)	 Use this third model to find the value of t at which the car reaches the sign.

Worked solution

Part (a)

THOUGHT PROCESS: We recognize this as a standard constant acceleration problem. We are given the initial speed ($u$), the constant acceleration ($a$, which is a deceleration so it's negative), and the displacement ($s$) over which this acceleration occurs. We need to find the final speed ($v$). The SUVAT equation that directly links $u$, $a$, $s$, and $v$ without needing time ($t$) is $v^2 = u^2 + 2as$.

WORKING:
Let $u$ be the initial speed, $v$ be the final speed, $a$ be the acceleration, and $s$ be the displacement.
Given values for the first model:
$u = 25 \text{ m/s}$ (initial speed)
$a = -6 \text{ m/s}^2$ (deceleration, hence negative)
$s = 48 \text{ m}$ (distance to the road sign)

Using the SUVAT equation:
$$v^2 = u^2 + 2as$$
Substitute the given values:
$$v^2 = (25)^2 + 2(-6)(48)$$
$$v^2 = 625 - 576$$
$$v^2 = 49$$
To find $v$, take the square root. Since speed is a scalar quantity and the car is still moving in the same direction, we take the positive root:
$$v = \sqrt{49}$$
$$v = 7 \text{ m/s}$$

Answer: The speed of the car as it reaches the sign is $7 \text{ m/s}$.

Part (b)

THOUGHT PROCESS: This model describes two distinct phases of motion: first, a period of uniform deceleration, and second, a period of constant speed. To find the total time, we need to calculate the time taken for each phase and sum them up.

Phase 1: Deceleration
We know the initial speed ($u=25 \text{ m/s}$), final speed ($v=13 \text{ m/s}$), and acceleration ($a=-6 \text{ m/s}^2$). We need to find the time ($t_1$) and distance ($s_1$) covered during this phase.

Phase 2: Constant Speed
Once we know $s_1$, we can find the remaining distance ($s_2 = 48 - s_1$). The car travels this distance at a constant speed of $13 \text{ m/s}$. We then use $s = vt$ to find the time ($t_2$).
Finally, the total time will be $t_1 + t_2$.

WORKING:
Phase 1: Deceleration from $25 \text{ m/s}$ to $13 \text{ m/s}$
Given:
$u = 25 \text{ m/s}$
$v = 13 \text{ m/s}$
$a = -6 \text{ m/s}^2$

First, find the time taken ($t_1$) for this phase using $v = u + at_1$:
$$13 = 25 + (-6)t_1$$
$$13 - 25 = -6t_1$$
$$-12 = -6t_1$$
$$t_1 = \frac{-12}{-6}$$
$$t_1 = 2 \text{ s}$$

Next, find the distance covered ($s_1$) during this phase using $v^2 = u^2 + 2as_1$:
$$(13)^2 = (25)^2 + 2(-6)s_1$$
$$169 = 625 - 12s_1$$
$$12s_1 = 625 - 169$$
$$12s_1 = 456$$
$$s_1 = \frac{456}{12}$$
$$s_1 = 38 \text{ m}$$

Phase 2: Constant speed of $13 \text{ m/s}$
Total distance to the sign is $48 \text{ m}$. The distance covered in Phase 1 was $38 \text{ m}$.
Remaining distance ($s_2$) to be covered at constant speed:
$$s_2 = 48 - s_1$$
$$s_2 = 48 - 38$$
$$s_2 = 10 \text{ m}$$

The car travels this distance at a constant speed of $13 \text{ m/s}$. To find the time taken ($t_2$), use $s = vt$:
$$10 = 13 \times t_2$$
$$t_2 = \frac{10}{13} \text{ s}$$

Total time to reach the sign:
$$T_{total} = t_1 + t_2$$
$$T_{total} = 2 + \frac{10}{13}$$
$$T_{total} = \frac{26}{13} + \frac{10}{13}$$
$$T_{total} = \frac{36}{13} \text{ s}$$
(Approximately $2.77 \text{ s}$ to 3 significant figures).

Answer: The value of $t$ at which the car reaches the sign is $\frac{36}{13} \text{ s}$.

Part (c)

THOUGHT PROCESS: This model is a three-phase problem. We need to consider each phase separately to calculate its time and distance, then sum the times.

Phase 1: Constant speed $25 \text{ m/s}$ for $t=0.2 \text{ s}$
Calculate the distance ($s_1$) covered during this initial period.

Phase 2: Deceleration from $25 \text{ m/s}$ to $13 \text{ m/s}$ at $a=-6 \text{ m/s}^2$
This is identical to Phase 1 of part (b). We need its time ($t_2$) and distance ($s_2$).

Phase 3: Constant speed $13 \text{ m/s}$ until the sign
Calculate the remaining distance ($s_3 = 48 - s_1 - s_2$) and the time ($t_3$) to cover it at $13 \text{ m/s}$.

Finally, the total time will be $t_1 + t_2 + t_3$.

WORKING:
Phase 1: Constant speed of $25 \text{ m/s}$ for $t_1 = 0.2 \text{ s}$
Distance covered ($s_1$) using $s = vt$:
$$s_1 = 25 \times 0.2$$
$$s_1 = 5 \text{ m}$$

Phase 2: Deceleration from $25 \text{ m/s}$ to $13 \text{ m/s}$
This phase is the same as Phase 1 in part (b). We already calculated:
Time taken ($t_2$) = $2 \text{ s}$
Distance covered ($s_2$) = $38 \text{ m}$

Phase 3: Constant speed of $13 \text{ m/s}$
Total distance to the sign is $48 \text{ m}$.
Distance covered in Phase 1: $s_1 = 5 \text{ m}$.
Distance covered in Phase 2: $s_2 = 38 \text{ m}$.

Remaining distance ($s_3$) to be covered at constant speed:
$$s_3 = 48 - s_1 - s_2$$
$$s_3 = 48 - 5 - 38$$
$$s_3 = 5 \text{ m}$$

The car travels this distance at a constant speed of $13 \text{ m/s}$. To find the time taken ($t_3$), use $s = vt$:
$$5 = 13 \times t_3$$
$$t_3 = \frac{5}{13} \text{ s}$$

Total time to reach the sign:
$$T_{total} = t_1 + t_2 + t_3$$
$$T_{total} = 0.2 + 2 + \frac{5}{13}$$
$$T_{total} = 2.2 + \frac{5}{13}$$
To combine these, convert $2.2$ to a fraction:
$$2.2 = \frac{22}{10} = \frac{11}{5}$$
$$T_{total} = \frac{11}{5} + \frac{5}{13}$$
Find a common denominator, which is $5 \times 13 = 65$:
$$T_{total} = \frac{11 \times 13}{5 \times 13} + \frac{5 \times 5}{13 \times 5}$$
$$T_{total} = \frac{143}{65} + \frac{25}{65}$$
$$T_{total} = \frac{143 + 25}{65}$$
$$T_{total} = \frac{168}{65} \text{ s}$$
(Approximately $2.58 \text{ s}$ to 3 significant figures).

Answer: The value of $t$ at which the car reaches the sign is $\frac{168}{65} \text{ s}$.

Key techniques

  • applying SUVAT equations
  • breaking motion into stages
  • distance-time relationships

Common pitfalls

  • Incorrectly handling the sign of acceleration (deceleration should be negative).
  • Failing to break down multi-stage problems into individual phases and apply SUVAT correctly to each.
  • Using incorrect distances or times between phases (e.g., using total distance for a partial phase, or total time for one phase).
  • Arithmetic errors when dealing with fractions or decimals, especially when combining them.
