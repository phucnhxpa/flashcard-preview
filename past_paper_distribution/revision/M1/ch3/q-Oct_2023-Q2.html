Worked solution

Part (a): Find the speed of the particle when $t=20$ s.

Thought Process:
We recognise this as a multi-stage kinematics problem because the particle's acceleration changes at specific time intervals. To find the final speed, we must consider each stage of the motion sequentially. The final speed of one stage becomes the initial speed for the subsequent stage. We'll use the constant acceleration formula $v = u + at$ for each segment of the journey.

Working:

Stage 1: Motion from $t=0$ s to $t=4$ s


  • Initial speed, $u = 8 \text{ m/s}$

  • Acceleration, $a = -0.5 \text{ m/s}^2$

  • Time duration, $\Delta t = 4 - 0 = 4 \text{ s}$

Applying the SUVAT equation $v = u + at$:
$$v_4 = 8 + (-0.5)(4)$$
$$v_4 = 8 - 2$$
$$v_4 = 6 \text{ m/s}$$

Stage 2: Motion from $t=4$ s to $t=10$ s


  • Initial speed (the speed at the end of Stage 1), $u = 6 \text{ m/s}$

  • Acceleration, $a = 0 \text{ m/s}^2$

  • Time duration, $\Delta t = 10 - 4 = 6 \text{ s}$

Applying the SUVAT equation $v = u + at$:
$$v_{10} = 6 + (0)(6)$$
$$v_{10} = 6 \text{ m/s}$$

Stage 3: Motion from $t=10$ s to $t=20$ s


  • Initial speed (the speed at the end of Stage 2), $u = 6 \text{ m/s}$

  • Acceleration, $a = 1.2 \text{ m/s}^2$

  • Time duration, $\Delta t = 20 - 10 = 10 \text{ s}$

Applying the SUVAT equation $v = u + at$:
$$v_{20} = 6 + (1.2)(10)$$
$$v_{20} = 6 + 12$$
$$v_{20} = 18 \text{ m/s}$$

Answer: The speed of the particle when $t=20$ s is $18 \text{ m/s}$.

Part (b): Sketch a speed-time graph for the motion of the particle for $0 \le t \le 20$.

Thought Process:
A speed-time graph plots speed on the vertical (y) axis and time on the horizontal (x) axis. For motion under constant acceleration, the graph will be represented by straight line segments. A positive acceleration results in a line with a positive gradient, zero acceleration results in a horizontal line, and negative acceleration results in a line with a negative gradient. We'll use the speeds calculated in part (a) at the key time points: $t=0$ (speed $8$), $t=4$ (speed $6$), $t=10$ (speed $6$), and $t=20$ (speed $18$).

Working:


  1. Begin at $(0,8)$, representing the initial speed at $t=0$.

  2. Draw a straight line segment downwards from $(0,8)$ to $(4,6)$. This shows the constant deceleration from $t=0$ to $t=4$.

  3. From $(4,6)$, draw a horizontal straight line segment to $(10,6)$. This indicates constant speed from $t=4$ to $t=10$.

  4. From $(10,6)$, draw a straight line segment upwards to $(20,18)$. This represents constant acceleration from $t=10$ to $t=20$.

The graph must clearly label the horizontal axis as 'Time (s)' and the vertical axis as 'Speed (m/s)'. The specific time values $0, 4, 10, 20$ should be marked on the time axis, and the specific speed values $6, 8, 18$ should be marked on the speed axis.

Part (c): Calculate the total distance travelled by the particle in the interval $0 \le t \le 20$.

Thought Process:
The total distance travelled by a particle is found by calculating the area under its speed-time graph. The graph for this motion can be divided into three distinct geometric shapes: a trapezium from $t=0$ to $t=4$, a rectangle from $t=4$ to $t=10$, and another trapezium from $t=10$ to $t=20$. We will calculate the area of each shape and sum them to determine the total distance.

Working:

Area 1 (Trapezium for $0 \le t \le 4$):
This trapezium has parallel sides (speeds) of $u=8 \text{ m/s}$ and $v_4=6 \text{ m/s}$, with a perpendicular height (time duration) of $4 \text{ s}$.
$$\text{Area}_1 = \frac{1}{2}(u + v_4) \Delta t_1$$
$$\text{Area}_1 = \frac{1}{2}(8 + 6)(4)$$
$$\text{Area}_1 = \frac{1}{2}(14)(4)$$
$$\text{Area}_1 = 7 \times 4 = 28 \text{ m}$$

Area 2 (Rectangle for $4 < t \le 10$):
This rectangle has a height (constant speed) of $6 \text{ m/s}$ and a width (time duration) of $(10 - 4) = 6 \text{ s}$.
$$\text{Area}_2 = \text{speed} \times \text{time duration}$$
$$\text{Area}_2 = 6 \times 6$$
$$\text{Area}_2 = 36 \text{ m}$$

Area 3 (Trapezium for $10 < t \le 20$):
This trapezium has parallel sides (speeds) of $u_{10}=6 \text{ m/s}$ and $v_{20}=18 \text{ m/s}$, with a perpendicular height (time duration) of $(20 - 10) = 10 \text{ s}$.
$$\text{Area}_3 = \frac{1}{2}(u_{10} + v_{20}) \Delta t_3$$
$$\text{Area}_3 = \frac{1}{2}(6 + 18)(10)$$
$$\text{Area}_3 = \frac{1}{2}(24)(10)$$
$$\text{Area}_3 = 12 \times 10 = 120 \text{ m}$$

Total Distance:
Total Distance = Area$_1$ + Area$_2$ + Area$_3$
Total Distance = $28 \text{ m} + 36 \text{ m} + 120 \text{ m}$
Total Distance = $184 \text{ m}$

Answer: The total distance travelled by the particle in the interval $0 \le t \le 20$ is $184 \text{ m}$.

Key techniques

  • applying $v=u+at$ over intervals
  • linking final speed of one stage as initial speed for next
  • drawing speed-time graphs with correct features
  • calculating area under graph for total distance

Common pitfalls

  • Using an incorrect time duration for a stage when applying SUVAT equations (e.g., using cumulative time instead of the duration of the specific interval).
  • Failing to correctly update the initial speed ($u$) for each new stage of motion, leading to incorrect calculations for subsequent stages.
  • Errors in calculating the area of geometric shapes under the graph, particularly misremembering the formula for a trapezium or using incorrect side lengths/heights.
