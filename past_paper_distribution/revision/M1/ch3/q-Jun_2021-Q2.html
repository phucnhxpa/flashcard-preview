2.	 A car moves along a straight horizontal road with constant acceleration a m s –2 
where a > 0
	
The car is modelled as a particle.
	
At time t = 0, the car passes point A and is moving with speed u m s –1
	
In the first three seconds after passing A the car travels 20 m.
	
In the fourth second after passing A the car travels 10 m.
	
The speed of the car as it passes point B is 20 m s –1 
	
Find the time taken for the car to travel from A to B.

Worked solution

We recognize this as a problem involving constant acceleration, which means we should use the suvat equations. The problem provides information about displacement over two different time intervals, which will allow us to set up two simultaneous equations to find the initial speed ($u$) and acceleration ($a$). Once $u$ and $a$ are known, we can use the final speed at point B to find the total time.

Step 1: Formulate an equation using the first 3 seconds of motion.

We are given that in the first three seconds ($t=3$), the car travels 20 m ($s=20$). We know the initial speed is $u$ and the acceleration is $a$. We use the suvat equation $s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2$.

$$20 = u(3) + \frac{1}{2}a(3^2)$$
$$20 = 3u + \frac{9}{2}a \quad \text{(Equation 1)}$$

Step 2: Formulate an equation using the fourth second of motion.

The car travels 10 m in the fourth second. This means the displacement from $t=3$ to $t=4$ is 10 m. We can express this as the total displacement at $t=4$ minus the total displacement at $t=3$.

Let $S_t$ be the displacement from A at time $t$.
We know $S_3 = 20$ m (from Step 1).
Displacement in the 4th second $= S_4 - S_3 = 10$ m.
Therefore, $S_4 = S_3 + 10 = 20 + 10 = 30$ m.

Now, we use $s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2$ for $t=4$ and $s=30$.

$$30 = u(4) + \frac{1}{2}a(4^2)$$
$$30 = 4u + 8a \quad \text{(Equation 2)}$$

We can simplify Equation 2 by dividing by 2:
$$15 = 2u + 4a \quad \text{(Equation 2 simplified)}$$

Step 3: Solve the simultaneous equations for $u$ and $a$.

We have:


  1. $20 = 3u + 4.5a$

  2. $15 = 2u + 4a$

To eliminate $u$, multiply Equation 1 by 2 and Equation 2 by 3:

Multiply (1) by 2: $40 = 6u + 9a \quad \text{(Equation 3)}$
Multiply (2) by 3: $45 = 6u + 12a \quad \text{(Equation 4)}$

Subtract Equation 3 from Equation 4:

$(45 - 40) = (6u + 12a) - (6u + 9a)$
$5 = 3a$
$a = \frac{5}{3} \text{ m s}^{-2}$

Substitute the value of $a$ into Equation 2 (simplified):

$15 = 2u + 4\left(\frac{5}{3}\right)$
$15 = 2u + \frac{20}{3}$
$15 - \frac{20}{3} = 2u$
$\frac{45 - 20}{3} = 2u$
$\frac{25}{3} = 2u$
$u = \frac{25}{6} \text{ m s}^{-1}$

Step 4: Find the time taken to travel from A to B.

We know the initial speed $u = \frac{25}{6}$ m s$^{-1}$, the acceleration $a = \frac{5}{3}$ m s$^{-2}$, and the final speed at B is $v = 20$ m s$^{-1}$. We need to find the time $t$.

We use the suvat equation $v = u + at$.

$$20 = \frac{25}{6} + \frac{5}{3}t$$
$$20 - \frac{25}{6} = \frac{5}{3}t$$
$$\frac{120 - 25}{6} = \frac{5}{3}t$$
$$\frac{95}{6} = \frac{5}{3}t$$
$$t = \frac{95}{6} \times \frac{3}{5}$$
$$t = \frac{95 \times 3}{6 \times 5}$$
$$t = \frac{19 \times 5 \times 3}{2 \times 3 \times 5}$$
$$t = \frac{19}{2}$$
$$t = 9.5 \text{ s}$$

Answer: The time taken for the car to travel from A to B is 9.5 seconds.

Key techniques

  • Apply suvat equations for constant acceleration
  • Formulate simultaneous equations from given conditions
  • Solve linear simultaneous equations
  • Calculate displacement during a specific second

Common pitfalls

  • Misinterpreting 'in the nth second' as displacement at time t=n rather than $S_n - S_{n-1}$.
  • Algebraic errors when solving the simultaneous equations for $u$ and $a$.
  • Choosing the incorrect suvat equation or mixing up variables.
