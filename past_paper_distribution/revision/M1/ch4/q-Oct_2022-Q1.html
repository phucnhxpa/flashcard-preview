Worked solution

We recognize this as a collision problem involving two objects that coalesce (stick together). The fundamental principle for such a scenario is the Conservation of Linear Momentum. We apply this to the system of both trucks before and after the collision to find their common speed. Then, for part (b), we need to find the impulse on one specific truck (S). Impulse is defined as the change in momentum of that object, so we'll use the Impulse-Momentum Principle for truck S.

First, convert the masses from tonnes to kilograms, as these are standard SI units for Mechanics problems:
Truck S: $m_S = 20 \text{ tonnes} = 20 \times 1000 \text{ kg} = 20000 \text{ kg}$
Truck T: $m_T = 30 \text{ tonnes} = 30 \times 1000 \text{ kg} = 30000 \text{ kg}$

Let the initial direction of motion of truck S be the positive direction.

(a) Find the common speed of the railway trucks immediately after the collision.

  • Momentum before collision:
  • Momentum of S ($P_S$) = $m_S u_S = 20000 \times 4 = 80000 \text{ kg m s}^{-1}$
  • Momentum of T ($P_T$) = $m_T u_T = 30000 \times 0 = 0 \text{ kg m s}^{-1}$ (since T is at rest)
  • Total momentum before = $80000 + 0 = 80000 \text{ kg m s}^{-1}$
  • Momentum after collision:
  • The trucks join, so their combined mass is $M = m_S + m_T = 20000 + 30000 = 50000 \text{ kg}$.
  • Let their common speed after the collision be $v \text{ m s}^{-1}$.
  • Total momentum after = $M v = 50000 v \text{ kg m s}^{-1}$
  • Applying Conservation of Linear Momentum (Total momentum before = Total momentum after):
$$80000 = 50000 v$$ To find $v$, we divide both sides by 50000: $$v = \frac{80000}{50000}$$ $$v = \frac{8}{5}$$ $$v = 1.6 \text{ m s}^{-1}$$

Answer (a): The common speed of the railway trucks immediately after the collision is $1.6 \text{ m s}^{-1}$.

(b) Find the magnitude of the impulse exerted on S in the collision, stating the units of your answer.

  • Impulse ($I$) is defined as the change in momentum. For truck S, this is:
$I_S = \text{final momentum of S} - \text{initial momentum of S}$ $I_S = m_S v - m_S u_S$ $I_S = m_S (v - u_S)$
  • Substitute the values:
  • Mass of S ($m_S$) = $20000 \text{ kg}$
  • Initial speed of S ($u_S$) = $4 \text{ m s}^{-1}$
  • Final speed of S ($v$) = $1.6 \text{ m s}^{-1}$ (from part a)

$$I_S = 20000 (1.6 - 4)$$
$$I_S = 20000 (-2.4)$$
$$I_S = -48000 \text{ N s}$$

  • The question asks for the *magnitude* of the impulse. The negative sign indicates that the impulse is in the opposite direction to the initial motion of S (i.e., a retarding impulse). Therefore, we take the absolute value.
Magnitude of impulse = $|-48000| = 48000 \text{ N s}$

Answer (b): The magnitude of the impulse exerted on S in the collision is $48000 \text{ N s}$.

Key techniques

  • Apply Conservation of Linear Momentum for coalescing objects
  • Apply Impulse-Momentum Principle to a single object
  • Ensure consistent positive direction for velocities
  • Convert all units to SI base units (e.g., tonnes to kg)

Common pitfalls

  • Forgetting to convert units from tonnes to kg, leading to incorrect calculations.
  • Incorrectly applying the Impulse-Momentum Principle, e.g., calculating the change in momentum for the *entire system* instead of just truck S, or mixing up initial and final velocities.
  • Not stating the units for impulse (N s or kg m s$^{-1}$) or providing incorrect units.
  • Leaving the impulse as a negative value instead of stating its magnitude as requested.
