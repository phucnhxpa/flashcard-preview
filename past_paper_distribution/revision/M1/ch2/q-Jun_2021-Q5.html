5.	 [In this question i and j are perpendicular horizontal unit vectors.]
	
A particle P is moving with constant acceleration.  At 2 pm, the velocity of P is 
(3i + 5j) km h –1 and at 2.30 pm the velocity of P is (i + 7j) km h –1
	
At time T hours after 2 pm, P is moving in the direction of the vector (– i + 2j)
	
(a)	 Find the value of T.
(6)
	
Another particle, Q, has velocity vQ km h –1 at time t hours after 2 pm, where
	
vQ = (– 4 –2t) i + ( μ + 3t) j
	
and μ is a constant.
	
Given that there is an instant when the velocity of P is equal to the velocity of Q,
	
(b)	 find the value of  μ.

Question 5 continued
Worked solution

Part (a): Find the value of $T$

Thought Process:
We are given the initial velocity ($u$) and a later velocity ($v_1$) at a specific time, and told the particle has constant acceleration. This immediately suggests using the constant acceleration formula $v = u + at$ in vector form to find the acceleration, $a$. Once $a$ is known, we can form a general expression for the velocity of particle P, $v_P(t)$. The condition that P is moving in the direction of vector $(-\mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j})$ means that its velocity vector at time $T$ must be a scalar multiple of this direction vector. We can then equate the components of $v_P(T)$ to solve for $T$.

Working:
Let $t$ be the time in hours after 2 pm.
At $t=0$ (2 pm), the initial velocity is $u = (3\mathbf{i} + 5\mathbf{j})$ km h⁻¹.
At $t=0.5$ (2:30 pm), the velocity is $v_1 = (\mathbf{i} + 7\mathbf{j})$ km h⁻¹.

Using the constant acceleration equation $v = u + at$:
$$ (\mathbf{i} + 7\mathbf{j}) = (3\mathbf{i} + 5\mathbf{j}) + a(0.5) $$
Rearrange to find $a(0.5)$:
$$ a(0.5) = (\mathbf{i} + 7\mathbf{j}) - (3\mathbf{i} + 5\mathbf{j}) $$
$$ a(0.5) = (1-3)\mathbf{i} + (7-5)\mathbf{j} $$
$$ a(0.5) = (-2\mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j}) $$
Multiply by 2 to find the acceleration $a$:
$$ a = (-4\mathbf{i} + 4\mathbf{j}) ext{ km h⁻²} $$

Now, form the general velocity vector for particle P at time $t$:
$$ v_P(t) = u + at $$
$$ v_P(t) = (3\mathbf{i} + 5\mathbf{j}) + t(-4\mathbf{i} + 4\mathbf{j}) $$
$$ v_P(t) = (3-4t)\mathbf{i} + (5+4t)\mathbf{j} $$

At time $T$, particle P is moving in the direction of $(-\mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j})$. This means its velocity vector $v_P(T)$ must be parallel to $(-\mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j})$, so it can be written as a scalar multiple of it. Let this scalar be $k$.
$$ v_P(T) = k(-\mathbf{i} + 2\mathbf{j}) = -k\mathbf{i} + 2k\mathbf{j} $$

Equating the components of $v_P(T)$:
For the $\mathbf{i}$ components: $3-4T = -k \quad (1)$
For the $\mathbf{j}$ components: $5+4T = 2k \quad (2)$

From equation (1), we can express $k$ as $k = -(3-4T) = 4T-3$. Substitute this into equation (2):
$$ 5+4T = 2(4T-3) $$
$$ 5+4T = 8T-6 $$
Gather terms involving $T$ on one side and constants on the other:
$$ 5+6 = 8T-4T $$
$$ 11 = 4T $$
$$ T = rac{11}{4} = 2.75 ext{ hours} $$

Answer: $T = 2.75$

Part (b): Find the value of $\mu$

Thought Process:
We are given the velocity of particle Q as $v_Q(t)$ and told that at some instant, the velocity of P is equal to the velocity of Q. This means $v_P(t) = v_Q(t)$ for a particular value of $t$. We can equate the $\mathbf{i}$ components of $v_P(t)$ and $v_Q(t)$ to find this specific time $t$. Once $t$ is known, we can then equate the $\mathbf{j}$ components and substitute the value of $t$ to solve for the unknown constant $\mu$.

Working:
From part (a), the velocity of particle P at time $t$ is:
$$ v_P(t) = (3-4t)\mathbf{i} + (5+4t)\mathbf{j} $$
The velocity of particle Q at time $t$ is given as:
$$ v_Q(t) = (-4 - 2t)\mathbf{i} + (\mu + 3t)\mathbf{j} $$

Given that $v_P(t) = v_Q(t)$ at some instant, we equate their corresponding $\mathbf{i}$ and $\mathbf{j}$ components.

Equating the $\mathbf{i}$ components:
$$ 3-4t = -4-2t $$
Gather terms involving $t$ on one side:
$$ 3+4 = 4t-2t $$
$$ 7 = 2t $$
$$ t = rac{7}{2} = 3.5 ext{ hours} $$

Now, equate the $\mathbf{j}$ components:
$$ 5+4t = \mu+3t $$
Substitute the value of $t=3.5$ hours into this equation:
$$ 5 + 4(3.5) = \mu + 3(3.5) $$
$$ 5 + 14 = \mu + 10.5 $$
$$ 19 = \mu + 10.5 $$
Solve for $\mu$:
$$ \mu = 19 - 10.5 $$
$$ \mu = 8.5 $$

Answer: $\mu = 8.5$

Key techniques

  • apply $v = u + at$ for vectors
  • find acceleration from two velocity points
  • interpret 'moving in the direction of a vector'
  • equate corresponding vector components
  • solve simultaneous equations

Common pitfalls

  • Calculation errors when rearranging vector equations or solving simultaneous equations.
  • Misinterpreting 'moving in the direction of' – forgetting to introduce a scalar multiple ($k$).
  • Not equating corresponding components correctly (e.g., trying to solve for $t$ and $\mu$ simultaneously from one component equation).
