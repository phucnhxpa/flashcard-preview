Calculate bearing and speed using position and velocity vectors for two moving particles.

Question (text transcript fallback) 
[In this question i and j are horizontal unit vectors due east and due north respectively and 
position vectors are given relative to a fixed origin O.]
	
At time t  =  0, a bird A leaves its nest, that is located at the point with position vector 
(20i  –  17j) m, and flies with constant velocity (–6i  +  7j) m s–1.  At the same time a second 
bird B leaves its nest which is located at the point with position vector (–8i  +  9j) m and 
flies with constant velocity ( pi  +  2pj) m s–1, where p is a constant.  At time t  =  4 s, bird B is 
south west of bird A.
	
(a)	Find the direction of motion of A, giving your answer as a bearing to the nearest degree.

Worked solution

Part (a)
*Thought process:* The direction of motion is given by the velocity vector $\mathbf{v}_A$. Bearings are measured clockwise from North. We need to determine the angle of $\mathbf{v}_A$ from North.

*Working:*
The velocity vector for bird A is $\mathbf{v}_A = -6\mathbf{i} + 7\mathbf{j}$. This vector has a negative $\mathbf{i}$ component (West) and a positive $\mathbf{j}$ component (North), placing it in the North-West quadrant.

Let $\alpha$ be the angle measured from the positive $\mathbf{j}$ axis (North) towards the West.
$$ \tan \alpha = \frac{\text{Horizontal component}}{\text{Vertical component}} = \frac{6}{7} $$
$$ \alpha = \arctan\left(\frac{6}{7}\right) \approx 40.601^\circ $$
The bearing is measured clockwise from North, so:
$$ \text{Bearing} = 360^\circ - \alpha = 360^\circ - 40.601^\circ = 319.399^\circ $$
Rounding to the nearest degree:

Answer: The direction of motion of A is $319^\circ$ (to the nearest degree).

Part (b)
*Thought process:* To find the speed of B, we first need to determine the value of the constant $p$ in its velocity vector $\mathbf{v}_B = (p\mathbf{i} + 2p\mathbf{j})$. We are given that at $t=4\text{ s}$, bird B is south west of bird A. This condition links their position vectors at $t=4\text{ s}$. We'll use $\mathbf{r} = \mathbf{r}_0 + t\mathbf{v}$ for both birds, then form the relative position vector $\mathbf{r}_{BA} = \mathbf{r}_B(4) - \mathbf{r}_A(4)$. The "south west" condition means that the $\mathbf{i}$ and $\mathbf{j}$ components of $\mathbf{r}_{BA}$ must be equal and negative. This allows us to solve for $p$, and subsequently for the speed $|\mathbf{v}_B|$.

*Working:*
Given initial positions and constant velocities:
Bird A: $\mathbf{r}_{A,0} = (20\mathbf{i} - 17\mathbf{j})\text{ m}$, $\mathbf{v}_A = (-6\mathbf{i} + 7\mathbf{j})\text{ m s}^{-1}$
Bird B: $\mathbf{r}_{B,0} = (-8\mathbf{i} + 9\mathbf{j})\text{ m}$, $\mathbf{v}_B = (p\mathbf{i} + 2p\mathbf{j})\text{ m s}^{-1}$

At time $t=4\text{ s}$:
Position of A, $\mathbf{r}_A(4)$:
$$ \mathbf{r}_A(4) = (20\mathbf{i} - 17\mathbf{j}) + 4(-6\mathbf{i} + 7\mathbf{j}) = (20 - 24)\mathbf{i} + (-17 + 28)\mathbf{j} = -4\mathbf{i} + 11\mathbf{j} $$
Position of B, $\mathbf{r}_B(4)$:
$$ \mathbf{r}_B(4) = (-8\mathbf{i} + 9\mathbf{j}) + 4(p\mathbf{i} + 2p\mathbf{j}) = (-8 + 4p)\mathbf{i} + (9 + 8p)\mathbf{j} $$

Relative position vector $\mathbf{r}_{BA} = \mathbf{r}_B(4) - \mathbf{r}_A(4)$:
$$ \mathbf{r}_{BA} = [(-8 + 4p)\mathbf{i} + (9 + 8p)\mathbf{j}] - [-4\mathbf{i} + 11\mathbf{j}] $$
$$ \mathbf{r}_{BA} = ((-8 + 4p) - (-4))\mathbf{i} + ((9 + 8p) - 11)\mathbf{j} $$
$$ \mathbf{r}_{BA} = (4p - 4)\mathbf{i} + (8p - 2)\mathbf{j} $$

The condition "bird B is south west of bird A" implies that the $\mathbf{i}$ and $\mathbf{j}$ components of $\mathbf{r}_{BA}$ must be equal and negative.
$$ 4p - 4 = 8p - 2 $$
$$ -2 = 4p $$
$$ p = -\frac{1}{2} $$
We check that both components are negative for $p = -1/2$: $4p - 4 = 4(-1/2) - 4 = -2 - 4 = -6$ and $8p - 2 = 8(-1/2) - 2 = -4 - 2 = -6$. Both are indeed negative, so $p = -1/2$ is correct.

Now, substitute $p = -1/2$ into the velocity vector for bird B:
$$ \mathbf{v}_B = (p\mathbf{i} + 2p\mathbf{j}) = \left(-\frac{1}{2}\mathbf{i} + 2\left(-\frac{1}{2}\right)\mathbf{j}\right) = -\frac{1}{2}\mathbf{i} - \mathbf{j} $$
Finally, calculate the speed of B, which is the magnitude of $\mathbf{v}_B$:
$$ \text{Speed of B} = |\mathbf{v}_B| = \sqrt{\left(-\frac{1}{2}\right)^2 + (-1)^2} $$
$$ |\mathbf{v}_B| = \sqrt{\frac{1}{4} + 1} = \sqrt{\frac{5}{4}} = \frac{\sqrt{5}}{2} $$

Answer: The speed of B is $\frac{\sqrt{5}}{2}$ m s$^{-1}$ (or $1.12$ m s$^{-1}$ to 3 s.f.).

Key techniques

  • r = r_0 + vt (constant velocity)
  • calculating bearings from velocity vectors
  • vector subtraction for relative position
  • interpreting relative position conditions
  • magnitude of a vector (speed)

Common pitfalls

  • Incorrectly converting a vector's angle to a bearing (e.g., measuring from the x-axis instead of North, or wrong direction of rotation).
  • Misinterpreting 'south west of' (e.g., incorrectly setting up the relative position vector or not equating its i and j components for a 45-degree angle).
  • Algebraic errors when solving for the unknown constant 'p', or forgetting to take the magnitude for speed.
