Worked solution

(a) Find the direction in which Q is travelling, giving your answer as a bearing.


  • Thought: The velocity vector $(20\mathbf{i} - 20\mathbf{j})$ shows components of motion. Calculate the angle from the East direction, then convert to a 3-figure bearing (clockwise from North).


The velocity of Q is $\mathbf{v}_Q = 20\mathbf{i} - 20\mathbf{j}$.
This vector indicates 20 units East and 20 units South.
Let $\alpha$ be the angle formed with the positive East axis.
$ \tan \alpha = \frac{\text{South component}}{\text{East component}} = \frac{20}{20} = 1 $
$ \alpha = \arctan(1) = 45^\circ $.
Since motion is East and South (South-East quadrant), the bearing is $90^\circ$ (East) + $45^\circ$ (towards South).
Bearing $= 90^\circ + 45^\circ = 135^\circ$.
Answer: $135^\circ$

(b) Show that $\vec{PQ} = [5t\mathbf{i} + (200 - 20t)\mathbf{j}]\text{m}$.


  • Thought: Use the formula $\mathbf{r} = \mathbf{r}_0 + \mathbf{v}t$ to find position vectors $\mathbf{p}$ and $\mathbf{q}$. Then compute the relative position vector $\vec{PQ} = \mathbf{q} - \mathbf{p}$.


For boat P:
Initial position $\mathbf{p}_0 = \mathbf{0}$, velocity $\mathbf{v}_P = 15\mathbf{i}$.
Position vector $\mathbf{p} = \mathbf{0} + 15t\mathbf{i} = 15t\mathbf{i}$.
For boat Q:
Initial position $\mathbf{q}_0 = 200\mathbf{j}$, velocity $\mathbf{v}_Q = 20\mathbf{i} - 20\mathbf{j}$.
Position vector $\mathbf{q} = 200\mathbf{j} + (20\mathbf{i} - 20\mathbf{j})t = 20t\mathbf{i} + (200 - 20t)\mathbf{j}$.
The relative position vector $\vec{PQ}$ is:
$ \vec{PQ} = \mathbf{q} - \mathbf{p} = (20t\mathbf{i} + (200 - 20t)\mathbf{j}) - 15t\mathbf{i} $
$ \vec{PQ} = (20t - 15t)\mathbf{i} + (200 - 20t)\mathbf{j} $
$ \vec{PQ} = [5t\mathbf{i} + (200 - 20t)\mathbf{j}]\text{m} $. (Shown)

(c) Find the bearing of P from Q when $t=10$.


  • Thought: First, substitute $t=10$ into $\vec{PQ}$. The bearing of P from Q requires the vector $\vec{QP} = -\vec{PQ}$. Convert this vector's direction to a 3-figure bearing.


At $t=10$:
$ \vec{PQ} = 5(10)\mathbf{i} + (200 - 20(10))\mathbf{j} = 50\mathbf{i} + (200 - 200)\mathbf{j} = 50\mathbf{i} $.
The vector representing P from Q is $\vec{QP} = -\vec{PQ} = -50\mathbf{i}$.
This vector indicates P is 50m directly West of Q.
On a bearing scale, West corresponds to $270^\circ$.
Answer: $270^\circ$

(d) Find the distance between P and Q when Q is north east of P.


  • Thought: "Q is North East of P" means the relative position vector $\vec{PQ}$ has equal and positive $\mathbf{i}$ and $\mathbf{j}$ components. Use this condition to find $t$, then calculate the magnitude of $\vec{PQ}$ at that time.


If Q is North East of P, then the $\mathbf{i}$ and $\mathbf{j}$ components of $\vec{PQ}$ are equal:
$ 5t = 200 - 20t $
$ 25t = 200 $
$ t = \frac{200}{25} = 8 $ seconds.
Substitute $t=8$ into the expression for $\vec{PQ}$:
$ \vec{PQ} = 5(8)\mathbf{i} + (200 - 20(8))\mathbf{j} $
$ \vec{PQ} = 40\mathbf{i} + (200 - 160)\mathbf{j} = 40\mathbf{i} + 40\mathbf{j} $.
The distance between P and Q is the magnitude of $\vec{PQ}$:
$ |\vec{PQ}| = \sqrt{40^2 + 40^2} = \sqrt{1600 + 1600} = \sqrt{3200} $
$ |\vec{PQ}| = \sqrt{1600 \times 2} = 40\sqrt{2} $ m.
Answer: $40\sqrt{2}$ m (or $56.6$ m, 3 s.f.)

(e) Find the times when P and Q are 200m apart.


  • Thought: The distance between P and Q is $ |\vec{PQ}| $. Set this equal to 200m, then square both sides to form a quadratic equation in $t$. Solve the quadratic to find the times.


Set the magnitude of $\vec{PQ}$ equal to 200 m:
$ |\vec{PQ}| = \sqrt{(5t)^2 + (200 - 20t)^2} = 200 $.
Square both sides:
$ (5t)^2 + (200 - 20t)^2 = 200^2 $
$ 25t^2 + (40000 - 8000t + 400t^2) = 40000 $
Combine like terms:
$ 425t^2 - 8000t + 40000 = 40000 $
$ 425t^2 - 8000t = 0 $
Factor out $t$:
$ t(425t - 8000) = 0 $.
This gives two possible values for $t$:
$ t = 0 $ or $ 425t - 8000 = 0 $
$ 425t = 8000 $
$ t = \frac{8000}{425} = \frac{320}{17} $ seconds.
Answer: $t = 0$ s and $t = \frac{320}{17}$ s (or $t \approx 18.8$ s, 3 s.f.)

Key techniques

  • Calculate position vector $\mathbf{r} = \mathbf{r}_0 + \mathbf{v}t$
  • Calculate relative position $\vec{PQ} = \mathbf{q} - \mathbf{p}$
  • Convert vector direction to bearing
  • Find magnitude of a vector
  • Solve quadratic equations

Common pitfalls

  • Confusing $\vec{PQ}$ with $\vec{QP}$ when calculating bearings or relative positions.
  • Incorrectly converting vector direction to a 3-figure bearing (e.g., measuring from East, or counter-clockwise from North).
  • Algebraic errors when expanding brackets or simplifying quadratic equations, especially in part (e).
