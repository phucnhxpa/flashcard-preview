Q4 — Discrete RV, $E(g(X))$, $\text{Var}(g(X))$, Conditional

Question

The discrete random variable $X$ has probability distribution

$x$ 1 2 3 4
$P(X=x)$ $\tfrac{1}{10}$ $\tfrac{1}{5}$ $\tfrac{3}{10}$ $\tfrac{2}{5}$

(a) Show that $E\!\left(\dfrac{1}{X}\right) = \dfrac{2}{5}$. (1)
(b) Find $\text{Var}\!\left(\dfrac{1}{X}\right)$. (3)
(c) The random variable $Y = \dfrac{30}{X}$. Find (i) $E(Y)$, (ii) $\text{Var}(Y)$. (3)
(d) Find $P(X < 3 \mid Y < 20)$. (5)

[12 marks]

Part (a)[1 mark] · 1 step

STEP 1/1

Show $E(1/X) = 2/5$:
$$E\!\left(\tfrac{1}{X}\right) = \tfrac{1}{1}\cdot \tfrac{1}{10} + \tfrac{1}{2}\cdot\tfrac{1}{5} + \tfrac{1}{3}\cdot\tfrac{3}{10} + \tfrac{1}{4}\cdot\tfrac{2}{5}$$
$$= \tfrac{1}{10} + \tfrac{1}{10} + \tfrac{1}{10} + \tfrac{1}{10} = \tfrac{4}{10} = \boxed{\tfrac{2}{5}}.\;\blacksquare$$

Target: $\boxed{\tfrac{2}{5}}$

Part (b)[3 marks] · 2 steps

Carry-in: Part (a) result: $\boxed{\tfrac{2}{5}}$
STEP 1/2

Compute $E(1/X^2)$:
$$E\!\left(\tfrac{1}{X^2}\right) = 1\cdot\tfrac{1}{10} + \tfrac{1}{4}\cdot\tfrac{1}{5} + \tfrac{1}{9}\cdot\tfrac{3}{10} + \tfrac{1}{16}\cdot\tfrac{2}{5}.$$

Terms:
- $1 \cdot \tfrac{1}{10} = \tfrac{1}{10}$.
- $\tfrac{1}{4} \cdot \tfrac{1}{5} = \tfrac{1}{20}$.
- $\tfrac{1}{9} \cdot \tfrac{3}{10} = \tfrac{3}{90} = \tfrac{1}{30}$.
- $\tfrac{1}{16} \cdot \tfrac{2}{5} = \tfrac{2}{80} = \tfrac{1}{40}$.

Common denominator $120$:
$$\tfrac{12}{120} + \tfrac{6}{120} + \tfrac{4}{120} + \tfrac{3}{120} = \tfrac{25}{120} = \tfrac{5}{24}.$$

STEP 2/2

Therefore
$$\text{Var}\!\left(\tfrac{1}{X}\right) = E\!\left(\tfrac{1}{X^2}\right) - \left[E\!\left(\tfrac{1}{X}\right)\right]^2 = \tfrac{5}{24} - \left(\tfrac{2}{5}\right)^2 = \tfrac{5}{24} - \tfrac{4}{25}.$$

Common denominator $600$:
$$= \tfrac{125}{600} - \tfrac{96}{600} = \tfrac{29}{600}.$$

$$\boxed{\text{Var}\!\left(\tfrac{1}{X}\right) = \tfrac{29}{600} \approx 0.0483.}$$

Target: $\boxed{\text{Var}\!\left(\tfrac{1}{X}\right) = \tfrac{29}{600} \approx 0.0483.}$

Part (c)1 step

Carry-in: Part (a) result: $\boxed{\tfrac{2}{5}}$
Part (b) result: $\boxed{\text{Var}\!\left(\tfrac{1}{X}\right) = \tfrac{29}{600} \approx 0.0483.}$
STEP 1/1

$Y = 30/X = 30 \cdot (1/X)$, so by linear scaling:
$$E(Y) = 30 \cdot E\!\left(\tfrac{1}{X}\right) = 30 \cdot \tfrac{2}{5} = \boxed{12}.$$
$$\text{Var}(Y) = 30^2 \cdot \text{Var}\!\left(\tfrac{1}{X}\right) = 900 \cdot \tfrac{29}{600} = \tfrac{900\cdot 29}{600} = \tfrac{3\cdot 29}{2} = \tfrac{87}{2} = \boxed{43.5}.$$

Target: $\boxed{43.5}$

Part (d)[5 marks] · 3 steps

Carry-in: Part (a) result: $\boxed{\tfrac{2}{5}}$
Part (b) result: $\boxed{\text{Var}\!\left(\tfrac{1}{X}\right) = \tfrac{29}{600} \approx 0.0483.}$
Part (c) result: $\boxed{43.5}$
STEP 1/3

Translate $Y < 20$: $\tfrac{30}{X} < 20 \iff X > \tfrac{30}{20} = 1.5$ (valid since $X > 0$). So $\{Y < 20\} = \{X \in \{2,3,4\}\}$.

Also $\{X < 3\} = \{X \in \{1,2\}\}$.

STEP 2/3

Intersection: $\{X \in \{2,3,4\}\} \cap \{X \in \{1,2\}\} = \{X = 2\}$.

$$P(X = 2) = \tfrac{1}{5} = \tfrac{2}{10}.$$
$$P(Y < 20) = P(X \in \{2,3,4\}) = \tfrac{2}{10} + \tfrac{3}{10} + \tfrac{4}{10} = \tfrac{9}{10}.$$
$$P(X<3 \mid Y<20) = \frac{P(X=2)}{P(Y<20)} = \frac{2/10}{9/10} = \boxed{\tfrac{2}{9}}.$$

Target: $\boxed{\tfrac{2}{9}}$
STEP 3/3

Key formula / concept.

  • $E(g(X)) = \sum g(x) P(X=x)$.
  • $\text{Var}(X) = E(X^2) - [E(X)]^2$.
  • $E(aX) = aE(X)$, $\text{Var}(aX) = a^2 \text{Var}(X)$.
  • For conditional on a function of $X$: translate first to a condition on $X$ directly.

Exam checklist.

  • [ ] Probabilities sum to $1$: $\tfrac{1}{10}+\tfrac{1}{5}+\tfrac{3}{10}+\tfrac{2}{5} = \tfrac{1+2+3+4}{10} = 1$. ✓
  • [ ] $\text{Var}(aX) = a^2\text{Var}(X)$ --- squared, not linear.
  • [ ] When translating $Y < c$ for $Y = k/X$, check the sign of $X$.
  • [ ] List which $x$-values qualify before applying the conditional formula.
  • [ ] Fractions kept to a common denominator throughout --- convert to decimals only at the end.

Common pitfalls.

  • Forgetting the square on $a$ in $\text{Var}(aX)$.
  • Treating $X$ and $Y = g(X)$ as independent --- they are perfectly dependent.
  • Arithmetic slip when finding common denominators (here $120$ for $E(1/X^2)$, then $600$ for the variance).

See also Q1(d), Q1(e) --- same conditional probability framework.
