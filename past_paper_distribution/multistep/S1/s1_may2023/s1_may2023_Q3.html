Q3 — Coded Mean / Variance, Box Plot, Outliers

Question

Jim records the length, $l$ mm, of 81 salmon. The data are coded using $x = l - 600$ with summary $\sum x = 3711$, $\sum x^2 = 475181$.
(a) Find the mean length of these salmon. (3)
(b) Find the variance of the lengths of these salmon. (2)
The weight, $w$ grams, of each of the 81 salmon is recorded to the nearest gram and summarised in the box plot with $Q_1 = 3000$, median $= 4000$, $Q_3 = 5500$, min $= 2000$, max $= 7700$.
(c) Find the maximum number of salmon that have weights in the interval $4600 < w \le 7700$. (1)
(d) Raj says that the box plot is incorrect as Jim has not included outliers. For these data an outlier is a value more than $1.5 \times \mathrm{IQR}$ above $Q_3$ or below $Q_1$. Show that there are no outliers. (3)

(a)
$$\bar x = \frac{\sum x}{n} = \frac{3711}{81} = 45.8148.$$
Reverse the shift:
$$\bar l = \bar x + 600 = 645.8148 \approx \boxed{645.8 \text{ mm}}.$$

Mistake guard from the attempt/revision notes.

What went wrong.. Two recurring mistakes in this question family:
1. Reverse-coding the variance ("$\mathrm{Var}(l) = \mathrm{Var}(x) - 600$" --- a non-sequitur; variance is invariant under translation).
2. In (c), computing $(n - \text{position of }Q_2)/2$ rather than reasoning about how many values can lie strictly above the median.

Phuc's likely incorrect approach (c).. Computing "$Q_3 - 4600 = 900$, scaled by something" --- trying to interpolate inside the box. This question is a concept question, not an interpolation question.

Target: $\boxed{645.8 \text{ mm}}$
STEP 2/5Part (b)

Strategy. Reveal this box only after you have chosen the method for Part (b). The goal is to reproduce the exam-safe working, not just the final answer.

(b) Variance is invariant under shift, so $\mathrm{Var}(l) = \mathrm{Var}(x)$:
$$\mathrm{Var}(x) = \frac{\sum x^2}{n} - \bar x^2 = \frac{475181}{81} - (45.8148)^2 = 5866.4321 - 2098.9966 = 3767.4355 \approx \boxed{3770 \text{ (3 s.f.)}}.$$

Target: $\boxed{3770 \text{ (3 s.f.)}}$
STEP 3/5Part (c)

Strategy. Reveal this box only after you have chosen the method for Part (c). The goal is to reproduce the exam-safe working, not just the final answer.

(c) The interval is $4600 < w \le 7700$. Since $4600 > \text{median} = 4000$, every value in the interval lies strictly above the median. By definition of the median (with $n=81$), at most $\lfloor 81/2 \rfloor = 40$ data points can lie strictly above 4000. Therefore the maximum count in $(4600, 7700]$ is $\boxed{40}$.

Target: $\boxed{40}$
STEP 4/5Part (d)

Strategy. Reveal this box only after you have chosen the method for Part (d). The goal is to reproduce the exam-safe working, not just the final answer.

(d) IQR = $5500 - 3000 = 2500$.
- Lower fence: $Q_1 - 1.5 \cdot \mathrm{IQR} = 3000 - 3750 = -750$.
- Upper fence: $Q_3 + 1.5 \cdot \mathrm{IQR} = 5500 + 3750 = 9250$.

Min $= 2000 > -750$ ✓ and Max $= 7700 < 9250$ ✓ --- neither extreme exceeds either fence, so no outliers exist. ✓
