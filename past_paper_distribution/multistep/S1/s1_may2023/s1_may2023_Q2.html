Q2 — Bivariate Data, Regression, Coding

Question

Two students, Olive and Shan, collect data on the weight, $w$ grams, and the tail length, $t$ cm, of 15 mice. Olive summarised the data as follows:
$$S_{tt} = 5.3173,\ \ \sum w^2 = 6089.12,\ \ \sum tw = 2304.53,\ \ \sum w = 297.8,\ \ \sum t = 114.8.$$
(a) Calculate the value of $S_{tw}$ and the value of $S_{ww}$. (3)
(b) Calculate the value of the product moment correlation coefficient between $w$ and $t$. (2)
(c) Show that the equation of the regression line of $w$ on $t$ can be written as $w = -16.7 + 4.77t$. (3)
(d) Give an interpretation of the gradient of the regression line. (1)
(e) Explain why it would not be appropriate to use the regression line in part (c) to estimate the weight of a mouse with a tail length of 2 cm. (2)
Shan decided to code the data using $x = t - 6$ and $y = \frac{w}{2} - 5$.
(f) Write down the value of the product moment correlation coefficient between $x$ and $y$. (1)
(g) Write down an equation of the regression line of $y$ on $x$. You do not need to simplify your equation. (1)

STEP 1/8Part (a)

Strategy. Reveal this box only after you have chosen the method for Part (a). The goal is to reproduce the exam-safe working, not just the final answer.

(a)
$$S_{tw} = \sum tw - \frac{\sum t \sum w}{n} = 2304.53 - \frac{114.8 \times 297.8}{15}.$$
$$\frac{114.8 \times 297.8}{15} = \frac{34187.44}{15} = 2279.16267.$$
$$S_{tw} = 2304.53 - 2279.16267 = 25.3673\overline{3} \approx \boxed{25.4}.$$

$$S_{ww} = \sum w^2 - \frac{(\sum w)^2}{n} = 6089.12 - \frac{297.8^2}{15} = 6089.12 - 5912.32267 = 176.79733 \approx \boxed{177}.$$

STEP 3/8Part (c)

Strategy. Reveal this box only after you have chosen the method for Part (c). The goal is to reproduce the exam-safe working, not just the final answer.

(c) Means:
$$\bar t = 114.8/15 = 7.65333,\qquad \bar w = 297.8/15 = 19.85333.$$
Gradient:
$$b = \frac{S_{tw}}{S_{tt}} = \frac{25.36733}{5.3173} = 4.77073 \approx 4.77.$$
Intercept:
$$a = \bar w - b\bar t = 19.85333 - 4.77073 \times 7.65333 = 19.85333 - 36.51324 = -16.65991 \approx -16.7.$$
Hence $w = -16.7 + 4.77t$. ✓

STEP 5/8Part (e)

(e) $\bar t = 7.65$ cm with $S_{tt} = 5.3173$ $\Rightarrow$ $s_t \approx \sqrt{5.3173/14} \approx 0.62$ cm; the data lie roughly in 6.4--8.9 cm. A tail length of 2 cm is far outside this range --- using the regression line at $t=2$ is extrapolation, and the prediction $w = -16.7 + 4.77(2) = -7.16$ g is physically impossible (negative weight), confirming the model is invalid there.

Formula anchor.

Key formula / concept.
$$S_{xy} = \sum xy - \frac{\sum x \sum y}{n},\quad r = \frac{S_{xy}}{\sqrt{S_{xx}S_{yy}}},\quad y = a+bx \text{ with } b = \frac{S_{xy}}{S_{xx}},\ a = \bar y - b\bar x.$$
Coding: $r$ invariant under positive linear coding; for the regression line, substitute the coding into the existing equation rather than recomputing from scratch.
