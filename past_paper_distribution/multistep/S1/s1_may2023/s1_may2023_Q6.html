Q6 — Mutually Exclusive, Conditional, Independence, Three-Set Venn

Question

Three events $A, B, C$ satisfy $P(A) = 0.1$, $P(B \mid A) = 0.3$, $P(A \cup B) = 0.25$, $P(C) = 0.5$. Given that $A$ and $C$ are mutually exclusive:
(a) Find $P(A \cup C)$. (1)
(b) Show that $P(B) = 0.18$. (3)
Given also that $B$ and $C$ are independent:
(c) Draw a Venn diagram for $A, B, C$ with all region probabilities. (5)

(a)
$$P(A \cup C) = P(A) + P(C) - P(A \cap C) = 0.1 + 0.5 - 0 = \boxed{0.6}.$$

Mistake guard from the attempt/revision notes.

What went wrong.. The classic error: confusing mutually exclusive with independent. Mutually exclusive means $P(A \cap C) = 0$; independent means $P(B \cap C) = P(B)P(C)$. They are different --- in fact, two events with positive probability cannot be both.

Phuc's likely incorrect approach (c).. Setting $P(B \cap C) = 0$ because the prior parts had a mutually-exclusive condition, instead of using the independence of $B$ and $C$ stated for part (c).

Target: $\boxed{0.6}$
STEP 2/4Part (b)

(b)
$$P(A \cap B) = P(A)\,P(B \mid A) = 0.1 \times 0.3 = 0.03.$$
Inclusion--exclusion:
$$P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cap B) \implies 0.25 = 0.1 + P(B) - 0.03,$$
$$P(B) = 0.25 - 0.1 + 0.03 = \boxed{0.18}. \quad ✓$$

Target: $\boxed{0.18}$
