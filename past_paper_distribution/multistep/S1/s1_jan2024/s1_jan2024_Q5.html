Q5 — Normal Distribution, Conditional Probability over Multiple Attempts

Question

The distance an athlete can throw a discus is normally distributed with mean 40 m and standard deviation 4 m.

(a) Using standardisation, show that the probability that this athlete throws the discus less than 38.8 m is 0.3821. (2)

This athlete enters a discus competition. To qualify for the final, they have 3 attempts to throw the discus a distance of more than 38.8 m. Once they qualify, they do not use any of their remaining attempts. Given that they qualified for the final and that throws are independent,

(b) find the probability that this athlete qualified for the final on their second throw with a distance of more than 44 m. (5)

[7 marks]

Part (a)[2 marks] · 3 steps

STEP 1/3

Standardise with $\mu = 40$, $\sigma = 4$:

STEP 2/3

$$z = \frac{38.8 - 40}{4} = \frac{-1.2}{4} = -0.30$$

STEP 3/3

$$P(X < 38.8) = P(Z < -0.30) = 1 - \Phi(0.30) = 1 - 0.6179 = \boxed{0.3821} \quad ✓$$

Target: $\boxed{0.3821}$

Part (b)[5 marks] · 5 steps

Carry-in: Part (a) result: $\boxed{0.3821}$
STEP 1/5

Define events on a single throw:
- $F$ = throw fails ($X < 38.8$): $P(F) = 0.3821$.
- $S$ = throw succeeds ($X > 38.8$): $P(S) = 0.6179$.

Compute $P(X > 44)$:

$$z = \frac{44 - 40}{4} = 1.00,\qquad P(X > 44) = 1 - \Phi(1.00) = 1 - 0.8413 = 0.1587$$

STEP 2/5

(Note $X > 44 \Rightarrow X > 38.8$, so a throw exceeding 44 automatically qualifies.)

Event "qualified on 2nd throw with $>44$": throw 1 fails AND throw 2 exceeds 44 m. Throws independent:

$$P(F_1 \cap [X_2 > 44]) = 0.3821 \times 0.1587 = 0.06064\ldots$$

STEP 3/5

Event "qualifies" (at least one of 3 throws succeeds): by complement,

$$P(\text{qualifies}) = 1 - P(F)^3 = 1 - 0.3821^3 = 1 - 0.0558 = 0.9442$$

Since the favourable event $F_1 \cap [X_2 > 44]$ is already a subset of "qualifies":

STEP 4/5

$$P(\text{2nd throw }>44 \mid \text{qualifies}) = \frac{P(F_1 \cap [X_2 > 44])}{P(\text{qualifies})} = \frac{0.06064}{0.9442}$$

$$= 0.06423\ldots \approx \boxed{0.0642\ (3\ \text{s.f.})}$$

Target: $\boxed{0.0642\ (3\ \text{s.f.})}$
STEP 5/5

Key formula / concept.

  • Standardisation: $Z = (X-\mu)/\sigma \sim N(0,1)$.
  • "At least one in $n$ independent trials": $1 - (P(\text{fail}))^n$.
  • Conditional probability: $P(A \mid B) = P(A \cap B)/P(B)$.

Exam checklist.

  • [ ] In (a), show the $z$-value AND the table lookup, not just the final 0.3821.
  • [ ] In (b), write the conditional formula on the page before computing.
  • [ ] Compute $P(\text{qualifies})$ using the complement of all-three-fail --- cleaner than summing three cases.
  • [ ] Multiply $P(F_1) \times P(X_2 > 44)$ for the joint (throws are independent --- stated in question).
  • [ ] Final answer to 3 s.f.

Common pitfalls.

  • Reporting the joint probability $0.0606$ as the answer (forgetting the denominator).
  • Double-counting: treating "throw 2 succeeds" and "throw 2 $>44$" as two separate conditions to multiply --- they're the same event since $X > 44 \Rightarrow X > 38.8$.
  • Including throw-3 considerations --- irrelevant, since qualification at throw 2 stops the sequence.
