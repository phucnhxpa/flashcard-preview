Q8 — Normal Distribution, Finding Parameters

Question

The random variable $X$ is normally distributed with mean $\mu$ and variance 36. Given that
$$P(\mu - 2k < X < \mu + 2k) = 0.6$$
(a) find the value of $k$. (4)

The random variable $Y$ is normally distributed with mean $\mu$ and standard deviation $\sigma$. Given that
$$2\mu = 3\sigma^2 \quad \text{and} \quad P\left(Y > \tfrac{3}{2}\mu\right) = 0.0668$$
(b) find the value of $\mu$ and the value of $\sigma$. (5)

[9 marks]

Part (a)[4 marks] · 4 steps

STEP 1/4

Variance 36 $\Rightarrow$ $\sigma_X = 6$.

Symmetric interval about $\mu$:

STEP 2/4

$$P(\mu - 2k < X < \mu + 2k) = P\left(-\frac{2k}{6} < Z < \frac{2k}{6}\right) = 0.6$$

By symmetry of the standard normal:

STEP 3/4

$$2\Phi\!\left(\frac{2k}{6}\right) - 1 = 0.6 \Rightarrow \Phi\!\left(\frac{2k}{6}\right) = 0.8$$

From inverse normal tables, $\Phi^{-1}(0.8) = 0.8416$:

STEP 4/4

$$\frac{2k}{6} = 0.8416 \Rightarrow 2k = 6 \times 0.8416 = 5.0496$$

$$\boxed{k = 2.52\ (3\ \text{s.f.})}$$

Target: $\boxed{k = 2.52\ (3\ \text{s.f.})}$

Part (b)[5 marks] · 5 steps

Carry-in: Part (a) result: $\boxed{k = 2.52\ (3\ \text{s.f.})}$
STEP 1/5

Standardise the second condition:

$$P\!\left(Y > \tfrac{3}{2}\mu\right) = P\!\left(Z > \frac{(3/2)\mu - \mu}{\sigma}\right) = P\!\left(Z > \frac{\mu}{2\sigma}\right) = 0.0668$$

STEP 2/5

So $\Phi(\mu / 2\sigma) = 1 - 0.0668 = 0.9332$. From tables, $\Phi(1.5) = 0.9332$ exactly:

$$\frac{\mu}{2\sigma} = 1.5 \Rightarrow \mu = 3\sigma \quad (\text{i})$$

STEP 3/5

Substitute into the given relation $2\mu = 3\sigma^2$:

$$2(3\sigma) = 3\sigma^2$$
$$6\sigma = 3\sigma^2$$
$$3\sigma^2 - 6\sigma = 0$$
$$3\sigma(\sigma - 2) = 0$$

STEP 4/5

So $\sigma = 0$ (rejected --- s.d. must be positive) or $\sigma = 2$:

$$\boxed{\sigma = 2,\qquad \mu = 3 \times 2 = 6}$$

Target: $\boxed{\sigma = 2,\qquad \mu = 3 \times 2 = 6}$
STEP 5/5

Key formula / concept.

  • Symmetric interval: $P(|Z| < z) = 2\Phi(z) - 1$.
  • Standardisation: $Z = (Y - \mu)/\sigma$.
  • $\Phi(1.5) = 0.9332$ --- a memorable table value.
  • Reject negative or zero standard-deviation roots.

Exam checklist.

  • [ ] In (a), convert two-tail probability 0.6 to one-tail $\Phi = 0.8$ before consulting tables.
  • [ ] Take care with the $2k$ factor --- it's $2k/\sigma$ inside the $Z$, NOT $k/\sigma$.
  • [ ] In (b), standardise first to get one equation in $\mu/\sigma$; use the algebraic relation as the second equation.
  • [ ] Reject non-positive $\sigma$ explicitly ("$\sigma > 0$, so $\sigma = 2$").
  • [ ] Substitute back to find both $\mu$ and $\sigma$ --- the question asks for both.

Common pitfalls.

  • Reading $\Phi^{-1}(0.6) = 0.2533$ directly into the equation instead of converting to one-tail.
  • Forgetting variance $\neq$ s.d. --- in (a), $\sigma_X = \sqrt{36} = 6$, not 36.
  • Keeping the $\sigma = 0$ root in the final answer.

See also Q5(a) --- same standardisation technique; Q2(a)(ii) --- relation between variance and s.d.

Key Formulas --- NOT in Formula Booklet (memorise)

Probability:
- $P(A \mid B) = \dfrac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)}$ --- the conditional formula.
- $P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cap B)$ --- addition rule.
- Independence: $P(A \cap B) = P(A) \cdot P(B)$.
- Mutually exclusive: $P(A \cap B) = 0$, so $P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B)$.
- Complement: $P(A') = 1 - P(A)$.
- At-least-one (independent): $1 - P(\text{fail})^n$.

Coding / variance algebra:
- $u = a + bx \Rightarrow \bar{u} = a + b\bar{x}$, $\sigma_u = |b|\sigma_x$.
- $\operatorname{Var}(aX + b) = a^2 \operatorname{Var}(X)$.
- $E(aX + b) = aE(X) + b$.
- $\operatorname{Var}(X) = E(X^2) - (E(X))^2$.
- $r$ invariant under positive linear coding on both variables.

Quartile positions (discrete $n$ values):
- $Q_1$ at $(n+1)/4$; median at $(n+1)/2$; $Q_3$ at $3(n+1)/4$.
- Non-integer position $\to$ interpolate between adjacent values.

Outlier rule:
- Upper fence $Q_3 + 1.5(Q_3 - Q_1)$; lower fence $Q_1 - 1.5(Q_3 - Q_1)$.

Median by interpolation (grouped data):
- $L + \dfrac{(n/2) - F_<}{f_{\text{class}}} \times w$.

Skew: mean $>$ median $\to$ positive; mean $<$ median $\to$ negative.

Memorable Normal table values:
- $\Phi(0.84) \approx 0.80$, $\Phi(1.00) = 0.8413$, $\Phi(1.28) \approx 0.90$.
- $\Phi(1.50) = 0.9332$, $\Phi(1.645) = 0.95$, $\Phi(1.96) = 0.975$, $\Phi(2.33) \approx 0.99$.

Key Formulas --- IN Booklet (know where to find them)

  • $S_{xx} = \sum x^2 - \dfrac{(\sum x)^2}{n}$, $S_{xy} = \sum xy - \dfrac{(\sum x)(\sum y)}{n}$.
  • $\sigma_x = \sqrt{S_{xx}/n}$ (population s.d., used throughout S1).
  • $r = \dfrac{S_{xy}}{\sqrt{S_{xx} S_{yy}}}$.
  • Regression $y = a + bx$: $b = S_{xy}/S_{xx}$, $a = \bar{y} - b\bar{x}$.
  • Discrete RV: $E(X) = \sum x P(X=x)$, $\operatorname{Var}(X) = E(X^2) - (E(X))^2$.
  • Normal $\Phi$ table and inverse-normal percentage points.

Exam Strategy

  1. First 10 minutes: scan the paper for "given that" triggers. Q3(d), Q5(b), Q6 all hinge on conditional probability. Mark them with a star; pre-write the formula in the margin.

  2. For every "given that" question, write $P(A \mid B) = P(A \cap B)/P(B)$ before computing. This single habit fixes Phuc's #1 weakness (cost 2 marks on Q3(d) in the live attempt).

  3. For "show that" questions, write at least 3 algebraic lines. Method marks dominate; final answer alone usually gets 1 of 3. Always quote one intermediate decimal before rounding (e.g. $8.5716 \to 8.57$).

  4. Cross-check tree-diagram probabilities by two routes. $P(R) = 1 - P(L)$ should equal the direct sum down the tree. Mismatch $\to$ catch the arithmetic slip before submitting.

  5. Memorise key Normal table values ($\Phi(1) = 0.8413$, $\Phi(1.5) = 0.9332$, $\Phi(1.645) = 0.95$, $\Phi(1.96) = 0.975$) to skip the lookup in roughly 30 s per Normal question --- saves ~90 s on a paper with 2--3 Normal sub-parts.

  6. For coding questions, recite the rule before applying: "Additive constant drops; multiplier applies (and squares for variance)." Avoids the "did I forget to subtract 32?" panic on Q2(c).

  7. For independence tests, compute BOTH sides numerically. $P(A) P(B) = 0.15$ AND $P(A \cap B) = 0.15$ --- equal, so independent. Don't just assert.

  8. Reject non-physical roots explicitly. "$\sigma > 0$, so $\sigma = 2$" on Q8(b). Negative probabilities, zero s.d., or $|r| > 1$ all warrant a one-line rejection note.

  9. For regression interpretation (1 mark), use units AND context. "Per additional mark in French, predicted Spanish mark increases by 1.04 marks" --- not "gradient is 1.04".

  10. For "more reliable" questions, use the words "interpolation" / "extrapolation" and quote the data range. Q4(e) wants $24 \le f \le 68$ explicitly stated.

  11. Time budget (90 min for 75 marks $\approx$ 1.2 min/mark): Q1 $\approx$ 10, Q2 $\approx$ 14, Q3 $\approx$ 10, Q4 $\approx$ 14, Q5 $\approx$ 10, Q6 $\approx$ 11, Q7 $\approx$ 12, Q8 $\approx$ 11. Total $\approx$ 92 --- tight. Save ~3 min by not flipping to the formula booklet.

  12. Keep fractions until the very end of variance computations. Q7(d): the 169 cancels cleanly only if you don't convert to decimals early.
