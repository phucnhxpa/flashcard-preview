Q6 — Set Probability, Simultaneous Equations, Independence

Question

The events $A$ and $B$ satisfy
$$P(A) = x,\qquad P(B) = y,\qquad P(A \cup B) = 0.65,\qquad P(B \mid A) = 0.3$$

(a) Show that $14x + 20y = 13$. (3)

The events $B$ and $C$ are mutually exclusive such that $P(B \cup C) = 0.85$ and $P(C) = \tfrac{1}{2}x + y$.

(b) (i) Find a second equation in $x$ and $y$.
(ii) Hence find the value of $x$ and the value of $y$. (4)

(c) Determine whether or not $A$ and $B$ are statistically independent. You must show your working clearly. (2)

[9 marks]

Part (a)[3 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2

From $P(B \mid A) = P(A \cap B)/P(A) = 0.3$:

$$P(A \cap B) = 0.3 \times P(A) = 0.3x$$

Addition rule:

STEP 2/2

$$P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cap B)$$
$$0.65 = x + y - 0.3x$$
$$0.65 = 0.7x + y$$

Multiply through by 20:

$$13 = 14x + 20y \quad ✓$$

Part (b)(i)2 steps

STEP 1/2

Since $B$ and $C$ are mutually exclusive, $P(B \cap C) = 0$:

$$P(B \cup C) = P(B) + P(C) = y + (\tfrac{1}{2}x + y) = \tfrac{1}{2}x + 2y$$

STEP 2/2

Given $P(B \cup C) = 0.85$:

$$\tfrac{1}{2}x + 2y = 0.85 \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad \boxed{x + 4y = 1.7}$$

Multiply by 5 to align denominators with eq (a): $5x + 20y = 8.5$.

Target: $\boxed{x + 4y = 1.7}$

Part (b)(ii)2 steps

Carry-in: Part (b) result: $\boxed{x + 4y = 1.7}$
STEP 1/2

Solve:

$$\begin{cases} 14x + 20y = 13 \\ 5x + 20y = 8.5 \end{cases}$$

Subtract:

STEP 2/2

$$9x = 4.5 \Rightarrow \boxed{x = 0.5}$$

Substitute into $x + 4y = 1.7$:

$$0.5 + 4y = 1.7 \Rightarrow 4y = 1.2 \Rightarrow \boxed{y = 0.3}$$

Target: $\boxed{y = 0.3}$

Part (c)[2 marks] · 4 steps

Carry-in: Part (b) result: $\boxed{y = 0.3}$
STEP 1/4

Independence test: compare $P(A) \cdot P(B)$ with $P(A \cap B)$.

STEP 2/4

$$P(A) \cdot P(B) = 0.5 \times 0.3 = 0.15$$
$$P(A \cap B) = 0.3x = 0.3 \times 0.5 = 0.15$$

STEP 3/4

Since $P(A \cap B) = P(A) \cdot P(B)$, $A$ and $B$ are statistically independent.

STEP 4/4

Key formula / concept.

  • $P(A \cap B) = P(B \mid A) \cdot P(A)$ (conditioning event in denominator $\to$ multiply through).
  • Addition rule: $P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cap B)$.
  • Mutually exclusive: $P(A \cap B) = 0$.
  • Independence: $P(A \cap B) = P(A) \cdot P(B)$.

Exam checklist.

  • [ ] In (a), clear the decimals by multiplying by 20 to match the required form.
  • [ ] In (b)(i), use the mutually-exclusive condition $P(B \cap C) = 0$ explicitly.
  • [ ] In (c), compute BOTH $P(A)P(B)$ AND $P(A \cap B)$ numerically, then state whether they match.
  • [ ] State the conclusion in words ("are independent" / "not independent").

Common pitfalls.

  • $P(A \cap B) = 0.3y$ instead of $0.3x$ (wrong conditioning direction).
  • Using $P(B \cup C) = P(B) + P(C) - P(B \cap C)$ and forgetting that $P(B \cap C) = 0$.
  • For independence, comparing $P(A \cup B)$ instead of $P(A \cap B)$.
