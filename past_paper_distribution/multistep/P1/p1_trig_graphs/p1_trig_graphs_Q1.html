Q1 — Trig graph: counting solutions of tan x = 1/x + 1

Question

Figure 4 shows a sketch of the curve y = tan x, −2π ≤ x < 2π. The line $l$ is an asymptote to y = tan x.
(a) State an equation for $l$. (1)
(b)(i) On Diagram 1, sketch y = 1/x + 1, −2π ≤ x < 2π.
(b)(ii) Hence, giving a reason, state the number of solutions of tan x = 1/x + 1 in −2π ≤ x < 2π. (4)
(c) State the number of solutions of tan x = 1/x + 1 in the region (i) 0 ≤ x ≤ 40π (2), (ii) −10π ≤ x ≤ 5π/2 (2).

[1 marks]

Part (a)[1 mark] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2

Equation of the asymptote $l$. (1)

Step 1: Recall where tan x blows up. The vertical asymptotes of y = tan x occur where cos x = 0, i.e. at $x = (2n+1)\frac{\pi}{2}$ — that is $x = \pm\frac{\pi}{2}, \pm\frac{3\pi}{2}, \dots$

STEP 2/2

Step 2: Pick the one drawn as $l$. The line highlighted in Figure 4 (rightmost) is $x = \dfrac{3\pi}{2}$.

Result: $l: x = \dfrac{3\pi}{2}$.

Part (b)2 steps

STEP 1/2

Sketch y = 1/x + 1 and count intersections on −2π ≤ x < 2π. (4)

Step 1: Sketch y = 1/x + 1. It is y = 1/x translated UP by 1. Vertical asymptote $x = 0$; horizontal asymptote $y = 1$ (NOT y = 0 — the +1 lifts the whole curve and its asymptote up by 1). The x > 0 branch sits just above y = 1; the x < 0 branch comes up from below.

STEP 2/2

Step 2: Overlay and count crossings. Each tan branch sweeps from −∞ to +∞, so the near-horizontal curve cuts each branch. Counting on −2π ≤ x < 2π gives 4 intersection points.

Step 3: Write the reason in mark-winning words. "There are 4 solutions because the graph of y = 1/x + 1 intersects the graph of y = tan x 4 times in the interval given." (Name both curves and the interval.)

Result: 4 solutions.

Part (c)(i)2 steps

STEP 1/2

Number of solutions on 0 ≤ x ≤ 40π. (2)

Step 1: Approximate the curve. For large x, 1/x + 1 ≈ 1 — a near-horizontal line just above y = 1.

STEP 2/2

Step 2: One crossing per π-branch. tan x has one branch every π; the line y ≈ 1 cuts each branch exactly once (IVT). Width 40π ⟹ 40 branches.

Result: 40 solutions.

Part (c)(ii)3 steps

STEP 1/3

Number of solutions on −10π ≤ x ≤ 5π/2. (2)

Step 1: Sketch carefully and split the range. Use Harry's grouping 10 + 1 + 3 = 14.

Step 2: The 10 ordinary branches. From −10π up to about −π/2: ten ordinary tan branches, each cut once by the near-horizontal part of 1/x + 1.

STEP 2/3

Step 3: The "odd one out below the axis". On (−π/2, 0), near the pole x = 0 the curve 1/x + 1 dives to −∞ and meets the rising tan branch BELOW the x-axis — 1 extra crossing. (This is the one easily missed.)

Step 4: The last 3. From 0 to 5π/2: the crossing on (0, π/2) plus the two ordinary branches centred at π and 2π.

Result: 10 + 1 + 3 = 14 solutions.

STEP 3/3

Common trap (this part).

Answered 12, missing the "odd one out below the axis" on (−π/2, 0). The crossing happens where 1/x + 1 plunges to −∞ at its pole and meets tan below the x-axis.

Exam checklist.

Draw a really good sketch; mark asymptotes as dashed lines; look BELOW the x-axis near each pole before counting; state the reason naming both curves and the interval.

Common pitfalls.

Putting the horizontal asymptote of 1/x + 1 at y = 0 instead of y = 1; forgetting the sub-branch that crosses below the axis next to x = 0.
