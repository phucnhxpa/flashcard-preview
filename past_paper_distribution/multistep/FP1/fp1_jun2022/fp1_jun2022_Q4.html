Q4 — Quartic with complex root

Question

$f(z) = 2z^4 - 19z^3 + Az^2 + Bz - 156$
where $A$ and $B$ are constants.
The complex number $5 - i$ is a root of the equation $f(z) = 0$
(a) Write down another complex root of this equation. (1)
(b) Solve the equation $f(z) = 0$ completely. (5)
(c) Determine the value of $A$ and the value of $B$. (2)

[8 marks]

Part (a)[1 mark] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2

By the Complex Conjugate Root Theorem (real coefficients ⟹ complex roots in conjugate pairs):

STEP 2/2

$$z = 5 + i$$

Part (b)4 steps

STEP 1/4

Form the quadratic factor from the conjugate pair using

$$z^2 - 2\operatorname{Re}(z_1)\,z + |z_1|^2$$

where $z_1 = 5 - i$, $\operatorname{Re}(z_1) = 5$, $|z_1|^2 = 25 + 1 = 26$:

$$(z - (5-i))(z - (5+i)) = z^2 - 10z + 26$$

STEP 2/4

From the Loom (Mr Fuat): "this is a specific kind of multiplication. It can be written as $z^2 - 2 \times \operatorname{Re}(z_1)\,z$ plus modulus square of $z_1$\ldots this is going to be $z^2 - 10z + 26$."

Long division of $f(z)$ by $z^2 - 10z + 26$:

Step Action Result
1 $2z^4 \div z^2 = 2z^2$ bring down: $2z^2(z^2-10z+26) = 2z^4 - 20z^3 + 52z^2$
2 Subtract $z^3 + (A-52)z^2 + Bz - 156$
3 $z^3 \div z^2 = z$ $z(z^2-10z+26) = z^3 - 10z^2 + 26z$
4 Subtract $(A-42)z^2 + (B-26)z - 156$
5 $(A-42)z^2 \div z^2 = (A-42)$ $(A-42)(z^2-10z+26) = (A-42)z^2 - 10(A-42)z + 26(A-42)$
6 Subtract --- remainder $[10A + B - 446]z + [-26A + 936]$

For $z^2 - 10z + 26$ to be a factor, remainder $\equiv 0$:

STEP 3/4

$$-26A + 936 = 0 \;\Longrightarrow\; A = 36$$
$$10(36) + B - 446 = 0 \;\Longrightarrow\; B = 86$$

Quotient is $2z^2 + z + (A - 42) = 2z^2 + z - 6$.

Solve $2z^2 + z - 6 = 0$:

$$2z^2 + 4z - 3z - 6 = 0 \;\Longrightarrow\; 2z(z+2) - 3(z+2) = 0$$
$$(2z - 3)(z + 2) = 0$$
$$z = \tfrac{3}{2} \text{ or } z = -2$$

STEP 4/4

$$\boxed{z = 5 - i,\;\; 5 + i,\;\; \tfrac{3}{2},\;\; -2}$$

Target: $\boxed{z = 5 - i,\;\; 5 + i,\;\; \tfrac{3}{2},\;\; -2}$

Part (c)[2 marks] · 2 steps

Carry-in: Part (b) result: $\boxed{z = 5 - i,\;\; 5 + i,\;\; \tfrac{3}{2},\;\; -2}$
STEP 1/2

From the long division: $A = 36$, $B = 86$.

STEP 2/2

What went wrong (Phuc, this part).

Phuc tagged b(ii) and (c) for redo. The Loom shows the confusion came after the long division setup: "Okay, because the A and B are constants, so how do we solve it?" He didn't immediately see that the remainder must be identically zero, which converts the unknowns into a 2-equation linear system. The fix is to internalise: "factor ⟹ zero remainder ⟹ each coefficient of remainder = 0".

From the Loom (Mr Fuat): "In that case, what this means is that the remainder must be zero, yes? That's why we are looking at."

Alternative method --- Vieta (often faster than long division):

Sum of roots: $z_1 + z_2 + z_3 + z_4 = -\dfrac{-19}{2} = \dfrac{19}{2}$. So $z_3 + z_4 = \dfrac{19}{2} - 10 = -\dfrac{1}{2}$.

Product: $z_1 z_2 z_3 z_4 = \dfrac{-156}{2} = -78$. Since $z_1 z_2 = 26$, we get $z_3 z_4 = -3$.

Form $z^2 - (\text{sum})z + (\text{product}) = 0$: $z^2 + \tfrac{1}{2}z - 3 = 0$, i.e. $2z^2 + z - 6 = 0$.

Key formula / concept.

  • Conjugate pair → quadratic factor: $z^2 - 2\operatorname{Re}(z_1)z + |z_1|^2$
  • Vieta for quartic $az^4 + bz^3 + \dots + e$: $\sum z_i = -b/a$, $\prod z_i = e/a$
  • "Factor ⟹ remainder $\equiv 0$" converts unknown coefficients into linear equations

Exam checklist.

  • [ ] Write the conjugate immediately for (a) --- 1 mark, no working
  • [ ] Build the real quadratic factor before attempting any division
  • [ ] Lay out long division neatly with placeholder columns for $A$ and $B$
  • [ ] Set both remainder coefficients to zero, solve as simultaneous equations
  • [ ] Solve the quotient quadratic and list all four roots

Common pitfalls.

  • Quoting "5 + i" but then expanding $(z-5-i)(z-5+i)$ incorrectly and getting $z^2 - 10z + 24$
  • Forgetting to set both remainder coefficients to zero (only handling the $z$-coefficient)
  • Arithmetic slips in $(A - 42)(z^2 - 10z + 26)$ --- track each term
  • Stopping after finding $A$, $B$ and forgetting to solve $2z^2 + z - 6 = 0$ for the remaining two roots

See also Q1 (conjugate-pair signage), Q5 (sum/product of roots to build a new quadratic).
