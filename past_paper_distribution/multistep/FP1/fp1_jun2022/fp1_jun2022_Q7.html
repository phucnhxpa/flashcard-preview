Q7 — Rotation matrix, composition, area scale factor

Question

$A = \begin{pmatrix} \tfrac{1}{2} & \tfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \\ -\tfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2} & \tfrac{1}{2} \end{pmatrix}$
(a) Determine the matrix $A^2$ (1)
(b) Describe fully the single geometrical transformation represented by the matrix $A^2$ (2)
(c) Hence determine the smallest positive integer value of $n$ for which $A^n = I$ (1)
The matrix $B$ represents a stretch scale factor 4 parallel to the x-axis.
(d) Write down the matrix $B$ (1)
The transformation represented by matrix $A$ followed by the transformation represented by matrix $B$ is represented by the matrix $C$
(e) Determine the matrix $C$ (2)
The parallelogram $P$ is transformed onto the parallelogram $P'$ by the matrix $C$
(f) Given that the area of parallelogram $P'$ is 20 square units, determine the area of parallelogram $P$ (2)

[9 marks]

Setup2 steps

STEP 1/2

Identify the transformation. Compare with the rotation form $\begin{pmatrix} \cos\theta & -\sin\theta \\ \sin\theta & \cos\theta \end{pmatrix}$:

STEP 2/2

$\cos\theta = \tfrac{1}{2}$, $-\sin\theta = \tfrac{\sqrt 3}{2}$ ⟹ $\sin\theta = -\tfrac{\sqrt 3}{2}$. Both signs (cos positive, sin negative) place $\theta$ in the fourth quadrant: $\theta = -60$^\circ$$ (clockwise $60$^\circ$$ about the origin), equivalently $300$^\circ$$ anticlockwise.

Part (a)[1 mark] · 3 steps

STEP 1/3

$A^2 = $ rotation by $-120$^\circ$$ (or $240$^\circ$$ anticlockwise). Compute directly:

STEP 2/3

$$A^2 = \begin{pmatrix} \tfrac{1}{2} & \tfrac{\sqrt 3}{2} \\ -\tfrac{\sqrt 3}{2} & \tfrac{1}{2} \end{pmatrix}^2 = \begin{pmatrix} -\tfrac{1}{2} & \tfrac{\sqrt 3}{2} \\ -\tfrac{\sqrt 3}{2} & -\tfrac{1}{2}\end{pmatrix}$$

STEP 3/3

(Spot-check: $[0,0] = \tfrac{1}{4} + \tfrac{\sqrt 3}{2}\cdot(-\tfrac{\sqrt 3}{2}) = \tfrac{1}{4} - \tfrac{3}{4} = -\tfrac{1}{2}$ $✓$.)

Part (b)[2 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2

$A^2$ represents a **rotation through $120$^\circ$$ clockwise about the origin** (equivalently $240$^\circ$$ anticlockwise).

STEP 2/2

Phuc's read (from Loom): "In order to find the angle that this matrix rotates, we can take the inverse cosine of the first term, one half, and after that we get 60 degrees. When you look at this side value and you see that the sine is negative, that means you pick the cosine positive and sine negative."

Part (c)[1 mark] · 1 step

STEP 1/1

Need total rotation a multiple of $360$^\circ$$. Each $A$ rotates by $60$^\circ$$ clockwise. Smallest $n$ with $60n \equiv 0 \pmod{360}$ is $n = 6$.

Part (d)[1 mark] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2

Stretch scale factor 4 parallel to $x$-axis:

STEP 2/2

$$B = \begin{pmatrix} 4 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix}$$

Part (e)[2 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2

"A followed by B" means apply $A$ first, then $B$, so $C = BA$:

STEP 2/2

$$C = \begin{pmatrix} 4 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix} \tfrac{1}{2} & \tfrac{\sqrt 3}{2} \\ -\tfrac{\sqrt 3}{2} & \tfrac{1}{2}\end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 2\sqrt 3 \\ -\tfrac{\sqrt 3}{2} & \tfrac{1}{2}\end{pmatrix}$$

Part (f)[2 marks] · 3 steps

STEP 1/3

Area scale factor $= |\det C|$.

$$\det C = 2\cdot\tfrac{1}{2} - 2\sqrt{3}\cdot\left(-\tfrac{\sqrt 3}{2}\right) = 1 + 3 = 4$$

STEP 2/3

$$\text{Area}(P') = |\det C|\cdot\text{Area}(P) \;\Longrightarrow\; 20 = 4 \cdot \text{Area}(P)$$

$$\boxed{\text{Area}(P) = 5 \text{ square units}}$$

Target: $\boxed{\text{Area}(P) = 5 \text{ square units}}$
STEP 3/3

What went wrong (Phuc, this part).

Phuc tagged part (b) specifically. The redo is about quadrant logic: $\cos^{-1}(1/2)$ on a calculator returns only the principal value $60$^\circ$$, but the matrix entries fix both $\cos$ and $\sin$ --- so the angle is determined uniquely by which signs the two off-diagonal entries carry. Don't trust calculator output alone; cross-check the sine sign.

From the Loom (Mr Fuat): "When we look at it here, cosine is plus, and then sine is minus. So in which quadrant is cosine plus and sine minus? Fourth quadrant, that's why."
Mr Fuat also reminded: "Calculator will only return within the principal range\ldots Always take θ with respect to the x-axis."

Key formula / concept.

  • Rotation by $\theta$ anticlockwise: $\begin{pmatrix} \cos\theta & -\sin\theta \\ \sin\theta & \cos\theta\end{pmatrix}$ (NOT in booklet)
  • Stretch s.f. $k$ parallel to $x$-axis: $\begin{pmatrix} k & 0 \\ 0 & 1\end{pmatrix}$
  • Composition "A then B" = $BA$ (right-acting on column vector)
  • Area scale factor $= |\det|$; going backwards (from $P'$ to $P$) divides by it

Exam checklist.

  • [ ] Quote the rotation/scaling/stretch form before reading off $\theta$ or $k$
  • [ ] State direction (clockwise/anticlockwise) AND the rotation point (origin)
  • [ ] For composition, write the order $BA$ explicitly
  • [ ] When given "$P'$ has area $\square$, find area of $P$", divide by $|\det|$ (don't multiply)
  • [ ] Use matrix mode on the calculator to spot-check $A^2$, $A^6$ (it's just button-pressing)

Common pitfalls.

  • Stating $\theta = 60$^\circ$$ without the direction
  • Computing $C = AB$ instead of $BA$
  • Confusing "stretch parallel to $x$" with "stretch parallel to $y$"
  • Multiplying by $|\det C| = 4$ to get area $80$ when the question goes from $P'$ back to $P$ --- common reversal slip

See also Q3 (matrix algebra and the reversal $(MN)^{-1} = N^{-1}M^{-1}$).
