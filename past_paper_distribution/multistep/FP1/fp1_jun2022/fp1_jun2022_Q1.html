Q1 — Complex numbers, modulus, Argand diagram

Question

$z_1 = 3 + 3i \quad z_2 = p + qi \quad p,q \in \mathbb{R}$
Given that $|z_1 z_2| = 15\sqrt{2}$
(a) determine $|z_2|$ (2)
Given also that $p = -4$
(b) determine the possible values of $q$ (2)
(c) Show $z_1$ and the possible positions for $z_2$ on the same Argand diagram. (2)

[6 marks]

Part (a)[2 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2

Use the multiplicative property of moduli: $|z_1 z_2| = |z_1|\,|z_2|$.

$$|z_1| = \sqrt{3^2 + 3^2} = \sqrt{18} = 3\sqrt{2}$$

STEP 2/2

$$|z_1|\,|z_2| = 15\sqrt{2} \;\Longrightarrow\; 3\sqrt{2}\,|z_2| = 15\sqrt{2}$$

$$|z_2| = \frac{15\sqrt{2}}{3\sqrt{2}} = 5$$

Part (b)[2 marks] · 3 steps

STEP 1/3

With $p = -4$:

STEP 2/3

$$|z_2|^2 = p^2 + q^2 = 25$$
$$(-4)^2 + q^2 = 25$$
$$q^2 = 25 - 16 = 9$$
$$q = \pm 3$$

STEP 3/3

So $z_2 = -4 + 3i$ or $z_2 = -4 - 3i$.

STEP 1/4

Argand diagram (sketch description):

STEP 2/4
Point Coordinates
$z_1$ $(3, 3)$ --- first quadrant
$z_2^{(1)}$ $(-4, 3)$ --- second quadrant
$z_2^{(2)}$ $(-4, -3)$ --- third quadrant

What went wrong (Phuc, this part).

The slack thread tagged this #redo-this-question (the image shows the attempt). The most common slip on this style of question is forgetting that $|z_1|\,|z_2| = |z_1 z_2|$ and instead computing $z_1 z_2$ algebraically, then taking the modulus --- algebraically valid but slow and error-prone. For (b), a frequent error is solving $|p+qi| = 5$ but then forgetting both signs of the square root and writing only $q = 3$.

Key formula / concept.

  • $|z_1 z_2| = |z_1| \cdot |z_2|$ (multiplicative property --- NOT in formula booklet)
  • $|a + bi| = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2}$
  • $q^2 = 9 \Rightarrow q = \pm 3$ (always two square roots unless context excludes one)

Exam checklist.

  • [ ] Compute $|z_1|$ first, separately, and check arithmetic ($\sqrt{18} = 3\sqrt{2}$, not $\sqrt{18}$ left unsimplified)
  • [ ] State $|z_1|\,|z_2| = |z_1 z_2|$ explicitly --- examiners credit the rule citation
  • [ ] In (b), give both signs of $q$
  • [ ] On Argand diagram: scale axes, label points $z_1, z_2$ (with values), use crosses
  • [ ] Check $|z_2| = \sqrt{16+9} = 5$ matches (a)

Common pitfalls.

  • Writing $|z_1| = \sqrt{18}$ without simplifying to $3\sqrt{2}$ --- costs accuracy mark when dividing
  • Forgetting the negative root in $q^2 = 9$
  • Mis-plotting $(-4, -3)$ in the fourth quadrant instead of the third
  • Drawing only the positive-$q$ position on the diagram

See also Q4 (complex conjugate roots: both signs of imaginary part).
