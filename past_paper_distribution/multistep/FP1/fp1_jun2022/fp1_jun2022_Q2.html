Q2 — Newton-Raphson and linear interpolation

Question

$f(x) = 10 - 2x - \dfrac{1}{2\sqrt{x}} - \dfrac{1}{x^3} \quad x>0$
(a) Show that the equation $f(x) = 0$ has a root $\alpha$ in the interval $[0.4, 0.5]$ (2)
(b) Determine $f'(x)$. (3)
(c) Using $x_0 = 0.5$ as a first approximation to $\alpha$, apply the Newton-Raphson procedure once to $f(x)$ to find a second approximation to $\alpha$, giving your answer to 3 decimal places. (2)
The equation $f(x) = 0$ has another root $\beta$ in the interval $[4.8, 4.9]$
(d) Use linear interpolation once on the interval $[4.8, 4.9]$ to find an approximation to $\beta$, giving your answer to 3 decimal places. (2)

[9 marks]

Part (a)3 steps

STEP 1/3

Rewrite as $f(x) = 10 - 2x - \tfrac{1}{2}x^{-1/2} - x^{-3}$.

STEP 2/3

$$f(0.4) = 10 - 0.8 - \tfrac{1}{2}(0.4)^{-1/2} - (0.4)^{-3}$$
$$= 9.2 - 0.7906... - 15.625 \approx -7.216 \;\; (<0)$$
$$f(0.5) = 10 - 1 - \tfrac{1}{2}(0.5)^{-1/2} - (0.5)^{-3}$$
$$= 9 - 0.7071... - 8 \approx 0.293 \;\; (>0)$$

STEP 3/3

Sign change with $f$ continuous on $[0.4, 0.5]$ ⟹ root $\alpha \in [0.4, 0.5]$.

Part (b)3 steps

STEP 1/3

Differentiate term-by-term using the power rule:

STEP 2/3

$$f(x) = 10 - 2x - \tfrac{1}{2}x^{-1/2} - x^{-3}$$
$$f'(x) = -2 - \tfrac{1}{2}\cdot(-\tfrac{1}{2})x^{-3/2} - (-3)x^{-4}$$
$$f'(x) = -2 + \tfrac{1}{4}x^{-3/2} + 3x^{-4}$$

STEP 3/3

i.e. $f'(x) = -2 + \dfrac{1}{4x^{3/2}} + \dfrac{3}{x^4}$.

Part (c)2 steps

STEP 1/2

Phuc's approach (from the Loom):

"After we get the derivative of the first derivative of the f-function, we can use the Newton-Raphson formula. The $x_n$ in this case is $x_0$. We substitute $x_0$ into the f-function and into the derivative function. After that, we get $x_1$."

This procedural recall is correct --- the redo was about executing the numerics cleanly to 3 d.p. Apply

$$x_1 = x_0 - \frac{f(x_0)}{f'(x_0)}$$

STEP 2/2

$$f(0.5) \approx 0.29289$$
$$f'(0.5) = -2 + \tfrac{1}{4}(0.5)^{-3/2} + 3(0.5)^{-4}$$
$$(0.5)^{-3/2} = 2\sqrt{2} \approx 2.8284,\quad (0.5)^{-4} = 16$$
$$f'(0.5) \approx -2 + 0.7071 + 48 = 46.7071$$

$$x_1 = 0.5 - \frac{0.29289}{46.7071} \approx 0.5 - 0.00627 = 0.49373$$

$$\boxed{x_1 \approx 0.494\;\;(3\text{ d.p.})}$$

Target: $\boxed{x_1 \approx 0.494\;\;(3\text{ d.p.})}$

Part (d)[2 marks] · 3 steps

Carry-in: Part (c) result: $\boxed{x_1 \approx 0.494\;\;(3\text{ d.p.})}$
STEP 1/3

Linear interpolation: place the root proportionally along $[4.8, 4.9]$.

$$f(4.8) = 10 - 9.6 - \tfrac{1}{2}(4.8)^{-1/2} - (4.8)^{-3} \approx 0.4 - 0.2282 - 0.00904 \approx 0.16275$$
$$f(4.9) = 10 - 9.8 - \tfrac{1}{2}(4.9)^{-1/2} - (4.9)^{-3} \approx 0.2 - 0.2259 - 0.00850 \approx -0.03439$$

STEP 2/3

Using similar triangles:

$$\beta \approx 4.8 + \frac{|f(4.8)|}{|f(4.8)| + |f(4.9)|} \times 0.1$$
$$= 4.8 + \frac{0.16275}{0.16275 + 0.03439}\times 0.1$$
$$= 4.8 + \frac{0.16275}{0.19714}\times 0.1 \approx 4.8 + 0.0826$$

$$\boxed{\beta \approx 4.883\;\;(3\text{ d.p.})}$$

Target: $\boxed{\beta \approx 4.883\;\;(3\text{ d.p.})}$
STEP 3/3

Common trap (this part).

Phuc tagged part (c) for redo even though he was "quite confident" overall. The procedural words were right --- the rebuild is about the numerical chain: keep at least 5 d.p. of intermediate values, then round at the end. Rounding $f(0.5)$ to 0.29 or $f'(0.5)$ to 47 early can flip the 3rd decimal place of the answer.

Key formula / concept.

  • Newton-Raphson: $x_{n+1} = x_n - \dfrac{f(x_n)}{f'(x_n)}$ (IN booklet)
  • Power rule: $\dfrac{d}{dx}\,x^n = nx^{n-1}$ (applies to negative and fractional $n$)
  • Linear interpolation: $\beta \approx a + \dfrac{|f(a)|}{|f(a)| + |f(b)|}(b-a)$ when $f(a)$ and $f(b)$ have opposite signs

Exam checklist.

  • [ ] State continuity and sign change explicitly in part (a)
  • [ ] In (b), rewrite roots/reciprocals as powers first, then differentiate --- show the chain
  • [ ] Keep 5 d.p. minimum in N-R intermediate steps; round only at the end
  • [ ] In (d), use absolute values of $f$ in the proportion (signs cancel in the formula)
  • [ ] Verify that the answer lies inside the bracketing interval

Common pitfalls.

  • Differentiating $\tfrac{1}{2}x^{-1/2}$ and forgetting the $\tfrac{1}{2}$ multiplier in front
  • Sign error on $-\tfrac{1}{2}\cdot(-\tfrac{1}{2}) = +\tfrac{1}{4}$
  • Using $f(x_0) \approx 0.3$ rather than $0.29289$ → answer becomes 0.493 instead of 0.494
  • Confusing N-R with linear interpolation --- they share the input $f(x_0)$ but the denominator differs
