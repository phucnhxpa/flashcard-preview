Q4 — Complex Polynomial Roots

Question

$f(z) = 2z^4 - 19z^3 + Az^2 + Bz - 156$, where $A$ and $B$ are constants. The complex number $5 - \mathrm i$ is a root of the equation $f(z) = 0$.
(a) Write down another complex root of this equation. --- (1)
(b) Solve the equation $f(z) = 0$ completely. --- (4)
(c) Determine the value of $A$ and the value of $B$. --- (3)

(a) Coefficients of $f$ are real, so the complex conjugate is also a root:

$$\boxed{z = 5 + \mathrm i}$$

STEP 2/4Part (b)

Strategy. Reveal this box only after you have chosen the method for Part (b). The goal is to reproduce the exam-safe working, not just the final answer.

(b) Build the real quadratic factor from the conjugate pair:
$$(z - (5-\mathrm i))(z - (5+\mathrm i)) = (z-5)^2 - \mathrm i^2 = z^2 - 10z + 25 + 1 = z^2 - 10z + 26.$$

Write $f(z) = (z^2 - 10z + 26)(2z^2 + cz + d)$. Compare coefficients (let $Q = 2z^2 + cz + d$):

Expand:
$$(z^2 - 10z + 26)(2z^2 + cz + d) = 2z^4 + cz^3 + dz^2 - 20z^3 - 10cz^2 - 10dz + 52z^2 + 26cz + 26d.$$

Collect:
- $z^4$: $2$ ✓
- $z^3$: $c - 20 = -19 \Rightarrow c = 1$
- $z^2$: $d - 10c + 52 = A$
- $z^1$: $-10d + 26c = B$
- $z^0$: $26d = -156 \Rightarrow d = -6$

So the second factor is $2z^2 + z - 6$. Factorise:
$$2z^2 + z - 6 = 2z^2 + 4z - 3z - 6 = 2z(z+2) - 3(z+2) = (2z-3)(z+2).$$

Roots: $z = \tfrac32$, $z = -2$.

All four roots: $z = 5 - \mathrm i,\ 5 + \mathrm i,\ \tfrac{3}{2},\ -2.$

STEP 3/4Part (c)

Strategy. Reveal this box only after you have chosen the method for Part (c). The goal is to reproduce the exam-safe working, not just the final answer.

(c) With $c = 1$, $d = -6$:
$$A = d - 10c + 52 = -6 - 10 + 52 = 36.$$
$$B = -10d + 26c = 60 + 26 = 86.$$

$$\boxed{A = 36,\quad B = 86}$$

Target: $\boxed{A = 36,\quad B = 86}$

Formula anchor.

Key formula / concept
Conjugate root theorem: if $f$ has real coefficients and $\alpha\in\mathbb C\setminus\mathbb R$ is a root, $\bar\alpha$ is too. The quadratic factor is
$$z^2 - 2\,\mathrm{Re}(\alpha)\,z + |\alpha|^2.$$

Exam checklist before moving on.

Exam checklist
- Note that coefficients are real (this is what licenses the conjugate).
- Compute $|5-\mathrm i|^2 = 25 + 1 = 26$ --- easy slip is to write $25-1$.
- Compare constant and leading coefficients first (cheapest equations).
- Factorise the second quadratic --- never leave it implicit.
- Verify $A,B$ by substituting $z = -2$: $2(16) - 19(-8) + 36(4) + 86(-2) - 156 = 32 + 152 + 144 - 172 - 156 = 0$ ✓.

Common traps to actively avoid.

Common pitfalls
- Computing $f(5-\mathrm i) = 0$ directly --- works but very long.
- Forgetting the factor of $2$ at the front (leading coefficient is 2, not 1) --- gives wrong $c,d$.
- Sign error in $|\alpha|^2 = 25 - (-1)^2 = 24$ (wrong: $|5-\mathrm i|^2 = 25 + 1$).
