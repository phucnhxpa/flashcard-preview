Q3 — Matrix Inverse, $(MN)^{-1}$

Question

$M = \begin{pmatrix} k & k \\ 3 & 5 \end{pmatrix}$, where $k$ is a non-zero constant.
(a) Determine $M^{-1}$, giving your answer in simplest form in terms of $k$. --- (2)
Hence, given that $N^{-1} = \begin{pmatrix} k & k \\ 4 & -1 \end{pmatrix}$,
(b) determine $(MN)^{-1}$, giving your answer in simplest form in terms of $k$. --- (2)

(a) $\det M = 5k - 3k = 2k$ (non-zero since $k\ne 0$).

$$M^{-1} = \frac{1}{2k}\begin{pmatrix} 5 & -k \\ -3 & k \end{pmatrix}.$$

STEP 2/3Part (b)

Strategy. Reveal this box only after you have chosen the method for Part (b). The goal is to reproduce the exam-safe working, not just the final answer.

(b) $(MN)^{-1} = N^{-1}M^{-1}$:

$$(MN)^{-1} = \begin{pmatrix} k & k \\ 4 & -1 \end{pmatrix}\cdot\frac{1}{2k}\begin{pmatrix} 5 & -k \\ -3 & k \end{pmatrix}.$$

Compute the product:
- $(1,1)$: $k(5) + k(-3) = 5k - 3k = 2k$
- $(1,2)$: $k(-k) + k(k) = -k^2 + k^2 = 0$
- $(2,1)$: $4(5) + (-1)(-3) = 20 + 3 = 23$
- $(2,2)$: $4(-k) + (-1)(k) = -4k - k = -5k$

$$(MN)^{-1} = \frac{1}{2k}\begin{pmatrix} 2k & 0 \\ 23 & -5k \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ \dfrac{23}{2k} & -\dfrac{5}{2} \end{pmatrix}.$$
