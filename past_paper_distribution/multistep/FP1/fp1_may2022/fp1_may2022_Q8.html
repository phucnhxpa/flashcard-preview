Q8 — Series, Sigma Standard Results

Question

(a) Use the standard results for $\sum_{r=1}^n r^2$ and $\sum_{r=1}^n r$ to show that for all positive integers $n$,
$$\sum_{r=0}^{n}(r+1)(r+2) = \tfrac{1}{3}(n+1)(n+2)(n+3).$$ --- (5)
(b) Hence determine the value of $10\times 11 + 11\times 12 + 12\times 13 + \dots + 100\times 101$. --- (3)

STEP 1/3Part (a)

Strategy. Reveal this box only after you have chosen the method for Part (a). The goal is to reproduce the exam-safe working, not just the final answer.

(a) Expand the summand:
$$(r+1)(r+2) = r^2 + 3r + 2.$$

The sum runs from $r=0$ to $r=n$, so there are $n+1$ terms.
$$\sum_{r=0}^{n}(r+1)(r+2) = \sum_{r=0}^{n}r^2 + 3\sum_{r=0}^{n}r + \sum_{r=0}^{n}2.$$

Note $\sum_{r=0}^{n}r^2 = \sum_{r=1}^{n}r^2$ (the $r=0$ term is zero), similarly for $\sum r$. And $\sum_{r=0}^{n}2 = 2(n+1)$.

Using booklet results:
$$\sum_{r=0}^{n}(r+1)(r+2) = \tfrac{1}{6}n(n+1)(2n+1) + 3\cdot\tfrac{1}{2}n(n+1) + 2(n+1).$$

Factor $(n+1)$:
$$= (n+1)\left[\tfrac{n(2n+1)}{6} + \tfrac{3n}{2} + 2\right].$$

Common denominator 6 inside the bracket:
$$= (n+1)\cdot\frac{n(2n+1) + 9n + 12}{6} = \frac{(n+1)\big(2n^2 + n + 9n + 12\big)}{6} = \frac{(n+1)(2n^2 + 10n + 12)}{6}.$$

Factor 2:
$$= \frac{2(n+1)(n^2 + 5n + 6)}{6} = \frac{(n+1)(n^2 + 5n + 6)}{3}.$$

Factorise $n^2 + 5n + 6 = (n+2)(n+3)$:

$$\boxed{\sum_{r=0}^{n}(r+1)(r+2) = \tfrac{1}{3}(n+1)(n+2)(n+3)}\ \ \blacksquare$$

STEP 2/3Part (b)

Strategy. Reveal this box only after you have chosen the method for Part (b). The goal is to reproduce the exam-safe working, not just the final answer.

(b) The terms $10\cdot 11, 11\cdot 12, \dots, 100\cdot 101$ are of the form $(r+1)(r+2)$ with $r+1 = 10,\dots,100$, i.e. $r = 9, 10, \dots, 99$.

$$S = \sum_{r=9}^{99}(r+1)(r+2) = \sum_{r=0}^{99}(r+1)(r+2) - \sum_{r=0}^{8}(r+1)(r+2).$$

Using (a):
$$\sum_{r=0}^{99} = \tfrac{1}{3}(100)(101)(102) = \tfrac{100 \cdot 101 \cdot 102}{3} = 100 \cdot 101 \cdot 34 = 343{,}400.$$

$$\sum_{r=0}^{8} = \tfrac{1}{3}(9)(10)(11) = \tfrac{990}{3} = 330.$$

$$\boxed{S = 343{,}400 - 330 = 343{,}070}$$

Target: $\boxed{S = 343{,}400 - 330 = 343{,}070}$
