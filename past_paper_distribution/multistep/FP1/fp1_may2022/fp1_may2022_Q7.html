Q7 — Matrix Transformations

Question

The matrix $A = \begin{pmatrix} \tfrac12 & -\tfrac{\sqrt 3}{2} \\ \tfrac{\sqrt 3}{2} & \tfrac12 \end{pmatrix}$.
(a) Determine the matrix $A^2$. --- (2)
(b) Describe fully the single geometrical transformation represented by the matrix $A^n$ (for the smallest positive integer $n$ such that $A^n = I$ context). --- (1)
(c) Hence determine the smallest positive integer value of $n$ for which $A^n = I$. --- (1)
(d) The matrix $B$ represents a stretch scale factor 4 parallel to the $x$-axis. Write down $B$. --- (1)
(e) The transformation represented by matrix $A$ followed by matrix $B$ is represented by matrix $C$. Determine $C$. --- (2)
(f) Given that the parallelogram $P$ of area 20 sq units is transformed onto $P'$ by $C$, determine the area of $P'$. --- (2)

STEP 1/8Setup

Rotation matrix template (anticlockwise by $\theta$):
$$R_\theta = \begin{pmatrix}\cos\theta & -\sin\theta \\ \sin\theta & \cos\theta\end{pmatrix}.$$

Comparing with $A$:
- $\cos\theta = \tfrac12$
- $\sin\theta = \tfrac{\sqrt 3}{2}$ (from bottom-left)
- Confirms $-\sin\theta = -\tfrac{\sqrt 3}{2}$ ✓ (top-right)

So $\theta = 60^\circ$ anticlockwise about $O$.

Mistake guard from the attempt/revision notes.

That means you have to look at the solution that has the cosine positive and the side negative, and you pick that one."

Phuc said "side negative" but in matrix $A$ the bottom-left entry is $+\sqrt 3/2$ (positive). His sentence describes the wrong sign-pattern. Also note Slack: "check how to do matrix multiplication using calculator" --- Casio MAT mode is essential here.

STEP 2/8Part (a)

Strategy. Reveal this box only after you have chosen the method for Part (a). The goal is to reproduce the exam-safe working, not just the final answer.

(a) $A^2$ is a rotation by $2\theta = 120^\circ$:
$$A^2 = \begin{pmatrix}\cos 120^\circ & -\sin 120^\circ \\ \sin 120^\circ & \cos 120^\circ\end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix}-\tfrac12 & -\tfrac{\sqrt 3}{2} \\ \tfrac{\sqrt 3}{2} & -\tfrac12\end{pmatrix}.$$

Check by direct multiplication:
- $(1,1)$: $\tfrac12\cdot\tfrac12 + (-\tfrac{\sqrt 3}{2})(\tfrac{\sqrt 3}{2}) = \tfrac14 - \tfrac34 = -\tfrac12$ ✓
- $(1,2)$: $\tfrac12(-\tfrac{\sqrt 3}{2}) + (-\tfrac{\sqrt 3}{2})\tfrac12 = -\tfrac{\sqrt 3}{2}$ ✓
- $(2,1)$: $\tfrac{\sqrt 3}{2}\cdot\tfrac12 + \tfrac12\cdot\tfrac{\sqrt 3}{2} = \tfrac{\sqrt 3}{2}$ ✓
- $(2,2)$: $\tfrac{\sqrt 3}{2}(-\tfrac{\sqrt 3}{2}) + \tfrac12\cdot\tfrac12 = -\tfrac34 + \tfrac14 = -\tfrac12$ ✓

STEP 6/8Part (e)

Strategy. Reveal this box only after you have chosen the method for Part (e). The goal is to reproduce the exam-safe working, not just the final answer.

(e) "$A$ followed by $B$" means apply $A$ first, then $B$: $C = BA$.
$$C = \begin{pmatrix}4&0\\0&1\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix}\tfrac12 & -\tfrac{\sqrt 3}{2} \\ \tfrac{\sqrt 3}{2} & \tfrac12\end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix}2 & -2\sqrt 3 \\ \tfrac{\sqrt 3}{2} & \tfrac12\end{pmatrix}.$$

STEP 7/8Part (f)

Strategy. Reveal this box only after you have chosen the method for Part (f). The goal is to reproduce the exam-safe working, not just the final answer.

(f) Area scale factor $= |\det C|$.

$\det A = \tfrac12\cdot\tfrac12 - (-\tfrac{\sqrt 3}{2})\cdot\tfrac{\sqrt 3}{2} = \tfrac14 + \tfrac34 = 1$ (rotations preserve area).
$\det B = 4$.
$\det C = \det B \cdot \det A = 4$.

Therefore $\text{Area}(P') = 4 \cdot 20 = 80$ sq units.
