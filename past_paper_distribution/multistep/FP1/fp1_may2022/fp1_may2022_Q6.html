Q6 — Parabola: Normal, Focus, Directrix, Triangle

Question

The parabola $C$ has equation $y^2 = 36x$. The point $P(9t^2, 18t)$, where $t\ne 0$, lies on $C$.
(a) Use calculus to show that the normal to $C$ at $P$ has equation $y + tx = 18t + 9t^3$. --- (4)
(b) Hence find the equations of the two normals to $C$ which pass through the point $(54, 0)$, giving your answers in the form $y = px + q$ where $p$ and $q$ are constants to be determined. --- (4)
(c) Given that the normals found in part (b) intersect the directrix of $C$ at the points $A$ and $B$, and the point $F$ is the focus of $C$, determine the area of triangle $AFB$. --- (3)

STEP 1/4Setup

Strategy. Reveal this box only after you have chosen the method for Setup. The goal is to reproduce the exam-safe working, not just the final answer.

(a) Implicit differentiation:
$$y^2 = 36x \Rightarrow 2y\frac{\mathrm dy}{\mathrm dx} = 36 \Rightarrow \frac{\mathrm dy}{\mathrm dx} = \frac{18}{y}.$$

At $P$: $\dfrac{\mathrm dy}{\mathrm dx} = \dfrac{18}{18t} = \dfrac{1}{t}$.

Gradient of normal $= -t$. Equation through $P(9t^2, 18t)$:
$$y - 18t = -t(x - 9t^2)$$
$$y - 18t = -tx + 9t^3$$
$$\boxed{y + tx = 18t + 9t^3}\ \blacksquare$$

Target: $\boxed{y + tx = 18t + 9t^3}$
STEP 2/4Part (b)

Strategy. Reveal this box only after you have chosen the method for Part (b). The goal is to reproduce the exam-safe working, not just the final answer.

(b) Substitute $(54, 0)$: $0 + 54t = 18t + 9t^3$:
$$9t^3 - 36t = 0 \Rightarrow 9t(t^2 - 4) = 0.$$

$t\ne 0$, so $t = \pm 2$.

  • $t=2$: $y + 2x = 36 + 72 = 108 \Rightarrow y = -2x + 108$.
  • $t=-2$: $y - 2x = -36 - 72 = -108 \Rightarrow y = 2x - 108$.

$$\boxed{y = -2x + 108\quad\text{and}\quad y = 2x - 108}$$

Target: $\boxed{y = -2x + 108\quad\text{and}\quad y = 2x - 108}$
STEP 3/4Part (c)

Strategy. Reveal this box only after you have chosen the method for Part (c). The goal is to reproduce the exam-safe working, not just the final answer.

(c) For $y^2 = 36x$: $4a = 36 \Rightarrow a = 9$. Focus $F = (9, 0)$; directrix $x = -9$.

Intersect first normal with $x = -9$: $y = -2(-9) + 108 = 126 \Rightarrow A = (-9, 126)$.
Intersect second normal: $y = 2(-9) - 108 = -126 \Rightarrow B = (-9, -126)$.

$AB$ is vertical along $x = -9$, length $|126 - (-126)| = 252$.
Perpendicular distance from $F(9,0)$ to the line $x = -9$ is $|9 - (-9)| = 18$.

$$\text{Area} = \tfrac12 \times 252 \times 18 = \boxed{2268\text{ sq units}}.$$

Target: $\boxed{2268\text{ sq units}}$
