Q2 — Numerical Methods

Question

$f(x) = 10 - 2x - \dfrac{1}{2\sqrt x} - \dfrac{1}{x^3},\ x > 0$
(a) Show that the equation $f(x)=0$ has a root $\alpha$ in $[0.4, 0.5]$. --- (2)
(b) Determine $f'(x)$. --- (3)
(c) Using $x_0 = 0.5$ as a first approximation to $\alpha$, apply the Newton--Raphson procedure once to $f(x)$ to find a second approximation to $\alpha$, giving your answer to 3 decimal places. --- (2)
The equation $f(x)=0$ has another root $\beta$ in $[4.8, 4.9]$.
(d) Use linear interpolation once on the interval $[4.8, 4.9]$ to find an approximation to $\beta$, giving your answer to 3 decimal places. --- (2)

STEP 1/3Setup

Strategy. Reveal this box only after you have chosen the method for Setup. The goal is to reproduce the exam-safe working, not just the final answer.

(a) Sign change.
$$f(0.4) = 10 - 0.8 - \frac{1}{2\sqrt{0.4}} - \frac{1}{0.4^3} = 9.2 - 0.7906 - 15.625 \approx -7.216 < 0.$$
$$f(0.5) = 10 - 1 - \frac{1}{2\sqrt{0.5}} - \frac{1}{0.5^3} = 9 - 0.7071 - 8 \approx 0.293 > 0.$$
$f$ is continuous on $[0.4, 0.5]$ with $f(0.4)<0<f(0.5)$, so by the Intermediate Value Theorem there exists $\alpha\in(0.4,0.5)$ with $f(\alpha)=0$. $\blacksquare$

Mistake guard from the attempt/revision notes.

What went wrong. From the voice note:

"after we cap the derivative of the first derivative of the f-function, we can use the Newton-Raphson formula. The $x_n$ in this case is $x_0$. We substitute $x_0$ into the f-function and into the derivative function. After that, we get $x_1$, and we use $x_1$ as the new $x_n$ again, and we apply it one more time."

The method description is correct, but Phuc said "apply it one more time" --- only one Newton--Raphson iteration is required here. The redo flag suggests an arithmetic slip on the double-negative terms in $f'(x)$ at $x_0 = 0.5$, where $(0.5)^{-3/2}$ and $(0.5)^{-4}$ produce large positive contributions that flip the sign of $f'$.

Phuc's incorrect approach (likely sign slip). Differentiating $-\tfrac12 x^{-1/2}$: getting $+\tfrac14 x^{-3/2}$ but then writing the sign wrong:

$$f'(x) \stackrel{\times}{=} -2 - \tfrac{1}{4}x^{-3/2} - 3x^{-4}\quad(\text{forgot the double negative}).$$

Then $f'(0.5) = -2 - 0.707 - 48 \approx -50.7$, and

$$x_1 = 0.5 - \frac{0.293}{-50.7} \approx 0.506,$$

which is the wrong direction.

STEP 2/3Part (b)

Strategy. Reveal this box only after you have chosen the method for Part (b). The goal is to reproduce the exam-safe working, not just the final answer.

(b) Rewrite using indices: $f(x) = 10 - 2x - \tfrac12 x^{-1/2} - x^{-3}$.
$$f'(x) = 0 - 2 - \tfrac12\cdot\left(-\tfrac12\right)x^{-3/2} - (-3)x^{-4}$$
$$\Rightarrow f'(x) = -2 + \tfrac{1}{4}x^{-3/2} + 3x^{-4}.$$

(c) Newton--Raphson with $x_0 = 0.5$.

$f(0.5) \approx 0.29289$.

$$f'(0.5) = -2 + \tfrac{1}{4}(0.5)^{-3/2} + 3(0.5)^{-4}$$
$$= -2 + \tfrac14\cdot 2\sqrt 2 + 3\cdot 16 = -2 + \tfrac{\sqrt 2}{2} + 48 \approx 46.7071.$$

$$x_1 = 0.5 - \frac{0.29289}{46.7071} \approx 0.5 - 0.00627 = 0.49373.$$

$$\boxed{x_1 \approx 0.494\ (3\text{ dp})}$$

(d) Linear interpolation on $[4.8, 4.9]$.
$$f(4.8) = 0.4 - \frac{1}{2\sqrt{4.8}} - \frac{1}{4.8^3} \approx 0.4 - 0.22822 - 0.00904 \approx 0.16274.$$
$$f(4.9) = 0.2 - \frac{1}{2\sqrt{4.9}} - \frac{1}{4.9^3} \approx 0.2 - 0.22587 - 0.00850 \approx -0.03437.$$

By similar triangles:
$$\beta \approx 4.8 + \frac{|f(4.8)|}{|f(4.8)| + |f(4.9)|}(4.9 - 4.8) = 4.8 + \frac{0.16274}{0.19711}(0.1) \approx 4.8 + 0.0826.$$

$$\boxed{\beta \approx 4.883\ (3\text{ dp})}$$

Target: $\boxed{\beta \approx 4.883\ (3\text{ dp})}$
