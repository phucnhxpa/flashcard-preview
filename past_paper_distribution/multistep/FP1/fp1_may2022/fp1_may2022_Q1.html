Q1 — Complex Numbers, Modulus, Argand Diagram

Question

$z_1 = 3 + 3\mathrm{i}\quad z_2 = p + q\mathrm{i}\quad p,q\in\mathbb{R}$
Given that $|z_1 z_2| = 15\sqrt{2}$,
(a) determine $|z_2|$ --- (2)
Given also that $p = -4$,
(b) determine the possible values of $q$ --- (2)
(c) Show $z_1$ and the possible positions for $z_2$ on the same Argand diagram. --- (2)

STEP 1/4Part (a)

Strategy. Reveal this box only after you have chosen the method for Part (a). The goal is to reproduce the exam-safe working, not just the final answer.

(a) $|z_1| = \sqrt{3^2 + 3^2} = \sqrt{18} = 3\sqrt 2.$

Using $|z_1 z_2| = |z_1||z_2|$:

$$3\sqrt 2 \cdot |z_2| = 15\sqrt 2 \;\Rightarrow\; |z_2| = \frac{15\sqrt 2}{3\sqrt 2} = 5.$$

Mistake guard from the attempt/revision notes.

What went wrong. Phuc tagged this #redo-this-question without a voice note. The two predictable traps are (i) using $|z_1z_2| = |z_1| + |z_2|$ (false), and (ii) returning only $q = +3$ in part (b) and forgetting the negative branch. In part (c) the most common slip is showing only one $z_2$ instead of both.

Phuc's incorrect approach (likely). $$|z_1 z_2| = |z_1| + |z_2| \;\;\Rightarrow\;\; 15\sqrt 2 = 3\sqrt 2 + |z_2| \;\;\Rightarrow\;\; |z_2| = 12\sqrt 2.$$

Then $p^2 + q^2 = (12\sqrt 2)^2 = 288$, leading to $q^2 = 272$, $q \approx \pm 16.5$. Wrong: $|z_1 z_2|$ is multiplicative, not additive.

Formula anchor.

Key formula / concept
$|z_1 z_2| = |z_1||z_2|$ and $\arg(z_1z_2) = \arg z_1 + \arg z_2$. Modulus is the length in the Argand plane: $|p+q\mathrm{i}| = \sqrt{p^2+q^2}$.
