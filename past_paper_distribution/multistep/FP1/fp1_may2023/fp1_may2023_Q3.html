Q3 — Normal to rectangular hyperbola $xy=9$

Question

The rectangular hyperbola $H$ has Cartesian equation $xy = 9$. The point $P$ with coordinates $\left(3t,\ \dfrac{3}{t}\right)$, where $t \neq 0$, lies on $H$.
(a) Use calculus to determine an equation for the normal to $H$ at the point $P$. Give your answer in the form $ty - t^3 x = f(t)$. (4)
Given that $t = 2$,
(b) determine the coordinates of the point where the normal meets $H$ again. Give your answer in simplest form. (3)

[7 marks]

Part (a)2 steps

STEP 1/2

From $xy = 9$: $y = \dfrac{9}{x}$, so $\dfrac{dy}{dx} = -\dfrac{9}{x^2}$.

At $P(3t,\, 3/t)$: $\dfrac{dy}{dx} = -\dfrac{9}{(3t)^2} = -\dfrac{9}{9t^2} = -\dfrac{1}{t^2}$.

Tangent gradient $m_T = -\dfrac{1}{t^2}$. Normal gradient $m_N = -\dfrac{1}{m_T} = t^2$.

STEP 2/2

Equation of the normal at $P$:
$$y - \tfrac{3}{t} = t^2(x - 3t).$$

Multiply both sides by $t$:
$$ty - 3 = t^3 x - 3t^4.$$

Rearrange to the required form:
$$\boxed{\;ty - t^3 x = 3 - 3t^4\;}\qquad \text{i.e. } f(t) = 3 - 3t^4.$$

Target: $\boxed{\;ty - t^3 x = 3 - 3t^4\;}$

Part (b)[3 marks] · 3 steps

Carry-in: Part (a) result: $\boxed{\;ty - t^3 x = 3 - 3t^4\;}$
STEP 1/3

With $t = 2$, the normal is $2y - 8x = 3 - 48 = -45$, so $2y = 8x - 45$, or $y = 4x - \tfrac{45}{2}$.

Intersect with $xy = 9$, i.e. $y = 9/x$:
$$\frac{9}{x} = 4x - \frac{45}{2} \implies \frac{18}{x} = 8x - 45 \implies 18 = 8x^2 - 45x \implies 8x^2 - 45x - 18 = 0.$$

We know $x = 3t = 6$ is one root (the point $P$ itself). Verify: $8(36) - 45(6) - 18 = 288 - 270 - 18 = 0$ $✓$.

STEP 2/3

Factor: $8x^2 - 45x - 18 = (x - 6)(8x + 3)$ (check: $-48x + 3x = -45x$ $✓$).

Other root: $x = -\tfrac38$, giving $y = \dfrac{9}{-3/8} = -24$.

Second intersection: $\boxed{\left(-\tfrac{3}{8},\ -24\right)}$.

Target: $\boxed{\left(-\tfrac{3}{8},\ -24\right)}$
STEP 3/3

Key formula / concept.

For $xy = c^2$, at $P(ct, c/t)$ the tangent gradient is $-1/t^2$ and the normal gradient is $t^2$. Use $y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$ and clear fractions by multiplying through by $t$ to land in the polynomial-in-$t$ form that examiners insist on.

Exam checklist.

  • [ ] Differentiated $y = 9/x$ (not $xy=9$ implicitly --- both work, but explicit is faster).
  • [ ] Used normal gradient $t^2$, not tangent gradient $-1/t^2$.
  • [ ] Cleared fractions by multiplying by $t$ to remove the $3/t$ term.
  • [ ] When intersecting, exploited that $x = 3t$ is already known as one root to factor the quadratic quickly.
  • [ ] Substituted into $xy = 9$ (not the normal equation) to find the partner $y$.

Common pitfalls.

  • Using the tangent gradient by mistake --- the question says normal.
  • Solving the resulting $8x^2 - 45x - 18 = 0$ from scratch with the quadratic formula, missing the trivial $x = 6$ shortcut.
  • Forgetting that $P$ itself lies on the normal --- the "meets $H$ again" wording is critical and means you must reject the $x = 3t$ root.
