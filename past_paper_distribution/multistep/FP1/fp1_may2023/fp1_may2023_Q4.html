Q4 — Matrix transformations and matrix algebra

Question

(i) $A = \begin{pmatrix} 3 & 8 \\ 3 & k \end{pmatrix}$ where $k$ is a constant. The transformation represented by $A$ transforms triangle $T$ to triangle $T'$. The area of triangle $T'$ is three times the area of triangle $T$. Determine the possible values of $k$. (4)

(ii) $B = \begin{pmatrix} a & 4 \\ 2 & 3 \end{pmatrix}$ and $BC = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 5 & 1 \\ -1 & 4 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$ where $a$ is a constant. Determine, in terms of $a$, the matrix $C$. (4)

[8 marks]

Part (i)[4 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2

Area scale factor under matrix $A$ is $|\det A|$. So $|\det A| = 3$.

$\det A = 3k - 8\cdot 3 = 3k - 24.$

STEP 2/2

$|3k - 24| = 3 \implies 3k - 24 = \pm 3 \implies k = 9 \quad \text{or} \quad k = 7.$

$\boxed{k = 9\ \text{or}\ k = 7}$.

Target: $\boxed{k = 9\ \text{or}\ k = 7}$

Part (ii)[4 marks] · 5 steps

Carry-in: Part (i) result: $\boxed{k = 9\ \text{or}\ k = 7}$
STEP 1/5

Since $BC$ is $2\times 3$ and $B$ is $2\times 2$, $C$ is $2\times 3$ with $C = B^{-1}(BC)$.

$\det B = 3a - 8$. So
$$B^{-1} = \frac{1}{3a - 8}\begin{pmatrix} 3 & -4 \\ -2 & a \end{pmatrix}.$$

STEP 2/5

Multiply $B^{-1}$ by $BC$:

Row 1 of $B^{-1}$ times columns of $BC$:
- Col 1: $3(2) + (-4)(-1) = 6 + 4 = 10$
- Col 2: $3(5) + (-4)(4) = 15 - 16 = -1$
- Col 3: $3(1) + (-4)(2) = 3 - 8 = -5$

STEP 3/5

Row 2 of $B^{-1}$ times columns of $BC$:
- Col 1: $-2(2) + a(-1) = -4 - a$
- Col 2: $-2(5) + a(4) = -10 + 4a$
- Col 3: $-2(1) + a(2) = -2 + 2a$

$$\boxed{\;C = \frac{1}{3a - 8}\begin{pmatrix} 10 & -1 & -5 \\ -4 - a & 4a - 10 & 2a - 2 \end{pmatrix}\;}$$

Target: $\boxed{\;C = \frac{1}{3a - 8}\begin{pmatrix} 10 & -1 & -5 \\ -4 - a & 4a - 10 & 2a - 2 \end{pmatrix}\;}$
STEP 4/5

(Equivalent form: each entry divided by $3a - 8$.)

STEP 5/5

Key formula / concept.

Two facts to internalise: (1) area scale factor = $|\det M|$ --- always take absolute value. (2) $B^{-1} = \dfrac{1}{\det B}\!\begin{pmatrix} d & -b \\ -c & a \end{pmatrix}$ for $B = \begin{pmatrix}a & b \\ c & d\end{pmatrix}$.

Exam checklist.

  • [ ] Wrote $|\det A| = 3$ with explicit modulus bars --- earns the mark even if one $k$ is wrong.
  • [ ] Reported both values of $k$.
  • [ ] Confirmed dimensions of $C$ before computing ($2\times 2$ times $2\times 3$ implies $C$ is $2\times 3$).
  • [ ] Computed $\det B = 3a - 8$ (not $3a + 8$ or $8 - 3a$).
  • [ ] Left $C$ with $\dfrac{1}{3a-8}$ scalar out front --- don't multiply through unnecessarily.

Common pitfalls.

  • Solving $3k - 24 = 3$ only and forgetting $3k - 24 = -3$.
  • Computing $B^{-1}(BC)$ vs $(BC)B^{-1}$ --- the equation $BC = D$ gives $C = B^{-1}D$, not $DB^{-1}$. Order matters because matrix multiplication is non-commutative.
  • Sign errors in the cofactor matrix when forming $B^{-1}$.

See also Q3 (algebra clean-up of fractions) and Q5 (multi-step substitution where order matters).
