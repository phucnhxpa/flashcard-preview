Q2 — Series identity using standard sums

Question

In this question you must show all stages of your working. Solutions relying entirely on calculator technology are not acceptable.
Use the standard results for $\displaystyle\sum_{r=1}^{n}r$ and $\displaystyle\sum_{r=1}^{n}r^2$ to show that for all positive integers $n$
$$\sum_{r=1}^{n}(7r-5)^2=\frac{n}{6}(7n+1)(An+B)$$
where $A$ and $B$ are integers to be determined. (6)

[6 marks]

Solution4 steps

STEP 1/4

Expand the summand:
$$(7r-5)^2=49r^2-70r+25$$

Split using linearity of $\sum$:
$$\sum_{r=1}^{n}(7r-5)^2=49\sum_{r=1}^{n}r^2-70\sum_{r=1}^{n}r+25\sum_{r=1}^{n}1$$

Apply the standard results (in the formula booklet):
$$=49\cdot\frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6}-70\cdot\frac{n(n+1)}{2}+25n$$

Bring everything onto the common denominator $6$:
$$=\frac{49n(n+1)(2n+1)-210n(n+1)+150n}{6}$$

STEP 2/4

Factor $n$:
$$=\frac{n}{6}\big[49(n+1)(2n+1)-210(n+1)+150\big]$$

Expand inside the bracket:
$$49(n+1)(2n+1)=49(2n^2+3n+1)=98n^2+147n+49$$
$$-210(n+1)=-210n-210$$

Add everything in the bracket:
$$98n^2+147n+49-210n-210+150 = 98n^2-63n-11$$

Now factorise the quadratic $98n^2-63n-11$. Discriminant $=63^2+4(98)(11)=3969+4312=8281=91^2$.
$$98n^2-63n-11=(7n+1)(14n-11)$$

STEP 3/4

Therefore
$$\sum_{r=1}^{n}(7r-5)^2=\frac{n}{6}(7n+1)(14n-11) \implies A=14,\ B=-11$$

Key formula / concept
$\sum r=\tfrac{n(n+1)}{2}$, $\sum r^2=\tfrac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6}$, $\sum c = cn$. All in the booklet.

Exam checklist
- Expand $(7r-5)^2$ correctly: middle term is $-70r$, not $-35r$.
- The third sum $\sum 25 = 25n$, not $25(n+1)$.
- Pull $\dfrac{n}{6}$ out before factorising --- the question's required form telegraphs this.
- Check at $n=1$: LHS $=(7-5)^2=4$; RHS $=\tfrac{1}{6}(8)(3)=4$. $✓$
- State $A,B$ as integers explicitly.

Common pitfalls
- Forgetting that $\sum_{r=1}^n 1 = n$ (writing $n+1$).
- Cross-multiplying signs in the discriminant --- keep $b=-63$, square it, the discriminant is positive.
- Quoting the factorisation $(14n+1)(7n-11)$ --- check by multiplying out; only one ordering gives $-63n$.
