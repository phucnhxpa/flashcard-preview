Q1 — Matrix product and determinant

Question

Given that
$$A=\begin{pmatrix}2&-1&3\\ -2&3&0\end{pmatrix} \qquad \text{and} \qquad B=\begin{pmatrix}1&k\\ 0&3\\ -2k&2\end{pmatrix}$$
where $k$ is a non-zero constant,
(a) determine the matrix $AB$ (2)
(b) determine the value of $k$ for which $\det(AB)=0$ (3)

[5 marks]

Part (a)[2 marks] · 3 steps

STEP 1/3

Compute $AB$ entry-by-entry. The product is $2\times 2$:

STEP 2/3

$$AB=\begin{pmatrix}(2)(1)+(-1)(0)+(3)(-2k) & (2)(k)+(-1)(3)+(3)(2)\\[4pt] (-2)(1)+(3)(0)+(0)(-2k) & (-2)(k)+(3)(3)+(0)(2)\end{pmatrix}$$

STEP 3/3

$$AB=\begin{pmatrix}2-6k & 2k+3\\ -2 & 9-2k\end{pmatrix}$$

Part (b)[3 marks] · 3 steps

STEP 1/3

Apply the $2\times2$ determinant formula:

$$\det(AB)=(2-6k)(9-2k)-(2k+3)(-2)$$
$$=18-4k-54k+12k^2+4k+6$$
$$=12k^2-54k+24$$
$$=6(2k^2-9k+4)$$
$$=6(2k-1)(k-4)$$

Setting $\det(AB)=0$:
$$(2k-1)(k-4)=0 \implies k=\tfrac{1}{2} \text{ or } k=4$$

STEP 2/3

Key formula / concept
$\det\begin{pmatrix}a&b\\c&d\end{pmatrix}=ad-bc$. For $(m\times n)(n\times p)$ products the result is $(m\times p)$ --- the inner dimensions must match.

From the Loom (Mr Fuat, Loom 1):
"In your calculator you can go to matrices\ldots but if there is an unknown inside the matrices, you cannot use your calculator. You must solve by hand."

Exam checklist
- Confirm output shape before multiplying: $(2\times3)(3\times2)\to 2\times2$.
- Carry the signs of every entry of $B$ through each dot product --- circle them on the question paper before computing.
- After expanding $\det$, factorise the quadratic in $k$ rather than using the formula --- cleaner mark trail.
- Check both roots are non-zero (the problem stated "$k$ is a non-zero constant").
- Sub one root back into $AB$ and check the resulting matrix is singular by inspection (rows proportional).

STEP 3/3

Common pitfalls
- Sign-flipping on $-2k$ in $B_{31}$ --- the most common slip on this question.
- Computing $\det(A)\det(B)$ --- invalid because $A,B$ are non-square.
- Treating $k=0$ as a valid root and reporting three solutions.
