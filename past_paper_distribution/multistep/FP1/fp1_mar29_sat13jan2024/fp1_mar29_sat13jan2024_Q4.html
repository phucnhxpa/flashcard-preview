Q4 — Composite matrix transformation, area

Question

$$A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 3 \end{pmatrix}$$

(a) Describe the single geometrical transformation represented by the matrix $A$. (2)

The matrix $B$ represents a rotation of $210$^\circ$$ anticlockwise about centre $(0, 0)$. > (b) Write down the matrix $B$, giving each element in exact form. (1) > > The transformation represented by matrix $A$ followed by the transformation represented by matrix $B$ is represented by the matrix $C$. > (c) Find $C$. (2) > > The hexagon $H$ is transformed onto the hexagon $H'$ by the matrix $C$. > (d) Given that the area of hexagon $H$ is 5 square units, determine the area of hexagon $H'$. (2) ### From the Loom (Mr. Fuat, video_analysis_2b) > *"It is a stretch scale factor 4 parallel to the x-axis. How we are going to write this one? \ldots 4 0 0 1. Well done."* (template for axis-stretch matrices) > > *"So we have C equals to B multiplied by A."* (composite order) > > *"P prime is the area scale factor times P."* (area ↔ |det|) ### Phuc's attempted approach (transferred from May paper) On the May paper Phuc wrote the reflection-in-y-axis matrix as $\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{pmatrix}$ (which is reflection in the x-axis). The general weakness: confusing **which axis is fixed** by a reflection/stretch matrix. Same risk here: a "stretch parallel to y-axis" has the $3$ in the **(2,2) position**, not (1,1). ### Correct method **(a)** Matrix $A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 3 \end{pmatrix}$ leaves x-coordinates unchanged and multiplies y-coordinates by 3. $\boxed{\text{Stretch, scale factor 3, parallel to the y-axis (i.e. with the x-axis invariant)}}$ **(b)** Rotation by angle $\theta$ anticlockwise: $$R_\theta = \begin{pmatrix} \cos\theta & -\sin\theta \\ \sin\theta & \cos\theta \end{pmatrix}$$ For $\theta = 210$^\circ$$: $\cos 210$^\circ$ = -\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$, $\sin 210$^\circ$ = -\frac{1}{2}$.

$$B = \begin{pmatrix} -\tfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2} & \tfrac{1}{2} \\ -\tfrac{1}{2} & -\tfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \end{pmatrix}$$

(Note $-\sin\theta = -(-\tfrac{1}{2}) = +\tfrac{1}{2}$ in the top-right entry --- sign care.)

(c) "$A$ followed by $B$" means $C = BA$ (the second transformation goes on the left):
$$C = BA = \begin{pmatrix} -\tfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2} & \tfrac{1}{2} \\ -\tfrac{1}{2} & -\tfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 3 \end{pmatrix}$$

Row-by-column:
- $(1,1)$: $-\tfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \cdot 1 + \tfrac{1}{2} \cdot 0 = -\tfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$
- $(1,2)$: $-\tfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \cdot 0 + \tfrac{1}{2} \cdot 3 = \tfrac{3}{2}$
- $(2,1)$: $-\tfrac{1}{2} \cdot 1 + (-\tfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2}) \cdot 0 = -\tfrac{1}{2}$
- $(2,2)$: $-\tfrac{1}{2} \cdot 0 + (-\tfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2}) \cdot 3 = -\tfrac{3\sqrt{3}}{2}$

$$C = \begin{pmatrix} -\tfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2} & \tfrac{3}{2} \\ -\tfrac{1}{2} & -\tfrac{3\sqrt{3}}{2} \end{pmatrix}$$

(d) Area scale factor $= |\det C|$. Use $\det(BA) = \det(B)\det(A)$:
- $\det(A) = (1)(3) - (0)(0) = 3$
- $\det(B) = (-\tfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2})(-\tfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2}) - (\tfrac{1}{2})(-\tfrac{1}{2}) = \tfrac{3}{4} + \tfrac{1}{4} = 1$ (any rotation matrix has determinant 1)
- $\det(C) = 1 \cdot 3 = 3$

$$\text{Area}(H') = |\det C| \cdot \text{Area}(H) = 3 \times 5 = \boxed{15 \text{ square units}}$$

What went wrong

The "stretch parallel to y-axis" trap: the scale factor lives in the (2,2) entry because that's the row that controls y. A stretch parallel to x-axis would put it in (1,1). Mnemonic: the diagonal entry tells you what gets scaled --- first diagonal = x, second diagonal = y.

Key formula / concept

  • Composite order: "$T_1$ then $T_2$" corresponds to the matrix product $T_2 T_1$ (apply $T_1$ first means it acts on the vector first, i.e. is rightmost).
  • Area scale factor: $|\det M|$. Always take the modulus --- a negative determinant means orientation is reversed but area is still positive.
  • Rotation matrices always have determinant $+1$.

Exam checklist

  • [ ] In (a), said "stretch" (not "enlargement"), specified scale factor, and named the invariant axis
  • [ ] In (b), used exact surd values (not decimals) and tracked the $-\sin\theta$ sign carefully
  • [ ] In (c), wrote $C = BA$ (not $AB$) --- the "followed by" order trap
  • [ ] In (d), used $|\det|$ and exploited $\det(BA) = \det(B)\det(A)$ rather than expanding $\det(C)$ from scratch
  • [ ] Verified rotation determinant equals 1 (sanity check)

Common pitfalls

  • Writing $C = AB$ instead of $BA$.
  • Computing $\cos 210$^\circ$$ as $+\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$ (forgetting third-quadrant signs).
  • For (d), computing $\det(C)$ from the messy entries directly instead of using $\det(BA) = \det B \cdot \det A$.
