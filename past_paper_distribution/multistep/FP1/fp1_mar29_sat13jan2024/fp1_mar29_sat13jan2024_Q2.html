Q2 — Cubic with complex root, Argand triangle

Question

$$f(z) = 2z^3 + pz^2 + qz - 41$$
where $p$ and $q$ are integers.

The complex number $5 - 4i$ is a root of the equation $f(z) = 0$.

(a) Write down another complex root of this equation. (1)
(b) Solve the equation $f(z) = 0$ completely. (4)
(c) Determine the value of $p$ and the value of $q$. (2)

When plotted on an Argand diagram, the points representing the roots of the equation $f(z) = 0$ form the vertices of a triangle.
(d) Determine the area of this triangle. (2)

From the Loom (Mr. Fuat, video_analysis_2 on the analogous quartic)

"that means this is the factor of this polynomial, yes? It is like $z - z_1$ and then $z - z_1$ conjugate. \ldots This is a specific kind of multiplication. It can be written as $z^2 - 2 \cdot \mathrm{Re}(z_1) \cdot z$ plus the square of the modulus of $z_1$."

"Because if this is the factor of this \ldots equation, that means that this \ldots equation must be divided with zero remainder with this equation."

Phuc's attempted approach (transferred from May paper Loom on Q3)

Phuc consistently forgets that on an Argand diagram the x-axis is the Real part, not the Imaginary part --- his own Slack message: "So for this question, I forget that the x-axis should be the real part, not the imaginary part." On a triangle-area question this matters because base and height are read off Re/Im coordinates.

Correct method

(a) Since $f$ has real coefficients and $5 - 4i$ is a root, the complex conjugate $\boxed{5 + 4i}$ is also a root.

(b) The cubic has three roots: $5 - 4i$, $5 + 4i$, and a third root which must be real (a cubic with real coefficients has either 3 real roots or 1 real + 1 conjugate pair).

Use Vieta's for product of roots (fastest route):
$$\text{Product of roots} = -\frac{(\text{constant})}{(\text{leading})} = -\frac{-41}{2} = \frac{41}{2}$$

Now $(5-4i)(5+4i) = 25 - (4i)^2 = 25 + 16 = 41$.

Let the third (real) root be $\alpha$. Then $41 \cdot \alpha = \frac{41}{2}$, so $\alpha = \tfrac{1}{2}$.

Roots: $z = 5 - 4i, \quad z = 5 + 4i, \quad z = \tfrac{1}{2}$.

(c) Use Vieta's for the sum and the sum of products in pairs:

Sum of roots $= -\dfrac{p}{2}$:
$$(5-4i) + (5+4i) + \tfrac{1}{2} = 10 + \tfrac{1}{2} = \tfrac{21}{2}$$
$$-\frac{p}{2} = \frac{21}{2} \implies p = -21$$

Sum of products in pairs $= \dfrac{q}{2}$:
$$(5-4i)(5+4i) + (5-4i)(\tfrac{1}{2}) + (5+4i)(\tfrac{1}{2}) = 41 + \tfrac{1}{2}\bigl[(5-4i)+(5+4i)\bigr] = 41 + \tfrac{1}{2}(10) = 46$$
$$\frac{q}{2} = 46 \implies q = 92$$

Verification (optional but recommended): Sum of products three at a time $= -\dfrac{-41}{2} = \tfrac{41}{2}$. Compute: $(5-4i)(5+4i)(\tfrac{1}{2}) = 41 \cdot \tfrac{1}{2} = \tfrac{41}{2}$. $✓$

(d) Plot the three roots on the Argand diagram (x-axis = Real, y-axis = Imaginary):
- $A = (5, -4)$
- $B = (5, +4)$
- $C = (\tfrac{1}{2}, 0)$

$A$ and $B$ share the same x-coordinate ($x = 5$), so $AB$ is a vertical segment.
- Base $|AB| = |4 - (-4)| = 8$
- Height = horizontal distance from $C$ to the line $x = 5$ $= |5 - \tfrac{1}{2}| = \tfrac{9}{2}$

$$\text{Area} = \tfrac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{height} = \tfrac{1}{2} \times 8 \times \tfrac{9}{2} = \boxed{18 \text{ square units}}$$

What went wrong

If Phuc plots $(5, -4)$ as $(-4, 5)$ --- swapping axes --- the triangle becomes degenerate or misshaped and the area calculation collapses. The Argand convention is fixed: horizontal = Real, vertical = Imaginary. Drill: any complex $a + bi$ → coordinate $(a, b)$ always. No exceptions.

Key formula / concept

  • Conjugate pair factor: if $a \pm bi$ are roots, the corresponding quadratic factor is $z^2 - 2az + (a^2 + b^2)$.
  • Vieta for cubic $az^3 + bz^2 + cz + d = 0$ with roots $\alpha, \beta, \gamma$:
  • $\alpha + \beta + \gamma = -b/a$
  • $\alpha\beta + \beta\gamma + \gamma\alpha = c/a$
  • $\alpha\beta\gamma = -d/a$

Exam checklist

  • [ ] Stated conjugate root in (a) using the words "complex conjugate" or noting "coefficients are real"
  • [ ] In (b), used Vieta's product (fastest) rather than expanding $(z^2 - 10z + 41)(2z - 1)$ unless asked
  • [ ] In (c), checked $p, q$ are integers as specified
  • [ ] In (d), drew a rough Argand sketch with x = Real and y = Imaginary clearly labelled
  • [ ] Identified the vertical-base / horizontal-height geometry rather than using the shoelace formula

Common pitfalls

  • Forgetting that $(5-4i)(5+4i) = 5^2 + 4^2 = 41$ (NOT $5^2 - 4^2 = 9$).
  • For (c), confusing the sign convention $\sum \alpha = -b/a$ --- the minus sign is easy to drop.
  • For (d), trying to use $\tfrac{1}{2}|x_1(y_2 - y_3) + \ldots|$ unnecessarily; the geometry is simpler.
