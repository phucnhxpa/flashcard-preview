Q2(a) — Show $S = 6x^2 + \dfrac{2592}{x}$ for a cuboid brick of volume $972\,\text{cm}^3$

Question

In this question you must show all stages of your working. Solutions based entirely on calculator technology are not acceptable.

A brick is in the shape of a cuboid with width $x$ cm, length $3x$ cm and height $h$ cm, as shown in Figure 2.

The volume of the brick is $972\,\text{cm}^3$.

(a) Show that the surface area of the brick, $S\,\text{cm}^2$, is given by

$$S \;=\; 6x^2 + \frac{2592}{x}$$

(3)

Scope of this card: part (a) only — Phuc's #redo flag was "only redo part a". Parts (b)–(e) on differentiation and stationary value are standard once (a) is established.

[3 marks]

Part (a) — Derive $S = 6x^2 + \dfrac{2592}{x}$ [3 marks] · 3 steps

Dimensions: width $x$ cm, length $3x$ cm, height $h$ cm. Volume: $V = 972\,\text{cm}^3$. Target: express $S$ in terms of $x$ alone.
STEP 1/3 M1 — eliminate h

Recognise the trigger. The target $S = 6x^2 + 2592/x$ is a function of $x$ alone, but the brick has two independent dimensions, $x$ and $h$. The volume constraint $V = 972$ is the bridge: it ties $h$ to $x$ so that you can eliminate $h$ and end up with a single-variable expression for $S$. Whenever an exam question gives you (i) two variables in the geometry, (ii) one constraint (here $V$), and (iii) a target that uses only one of them, the move is always the same — solve the constraint for the unwanted variable and substitute.

Why this method. The differentiation in parts (b)–(d) needs $S$ to be a one-variable function so that $dS/dx$ makes sense. Keeping $h$ in the expression would make $dS/dx$ ambiguous (partial vs total derivative — off-syllabus at IAL P2).

State the tool. Volume of a cuboid:

$$V \;=\; \text{width} \times \text{length} \times \text{height} \;=\; x \cdot 3x \cdot h \;=\; 3x^2 h.$$

Apply. Set this equal to the given volume and solve for $h$:

$$3x^2 h \;=\; 972$$

$$h \;=\; \frac{972}{3x^2} \;=\; \frac{324}{x^2}.$$

Sense check. Units work: $972\,\text{cm}^3 \div (\text{cm} \cdot 3\text{cm}) = $ cm ✓. Behaviour check: as $x$ grows, $h$ shrinks like $1/x^2$ — a wider, longer brick is flatter, which matches physical intuition.

The dominant slip on "show that" questions is forgetting to write down $V = x \cdot 3x \cdot h$ explicitly — students go straight to $h = 324/x^2$ and lose the M1 mark which is awarded for setting up the volume equation. The mark scheme wants to see the volume formula on the page before the rearrangement.
$h \;=\; \dfrac{324}{x^2}$ (from $V = 3x^2 h = 972$).
Carry-in from previous stepFrom STEP 1: $h = \dfrac{324}{x^2}$.
STEP 2/3 M1 — surface area in $x,h$

Recognise the trigger. Now build the surface area $S$ from the geometry. A closed cuboid has six faces in three matching pairs. Naming the dimensions clearly stops you double-counting or missing a face.

Why this method. Working face-by-face (rather than memorising a formula) is robust to the question changing the aspect ratio. Here the length is fixed at $3$ times the width, so two of the three face-pairs are unequal — the formula $2(\ell w + \ell h + wh)$ is the safe template.

State the tool. For a cuboid with width $w$, length $\ell$, height $h$:

$$S \;=\; 2\bigl(\,\underbrace{\ell w}_{\text{top/bottom}} \;+\; \underbrace{\ell h}_{\text{front/back}} \;+\; \underbrace{w h}_{\text{left/right}}\,\bigr).$$

Apply with $w = x$, $\ell = 3x$, height $h$:

$$S \;=\; 2\bigl((3x)(x) \;+\; (3x)(h) \;+\; (x)(h)\bigr)$$

$$\;=\; 2\bigl(3x^2 \;+\; 3xh \;+\; xh\bigr)$$

$$\;=\; 2\bigl(3x^2 \;+\; 4xh\bigr) \;=\; 6x^2 \;+\; 8xh.$$

Sense check. Three terms, each with units cm$^2$ ✓. The $6x^2$ is the contribution from the two top/bottom rectangles (each of area $3x \cdot x = 3x^2$, doubled). The $8xh$ groups the four vertical faces (two of area $3xh$ and two of area $xh$). Total face count: $2 + 2 + 2 = 6$ ✓.

A frequent error is to write $S = 2(x^2 + xh + 3xh)$ — i.e. forget that the top/bottom face is $3x \times x$, not $x \times x$. Drawing a tiny sketch in the margin with each face labelled stops this. The other classic slip is to write only 3 faces (the visible ones in the figure) and forget the back/bottom/right hidden faces.
$S \;=\; 6x^2 \;+\; 8xh$ (still in terms of $x$ and $h$).
Carry-in from previous stepFrom STEP 1: $h = \dfrac{324}{x^2}$. From STEP 2: $S = 6x^2 + 8xh$.
STEP 3/3 A1 — substitute & simplify

Recognise the trigger. You now have $S$ in two variables and $h$ as a function of $x$. Substitution closes the loop and gives $S(x)$.

Apply. Replace $h$ in the surface-area expression:

$$S \;=\; 6x^2 \;+\; 8x \cdot \underbrace{\frac{324}{x^2}}_{=\,h}.$$

Simplify the second term. Cancel one factor of $x$:

$$8x \cdot \frac{324}{x^2} \;=\; \frac{8 \cdot 324 \cdot x}{x^2} \;=\; \frac{8 \cdot 324}{x} \;=\; \frac{2592}{x}.$$

(Arithmetic: $8 \times 324 = 8 \times 300 + 8 \times 24 = 2400 + 192 = 2592$.)

Therefore

$$\boxed{\,S \;=\; 6x^2 \;+\; \frac{2592}{x}\,} \qquad \text{as required.} \;\blacksquare$$

Sense check. Both terms are positive for $x > 0$ ✓. As $x \to 0^+$, $S \to \infty$ (the brick becomes a thin tall column with huge side area); as $x \to \infty$, $S \to \infty$ (the brick spreads out flat with huge top/bottom area). So $S$ must have a minimum somewhere in between — that's precisely what parts (c)–(e) ask you to find.

For "show that" questions, the A1 is awarded only if your final line matches the printed expression exactly. If you wrote $S = 6x^2 + 2592x^{-1}$ instead, you'd still get A1 (equivalent form), but $S = 6x^2 + 2592 \div x$ would not — convention demands the fraction form when the question shows it that way. Also: do not skip arithmetic — write $8 \times 324 = 2592$ on the page. "Show that" rewards every visible step.
$\boxed{S = 6x^2 + \dfrac{2592}{x}}$ — required form. ✓
