Q6 · Make b the subject of a = (b²+2)/(3b²−1)

Question

Given $a=\dfrac{b^{2}+2}{3b^{2}-1}$, express $b$ in terms of $a$.

A. $b=\pm\sqrt{\dfrac{a+2}{3a+1}}$    B. $b=\pm\sqrt{\dfrac{a+2}{3a-1}}$    C. $b=\pm\sqrt{\dfrac{2-a}{3a+1}}$

D. $b=\pm\sqrt{\dfrac{2-a}{3a-1}}$    E. $b=\pm\sqrt{\dfrac{3}{3a+1}}$    F. $b=\pm\sqrt{\dfrac{3}{3a-1}}$

[1 marks]

Solution [1 marks] · 4 steps

algebra-only; no diagram needed. Scan the options first: every one has the form $\pm\sqrt{(\cdots)/(\cdots)}$. That tells you in advance to (i) isolate $b^{2}$, (ii) take a square root, and (iii) keep two signs. Note $b$ appears only as $b^{2}$ throughout, so the equation is really linear in the single unknown $b^{2}$ — that is the key that makes a clean rearrangement possible.
STEP 1/4clear fraction

Strategy. Clear the denominator by multiplying both sides by $(3b^{2}-1)$, then expand the left side.

Cross-multiply (×$(3b^{2}-1)$)

$$a=\dfrac{b^{2}+2}{3b^{2}-1}\;\Rightarrow\;a(3b^{2}-1)=b^{2}+2.$$

Expand the bracket

$$3ab^{2}-a=b^{2}+2.$$
Technique (match the answer form). Because all six options are $\pm\sqrt{\text{fraction}}$, the target is $b^{2}=\text{(something)}$. Clearing the fraction is the first move toward that target and avoids dead-end paths.

Sense check. Both sides now carry a $b^{2}$ term ($3ab^{2}$ on the left, $b^{2}$ on the right); they must be gathered together before $b^{2}$ can be isolated.

$3ab^{2}-a=b^{2}+2$.
↳ from STEP 2: $3ab^{2}-a=b^{2}+2$.
STEP 2/4collect b²

Strategy. Move every $b^{2}$ term to the left and every constant to the right. A term flips sign when it crosses the equals sign: the $+b^{2}$ on the right becomes $-b^{2}$ on the left; the $-a$ on the left becomes $+a$ on the right.

Collect $b^{2}$ left, constants right

$$3ab^{2}-b^{2}=a+2.$$
Common trap (sign on moving terms). A frequent slip here is writing $3ab^{2}-b^{2}-a+2=0$, carrying $+2$ to the left with the wrong sign. Moving $b^{2}+2$ across the equals sign makes it $-b^{2}-2$. The clean route keeps $b^{2}$ terms left and constants right: $3ab^{2}-b^{2}=a+2$.

Sense check. The right side $a+2$ is constant in $b$; the left collects both $b^{2}$ terms, ready to factor.

$3ab^{2}-b^{2}=a+2$.
↳ from STEP 3: $3ab^{2}-b^{2}=a+2$.
STEP 3/4factor + isolate

Strategy. Factor $b^{2}$ out of the left side and divide to isolate $b^{2}$.

Factorise $b^{2}$

$$b^{2}(3a-1)=a+2.$$

Isolate $b^{2}$

$$b^{2}=\dfrac{a+2}{3a-1}.$$

Sense check. The denominator is $3a-1$ (from $3ab^2-b^2$), which is the factor distinguishing the correct option from the $3a+1$ distractors.

$b^{2}=\dfrac{a+2}{3a-1}$.
↳ from STEP 4: $b^{2}=\dfrac{a+2}{3a-1}$.
STEP 4/4square root ±

Strategy. Take the square root of both sides, keeping both signs because $b$ enters the original equation only as $b^{2}$.

Square root (both signs)

$$b=\pm\sqrt{\dfrac{a+2}{3a-1}}.$$
Common trap (keep the ±). Since $b$ appears only as $b^{2}$, both $+\sqrt{\cdots}$ and $-\sqrt{\cdots}$ satisfy the original equation. Dropping the $\pm$ loses a valid root.
Eliminate the look-alikes. The numerator is $a+2$ (not $2-a$), so Options C and D fall away; the denominator is $3a-1$ (not $3a+1$), so Options A and E fall away. Option F has numerator $3$, which never appeared. Only Option B survives both checks.
Exam pace. Once you spot the answer form $\pm\sqrt{\text{fraction}}$, this is a $\approx30$ s question: cross-multiply, collect $b^{2}$, factor, root.

Sense check. Numerator $a+2$ and denominator $3a-1$ match Option B exactly; the $\pm$ and the square-root shell match every option’s form, as foreseen from scanning the answers.

$\boxed{b=\pm\sqrt{\dfrac{a+2}{3a-1}}}$ — Option B.
