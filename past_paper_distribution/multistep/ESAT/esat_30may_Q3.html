Q3 · Which expression is largest for 0 < x < 1

Question

Which of the expressions below has the largest value for $0<x<1$?

A. $\dfrac1x$    B. $x^{2}$    C. $\dfrac{1}{1+x}$    D. $\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{x}}$    E. $\sqrt{x}$

[1 marks]

Solution [1 marks] · 3 steps

Numbers strictly between 0 and 1 behave counter-intuitively: squaring shrinks, square-rooting grows, and reciprocals exceed 1. Find each function’s range on $(0,1)$, eliminate everything below 1, then settle the two survivors with a test value.
STEP 1/3eliminate <1

Strategy. Find the range of each expression on $0<x<1$ from its endpoint behaviour, and discard any that never exceed 1.

Ranges on $0<x<1$

$$x^{2}\in(0,1),\quad \sqrt{x}\in(0,1),\quad \dfrac{1}{1+x}\in\left(\tfrac12,1\right).$$

All three stay below 1 — eliminated. The reciprocals survive:

$$\dfrac1x\in(1,\infty),\qquad \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{x}}\in(1,\infty).$$
Common trap ($\tfrac{1}{1+x}$ is below 1). Since $1+x>1$ for $x>0$, we have $\tfrac{1}{1+x}<1$ — it can never be the largest. Confusing the direction of the inequality on reciprocals is the usual error here.

Sense check. Any winner must exceed 1, so only $\tfrac1x$ and $\tfrac{1}{\sqrt{x}}$ remain in contention.

Candidates: $\dfrac1x$ and $\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{x}}$ (both $>1$).
↳ from STEP 2: only $\dfrac1x$ and $\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{x}}$ remain.
STEP 2/3test x=1/4

Strategy. Settle the two candidates with the decisive test value $x=\tfrac14$ (easy arithmetic).

Substitute $x=\tfrac14$

$$\sqrt{\tfrac14}=\tfrac12\;\Rightarrow\; \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{x}}=\dfrac{1}{1/2}=2,$$$$\dfrac1x=\dfrac{1}{1/4}=4.$$
Phuc's mistake (≈15:30). Testing $x=\tfrac12$ for $x^{2}$, Phuc wrote $(1/2)^{2}=1$. Correct: $(1/2)^{2}=\tfrac12\times\tfrac12=\tfrac14$. Squaring a fraction squares both numerator and denominator: $(a/b)^2=a^2/b^2$.

Sense check. At $x=\tfrac14$, $\dfrac1x=4>2=\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{x}}$, so $\dfrac1x$ wins this test.

At $x=\tfrac14$: $\dfrac1x=4$, $\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{x}}=2$.
↳ from STEP 3: $\dfrac1x=4>2=\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{x}}$ at $x=\tfrac14$.
STEP 3/3general reason

Strategy. Confirm the result holds for ALL of $0<x<1$, not just the test point, by comparing denominators.

General argument

$$0<x<1\;\Rightarrow\; x<\sqrt{x}.$$

The smaller denominator gives the larger fraction:

$$x<\sqrt{x}\;\Rightarrow\;\dfrac1x>\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{x}}.$$

Sense check. Since $x<\sqrt{x}$ throughout $(0,1)$, $\dfrac1x$ beats $\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{x}}$ everywhere on the interval — the test value was representative. Answer: $\dfrac1x$ (Option A).

$\boxed{\dfrac1x\text{ is largest}}$ — Option A.
