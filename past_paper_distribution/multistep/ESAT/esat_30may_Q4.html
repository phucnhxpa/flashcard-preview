Q4 · Similar triangles giving a quadratic in x

Question

A shape is formed by drawing a triangle $ABC$ inside the triangle $ADE$. $BC$ is parallel to $DE$. $AB=4$ cm, $BC=x$ cm, $DE=(x+3)$ cm, $DB=(x-4)$ cm [diagram not to scale]. What is the length of $DE$ in cm?

A. $5$    B. $7$    C. $9$    D. $4+2\sqrt7$    E. $7+2\sqrt7$

[1 marks]

Solution [1 marks] · 4 steps

Because $BC\parallel DE$, triangles $ABC$ and $ADE$ share angle $A$ and have equal corresponding angles, so $\triangle ABC\sim\triangle ADE$ (AA). First express the full side $AD=AB+BD=4+(x-4)=x$.
STEP 1/4proportion

Strategy. Set up the similar-triangle proportion of corresponding sides, using $AD=x$.

Find $AD$ first

$$AD=AB+BD=4+(x-4)=x.$$

Similar-triangle ratio (AA)

$$\dfrac{AB}{AD}=\dfrac{BC}{DE}\;\Rightarrow\;\dfrac{4}{x}=\dfrac{x}{x+3}.$$
Common trap (compute AD). $AD$ is the whole side, $AB+BD=x$, not just $4$. Pairing $AB$ with $AD$ and $BC$ with $DE$ keeps small-with-large consistent.

Sense check. Both ratios are ‘small side over large side’, so the proportion is set up consistently.

$\dfrac{4}{x}=\dfrac{x}{x+3}$, with $AD=x$.
↳ from STEP 2: $\dfrac{4}{x}=\dfrac{x}{x+3}$.
STEP 2/4quadratic

Strategy. Cross-multiply to clear the fractions and rearrange into a standard quadratic.

Cross-multiply

$$4(x+3)=x^{2}\;\Rightarrow\;x^{2}=4x+12$$$$x^{2}-4x-12=0.$$

Sense check. A quadratic with integer coefficients — the clean-integer answers in the options suggest it factorises.

$x^{2}-4x-12=0$.
↳ from STEP 3: $x^{2}-4x-12=0$.
STEP 3/4factorise

Strategy. Factorise by the product–sum method: two numbers multiplying to $-12$ and summing to $-4$.

Product $=-12$, sum $=-4$

$$2\times(-6)=-12,\quad 2+(-6)=-4\;\Rightarrow\;(x+2)(x-6)=0.$$

Roots

$$x=-2\ (\text{rejected, length}>0)\quad\text{or}\quad x=6.$$
Technique (manual factoring). For clean-integer ESAT quadratics the product–sum table is faster than the formula. Reject $x=-2$ because a length cannot be negative.

Sense check. $x=6$ is positive, so $BC=6$, $BD=2$, $AD=6$ — all valid lengths.

$x=6$ (taking the positive root).
↳ from STEP 4: $x=6$; and $DE=x+3$.
STEP 4/4find DE

Strategy. Substitute $x=6$ into $DE=x+3$ to answer the actual question (DE, not x).

Substitute

$$DE=x+3=6+3=9\text{ cm}.$$

Sense check. Check the ratio: $\dfrac{AB}{AD}=\dfrac{4}{6}=\dfrac23$ and $\dfrac{BC}{DE}=\dfrac{6}{9}=\dfrac23$ — equal, so the similarity holds. Confirms Option C.

$\boxed{DE=9\text{ cm}}$ — Option C.
