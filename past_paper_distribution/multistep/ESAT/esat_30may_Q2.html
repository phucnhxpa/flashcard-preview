Q2 · Fraction of a cylinder filled by an inscribed sphere

Question

A solid sphere of radius $r$ fits inside a hollow cylinder. The cylinder has the same internal diameter and length as the diameter of the sphere. The volume of a sphere is $\tfrac{4}{3}\pi r^{3}$, where $r$ is the radius of the sphere. What fraction of the space inside the cylinder is taken up by the sphere?

A. $\dfrac14$    B. $\dfrac13$    C. $\dfrac12$    D. $\dfrac23$    E. $\dfrac34$

[1 marks]

Solution [1 marks] · 3 steps

The sphere has radius $r$, so its diameter is $2r$. The cylinder shares that diameter and length, so the cylinder has radius $r$ and height $h=2r$. Given $V_{\text{sphere}}=\tfrac43\pi r^{3}$.
STEP 1/3cylinder volume

Strategy. Compute the cylinder volume using $h=2r$ — the height equals the sphere’s diameter, not its radius.

Formula

$$V_{\text{cyl}}=\pi r^{2}h.$$

Substitute $h=2r$

$$V_{\text{cyl}}=\pi r^{2}\times2r=2\pi r^{3}.$$
Common trap (height is 2r). The cylinder length equals the sphere’s diameter $2r$, not its radius. Using $h=r$ gives $V_{\text{cyl}}=\pi r^{3}$ and a fraction of $\tfrac43>1$ — impossible, which is the tell-tale of this slip.

Sense check. $2\pi r^{3}>\tfrac43\pi r^{3}$, so the cylinder is larger than the sphere — the fraction must be below 1.

$V_{\text{cyl}}=2\pi r^{3}$.
↳ from STEP 2: $V_{\text{cyl}}=2\pi r^{3}$; given $V_{\text{sphere}}=\tfrac43\pi r^{3}$.
STEP 2/3form fraction

Strategy. Form the ratio of sphere volume to cylinder volume and cancel the common factor $\pi r^{3}$ before any arithmetic.

Set up

$$\text{fraction}=\dfrac{V_{\text{sphere}}}{V_{\text{cyl}}}=\dfrac{\tfrac43\pi r^{3}}{2\pi r^{3}}.$$

Cancel $\pi r^{3}$

$$=\dfrac{4/3}{2}.$$
Technique (cancel early). Cancel the common $\pi r^{3}$ immediately — the answer is independent of $r$, so carrying it is wasted effort and a rounding risk.

Sense check. Cancelling leaves a pure number, confirming the fraction does not depend on $r$.

$\text{fraction}=\dfrac{4/3}{2}$.
↳ from STEP 3: $\text{fraction}=\dfrac{4/3}{2}$.
STEP 3/3divide

Strategy. Divide the fractions with the ‘burger method’ (multiply by the reciprocal).

Burger method: $\dfrac{a/b}{c}=\dfrac{a}{bc}$

$$\dfrac{4/3}{2}=\dfrac43\times\dfrac12=\dfrac46=\dfrac23.$$

Sense check. $\tfrac23\approx0.67<1$ — the sphere fills two-thirds of the cylinder, Archimedes’ classical result. Confirms Option D.

$\boxed{\text{fraction}=\dfrac23}$ — Option D (Archimedes).
