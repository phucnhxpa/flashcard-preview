Q5 · Percentage change in P when Q rises 40%

Question

Two variables are connected by the relation $P\propto\dfrac{1}{Q^{2}}$. $Q$ is increased by 40%. What is the percentage change in $P$ to the nearest whole number?

A. 29% decrease    B. 44% decrease    C. 49% decrease    D. 51% decrease    E. 80% decrease    F. 96% decrease

[1 marks]

Solution [1 marks] · 3 steps

algebra-only; the bar comparison (above) just visualises the result. Write the proportion as an equation, scale $Q$ by the exact fraction $\tfrac75$ for $+40\%$, and compare $P_{\text{new}}$ with $P_{\text{old}}$.
STEP 1/3equation

Strategy. Turn the proportionality into an equation with a constant $k$, and write the new $Q$ as an exact fraction.

Proportion to equation

$$P=\dfrac{k}{Q^{2}}.$$

New $Q$ (exact)

$$Q_{\text{new}}=1.4\,Q=\dfrac{14}{10}Q=\dfrac{7}{5}Q.$$
Technique (use 7/5, not 1.4). Keep $\tfrac75$ exact: $\left(\tfrac75\right)^2=\tfrac{49}{25}$ gives an exact answer, whereas $1.4^2=1.96$ then $1/1.96$ risks premature rounding.

Sense check. $+40\%$ multiplies $Q$ by $1.4=\tfrac75>1$, so $Q$ grows — and since $P\propto1/Q^2$, $P$ must shrink.

$P=\dfrac{k}{Q^{2}}$, $\;Q_{\text{new}}=\dfrac75Q$.
↳ from STEP 2: $P=\dfrac{k}{Q^{2}}$, $Q_{\text{new}}=\dfrac75Q$.
STEP 2/3new P

Strategy. Substitute the new $Q$ into $P$ and express $P_{\text{new}}$ as a multiple of $P_{\text{old}}=k/Q^{2}$.

Substitute

$$P_{\text{new}}=\dfrac{k}{\left(\tfrac75Q\right)^{2}}=\dfrac{k}{\tfrac{49}{25}Q^{2}}=\dfrac{25}{49}\cdot\dfrac{k}{Q^{2}}.$$

Recognise $P_{\text{old}}=k/Q^{2}$

$$P_{\text{new}}=\dfrac{25}{49}\,P_{\text{old}}.$$

Sense check. $\tfrac{25}{49}\approx0.51$, so just over half of $P$ survives — matching the ‘51% left’ bar.

$P_{\text{new}}=\dfrac{25}{49}P_{\text{old}}$.
↳ from STEP 3: $P_{\text{new}}=\dfrac{25}{49}P_{\text{old}}$.
STEP 3/3percent change

Strategy. Convert the ratio into a percentage change with $\left(\tfrac{P_{\text{new}}}{P_{\text{old}}}-1\right)\times100\%$.

Percentage change

$$\%\Delta P=\left(\dfrac{25}{49}-1\right)\times100\%=-\dfrac{24}{49}\times100\%\approx-48.98\%.$$
Common trap (asks for CHANGE, not new value). Report the change $\tfrac{25}{49}-1=-\tfrac{24}{49}\approx-49\%$, not the surviving $51\%$. Option D (51% decrease) is the trap for students who subtract the wrong way.

Sense check. $-48.98\%$ rounds to a $49\%$ decrease — between ‘half gone’ and ‘all gone’, sensible for squaring a $1.4\times$ growth. Confirms Option C.

$\boxed{\approx49\%\text{ decrease}}$ — Option C.
