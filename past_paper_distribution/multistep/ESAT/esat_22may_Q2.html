Q2 · Perpendicular from the hypotenuse (similar triangles)

Question

In a right-angled triangle $PQR$, the hypotenuse is the side $PR$. The length of side $PQ$ is 20 cm and the ratio $RQ:PQ$ is $1:2$. What is the length of the perpendicular from the hypotenuse to the point $Q$?

A. $\dfrac{8}{\sqrt5}$ cm    B. $10\sqrt2$ cm    C. $2\sqrt5$ cm    D. $5\sqrt2$ cm    E. $4\sqrt5$ cm

[1 marks]

Solution [1 marks] · 3 steps

Right angle at $Q$. $PQ=20$ cm. The ratio $RQ:PQ=1:2$ gives $RQ=\tfrac12\times20=10$ cm. The foot of the perpendicular from $Q$ onto hypotenuse $PR$ is $T$; the required length is $QT$.
STEP 1/3hypotenuse

Strategy. Find the hypotenuse $PR$ by 2D Pythagoras on the two legs $PQ$ and $RQ$.

Formula

$$PR^{2}=PQ^{2}+RQ^{2}.$$

Substitute

$$PR^{2}=20^{2}+10^{2}=400+100=500$$$$PR=\sqrt{500}=\sqrt{100\times5}=10\sqrt5.$$

Sense check. $10\sqrt5\approx22.4$ cm, longer than either leg ($20$ and $10$) — correct for a hypotenuse.

$PR=10\sqrt5$ cm.
↳ from STEP 2: $PR=10\sqrt5$ cm; legs $PQ=20$, $RQ=10$.
STEP 2/3altitude

Strategy. Compute the altitude with the two-area identity: the same triangle area equals $\tfrac12\,(\text{legs})$ and $\tfrac12\,(\text{hyp}\times\text{altitude})$. This is faster than the full similarity argument.

Formula (equate two area expressions)

$$\tfrac12\,PQ\cdot RQ=\tfrac12\,PR\cdot QT\;\Rightarrow\; QT=\dfrac{PQ\cdot RQ}{PR}.$$

Substitute

$$QT=\dfrac{20\times10}{10\sqrt5}=\dfrac{200}{10\sqrt5}=\dfrac{20}{\sqrt5}.$$
Why similar triangles work. Dropping a perpendicular from the right angle creates $\triangle PQR\sim\triangle PTQ\sim\triangle QTR$ (AA). The altitude formula $QT=\tfrac{PQ\cdot RQ}{PR}$ is exactly the similarity ratio, but the equal-area route reaches it in one line.

Sense check. $QT=\tfrac{20}{\sqrt5}\approx8.9$ cm — shorter than both legs, as an altitude inside the triangle must be.

$QT=\dfrac{20}{\sqrt5}$ cm (not yet rationalised).
↳ from STEP 3: $QT=\dfrac{20}{\sqrt5}$ cm.
STEP 3/3rationalise

Strategy. Rationalise the denominator so the answer matches the surd-in-numerator form used by every option.

Rationalise

$$QT=\dfrac{20}{\sqrt5}\cdot\dfrac{\sqrt5}{\sqrt5}=\dfrac{20\sqrt5}{5}=4\sqrt5.$$
Technique (sticky note). ‘Rationalise the denominator.’ ESAT options never leave $\sqrt{}$ on the bottom — the un-rationalised $20/\sqrt5$ is not in the list, so always rationalise before scanning.

Sense check. $4\sqrt5\approx8.94$ cm, identical to $20/\sqrt5$ — only the form changed, confirming Option E.

$\boxed{QT=4\sqrt5\text{ cm}}$ — Option E.
