Q4 · Combine the ratios Q:R and R:S

Question

The ratio of $Q:R$ is $5:2$ and the ratio of $R:S$ is $3:10$. Which one of the following gives the ratio $Q:S$ in its simplest form?

A. $1:2$    B. $2:1$    C. $3:4$    D. $3:25$    E. $4:3$    F. $25:3$

[1 marks]

Solution [1 marks] · 3 steps

algebra-only; a quick number-line anchor (above) is enough — no scale diagram needed. The two ratios share the letter $R$, but $R$ is measured in different units in each ($R=2$ in the first, $R=3$ in the second). To chain them, rescale so $R$ takes the same value in both, then the three quantities live on one common scale and $Q:S$ can be read off directly.
STEP 1/3match R

Strategy. Line the two ratios up vertically with $R$ in the same column. A ratio is unchanged when every term is multiplied by the same number ($a:b=ka:kb$), so scale each ratio to make the shared $R$ equal to the lowest common multiple of its two values.

Identify the shared term

$$Q:R=5:\underline{2},\qquad R:S=\underline{3}:10.$$

LCM of the two R-values

$$\operatorname{lcm}(2,3)=6.$$

Scale each ratio so $R=6$ (multiply EVERY term)

$$Q:R=5:2\;\xrightarrow{\times3}\;15:6,$$$$R:S=3:10\;\xrightarrow{\times2}\;6:20.$$

Sense check. $2\times3=6$ and $3\times2=6$: both rows now show $R=6$, so they describe the same $R$ and can be merged into a single chain.

$Q:R=15:6$ and $R:S=6:20$.
↳ from STEP 2: $Q:R:S=15:6:20$.
STEP 2/3combine

Strategy. With $R$ equal at $6$ in both rows, stitch the two ratios into a single three-way ratio, then extract the $Q:S$ pair by ignoring the middle term.

Combine (R is the bridge)

$$Q:R:S=15:\underline{6}:20$$

Extract $Q:S$ (drop $R$)

$$Q:S=15:20.$$
Phuc's mistake (≈46:00). When scaling $R:S=3:10$ by $\times2$, Phuc multiplied $R$ to $6$ but left $S=10$, giving $Q:S=15:10=3:2$. Scaling a ratio means multiplying both numbers — circle the multiplier and draw an arrow to each term.

Sense check. Reading off the outer pair $15:20$ correctly drops the middle term $R$; the answer is independent of how $R$ was scaled, which is exactly why $R$ had to be made equal first.

$Q:S=15:20$.
↳ from STEP 3: $Q:S=15:20$.
STEP 3/3simplify

Strategy. Divide both parts by their highest common factor to reach simplest form, then match against the options.

Highest common factor

$$\gcd(15,20)=5.$$

Simplify ($\div5$)

$$Q:S=15:20=\dfrac{15}{5}:\dfrac{20}{5}=3:4.$$
Verify by elimination. $Q$ is the larger quantity per unit of $R$ ($5$ vs $2$) but $S$ is much larger ($10$ vs $3$), so overall $S>Q$ and the ratio $Q:S$ must be ‘small : large’. That rules out $4:3$, $2:1$ and $25:3$ immediately, leaving $3:4$ as the only simplest small:large match.
Alternative route (fractions). Treat ratios as fractions: $\dfrac{Q}{R}=\dfrac52$ and $\dfrac{R}{S}=\dfrac{3}{10}$, so $\dfrac{Q}{S}=\dfrac{Q}{R}\times\dfrac{R}{S}=\dfrac52\times\dfrac{3}{10}=\dfrac{15}{20}=\dfrac34$, i.e. $Q:S=3:4$ — the same answer in one multiplication. The $R$ cancels, mirroring why it had to be matched in the vertical method.
Exam pace. At $\approx90$ s per ESAT question the fraction route is the one to drill: two multiplications and a cancel, well under $30$ s.

Sense check. $3:4$ has no common factor, so it is fully simplified — Option C. The incorrect $3:2$ from the un-scaled-$S$ slip is not even on the list, which is itself a warning sign that $S$ was forgotten.

$\boxed{Q:S=3:4}$ — Option C.
