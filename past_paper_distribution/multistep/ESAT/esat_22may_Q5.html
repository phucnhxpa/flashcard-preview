Q5 · Angle between the hands at 9:45

Question

If you look at a clock and the time is 9:45, what is the angle between the hour and the minute hands?

A. $0°$    B. $7.5°$    C. $15°$    D. $22.5°$    E. $30°$

[1 marks]

Solution [1 marks] · 3 steps

A full turn is $360°$; each hour mark is $\tfrac{360°}{12}=30°$. Measure both hands as degrees clockwise from the 12, then subtract. Key fact: the hour hand moves continuously, not in jumps.
STEP 1/3minute hand

Strategy. Locate the minute hand. At $45$ minutes it points at the 9, three-quarters of the way round from 12.

Formula

$$\theta_{\text{min}}=\dfrac{M}{60}\times360°=6M.$$

Substitute $M=45$

$$\theta_{\text{min}}=\dfrac{45}{60}\times360°=270°.$$

Sense check. $270°$ is exactly the 9-o’clock direction (three-quarters of $360°$) — matches the diagram.

Minute hand at $270°$ from 12.
↳ from STEP 2: minute hand at $270°$. Each hour mark $=30°$.
STEP 2/3hour hand

Strategy. Locate the hour hand. At 9:45 it is three-quarters of the way from the 9 to the 10, NOT sitting on the 9.

Formula

$$\theta_{\text{hour}}=30H+0.5M.$$

Substitute $H=9,\;M=45$

$$\theta_{\text{hour}}=9\times30°+\dfrac{45}{60}\times30°=270°+22.5°=292.5°.$$
Phuc's mistake (stationary hour hand). The natural (wrong) reading Harry corrected: at ‘9:45’ the hour hand sits on the 9. Harry’s key point — ‘the hour hand does not jump between numbers, it moves continuously.’ Assuming it is stationary gives $270°-270°=0°$ (Option A, the trap). At $45$ min it has advanced $\tfrac34\times30°=22.5°$ past the 9.

Sense check. $292.5°$ sits just past the 9 toward the 10 — exactly where the short hand is drawn.

Hour hand at $292.5°$ from 12.
↳ from STEP 3: minute $=270°$, hour $=292.5°$.
STEP 3/3subtract

Strategy. Subtract the two positions to get the angle, taking the smaller value (under $180°$).

Subtract

$$\theta=\theta_{\text{hour}}-\theta_{\text{min}}=292.5°-270°=22.5°.$$

Check with the shortcut

$$\theta=|30H-5.5M|=|270-247.5|=22.5°.$$

Sense check. $22.5°<180°$, so it is already the smaller angle; both methods agree, confirming Option D. $7.5°$ would only arise if the hand had moved a quarter-hour, not three-quarters.

$\boxed{\theta=22.5°}$ — Option D.
