01The rules of the game

How grades actually work.

Each unit is marked out of 75 raw, then converted to a 100-point UMS scale. Six units add up to 600 UMS per qualification — Math (P1+P2+P3+P4+M1+S1) and FM (FP1+FP2+FP3+P3+P4+S2). That conversion hides everything that matters.

Goal A — A* Math + A FM

  • Math A: aggregate ≥ 480/600 UMS
  • A* gate: P3+P4 ≥ 180/200 UMS
  • FM A: aggregate ≥ 480/600 UMS — no A* lever needed
  • Effective targets: P3, P4 → 90 UMS; all others → 80 UMS

Goal B — A* Math + A* FM

  • Math A* gate: same as above
  • FM A* gate: best 3 A2 units (P3, P4, FP2, FP3, S2) ≥ 270/300 UMS
  • Five A2 units now operate at the 90-UMS lever
  • Effective targets: P3, P4, FP2, FP3, S2 → 90 UMS; rest → 80 UMS
The trap: A* isn't a single threshold — it's aggregate floor + A2 lever. You can score 540/600 and still miss A* if P3+P4 < 180/200. The lever units have asymmetric weight even though they're 1/6 of the aggregate.
02Convert UMS to raw marks

UMS lies. Raw marks are real.

To score 90 UMS on P3 you don't need 90% — the boundary curve is steeper at the top. P3's A* threshold sits at 65/75 raw; the gap from A→A* is only 9 raw marks. This is the entire margin you're optimising for.

03The chapter distribution lens

Where do those raw marks come from?

Now we know the targets. The question is — which chapters carry the marks? P3 is the simplest case: only 6 chapters, but the top 5 cover 92.7% of every paper on average. Master those, and you've already cleared 90 UMS.

It's the same shape for every unit, but the curve is steeper for some. S2 is the most concentrated — its top 3 chapters average 84.0% of every paper. That makes S2 ch2 (PDF/CDF) at 34.7% the single highest-priority chapter under Goal B.

The implication: for every unit, there's a small handful of chapters that have to be solid before anything else matters. The top-N coverage curve tells you exactly how many that is.

05The priority formula

One number per chapter.

To compare a P4 chapter against an S2 chapter against an FP3 chapter, we need a single score. The formula collapses four signals — mark share, frequency, criticality (how close the target is to a unit's ceiling), and consistency — into one priority number.

priority  =  mark_share  ×  frequency  ×  100100 − UMS_target  ×  consistency

The 100 / (100 − target) term is the lever. If your target is 80 UMS, that's 100/20 = 5. If it's 90 UMS, it's 100/10 = 10. Criticality doubles when you switch on the A* lever. That's why a chapter that ranks 7th in Goal A can become 1st in Goal B without its mark share changing at all.

07Concrete recommendations

Where the hours actually go.

Goal A · A* Math + A FM

The safe play

P3 and P4 carry the A* gate. Spend two-thirds of mastery time inside Pure A2.

    Master these 5 → secured 90 UMS lever in P3+P4. Combined with 80-UMS targets elsewhere, that's an A* in Math and a comfortable A in FM.
    Risk: if P3 or P4 slips below 90 UMS, the math A* gate fails even at 540/600 aggregate.
    Goal B · A* Math + A* FM

    The stretch

    Five A2 units now matter. S2 ch2 alone is 34.7% of every S2 paper — it's the single highest-priority chapter overall.

      Master these 5 → still need ≥90 UMS on three of (P3, P4, FP2, FP3, S2). One slip into the high-80s in two of those, and Goal B fails.
      Risk: Goal B has five 90-UMS bets. Probability that all five clear is structurally lower — Goal A is the safer all-or-nothing trade.

      Anchor chapters — matter equally for both goals

      Eight chapters whose priority rank doesn't change between Goal A and Goal B. These are the foundation — invest first, regardless of which goal you commit to.
