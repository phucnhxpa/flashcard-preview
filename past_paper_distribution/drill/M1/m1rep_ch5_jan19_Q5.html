Jan 2019 Q5 — Box B (6 kg) hangs from ropes BP and BQ; find tensions [10m]

Question

A box of mass $6$ kg is attached at $B$ to two ropes $BP$ and $BQ$. Fixed points $P$ and $Q$ are on a horizontal ceiling with $PQ = 3.5$ m. The box hangs in equilibrium $2$ m below the ceiling. Angle $BQP = 45\u00b0$. ($g = 9.8$ m/s².)

(i) Find the tension in $BP$. [5]

(ii) Find the tension in $BQ$. [5]

[10 marks]

Solution [10 marks] · 3 steps

STEP 1/3

Strategy: the question gives the angle at $Q$ ($\angle BQP = 45\u00b0$) but not the angle at $P$. Deduce it from the geometry.

Drop a perpendicular from $B$ to the ceiling line $PQ$; call the foot $D$. The vertical distance $BD = 2$ m.

At Q (right-angle triangle $BQD$, $\angle BQP = 45\u00b0$):

$$QD = \frac{BD}{\tan 45\u00b0} = \frac{2}{1} = 2\;\text{m}$$

At P: $PD = PQ - QD = 3.5 - 2 = 1.5$ m.

$$\tan\alpha = \frac{BD}{PD} = \frac{2}{1.5} = \frac{4}{3}$$

This is a $3$-$4$-$5$ triangle: $\sin\alpha = \frac{4}{5}$, $\cos\alpha = \frac{3}{5}$.

$$\boxed{\;\text{Angle of } BP \text{ with horizontal} = \alpha,\; \sin\alpha = \tfrac{4}{5};\quad \text{Angle of } BQ = 45\u00b0\;}$$

Why find the angle at P? To resolve the tensions into horizontal and vertical components, I need the angle each rope makes with the horizontal. The problem gave $\angle BQP$ (at Q), which equals the angle the rope makes with the horizontal line $PQ$.
$\angle BP$: $\sin\alpha = 4/5$, $\cos\alpha = 3/5$; $\angle BQ = 45\u00b0$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $\sin\alpha = 4/5$, $\cos\alpha = 3/5$; angle at Q = 45°.
STEP 2/3

Strategy: particle at $B$ is in equilibrium under three forces: $T_{BP}$ along $BP$ (up and to the right), $T_{BQ}$ along $BQ$ (up and to the left), and weight $6g$ downward. Apply $\Sigma F_x = 0$ and $\Sigma F_y = 0$.

Horizontal equilibrium (rightward = positive, $T_{BP}$ has a rightward component, $T_{BQ}$ has a leftward component):

$$T_{BP}\cos\alpha - T_{BQ}\cos 45\u00b0 = 0$$

$$T_{BP} \cdot \frac{3}{5} = T_{BQ} \cdot \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \quad \cdots(1)$$

Express $T_{BQ}$ in terms of $T_{BP}$:

$$T_{BQ} = \frac{3}{5} \cdot \sqrt{2} \cdot T_{BP} = \frac{3\sqrt{2}}{5}\,T_{BP}$$

$T_{BQ} = \dfrac{3\sqrt{2}}{5}\,T_{BP}$
↳ from STEP 2:From STEP 2: $T_{BQ} = 3\sqrt{2}\,T_{BP}/5$.
STEP 3/3

Vertical equilibrium (upward = positive):

$$T_{BP}\sin\alpha + T_{BQ}\sin 45\u00b0 = 6g$$

Substitute $\sin\alpha = 4/5$, $\sin 45\u00b0 = 1/\sqrt{2}$, and $T_{BQ} = \frac{3\sqrt{2}}{5}T_{BP}$:

$$T_{BP}\cdot\frac{4}{5} + \frac{3\sqrt{2}}{5}T_{BP} \cdot \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} = 6g$$

$$T_{BP}\left(\frac{4}{5} + \frac{3}{5}\right) = 6g$$

$$T_{BP}\cdot\frac{7}{5} = 6 \times 9.8 = 58.8$$

$$T_{BP} = \frac{58.8 \times 5}{7} = \frac{294}{7} = 42\;\text{N}$$

$$T_{BQ} = \frac{3\sqrt{2}}{5} \times 42 = \frac{126\sqrt{2}}{5} = 25.2\sqrt{2} \approx 35.6\;\text{N}$$

$$\boxed{\;T_{BP} = 42\;\text{N}, \quad T_{BQ} = 25.2\sqrt{2} \approx 35.6\;\text{N}\;}$$

Elegance check: the $\sqrt{2}$ from $T_{BQ}$ and the $1/\sqrt{2}$ from $\sin 45\u00b0$ cancel perfectly in the vertical equation, leaving a clean $3/5$ fraction — this is why 3-4-5 geometry is so common in exam questions.
$\boxed{T_{BP} = 42\,\text{N};\; T_{BQ} = 25.2\sqrt{2} \approx 35.6\,\text{N}}$
