Q6 — Agatha & Brionie Roller-Skating: Relative Position & Minimum Distance

Question

Agatha: initial position $(11\mathbf{i}+11\mathbf{j})$ m, velocity $(3\mathbf{i}-\mathbf{j})$ m s$^{-1}$.

Brionie: initial position $(7\mathbf{i}+16\mathbf{j})$ m, velocity $(4\mathbf{i}-2\mathbf{j})$ m s$^{-1}$.

(a) Position vector of Agatha at time $t$ seconds. [2]

(b) Show Brionie also passes through point P (where Agatha is at $t=6$); find this $t$. [4]

(c) Show $\vec{AB} = [(t-4)\mathbf{i}+(5-t)\mathbf{j}]$ m. [2]

(d) Find the distance between the girls when closest together. [4]

[12 marks]

Part (a) — Position of Agatha [2 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 M1 A1

Strategy: Constant velocity motion: position = initial position + velocity × time.

Formula (Constant Velocity Position): $\mathbf{r}(t) = \mathbf{r}_0 + t\mathbf{v}$

Initial position: $\mathbf{r}_{A0} = 11\mathbf{i}+11\mathbf{j}$

Velocity: $\mathbf{v}_A = 3\mathbf{i}-\mathbf{j}$

$\mathbf{r}_A = (11\mathbf{i}+11\mathbf{j}) + t(3\mathbf{i}-\mathbf{j})$

$= (11+3t)\mathbf{i} + (11-t)\mathbf{j}$ m

Pitfall: Writing $\mathbf{r}_A = (11+3)t\mathbf{i}+…$ — forgetting that 11 is the initial position and $3t$ is the displacement. They add, not multiply: $(11+3t)$, not $14t$.
$\mathbf{r}_A = (11+3t)\mathbf{i}+(11-t)\mathbf{j}$ m

Part (b) — Brionie passes through P [4 marks] · 1 step

From (a): $\mathbf{r}_A = (11+3t)\mathbf{i}+(11-t)\mathbf{j}$. Find P then verify B reaches P.
STEP 1/1 M1

Strategy: Find point P = Agatha's position at $t=6$, then write Brionie's position vector and equate components to P.

Point P at $t=6$: $\mathbf{r}_P = (11+18)\mathbf{i}+(11-6)\mathbf{j} = 29\mathbf{i}+5\mathbf{j}$

Brionie's position: $\mathbf{r}_B = (7\mathbf{i}+16\mathbf{j}) + t(4\mathbf{i}-2\mathbf{j})$

$= (7+4t)\mathbf{i}+(16-2t)\mathbf{j}$

Set $\mathbf{r}_B = \mathbf{r}_P = 29\mathbf{i}+5\mathbf{j}$:

$\mathbf{i}$: $7+4t=29 \Rightarrow t=5.5$

$\mathbf{j}$: $16-2t=5 \Rightarrow t=5.5$ ✓ (consistent)

Pitfall: Only checking one component and concluding "shown". Must verify both i and j components give the same $t$ — if only one equation used, the mark is lost. E.g. if only i-component: could pass through the right East position at a different height.
Brionie passes through P at $t = 5.5$ s

Part (c) — Show $\vec{AB}=(t-4)\mathbf{i}+(5-t)\mathbf{j}$ [2 marks] · 1 step

From (a)/(b): $\mathbf{r}_A=(11+3t)\mathbf{i}+(11-t)\mathbf{j}$; $\mathbf{r}_B=(7+4t)\mathbf{i}+(16-2t)\mathbf{j}$.
STEP 1/1 M1 A1

Strategy: $\vec{AB} = \mathbf{r}_B - \mathbf{r}_A$. Subtract component by component.

$\mathbf{i}$: $(7+4t)-(11+3t) = 7+4t-11-3t = t-4$ ✓

$\mathbf{j}$: $(16-2t)-(11-t) = 16-2t-11+t = 5-t$ ✓

$\vec{AB} = (t-4)\mathbf{i}+(5-t)\mathbf{j}$ m $\quad \checkmark$ (shown)

Pitfall: Sign errors when subtracting $(11-t)$: writing $-11-t$ instead of $-11+t$. Always distribute the minus sign: $-(11-t) = -11+t$. Check: at $t=4$, $\vec{AB}=0\mathbf{i}+1\mathbf{j}$ (Agatha is 1 m south of Brionie). At $t=5$, $\vec{AB}=1\mathbf{i}+0\mathbf{j}$ (directly east).
$\vec{AB} = (t-4)\mathbf{i}+(5-t)\mathbf{j}$ m (shown)

Part (d) — Minimum distance [4 marks] · 2 steps

From (c): $\vec{AB}=(t-4)\mathbf{i}+(5-t)\mathbf{j}$. Minimise $|\vec{AB}|$.
STEP 1/2 M1

Strategy: Minimise $D^2 = |\vec{AB}|^2$ (easier than $D$ as avoids square roots). Differentiate and set to zero.

Formula: $D^2 = (t-4)^2 + (5-t)^2$

$D^2 = t^2-8t+16 + 25-10t+t^2 = 2t^2-18t+41$

$\dfrac{dD^2}{dt} = 4t - 18 = 0$

$t^* = \dfrac{18}{4} = 4.5$ s

Pitfall: Differentiating $D$ (not $D^2$) — requires chain rule and gets messy. Since $D\geq0$, minimising $D^2$ is equivalent and far cleaner. Also: forgetting to expand $(5-t)^2$ as $25-10t+t^2$, not $25-t^2$.
$t^* = 4.5$ s
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: minimum occurs at $t=4.5$ s
STEP 2/2 M1 A1 A1

Strategy: Substitute $t=4.5$ into $\vec{AB}$ and compute $|\vec{AB}|$.

$\vec{AB}\big|_{t=4.5} = (4.5-4)\mathbf{i}+(5-4.5)\mathbf{j} = 0.5\mathbf{i}+0.5\mathbf{j}$

$D_{\min}^2 = 0.5^2+0.5^2 = 0.25+0.25 = 0.5$

$D_{\min} = \sqrt{0.5} = \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}} = \dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$ m

Check: $2t^2-18t+41$ at $t=4.5$: $2(20.25)-81+41=40.5-81+41=0.5$ ✓

Pitfall: Giving the answer as $D^2=0.5$ instead of $D=\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\approx 0.707$ m. Always take the square root at the end — the question asks for the distance, not distance-squared.
$\boxed{D_{\min} = \dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \approx 0.707 \text{ m}}$
