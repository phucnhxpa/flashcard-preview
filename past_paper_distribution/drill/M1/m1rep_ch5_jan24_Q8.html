Jan 2024 Q8 — Find μ (equilibrium); string cut: find acceleration and speed [12m]

Question

Particle $P$ (mass $m$) rests on a rough inclined plane, angle $\alpha$ where $\sin\alpha = \tfrac{5}{13}$, $\cos\alpha = \tfrac{12}{13}$. A light inextensible string connects $P$ up the slope, over a smooth pulley, to particle $Q$ (mass $\tfrac{m}{2}$) hanging freely. System is in limiting equilibrium with $P$ on the point of sliding up the slope.

(a) Find the coefficient of friction $\mu$. [8]

The string is now cut. $P$ starts from rest and slides down the slope, reaching a ledge after sliding 0.8 m.

(b) Show that $P$ slides down after the string is cut. Find the acceleration of $P$ and its speed when it reaches the ledge. [4]

[12 marks]

(a) — Find the coefficient of friction μ [8 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 M1A1 (perp) + M1A1 (parallel P) + B1 (Q equation) + B1 (F=μR)

Strategy: Limiting equilibrium → friction at maximum $F = \mu R$. P about to slide UP → friction acts DOWN the slope.

For particle Q (hanging, equilibrium):

$$T = \frac{mg}{2}$$

For particle P — perpendicular to slope (no acceleration):

$$R = mg\cos\alpha = mg \cdot \frac{12}{13} = \frac{12mg}{13}$$

For particle P — parallel to slope (up = positive, $a=0$, friction acts down because P is about to slide up):

$$T - mg\sin\alpha - F = 0$$

$$\frac{mg}{2} - mg\cdot\frac{5}{13} - \mu \cdot \frac{12mg}{13} = 0$$

Divide by $mg$:

$$\frac{1}{2} - \frac{5}{13} = \frac{12\mu}{13} \implies \frac{3}{26} = \frac{12\mu}{13} \implies \mu = \frac{1}{8}$$

Watch: Since P is about to slide UP, friction acts DOWN (opposing tendency). If you get $\mu < 0$, you have the friction direction wrong. Also: $F = \mu R$ only at limiting equilibrium.
$\boxed{\mu = \dfrac{1}{8}}$

(b) — After string cut: show P slides down, find acceleration and speed after 0.8 m [4 marks] · 2 steps

From (a): $\mu = \tfrac{1}{8}$; $R = \tfrac{12mg}{13}$; P starts from rest.
STEP 1/2 M1A1 (N2L equation + correct trig)

Check slide direction:

Weight component down slope: $mg\sin\alpha = \tfrac{5mg}{13}$

Max friction (now acting UP if sliding down): $\mu R = \tfrac{1}{8} \cdot \tfrac{12mg}{13} = \tfrac{12mg}{104} = \tfrac{3mg}{26}$

Compare: $\tfrac{5}{13} = \tfrac{10}{26} > \tfrac{3}{26}$ → weight component exceeds max friction → P slides down. ✓

Newton's 2nd Law parallel to slope (down = positive):

$$mg\sin\alpha - \mu R = ma$$

$$mg\cdot\frac{5}{13} - \frac{1}{8}\cdot\frac{12mg}{13} = ma$$

$$mg\left(\frac{40}{104} - \frac{12}{104}\right) = ma \implies a = \frac{28g}{104} = \frac{7g}{26} \approx 2.64 \text{ m/s}^2$$

$a = \dfrac{7g}{26} \approx 2.64 \text{ m/s}^2$ (down slope)
↳ from STEP 1:$a = \tfrac{7g}{26}$; $u = 0$; $s = 0.8$ m
STEP 2/2 M1A1

Formula (SUVAT): $v^2 = u^2 + 2as$

$u = 0$ (starts from rest), $a = \tfrac{7g}{26}$, $s = 0.8$ m:

$$v^2 = 0 + 2 \cdot \frac{7g}{26} \cdot 0.8 = \frac{14g \times 0.8}{26} = \frac{11.2g}{26} = \frac{11.2 \times 9.8}{26} = \frac{109.76}{26} \approx 4.22$$

$$v = \sqrt{4.22} \approx 2.05 \text{ m/s}$$

Watch: The sliding distance is 0.8 m (not 1.3 m). Using 1.3 m gives the wrong speed of 2.62 m/s — a common error from misreading the question paper.
$\boxed{v \approx 2.05 \text{ m/s}}$ (2 or 3 s.f.)
