Q2 — Three Forces on Particle in Equilibrium: Find F₃, Angle of Resultant, Mass

Question

[In this question i and j are horizontal perpendicular unit vectors.]

A particle P rests in equilibrium on a smooth horizontal plane. A system of three forces, $\mathbf{F}_1$ N, $\mathbf{F}_2$ N and $\mathbf{F}_3$ N where

$$\mathbf{F}_1 = (3c\mathbf{i} + 4c\mathbf{j}) \qquad \mathbf{F}_2 = (-14\mathbf{i} + 7\mathbf{j})$$

is applied to P. Given that P remains in equilibrium,

(a) find $\mathbf{F}_3$ in terms of $c$, $\mathbf{i}$ and $\mathbf{j}$. [2]

The force $\mathbf{F}_3$ is removed from the system. Given that $c = 2$,

(b) find the size of the angle between the direction of $\mathbf{i}$ and the direction of the resultant force acting on P. [4]

The mass of P is $m$ kg. Given that the magnitude of the acceleration of P is $8.5\ \text{m s}^{-2}$,

(c) find the value of $m$. [4]

[10 marks total]

Part (a) — Find F₃ in terms of c [2 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 M1 A1

For equilibrium: $\mathbf{F}_1 + \mathbf{F}_2 + \mathbf{F}_3 = \mathbf{0}$

$$\mathbf{F}_3 = -(\mathbf{F}_1 + \mathbf{F}_2)$$

$\mathbf{i}$-component: $-(3c + (-14)) = (14 - 3c)$

$\mathbf{j}$-component: $-(4c + 7) = (-4c - 7)$

$$\mathbf{F}_3 = (14 - 3c)\mathbf{i} + (-4c - 7)\mathbf{j}\ \text{N}$$

$\mathbf{F}_3 = (14 - 3c)\mathbf{i} - (4c + 7)\mathbf{j}\ \text{N}$

Part (b) — Angle between i and resultant (c = 2, F₃ removed) [4 marks] · 2 steps

Setup: $\mathbf{F}_3$ removed; only $\mathbf{F}_1$ and $\mathbf{F}_2$ act. Set $c = 2$.
STEP 1/2 M1 A1

With $c = 2$: $\mathbf{F}_1 = (6\mathbf{i} + 8\mathbf{j})$, $\mathbf{F}_2 = (-14\mathbf{i} + 7\mathbf{j})$

Resultant $\mathbf{R} = \mathbf{F}_1 + \mathbf{F}_2$:

$\mathbf{i}$: $6 + (-14) = -8$

$\mathbf{j}$: $8 + 7 = 15$

$$\mathbf{R} = -8\mathbf{i} + 15\mathbf{j}\ \text{N}$$

$\mathbf{R} = -8\mathbf{i} + 15\mathbf{j}\ \text{N}$
↳ from STEP 1: $\mathbf{R} = -8\mathbf{i} + 15\mathbf{j}$
STEP 2/2 M1 A1

Angle $\theta$ between $\mathbf{i}$ direction (positive $x$-axis) and $\mathbf{R}$:

$$\tan\theta = \frac{|j\text{-component}|}{|i\text{-component}|} = \frac{15}{8}$$

The resultant is in the 2nd quadrant ($-\mathbf{i}$, $+\mathbf{j}$).

Reference angle: $\arctan\!\left(\frac{15}{8}\right) \approx 61.93°$

Angle from positive $\mathbf{i}$: $180° - 61.93° \approx 118.07°$

Pitfall: The question asks for the angle between direction of $\mathbf{i}$ and direction of resultant — this is measured from $+\mathbf{i}$ anticlockwise to $\mathbf{R}$, so the obtuse angle $\approx 118°$.
Angle $\approx 118°$ (3 s.f.)

Part (c) — Find mass m [4 marks] · 2 steps

From (b): Resultant $\mathbf{R} = -8\mathbf{i} + 15\mathbf{j}$. Magnitude of acceleration $= 8.5\ \text{m s}^{-2}$.
STEP 1/2 M1 A1

Find magnitude of resultant force $|\mathbf{R}|$:

$$|\mathbf{R}| = \sqrt{(-8)^2 + 15^2} = \sqrt{64 + 225} = \sqrt{289} = 17\ \text{N}$$

$|\mathbf{R}| = 17\ \text{N}$
↳ from STEP 1: $|\mathbf{R}| = 17$ N, acceleration $= 8.5$ m s⁻²
STEP 2/2 M1 A1

Apply Newton's 2nd Law: $|\mathbf{R}| = m \times |\mathbf{a}|$

$$17 = m \times 8.5$$

$$m = \frac{17}{8.5} = 2\ \text{kg}$$

$m = 2\ \text{kg}$
