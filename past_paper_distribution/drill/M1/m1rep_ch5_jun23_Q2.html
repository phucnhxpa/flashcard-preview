M1 June 2023 Paper – Question 2

Question

Three forces F1, F2 and F3 act on a particle which is in equilibrium.

F1 = (5i − 2j) N and F3 = (−3i − 2j) N

(a) Find F2.

(b) Find the magnitude of F2.

(c) Find the bearing of F2.

[[10] marks]

(a) [3 marks] · 3 steps

F₁ = (5i − 2j) N, F₃ = (−3i − 2j) N, particle in equilibrium
STEP 1/3 M1

For equilibrium: $\mathbf{F}_1 + \mathbf{F}_2 + \mathbf{F}_3 = \mathbf{0}$

Therefore: $\mathbf{F}_2 = -(\mathbf{F}_1 + \mathbf{F}_3)$

$\mathbf{F}_2 = -(\mathbf{F}_1 + \mathbf{F}_3)$
↳ from STEP 1:$\mathbf{F}_2 = -(\mathbf{F}_1 + \mathbf{F}_3)$
STEP 2/3 A1

$\mathbf{F}_1 + \mathbf{F}_3 = (5\mathbf{i} - 2\mathbf{j}) + (-3\mathbf{i} - 2\mathbf{j})$

$= (5-3)\mathbf{i} + (-2-2)\mathbf{j} = 2\mathbf{i} - 4\mathbf{j}$

$\mathbf{F}_1 + \mathbf{F}_3 = 2\mathbf{i} - 4\mathbf{j}$
↳ from STEP 2:$\mathbf{F}_1 + \mathbf{F}_3 = 2\mathbf{i} - 4\mathbf{j}$
STEP 3/3 A1

$\mathbf{F}_2 = -(2\mathbf{i} - 4\mathbf{j})$

$\mathbf{F}_2 = -2\mathbf{i} + 4\mathbf{j}$

$\boxed{\mathbf{F}_2 = (-2\mathbf{i} + 4\mathbf{j})\text{ N}}$

(b) [2 marks] · 2 steps

F₂ = (−2i + 4j) N
STEP 1/2 M1

For vector $\mathbf{F}_2 = a\mathbf{i} + b\mathbf{j}$:

$|\mathbf{F}_2| = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2}$

$|\mathbf{F}_2| = \sqrt{(-2)^2 + 4^2}$
↳ from STEP 1:$|\mathbf{F}_2| = \sqrt{(-2)^2 + 4^2}$
STEP 2/2 A1

$|\mathbf{F}_2| = \sqrt{4 + 16} = \sqrt{20}$

$= \sqrt{4 \times 5} = 2\sqrt{5}$

$\boxed{|\mathbf{F}_2| = 2\sqrt{5}\text{ N}}$

(c) [5 marks] · 5 steps

F₂ = (−2i + 4j) N
STEP 1/5 M1

$\mathbf{F}_2 = -2\mathbf{i} + 4\mathbf{j}$

The $\mathbf{i}$ component is negative (West) and $\mathbf{j}$ component is positive (North).

So $\mathbf{F}_2$ points into the second quadrant (North-West).

$\mathbf{F}_2$ is in the NW quadrant
↳ from STEP 1:$\mathbf{F}_2$ is in the NW quadrant
STEP 2/5 M1

Reference angle $\alpha = \arctan\left(\frac{|\text{West component}|}{|\text{North component}|}\right)$

$\alpha = \arctan\left(\frac{2}{4}\right) = \arctan(0.5)$

$\alpha = \arctan(0.5)$
↳ from STEP 2:$\alpha = \arctan(0.5)$
STEP 3/5 A1

$\alpha = \arctan(0.5) = 26.565...°$

$\alpha \approx 26.6°$

$\alpha = 26.6°$
↳ from STEP 3:$\alpha = 26.6°$, direction is NW
STEP 4/5 M1

Bearing is measured clockwise from North.

For a direction West of North: Bearing $= 360° - \alpha$

This can also be written as N$26.6°$W.

Bearing $= 360° - 26.6°$
↳ from STEP 4:Bearing $= 360° - 26.6°$
STEP 5/5 A1

Bearing $= 360° - 26.6° = 333.4°$

$\boxed{\text{Bearing} = 333.4° \text{ (or N}26.6°\text{W)}}$
