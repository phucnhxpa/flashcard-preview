Q5 — Car and Motorcyclist: Equal Speed Time, Sketch v-t Graph, Quadratic for Catch-up

Question

Car travels at constant 40 m s$^{-1}$. At $t=0$, car passes motorcyclist at rest.

Motorcyclist: accelerates at 4 m s$^{-2}$ for 15 s, then at 1 m s$^{-2}$ for further $T$ s until catching up.

(a) Sketch speed-time graphs (same axes) from $t=0$ to catch-up. [2]

(b) Find $t_1$ when car and motorcyclist have same speed. [2]

(c) Show $T^2 + kT - 300 = 0$, find $k$. [6]

[10 marks]

Part (a) — Sketch v-t graphs [2 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 B1 B1

Strategy: Car = horizontal line. Motorcyclist = two connected straight segments of different slopes.

Car: Horizontal line at $v=40$ m s$^{-1}$ from $t=0$ to $t=15+T$.

Motorcyclist — Phase 1 ($0\le t\le15$):

Starts at $(0,0)$; gradient $=4$ m s$^{-2}$; ends at $(15, 60)$ since $v=0+4(15)=60$.

Motorcyclist — Phase 2 ($15\le t\le 15+T$):

Starts at $(15,60)$; gradient $=1$ m s$^{-2}$; ends at $(15+T, 60+T)$.

Note: motorcyclist reaches 60 m s$^{-1}$ at $t=15$ — faster than car — so it overtakes eventually.

Pitfall: Drawing motorcyclist's graph as a single straight line from origin. It has TWO phases with different gradients (4 then 1). The gradient CHANGES at $t=15$ s. Mark the kink point $(15, 60)$ explicitly on the sketch.
Car: horizontal at 40; Motorcyclist: $(0,0)\to(15,60)\to(15+T, 60+T)$

Part (b) — Find $t_1$: equal speeds [2 marks] · 1 step

From (a): Motorcyclist reaches 60 m s$^{-1}$ at $t=15$ s; car is at 40 m s$^{-1}$ throughout.
STEP 1/1 M1 A1

Strategy: Equal speeds means motorcyclist's speed = car's speed = 40 m s$^{-1}$. Since motorcyclist reaches 60 m s$^{-1}$ by $t=15$, equal-speed must occur during Phase 1 ($0\le t\le15$).

Phase 1 motorcyclist speed: $v_m = 4t_1$

Set equal to car: $4t_1 = 40$

$t_1 = 10$ s

Check: $t_1=10 < 15$ ✓ (within Phase 1).

Pitfall: Using Phase 2 formula $v=60+1\cdot(t-15)$ to find equal speed, getting $60+(t_1-15)=40 \Rightarrow t_1=-5$ (negative — impossible). Correctly identify that equal speed happens in Phase 1 before motorcyclist passes 40 m s$^{-1}$.
$\boxed{t_1 = 10 \text{ s}}$

Part (c) — Show $T^2+kT-300=0$, find $k$ [6 marks] · 2 steps

Setup: At catch-up, total distances are equal. Car distance = motorcyclist distance.
STEP 1/2 M1 A1 A1

Strategy: Total time for both = $15+T$. Use areas under v-t graph (= distances) and set them equal.

Car distance (rectangle under horizontal line):

$s_{\text{car}} = 40(15+T) = 600+40T$

Motorcyclist distance (areas of two trapezoids/triangles):

Phase 1: triangle from $(0,0)$ to $(15,60)$: $s_1 = \tfrac{1}{2}(15)(60) = 450$ m

Phase 2: trapezoid from $(15,60)$ to $(15+T, 60+T)$: $s_2 = \tfrac{1}{2}(60 + 60+T)\cdot T = \tfrac{1}{2}(120+T)T = 60T+\tfrac{T^2}{2}$

Total motorcyclist: $450 + 60T + \tfrac{T^2}{2}$

$s_{\text{car}}=600+40T$; $s_{\text{moto}}=450+60T+T^2/2$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $600+40T = 450+60T+T^2/2$
STEP 2/2 M1 A1 A1

Strategy: Set equal, simplify to quadratic, read off $k$.

$600+40T = 450+60T+\tfrac{T^2}{2}$

$600-450 = 60T-40T + \tfrac{T^2}{2}$

$150 = 20T + \tfrac{T^2}{2}$

Multiply by 2: $300 = 40T + T^2$

$T^2 + 40T - 300 = 0$

So $\boxed{k=40}$

Pitfall: Phase 2 area: using $\frac{1}{2}(60)(T)=30T$ (triangle) instead of $\frac{1}{2}(60+(60+T))T=60T+T^2/2$ (trapezoid). Phase 2 starts at 60 m/s and ends at $60+T$ m/s — it's a trapezoid (two unequal parallel sides), not a triangle. This error gives $T^2+60T-300=0$ ($k=60$ wrong).
$\boxed{T^2+40T-300=0}$, so $k=40$
