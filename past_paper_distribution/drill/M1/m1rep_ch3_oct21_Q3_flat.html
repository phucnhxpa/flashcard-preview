Q3 — Car Approaching Road Sign: Three Deceleration Models, Speed and Time

Question

Car at constant 25 m s$^{-1}$ at point A. Road sign is 48 m ahead. Deceleration $6$ m s$^{-2}$.

Model 1: Decelerates uniformly at 6 m s$^{-2}$ from A to sign.

(a) Speed at sign (Model 1). [2]

Model 2: Decelerates at 6 m s$^{-2}$ until speed = 13 m s$^{-1}$, then constant 13 m s$^{-1}$ to sign.

(b) Time to reach sign (Model 2). [4]

Model 3: Constant 25 m s$^{-1}$ until $t=0.2$ s, then decelerates at 6 m s$^{-2}$ to 13 m s$^{-1}$, then constant 13 to sign.

(c) Time to reach sign (Model 3). [4]

[10 marks]

Part (a) — Speed at sign: Model 1 [2 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 M1 A1

Strategy: Single-phase uniform deceleration: use $v^2=u^2+2as$ (avoids time).

Formula (SUVAT): $v^2 = u^2 + 2as$

$u = 25$ m s$^{-1}$; $a = -6$ m s$^{-2}$; $s = 48$ m

$v^2 = 25^2 + 2(-6)(48)$

$v^2 = 625 - 576 = 49$

$v = 7$ m s$^{-1}$

Pitfall: Treating deceleration as positive ($a=+6$), getting $v^2=625+576=1201$, $v\approx34.7$ m s$^{-1}$. Deceleration means $a$ is negative (opposing motion): $a=-6$ m s$^{-2}$.
$\boxed{v = 7 \text{ m s}^{-1}}$

Part (b) — Time to sign: Model 2 [4 marks] · 2 steps

Model 2: Phase 1: decelerate at 6 from 25 to 13. Phase 2: constant 13 m s$^{-1}$ for remaining distance.
STEP 1/2 M1 A1

Strategy: Phase 1 — find time $t_1$ and distance $s_1$. Phase 2 — find remaining distance $s_2$ and time $t_2$.

Phase 1 ($u=25$, $v=13$, $a=-6$):

$v = u + at_1 \Rightarrow 13 = 25-6t_1 \Rightarrow t_1 = 2$ s

$s_1 = \tfrac{u+v}{2}\cdot t_1 = \tfrac{25+13}{2}\cdot 2 = 38$ m

Phase 2: $s_2 = 48-38 = 10$ m at constant 13 m s$^{-1}$

$t_2 = s_2/v = 10/13$ s

$t_1=2$ s, $s_1=38$ m; $t_2=10/13$ s
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $t_1=2$ s, $t_2=10/13$ s
STEP 2/2 A1 A1

Strategy: Total time = $t_1 + t_2$.

$t = 2 + \dfrac{10}{13} = \dfrac{26+10}{13} = \dfrac{36}{13} \approx 2.77$ s

Pitfall: Using total distance 48 m with $u=25$, $v=13$ in one SUVAT — invalid because acceleration changes. This gives wrong $t$. Must split into phases. Also: average speed method for $s_1$: $s=\frac{u+v}{2}t$ only works for constant acceleration.
$\boxed{t = \dfrac{36}{13} \approx 2.77 \text{ s}}$

Part (c) — Time to sign: Model 3 [4 marks] · 1 step

Model 3: Phase 0 (0 to 0.2 s): constant 25. Phase 1: decelerate 6 from 25 to 13. Phase 2: constant 13.
STEP 1/1 M1 A1 A1 A1

Strategy: Three phases. Same deceleration phase as Model 2 but now preceded by 0.2 s at 25 m s$^{-1}$. Calculate each phase's distance, find remaining for Phase 2, compute $t_3$.

Phase 0 ($0\le t\le0.2$, constant 25 m s$^{-1}$):

$s_0 = 25\times0.2 = 5$ m

Phase 1 (decelerate 25→13 at $-6$): same as Model 2:

$t_1 = 2$ s; $s_1 = 38$ m

Distance used: $s_0+s_1 = 5+38 = 43$ m

Phase 2 (constant 13 m s$^{-1}$):

$s_2 = 48-43 = 5$ m; $t_2 = 5/13$ s

Total: $t = 0.2 + 2 + \dfrac{5}{13} = 2.2 + \dfrac{5}{13} = \dfrac{28.6+5}{13} = \dfrac{33.6}{13}$

$= 2.2 + 0.385... \approx 2.58$ s

Pitfall: Forgetting Phase 0 distance and using $s_2=48-38=10$ m (Model 2 value). In Model 3, Phase 0 consumes 5 m, leaving only 5 m for Phase 2 (not 10 m). Always recompute remaining distance for the final constant-speed phase in each model.
$\boxed{t = 2.2+\tfrac{5}{13} \approx 2.58 \text{ s}}$
