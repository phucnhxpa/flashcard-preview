Q5 — Falling Particle P (250 kg): Free Fall then Parachute; Distance, Speed, Time & v-t Sketch

Question

Particle P of mass 250 kg released from rest. Falls freely under gravity ($g=9.8$ m s$^{-2}$) for 5 s. At $t=5$ s, parachute opens creating upward resistive force of 3200 N.

Total distance fallen = 520 m.

(a) Find distance fallen in the first 5 s. [2]

(b) Find speed when P hits the ground. [4]

(c) Find total time for the motion. [3]

(d) Sketch a speed-time graph for the full motion. [3]

[12 marks]

Part (a) — Distance in first 5 s (free fall) [2 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 M1 A1

Strategy: Phase 1 is constant acceleration (free fall). Use SUVAT with $u=0$ (released from rest), $a=g=9.8$ m s$^{-2}$, $t=5$ s.

Formula (SUVAT): $s = ut + \tfrac{1}{2}at^2$

Recall: Released from rest $\Rightarrow u=0$. Taking downward as positive.

$s_1 = (0)(5) + \tfrac{1}{2}(9.8)(5)^2$

$= 0 + 4.9 \times 25$

$= 122.5$ m

Pitfall: Using $s=\frac{1}{2}gt^2$ but writing $g=9.81$ or $g=10$. Always use $g=9.8$ for Edexcel IAL M1 unless stated. With $g=9.8$: $s=\frac{1}{2}(9.8)(25)=122.5$ m.
$s_1 = 122.5$ m

Part (b) — Speed when hitting ground [4 marks] · 2 steps

From (a): $s_1=122.5$ m so phase 2 distance $s_2 = 520-122.5 = 397.5$ m.
STEP 1/2 M1 A1

Strategy: Find speed at end of Phase 1 (= start of Phase 2), then find the new acceleration via $F=ma$.

Formula (SUVAT): $v = u + at$

Phase 1 final speed: $v_1 = 0 + 9.8 \times 5 = 49$ m s$^{-1}$ (downward)

Phase 2 acceleration (parachute open):

Weight (down): $W = 250 \times 9.8 = 2450$ N

Resistance (up): $R = 3200$ N

Net force (down): $F_{\text{net}} = 2450 - 3200 = -750$ N

$a_2 = F_{\text{net}}/m = -750/250 = -3$ m s$^{-2}$ (deceleration)

Phase 2: $u_2=49$ m s$^{-1}$, $a_2=-3$ m s$^{-2}$, $s_2=397.5$ m
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: Phase 2: $u=49$, $a=-3$, $s=397.5$
STEP 2/2 M1 A1

Strategy: Apply SUVAT $v^2=u^2+2as$ for Phase 2 to find final speed.

Formula (SUVAT): $v^2 = u^2 + 2as$

$v_2^2 = (49)^2 + 2(-3)(397.5)$

$= 2401 - 2385$

$= 16$

$v_2 = 4$ m s$^{-1}$

Pitfall: Using total distance 520 m with $u=0$ in a single SUVAT. This ignores the change in acceleration at $t=5$ s. Must split into two phases with their own SUVAT calculations.
$\boxed{v_2 = 4 \text{ m s}^{-1}}$

Part (c) — Total time [3 marks] · 1 step

From (b): Phase 2: $u=49$, $v=4$, $a=-3$.
STEP 1/1 M1 A1 A1

Strategy: Phase 1 time is given (5 s). Find Phase 2 time using $v=u+at_2$.

Formula (SUVAT): $v = u + at$

$4 = 49 + (-3)t_2$

$-45 = -3t_2$

$t_2 = 15$ s

Total time $= t_1 + t_2 = 5 + 15 = 20$ s

Pitfall: Solving for $t_2$ using $s=ut+\frac{1}{2}at^2$ with $s=397.5$, getting a quadratic. While valid, $v=u+at$ is far cleaner here since we already know $v=4$ from (b). Always use the simplest applicable SUVAT equation first.
$\boxed{\text{Total time} = 20 \text{ s}}$

Part (d) — Speed-time graph [3 marks] · 1 step

From (a)-(c): Phase 1: $(0,0)$ to $(5,49)$; Phase 2: $(5,49)$ to $(20,4)$.
STEP 1/1 B1 B1 B1

Strategy: Two phases of constant acceleration = two straight line segments on speed-time graph.

Phase 1 ($0\le t\le 5$ s): starts at $(0,0)$, ends at $(5,49)$. Positive gradient $= +9.8$ m s$^{-2}$ (speeding up).

Phase 2 ($5\le t\le 20$ s): starts at $(5,49)$, ends at $(20,4)$. Negative gradient $= -3$ m s$^{-2}$ (slowing down from 49 to 4).

Key coordinate labels on axes: speed values 49 and 4 on $v$-axis; times 5 and 20 on $t$-axis.

Graph does NOT touch the $t$-axis (speed never reaches zero — P always moving downward).

Pitfall: Drawing the graph as a triangle reaching $v=0$. P decelerates from 49 to 4 m s$^{-1}$ — it slows but never stops before hitting the ground. The graph ends at $(20, 4)$, above the $t$-axis.
Two-segment v-t graph: $(0,0)\to(5,49)\to(20,4)$; gradient $+9.8$ then $-3$.
