Oct 2022 Q4 — Lift accelerates upward; find $a$ and Alan’s mass [6m]

Question

A lift (mass $250$ kg) is raised by a cable with tension $T = 3616$ N. Two children are inside: the floor exerts $565$ N on Alan and $226$ N on Bhavana. ($g = 9.8$ m/s².)

(a) Considering forces on the lift only, find the acceleration $a$. [3]

(b) Find the mass of Alan. [3]

[6 marks]

Part (a) — Acceleration of lift [3 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1

Key instruction: “consider forces on the lift only” — this means treating the 250 kg lift structure as the system, excluding Alan and Bhavana’s masses.

Forces on lift structure (upward = positive):

• Cable tension $T = 3616$ N upward (+)

• Lift’s own weight $250g = 250 \times 9.8 = 2450$ N downward (−)

• By Newton’s 3rd Law: Alan pushes floor down with $565$ N (equal and opposite to floor’s $565$ N on Alan) (−)

• Bhavana pushes floor down with $226$ N (−)

Formula (N2L on lift, upward positive):

$$T - 250g - 565 - 226 = 250a$$

$$3616 - 2450 - 565 - 226 = 250a$$

$$375 = 250a$$

$$\boxed{\;a = 1.5\;\text{m/s}^2\;\text{upward}\;}$$

N3L trap: The floor pushes Alan UP at 565 N. By N3L, Alan pushes the floor DOWN at 565 N. These 565 N push down on the lift (floor), not on Alan. Do NOT include 565 N as an upward force in the lift equation.
$a = 1.5$ m/s² upward

Part (b) — Mass of Alan [3 marks] · 1 step

From (a): $a = 1.5$ m/s² upward (same for all objects in the lift).
STEP 1/1

Strategy: Alan moves with the same acceleration as the lift. Apply N2L to Alan as a separate system.

Forces on Alan (upward = positive):

• Normal reaction from floor $R = 565$ N upward

• Alan’s weight $Mg$ downward

Formula (N2L for Alan):

$$R - Mg = Ma$$

$$565 = M(g + a) = M(9.8 + 1.5) = 11.3M$$

$$M = \frac{565}{11.3} = 50\;\text{kg}$$

$$\boxed{\;M_{\text{Alan}} = 50\;\text{kg}\;}$$

Apparent weight insight: Alan feels a normal force of 565 N, but his actual weight is $50 \times 9.8 = 490$ N. He feels 565/490 ≈ 15% heavier than usual — the extra is the "pseudo-force" from upward acceleration. In a downward-accelerating lift, $R < Mg$ (feels lighter). In free fall, $R = 0$ (weightlessness).
$\boxed{M_{\text{Alan}} = 50\,\text{kg}}$
