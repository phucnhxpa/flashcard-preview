Q6 — Force $\mathbf{F}=(10\mathbf{i}+\mathbf{j})$ N: Magnitude, Angle, Unknown Component

Question

A force $\mathbf{F} = (10\mathbf{i}+\mathbf{j})$ N.

(a) Find the exact magnitude of $\mathbf{F}$. [2]

(b) Find the angle between $\mathbf{F}$ and the vector $(\mathbf{i}+\mathbf{j})$. [4]

The resultant of $\mathbf{F}$ and $(-15\mathbf{i}+a\mathbf{j})$ N is parallel to but opposite to $(2\mathbf{i}-3\mathbf{j})$.

(c) Find the value of $a$. [5]

[11 marks]

Part (a) — Exact magnitude of F [2 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 M1 A1

Strategy: Magnitude of a 2D vector uses Pythagoras on its components.

Formula (Vector magnitude): $|\mathbf{v}| = \sqrt{v_i^2+v_j^2}$

$|\mathbf{F}| = \sqrt{10^2+1^2}$

$= \sqrt{100+1}$

$= \sqrt{101}$ N

Recall: "Exact value" means leave in surd form — do not approximate to decimal.

Pitfall: Writing $\sqrt{10+1}=\sqrt{11}$ — forgetting to square each component. It's $10^2+1^2=101$, not $10+1=11$. Pythagoras requires squares of the side lengths.
$\boxed{|\mathbf{F}| = \sqrt{101} \text{ N}}$

Part (b) — Angle between F and (i+j) [4 marks] · 1 step

From (a): $|\mathbf{F}|=\sqrt{101}$. Use dot product formula.
STEP 1/1 M1 A1 A1 A1

Strategy: Use the dot product formula to find the angle between two vectors.

Formula (Scalar/Dot Product): $\mathbf{a}\cdot\mathbf{b} = |\mathbf{a}||\mathbf{b}|\cos\theta$ $\Rightarrow \cos\theta = \dfrac{\mathbf{a}\cdot\mathbf{b}}{|\mathbf{a}||\mathbf{b}|}$

Let $\mathbf{a}=10\mathbf{i}+\mathbf{j}$, $\mathbf{b}=\mathbf{i}+\mathbf{j}$.

Dot product: $\mathbf{a}\cdot\mathbf{b} = (10)(1)+(1)(1) = 11$

Magnitudes: $|\mathbf{a}|=\sqrt{101}$; $|\mathbf{b}|=\sqrt{1^2+1^2}=\sqrt{2}$

$\cos\theta = \dfrac{11}{\sqrt{101}\cdot\sqrt{2}} = \dfrac{11}{\sqrt{202}}$

$\theta = \arccos\!\left(\dfrac{11}{\sqrt{202}}\right) \approx 39.3^\circ$

Pitfall: Using $\mathbf{a}\cdot\mathbf{b}=10+1=11$ correctly but then forgetting to include $|\mathbf{b}|=\sqrt{2}$ in the denominator. Check: answer should be between 0° and 90° since dot product is positive ($\cos\theta>0$). $39.3°$ is plausible (F leans very east, (i+j) is NE).
$\boxed{\theta \approx 39.3^\circ}$

Part (c) — Find $a$ for opposite-parallel resultant [5 marks] · 2 steps

Setup: Resultant $\mathbf{R} = \mathbf{F}+(-15\mathbf{i}+a\mathbf{j})$ is parallel but opposite to $(2\mathbf{i}-3\mathbf{j})$.
STEP 1/2 M1 A1

Strategy: Form the resultant vector, then use the condition that parallel-but-opposite vectors are scalar multiples with a negative scalar.

$\mathbf{R} = (10\mathbf{i}+\mathbf{j}) + (-15\mathbf{i}+a\mathbf{j})$

$= (10-15)\mathbf{i}+(1+a)\mathbf{j}$

$= -5\mathbf{i}+(1+a)\mathbf{j}$

Recall: "Parallel but opposite to $(2\mathbf{i}-3\mathbf{j})$" $\Rightarrow$ $\mathbf{R} = k(-)(2\mathbf{i}-3\mathbf{j}) = \lambda(-2\mathbf{i}+3\mathbf{j})$ for $\lambda>0$.

$\mathbf{R} = -5\mathbf{i}+(1+a)\mathbf{j}$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $\mathbf{R}=-5\mathbf{i}+(1+a)\mathbf{j}$ must equal $\lambda(-2\mathbf{i}+3\mathbf{j})$
STEP 2/2 M1 A1 A1

Strategy: For parallel vectors, ratios of corresponding components must be equal.

$\dfrac{-5}{-2} = \dfrac{1+a}{3}$ (equating $\mathbf{i}$ and $\mathbf{j}$ ratios)

$\dfrac{5}{2} = \dfrac{1+a}{3}$

$3 \times \dfrac{5}{2} = 1+a$

$\dfrac{15}{2} = 1+a$

$a = \dfrac{15}{2} - 1 = \dfrac{13}{2} = 6.5$

Pitfall: Setting $\mathbf{R} \parallel (2\mathbf{i}-3\mathbf{j})$ (wrong direction) which gives $(-5)/2 = (1+a)/(-3)$, yielding $a=-8.5$. "Opposite to $(2\mathbf{i}-3\mathbf{j})$" means parallel to $(-2\mathbf{i}+3\mathbf{j})$. Check: $\lambda=5/2$, $\mathbf{R}=-5\mathbf{i}+\tfrac{13}{2}\mathbf{j}$; $\tfrac{5}{2}(-2\mathbf{i}+3\mathbf{j})=-5\mathbf{i}+\tfrac{15}{2}\mathbf{j}$… wait: $(1+6.5)=7.5=15/2=3\times5/2$ ✓.
$\boxed{a = 6.5}$
