Q3 — Car Braking: Three Models, Deceleration 6 m s⁻²

Question

A car is moving at a constant speed of $25\ \text{m s}^{-1}$ along a straight horizontal road. The car is modelled as a particle.

At time $t = 0$, the car is at the point A and the driver sees a road sign $48\ \text{m}$ ahead. Let $t$ seconds be the time that elapses after the car passes A.

Model 1: The car decelerates uniformly at $6\ \text{m s}^{-2}$ from A until it reaches the road sign.

(a) Use Model 1 to find the speed of the car as it reaches the sign. [2]

Model 2: The car decelerates uniformly at $6\ \text{m s}^{-2}$ from A until it reaches a speed of $13\ \text{m s}^{-1}$, then maintains this speed until it reaches the sign.

(b) Use Model 2 to find the value of $t$ at which the car reaches the sign. [4]

Model 3: The car moves at constant speed $25\ \text{m s}^{-1}$ from A until $t = 0.2$, then decelerates uniformly at $6\ \text{m s}^{-2}$ until it reaches $13\ \text{m s}^{-1}$, then maintains this speed until it reaches the sign.

(c) Use Model 3 to find the value of $t$ at which the car reaches the sign. [4]

[10 marks total]

Part (a) — Model 1: Speed at the sign [2 marks] · 1 step

Given: $u = 25\ \text{m s}^{-1}$, $a = -6\ \text{m s}^{-2}$, $s = 48\ \text{m}$. Find $v$.
STEP 1/1 M1 A1

Use $v^2 = u^2 + 2as$ with $u = 25$, $a = -6$, $s = 48$:

$$v^2 = 25^2 + 2(-6)(48) = 625 - 576 = 49$$

$$v = 7\ \text{m s}^{-1}$$

Note: $v^2 = 49 > 0$, so car hasn't stopped before sign — Model 1 is physically consistent.
Speed at sign $= \mathbf{7}\ \text{m s}^{-1}$

Part (b) — Model 2: Time to reach sign [4 marks] · 2 steps

Model 2: Decelerate at $6\ \text{m s}^{-2}$ from $25\ \text{m s}^{-1}$ to $13\ \text{m s}^{-1}$, then constant $13\ \text{m s}^{-1}$ to sign.
STEP 1/2 M1

Phase 1 — braking: Find time $t_1$ and distance $s_1$.

$v = u + at_1$: $\quad 13 = 25 - 6t_1 \Rightarrow t_1 = 2\ \text{s}$

$s_1 = \frac{u+v}{2} \cdot t_1 = \frac{25+13}{2} \times 2 = 38\ \text{m}$

Phase 1: $t_1 = 2\ \text{s}$, $s_1 = 38\ \text{m}$
↳ from STEP 1: $s_1 = 38$ m used, remaining distance $= 48 - 38 = 10$ m
STEP 2/2 M1 A1 A1

Phase 2 — constant speed: $s_2 = 48 - 38 = 10\ \text{m}$ at $v = 13\ \text{m s}^{-1}$.

$t_2 = \dfrac{s_2}{v} = \dfrac{10}{13}\ \text{s}$

Total time: $t = t_1 + t_2 = 2 + \dfrac{10}{13} = \dfrac{36}{13} \approx 2.77\ \text{s}$

$t = \dfrac{36}{13} \approx 2.77\ \text{s}$

Part (c) — Model 3: Time to reach sign [4 marks] · 3 steps

Model 3: Constant $25\ \text{m s}^{-1}$ for $0 \le t \le 0.2$, then decelerate at $6\ \text{m s}^{-2}$ to $13\ \text{m s}^{-1}$, then constant $13\ \text{m s}^{-1}$.
STEP 1/3 M1

Phase 1 — constant speed (0 to 0.2 s):

$s_1 = 25 \times 0.2 = 5\ \text{m}$

Remaining distance for Phases 2 & 3: $48 - 5 = 43\ \text{m}$

$s_1 = 5\ \text{m}$, remaining $= 43\ \text{m}$
↳ from STEP 1: 43 m remaining after $t = 0.2$ s
STEP 2/3 M1

Phase 2 — braking (same as Model 2):

$t_2 = 2\ \text{s}$, $s_2 = 38\ \text{m}$ (same calculation as (b))

Remaining for Phase 3: $43 - 38 = 5\ \text{m}$

$s_2 = 38\ \text{m}$, $t_2 = 2\ \text{s}$, remaining $= 5\ \text{m}$
↳ from STEP 2: 5 m at constant 13 m s⁻¹
STEP 3/3 M1 A1

Phase 3 — constant 13 m s⁻¹:

$t_3 = \dfrac{5}{13}\ \text{s}$

Total: $t = 0.2 + 2 + \dfrac{5}{13} = 2.2 + \dfrac{5}{13} = \dfrac{28.6 + 5}{13} = \dfrac{33.6}{13}$

$$t = \frac{5}{13} + 2.2 \approx 2.58\ \text{s}$$

$t \approx 2.58\ \text{s}$ $\left(= \dfrac{33.6}{13}\right)$
