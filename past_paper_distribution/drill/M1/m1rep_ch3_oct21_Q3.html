M1 October 2021 Paper – Question 3

Question

A car starts from rest and accelerates uniformly at 1.25 m s⁻² until it reaches a speed of 20 m s⁻¹. It then travels at this constant speed. The total distance travelled by the car is 600 m. Find the total time for the journey.

[[10] marks]

[10 marks] · 5 steps

$u_1 = 0$, $a_1 = 1.25$ m s⁻², $v_1 = 20$ m s⁻¹, total distance $= 600$ m
STEP 1/5 M1 A1

Using $v = u + at$ for Phase 1:

$20 = 0 + 1.25 \times t_1$

$t_1 = \dfrac{20}{1.25} = 16$ s

$t_1 = \boxed{16 \text{ s}}$
↳ from STEP 1:Using $t_1 = 16$ s
STEP 2/5 M1 A1

Using $s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2$ for Phase 1:

$s_1 = 0 \times 16 + \frac{1}{2} \times 1.25 \times 16^2$

$s_1 = \frac{1}{2} \times 1.25 \times 256 = 160$ m

$s_1 = \boxed{160 \text{ m}}$
↳ from STEP 2:Using $s_1 = 160$ m and total distance $= 600$ m
STEP 3/5 M1 A1

Distance in Phase 2:

$s_2 = 600 - 160 = 440$ m

$s_2 = \boxed{440 \text{ m}}$
↳ from STEP 3:Using $s_2 = 440$ m and $v = 20$ m s⁻¹
STEP 4/5 M1 A1

Using $s = vt$ for constant velocity:

$440 = 20 \times t_2$

$t_2 = \dfrac{440}{20} = 22$ s

$t_2 = \boxed{22 \text{ s}}$
↳ from STEP 4:Using $t_1 = 16$ s and $t_2 = 22$ s
STEP 5/5 M1 A1

Total time:

$T = t_1 + t_2 = 16 + 22 = 38$ s

$T = \boxed{38 \text{ s}}$
