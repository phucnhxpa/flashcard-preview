Q6 — Particle on Rough Surface: Applied Force at Angle α, Show $P \leq \tfrac{5}{8}W$

Question

A particle of weight $W$ newtons lies at rest on a rough horizontal surface.

A force of magnitude $P$ newtons is applied to the particle. The force acts at an angle $\alpha$ to the horizontal, where $\tan\alpha = \dfrac{4}{3}$.

The coefficient of friction between the particle and the surface is $\dfrac{1}{4}$.

Given that the particle does not move, show that $P \leq \dfrac{5}{8}W$. [7]

[7 marks — show that]

Proof: Show $P \leq \dfrac{5}{8}W$ [7 marks] · 4 steps

Given: $\tan\alpha = \frac{4}{3}$, so $\sin\alpha = \frac{4}{5}$, $\cos\alpha = \frac{3}{5}$. Coefficient of friction $\mu = \frac{1}{4}$. Particle does not move.
STEP 1/4 M1

Resolve components of applied force:

Horizontal component of $P$: $P\cos\alpha = \dfrac{3P}{5}$

Vertical component of $P$: $P\sin\alpha = \dfrac{4P}{5}$

$\tan\alpha = 4/3$ → opposite $= 4$, adjacent $= 3$, hypotenuse $= 5$.
STEP 2/4 M1 A1

Resolve vertically (equilibrium):

$$N + P\sin\alpha = W$$

$$N = W - \frac{4P}{5}$$

$N = W - \dfrac{4P}{5}$
↳ from STEP 2: $N = W - \frac{4P}{5}$
STEP 3/4 M1 A1

Friction condition (particle on verge of motion):

For no movement, friction $F \leq \mu N$:

Horizontal equilibrium: $F = P\cos\alpha = \dfrac{3P}{5}$

$$\frac{3P}{5} \leq \mu N = \frac{1}{4}\!\left(W - \frac{4P}{5}\right)$$

$\dfrac{3P}{5} \leq \dfrac{1}{4}\!\left(W - \dfrac{4P}{5}\right)$
↳ from STEP 3: Inequality to expand and simplify
STEP 4/4 M1 A1 A1

Expand and solve the inequality:

$$\frac{3P}{5} \leq \frac{W}{4} - \frac{4P}{20} = \frac{W}{4} - \frac{P}{5}$$

$$\frac{3P}{5} + \frac{P}{5} \leq \frac{W}{4}$$

$$\frac{4P}{5} \leq \frac{W}{4}$$

$$P \leq \frac{5W}{4 \times 4} = \frac{5W}{8} \quad \checkmark$$

Also check: $N \geq 0$ requires $W \geq \frac{4P}{5}$, i.e. $P \leq \frac{5W}{4}$. Since $\frac{5}{8} < \frac{5}{4}$, the friction constraint is more restrictive — consistent.
$\boxed{P \leq \dfrac{5}{8}W}$ (shown) ✓
