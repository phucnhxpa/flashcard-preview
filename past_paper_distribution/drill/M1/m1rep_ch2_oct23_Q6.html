Q6 — Ships P and Q: Velocity, Interception Point, Distance After Course Change

Question

i and j are unit vectors east and north; positions in km, time $t$ in hours from 12:00.

Ship P: velocity $(10\mathbf{i}-4\mathbf{j})$ km h$^{-1}$; at 12:00 position $(15\mathbf{i}+36\mathbf{j})$ km.

Ship Q: at 12:00 position $(42\mathbf{i}+9\mathbf{j})$ km; velocity $(-8\mathbf{i}+14\mathbf{j})$ km h$^{-1}$.

(a) Write position vector of P at time $t$. [2]

(b) Find when and where P and Q are at the same position (they collide). [4]

At 12:30, Q changes course. New velocity $15\mathbf{j}$ km h$^{-1}$.

(c) Find distance between P and Q at 14:30. [5]

[11 marks]

Part (a) — Position of P at time $t$ [2 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 M1 A1

Strategy: Apply constant-velocity position formula for P.

Formula (Constant Velocity): $\mathbf{r}(t)=\mathbf{r}_0+\mathbf{v}t$

$\mathbf{p}(t) = (15\mathbf{i}+36\mathbf{j}) + (10\mathbf{i}-4\mathbf{j})\cdot t$

$\mathbf{i}$-component: $15+10t$

$\mathbf{j}$-component: $36-4t$

$\mathbf{p}(t) = (15+10t)\mathbf{i}+(36-4t)\mathbf{j}$ km

Pitfall: Forgetting the initial position (using just $\mathbf{v}t$). $t=0$ at 12:00 corresponds to starting position $(15\mathbf{i}+36\mathbf{j})$, not origin. Check: at $t=0$, $\mathbf{p}=15\mathbf{i}+36\mathbf{j}$ ✓.
$\mathbf{p}(t) = (15+10t)\mathbf{i}+(36-4t)\mathbf{j}$ km

Part (b) — Collision: when and where [4 marks] · 1 step

From (a): $\mathbf{p}(t)=(15+10t)\mathbf{i}+(36-4t)\mathbf{j}$. Q's position: $\mathbf{q}(t)=(42-8t)\mathbf{i}+(9+14t)\mathbf{j}$.
STEP 1/1 M1 A1 A1 A1

Strategy: Collision occurs when $\mathbf{p}(t)=\mathbf{q}(t)$. Equate i and j components separately to solve for $t$. Must verify both components give the same $t$.

Equate $\mathbf{i}$: $15+10t = 42-8t$

$18t = 27 \Rightarrow t = 1.5$ h

Equate $\mathbf{j}$: $36-4t = 9+14t$

$27 = 18t \Rightarrow t = 1.5$ h ✓ (consistent)

Collision at $t=1.5$ h (i.e. 13:30)

Position: $\mathbf{p}(1.5)=(15+15)\mathbf{i}+(36-6)\mathbf{j}=30\mathbf{i}+30\mathbf{j}$ km

Pitfall: Only solving one component equation and assuming the other matches. Always check both — a common trap where the $\mathbf{j}$ component doesn't match, meaning they don't collide. If $t$ values disagree, write "paths cross but at different times — no collision".
Collision at $t=1.5$ h (13:30), position $30\mathbf{i}+30\mathbf{j}$ km

Part (c) — Distance between P and Q at 14:30 [5 marks] · 2 steps

From (b): Collision at 13:30. Q changes velocity to $15\mathbf{j}$ at 12:30 ($t=0.5$). Find positions at 14:30 ($t=2.5$).
STEP 1/2 M1 A1

Strategy: Find P's position at $t=2.5$ (straightforward). For Q: find its position at $t=0.5$ (velocity change point), then propagate 2 hours with new velocity $15\mathbf{j}$.

P at 14:30 ($t=2.5$):

$\mathbf{p}(2.5) = (15+25)\mathbf{i}+(36-10)\mathbf{j} = 40\mathbf{i}+26\mathbf{j}$ km

Q at 12:30 ($t=0.5$, before velocity change):

$\mathbf{q}(0.5) = (42-4)\mathbf{i}+(9+7)\mathbf{j} = 38\mathbf{i}+16\mathbf{j}$ km

P at 14:30: $40\mathbf{i}+26\mathbf{j}$; Q at 12:30: $38\mathbf{i}+16\mathbf{j}$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: Q at 12:30 is at $38\mathbf{i}+16\mathbf{j}$ km, then moves with $\mathbf{v}=15\mathbf{j}$ for 2 h
STEP 2/2 M1 A1 A1

Strategy: Q travels with new velocity $15\mathbf{j}$ from 12:30 to 14:30 ($\Delta t = 2$ hours). Then find the distance $|\vec{PQ}|$ at 14:30.

Q at 14:30:

$\mathbf{q}_{14:30} = (38\mathbf{i}+16\mathbf{j}) + 2\times15\mathbf{j}$

$= 38\mathbf{i}+(16+30)\mathbf{j} = 38\mathbf{i}+46\mathbf{j}$ km

Vector PQ at 14:30:

$\vec{PQ} = (38-40)\mathbf{i}+(46-26)\mathbf{j} = -2\mathbf{i}+20\mathbf{j}$ km

$|\vec{PQ}| = \sqrt{(-2)^2+20^2} = \sqrt{4+400} = \sqrt{404} = 2\sqrt{101}$ km

Pitfall: Using $\Delta t = 2.5$ (from 12:00) instead of $\Delta t=2$ (from 12:30). Q changed velocity at 12:30, so the propagation time is 14:30 minus 12:30 = 2 hours, not 2.5.
$\boxed{|\vec{PQ}| = 2\sqrt{101} \approx 20.1 \text{ km}}$
