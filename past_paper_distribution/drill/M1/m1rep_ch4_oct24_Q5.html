Q5 — Particles P (3 kg) on smooth table, Q (5 kg) hanging; find $a$, $T$, pulley force, final speed [13m]

Question

Two particles $P$ and $Q$, of masses 3 kg and 5 kg respectively, are connected by a light inextensible string. Particle $P$ rests on a smooth horizontal table. The string passes over a small smooth pulley fixed at the edge of the table. Particle $Q$ hangs freely below the pulley, as shown in Figure 3.

Initially $P$ is 2 m from the pulley and $Q$ is 8 m above horizontal ground. The system is released from rest.

(a) Find the acceleration of the system. [3]

(b) Find the tension $T$ in the string. [2]

(c) Find the magnitude of the force that the string exerts on the pulley. [2]

(d) Find the speed of $Q$ at the instant it hits the ground. [6]

[13 marks]

(a) [3 marks] · 3 steps

STEP 1/3 M1

Equation of motion for $P$ (horizontal):

Since the table is smooth, there is no friction. Taking the direction towards the pulley as positive:

$$T = 3a \quad \text{--- equation (1)}$$

Pitfall: Some students forget that on a smooth horizontal table, the only horizontal force is the tension. Don't include $mg$ horizontally!
$T = 3a$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $T = 3a$
STEP 2/3 M1

Equation of motion for $Q$ (vertical):

Taking downward as positive (direction of motion for heavier particle):

$$5g - T = 5a \quad \text{--- equation (2)}$$

Pitfall: Students often get the sign wrong. If $Q$ accelerates downward, then $5g > T$, so the equation must be $5g - T = 5a$, not $T - 5g$.
$5g - T = 5a$
↳ from STEP 2:From STEP 1: $T = 3a$  |  From STEP 2: $5g - T = 5a$
STEP 3/3 A1

Add equations (1) and (2) to eliminate $T$:

$$T + (5g - T) = 3a + 5a$$

$$5g = 8a$$

$$a = \frac{5g}{8} = \frac{5 \times 9.8}{8} = \frac{49}{8} = 6.125 \text{ m s}^{-2}$$

Pitfall: Some students divide by wrong total mass (using 5 or 3 instead of 8). Remember: when adding equations, the mass is the total system mass.
$\boxed{a = 6.125 \text{ m s}^{-2}}$

(b) [2 marks] · 1 step

↳ from STEP 0:From (a): $a = \frac{5g}{8}$
STEP 1/1 M1 A1

Substitute $a$ into equation for $P$:

$$T = 3a = 3 \times \frac{5g}{8} = \frac{15g}{8}$$

$$T = \frac{15 \times 9.8}{8} = \frac{147}{8} = 18.375 \text{ N}$$

Verification via $Q$: $T = 5g - 5a = 5(9.8) - 5(6.125) = 49 - 30.625 = 18.375$ ✓

Pitfall: Students sometimes use the wrong equation or substitute incorrectly. Always verify your answer using the other equation when possible.
$\boxed{T = 18.4 \text{ N (3 s.f.)}}$

(c) [2 marks] · 2 steps

↳ from STEP 0:From (b): $T = \frac{15g}{8}$
STEP 1/2 M1

Analyse forces on the pulley:

The string exerts two forces on the pulley:

  • Horizontal pull of magnitude $T$ (from the table side)
  • Vertical pull of magnitude $T$ (from the hanging side)

These forces are perpendicular (at 90°), so we use Pythagoras:

$$F = \sqrt{T^2 + T^2} = \sqrt{2T^2} = T\sqrt{2}$$

Pitfall: Students often forget that the pulley experiences tension from BOTH sides of the string. Some incorrectly use $2T$ (adding magnitudes) instead of finding the vector sum.
$F = T\sqrt{2}$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $F = T\sqrt{2}$, $T = 18.375$ N
STEP 2/2 A1

Calculate the magnitude:

$$F = T\sqrt{2} = \frac{15g}{8} \times \sqrt{2} = \frac{15 \times 9.8 \times \sqrt{2}}{8}$$

$$F = 18.375 \times 1.414... = 25.98 \text{ N}$$

Pitfall: Some students leave the answer as $T\sqrt{2}$ without substituting the numerical value, or make arithmetic errors with $\sqrt{2} \approx 1.414$.
$\boxed{F = 26.0 \text{ N (3 s.f.)}}$

(d) [6 marks] · 4 steps

STEP 1/4 M1

Identify the two phases of motion:

Phase 1: Connected motion while $P$ travels 2 m to reach the pulley (both particles accelerate at $a = \frac{5g}{8}$)

Phase 2: After $P$ hits the pulley, the string goes slack and $Q$ falls freely under gravity for the remaining distance

When $P$ has moved 2 m, $Q$ has descended 2 m (inextensible string), so $Q$ is now $8 - 2 = 6$ m above ground.

Pitfall: Many students miss that this is a TWO-PHASE problem. They incorrectly use constant acceleration for the entire 8 m, ignoring that the constraint changes when $P$ reaches the pulley.
Phase 1: 2 m connected, Phase 2: 6 m free fall
↳ from STEP 1:From (a): $a = \frac{5g}{8}$  |  From STEP 1: Phase 1 distance = 2 m
STEP 2/4 M1 A1

Phase 1: Find speed $v_1$ after 2 m of connected motion

Using $v^2 = u^2 + 2as$ with $u = 0$, $a = \frac{5g}{8}$, $s = 2$:

$$v_1^2 = 0 + 2 \times \frac{5g}{8} \times 2 = \frac{20g}{8} = \frac{5g}{2}$$

$$v_1^2 = \frac{5 \times 9.8}{2} = 24.5$$

$$v_1 = \sqrt{24.5} = 4.950 \text{ m s}^{-1}$$

Pitfall: Students sometimes use the wrong SUVAT equation or forget that $u = 0$ (released from rest). Always check which variables you know before selecting the equation.
$v_1 = \sqrt{\frac{5g}{2}} \approx 4.95 \text{ m s}^{-1}$
↳ from STEP 2:From STEP 2: $v_1 = \sqrt{\frac{5g}{2}}$  |  From STEP 1: $Q$ is 6 m above ground
STEP 3/4 M1 A1

Phase 2: Free fall for 6 m

Now $Q$ falls freely with $u = v_1$, $a = g$, $s = 6$ m:

Using $v^2 = u^2 + 2as$:

$$v_2^2 = v_1^2 + 2g(6) = \frac{5g}{2} + 12g = \frac{5g + 24g}{2} = \frac{29g}{2}$$

$$v_2^2 = \frac{29 \times 9.8}{2} = 142.1$$

Pitfall: Students forget to use the Phase 1 final velocity as the initial velocity for Phase 2. The motion is continuous — $Q$ doesn't stop when the string breaks!
$v_2^2 = \frac{29g}{2}$
↳ from STEP 3:From STEP 3: $v_2^2 = \frac{29g}{2} = 142.1$
STEP 4/4 A1

Calculate final speed:

$$v_2 = \sqrt{142.1} = 11.92 \text{ m s}^{-1}$$

Exact form: $v_2 = \sqrt{\frac{29g}{2}} = \frac{\sqrt{29g}}{\sqrt{2}} = \frac{\sqrt{58g}}{2}$

Pitfall: Rounding errors can accumulate. It's best to keep exact values (in terms of $g$) until the final step, then substitute $g = 9.8$ at the end.
$\boxed{v = 11.9 \text{ m s}^{-1} \text{ (3 s.f.)}}$
