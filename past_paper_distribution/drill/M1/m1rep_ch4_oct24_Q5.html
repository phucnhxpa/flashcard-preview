Q5 — P (3 kg) & Q (5 kg) Connected over Pulley, Both Hanging Vertically

Question

Two particles, P and Q, have masses $3\ \text{kg}$ and $5\ \text{kg}$ respectively. The particles are connected by a light inextensible string which passes over a small smooth fixed pulley.

The particles are released from rest with the string taut and the hanging parts of the string vertical. Immediately after release, P moves upwards with acceleration $a\ \text{m s}^{-2}$ and the tension in the string is $T$ newtons.

(a) Write down an equation of motion for P. [2]

(b) Find the value of $T$. [4]

The total force acting on the pulley due to the string has magnitude $F$ newtons.

(c) Find the value of $F$. [2]

Initially Q is $10\ \text{m}$ above horizontal ground and P is more than $2\ \text{m}$ below the pulley. At the instant when Q has descended $2\ \text{m}$, the string breaks and Q falls to the ground.

(d) Find the speed of Q at the instant it hits the ground. [5]

[13 marks total]

Part (a) — Equation of motion for P [2 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 M1 A1

Apply Newton's 2nd Law to P (mass 3 kg, accelerating upward at $a$):

Forces on P: tension $T$ upward, weight $3g$ downward.

$$T - 3g = 3a$$

Key: Both particles accelerate at the same magnitude $a$ (inextensible string). P goes up, Q goes down.
$T - 3g = 3a$

Part (b) — Find T [4 marks] · 2 steps

From (a): $T - 3g = 3a$. Need equation for Q to solve simultaneously.
STEP 1/2 M1 A1

Apply Newton's 2nd Law to Q (5 kg, accelerating downward at $a$):

$$5g - T = 5a \quad \text{(Eq. 2)}$$

Add equations (a) + Eq.2 to eliminate $T$:

$$5g - 3g = 5a + 3a \Rightarrow 2g = 8a$$

$$a = \frac{g}{4} = \frac{9.8}{4} = 2.45\ \text{m s}^{-2}$$

$a = 2.45\ \text{m s}^{-2}$
↳ from STEP 1: $a = 2.45\ \text{m s}^{-2}$
STEP 2/2 M1 A1

Substitute $a = \frac{g}{4}$ into equation for P:

$$T = 3g + 3a = 3g + \frac{3g}{4} = \frac{15g}{4}$$

$$T = \frac{15 \times 9.8}{4} = \frac{147}{4} = 36.75\ \text{N}$$

$T = 36.75\ \text{N}$

Part (c) — Force on pulley [2 marks] · 1 step

From (b): $T = 36.75$ N. The string acts on both sides of the smooth pulley.
STEP 1/1 M1 A1

The string pulls the pulley with tension $T$ on each vertical side. Both tensions act downward on the pulley.

$$F = 2T = 2 \times 36.75 = 73.5\ \text{N}$$

Key: Both hanging sections pull the pulley downward with equal tension (smooth pulley → same $T$ throughout string). Total force $= 2T$.
$F = 73.5\ \text{N}$

Part (d) — Speed of Q when it hits the ground [5 marks] · 3 steps

Setup: Q initially $10\ \text{m}$ above ground. Q descends $2\ \text{m}$ while connected (at $a = 2.45\ \text{m s}^{-2}$), then string breaks, Q falls freely remaining $8\ \text{m}$.
STEP 1/3 M1

Phase 1: Q descends $2\ \text{m}$ with $a = 2.45\ \text{m s}^{-2}$ from rest.

$v^2 = u^2 + 2as$

$v_1^2 = 0 + 2(2.45)(2) = 9.8$

$v_1 = \sqrt{9.8}\ \text{m s}^{-1}$ (speed when string breaks)

$v_1 = \sqrt{9.8} \approx 3.13\ \text{m s}^{-1}$ downward
↳ from STEP 1: After string breaks, Q is $10-2=8\ \text{m}$ above ground, speed $v_1 = \sqrt{9.8}\ \text{m s}^{-1}$ downward
STEP 2/3 M1 A1

Phase 2: Q falls freely $8\ \text{m}$ under gravity only ($g = 9.8\ \text{m s}^{-2}$).

$v_2^2 = v_1^2 + 2g \times 8$

$v_2^2 = 9.8 + 2(9.8)(8) = 9.8 + 156.8 = 166.6$

$v_2^2 = 166.6\ \text{m}^2\text{s}^{-2}$
↳ from STEP 2: $v_2^2 = 166.6$
STEP 3/3 A1 A1

$v_2 = \sqrt{166.6} \approx 12.9\ \text{m s}^{-1}$

Check: $v_1^2 = 2 \times \frac{g}{4} \times 2 = g = 9.8$ ✓. Free-fall adds $2g \times 8 = 156.8$. Total $= 166.6$.
Speed of Q at ground $\approx \mathbf{12.9}\ \text{m s}^{-1}$ (3 s.f.)
