Q5 — Ball Projected at 29.4 m s$^{-1}$ from 19.6 m: Speed Conditions & Speed-Time Sketch

Question

A ball projected vertically upward at 29.4 m s$^{-1}$ from point A, which is 19.6 m above horizontal ground. Ball moves freely under gravity until hitting ground (no rebound). $g=9.8$ m s$^{-2}$.

(a) Find the distance travelled while speed is less than 14.7 m s$^{-1}$. [3]

(b) Find the time for which ball moves with speed more than 29.4 m s$^{-1}$. [3]

(c) Sketch speed-time graph from projection to hitting ground. Show where graph meets axes. [3]

[📐 Diagram]

[9 marks]

Part (a) — Distance while speed $< 14.7$ m s$^{-1}$ [3 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 M1 A1 A1

Strategy: Speed $< 14.7$ m s$^{-1}$ around the apex (where speed = 0). By symmetry: find height where $v=14.7$ going UP, then double the distance from there to apex.

Formula (SUVAT): $v^2 = u^2 + 2as$ (upward positive, $u=29.4$, $a=-9.8$)

Maximum height above A (where $v=0$):

$0 = (29.4)^2 - 2(9.8)s_{\max}$

$s_{\max} = 864.36/(19.6) = 44.1$ m

Height where speed = 14.7 (going up):

$(14.7)^2 = (29.4)^2 - 2(9.8)s_{14.7}$

$216.09 = 864.36 - 19.6 s_{14.7}$

$s_{14.7} = 648.27/19.6 = 33.075$ m

Total distance at speed $<14.7$: $2(44.1-33.075) = 2(11.025) = 22.05$ m

Pitfall: Calculating only the upward portion (11.025 m) and forgetting the symmetric downward portion. By symmetry (same height going down as going up), the total is $\times 2 = 22.05$ m.
$\boxed{\text{Distance} = 22.05 \text{ m}}$

Part (b) — Time with speed $> 29.4$ m s$^{-1}$ [3 marks] · 1 step

Setup: Speed exceeds 29.4 m s$^{-1}$ only when ball has fallen below A (more than 19.6 m additional fall).
STEP 1/1 M1 A1 A1

Strategy: By symmetry, ball returns to height A (going downward) at $t=6$ s with speed 29.4 m s$^{-1}$. After that it accelerates further until hitting ground. Find total flight time, subtract 6 s.

Time to reach A going down:

From projection: $v=u+at \Rightarrow -29.4 = 29.4 - 9.8t$

$t_A = 58.8/9.8 = 6$ s (back at height of A, speed = 29.4 downward)

Total flight time (from A to ground, displacement $= -19.6$ m):

$-19.6 = 29.4t - 4.9t^2 \Rightarrow 4.9t^2 - 29.4t - 19.6 = 0$

$t^2 - 6t - 4 = 0 \Rightarrow t = 3 + \sqrt{13}$ (positive root)

Duration with speed $>29.4$:

$(3+\sqrt{13}) - 6 = \sqrt{13}-3 \approx 0.606$ s

Pitfall: Claiming speed $>29.4$ for the full descent from apex. Speed exceeds 29.4 only when ball is below point A (fallen beyond the 19.6 m). From apex to A, speed goes 0→29.4 (not $>29.4$). Only below A does speed exceed 29.4.
$\boxed{t = \sqrt{13}-3 \approx 0.61 \text{ s}}$

Part (c) — Speed-time sketch [3 marks] · 1 step

From (a)/(b): Key points: $(0,29.4)$, $(3,0)$ [apex], $(6,29.4)$ [back at A level], $(3+\sqrt{13}, v_{\text{ground}})$.
STEP 1/1 B1 B1 B1

Strategy: Speed-time graph always $\geq 0$. Gradient is constant $= g = 9.8$ m s$^{-2}$ in speed terms (constant change in speed rate when above A). Below A, still same constant gradient.

Graph shape:

Starts at $(0, 29.4)$ — V-shaped dip to $(3, 0)$ at apex, then rises.

Returns to 29.4 at $t=6$ s, then continues rising to $v_{\text{gnd}}$ at $t=3+\sqrt{13}$ s.

$v_{\text{gnd}} = \sqrt{29.4^2+2(9.8)(19.6)} = \sqrt{864.36+384.16} = \sqrt{1248.52} \approx 35.3$ m s$^{-1}$

Axes intercepts: Meets time-axis at $t=3$ s (apex); starts at $v=29.4$ when $t=0$.

Pitfall: Drawing a straight line from $(0,29.4)$ to ground — ignoring the V-shape. Speed is NOT monotonically increasing or decreasing. It decreases to 0 at apex then increases. The minimum speed is 0 at $t=3$ s, not at $t=0$.
V-shape: $(0,29.4)\to(3,0)\to(6,29.4)\to(3+\sqrt{13},35.3)$
