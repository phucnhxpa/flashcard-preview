Sequences & Series — Real-World AP/GP Application

Question

A runner is preparing for a marathon. The runner plans to complete a number of long Sunday runs. They will also run a fixed distance of $x$ km on Wednesdays, where $x$ is a positive integer.

The runner is considering two different training plans for the Sunday runs. Both training plans last 12 weeks and in both plans the first Sunday run is 15 km and the last Sunday run is 37 km.

(a) In Plan A the Sunday runs form an arithmetic sequence. Find the distance of the Sunday run in week 5. [3]

(b) In Plan B the Sunday runs form a geometric sequence. Find the distance of the Sunday run in week 5, giving your answer to 3 significant figures. [3]

(c) Given that the runner wants to run no more than 360 km in total, find the greatest value of $x$ if the runner follows

(i) Plan A, [3]

(ii) Plan B. [2]

[11 marks]

Part (a) — AP: Find week 5 distance [3 marks] · 2 steps

$a = 15$, $a_{12} = 37$, arithmetic sequence
STEP 1/2 M1

Formula: For an AP, the $n$th term is $a_n = a + (n-1)d$

$$a_{12} = a + 11d = 37$$

$$11d = 37 - 15 = 22$$

$$d = 2$$

Pitfall: Using $n = 12$ instead of $n - 1 = 11$ when finding the common difference.
$\boxed{d = 2}$
Carry-in from previous step$d = 2$
STEP 2/2 M1A1

Week 5 distance: Use $a_5 = a + 4d$

$$a_5 = 15 + 4(2) = 15 + 8 = 23$$

Pitfall: Week 5 is the 5th term, so use exponent 4 (not 5) in the formula.
$\boxed{23 \text{ km}}$

Part (b) — GP: Find week 5 distance [3 marks] · 2 steps

$a = 15$, $a_{12} = 37$, geometric sequence
STEP 1/2 M1

Formula: For a GP, the $n$th term is $a_n = ar^{n-1}$

$$a_{12} = ar^{11} = 37$$

$$15r^{11} = 37 \implies r^{11} = \frac{37}{15}$$

$$r = \left(\frac{37}{15}\right)^{\frac{1}{11}}$$

Pitfall: The 12th term uses $r^{11}$, not $r^{12}$.
$\boxed{r = \left(\frac{37}{15}\right)^{\frac{1}{11}}}$
Carry-in from previous step$r = \left(\frac{37}{15}\right)^{\frac{1}{11}}$
STEP 2/2 M1A1

Week 5 distance: Use $a_5 = ar^4$

$$a_5 = 15 \times \left(\frac{37}{15}\right)^{\frac{4}{11}}$$

$$a_5 = 15 \times (2.4\overline{6})^{0.3636...} \approx 15 \times 1.3889 \approx 20.8$$

Pitfall: Ensure you use $r^4$ for week 5, and round to 3 s.f. as required.
$\boxed{20.8 \text{ km}}$

Part (c)(i) — Plan A: Find greatest x [3 marks] · 2 steps

From (a): $d = 2$, $a = 15$, $a_{12} = 37$
STEP 1/2 M1

Formula: AP sum $S_n = \frac{n}{2}(a + l)$

$$S_{12} = \frac{12}{2}(15 + 37) = 6 \times 52 = 312 \text{ km}$$

Pitfall: This is only the Sunday total — don't forget the Wednesday runs.
$\boxed{S_{12} = 312 \text{ km}}$
Carry-in from previous step$S_{12} = 312$ km
STEP 2/2 M1A1

Set up inequality: Total $\leq 360$ km

$$312 + 12x \leq 360$$

$$12x \leq 48$$

$$x \leq 4$$

Since $x$ is a positive integer, the greatest value is $x = 4$.

Pitfall: Remember there are 12 Wednesday runs, each of $x$ km.
$\boxed{x = 4}$

Part (c)(ii) — Plan B: Find greatest x [2 marks] · 2 steps

From (b): $r = \left(\frac{37}{15}\right)^{\frac{1}{11}}$, $a = 15$
STEP 1/2 M1

Formula: GP sum $S_n = \frac{a(r^n - 1)}{r - 1}$

With $r^{11} = \frac{37}{15}$ and $r \approx 1.08527$:

$$r^{12} = r^{11} \times r = \frac{37}{15} \times 1.08527 \approx 2.6773$$

$$S_{12} = \frac{15(2.6773 - 1)}{1.08527 - 1} = \frac{15 \times 1.6773}{0.08527} \approx 295.1 \text{ km}$$

Pitfall: Use exact values where possible to avoid cumulative rounding errors.
$\boxed{S_{12} \approx 295.1 \text{ km}}$
Carry-in from previous step$S_{12} \approx 295.1$ km
STEP 2/2 A1

Set up inequality:

$$295.1 + 12x \leq 360$$

$$12x \leq 64.9$$

$$x \leq 5.408...$$

Since $x$ must be a positive integer, round down.

Pitfall: Must round DOWN to satisfy the inequality — $x = 5$, not 6.
$\boxed{x = 5}$
