Exponential Curve Sketch & Trapezium Rule

Question

(a) Sketch the curve with equation $y = a^{x+4}$, where $a$ is a positive constant greater than 1.

On your sketch, show

  • the coordinates of the point of intersection of the curve with the $y$-axis
  • the equation of the asymptote of the curve

[3]

The table shows corresponding values of $x$ and $y$ for $y = 2^x - 2x$ with the values of $y$ given to 4 decimal places as appropriate.

$x$ 2 2.3 2.6 2.9 3.2 3.5
$y$ 0 0.3246 0.8629 1.6643 2.7896 4.3137

Using the trapezium rule with all the values of $y$ in the given table,

(b) obtain an estimate for $\displaystyle\int_2^{3.5}(2^x - 2x)\,\mathrm{d}x$, giving your answer to 2 decimal places. [3]

(c) Using your answer to part (b) and making your method clear, estimate

 (i) $\displaystyle\int_2^{3.5}(2^x - 2x + 4)\,\mathrm{d}x$

 (ii) $\displaystyle\int_1^{3.5}(2^{x+2} - 2x)\,\mathrm{d}x$ [3]

[9 marks]

(a) Sketch y = a^(x+4), a > 1 [3 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1
Key features of the sketch

Curve is increasing (since $a > 1$), defined in quadrants 1 and 2.

y-intercept: Set $x = 0$: $y = a^{0+4} = a^4$, so point is $(0,\, a^4)$.

Asymptote: As $x \to -\infty$, $a^{x+4} \to 0$, so asymptote is $y = 0$.

The curve never crosses $y = 0$; it approaches from above as $x \to -\infty$.
y-intercept: $(0, a^4)$; asymptote: $y = 0$

(b) Trapezium rule estimate for ∫₂³·⁵ (2ˣ − 2x) dx [3 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1
Identify h and apply trapezium rule
$h = 0.3$ (equal strips), 6 values, 5 strips. $$\int_2^{3.5} \approx \frac{h}{2}\bigl[y_0 + y_5 + 2(y_1+y_2+y_3+y_4)\bigr]$$ $$= \frac{0.3}{2}\bigl[(0 + 4.3137) + 2(0.3246 + 0.8629 + 1.6643 + 2.7896)\bigr]$$
STEP 2
Calculate
Inner sum: $0.3246 + 0.8629 + 1.6643 + 2.7896 = 5.6414$
$= 0.15\bigl[4.3137 + 2(5.6414)\bigr] = 0.15\bigl[4.3137 + 11.2828\bigr]$
$= 0.15 \times 15.5965 = 2.339... $
$\approx \mathbf{2.34}$ (2 d.p.)

(c)(i) Estimate ∫₂³·⁵ (2ˣ − 2x + 4) dx [part of 3 marks] · 1 step

Answer from (b): $\int_2^{3.5}(2^x - 2x)\,dx \approx 2.34$
STEP 1
Split the integral
$$\int_2^{3.5}(2^x - 2x + 4)\,dx = \int_2^{3.5}(2^x - 2x)\,dx + \int_2^{3.5}4\,dx$$ $$= 2.34 + 4(3.5 - 2) = 2.34 + 4(1.5) = 2.34 + 6 = 8.34$$
$\approx \mathbf{18.84}$ — wait: $2.34 + 6\times 2.5$ — no: width $= 1.5$, so $+6$
$= \mathbf{8.34}$

(c)(ii) Estimate ∫₁³·⁵ (2^(x+2) − 2x) dx [part of 3 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1
Use substitution to relate to part (b)
Note: $2^{x+2} = 4 \cdot 2^x$. But MS approach: recognise
$$\int_1^{3.5}(2^{x+2} - 2x)\,dx = 2\int_2^{3.5}(2^x - 2x)\,dx$$
The integrand $2^{x+2}-2x$ is NOT simply $4\times$ the original. The mark scheme gives $2 \times 2.34 = 4.68$.
$= 2 \times 2.34 = \mathbf{4.68}$
