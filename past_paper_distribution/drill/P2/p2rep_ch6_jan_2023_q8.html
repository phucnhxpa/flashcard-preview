Trigonometry — Two Trig Equations

Question

In this question you must show all stages of your working. Solutions based entirely on calculator technology are not acceptable.

(i) Solve, for $-\dfrac{\pi}{2} < x < \pi$, the equation

$$5\sin(3x + 0.1) + 2 = 0$$

giving your answers, in radians, to 2 decimal places. [4]

(ii) Solve, for $0 < \theta < 360°$, the equation

$$2\tan\theta\sin\theta = 5 + \cos\theta$$

giving your answers, in degrees, to one decimal place. [5]

[9 marks]

Part (i) — Solve 5sin(3x + 0.1) + 2 = 0 [4 marks] · 4 steps

$-\frac{\pi}{2} < x < \pi$
STEP 1/4 M1

Rearrange: $\sin(3x + 0.1) = -\dfrac{2}{5} = -0.4$

Range for $u = 3x + 0.1$:

When $x \in (-\frac{\pi}{2}, \pi)$: $u = 3x + 0.1 \in (-\frac{3\pi}{2}+0.1,\ 3\pi+0.1)$

i.e. $u \in (-4.61,\ 9.52)$ approximately

Carry-in:$\sin u = -0.4$, $u \in (-4.61, 9.52)$
STEP 2/4 M1

Principal value: $\sin^{-1}(-0.4) = -0.4115...\approx -0.41$ rad

All solutions for $\sin u = -0.4$ in range:

$u = \pi - (-0.41) = \pi + 0.41 \approx 3.55$ (3rd quadrant)

$u = -0.41$ (4th quad, but check: $-0.41 \in (-4.61,9.52)$ ✓)

$u = 2\pi + (-0.41) \approx 5.87$

$u = 2\pi + 3.55 \approx 9.83 > 9.52$ ✗

$u = -\pi - (-0.41) \approx -2.73$

Carry-in:$u \approx -0.41,\ -2.73,\ 3.55,\ 5.87$
STEP 3/4 A1

Convert back: $x = \dfrac{u - 0.1}{3}$

$u = -0.41 \Rightarrow x = \frac{-0.51}{3} = -0.17$

$u = -2.73 \Rightarrow x = \frac{-2.83}{3} = -0.94$

$u = 3.55 \Rightarrow x = \frac{3.45}{3} = 1.15$

$u = 5.87 \Rightarrow x = \frac{5.77}{3} = 1.92$

Carry-in:All candidates computed
STEP 4/4 A1

Check all $x \in (-\frac{\pi}{2}, \pi) \approx (-1.57, 3.14)$:

$x = -0.94$ ✓, $x = -0.17$ ✓, $x = 1.15$ ✓, $x = 1.92$ ✓

$x \approx -0.94,\ -0.17,\ 1.15,\ 1.92$ (2 d.p.)

Part (ii) — Solve 2tan θ sin θ = 5 + cos θ [5 marks] · 4 steps

$0 < \theta < 360°$
STEP 1/4 M1

Replace $\tan\theta = \frac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta}$:

$$2 \cdot \frac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta} \cdot \sin\theta = 5 + \cos\theta$$

$$\frac{2\sin^2\theta}{\cos\theta} = 5 + \cos\theta$$

Multiply by $\cos\theta$:

$$2\sin^2\theta = 5\cos\theta + \cos^2\theta$$

Carry-in:$2\sin^2\theta = 5\cos\theta + \cos^2\theta$
STEP 2/4 M1

Use $\sin^2\theta = 1 - \cos^2\theta$:

$$2(1 - \cos^2\theta) = 5\cos\theta + \cos^2\theta$$

$$2 - 2\cos^2\theta = 5\cos\theta + \cos^2\theta$$

$$3\cos^2\theta + 5\cos\theta - 2 = 0$$

Factor: $(3\cos\theta - 1)(\cos\theta + 2) = 0$
Carry-in:$(3\cos\theta-1)(\cos\theta+2)=0$
STEP 3/4 A1

$\cos\theta + 2 = 0 \Rightarrow \cos\theta = -2$ — impossible

$\cos\theta = \dfrac{1}{3}$

$\theta = \cos^{-1}\!\left(\frac{1}{3}\right) = 70.5°$

Also in 4th quadrant: $\theta = 360° - 70.5° = 289.5°$

Carry-in:$\theta = 70.5°$ or $289.5°$
STEP 4/4 A1A1

Check $\cos\theta \neq 0$ (to validate $\tan\theta$): $\frac{1}{3} \neq 0$ ✓

$\theta \approx 70.5°$ and $\theta \approx 289.5°$ (1 d.p.)
