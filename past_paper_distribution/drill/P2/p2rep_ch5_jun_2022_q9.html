Exponentials & Logs — Exponential Growth Model + Rates of Change

Question

The amount of carbon-14, $N$ grams, in an item of archaeological interest can be modelled by the equation $$N = k\lambda^t$$ where $t$ is the age in years, $k$ and $\lambda$ are positive constants with $0 < \lambda < 1$

(a) Sketch the graph of $N$ against $t$ for $k = 1$ [2]

The half-life of carbon-14 is 5700 years. This means that the amount of carbon-14 in an item reduces to half its initial value in 5700 years.

(b) Show that the value of the constant $\lambda$ is 0.999878 to 6 decimal places. [3]

Item A originally contained 15 grams of carbon-14 and is 3250 years old.

(c) Calculate how much carbon-14 the equation predicts is currently in Item A. [1]

Item B originally contained 25 grams of carbon-14 and currently contains 18 grams of carbon-14.

(d) Use algebra to calculate the age of Item B to the nearest 100 years. [3]

[9 marks]

Part (a) — Sketch graph of N against t for k = 1 [2 marks] · 2 steps

$N = k\lambda^t$ with $k = 1$ and $0 < \lambda < 1$
STEP 1/2 B1

Find key features of $N = \lambda^t$

$$\text{When } t = 0: \quad N = \lambda^0 = 1$$

$$\text{As } t \to \infty: \quad N \to 0 \text{ (since } 0 < \lambda < 1\text{)}$$

Since $0 < \lambda < 1$, the function is exponential decay, not growth!
$\boxed{\text{y-intercept at } (0, 1), \text{ asymptote } N = 0}$
Carry-in from previous stepy-intercept at $(0, 1)$, horizontal asymptote $N = 0$
STEP 2/2 B1

Sketch the decay curve

Draw a smooth decreasing curve:

• Starting at $(0, 1)$

• Decreasing and approaching $t$-axis

• Never touching or crossing $N = 0$

• Label axes $t$ (horizontal) and $N$ (vertical)

The curve must be smooth and convex — not linear or crossing the asymptote.
$\boxed{\text{Decay curve through } (0,1) \text{ approaching } N = 0}$

Part (b) — Show λ = 0.999878 [3 marks] · 3 steps

Half-life of carbon-14 is 5700 years
STEP 1/3 M1

Set up half-life equation

$$\text{Half-life means: when } t = 5700, \quad N = \frac{k}{2}$$

$$\text{Substitute into } N = k\lambda^t:$$

$$\frac{k}{2} = k\lambda^{5700}$$

Half-life means the amount halves — so $N = \frac{k}{2}$, not $N = 2k$!
$\boxed{\frac{k}{2} = k\lambda^{5700}}$
Carry-in from previous step$\frac{k}{2} = k\lambda^{5700}$
STEP 2/3 M1

Solve for λ

$$\text{Divide both sides by } k:$$

$$\frac{1}{2} = \lambda^{5700}$$

$$\text{Raise both sides to power } \frac{1}{5700}:$$

$$\lambda = \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{\frac{1}{5700}} = 2^{-\frac{1}{5700}}$$

To isolate the base, use the reciprocal power: $(a^n)^{1/n} = a$
$\boxed{\lambda = \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{\frac{1}{5700}}}$
Carry-in from previous step$\lambda = \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{\frac{1}{5700}}$
STEP 3/3 A1

Calculate to 6 decimal places

$$\lambda = (0.5)^{0.0001754...} = 0.999878201...$$

$$\lambda = 0.999878 \text{ (6 d.p.)}$$

Must show the unrounded value before giving 6 d.p. to justify the rounding.
$\boxed{\lambda = 0.999878}$

Part (c) — Calculate carbon-14 in Item A [1 marks] · 1 step

$\lambda = 0.999878$, Item A: $k = 15$ grams, $t = 3250$ years
STEP 1/1 B1

Substitute values into model

$$N = k\lambda^t = 15 \times (0.999878)^{3250}$$

$$N = 15 \times 0.67292...$$

$$N = 10.0938...$$

Use the full value 0.999878 — rounding λ further causes significant error over large t.
$\boxed{N = 10.1 \text{ grams}}$

Part (d) — Calculate age of Item B [3 marks] · 3 steps

$\lambda = 0.999878$, Item B: $k = 25$ grams, $N = 18$ grams
STEP 1/3 M1

Set up equation and isolate exponential

$$N = k\lambda^t \implies 18 = 25 \times (0.999878)^t$$

$$\text{Divide by 25:}$$

$$(0.999878)^t = \frac{18}{25} = 0.72$$

Always isolate the exponential term before taking logarithms.
$\boxed{(0.999878)^t = 0.72}$
Carry-in from previous step$(0.999878)^t = 0.72$
STEP 2/3 M1

Take logarithms of both sides

$$\ln(0.999878)^t = \ln(0.72)$$

$$t \ln(0.999878) = \ln(0.72)$$

$$t = \frac{\ln(0.72)}{\ln(0.999878)}$$

Use the log law $\ln(a^t) = t\ln(a)$ to bring down the power.
$\boxed{t = \frac{\ln(0.72)}{\ln(0.999878)}}$
Carry-in from previous step$t = \frac{\ln(0.72)}{\ln(0.999878)}$
STEP 3/3 A1

Calculate and round to nearest 100 years

$$t = \frac{-0.32850...}{-0.000122...} = 2692.67...$$

$$t \approx 2700 \text{ years (nearest 100)}$$

Both logarithms are negative (values < 1), so the ratio is positive — check your signs!
$\boxed{t = 2700 \text{ years}}$
