Coordinate Geometry: Differentiation & Area

Question

The curve $C$ has equation $y = x^2 - 6x + 5$.

The point $P(2, -3)$ lies on $C$.

(a) Find an equation of the tangent to $C$ at $P$. [4]

The tangent to $C$ at $P$ crosses the x-axis at the point $A$.

(b) Find the coordinates of $A$. [1]

The normal to $C$ at $P$ crosses the y-axis at the point $B$.

(c) Find the area of triangle $PAB$. [5]

[10 marks]

Part (a) — Equation of tangent [4 marks] · 4 steps

STEP 1/4 M1

Differentiate to find gradient function

$$y = x^2 - 6x + 5$$

$$\frac{dy}{dx} = 2x - 6$$

Power rule: bring down the power, reduce by 1.
$\boxed{\frac{dy}{dx} = 2x - 6}$
Carry-in from previous step$\frac{dy}{dx} = 2x - 6$
STEP 2/4 A1

Evaluate gradient at $x = 2$

$$m_T = 2(2) - 6 = 4 - 6 = -2$$

Pitfall: Substitute the x-coordinate of P, not the y-coordinate.
$\boxed{m_T = -2}$
Carry-in from previous step$m_T = -2$
STEP 3/4 M1

Apply point-gradient formula

$$y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$$

$$y - (-3) = -2(x - 2)$$

$$y + 3 = -2x + 4$$

Pitfall: Watch signs carefully with $P(2, -3)$ — double negative gives $y + 3$.
$\boxed{y + 3 = -2(x - 2)}$
Carry-in from previous step$y + 3 = -2x + 4$
STEP 4/4 A1

Simplify to standard form

$$y = -2x + 4 - 3$$

$$y = -2x + 1$$

Accept equivalent forms: $2x + y = 1$ or $y = 1 - 2x$.
$\boxed{y = -2x + 1}$

Part (b) — Coordinates of A [1 marks] · 1 step

Tangent equation: $y = -2x + 1$
STEP 1/1 B1

Set $y = 0$ for x-intercept

$$0 = -2x + 1$$

$$2x = 1$$

$$x = \frac{1}{2}$$

Pitfall: x-axis means $y = 0$, not $x = 0$.
$\boxed{A = \left(\frac{1}{2}, 0\right)}$

Part (c) — Area of triangle PAB [5 marks] · 5 steps

$P(2, -3)$, $A\left(\frac{1}{2}, 0\right)$, tangent gradient $m_T = -2$
STEP 1/5 M1

Find gradient of normal

$$m_N = -\frac{1}{m_T} = -\frac{1}{-2} = \frac{1}{2}$$

Pitfall: Normal gradient is NEGATIVE reciprocal — don't forget the sign change!
$\boxed{m_N = \frac{1}{2}}$
Carry-in from previous step$m_N = \frac{1}{2}$
STEP 2/5 A1

Find equation of normal through $P(2, -3)$

$$y - (-3) = \frac{1}{2}(x - 2)$$

$$y + 3 = \frac{1}{2}x - 1$$

$$y = \frac{1}{2}x - 4$$

Same point-gradient method as part (a).
$\boxed{y = \frac{1}{2}x - 4}$
Carry-in from previous step$y = \frac{1}{2}x - 4$
STEP 3/5 A1

Find $B$ where normal crosses y-axis

Set $x = 0$:

$$y = \frac{1}{2}(0) - 4 = -4$$

Pitfall: y-axis means $x = 0$, not $y = 0$.
$\boxed{B = (0, -4)}$
Carry-in from previous step$P(2, -3)$, $A\left(\frac{1}{2}, 0\right)$, $B(0, -4)$
STEP 4/5 M1

Apply shoelace formula for triangle area

$$\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2}|x_P(y_A - y_B) + x_A(y_B - y_P) + x_B(y_P - y_A)|$$

$$= \frac{1}{2}|2(0 - (-4)) + \frac{1}{2}(-4 - (-3)) + 0(-3 - 0)|$$

$$= \frac{1}{2}|2(4) + \frac{1}{2}(-1) + 0|$$

Pitfall: With negative coordinates, track signs carefully in each bracket.
$\boxed{\frac{1}{2}|8 - \frac{1}{2}|}$
Carry-in from previous step$\frac{1}{2}|8 - \frac{1}{2}|$
STEP 5/5 A1

Simplify to final answer

$$= \frac{1}{2}\left|\frac{16}{2} - \frac{1}{2}\right| = \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{15}{2} = \frac{15}{4}$$

Can also write as $3.75$ or $3\frac{3}{4}$ square units.
$\boxed{\frac{15}{4}}$
