Sequences & Series — Binomial Expansion

Question

(a) Find the first 4 terms, in ascending powers of $x$, of the binomial expansion of $\left(2 - \frac{x}{4}\right)^{10}$, giving each term in simplest form. [4]

(b) Hence find the constant term in the expansion of $(3x - 1)\left(2 - \frac{x}{4}\right)^{10}$. [2]

[6 marks]

Part (a) — Binomial expansion [4 marks] · 4 steps

STEP 1/4 M1

Set up binomial expansion

For $(a + b)^n$, use $\binom{n}{r}a^{n-r}b^r$ with $a = 2$, $b = -\frac{x}{4}$, $n = 10$

$$\left(2 - \frac{x}{4}\right)^{10} = \sum_{r=0}^{10} \binom{10}{r}(2)^{10-r}\left(-\frac{x}{4}\right)^r$$

Pitfall: Must include the negative sign with $b = -\frac{x}{4}$, not just $\frac{x}{4}$.
$\boxed{a=2,\ b=-\frac{x}{4},\ n=10}$
Carry-in from previous step$a=2$, $b=-\frac{x}{4}$, $n=10$
STEP 2/4 A1

Calculate first two terms ($r=0, 1$)

$$r=0:\ \binom{10}{0}(2)^{10}(-\frac{x}{4})^0 = 1 \times 1024 \times 1 = 1024$$

$$r=1:\ \binom{10}{1}(2)^9(-\frac{x}{4})^1 = 10 \times 512 \times (-\frac{x}{4}) = -1280x$$

Pitfall: $512 \times (-\frac{1}{4}) = -128$, then $10 \times (-128) = -1280$. Track the negative!
$\boxed{1024 - 1280x}$
Carry-in from previous step$1024 - 1280x$
STEP 3/4 A1

Calculate third term ($r=2$)

$$r=2:\ \binom{10}{2}(2)^8\left(-\frac{x}{4}\right)^2 = \frac{10 \times 9}{2} \times 256 \times \frac{x^2}{16}$$

$$= 45 \times 256 \times \frac{1}{16} \times x^2 = 45 \times 16 \times x^2 = 720x^2$$

Pitfall: $\left(-\frac{x}{4}\right)^2 = +\frac{x^2}{16}$ — even power makes it positive!
$\boxed{1024 - 1280x + 720x^2}$
Carry-in from previous step$1024 - 1280x + 720x^2$
STEP 4/4 A1

Calculate fourth term ($r=3$)

$$r=3:\ \binom{10}{3}(2)^7\left(-\frac{x}{4}\right)^3 = \frac{10 \times 9 \times 8}{6} \times 128 \times \left(-\frac{x^3}{64}\right)$$

$$= 120 \times 128 \times (-\frac{1}{64}) \times x^3 = 120 \times (-2) \times x^3 = -240x^3$$

Pitfall: $\left(-\frac{x}{4}\right)^3 = -\frac{x^3}{64}$ — odd power keeps it negative!
$\boxed{1024 - 1280x + 720x^2 - 240x^3}$

Part (b) — Constant term [2 marks] · 2 steps

From (a): $\left(2 - \frac{x}{4}\right)^{10} = 1024 - 1280x + 720x^2 - 240x^3 + \ldots$
STEP 1/2 M1

Identify which products give constant terms

Expand $(3x - 1)(1024 - 1280x + 720x^2 - \ldots)$

Constant term arises from:

$$(-1) \times (\text{constant from expansion}) + (3x) \times (x^{-1}\text{ term})$$

The expansion has no $x^{-1}$ term, so only first product contributes.

Pitfall: Don't waste time looking for $x^{-1}$ terms — binomial expansions with positive integer powers only have non-negative powers of $x$.
$\boxed{\text{Only } (-1) \times 1024 \text{ contributes}}$
Carry-in from previous stepOnly $(-1) \times 1024$ contributes
STEP 2/2 A1

Calculate the constant term

$$\text{Constant term} = (-1) \times 1024 = -1024$$

Pitfall: Students sometimes multiply $3x \times (-1280x) = -3840x^2$ and think this is constant — it's not!
$\boxed{-1024}$
