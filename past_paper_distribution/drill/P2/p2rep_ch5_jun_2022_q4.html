Exponentials & Logs — Simultaneous Equations

Question

Given that $a$ and $b$ are positive constants and

$$a - b = 8$$

$$\log_4 a + \log_4 b = 3$$

find the value of $a$ and the value of $b$, giving your answers in fully simplified surd form. [6]

[6 marks]

Full Solution — Solve simultaneous equations [6 marks] · 6 steps

STEP 1/6 M1

Apply log product rule to combine logarithms

$$\log_4 a + \log_4 b = \log_4(ab)$$

So the second equation becomes:

$$\log_4(ab) = 3$$

Pitfall: $\log a + \log b \neq \log(a+b)$ — it's the product rule, not sum!
$\boxed{\log_4(ab) = 3}$
Carry-in from previous step$\log_4(ab) = 3$
STEP 2/6 A1

Convert logarithmic form to index form

$$\log_c N = P \iff N = c^P$$

Applying this:

$$ab = 4^3 = 64$$

Pitfall: Remember the base is 4, not 10 or $e$.
$\boxed{ab = 64}$
Carry-in from previous step$a - b = 8$ and $ab = 64$
STEP 3/6 M1

Substitute to form a quadratic

From $a - b = 8$: $a = 8 + b$

Substitute into $ab = 64$:

$$(8 + b)b = 64$$

$$b^2 + 8b - 64 = 0$$

Pitfall: Careful with signs when rearranging to standard form.
$\boxed{b^2 + 8b - 64 = 0}$
Carry-in from previous step$b^2 + 8b - 64 = 0$
STEP 4/6 M1

Apply quadratic formula

$$b = \frac{-8 \pm \sqrt{64 + 256}}{2} = \frac{-8 \pm \sqrt{320}}{2}$$

Simplify the surd: $\sqrt{320} = \sqrt{64 \times 5} = 8\sqrt{5}$

$$b = \frac{-8 \pm 8\sqrt{5}}{2} = -4 \pm 4\sqrt{5}$$

Pitfall: $\sqrt{320} \neq \sqrt{320}$ left unsimplified — find largest perfect square factor (64).
$\boxed{b = -4 \pm 4\sqrt{5}}$
Carry-in from previous step$b = -4 \pm 4\sqrt{5}$
STEP 5/6 A1

Select valid solution (domain check)

Since $b$ must be positive (for $\log_4 b$ to exist):

$b = -4 + 4\sqrt{5} \approx 4.94 > 0$ ✓

$b = -4 - 4\sqrt{5} < 0$ ✗ (reject)

$$b = -4 + 4\sqrt{5}$$

Pitfall: Forgetting domain restriction — both $a$ and $b$ must be positive for logarithms to be defined.
$\boxed{b = -4 + 4\sqrt{5}}$
Carry-in from previous step$b = -4 + 4\sqrt{5}$ and $a = 8 + b$
STEP 6/6 A1

Find $a$ using the linear equation

$$a = 8 + b = 8 + (-4 + 4\sqrt{5})$$

$$a = 4 + 4\sqrt{5}$$

Check: $a = 4 + 4\sqrt{5} > 0$ ✓

Pitfall: Always verify your answer is positive and check by substitution if time permits.
$\boxed{a = 4 + 4\sqrt{5}, \ b = -4 + 4\sqrt{5}}$
