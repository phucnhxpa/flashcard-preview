Binomial Expansion — $(2 - kx)^5$

Question

A curve $C$ has equation $y = f(x)$ where $f(x) = (2 - kx)^5$ and $k$ is a constant.

Given that when $f(x)$ is divided by $(4x - 5)$ the remainder is $\dfrac{243}{32}$,

(a) show that $k = \dfrac{2}{5}$ [2]

(b) Find the first three terms, in ascending powers of $x$, of the binomial expansion of $\left(2 - \dfrac{2x}{5}\right)^5$, giving each term in simplest form. [3]

Using the solution to part (b) and making your method clear,

(c) find the gradient of $C$ at the point where $x = 0$ [2]

[7 marks]

Part (a) — Show k = 2/5 [2 marks] · 2 steps

$f(x) = (2-kx)^5$; remainder when divided by $(4x-5)$ is $\frac{243}{32}$
STEP 1/2 M1

Remainder theorem: when $f(x) \div (4x-5)$, remainder $= f\!\left(\frac{5}{4}\right)$

$$f\!\left(\tfrac{5}{4}\right) = \left(2 - k \cdot \tfrac{5}{4}\right)^5 = \frac{243}{32}$$

Note $\frac{243}{32} = \left(\frac{3}{2}\right)^5$, so:

$$2 - \frac{5k}{4} = \frac{3}{2}$$

$(4x-5)=0 \Rightarrow x=\frac{5}{4}$; also $\left(\frac{3}{2}\right)^5 = \frac{243}{32}$
Carry-in:$2 - \frac{5k}{4} = \frac{3}{2}$
STEP 2/2 A1

$$\frac{5k}{4} = 2 - \frac{3}{2} = \frac{1}{2}$$

$$k = \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{4}{5} = \boxed{\frac{2}{5}}$$

Multiply both sides by $\frac{4}{5}$

Part (b) — First three terms of expansion [3 marks] · 3 steps

$f(x) = \left(2 - \frac{2x}{5}\right)^5$
STEP 1/3 M1

Binomial expansion: $(a+b)^5 = a^5 + 5a^4b + 10a^3b^2 + \ldots$

Here $a = 2$, $b = -\frac{2x}{5}$:

Term 1 (constant): $2^5 = 32$

Term 2 ($x^1$): $5 \cdot 2^4 \cdot \left(-\frac{2x}{5}\right) = 5 \cdot 16 \cdot \left(-\frac{2x}{5}\right)$

Carry-in:Term 2 $= 5\cdot16\cdot(-\frac{2x}{5})$
STEP 2/3 A1

Term 2: $= -\frac{5 \times 16 \times 2x}{5} = -32x$

Term 3 ($x^2$): $10 \cdot 2^3 \cdot \left(-\frac{2x}{5}\right)^2 = 10 \cdot 8 \cdot \frac{4x^2}{25}$

$= \frac{320x^2}{25} = \frac{64x^2}{5}$

Carry-in:Terms computed
STEP 3/3 A1
$\left(2-\dfrac{2x}{5}\right)^5 = 32 - 32x + \dfrac{64x^2}{5} + \ldots$

Part (c) — Gradient at x = 0 [2 marks] · 2 steps

$f(x) \approx 32 - 32x + \frac{64x^2}{5}$ near $x=0$
STEP 1/2 M1

Differentiate the expansion:

$$f'(x) = \frac{d}{dx}\!\left(32 - 32x + \frac{64x^2}{5} + \ldots\right)$$

$$= -32 + \frac{128x}{5} + \ldots$$

Differentiate term-by-term: $\frac{d}{dx}(32)=0$, $\frac{d}{dx}(-32x)=-32$, etc.
Carry-in:$f'(x) = -32 + \frac{128x}{5} + \ldots$
STEP 2/2 A1

At $x = 0$:

$$f'(0) = -32 + 0 = -32$$

Gradient of $C$ at $x=0$ is $\boxed{-32}$
