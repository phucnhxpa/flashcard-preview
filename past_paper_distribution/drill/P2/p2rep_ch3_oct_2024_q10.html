Coordinate Geometry — Circle, Tangent & Points of Contact

Question

The circle $C$ has equation $x^2 + y^2 + 4x - 30y + 209 = 0$

(a) Find (i) the coordinates of the centre of $C$, (ii) the exact value of the radius of $C$. [3]

The line $L$ has equation $y = mx + 1$, where $m$ is a constant.

Given that $L$ is the tangent to $C$ at the point $P$,

(b) show that $2m^2 - 7m - 22 = 0$ [3]

(c) Hence find the possible pairs of coordinates of $P$. [4]

[10 marks]

Part (a) — Centre and radius [3 marks] · 2 steps

$x^2 + y^2 + 4x - 30y + 209 = 0$
STEP 1/2 M1

Complete the square in $x$ and $y$:

$$(x^2 + 4x) + (y^2 - 30y) + 209 = 0$$

$$(x+2)^2 - 4 + (y-15)^2 - 225 + 209 = 0$$

$$(x+2)^2 + (y-15)^2 = 20$$

Half the coefficient of $x$: $4/2=2$; half of $-30$: $-15$
Carry-in:$(x+2)^2+(y-15)^2=20$
STEP 2/2 A1A1

Standard form $(x-a)^2+(y-b)^2=r^2$:

Centre $= (-2,\ 15)$, radius $= \sqrt{20} = 2\sqrt{5}$
$r^2 = 20$, so $r = \sqrt{20} = 2\sqrt{5}$ (leave in surd form)

Part (b) — Show 2m²−7m−22 = 0 [3 marks] · 3 steps

Centre $(-2, 15)$, $r^2 = 20$; tangent line $y = mx + 1$
STEP 1/3 M1

Distance from centre to tangent = radius

Rewrite $L$: $mx - y + 1 = 0$

Distance from $(-2, 15)$ to line:

$$d = \frac{|m(-2) - 15 + 1|}{\sqrt{m^2 + 1}} = \frac{|-2m - 14|}{\sqrt{m^2+1}}$$

Carry-in:$d = \frac{|2m+14|}{\sqrt{m^2+1}}$
STEP 2/3 M1

Set $d = r = \sqrt{20}$:

$$\frac{(2m+14)^2}{m^2+1} = 20$$

$$(2m+14)^2 = 20(m^2+1)$$

$$4m^2 + 56m + 196 = 20m^2 + 20$$

Carry-in:$4m^2+56m+196=20m^2+20$
STEP 3/3 A1

$$0 = 16m^2 - 56m - 176$$

Divide by 8:

$$\boxed{2m^2 - 7m - 22 = 0}$$

$16m^2 - 56m - 176 = 0$; divide by 8: $2m^2 - 7m - 22 = 0$ ✓

Part (c) — Coordinates of P [4 marks] · 4 steps

$2m^2 - 7m - 22 = 0$; tangent $y = mx + 1$; circle centre $(-2, 15)$
STEP 1/4 M1

Solve $2m^2 - 7m - 22 = 0$:

$$m = \frac{7 \pm \sqrt{49 + 176}}{4} = \frac{7 \pm \sqrt{225}}{4} = \frac{7 \pm 15}{4}$$

$m = \dfrac{22}{4} = \dfrac{11}{2}$ or $m = \dfrac{-8}{4} = -2$
Carry-in:$m = \frac{11}{2}$ or $m = -2$
STEP 2/4 M1

Find $P$ on each tangent. $P$ is foot of perpendicular from centre to tangent.

For $m = -2$: tangent $y = -2x + 1$

Perpendicular from $(-2,15)$ has gradient $\frac{1}{2}$:

$y - 15 = \frac{1}{2}(x+2) \Rightarrow y = \frac{x}{2} + 16$

Carry-in:$m=-2$: tangent $y=-2x+1$, perp $y=\frac{x}{2}+16$
STEP 3/4 A1

Solve $-2x+1 = \frac{x}{2}+16$: $-\frac{5x}{2}=15 \Rightarrow x=-6$, $y=13$

For $m=\frac{11}{2}$: tangent $y=\frac{11x}{2}+1$

Perp gradient $= -\frac{2}{11}$: $y-15=-\frac{2}{11}(x+2)$

Solve simultaneously: $x = 2$, $y = 12$

Carry-in:$P_1=(-6,13)$, $P_2=(2,12)$
STEP 4/4 A1
$P = (-6,\ 13)$ or $P = (2,\ 12)$
Verify: $(-6)^2+13^2+4(-6)-30(13)+209 = 36+169-24-390+209=0$ ✓
