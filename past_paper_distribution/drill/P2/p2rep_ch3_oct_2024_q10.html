Coordinate Geometry: Perpendicular Lines & Area

Question

The line $L_1$ has equation $3x - 2y + 12 = 0$.

The line $L_2$ passes through the point $P(4, 2)$ and is perpendicular to $L_1$.

Line $L_1$ crosses the $x$-axis at point $A$ and the $y$-axis at point $B$.

Line $L_2$ crosses the $x$-axis at point $C$.

(a) Find the equation of line $L_2$. [4]

(b) Find the coordinates of $A$, $B$ and $C$. [3]

(c) Calculate the area of triangle $ABC$. [3]

[10 marks]

Part (a) — Equation of L₂ [4 marks] · 4 steps

STEP 1/4 M1

Rearrange $L_1$ to find gradient

$$3x - 2y + 12 = 0$$

$$2y = 3x + 12$$

$$y = \frac{3}{2}x + 6$$

So $m_1 = \frac{3}{2}$

Must rearrange to $y = mx + c$ form to read off gradient — don't confuse coefficient of $x$ in original form.
$\boxed{m_1 = \frac{3}{2}}$
Carry-in from previous step$m_1 = \frac{3}{2}$
STEP 2/4 A1

Apply perpendicular gradient condition

$$m_1 \times m_2 = -1$$

$$\frac{3}{2} \times m_2 = -1$$

$$m_2 = -\frac{2}{3}$$

For perpendicular lines: flip and negate. Common error: using same gradient (parallel) or just negating without flipping.
$\boxed{m_2 = -\frac{2}{3}}$
Carry-in from previous step$m_2 = -\frac{2}{3}$
STEP 3/4 M1

Use point-gradient formula with $P(4, 2)$

$$y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$$

$$y - 2 = -\frac{2}{3}(x - 4)$$

Multiply through by 3:

$$3(y - 2) = -2(x - 4)$$

$$3y - 6 = -2x + 8$$

Multiply by denominator early to avoid fraction errors in expansion.
$\boxed{3y - 6 = -2x + 8}$
Carry-in from previous step$3y - 6 = -2x + 8$
STEP 4/4 A1

Rearrange to general form $ax + by + c = 0$

$$3y - 6 = -2x + 8$$

$$2x + 3y - 6 - 8 = 0$$

$$2x + 3y - 14 = 0$$

Check: substitute $P(4,2)$ back in: $2(4) + 3(2) - 14 = 8 + 6 - 14 = 0$ ✓
$\boxed{2x + 3y - 14 = 0}$

Part (b) — Coordinates of A, B and C [3 marks] · 3 steps

$L_1: 3x - 2y + 12 = 0$, $L_2: 2x + 3y - 14 = 0$
STEP 1/3 B1

Find $A$ (x-intercept of $L_1$): set $y = 0$

$$3x - 2(0) + 12 = 0$$

$$3x = -12$$

$$x = -4$$

x-intercept means the line crosses the x-axis where $y = 0$.
$\boxed{A(-4, 0)}$
Carry-in from previous step$A(-4, 0)$
STEP 2/3 B1

Find $B$ (y-intercept of $L_1$): set $x = 0$

$$3(0) - 2y + 12 = 0$$

$$-2y = -12$$

$$y = 6$$

y-intercept means the line crosses the y-axis where $x = 0$.
$\boxed{B(0, 6)}$
Carry-in from previous step$A(-4, 0)$, $B(0, 6)$
STEP 3/3 B1

Find $C$ (x-intercept of $L_2$): set $y = 0$

$$2x + 3(0) - 14 = 0$$

$$2x = 14$$

$$x = 7$$

Use the equation from part (a) for $L_2$.
$\boxed{C(7, 0)}$

Part (c) — Area of triangle ABC [3 marks] · 3 steps

$A(-4, 0)$, $B(0, 6)$, $C(7, 0)$
STEP 1/3 M1

Identify base and height

Notice $A(-4, 0)$ and $C(7, 0)$ both lie on the x-axis.

Use $AC$ as the base of the triangle.

$$\text{Base } AC = |7 - (-4)| = |7 + 4| = 11$$

Spotting that two points share the same y-coordinate simplifies the problem massively — no need for the shoelace formula.
$\boxed{\text{Base} = 11}$
Carry-in from previous step$\text{Base} = 11$
STEP 2/3 M1

Find perpendicular height from $B$ to base

Height = perpendicular distance from $B(0, 6)$ to the x-axis.

$$\text{Height} = |6 - 0| = 6$$

When base lies on x-axis, height is simply the y-coordinate of the third vertex.
$\boxed{\text{Height} = 6}$
Carry-in from previous step$\text{Base} = 11$, $\text{Height} = 6$
STEP 3/3 A1

Apply triangle area formula

$$\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{height}$$

$$\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times 11 \times 6$$

$$\text{Area} = \frac{66}{2} = 33$$

Don't forget the $\frac{1}{2}$ — a classic error under exam pressure!
$\boxed{33 \text{ square units}}$
