Area Between Two Curves — Verification, Intersection & Integration

Question

Figure 2 shows a sketch of part of the curves $C_1$ and $C_2$ with equations

$C_1{:}\ y = 10x - x^2 - 8\quad (x \geqslant 0)$

$C_2{:}\ y = x^3\quad (x \geqslant 0)$

The curves $C_1$ and $C_2$ intersect at the points $A$ and $B$.

(a) Verify that the point $A$ has coordinates $(1, 1)$. [1]

(b) Use algebra to find the coordinates of the point $B$. [6]

The finite region $R$ is bounded by $C_1$ and $C_2$.

(c) Use calculus to find the exact area of $R$. [5]

[12 marks]

(a) Verify A(1, 1) lies on both curves [1 mark] · 1 step

STEP 1
Substitute x = 1 into both curves
$C_1$: $y = 10(1) - (1)^2 - 8 = 10 - 1 - 8 = 1$ ✓
$C_2$: $y = (1)^3 = 1$ ✓
$(1, 1)$ lies on both $C_1$ and $C_2$. ✓

(b) Find coordinates of B [6 marks] · 3 steps

STEP 1
Set curves equal
$$10x - x^2 - 8 = x^3$$ $$x^3 + x^2 - 10x + 8 = 0$$
Rearrange so all terms are on one side.
STEP 2
Factor out (x − 1) since x = 1 is a root
Divide $x^3 + x^2 - 10x + 8$ by $(x - 1)$: $$(x-1)(x^2 + 2x - 8) = 0$$ Factor the quadratic: $$(x-1)(x+4)(x-2) = 0$$
STEP 3
Find B (reject negative x)
$x = 1$ (point A), $x = -4$ (rejected, $x \geqslant 0$), $x = 2$
At $x = 2$: $y = 2^3 = 8$
$B = (2,\, 8)$

(c) Exact area of R [5 marks] · 3 steps

$C_1$ is above $C_2$ between $x = 1$ and $x = 2$ (check midpoint: at $x = 1.5$, $C_1 = 10(1.5)-(1.5)^2-8 = 6.75$ vs $C_2 = 3.375$).
STEP 1
Set up the integral
$$\text{Area} = \int_1^2 \bigl[(10x - x^2 - 8) - x^3\bigr]\,dx$$ $$= \int_1^2 (10x - x^2 - 8 - x^3)\,dx$$
STEP 2
Integrate
$$= \left[5x^2 - \frac{x^3}{3} - 8x - \frac{x^4}{4}\right]_1^2$$
STEP 3
Apply limits
At $x=2$: $5(4) - \tfrac{8}{3} - 16 - 4 = 20 - \tfrac{8}{3} - 20 = -\tfrac{8}{3}$
At $x=1$: $5 - \tfrac{1}{3} - 8 - \tfrac{1}{4} = -3 - \tfrac{1}{3} - \tfrac{1}{4} = -3 - \tfrac{7}{12} = -\tfrac{43}{12}$
Area $= -\tfrac{8}{3} - (-\tfrac{43}{12}) = -\tfrac{32}{12} + \tfrac{43}{12} = \tfrac{11}{12}$
Exact area $= \dfrac{11}{12}$
