Question

The curve $C$ has equation $y = x^2 e^{2x}$.

(a) Find $\int x^2 e^{2x} \, dx$. [8]

(b) Hence find the exact value of the area of the region bounded by the curve $C$, the $x$-axis, and the lines $x=0$ and $x=1$. [4]

[12 marks]

Part (a) â€” Indefinite integral [8 marks] Â· 6 steps

STEP 1/6 M1

Set up first integration by parts

Use $\int u \frac{dv}{dx} \, dx = uv - \int v \frac{du}{dx} \, dx$ with LIATE order:

$$u = x^2 \implies \frac{du}{dx} = 2x$$

$$\frac{dv}{dx} = e^{2x} \implies v = \frac{1}{2}e^{2x}$$

Choose algebraic for $u$ (differentiates to simpler form) and exponential for $\frac{dv}{dx}$.
$\boxed{u = x^2, \quad v = \frac{1}{2}e^{2x}}$
Carry-in from previous step$u = x^2, \quad v = \frac{1}{2}e^{2x}$
STEP 2/6 A1

Apply first integration by parts

$$\int x^2 e^{2x} \, dx = x^2 \cdot \frac{1}{2}e^{2x} - \int \frac{1}{2}e^{2x} \cdot 2x \, dx$$

$$= \frac{1}{2}x^2 e^{2x} - \int x e^{2x} \, dx$$

The new integral $\int x e^{2x} \, dx$ still has a product â€” need parts again.
$\boxed{\frac{1}{2}x^2 e^{2x} - \int x e^{2x} \, dx}$
Carry-in from previous step$\frac{1}{2}x^2 e^{2x} - \int x e^{2x} \, dx$
STEP 3/6 M1

Set up second integration by parts

For $\int x e^{2x} \, dx$:

$$u = x \implies \frac{du}{dx} = 1$$

$$\frac{dv}{dx} = e^{2x} \implies v = \frac{1}{2}e^{2x}$$

Same logic: algebraic $x$ differentiates to constant, exponential integrates cleanly.
$\boxed{u = x, \quad v = \frac{1}{2}e^{2x}}$
Carry-in from previous step$u = x, \quad v = \frac{1}{2}e^{2x}$
STEP 4/6 A1

Apply second integration by parts

$$\int x e^{2x} \, dx = x \cdot \frac{1}{2}e^{2x} - \int \frac{1}{2}e^{2x} \cdot 1 \, dx$$

$$= \frac{1}{2}x e^{2x} - \frac{1}{2} \int e^{2x} \, dx$$

$$= \frac{1}{2}x e^{2x} - \frac{1}{4}e^{2x}$$

Don't forget the $\frac{1}{2}$ coefficient when integrating $e^{2x}$.
$\boxed{\frac{1}{2}x e^{2x} - \frac{1}{4}e^{2x}}$
Carry-in from previous step$\int x e^{2x} \, dx = \frac{1}{2}x e^{2x} - \frac{1}{4}e^{2x}$
STEP 5/6 M1A1

Substitute back into first result

$$\int x^2 e^{2x} \, dx = \frac{1}{2}x^2 e^{2x} - \left(\frac{1}{2}x e^{2x} - \frac{1}{4}e^{2x}\right)$$

$$= \frac{1}{2}x^2 e^{2x} - \frac{1}{2}x e^{2x} + \frac{1}{4}e^{2x}$$

Distribute the negative sign carefully â€” common source of sign errors.
$\boxed{\frac{1}{2}x^2 e^{2x} - \frac{1}{2}x e^{2x} + \frac{1}{4}e^{2x}}$
Carry-in from previous step$\frac{1}{2}x^2 e^{2x} - \frac{1}{2}x e^{2x} + \frac{1}{4}e^{2x}$
STEP 6/6 A1

Factor and add constant

Factor out $\frac{1}{4}e^{2x}$:

$$= \frac{1}{4}e^{2x}(2x^2 - 2x + 1) + C$$

Always include $+C$ for indefinite integrals â€” marks are lost without it.
$\boxed{\frac{1}{4}e^{2x}(2x^2 - 2x + 1) + C}$

Part (b) â€” Definite integral for area [4 marks] Â· 4 steps

From (a): $\int x^2 e^{2x} \, dx = \frac{1}{4}e^{2x}(2x^2 - 2x + 1) + C$
STEP 1/4 M1

Set up definite integral

Area $= \int_0^1 x^2 e^{2x} \, dx = \left[\frac{1}{4}e^{2x}(2x^2 - 2x + 1)\right]_0^1$

No $+C$ needed for definite integrals â€” it cancels in subtraction.
$\boxed{\left[\frac{1}{4}e^{2x}(2x^2 - 2x + 1)\right]_0^1}$
Carry-in from previous step$\left[\frac{1}{4}e^{2x}(2x^2 - 2x + 1)\right]_0^1$
STEP 2/4 A1

Evaluate at upper limit $x=1$

$$\frac{1}{4}e^{2(1)}(2(1)^2 - 2(1) + 1) = \frac{1}{4}e^2(2 - 2 + 1) = \frac{1}{4}e^2$$

Bracket simplifies to $(1)$ â€” check arithmetic carefully.
$\boxed{\frac{1}{4}e^2}$
Carry-in from previous stepUpper limit: $\frac{1}{4}e^2$
STEP 3/4 A1

Evaluate at lower limit $x=0$

$$\frac{1}{4}e^{2(0)}(2(0)^2 - 2(0) + 1) = \frac{1}{4}e^0(1) = \frac{1}{4}(1)(1) = \frac{1}{4}$$

Remember $e^0 = 1$, not $0$ â€” a frequent error.
$\boxed{\frac{1}{4}}$
Carry-in from previous stepUpper: $\frac{1}{4}e^2$, Lower: $\frac{1}{4}$
STEP 4/4 A1

Subtract to find area

$$\text{Area} = \frac{1}{4}e^2 - \frac{1}{4} = \frac{1}{4}(e^2 - 1)$$

Leave answer exact with $e^2$ â€” do not convert to decimal.
$\boxed{\frac{1}{4}(e^2 - 1)}$
