Trigonometric Identities & Equation Solving

Question

(a) Show that the equation $$\frac{3\sin\theta\cos\theta}{2\sin\theta - 1} = \frac{5\sin\theta}{\cos\theta}$$ can be written as $$3\sin^3\theta + 10\sin^2\theta - 8\sin\theta = 0$$ [4]

(b) Hence solve, for $-\frac{\pi}{4} < x < \frac{3\pi}{2}$, $$\frac{3\sin 2x\cos 2x}{2\sin 2x - 1} = \frac{5\sin 2x}{\cos 2x}$$ giving your answers to 3 decimal places where appropriate. [5]

[9 marks]

Part (a) — Show the given form [4 marks] · 3 steps

STEP 1/3 M1

Cross-multiply to clear fractions

$$\frac{3\sin\theta\cos\theta}{2\sin\theta - 1} = \frac{5\sin\theta}{\cos\theta}$$

$$3\sin\theta\cos^2\theta = 5\sin\theta(2\sin\theta - 1)$$

Don't forget: $\tan\theta = \frac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta}$ — the RHS is effectively $5\tan\theta$.
$\boxed{3\sin\theta\cos^2\theta = 5\sin\theta(2\sin\theta - 1)}$
Carry-in from previous step$3\sin\theta\cos^2\theta = 5\sin\theta(2\sin\theta - 1)$
STEP 2/3 M1

Substitute $\cos^2\theta = 1 - \sin^2\theta$

$$3\sin\theta(1 - \sin^2\theta) = 5\sin\theta(2\sin\theta - 1)$$

$$3\sin\theta - 3\sin^3\theta = 10\sin^2\theta - 5\sin\theta$$

This is the key identity for converting to a single trig function.
$\boxed{3\sin\theta - 3\sin^3\theta = 10\sin^2\theta - 5\sin\theta}$
Carry-in from previous step$3\sin\theta - 3\sin^3\theta = 10\sin^2\theta - 5\sin\theta$
STEP 3/3 A1

Rearrange to standard form

Bring all terms to one side:

$$3\sin\theta - 3\sin^3\theta - 10\sin^2\theta + 5\sin\theta = 0$$

$$-3\sin^3\theta - 10\sin^2\theta + 8\sin\theta = 0$$

Multiply by $-1$:

$$3\sin^3\theta + 10\sin^2\theta - 8\sin\theta = 0 \quad \square$$

Check signs carefully — a common error is getting $+8\sin\theta$ vs $-8\sin\theta$.
$\boxed{3\sin^3\theta + 10\sin^2\theta - 8\sin\theta = 0}$

Part (b) — Solve for x [5 marks] · 5 steps

$3\sin^3\theta + 10\sin^2\theta - 8\sin\theta = 0$ from part (a)
STEP 1/5 M1

Set up substitution and adjust range

Let $y = 2x$. Since $-\frac{\pi}{4} < x < \frac{3\pi}{2}$:

$$-\frac{\pi}{2} < y < 3\pi$$

The equation becomes:

$$3\sin^3 y + 10\sin^2 y - 8\sin y = 0$$

Always double the range when substituting $y = 2x$ — missing this loses solutions!
$\boxed{-\frac{\pi}{2} < y < 3\pi}$
Carry-in from previous step$3\sin^3 y + 10\sin^2 y - 8\sin y = 0$ with $-\frac{\pi}{2} < y < 3\pi$
STEP 2/5 M1

Factorise the cubic

$$\sin y(3\sin^2 y + 10\sin y - 8) = 0$$

$$\sin y(3\sin y - 2)(\sin y + 4) = 0$$

Three cases: $\sin y = 0$, $\sin y = \frac{2}{3}$, $\sin y = -4$ (impossible)

$\sin y = 0$ gives valid solutions — both sides of original equation equal zero!
$\boxed{\sin y = 0 \text{ or } \sin y = \frac{2}{3}}$
Carry-in from previous step$\sin y = 0$ with $-\frac{\pi}{2} < y < 3\pi$
STEP 3/5 A1

Solve $\sin y = 0$

In $-\frac{\pi}{2} < y < 3\pi$:

$$y = 0, \pi, 2\pi$$

Convert to $x = \frac{y}{2}$:

$$x = 0, \frac{\pi}{2}, \pi$$

Verify these work: when $\sin y = 0$, we have $\cos y = \pm 1 \neq 0$, so $\tan y$ exists and both sides equal 0.
$\boxed{x = 0, \frac{\pi}{2}, \pi}$
Carry-in from previous step$\sin y = \frac{2}{3}$ with $-\frac{\pi}{2} < y < 3\pi$
STEP 4/5 A1

Solve $\sin y = \frac{2}{3}$

Reference angle: $y_1 = \arcsin\left(\frac{2}{3}\right) \approx 0.7297$

In $-\frac{\pi}{2} < y < 3\pi$:

$$y = 0.7297, \; \pi - 0.7297, \; 2\pi + 0.7297, \; 3\pi - 0.7297$$

$$y \approx 0.7297, \; 2.4119, \; 7.0129, \; 8.6951$$

The range extends past $2\pi$ — don't forget solutions in the third revolution!
$\boxed{y \approx 0.730, 2.412, 7.013, 8.695}$
Carry-in from previous step$y \approx 0.730, 2.412, 7.013, 8.695$ and $x = 0, \frac{\pi}{2}, \pi$
STEP 5/5 A1

Convert to x and give final answers

From $\sin y = \frac{2}{3}$: $x = \frac{y}{2}$

$$x \approx 0.365, \; 1.206, \; 3.506, \; 4.348$$

Combined with exact values:

$$x = 0, \; 0.365, \; 1.206, \; \frac{\pi}{2}, \; \pi, \; 3.506, \; 4.348$$

Give exact values where possible ($0, \frac{\pi}{2}, \pi$) and 3 d.p. for others.
$\boxed{x = 0, \; 0.365, \; 1.206, \; \frac{\pi}{2}, \; \pi, \; 3.506, \; 4.348}$
