Coordinate Geometry — Circle & Tangent

Question

The points P(−11, 6) and Q(−3, 12) lie on the circle C.

Given that PQ is a diameter of C,

(a) (i) find the coordinates of the centre of C, [2]

(ii) find the radius of C. [2]

(b) Hence find an equation of C. [2]

(c) Find an equation of the tangent to C at the point Q, giving your answer in the form ax + by + c = 0, where a, b and c are integers. [3]

[9 marks]

Part (a)(i) — Centre of circle [2 marks] · 2 steps

P(−11, 6), Q(−3, 12), PQ is diameter
STEP 1/2 M1

Formula: Centre = midpoint of diameter

$$\text{Midpoint} = \left(\frac{x_1 + x_2}{2}, \frac{y_1 + y_2}{2}\right)$$

$$= \left(\frac{-11 + (-3)}{2}, \frac{6 + 12}{2}\right)$$

Watch signs: −11 + (−3) = −14, not −8
Carry-in from previous step\frac{-14}{2}, \frac{18}{2}
STEP 2/2 A1

Simplify:

$$= \left(\frac{-14}{2}, \frac{18}{2}\right) = (-7, 9)$$

Centre lies at midpoint — always check it's between P and Q
$\boxed{(-7, 9)}$

Part (a)(ii) — Radius of circle [2 marks] · 2 steps

Centre (−7, 9) from (a)(i)
Carry-in from previous stepCentre = (-7, 9)
STEP 1/2 M1

Formula: Radius = distance from centre to Q

$$r = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2}$$

$$= \sqrt{(-3 - (-7))^2 + (12 - 9)^2}$$

$$= \sqrt{(4)^2 + (3)^2}$$

Common error: using diameter length instead of radius
Carry-in from previous step\sqrt{16 + 9}
STEP 2/2 A1

Evaluate:

$$= \sqrt{16 + 9} = \sqrt{25} = 5$$

Recognise 3-4-5 Pythagorean triple
$\boxed{5}$

Part (b) — Equation of circle [2 marks] · 2 steps

From (a): centre (−7, 9), radius 5
Carry-in from previous stepCentre = (-7, 9), r = 5
STEP 1/2 M1

Formula: Circle equation with centre (a, b) and radius r:

$$(x - a)^2 + (y - b)^2 = r^2$$

Substituting centre (−7, 9):

$$(x - (-7))^2 + (y - 9)^2 = r^2$$

Double negative: x − (−7) becomes x + 7
Carry-in from previous step(x + 7)^2 + (y - 9)^2 = r^2
STEP 2/2 A1

Substitute r = 5:

$$(x + 7)^2 + (y - 9)^2 = 25$$

Remember: RHS is r², not r. Here 5² = 25
$\boxed{(x + 7)^2 + (y - 9)^2 = 25}$

Part (c) — Tangent at Q [3 marks] · 3 steps

From (a): centre (−7, 9), Q(−3, 12)
Carry-in from previous stepCentre = (-7, 9), Q = (-3, 12)
STEP 1/3 M1

Key property: Tangent ⊥ radius at point of contact

$$m_{CQ} = \frac{12 - 9}{-3 - (-7)} = \frac{3}{4}$$

Perpendicular gradient:

$$m_{\text{tangent}} = -\frac{1}{m_{CQ}} = -\frac{4}{3}$$

Perpendicular means negative reciprocal: flip and change sign
Carry-in from previous stepm_{\text{tangent}} = -\frac{4}{3}, Q = (-3, 12)
STEP 2/3 M1

Line equation: Using y − y₁ = m(x − x₁) at Q(−3, 12):

$$y - 12 = -\frac{4}{3}(x - (-3))$$

$$y - 12 = -\frac{4}{3}(x + 3)$$

Multiply by 3:

$$3y - 36 = -4(x + 3)$$

Multiply through to clear fractions before rearranging
Carry-in from previous step3y - 36 = -4x - 12
STEP 3/3 A1

Rearrange to ax + by + c = 0:

$$3y - 36 = -4x - 12$$

$$4x + 3y - 36 + 12 = 0$$

$$4x + 3y - 24 = 0$$

Check: a, b, c must be integers with no common factor
$\boxed{4x + 3y - 24 = 0}$
