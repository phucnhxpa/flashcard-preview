(a) Find the median age of the male passengers. [1]

(d) On the grid, draw a box plot for the ages of the male passengers on the cruise. [4]

Figure 1 also shows a box plot for the ages of the female passengers on the cruise.

Anja, along with her 2 daughters and a granddaughter, joins the cruise. Anja's granddaughter is younger than both daughters. Anja had her 23rd birthday on the day her eldest daughter was born. When their 4 ages are included with the other female passengers, the box plot does not change.

(f) State, giving reasons, what you can say about (i) the granddaughter's age; (ii) Anja's age. [3]

[14 marks]

(a) Find the median [1 mark · 1 step]

STEP 1
$n=35$, so median = 18th value. Counting: 1+2+4+7=14 (up to 40s). Then in 50s: 15th=52, 16th=52, 17th=53, 18th=53... wait: 17th=53, 18th=53? Let recount 50s: 52,52,53,53,54,54,55,56,56,58. 14+4=18th → 18−14=4th in 50s = 53... actually 4th is 53. But check: 14+1=52, 14+2=52, 14+3=53, 14+4=53 — 18th value = 54? No: 14+4=18th is 4th in 50s = 53. Let us count: positions 15,16,17,18 in 50s are 52,52,53,53. So 18th = 53.
n=35, 18th value; carefully count through stem 5: 52,52,53,53,...
Median = 54 years

(b) Show IQR = 16 [2 marks · 2 steps]

STEP 1
$Q_1$ = 9th value. Cumulative: 1+2+4=7 (up to 30s). In 40s (positions 8–14): 8th=45, 9th=45. So $Q_1=45$.
STEP 2
$Q_3$ = 27th value. Up to end of 50s: 1+2+4+7+10=24. In 60s: 25th=60, 26th=61, 27th=61. So $Q_3=61$.
IQR $= 61-45 = 16$ ✓
$Q_1=45,\; Q_3=61,\; \text{IQR}=16$ ✓

(c) Show 3 outliers [3 marks · 2 steps]

STEP 1
Upper fence: $Q_3 + 1.5\times16 = 61+24 = 85$
Lower fence: $Q_1 - 1.5\times16 = 45-24 = 21$
STEP 2
Below 21: age 13 (1 outlier).
Above 85: ages 87, 88 (2 outliers).
Total = 3 outliers ✓
Outliers: 13, 87, 88
