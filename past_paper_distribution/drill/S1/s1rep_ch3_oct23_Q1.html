Events $A$ and $B$ with $P(A) = 0.4$, $P(B) = 0.5$, $P(A \cap B) = 0.15$

Question

Two events $A$ and $B$ are such that

$P(A) = 0.4$,   $P(B) = 0.5$,   $P(A \cap B) = 0.15$

(a) Draw a Venn diagram to represent these two events and the sample space $S$.

(b) Find $P(A \cup B)$.

(c) Find $P(A' \cap B')$.

(d) Find $P(A \mid B')$.

(e) Determine whether or not $A$ and $B$ are independent. Give a reason for your answer.

[10 marks]

(a) [2 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2 M1

Calculate the probability for each region of the Venn diagram:

Region: $A$ only = $P(A) - P(A \cap B) = 0.4 - 0.15 = 0.25$

Region: $B$ only = $P(B) - P(A \cap B) = 0.5 - 0.15 = 0.35$

Region: $A \cap B$ (intersection) = $0.15$

Exam pitfall: The values inside each circle region must NOT include the intersection twice.
$\boxed{A \text{ only} = 0.25, \; B \text{ only} = 0.35, \; A \cap B = 0.15}$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $A$ only $= 0.25$, $B$ only $= 0.35$, $A \cap B = 0.15$
STEP 2/2 A1

Region outside both circles:

$P(A' \cap B') = 1 - P(A \cup B) = 1 - (0.25 + 0.15 + 0.35) = 1 - 0.75 = 0.25$

Exam pitfall: Always include the outside region — probabilities in all four regions must sum to 1.
$\boxed{\text{Venn diagram with } 0.25, 0.15, 0.35, 0.25}$

(b) [2 marks] · 2 steps

From (a): Venn diagram regions calculated
STEP 1/2 M1

Formula for union: $P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cap B)$

Substituting the given values:

$P(A \cup B) = 0.4 + 0.5 - 0.15$

Exam pitfall: You MUST subtract $P(A \cap B)$ to avoid double-counting the intersection.
$\boxed{P(A \cup B) = 0.75}$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $P(A \cup B) = 0.4 + 0.5 - 0.15$
STEP 2/2 A1

Complete the calculation:

$P(A \cup B) = 0.9 - 0.15 = 0.75$

Verification from Venn diagram: $0.25 + 0.15 + 0.35 = 0.75$ ✓

Exam tip: Cross-check with Venn diagram values — must match the sum of three inner regions.
$\boxed{P(A \cup B) = 0.75}$

(c) [2 marks] · 2 steps

From (b): $P(A \cup B) = 0.75$
STEP 1/2 M1

By De Morgan's Law: $A' \cap B' = (A \cup B)'$

Formula: $P(A' \cap B') = P((A \cup B)') = 1 - P(A \cup B)$

Exam pitfall: $A' \cap B'$ is the region OUTSIDE both circles — not $P(A') \times P(B')$.
$\boxed{P(A' \cap B') = 1 - P(A \cup B)}$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $P(A' \cap B') = 1 - P(A \cup B)$
STEP 2/2 A1

Substituting $P(A \cup B) = 0.75$:

$P(A' \cap B') = 1 - 0.75 = 0.25$

This matches the outside region in our Venn diagram ✓

Exam tip: This is always the region outside both circles in the sample space.
$\boxed{P(A' \cap B') = 0.25}$

(d) [2 marks] · 2 steps

From (a): Venn diagram with $P(A \cap B') = 0.25$, $P(B') = 0.5$
STEP 1/2 M1

Formula for conditional probability: $P(A \mid B') = \dfrac{P(A \cap B')}{P(B')}$

Finding the required probabilities:

$P(B') = 1 - P(B) = 1 - 0.5 = 0.5$

$P(A \cap B')$ = region in $A$ but NOT in $B$ = $P(A) - P(A \cap B) = 0.4 - 0.15 = 0.25$

Exam pitfall: $P(A \cap B')$ is the "A only" region, NOT $P(A) \times P(B')$.
$\boxed{P(A \cap B') = 0.25, \; P(B') = 0.5}$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $P(A \cap B') = 0.25$, $P(B') = 0.5$
STEP 2/2 A1

Substituting into the conditional probability formula:

$P(A \mid B') = \dfrac{0.25}{0.5} = \dfrac{25}{50} = \dfrac{1}{2} = 0.5$

Exam tip: Read carefully — $P(A \mid B')$ conditions on $B'$, not on $B$.
$\boxed{P(A \mid B') = 0.5}$

(e) [2 marks] · 2 steps

From question: $P(A) = 0.4$, $P(B) = 0.5$, $P(A \cap B) = 0.15$
STEP 1/2 M1

Test for independence: Events $A$ and $B$ are independent if and only if:

$P(A \cap B) = P(A) \times P(B)$

Calculate $P(A) \times P(B)$:

$P(A) \times P(B) = 0.4 \times 0.5 = 0.20$

Exam pitfall: Independence requires EXACT equality — close is not enough.
$\boxed{P(A) \times P(B) = 0.20}$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $P(A) \times P(B) = 0.20$
STEP 2/2 A1

Compare with given value:

$P(A \cap B) = 0.15$

$P(A) \times P(B) = 0.20$

Since $0.15 \neq 0.20$:

$P(A \cap B) \neq P(A) \times P(B)$

Therefore $A$ and $B$ are NOT independent.

Exam pitfall: You MUST show the numerical comparison and state the conclusion with reason.
$\boxed{\text{Not independent, since } P(A \cap B) = 0.15 \neq 0.20 = P(A) \times P(B)}$
