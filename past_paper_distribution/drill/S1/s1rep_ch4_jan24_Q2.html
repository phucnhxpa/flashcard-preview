Kolkata Temperatures — PMCC & Linear Coding

Question

The average minimum monthly temperature, $x$°F, and average maximum monthly temperature, $y$°F, in Kolkata were recorded for 12 months.

Summary statistics: $\Sigma x = 862$, $\Sigma x^2 = 62802$, $S_{yy} = 413.67$, $S_{xy} = 512.67$, $n = 12$

Conversion formula: $c = \frac{5}{9}(f - 32)$

(a) (i) Find $\bar{x}$    (ii) Show $\sigma = 8.57$°F    (b) Find PMCC $r$    (c) Find mean and SD in °C    (d) State PMCC between $u$ and $v$ (°C) with reason

[12 marks]

(a) [3 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2 B1

Formula: $\bar{x} = \dfrac{\Sigma x}{n}$

Substitute: $\bar{x} = \dfrac{862}{12} = 71.8\overline{3}$

Exam pitfall: do not round prematurely — keep full precision for subsequent parts.
$\boxed{\bar{x} = 71.8°\text{F}}$
Carry-in from previous stepFrom STEP 1: $\bar{x} = 71.8\overline{3}$°F
STEP 2/2 M1 A1

Calculate $S_{xx}$: $S_{xx} = \Sigma x^2 - \dfrac{(\Sigma x)^2}{n} = 62802 - \dfrac{862^2}{12}$

$= 62802 - \dfrac{743044}{12} = 62802 - 61920.\overline{3} = 881.6\overline{7}$

Standard deviation: $\sigma = \sqrt{\dfrac{S_{xx}}{n}} = \sqrt{\dfrac{881.67}{12}} = \sqrt{73.47} = 8.571...$

Exam pitfall: use $\sigma = \sqrt{S_{xx}/n}$ (population SD), NOT $\sqrt{S_{xx}/(n-1)}$ — the question says "12 months recorded", a complete dataset.
$\sigma = 8.57$°F ✓ (shown to 3 s.f.)

(b) [3 marks] · 2 steps

From (a): $S_{xx} = 881.67$
STEP 1/2 M1

PMCC formula: $r = \dfrac{S_{xy}}{\sqrt{S_{xx} \cdot S_{yy}}}$

Substitute: $r = \dfrac{512.67}{\sqrt{881.67 \times 413.67}}$

Exam pitfall: make sure to use $S_{xy}$ (not $\Sigma xy$) and both $S_{xx}$, $S_{yy}$ (not raw $\Sigma x^2$, $\Sigma y^2$).
Carry-in from previous stepFrom STEP 1: denominator = $\sqrt{881.67 \times 413.67}$
STEP 2/2 A1 A1

$\sqrt{881.67 \times 413.67} = \sqrt{364861} = 604.0$

$r = \dfrac{512.67}{604.0} = 0.8489...$

Exam pitfall: $r$ must satisfy $-1 \le r \le 1$; if you get outside this range, recheck $S_{xx}$ and $S_{yy}$.
$\boxed{r = 0.849}$

(c) [4 marks] · 2 steps

From (a): $\bar{x} = 71.8\overline{3}$°F, $\sigma_x = 8.571$°F; formula: $c = \frac{5}{9}(f - 32)$
STEP 1/2 M1 A1

Linear transformation of mean: $\bar{c} = \dfrac{5}{9}(\bar{f} - 32)$

$\bar{c} = \dfrac{5}{9}(71.8\overline{3} - 32) = \dfrac{5}{9}(39.8\overline{3})$

$= \dfrac{5}{9} \times 39.8\overline{3} = 22.13...$°C

Exam pitfall: subtract 32 BEFORE multiplying by 5/9 — the additive shift changes the mean but not the SD.
$\boxed{\bar{c} = 22.1°\text{C}}$
Carry-in from previous stepFrom STEP 1: $\bar{c} = 22.1$°C
STEP 2/2 M1 A1

SD under linear transformation $c = af + b$: $\sigma_c = |a| \cdot \sigma_f$

Here $a = \dfrac{5}{9}$, $b = -\dfrac{160}{9}$ (constant shift has no effect on SD)

$\sigma_c = \dfrac{5}{9} \times 8.571 = 4.762$°C

Exam pitfall: the $-32$ shift does NOT affect the SD; only the multiplicative factor $\frac{5}{9}$ scales it.
$\boxed{\sigma_c = 4.76°\text{C}}$

(d) [2 marks] · 1 step

From (b): $r = 0.849$ between $x$°F and $y$°F; $u = \frac{5}{9}(x-32)$, $v = \frac{5}{9}(y-32)$
STEP 1/1 B1 B1

Since $u$ and $v$ are both linear functions of $x$ and $y$ (with positive scale factor $a = +\frac{5}{9}$), the PMCC is invariant under linear transformations.

PMCC between $u$ and $v$ = PMCC between $x$ and $y$ = $0.849$

Sign: positive, because both transformations have $a = +\frac{5}{9} > 0$ — the positive correlation is preserved.

Exam pitfall: if either transformation had a negative scale factor $a < 0$, the sign of $r$ would flip. Always check sign of $a$.
$\boxed{r_{uv} = 0.849}$ — linear coding preserves PMCC
