Histogram — Heights of 408 Plants

Question

The histogram below shows the distribution of the heights, to the nearest cm, of 408 plants.

(a) Use the histogram to complete the following table. [2]

Height ($h$ cm)$5\leqslant h<9$$9\leqslant h<13$$13\leqslant h<15$$15\leqslant h<17$$17\leqslant h<25$
Frequency32152  120

(b) Use interpolation to estimate the median. [2]

The mean height of these plants is 13.2 cm correct to one decimal place.

(c) Describe the skew of these data. Give a reason for your answer. [1]

Two of these plants are chosen at random.

(d) Estimate the probability that both of their heights are between 8 cm and 14 cm. [3]

[8 marks]

(a) Complete the table [2 marks · 1 step]

STEP 1
Total = 408. Known: 32+152+120 = 304. Remaining = 104 split over 13–15 and 15–17.
From histogram: both bars appear equal height (width 2 each). Each has frequency = 52.
Check: 32+152+52+52+120 = 408 ✓
13–15: 52; 15–17: 52

(b) Estimate median [2 marks · 1 step]

STEP 1
Median = 204th value. Cumulative: 32 (up to 9), 184 (up to 13), 236 (up to 15). Median in 13–15 class:
$M = 13 + \frac{204-184}{52}\times 2 = 13 + \frac{40}{52} = 13 + 0.769 \approx 13.8$
Median $\approx$ 13.8 cm

(c) Describe skew [1 mark · 1 step]

Mean = 13.2 cm, Median ≈ 13.8 cm
STEP 1
Mean (13.2) < Median (13.8) → distribution is negatively skewed.
Negatively skewed (mean < median)

(d) P(both heights in 8–14 cm) [3 marks · 2 steps]

STEP 1
Heights 8–14: part of 5–9 (8 to 9, width 4 → $\frac{1}{4}\times 32 = 8$ plants) + full 9–13 (152) + part of 13–15 (13 to 14, width 2 → $\frac{1}{2}\times 52 = 26$).
Total in 8–14: $8+152+26 = 186$ plants.
STEP 2
$P(\text{both}) = \frac{186}{408}\times\frac{185}{407} = \frac{186\times185}{408\times407} \approx 0.207$
$P \approx 0.207$
