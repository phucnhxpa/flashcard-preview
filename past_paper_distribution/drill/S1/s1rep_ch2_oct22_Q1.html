Stem Lengths of 120 Tulips — Histogram Areas & Quartiles

Question

The stem lengths of a sample of 120 tulips are recorded in the grouped frequency table below.

Stem length (cm)$40\leqslant x<42$$42\leqslant x<45$$45\leqslant x<50$$50\leqslant x<55$$55\leqslant x<58$$58\leqslant x<60$
Frequency12182335248

A histogram is drawn to represent these data. The area of the bar representing the $40\leqslant x<42$ class is 16.5 cm².

(a) Calculate the exact area of the bar representing the $42\leqslant x<45$ class. [2]

The height of the tallest bar in the histogram is 10 cm.

(b) Find the exact height of the second tallest bar. [3]

$Q_1$ for these data is 45 cm.

(c) Use linear interpolation to find an estimate for (i) $Q_2$; (ii) the interquartile range. [4]

One measure of skewness is given by $\dfrac{Q_3 - Q_2 - (Q_2 - Q_1)}{Q_3 - Q_1}$.

(d) By calculating this measure, describe the skewness of these data. [2]

[11 marks]

(a) Area of 42–45 bar [2 marks · 1 step]

STEP 1
Area proportional to frequency. 40–42: freq=12, area=16.5 cm².
Scale: $\frac{16.5}{12} = 1.375$ cm² per frequency unit.
42–45: freq=18, area $= 18\times1.375 = 24.75$ cm².
Area $= \dfrac{99}{4} = 24.75$ cm²

(b) Height of second tallest bar [3 marks · 2 steps]

STEP 1
Freq densities: 40–42: 12/2=6; 42–45: 18/3=6; 45–50: 23/5=4.6; 50–55: 35/5=7; 55–58: 24/3=8 (tallest); 58–60: 8/2=4.
Tallest bar: 55–58 (fd=8) → height=10 cm.
STEP 2
Second tallest: 50–55 (fd=7). Height $= \frac{7}{8}\times10 = 8.75$ cm.
Height $= \dfrac{35}{4} = 8.75$ cm

(c) Q₂ and IQR by interpolation [4 marks · 2 steps]

Given $Q_1 = 45$ cm
STEP 1
$Q_2$ (60th value). Cumulative: 12, 30, 53, 88 — 60th value in 50–55 class:
$Q_2 = 50+\frac{60-53}{35}\times5 = 50+1 = 51$
STEP 2
$Q_3$ (90th value): still in 50–55 class — cumulative at 55 is 88, so 90th is in 55–58:
$Q_3 = 55+\frac{90-88}{24}\times3 = 55+0.25 = 55.25$
IQR $= Q_3-Q_1 = 55.25-45 = 10.25$
$Q_2=51$, IQR $=10.25$ cm

(d) Skewness measure [2 marks · 1 step]

STEP 1
$\frac{Q_3-Q_2-(Q_2-Q_1)}{Q_3-Q_1} = \frac{55.25-51-(51-45)}{55.25-45} = \frac{4.25-6}{10.25} = \frac{-1.75}{10.25} \approx -0.171$
Negative → negatively skewed.
Skewness $\approx -0.171$ → negatively skewed
