Product moment correlation coefficient and regression line for bivariate data

Question

A researcher is investigating the relationship between the age, $x$ years, and the systolic blood pressure, $y$ mmHg, of a sample of 8 adults.

The data are summarised as follows:

$\sum x = 392$,   $\sum y = 1024$,   $\sum x^2 = 20184$,   $\sum y^2 = 132480$,   $\sum xy = 51320$

(a) Calculate the product moment correlation coefficient between $x$ and $y$.

(b) Interpret your answer to part (a) in context.

(c) Find the equation of the regression line of $y$ on $x$ in the form $y = a + bx$.

(d) Use your equation to estimate the systolic blood pressure of an adult aged 55 years.

(e) Comment on the reliability of your estimate in part (d).

[12 marks]

(a) [4 marks] · 4 steps

STEP 1/4 M1

Formula for $S_{xx}$: $S_{xx} = \sum x^2 - \dfrac{(\sum x)^2}{n}$

Substituting: $S_{xx} = 20184 - \dfrac{392^2}{8}$

$S_{xx} = 20184 - \dfrac{153664}{8} = 20184 - 19208$

Exam pitfall: must use $n=8$ not $n-1$ for these summary statistics formulae.
$\boxed{S_{xx} = 976}$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $S_{xx} = 976$
STEP 2/4 M1

Formula for $S_{yy}$: $S_{yy} = \sum y^2 - \dfrac{(\sum y)^2}{n}$

Substituting: $S_{yy} = 132480 - \dfrac{1024^2}{8}$

$S_{yy} = 132480 - \dfrac{1048576}{8} = 132480 - 131072$

Exam pitfall: careful with large numbers — use calculator memory to avoid rounding errors mid-calculation.
$\boxed{S_{yy} = 1408}$
↳ from STEP 2:From STEP 2: $S_{xx} = 976$, $S_{yy} = 1408$
STEP 3/4 M1

Formula for $S_{xy}$: $S_{xy} = \sum xy - \dfrac{(\sum x)(\sum y)}{n}$

Substituting: $S_{xy} = 51320 - \dfrac{392 \times 1024}{8}$

$S_{xy} = 51320 - \dfrac{401408}{8} = 51320 - 50176$

Exam pitfall: $S_{xy}$ uses PRODUCT of sums, not sum of products in the correction term.
$\boxed{S_{xy} = 1144}$
↳ from STEP 3:From STEP 3: $S_{xx} = 976$, $S_{yy} = 1408$, $S_{xy} = 1144$
STEP 4/4 A1

Formula for PMCC: $r = \dfrac{S_{xy}}{\sqrt{S_{xx} \times S_{yy}}}$

Substituting: $r = \dfrac{1144}{\sqrt{976 \times 1408}}$

$r = \dfrac{1144}{\sqrt{1374208}} = \dfrac{1144}{1172.27...}$

$r = 0.9759...$

Exam pitfall: give PMCC to 3 significant figures (or more) unless question specifies otherwise.
$\boxed{r = 0.976}$

(b) [1 marks] · 1 step

From (a): $r = 0.976$
STEP 1/1 B1

The value $r = 0.976$ is close to $+1$.

Interpretation: There is a strong positive correlation between age and systolic blood pressure.

In context: As age increases, systolic blood pressure tends to increase.

Exam pitfall: must interpret IN CONTEXT (mention age and blood pressure specifically) — not just "strong positive correlation" alone.
$\boxed{\text{Strong positive correlation: older adults tend to have higher blood pressure}}$

(c) [4 marks] · 4 steps

From (a): $S_{xx} = 976$, $S_{xy} = 1144$
STEP 1/4 M1

Formula for gradient $b$: $b = \dfrac{S_{xy}}{S_{xx}}$

Substituting: $b = \dfrac{1144}{976}$

$b = 1.1721...$

Exam pitfall: for regression of $y$ on $x$, gradient is $S_{xy}/S_{xx}$ (not $S_{xy}/S_{yy}$).
$\boxed{b = 1.172}$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $b = 1.172$
STEP 2/4 M1

Calculate means: $\bar{x} = \dfrac{\sum x}{n} = \dfrac{392}{8} = 49$

$\bar{y} = \dfrac{\sum y}{n} = \dfrac{1024}{8} = 128$

Exam pitfall: the regression line always passes through $(\bar{x}, \bar{y})$ — this is a key property.
$\boxed{\bar{x} = 49, \quad \bar{y} = 128}$
↳ from STEP 2:From STEP 2: $b = 1.172$, $\bar{x} = 49$, $\bar{y} = 128$
STEP 3/4 M1

Formula for intercept: $a = \bar{y} - b\bar{x}$

Substituting: $a = 128 - 1.1721... \times 49$

$a = 128 - 57.434... = 70.565...$

Exam pitfall: use unrounded value of $b$ in calculation to avoid rounding errors in $a$.
$\boxed{a = 70.6}$
↳ from STEP 3:From STEP 3: $a = 70.6$, $b = 1.17$
STEP 4/4 A1

The equation of the regression line of $y$ on $x$ is:

$y = a + bx$

$y = 70.6 + 1.17x$

Exam pitfall: state the full equation clearly — coefficients typically to 3 s.f.
$\boxed{y = 70.6 + 1.17x}$

(d) [1 marks] · 1 step

From (c): $y = 70.6 + 1.17x$
STEP 1/1 B1

To estimate blood pressure when $x = 55$:

$y = 70.6 + 1.17 \times 55$

$y = 70.6 + 64.35$

$y = 134.95$

Exam pitfall: use unrounded regression coefficients if available for more accurate prediction.
$\boxed{y = 135 \text{ mmHg}}$

(e) [2 marks] · 2 steps

From (d): estimate for $x = 55$ is $y = 135$ mmHg
STEP 1/2 B1

Check if $x = 55$ is within the range of the data:

With $n = 8$ and $\sum x = 392$, we have $\bar{x} = 49$.

Since $x = 55$ is close to the mean and likely within the data range, this is interpolation.

Exam pitfall: must determine whether prediction involves interpolation (reliable) or extrapolation (unreliable).
$\boxed{\text{Interpolation — within data range}}$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: This is interpolation
STEP 2/2 B1

Reliability assessment:

• The correlation coefficient $r = 0.976$ is very strong (close to 1)

• The prediction is interpolation (within the data range)

• Therefore the estimate is likely to be reliable

Exam pitfall: reliability depends on BOTH strength of correlation AND whether interpolating/extrapolating.
$\boxed{\text{Reliable — strong correlation and interpolation}}$
