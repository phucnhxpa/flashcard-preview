Histogram — Times to Swim 500 m (100 People)

Question

The histogram shows the times taken, $t$ minutes, by each of 100 people to swim 500 metres.

(a) Use the histogram to complete the frequency table. [1]

Time ($t$ min)5 – 1010 – 1414 – 1818 – 2525 – 40
Frequency101624  

(b) Estimate the number of people who took less than 16 minutes to swim 500 metres. [2]

(c) Find an estimate for the mean time taken to swim 500 metres. [2]

Given that $\sum ft^2 = 41\,033$

(d) find an estimate for the standard deviation of the times taken to swim 500 metres. [2]

Given that $Q_3 = 23$

(e) use linear interpolation to estimate the interquartile range of the times taken to swim 500 metres. [3]

[10 marks]

(a) Complete the frequency table [1 mark · 1 step]

STEP 1
Total = 100. Known: 10+16+24 = 50. Remaining 50 split between 18–25 and 25–40 classes.
Read from histogram bars using freq density × width: 18–25 (width 7) → freq = 28; 25–40 (width 15) → freq = 22. Check: 50+28+22=100 ✓
18–25: 28; 25–40: 22

(b) Estimate people with t < 16 min [2 marks · 1 step]

STEP 1
5–10: all 10. 10–14: all 16. 14–18 (freq=24, width=4): 14 to 16 = 2 units → $\frac{2}{4}\times 24 = 12$.
Total: $10+16+12 = 38$
Estimate: 38 people

(c) Estimate mean time [2 marks · 1 step]

STEP 1
Midpoints: 7.5, 12, 16, 21.5, 32.5
$\sum ft = 10(7.5)+16(12)+24(16)+28(21.5)+22(32.5) = 75+192+384+602+715 = 1968$
$\bar{t} = \frac{1968}{100} = 19.68$
Mean $\approx$ 19.68 minutes

(d) Estimate standard deviation [2 marks · 1 step]

$\sum ft^2 = 41\,033,\; n = 100,\; \bar{t} = 19.68$
STEP 1
$s = \sqrt{\frac{\sum ft^2}{n} - \bar{t}^2} = \sqrt{\frac{41033}{100} - (19.68)^2} = \sqrt{410.33 - 387.30} = \sqrt{23.03} \approx 4.80$
Standard deviation $\approx$ 4.80 minutes

(e) Estimate IQR by linear interpolation [3 marks · 2 steps]

$Q_3 = 23$
STEP 1
$Q_1$: 25th value. Cumulative: 10, 26 (at end of 10–14). 25th value in 10–14 class:
$Q_1 = 10 + \frac{25-10}{16}\times 4 = 10 + 3.75 = 13.75$
STEP 2
$\text{IQR} = Q_3 - Q_1 = 23 - 13.75 = 9.25$
IQR $\approx$ 9.25 minutes
