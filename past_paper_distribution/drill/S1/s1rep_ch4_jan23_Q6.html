Product moment correlation coefficient and least squares regression line for bivariate data

Question

A scientist is investigating the relationship between the temperature, $t$ °C, and the rate of a chemical reaction, $r$ mg per second.

The scientist measures the rate of reaction at 8 different temperatures. The results are summarised below.

$$S_{tt} = 1460 \qquad S_{rr} = 83.72 \qquad S_{tr} = 312.6$$

$$\sum t = 420 \qquad \sum r = 59.2$$

(a) Calculate the product moment correlation coefficient between $t$ and $r$.

(b) Give an interpretation of your answer to part (a).

(c) Find the equation of the regression line of $r$ on $t$ in the form $r = a + bt$.

(d) Use your regression line to estimate the rate of reaction when the temperature is 45 °C.

(e) Comment on the reliability of your estimate in part (d).

[10 marks]

(a) [2 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2 M1

Formula for PMCC: $r = \dfrac{S_{tr}}{\sqrt{S_{tt} \times S_{rr}}}$

Substituting the given values:

$r = \dfrac{312.6}{\sqrt{1460 \times 83.72}}$

$r = \dfrac{312.6}{\sqrt{122231.2}}$

$r = \dfrac{312.6}{349.6157...}$

Exam pitfall: Keep full calculator precision in the denominator before final rounding — rounding too early loses accuracy marks.
$r = 0.8941...$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $r = 0.8941...$
STEP 2/2 A1

Round to 3 significant figures (or 3 decimal places as appropriate for correlation coefficients):

$r = 0.894$ (3 s.f.)

Exam pitfall: PMCC must satisfy $-1 \leq r \leq 1$. If your answer is outside this range, you have made a calculation error.
$\boxed{r = 0.894}$

(b) [1 marks] · 1 step

From (a): $r = 0.894$
STEP 1/1 B1

Since $r = 0.894$ is positive and close to 1:

There is a strong positive correlation between temperature and rate of reaction.

As temperature increases, the rate of reaction tends to increase.

Exam pitfall: You must interpret in CONTEXT — mention "temperature" and "rate of reaction", not just "x and y". Generic statements like "positive correlation" without context lose marks.
$\boxed{\text{Strong positive correlation: as temperature increases, rate of reaction increases}}$

(c) [4 marks] · 4 steps

From (a): $r = 0.894$
STEP 1/4 M1

Formula for regression coefficient $b$:

$b = \dfrac{S_{tr}}{S_{tt}}$

Substituting:

$b = \dfrac{312.6}{1460}$

$b = 0.2141095...$

Exam pitfall: For regression of $r$ on $t$, use $S_{tr}/S_{tt}$ (divide by the sum of squares of the EXPLANATORY variable $t$). Using $S_{rr}$ instead gives wrong gradient.
$b = 0.214$ (3 s.f.)
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $b = 0.2141095...$
STEP 2/4 A1

Calculate the means:

$\bar{t} = \dfrac{\sum t}{n} = \dfrac{420}{8} = 52.5$

$\bar{r} = \dfrac{\sum r}{n} = \dfrac{59.2}{8} = 7.4$

Exam pitfall: $n = 8$ (number of data points) — make sure you use the correct sample size given in the question.
$\bar{t} = 52.5, \quad \bar{r} = 7.4$
↳ from STEP 2:From STEP 2: $\bar{t} = 52.5$, $\bar{r} = 7.4$, $b = 0.2141095...$
STEP 3/4 M1

Formula for intercept $a$:

$a = \bar{r} - b\bar{t}$

Substituting:

$a = 7.4 - (0.2141095... \times 52.5)$

$a = 7.4 - 11.2407...$

$a = -3.8407...$

Exam pitfall: Use the UNROUNDED value of $b$ in this calculation to avoid accumulating rounding errors.
$a = -3.84$ (3 s.f.)
↳ from STEP 3:From STEP 3: $a = -3.84$, $b = 0.214$
STEP 4/4 A1

Write the regression equation in the required form:

$r = a + bt$

$r = -3.84 + 0.214t$

Exam pitfall: Write the equation using $r$ and $t$ (the variables in the question), NOT $y$ and $x$. Also ensure the form matches what is asked.
$\boxed{r = -3.84 + 0.214t}$

(d) [2 marks] · 2 steps

From (c): $r = -3.84 + 0.214t$
STEP 1/2 M1

Substitute $t = 45$ into the regression equation:

$r = -3.84 + 0.214 \times 45$

$r = -3.84 + 9.63$

Exam pitfall: Use your regression equation coefficients (even if slightly different from mark scheme) — follow-through marks are available.
$r = 5.79$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $r = 5.79$
STEP 2/2 A1

State the estimate with appropriate units:

Estimated rate of reaction = 5.79 mg per second (or 5.8 mg/s to 2 s.f.)

Exam pitfall: Include UNITS in your final answer — the question states rate is measured in "mg per second".
$\boxed{r = 5.79 \text{ mg per second}}$

(e) [1 marks] · 1 step

From (d): estimate at $t = 45$ °C
STEP 1/1 B1

Consider whether $t = 45$ is within the range of the data:

Mean temperature $\bar{t} = 52.5$ °C, so $t = 45$ °C is likely within or close to the data range.

The estimate is likely to be reliable because:

  • $t = 45$ is within the range of the data (interpolation)
  • The correlation coefficient $r = 0.894$ is strong, indicating a good linear relationship
Exam pitfall: You MUST justify reliability by referring to interpolation (within data range) vs extrapolation (outside range). Also mention the strength of correlation as supporting evidence.
$\boxed{\text{Reliable — interpolation within data range and strong correlation}}$
