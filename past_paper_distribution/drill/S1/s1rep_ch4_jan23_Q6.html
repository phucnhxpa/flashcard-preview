Sugar Dissolving — Regression & Correlation

Question

A research student is investigating the maximum weight, $y$ grams, of sugar that will dissolve in 100 grams of water at various temperatures, $x\,^{\circ}\text{C}$, where $10 \leq x \leq 80$.

The research student calculated the regression line of $y$ on $x$:

$\hat{y} = 151.2 + 2.72x$

(a) Give an interpretation of the gradient of the regression line. [1]

(b) Use the regression line to estimate the maximum weight of sugar that will dissolve in 100 grams of water when the temperature is 90°C. [2]

(c) Comment on the reliability of your estimate, giving a reason for your answer. [2]

Using the regression line of $y$ on $x$ and the following summary statistics:

$\Sigma y = 3119,\quad \Sigma y^2 = 851093,\quad \Sigma x^2 = 24500,\quad n = 12$

(d) Show that the product moment correlation coefficient for these data is $0.988$ to 3 decimal places. [7]

(e) With reference to both the scatter diagram and the correlation coefficient, discuss the suitability of a linear regression model to describe the relationship between $x$ and $y$. [2]

[15 marks]

(a) [1 marks] · 1 step

Regression line: $\hat{y} = 151.2 + 2.72x$
STEP 1/1
Gradient interpretation
The gradient is $2.72$, which is the coefficient of $x$.
For each 1°C increase in temperature, the maximum weight of sugar that will dissolve in 100 g of water increases by 2.72 grams.
$\text{+2.72 g of sugar per }1^{\circ}\text{C increase}$

(b) [2 marks] · 1 step

Regression line: $\hat{y} = 151.2 + 2.72x$
STEP 1/1
Substitute $x = 90$
$$\hat{y} = 151.2 + 2.72 \times 90$$ $$= 151.2 + 244.8 = 396$$
$\hat{y} = \boxed{396 \text{ grams}}$

(c) [2 marks] · 1 step

Range of data: $10 \leq x \leq 80$; estimate at $x = 90$
STEP 1/1
Reliability comment
$x = 90^{\circ}\text{C}$ is outside the range of the data $[10, 80]$.
This means we are extrapolating beyond the observed range.
The estimate is unreliable because we cannot assume the linear relationship holds outside the given range.
$\text{Unreliable — extrapolation beyond } [10,\,80]$

(d) [7 marks] · 5 steps

$\Sigma y = 3119,\;\Sigma y^2 = 851093,\;\Sigma x^2 = 24500,\;n = 12,\;\hat{y} = 151.2 + 2.72x$
STEP 1/5
Step 1: Find $\bar{y}$ and $\bar{x}$
$$\bar{y} = \frac{\Sigma y}{n} = \frac{3119}{12} = 259.9\overline{16}$$ From the regression line, $\bar{y} = 151.2 + 2.72\bar{x}$:
$$\bar{x} = \frac{\bar{y} - 151.2}{2.72} = \frac{259.9\overline{16} - 151.2}{2.72} = \frac{108.7\overline{16}}{2.72} \approx 39.97$$
$\bar{y} \approx 259.917,\quad \bar{x} \approx 39.97$
Carry-in from previous step$\bar{x} \approx 39.97,\;\bar{y} \approx 259.917$
STEP 2/5
Step 2: Calculate $S_{xx}$
$$S_{xx} = \Sigma x^2 - \frac{(\Sigma x)^2}{n} = \Sigma x^2 - n\bar{x}^2$$ $$= 24500 - 12 \times (39.97)^2$$ $$= 24500 - 12 \times 1597.60 = 24500 - 19171.20$$ $$\approx 5328.8 \approx 5329.4$$
(Using exact $\bar{x}$: $S_{xx} = 24500 - 12\times(479.6\overline{3}/12)^2 = 5329.4$)
$S_{xx} \approx 5329.4$
Carry-in from previous step$\bar{y} \approx 259.917,\;\Sigma y^2 = 851093$
STEP 3/5
Step 3: Calculate $S_{yy}$
$$S_{yy} = \Sigma y^2 - \frac{(\Sigma y)^2}{n} = 851093 - \frac{3119^2}{12}$$ $$= 851093 - \frac{9728161}{12} = 851093 - 810680.08\overline{3}$$ $$= 40412.9\overline{16} \approx 40412.92$$
$S_{yy} \approx 40412.92$
Carry-in from previous step$S_{xx} \approx 5329.4$; gradient $b = 2.72$
STEP 4/5
Step 4: Calculate $S_{xy}$
The gradient of the regression line is $b = \dfrac{S_{xy}}{S_{xx}}$, so:
$$S_{xy} = b \times S_{xx} = 2.72 \times 5329.4 = 14495.97$$
$S_{xy} \approx 14495.97$
Carry-in from previous step$S_{xx} \approx 5329.4,\;S_{yy} \approx 40412.92,\;S_{xy} \approx 14495.97$
STEP 5/5
Step 5: Calculate $r$
$$r = \frac{S_{xy}}{\sqrt{S_{xx} \cdot S_{yy}}} = \frac{14495.97}{\sqrt{5329.4 \times 40412.92}}$$ $$= \frac{14495.97}{\sqrt{215,358,285}} = \frac{14495.97}{14678.2}$$ $$\approx 0.9876\ldots \approx 0.988 \checkmark$$
$r = \boxed{0.988}$ (3 d.p.) $\checkmark$

(e) [2 marks] · 1 step

$r = 0.988$; scatter diagram shows points close to the regression line
STEP 1/1
Suitability of linear model
• $r = 0.988 \approx 1$ indicates a very strong positive linear correlation.
• The scatter diagram shows the data points lie close to the regression line with no obvious curved pattern.
Both pieces of evidence support the use of a linear regression model — it is suitable to describe the relationship between $x$ and $y$.
$\text{Linear model is suitable/appropriate}$
