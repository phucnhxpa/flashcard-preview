Events $A$ and $B$ with $P(A) = 0.4$, $P(B) = 0.5$, $P(A \cap B) = 0.15$

Question

The events $A$ and $B$ are such that $P(A) = 0.4$, $P(B) = 0.5$ and $P(A \cap B) = 0.15$.

(a) Find $P(A \cup B)$.

(b) Find $P(A \mid B)$.

(c) Find $P(B \mid A)$.

(d) Determine whether events $A$ and $B$ are independent. Give a reason for your answer.

[10 marks]

(a) [2 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2 M1

Addition rule formula: $P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cap B)$

Substituting given values:

$P(A \cup B) = 0.4 + 0.5 - 0.15$

$P(A \cup B) = 0.9 - 0.15$

Exam pitfall: forgetting to subtract $P(A \cap B)$ leads to double-counting the intersection.
$P(A \cup B) = 0.75$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $P(A \cup B) = 0.75$
STEP 2/2 A1

Final answer:

Exam pitfall: always check your answer is between 0 and 1 for a valid probability.
$\boxed{P(A \cup B) = 0.75}$

(b) [2 marks] · 2 steps

Given: $P(A \cap B) = 0.15$, $P(B) = 0.5$
STEP 1/2 M1

Conditional probability formula: $P(A \mid B) = \dfrac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)}$

Substituting the given values:

$P(A \mid B) = \dfrac{0.15}{0.5}$

Exam pitfall: confusing $P(A \mid B)$ with $P(B \mid A)$ — the denominator must match the condition (event $B$ here).
$P(A \mid B) = \dfrac{0.15}{0.5}$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $P(A \mid B) = \dfrac{0.15}{0.5}$
STEP 2/2 A1

Simplifying the fraction:

$P(A \mid B) = \dfrac{0.15}{0.5} = \dfrac{15}{50} = \dfrac{3}{10} = 0.3$

Exam pitfall: leaving the answer as a fraction over decimals — simplify fully or convert to decimal.
$\boxed{P(A \mid B) = 0.3}$

(c) [2 marks] · 2 steps

Given: $P(A \cap B) = 0.15$, $P(A) = 0.4$
STEP 1/2 M1

Conditional probability formula: $P(B \mid A) = \dfrac{P(A \cap B)}{P(A)}$

Substituting the given values:

$P(B \mid A) = \dfrac{0.15}{0.4}$

Exam pitfall: using $P(B)$ in denominator instead of $P(A)$ — always check which event is the condition.
$P(B \mid A) = \dfrac{0.15}{0.4}$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $P(B \mid A) = \dfrac{0.15}{0.4}$
STEP 2/2 A1

Simplifying the fraction:

$P(B \mid A) = \dfrac{0.15}{0.4} = \dfrac{15}{40} = \dfrac{3}{8} = 0.375$

Exam pitfall: calculator errors with decimals — verify by multiplying back: $0.375 \times 0.4 = 0.15$ ✓
$\boxed{P(B \mid A) = 0.375}$

(d) [4 marks] · 4 steps

From (b): $P(A \mid B) = 0.3$. From (c): $P(B \mid A) = 0.375$. Given: $P(A) = 0.4$, $P(B) = 0.5$, $P(A \cap B) = 0.15$
STEP 1/4 M1

For independence, we need to test ONE of these equivalent conditions:

• $P(A \cap B) = P(A) \times P(B)$

• $P(A \mid B) = P(A)$

• $P(B \mid A) = P(B)$

Exam pitfall: you must explicitly state which independence condition you are testing.
Independence condition identified

Calculate $P(A) \times P(B)$:

$P(A) \times P(B) = 0.4 \times 0.5 = 0.2$

Compare with given $P(A \cap B) = 0.15$

Exam pitfall: only calculating one side — you must compare BOTH values explicitly.
$P(A) \times P(B) = 0.2$ vs $P(A \cap B) = 0.15$
↳ from STEP 2:From STEP 2: $P(A) \times P(B) = 0.2$ and $P(A \cap B) = 0.15$
STEP 3/4 A1

Since $P(A \cap B) = 0.15 \neq 0.2 = P(A) \times P(B)$

The independence condition is NOT satisfied.

Exam pitfall: stating just "not equal" without showing the actual values being compared loses marks.
$0.15 \neq 0.2$
↳ from STEP 3:From STEP 3: $P(A \cap B) \neq P(A) \times P(B)$

Conclusion with clear reasoning:

$A$ and $B$ are NOT independent

Reason: $P(A \cap B) = 0.15 \neq 0.2 = P(A) \times P(B)$

Alternative valid reason: $P(A \mid B) = 0.3 \neq 0.4 = P(A)$
