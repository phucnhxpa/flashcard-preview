S1 (WST01/01) · Q2 · January 2023

Conditional probability and independence for events $A$ and $B$

Question

Two events $A$ and $B$ are such that

$P(A) = 0.4$,   $P(B) = 0.5$,   $P(A \cup B) = 0.6$

(a) Find $P(A \cap B)$.

(b) Find $P(A \mid B)$.

(c) Determine whether or not $A$ and $B$ are independent. Give a reason for your answer.

[7 marks]

(a) [2 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2 M1

Addition rule formula: $P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cap B)$

Rearranging for $P(A \cap B)$:

$P(A \cap B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cup B)$

Exam pitfall: students often add probabilities without subtracting the intersection — always use the full addition rule.
$P(A \cap B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cup B)$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $P(A \cap B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cup B)$
STEP 2/2 A1

Substituting the given values:

$P(A \cap B) = 0.4 + 0.5 - 0.6$

$P(A \cap B) = 0.9 - 0.6$

$P(A \cap B) = 0.3$

Exam pitfall: check your answer makes sense — $P(A \cap B)$ must be ≤ both $P(A)$ and $P(B)$. Here $0.3 \leq 0.4$ ✓ and $0.3 \leq 0.5$ ✓
$\boxed{P(A \cap B) = 0.3}$

(b) [2 marks] · 2 steps

From (a): $P(A \cap B) = 0.3$
STEP 1/2 M1

Conditional probability formula: $P(A \mid B) = \dfrac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)}$

This gives the probability of $A$ occurring given that $B$ has occurred.

Exam pitfall: students confuse $P(A \mid B)$ with $P(B \mid A)$ — always divide by the probability of the "given" event (the one after the vertical bar).
$P(A \mid B) = \dfrac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)}$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $P(A \mid B) = \dfrac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)}$
STEP 2/2 A1

Substituting values:

$P(A \mid B) = \dfrac{0.3}{0.5}$

$P(A \mid B) = \dfrac{3}{10} \div \dfrac{5}{10} = \dfrac{3}{10} \times \dfrac{10}{5}$

$P(A \mid B) = \dfrac{3}{5} = 0.6$

Exam pitfall: leave answer as exact fraction or decimal to required d.p. — here $\frac{3}{5}$ or $0.6$ both acceptable.
$\boxed{P(A \mid B) = 0.6}$

(c) [3 marks] · 3 steps

From (a): $P(A \cap B) = 0.3$; Given: $P(A) = 0.4$, $P(B) = 0.5$
STEP 1/3 M1

For independence, we test whether: $P(A \cap B) = P(A) \times P(B)$

Calculate $P(A) \times P(B)$:

$P(A) \times P(B) = 0.4 \times 0.5$

$P(A) \times P(B) = 0.2$

Exam pitfall: you MUST state the independence condition you are testing — just saying "they are not equal" without showing what should be equal loses marks.
$P(A) \times P(B) = 0.2$
↳ from STEP 1:From STEP 1: $P(A) \times P(B) = 0.2$
STEP 2/3 A1

Compare with $P(A \cap B)$ from part (a):

$P(A \cap B) = 0.3$

$P(A) \times P(B) = 0.2$

Since $0.3 \neq 0.2$:

$P(A \cap B) \neq P(A) \times P(B)$

Exam pitfall: always show both numerical values being compared — do not just write "they are different".
$0.3 \neq 0.2$
↳ from STEP 2:From STEP 2: $P(A \cap B) = 0.3 \neq 0.2 = P(A) \times P(B)$
STEP 3/3 A1

Conclusion:

$A$ and $B$ are NOT independent

Reason: Because $P(A \cap B) \neq P(A) \times P(B)$

(Equivalently: $P(A \mid B) = 0.6 \neq 0.4 = P(A)$, so knowing $B$ occurred changes the probability of $A$)

Exam pitfall: you must give a REASON — just stating "not independent" without justification scores 0 for this mark. State the specific values that are unequal.
$\boxed{\text{Not independent because } P(A \cap B) = 0.3 \neq 0.2 = P(A) \times P(B)}$
