Jan 2020 Q10 — Sketch cubic, find transformed roots and g(x) [8m]

Question

The curve $C_1$ has equation $y = f(x)$, where

$$f(x) = (4x - 3)(x - 5)^2$$

(a) Sketch $C_1$ showing the coordinates of any point where the curve touches or crosses the coordinate axes. [3]

(b) Hence or otherwise:

(i) find the values of $x$ for which $f\!\left(\tfrac{x}{4}\right) = 0$

(ii) find the value of the constant $p$ such that the curve with equation $y = f(x) + p$ passes through the origin. [2]

A second curve $C_2$ has equation $y = g(x)$, where $g(x) = f(x + 1)$.

(c) (i) Find, in simplest form, $g(x)$. You may leave your answer in a factorised form.

(ii) Hence, or otherwise, find the $y$-intercept of curve $C_2$. [3]

[8 marks]

(a) — Sketch C₁ [3 marks] · 1 step

$f(x) = (4x - 3)(x - 5)^2$
STEP 1/1 B1 B1 B1

Strategy: Factorised form reveals roots directly. Single factor → crosses; squared factor → touches.

x-intercepts (set $f(x) = 0$):

$$4x - 3 = 0 \implies x = \frac{3}{4} \quad \text{(crosses)}$$

$$(x - 5)^2 = 0 \implies x = 5 \quad \text{(touches — even multiplicity)}$$

y-intercept ($x = 0$):

$$f(0) = (0 - 3)(0 - 5)^2 = (-3)(25) = -75$$

Shape: Leading term $4x \cdot x^2 = 4x^3$ (positive cubic): rises from bottom-left to top-right.

Watch: Do not confuse the actual function $(4x-3)(x-5)^2$ with a simple cubic $x^3$. The y-intercept is $(0, -75)$ — a large negative value. The curve crosses at $(\tfrac{3}{4}, 0)$ and touches (bounces off) at $(5, 0)$.
$\boxed{\text{Crosses at } (\tfrac{3}{4}, 0) \text{ and } (0, -75); \text{ touches at } (5, 0)}$

(b)(i) — Values of x where f(x/4) = 0 [1 marks] · 1 step

Roots of $f(x) = 0$ are $x = \tfrac{3}{4}$ and $x = 5$
STEP 1/1 B1

Strategy: $f\!\left(\tfrac{x}{4}\right) = 0$ when $\tfrac{x}{4}$ equals a root of $f$. This is a horizontal stretch by factor 4.

$$\frac{x}{4} = \frac{3}{4} \implies x = 3$$

$$\frac{x}{4} = 5 \implies x = 20$$

Watch: Each root is multiplied by 4, not divided. $y = f(ax)$ stretches horizontally by $\tfrac{1}{a}$, so $y = f(\tfrac{x}{4})$ stretches by factor 4.
$\boxed{x = 3 \text{ and } x = 20}$

(b)(ii) — Find p such that y = f(x) + p passes through origin [1 marks] · 1 step

$f(0) = -75$
STEP 1/1 B1

The curve $y = f(x) + p$ passes through origin means $(0, 0)$ is on it:

$$0 = f(0) + p = -75 + p \implies p = 75$$

This is a vertical translation of 75 units upward.

$\boxed{p = 75}$

(c)(i) — Find g(x) = f(x + 1) in factorised form [2 marks] · 1 step

$f(x) = (4x - 3)(x - 5)^2$
STEP 1/1 M1A1

Strategy: Horizontal translation 1 unit left. Replace every $x$ in $f(x)$ with $(x + 1)$.

$$g(x) = f(x + 1) = \bigl(4(x+1) - 3\bigr)\bigl((x+1) - 5\bigr)^2$$

Simplify each bracket:

$$4(x+1) - 3 = 4x + 4 - 3 = 4x + 1$$

$$(x + 1) - 5 = x - 4$$

$$g(x) = (4x + 1)(x - 4)^2$$

Watch: Do not expand unnecessarily — leaving in factorised form is explicitly allowed and preferred.
$\boxed{g(x) = (4x + 1)(x - 4)^2}$

(c)(ii) — Find y-intercept of C₂ [1 marks] · 1 step

$g(x) = (4x + 1)(x - 4)^2$
STEP 1/1 B1

$$g(0) = (4(0) + 1)(0 - 4)^2 = (1)(16) = 16$$

The $y$-intercept of $C_2$ is $(0, 16)$.

$\boxed{y\text{-intercept} = 16}$
