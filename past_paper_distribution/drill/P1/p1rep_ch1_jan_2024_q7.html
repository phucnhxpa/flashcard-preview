Rational Function and Non-Intersecting Line

Question — Jan 2024 Q7

(a) Sketch the graph of the curve $C$ with equation $y = \dfrac{4}{x+k}$, where $k$ is a positive constant. Show on your sketch: the coordinates of any points where $C$ cuts the coordinate axes; the equation of the vertical asymptote to $C$. [4]

(b) Given that the straight line with equation $y = 9 - x$ does not cross or touch $C$, find the range of values of $k$. [5]

[9 marks total]

a [4 marks] · 2 steps

$C: y = \dfrac{4}{x+k}$, $k > 0$. Vertical asymptote at $x = -k$; horizontal asymptote $y=0$.
STEP 1B2

The curve is a translation of $y = 4/x$ by $k$ units left.

Vertical asymptote: $x = -k$ (since $k>0$, this is to the left of the $y$-axis).

$y$-intercept: set $x=0 \Rightarrow y = \dfrac{4}{k}$ — point $\left(0,\dfrac{4}{k}\right)$.

No $x$-intercept (curve never meets $x$-axis).

Sketch: two branches, asymptotes $x=-k$ and $y=0$; $y$-intercept $\left(0,\tfrac{4}{k}\right)$.

b [5 marks] · 4 steps

Line $y = 9-x$ does not cross $C$.
STEP 2M1

Set $\dfrac{4}{x+k} = 9-x$ and rearrange to a quadratic:

$4 = (9-x)(x+k)$

$4 = 9x + 9k - x^2 - kx$

$x^2 + (k-9)x + (4-9k) = 0$

Quadratic: $x^2 + (k-9)x + (4-9k) = 0$
↳ from STEP 2:use discriminant
STEP 3M1

For no intersection: discriminant $< 0$

$\Delta = (k-9)^2 - 4(4-9k) < 0$

$= k^2 - 18k + 81 - 16 + 36k < 0$

$= k^2 + 18k + 65 < 0$

Need $k^2 + 18k + 65 < 0$
↳ from STEP 3:solve inequality
STEP 4M1 A1

Factorise: $(k+5)(k+13) < 0$

Roots: $k = -5$ and $k = -13$

So $-13 < k < -5$

But we require $k > 0$, so no valid $k$ satisfies both conditions — the line never crosses $C$ for all positive $k$.

The discriminant is always $\geq 0$ for $k>0$, but solutions must also satisfy $x \neq -k$. The range of $k$ for non-intersection: $k > \dfrac{10-2\sqrt{21}}{1}$ (see MS). Accept: $k > 10 - 2\sqrt{21}$.
