Q7 — Sketch y=1/(2−x); show k²+16k+48>0; find range of k

Question

(a) The curve $C$ has equation $y = \dfrac{1}{2-x}$

Sketch the graph of $C$. Show coordinates of any intersections with the axes and state clearly the equations of any asymptotes.   [3]

(b) The line $\ell$ has equation $y = 4x + k$, where $k$ is a constant. Given that $\ell$ meets $C$ at two distinct points, show that $k^2 + 16k + 48 > 0$   [4]

(c) Hence find the range of possible values for $k$.   [4]

[11 marks]

Solution [11 marks] · 8 steps

STEP 1/8 M1
The vertical asymptote occurs where the denominator equals zero.

Set \( 2 - x = 0 \)

\( x = 2 \)

Pitfall: Writing x = −2. The denominator is (2−x), not (x−2) or (x+2).
\boxed{x = 2}
↳ from STEP 1:VA: \( x = 2 \)
STEP 2/8 M1
Horizontal asymptote: As \( x \to \pm\infty \), \( y = \frac{1}{2-x} \to 0 \)

So HA: \( y = 0 \)

y-intercept: Set \( x = 0 \):

\( y = \frac{1}{2-0} = \frac{1}{2} \)

x-intercept: \( \frac{1}{2-x} = 0 \) has no solution (numerator is never zero)

Pitfall: Claiming there is an x-intercept. A reciprocal function \(\frac{1}{f(x)}\) never crosses the x-axis.
\boxed{y = 0, \quad \left(0, \frac{1}{2}\right)}
↳ from STEP 2:VA: \( x = 2 \), HA: \( y = 0 \), y-int: \( \left(0, \frac{1}{2}\right) \)
STEP 3/8 M1
The curve \( y = \frac{1}{2-x} \) is a rectangular hyperbola.

• For \( x < 2 \): \( 2 - x > 0 \), so \( y > 0 \) (curve above x-axis)

• For \( x > 2 \): \( 2 - x < 0 \), so \( y < 0 \) (curve below x-axis)

The curve passes through \( \left(0, \frac{1}{2}\right) \) and approaches both asymptotes.

Pitfall: Drawing branches in wrong quadrants. Check sign of y on each side of the VA.
\boxed{\text{Branch in Q1/Q2 region for } x<2; \text{ Branch in Q3/Q4 region for } x>2}
↳ from STEP 3:C: \( y = \frac{1}{2-x} \)
STEP 4/8 M1
At intersection points: \( 4x + k = \frac{1}{2-x} \)

Multiply both sides by \( (2-x) \):

\( (4x + k)(2-x) = 1 \)

Expand: \( 8x - 4x^2 + 2k - kx = 1 \)

Rearrange: \( -4x^2 + (8-k)x + (2k-1) = 1 \)

\( -4x^2 + (8-k)x + (2k-1) - 1 = 0 \)

Multiply by \(-1\): \( 4x^2 + (k-8)x + (1-2k) = 0 \)

Pitfall: Sign errors when expanding (4x+k)(2−x). Take extra care with the −x term.
\boxed{4x^2 + (k-8)x + (1-2k) = 0}
↳ from STEP 4:Quadratic: \( 4x^2 + (k-8)x + (1-2k) = 0 \)
STEP 5/8 M1
For two distinct real roots: \( \Delta > 0 \)

Here \( a = 4 \), \( b = (k-8) \), \( c = (1-2k) \)

\( \Delta = b^2 - 4ac = (k-8)^2 - 4(4)(1-2k) \)

\( = (k-8)^2 - 16(1-2k) \)

Expand \( (k-8)^2 = k^2 - 16k + 64 \)

\( \Delta = k^2 - 16k + 64 - 16(1-2k) \)

Pitfall: Forgetting the middle term in \((k-8)^2\). Use \((a-b)^2 = a^2 - 2ab + b^2\).
\boxed{\Delta = k^2 - 16k + 64 - 16(1-2k) > 0}
↳ from STEP 5:\( \Delta = k^2 - 16k + 64 - 16(1-2k) > 0 \)
STEP 6/8 M1
Expand \( -16(1-2k) = -16 + 32k \)

\( \Delta = k^2 - 16k + 64 - 16 + 32k \)

Collect like terms:

• Constant: \( 64 - 16 = 48 \)

• k terms: \( -16k + 32k = 16k \)

\( \Delta = k^2 + 16k + 48 \)

For two distinct points: \( k^2 + 16k + 48 > 0 \) ✓

Pitfall: Sign error with \(-16(1-2k)\). The \(-16 \times -2k = +32k\).
\boxed{k^2 + 16k + 48 > 0 \text{ (as required)}}
↳ from STEP 6:\( k^2 + 16k + 48 > 0 \)
STEP 7/8 M1
Find two numbers that multiply to 48 and add to 16.

Factors of 48: \( 1 \times 48, 2 \times 24, 3 \times 16, 4 \times 12, 6 \times 8 \)

\( 4 + 12 = 16 \) ✓

So: \( k^2 + 16k + 48 = (k + 4)(k + 12) \)

Check: \( (k+4)(k+12) = k^2 + 12k + 4k + 48 = k^2 + 16k + 48 \) ✓

Pitfall: Using wrong signs. Both factors of 48 must be positive since we need +16 (sum) and +48 (product).
\boxed{(k+4)(k+12)}
↳ from STEP 7:\( (k+4)(k+12) > 0 \)
STEP 8/8 M1
Critical values: \( k = -4 \) and \( k = -12 \)

Test intervals for \( (k+4)(k+12) \):

• \( k < -12 \): e.g., \( k = -13 \): \( (-9)(-1) = +9 > 0 \) ✓

• \( -12 < k < -4 \): e.g., \( k = -8 \): \( (-4)(4) = -16 < 0 \) ✗

• \( k > -4 \): e.g., \( k = 0 \): \( (4)(12) = 48 > 0 \) ✓

Product is positive when both factors same sign (outside the roots).

Pitfall: Writing \(-12 < k < -4\). This is where the product is NEGATIVE. We need the regions OUTSIDE the roots.
\boxed{k < -12 \text{ or } k > -4}
