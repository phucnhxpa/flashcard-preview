Jun 2022 Q5 — Quadratic from vertex, perpendicular line, define region [9m]

Question

The curve $C$ has equation $y = f(x)$. Given that $f(x)$ is a quadratic expression, the maximum turning point on $C$ has coordinates $(-2, 12)$, and $C$ cuts the negative $x$-axis at $-5$:

(a) Find $f(x)$. [4]

The line $l_1$ has equation $y = \tfrac{4}{5}x$. Given that line $l_2$ is perpendicular to $l_1$ and passes through $(-5, 0)$:

(b) Find an equation for $l_2$, writing your answer in the form $y = mx + c$. [3]

(c) Define the region $R$, shown shaded in Figure 2, using inequalities. [2]

[9 marks]

(a) — Find f(x) [4 marks] · 2 steps

STEP 1/2 M1A1

Strategy: Maximum turning point $(-2, 12)$ → use vertex form.

Formula (vertex form): $f(x) = a(x - h)^2 + k$ where $(h,k)$ is vertex.

$$f(x) = a(x + 2)^2 + 12$$

Use $f(-5) = 0$ (curve cuts $x$-axis at $-5$):

$$0 = a(-5 + 2)^2 + 12 = 9a + 12 \implies a = -\frac{4}{3}$$

Watch: $a$ must be negative (maximum, not minimum). Positive $a$ would give a minimum — contradicts given maximum.
$f(x) = -\dfrac{4}{3}(x+2)^2 + 12$
↳ from STEP 1:$a = -\tfrac{4}{3}$
STEP 2/2 A1A1

Expand:

$$f(x) = -\frac{4}{3}(x^2 + 4x + 4) + 12 = -\frac{4}{3}x^2 - \frac{16}{3}x - \frac{16}{3} + \frac{36}{3}$$

$$f(x) = -\frac{4}{3}x^2 - \frac{16}{3}x + \frac{20}{3}$$

$\boxed{f(x) = -\dfrac{4}{3}x^2 - \dfrac{16}{3}x + \dfrac{20}{3}}$

(b) — Find equation of l₂ [3 marks] · 1 step

Given: $l_1: y = \tfrac{4}{5}x$; $l_2 \perp l_1$ through $(-5, 0)$.
STEP 1/1 M1M1A1

Strategy: Gradient of $l_1$ is $\tfrac{4}{5}$. Perpendicular gradient: multiply by $-1$ and invert.

$$m_{l_2} = -\frac{1}{4/5} = -\frac{5}{4}$$

Point-slope form through $(-5, 0)$:

$$y - 0 = -\frac{5}{4}(x - (-5)) = -\frac{5}{4}(x + 5)$$

$$y = -\frac{5}{4}x - \frac{25}{4}$$

Watch: Don't just negate or just invert — do both. Product of perpendicular gradients = $-1$: $(\tfrac{4}{5})(-\tfrac{5}{4}) = -1$ ✓
$\boxed{y = -\dfrac{5}{4}x - \dfrac{25}{4}}$

(c) — Define region R with inequalities [2 marks] · 1 step

From (a) & (b): $f(x) = -\tfrac{4}{3}x^2 - \tfrac{16}{3}x + \tfrac{20}{3}$; $l_1: y = \tfrac{4}{5}x$; $l_2: y = -\tfrac{5}{4}x - \tfrac{25}{4}$.
STEP 1/1 M1A1

Strategy: Region $R$ is enclosed by $C$, $l_1$, $l_2$. Check each boundary with a test point inside $R$.

Boundary 1 — curve C: $R$ is below the parabola $C$ (the curve opens downward; $R$ is inside the 'hill').

$$y \leq f(x) = -\frac{4}{3}x^2 - \frac{16}{3}x + \frac{20}{3}$$

Boundary 2 — line $l_1$: $R$ is below $l_1$. Test point $(0, -5)$: $-5 \leq \tfrac{4}{5}(0) = 0$ ✓

$$y \leq \frac{4}{5}x$$

Boundary 3 — line $l_2$: $R$ is above $l_2$. Test point $(0, -5)$: $-5 \geq -\tfrac{25}{4} = -6.25$ ✓

$$y \geq -\frac{5}{4}x - \frac{25}{4}$$

Watch: The most common error is writing $y \geq \tfrac{4}{5}x$ for the $l_1$ boundary. Region $R$ is below $l_1$ (between $l_1$ and the $x$-axis region), so the correct inequality is $y \leq \tfrac{4}{5}x$. Always test a point in the shaded region.
$\boxed{y \leq -\dfrac{4}{3}x^2 - \dfrac{16}{3}x + \dfrac{20}{3}, \quad y \leq \dfrac{4}{5}x, \quad y \geq -\dfrac{5}{4}x - \dfrac{25}{4}}$
