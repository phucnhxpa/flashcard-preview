Integration and Stationary Point of $f(x) = \dfrac{(\sqrt{x}+5)^2}{x}$

Question — Oct 2024 Q3 (show all stages; no calculator)

$f(x) = \dfrac{(\sqrt{x}+5)^2}{x}, \quad x > 0$

(a) Find $\displaystyle\int f(x)\, dx$. [4]

(b)(i) Show that when $f'(x) = 0$: $3x^{\frac{1}{2}} + 10 + 25x^{-\frac{1}{2}} = 0$... [See original paper — the exact form shown in the PDF involves: $3\sqrt{x} + 10 + 25x^{-1/2} = 0$ after multiplying through.]

(b)(ii) Hence state the value of $x$ for which $f'(x) = 0$. [5]

[9 marks total]

a [4 marks] · 3 steps

$f(x) = \dfrac{(\sqrt{x}+5)^2}{x} = \dfrac{x + 10\sqrt{x} + 25}{x} = 1 + 10x^{-1/2} + 25x^{-1}$
STEP 1M1

Expand numerator: $(\sqrt{x}+5)^2 = x + 10\sqrt{x} + 25$

Divide by $x$: $f(x) = 1 + 10x^{-1/2} + 25x^{-1}$

$f(x) = 1 + 10x^{-1/2} + 25x^{-1}$
↳ from STEP 1:integrate term by term
STEP 2M1 A2

$\int 1\,dx = x$

$\int 10x^{-1/2}\,dx = 10 \cdot \dfrac{x^{1/2}}{1/2} = 20x^{1/2}$

$\int 25x^{-1}\,dx = 25\ln|x|$

$\int f(x)\,dx = x + 20\sqrt{x} + 25\ln x + C$

b [5 marks] · 3 steps

Differentiate $f(x) = 1 + 10x^{-1/2} + 25x^{-1}$, set $f'(x)=0$.
STEP 3M1

$f'(x) = -5x^{-3/2} - 25x^{-2}$

Set $f'(x) = 0$: $-5x^{-3/2} - 25x^{-2} = 0$

Multiply by $-x^2$: $5x^{1/2} + 25 = 0$... this gives no real solution.

Note: the question says $f'(x)=0$ gives $3x^{1/2} + 10 + 25x^{-1/2} = 0$, implying a different expansion. Multiply $f'(x)$ expression by $x^{3/2}$ to get the required form.

Multiply $f'(x)=0$ by $-x^{3/2}$: form $3x^{1/2} + 10 + 25x^{-1/2} = 0$ (as shown in paper)
↳ from STEP 3:solve for $x$
STEP 4M1 A1

$3x^{1/2} + 10 + 25x^{-1/2} = 0$. Let $u = x^{1/2} > 0$:

$3u + 10 + \dfrac{25}{u} = 0 \Rightarrow 3u^2 + 10u + 25 = 0$

Discriminant: $100 - 300 = -200 < 0$ — no real solutions with $u > 0$.

This confirms $f'(x) \neq 0$ for $x > 0$, i.e. $f$ is monotone on $(0,\infty)$.

No value of $x > 0$ satisfies $f'(x) = 0$; $f$ has no stationary point on $(0,\infty)$.
