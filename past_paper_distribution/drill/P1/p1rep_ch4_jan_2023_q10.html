Question

The curve C has equation $y = x^3 - 6x^2 + 9x + 1$.

(a) Find the coordinates of the stationary points of C.

(b) Determine the nature of each stationary point.

(c) Sketch the curve C, showing the coordinates of the stationary points and the point where C crosses the y-axis.

[10 marks]

(a) Stationary Points [5 marks] · 3 steps

STEP 1/3 M1

Strategy: Find where $\frac{dy}{dx} = 0$ for stationary points

Formula: $\frac{d}{dx}(x^n) = nx^{n-1}$

$y = x^3 - 6x^2 + 9x + 1$

$\frac{dy}{dx} = 3x^2 - 12x + 9$

$\frac{dy}{dx} = 3x^2 - 12x + 9$
↳ from STEP 1:$\frac{dy}{dx} = 3x^2 - 12x + 9$
STEP 2/3 M1 A1

At stationary points: $3x^2 - 12x + 9 = 0$

Divide by 3: $x^2 - 4x + 3 = 0$

Factorise: $(x - 1)(x - 3) = 0$

$x = 1$ or $x = 3$

$x = 1$ or $x = 3$
↳ from STEP 2:$x = 1$ or $x = 3$
STEP 3/3 A1 A1

When $x = 1$:

$y = (1)^3 - 6(1)^2 + 9(1) + 1 = 1 - 6 + 9 + 1 = 5$

When $x = 3$:

$y = (3)^3 - 6(3)^2 + 9(3) + 1 = 27 - 54 + 27 + 1 = 1$

$\boxed{(1, 5) \text{ and } (3, 1)}$

(b) Classification [3 marks] · 2 steps

From previous: see above.
↳ from STEP 0:$\frac{dy}{dx} = 3x^2 - 12x + 9$
STEP 1/2 M1

Strategy: Use second derivative test to classify

$\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} = 6x - 12$

$\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} = 6x - 12$
↳ from STEP 1:$\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} = 6x - 12$
STEP 2/2 A1 A1

At $x = 1$: $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} = 6(1) - 12 = -6 < 0$

Since $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} < 0$, this is a maximum

At $x = 3$: $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} = 6(3) - 12 = 6 > 0$

Since $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} > 0$, this is a minimum

Watch: Negative second derivative means maximum (curve bends downward)
$\boxed{(1, 5) \text{ is a maximum; } (3, 1) \text{ is a minimum}}$

(c) Sketch [2 marks] · 1 step

From previous: see above.
↳ from STEP 0:Maximum at $(1, 5)$, Minimum at $(3, 1)$
STEP 1/1 B1 B1

Key features to include:

• y-intercept: When $x = 0$, $y = 0 - 0 + 0 + 1 = 1$, so $(0, 1)$

• Maximum point at $(1, 5)$

• Minimum point at $(3, 1)$

• Cubic with positive leading coefficient: starts bottom-left, ends top-right

Sketch should show:

• Curve passing through $(0, 1)$ on y-axis

• Rising to maximum at $(1, 5)$

• Falling to minimum at $(3, 1)$

• Rising again for $x > 3$

$\boxed{\text{Cubic curve with max at } (1,5), \text{ min at } (3,1), \text{ y-intercept } (0,1)}$
