Cubic $f(x)=(3x+20)(x+6)(2x-3)$ — Tangent and Intersections

Question — Jan 2023 Q10

Figure 4 shows a sketch of part of the curve $C$ with equation $y = f(x)$, where

$f(x) = (3x+20)(x+6)(2x-3)$

(a) Use the given information to state the values of $x$ for which $f(x) > 0$. [2]

(b) Expand $(3x+20)(x+6)(2x-3)$, writing your answer as a polynomial in simplest form. [3]

The straight line $\ell$ is the tangent to $C$ at the point where $C$ cuts the $y$-axis. Given that $\ell$ cuts $C$ at the point $P$,

(c) find, using algebra, the $x$ coordinate of $P$. (Solutions based on calculator technology are not acceptable.) [5]

[10 marks total]

a, b, c [10 marks] · 5 steps

Roots of $f(x)$: $x = -\tfrac{20}{3}$, $x = -6$, $x = \tfrac{3}{2}$. Cubic with positive leading coefficient.
STEP 1B2

(a) From the sketch (positive cubic), $f(x) > 0$ between the first and third roots (or to the right of the largest root).

$f(x) > 0$ when $-\tfrac{20}{3} < x < -6$ is wrong (roots in order: $-\tfrac{20}{3} \approx -6.67$, $-6$, $\tfrac{3}{2}$).

Positive cubic rises to $+\infty$: $f(x)>0$ for $-6.67 < x < -6$ and $x > \tfrac{3}{2}$.

$f(x)>0$ for $-\tfrac{20}{3} < x < -6$ and $x > \tfrac{3}{2}$
STEP 2M1 A2

(b) Expand step by step:

$(3x+20)(x+6) = 3x^2+18x+20x+120 = 3x^2+38x+120$

$(3x^2+38x+120)(2x-3) = 6x^3-9x^2+76x^2-114x+240x-360$

$= 6x^3+67x^2+126x-360$

$f(x) = 6x^3 + 67x^2 + 126x - 360$
↳ from STEP 2:find tangent at $y$-axis
STEP 3M1

At $x=0$: $f(0) = -360$; point is $(0, -360)$.

$f'(x) = 18x^2 + 134x + 126$; $f'(0) = 126$.

Tangent: $y = 126x - 360$

Tangent $\ell$: $y = 126x - 360$
↳ from STEP 3:find intersections of $\ell$ with $C$
STEP 4M1

Set $6x^3+67x^2+126x-360 = 126x-360$:

$6x^3 + 67x^2 = 0$

$x^2(6x+67) = 0$

$x=0$ (double, tangent point) or $x = -\dfrac{67}{6}$
STEP 5A1

$P$ is the other intersection: $x = -\dfrac{67}{6}$

$x$-coordinate of $P = -\dfrac{67}{6}$
