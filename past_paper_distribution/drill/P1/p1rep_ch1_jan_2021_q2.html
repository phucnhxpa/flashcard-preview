Tree Height Model — Simultaneous Equations

Question — Jan 2021 Q2

A tree was planted. Exactly 3 years after it was planted, the height of the tree was 2 m. Exactly 5 years after it was planted, the height of the tree was 2.4 m.

Given that the height, $H$ metres, of the tree, $t$ years after it was planted, can be modelled by the equation $H^3 = pt^2 + q$, where $p$ and $q$ are constants,

(a) find, to 3 significant figures where necessary, the value of $p$ and the value of $q$. [4]

Exactly $T$ years after the tree was planted, its height was 5 m.

(b) Find the value of $T$ according to the model, giving your answer to one decimal place. [2]

[6 marks total]

a [4 marks] · 4 steps

$H^3 = pt^2 + q$; when $t=3, H=2$; when $t=5, H=2.4$
STEP 1M1

Substitute both data points into $H^3 = pt^2 + q$:

At $t=3, H=2$: $\quad 2^3 = p(3^2) + q \Rightarrow 8 = 9p + q$

At $t=5, H=2.4$: $\quad (2.4)^3 = p(5^2) + q \Rightarrow 13.824 = 25p + q$

Two equations: $9p + q = 8$ and $25p + q = 13.824$
↳ from STEP 1:subtract to eliminate $q$
STEP 2dM1

Subtract equation 1 from equation 2:

$16p = 13.824 - 8 = 5.824$

$p = \dfrac{5.824}{16} = 0.364$

$p = 0.364$ (3 s.f.)
↳ from STEP 2:find $q$
STEP 3A1

Substitute $p = 0.364$ into $9p + q = 8$:

$q = 8 - 9(0.364) = 8 - 3.276 = 4.724$

$q = 4.72$ (3 s.f.)
STEP 4 (part b)M1 A1

When $H = 5$: $5^3 = 0.364T^2 + 4.724$

$125 = 0.364T^2 + 4.724$

$T^2 = \dfrac{120.276}{0.364} = 330.43...$

$T = \sqrt{330.43} \approx 18.178...$

$T = 18.2$ years (1 d.p.)
