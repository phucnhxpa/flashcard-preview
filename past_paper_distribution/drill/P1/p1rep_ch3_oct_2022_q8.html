P1 Oct 2022 Q8 (14m)

Question

A circle has centre O and radius 8 cm. Points A and B lie on the circle. The perpendicular from O to AB meets AB at M, where OM = 5 cm. The angle AOB = 2α radians.

(a) Show that cos α = 5/8.

(b) Find the area of the shaded region bounded by the chord AB and the minor arc AB.

(c) Find the perimeter of this shaded region.

[14 marks]

(a) [3 marks] · 1 step

STEP 1/1 M1 A1 A1

Strategy: Use the right triangle formed by the perpendicular bisector

Setup: In triangle OMA, angle OMA = 90°

The perpendicular OM bisects chord AB and bisects angle AOB

So angle AOM = α

Using the right triangle OMA:

$\cos\alpha = \frac{\text{adjacent}}{\text{hypotenuse}} = \frac{OM}{OA} = \frac{5}{8}$

Key: The perpendicular from centre to chord bisects both the chord AND the angle at centre
$\boxed{\cos\alpha = \frac{5}{8}}$ as required

(b) [6 marks] · 1 step

From previous: see above.
STEP 1/1 M1 A1 M1 A1 M1 A1

Strategy: Shaded area = Area of sector - Area of triangle OAB

Find α:

$\cos\alpha = \frac{5}{8}$

$\alpha = \cos^{-1}(0.625) = 0.8957...$ radians

So $2\alpha = 1.7914...$ radians

Area of sector OAB:

$A_{sector} = \frac{1}{2}r^2\theta = \frac{1}{2}(8)^2(2\alpha) = 32 \times 1.7914... = 57.33$ cm²

Find AM using Pythagoras:

$AM = \sqrt{OA^2 - OM^2} = \sqrt{64 - 25} = \sqrt{39}$

So $AB = 2\sqrt{39}$

Area of triangle OAB:

$A_{triangle} = \frac{1}{2} \times base \times height = \frac{1}{2} \times 2\sqrt{39} \times 5 = 5\sqrt{39} = 31.22$ cm²

Shaded area:

$A = 57.33 - 31.22 = 26.1$ cm²

$\boxed{26.1 \text{ cm}^2}$ (3 s.f.)

(c) [5 marks] · 1 step

From previous: see above.
STEP 1/1 M1 A1 M1 A1 A1

Strategy: Perimeter = arc AB + chord AB

Arc length AB:

$s = r\theta = 8 \times 2\alpha = 8 \times 1.7914... = 14.33$ cm

Chord length AB:

From part (b): $AB = 2\sqrt{39} = 12.49$ cm

Perimeter:

$P = 14.33 + 12.49 = 26.8$ cm

Watch: Make sure to use 2α (not α) for the full angle AOB in arc length calculation
$\boxed{26.8 \text{ cm}}$ (3 s.f.)
