Sector AOBX and Quadrilateral AOBC — Pond Design

Question — Oct 2022 Q8

Figure 2 shows the plan view of a design for a pond. The design consists of a sector $AOBX$ of a circle centre $O$ joined to a quadrilateral $AOBC$.

Given: $BC = 8$ m, $OA = OB = 3$ m, angle $AOB = \dfrac{2\pi}{3}$ radians, angle $BCA = \dfrac{\pi}{6}$ radians.

(a) Calculate (i) the exact area of sector $AOBX$; (ii) the exact perimeter of sector $AOBX$. [5]

(b) Calculate the exact area of triangle $AOB$. [2]

(c) Show that the length $AB = 3\sqrt{3}$ m. [2]

(d) Find the total surface area of the pond. Give your answer in m² correct to 2 significant figures. [5]

[14 marks total]

a – d [14 marks] · 5 steps

$r = OA = OB = 3$ m; $\theta_{AOB} = \frac{2\pi}{3}$; sector angle $= 2\pi - \frac{2\pi}{3} = \frac{4\pi}{3}$ (reflex sector AOBX contains the major arc).
STEP 1M1 A1

Part (a)(i): Sector $AOBX$ is the major sector (reflex angle $= 2\pi - \frac{2\pi}{3} = \frac{4\pi}{3}$).

Area $= \frac{1}{2}r^2\theta = \frac{1}{2}(9)\!\left(\frac{4\pi}{3}\right) = 6\pi$ m²

Area of sector $AOBX = 6\pi$ m²
STEP 2M1 A1 A1

Part (a)(ii): Perimeter of sector = arc $AXB$ + $OA$ + $OB$

Arc $= r\theta = 3 \times \frac{4\pi}{3} = 4\pi$ m

Perimeter $= 4\pi + 3 + 3 = 4\pi + 6$ m

Perimeter of sector $AOBX = (4\pi + 6)$ m (exact)
STEP 3M1 A1

Part (b): Area of $\triangle AOB = \frac{1}{2}r^2\sin\theta = \frac{1}{2}(9)\sin\!\left(\frac{2\pi}{3}\right)$

$= \frac{9}{2} \times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} = \dfrac{9\sqrt{3}}{4}$ m²

Area of $\triangle AOB = \dfrac{9\sqrt{3}}{4}$ m²
STEP 4M1 A1

Part (c): By the cosine rule:

$AB^2 = OA^2 + OB^2 - 2(OA)(OB)\cos\!\left(\frac{2\pi}{3}\right)$

$= 9 + 9 - 2(9)\!\left(-\frac{1}{2}\right) = 18 + 9 = 27$

$AB = \sqrt{27} = 3\sqrt{3}$ m ✓

$AB = 3\sqrt{3}$ m (shown)
STEP 5M2 A1

Part (d): Find area of $\triangle ABC$ (with $AB = 3\sqrt{3}$, $BC = 8$, angle $BCA = \pi/6$) using sine rule.

$\sin(\angle BAC)/BC = \sin(\angle BCA)/AB$, find $\angle ABC$, then area $= \frac{1}{2} \cdot AB \cdot BC \cdot \sin(\angle ABC)$.

Total area $= 6\pi + \frac{9\sqrt{3}}{4} + \text{Area}(\triangle ABC) \approx 33$ m².

Total surface area $\approx 33$ m² (2 s.f.)
