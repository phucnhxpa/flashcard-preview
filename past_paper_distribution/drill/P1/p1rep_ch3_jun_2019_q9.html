Trig Graphs: $\sin\theta$, $4\sin\theta$, $1+\sin\theta$, $\tan\theta$

Question — Jun 2019 Q9

Figure 3 shows a plot of the curve with equation $y = \sin\theta$, $0 \leq \theta \leq 360°$.

(a) State the coordinates of the minimum point on the curve with equation $y = 4\sin\theta$, $0 \leq \theta \leq 360°$. [2]

(b) On Diagram 1, sketch and label the curves (i) $y = 1 + \sin\theta$, $0 \leq \theta \leq 360°$    (ii) $y = \tan\theta$, $0 \leq \theta \leq 360°$. [2]

(c) Hence find the number of solutions of: (i) $\tan\theta = 1 + \sin\theta$ in $0 \leq \theta \leq 2160°$    (ii) $\tan\theta = 1 + \sin\theta$ in $0 \leq \theta \leq 1980°$. [3]

[7 marks total]

a, b, c [7 marks] · 5 steps

$y = \sin\theta$ has minimum at $(270°, -1)$; amplitude scales by factor of 4 for $4\sin\theta$.
STEP 1B2

Part (a): $y = 4\sin\theta$ has amplitude 4, so its minimum is 4 times the minimum of $\sin\theta$.

Minimum of $\sin\theta$ is $-1$ at $\theta = 270°$.

Minimum point: $(270°, -4)$
STEP 2B1

Part (b)(i): $y = 1 + \sin\theta$ is $\sin\theta$ shifted up 1 unit.

Range: $[0, 2]$. Passes through $(0°,1)$, min at $(270°,0)$, max at $(90°,2)$.

Sketch: same shape as $\sin\theta$, translated 1 unit upward.
STEP 3B1

Part (b)(ii): $y = \tan\theta$ has vertical asymptotes at $\theta = 90°$ and $\theta = 270°$.

Period $180°$. Passes through $(0°,0)$, $(180°,0)$, $(360°,0)$.

Two full branches of $\tan\theta$ in $[0°, 360°]$ with asymptotes at $90°$ and $270°$.
STEP 4M1 A1

Part (c): Count intersections of $\tan\theta$ and $1+\sin\theta$ per cycle ($360°$).

In $0°$ to $360°$: 2 intersections per period (one in each branch).

For $0 \leq \theta \leq 2160°$: that is $6$ complete periods $\Rightarrow 6 \times 2 = 12$ solutions.

(i) 12 solutions in $[0°, 2160°]$
STEP 5A1

For $0 \leq \theta \leq 1980°$: $1980/360 = 5.5$ cycles.

In 5 complete cycles: $5 \times 2 = 10$ solutions. In the half-cycle $[1800°, 1980°]$: 1 more intersection.

(ii) 11 solutions in $[0°, 1980°]$
