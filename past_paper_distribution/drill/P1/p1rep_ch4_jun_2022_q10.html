Normal to y=x² at P(3,9), find Q and area

Question

The curve C has equation y = x². The normal to C at the point P(3, 9) meets C again at point Q. Find the coordinates of Q and the area of the region bounded by the normal PQ and the curve C.

[[12] marks]

Solution [12 marks] · 9 steps

y = x², P(3, 9)
STEP 1/9Find the gradient of the curve at P M1

Strategy: Differentiate and substitute x = 3

Formula: $\frac{dy}{dx} = 2x$

At P(3, 9): $\frac{dy}{dx} = 2(3) = 6$

$\boxed{\text{Gradient of tangent} = 6}$
↳ from STEP 1:Tangent gradient = 6
STEP 2/9Find the gradient of the normal A1

Strategy: Normal is perpendicular to tangent

Formula: $m_{\text{normal}} = -\frac{1}{m_{\text{tangent}}}$

$m_{\text{normal}} = -\frac{1}{6}$

$\boxed{m_{\text{normal}} = -\frac{1}{6}}$
↳ from STEP 2:Normal gradient = -1/6, passes through (3, 9)
STEP 3/9Find the equation of the normal M1 A1

Strategy: Use point-gradient form

Formula: $y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$

$y - 9 = -\frac{1}{6}(x - 3)$

$y = -\frac{x}{6} + \frac{1}{2} + 9 = -\frac{x}{6} + \frac{19}{2}$

$\boxed{y = -\frac{x}{6} + \frac{19}{2}}$
↳ from STEP 3:Normal: y = -x/6 + 19/2, Curve: y = x²
STEP 4/9Find where normal meets curve again M1

Strategy: Substitute normal equation into curve equation

Formula: Set $x^2 = -\frac{x}{6} + \frac{19}{2}$

$x^2 + \frac{x}{6} - \frac{19}{2} = 0$

Multiply by 6: $6x^2 + x - 57 = 0$

$\boxed{6x^2 + x - 57 = 0}$
↳ from STEP 4:6x² + x - 57 = 0
STEP 5/9Solve the quadratic for x M1 A1

Strategy: Factorise (we know x = 3 is one root)

Formula: $(x - 3)(6x + 19) = 0$

$x = 3$ (point P) or $x = -\frac{19}{6}$ (point Q)

Check: 6(3)² + 3 - 57 = 54 + 3 - 57 = 0 ✓
$\boxed{x_Q = -\frac{19}{6}}$
↳ from STEP 5:x_Q = -19/6, y = x²
STEP 6/9Find the y-coordinate of Q A1

Strategy: Substitute x into y = x²

Formula: $y = \left(-\frac{19}{6}\right)^2$

$y = \frac{361}{36}$

$\boxed{Q = \left(-\frac{19}{6}, \frac{361}{36}\right)}$
↳ from STEP 6:Normal: y = -x/6 + 19/2, Curve: y = x², limits -19/6 to 3
STEP 7/9Set up the integral for the area M1

Strategy: Area = ∫(upper - lower) dx, normal is above curve between Q and P

Formula: $A = \int_{-19/6}^{3} \left[\left(-\frac{x}{6} + \frac{19}{2}\right) - x^2\right] dx$

$\boxed{A = \int_{-19/6}^{3} \left(-\frac{x}{6} + \frac{19}{2} - x^2\right) dx}$
↳ from STEP 7:Integrate -x/6 + 19/2 - x²
STEP 8/9Integrate the expression M1 A1

Strategy: Integrate term by term

Formula: $\int\left(-\frac{x}{6} + \frac{19}{2} - x^2\right)dx = -\frac{x^2}{12} + \frac{19x}{2} - \frac{x^3}{3}$

$\boxed{-\frac{x^2}{12} + \frac{19x}{2} - \frac{x^3}{3}}$
↳ from STEP 8:F(x) = -x²/12 + 19x/2 - x³/3, limits -19/6 to 3
STEP 9/9Evaluate the definite integral M1 A1

Strategy: Calculate F(3) - F(-19/6)

At x = 3: $-\frac{9}{12} + \frac{57}{2} - 9 = -\frac{3}{4} + \frac{57}{2} - 9 = \frac{-3 + 114 - 36}{4} = \frac{75}{4}$

At x = -19/6: $-\frac{361}{432} + \frac{19(-19/6)}{2} - \frac{(-19/6)^3}{3} = -\frac{361}{432} - \frac{361}{12} + \frac{6859}{648}$

Area $= \frac{75}{4} - \left(-\frac{361}{432} - \frac{361}{12} + \frac{6859}{648}\right) = \frac{15625}{216}$

Common error: wrong limits or sign errors in evaluation
$\boxed{\text{Area} = \frac{15625}{216}}$
