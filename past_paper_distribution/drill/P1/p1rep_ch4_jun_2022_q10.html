Normal at A is Also Tangent at B

Question — Jun 2022 Q10

Figure 5 shows a sketch of the curve $C$ with equation $y = \dfrac{2}{7}x^3 + \dfrac{1}{7}x^2 - \dfrac{5}{2}x + k$, where $k$ is a constant.

(a) Find $\dfrac{dy}{dx}$. [2]

The line $\ell$ is the normal to $C$ at the point $A$ with $x$ coordinate $-\dfrac{7}{2}$. Given that $\ell$ is also a tangent to $C$ at the point $B$,

(b) show that the $x$ coordinate of the point $B$ is a solution of $12x^2 + 4x - 33 = 0$. [4]

(c) Hence find the $x$ coordinate of $B$, justifying your answer. [2]

Given that the $y$ intercept of $\ell$ is $-1$,

(d) find the value of $k$. [4]

[12 marks total]

a – d [12 marks] · 5 steps

$y = \tfrac{2}{7}x^3 + \tfrac{1}{7}x^2 - \tfrac{5}{2}x + k$
STEP 1B2

(a) $\dfrac{dy}{dx} = \dfrac{6}{7}x^2 + \dfrac{2}{7}x - \dfrac{5}{2}$

$\dfrac{dy}{dx} = \dfrac{6}{7}x^2 + \dfrac{2}{7}x - \dfrac{5}{2}$
↳ from STEP 1:gradient of tangent at $A$, then normal
STEP 2M1

At $x = -\tfrac{7}{2}$: $\dfrac{dy}{dx} = \tfrac{6}{7} \cdot \tfrac{49}{4} + \tfrac{2}{7} \cdot (-\tfrac{7}{2}) - \tfrac{5}{2}$

$= \tfrac{294}{28} - 1 - \tfrac{5}{2} = \tfrac{21}{2} - 1 - \tfrac{5}{2} = \tfrac{21-2-5}{2} = \tfrac{14}{2} = 7$

Gradient of normal at $A = -\tfrac{1}{7}$

Normal gradient $= -\dfrac{1}{7}$
↳ from STEP 2:tangent at $B$ must have same gradient $-1/7$
STEP 3M1 A1

Set $\dfrac{dy}{dx} = -\dfrac{1}{7}$ at point $B$ (x-coord = $t$):

$\dfrac{6}{7}t^2 + \dfrac{2}{7}t - \dfrac{5}{2} = -\dfrac{1}{7}$

Multiply by 14: $12t^2 + 4t - 35 = -2$

$12t^2 + 4t - 33 = 0$ ✓

$12x^2 + 4x - 33 = 0$ (shown)
↳ from STEP 3:solve for $x_B$
STEP 4M1 A1

$12x^2 + 4x - 33 = 0$

$(6x+11)(2x-3) = 0 \Rightarrow x = -\tfrac{11}{6}$ or $x = \tfrac{3}{2}$

Since $x_A = -\tfrac{7}{2}$, $B \neq A$; both are valid candidates but $x = -\tfrac{11}{6}$ is the other solution (not $-7/2$).

$x_B = \dfrac{3}{2}$ or $x_B = -\dfrac{11}{6}$ (justify which is $B$, not $A$)
STEP 5M2 A1

(d) Normal line: $y = -\tfrac{1}{7}x - 1$ (since $y$-intercept $= -1$).

At $A$: $y_A = -\tfrac{1}{7}(-\tfrac{7}{2}) - 1 = \tfrac{1}{2} - 1 = -\tfrac{1}{2}$

From curve: $-\tfrac{1}{2} = \tfrac{2}{7}(-\tfrac{7}{2})^3 + \tfrac{1}{7}(-\tfrac{7}{2})^2 - \tfrac{5}{2}(-\tfrac{7}{2}) + k$

$= -\tfrac{49}{4} + \tfrac{7}{4} + \tfrac{35}{4} + k = -\tfrac{7}{4} + k$

$k = -\tfrac{1}{2} + \tfrac{7}{4} = \tfrac{5}{4}$

$k = \dfrac{5}{4}$
