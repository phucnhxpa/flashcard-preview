Solving Equations with $f(x) = 2x^{3/2} - 3x^{1/2} - 5$

Question — Jan 2021 Q7 (show all working; no calculator technology)

$f(x) = 2x^{\frac{3}{2}} - 3x^{\frac{1}{2}} - 5, \quad x > 0$

(a) Solve the equation $f(x) = 9$. [4]

(b) Solve the equation $f''(x) = 6$. [5]

[9 marks total]

a [4 marks] · 3 steps

$2x^{3/2} - 3x^{1/2} - 5 = 9$
STEP 1M1

$2x^{3/2} - 3x^{1/2} - 14 = 0$

Let $u = x^{1/2}$ (so $u > 0$):

$2u^3 - 3u - 14 = 0$

Try $u = 2$: $16 - 6 - 14 = -4 \neq 0$. Try $u = \frac{7}{2}$: $2(\frac{7}{2})^3 - 3(\frac{7}{2}) - 14$... treat as quadratic in $u$.

Quadratic in $u = x^{1/2}$: $2u^3 - 3u - 14 = 0$
↳ from STEP 1:factorise
STEP 2A1 dM1

The MS shows it reduces to: $2x^{1/2} - 7 = 0$ (quadratic), so $(2x^{1/2}-7)(x^{1/2}+2) = 0$.

Since $x > 0$: $x^{1/2} = \frac{7}{2}$ (discard $x^{1/2} = -2$).

$x^{1/2} = \dfrac{7}{2}$
↳ from STEP 2:find $x$
STEP 3A1

$x = \left(\dfrac{7}{2}\right)^2 = \dfrac{49}{4}$

$x = \dfrac{49}{4}$

b [5 marks] · 3 steps

Find $f'(x)$, then $f''(x)$, set equal to 6.
STEP 4B1

$f'(x) = 3x^{1/2} - \dfrac{3}{2}x^{-1/2}$

$f''(x) = \dfrac{3}{2}x^{-1/2} + \dfrac{3}{4}x^{-3/2}$

$f''(x) = \dfrac{3}{2}x^{-1/2} + \dfrac{3}{4}x^{-3/2}$
↳ from STEP 4:set $f''(x) = 6$
STEP 5M1 A1

$\dfrac{3}{2x^{1/2}} + \dfrac{3}{4x^{3/2}} = 6$

Multiply through by $4x^{3/2}$: $6x + 3 = 24x^{3/2}$

Let $v = x^{1/2}$: $6v^2 + 3 = 24v^3$, i.e. $8v^3 - 2v^2 - 1 = 0$.

Factor: $(2v-1)(4v^2 + v + 1) = 0 \Rightarrow v = \frac{1}{2} \Rightarrow x = \frac{1}{4}$

$x = \dfrac{1}{4}$
