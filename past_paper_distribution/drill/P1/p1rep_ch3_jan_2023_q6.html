P1 Jan 2023 Q6 — Stage Design: Sector + Triangles

Question

Figure 1 shows the plan view for the design of a stage.

The design consists of a sector $OBC$ of a circle, with centre $O$, joined to two congruent triangles $OAB$ and $ODC$.

Given that

  • angle $BOC = 2.4$ radians
  • area of sector $BOC = 40$ m²
  • $AOD$ is a straight line of length $12.5$ m

(a) find the radius of the sector, giving your answer, in m, to 2 decimal places,   [2]

(b) find the size of angle $AOB$, in radians, to 2 decimal places.   [1]

Hence find

(c) the total area of the stage, giving your answer, in m², to one decimal place,   [3]

(d) the total perimeter of the stage, giving your answer, in m, to one decimal place.   [4]

[10 marks]

a [4 marks] · 4 steps

Sector OAB with radius r and angle θ. The perimeter of the sector equals 3.2r.
STEP 1/4Write the formula for the perimeter of a sector M1

Strategy: The perimeter of a sector consists of two radii plus the arc length.

Formula (Arc Length): $s = r\theta$

Perimeter $= r + r + r\theta = 2r + r\theta$

$\text{Perimeter} = 2r + r\theta$
↳ from STEP 1:$\text{Perimeter} = 2r + r\theta$
STEP 2/4Set up the equation using the given perimeter A1

Strategy: Substitute the given perimeter value and solve for θ.

$2r + r\theta = 3.2r$

$2r + r\theta = 3.2r$
↳ from STEP 2:$2r + r\theta = 3.2r$
STEP 3/4Factor out r and simplify M1

Strategy: Divide both sides by r (since r > 0).

$r(2 + \theta) = 3.2r$

$2 + \theta = 3.2$

Common error: forgetting that r ≠ 0 is required to divide by r, but this is given implicitly as r is a radius.
$2 + \theta = 3.2$
↳ from STEP 3:$2 + \theta = 3.2$
STEP 4/4Solve for θ A1

Strategy: Subtract 2 from both sides.

$\theta = 3.2 - 2$

$\theta = 1.2$

$\boxed{\theta = 1.2 \text{ radians}}$

b [6 marks] · 6 steps

The radius r = 5 cm and angle θ = 1.2 radians.
STEP 1/6Write the formula for the area of a sector M1

Strategy: Use the sector area formula with angle in radians.

Formula (Sector Area): $A = \frac{1}{2}r^2\theta$

$A = \frac{1}{2}r^2\theta$
↳ from STEP 1:$A = \frac{1}{2}r^2\theta$
STEP 2/6Substitute the values to find the area A1

Strategy: Substitute r = 5 and θ = 1.2 into the formula.

$A = \frac{1}{2} \times 5^2 \times 1.2$

$A = \frac{1}{2} \times 25 \times 1.2$

$A = 12.5 \times 1.2$

$A = 15$
↳ from STEP 2:$A = 15$
STEP 3/6State the area with correct units A1

Strategy: Include appropriate units for area.

Always include units in your final answer — marks are often lost for missing units.
$\boxed{\text{Area} = 15 \text{ cm}^2}$
STEP 4/6Calculate the arc length M1

Strategy: Use the arc length formula.

Formula (Arc Length): $s = r\theta$

$s = 5 \times 1.2$

$s = 6 \text{ cm}$
↳ from STEP 4:$s = 6 \text{ cm}$
STEP 5/6Calculate the perimeter of the sector M1

Strategy: Add the arc length and two radii.

Perimeter $= 2r + s$

Perimeter $= 2(5) + 6$

Perimeter $= 10 + 6$

$\text{Perimeter} = 16$
↳ from STEP 5:$\text{Perimeter} = 16$
STEP 6/6State the perimeter with correct units A1

Strategy: Present the final answer with units.

$\boxed{\text{Perimeter} = 16 \text{ cm}}$
