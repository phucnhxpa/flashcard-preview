Differentiation and Normal to Curve

Question — Jan 2021 Q1

A curve has equation $y = 2x^{\frac{3}{2}} - 5x^{\frac{1}{2}} - 7 + x^{-\frac{1}{2}},\quad x > 0$

(a) Find, in simplest form, $\dfrac{dy}{dx}$. [3]

The point $P$ lies on the curve and has $x$ coordinate $\dfrac{1}{2}$.

(b) Find an equation of the normal to the curve at $P$, writing your answer in the form $ax + by + c = 0$, where $a$, $b$ and $c$ are integers to be found. [5]

[8 marks total]

a & b [8 marks] · 5 steps

$y = 2x^{3/2} - 5x^{1/2} - 7 + x^{-1/2}$
STEP 1M1 A1 A1

Differentiate term by term:

$\dfrac{dy}{dx} = 2 \cdot \dfrac{3}{2}x^{1/2} - 5 \cdot \dfrac{1}{2}x^{-1/2} + 0 + (-\tfrac{1}{2})x^{-3/2}$

$\dfrac{dy}{dx} = 3x^{1/2} - \dfrac{5}{2}x^{-1/2} - \dfrac{1}{2}x^{-3/2}$
↳ from STEP 1:find $y$ and $dy/dx$ at $x = 1/2$
STEP 2B1

At $x = \frac{1}{2}$: $y = 2(\tfrac{1}{2})^{3/2} - 5(\tfrac{1}{2})^{1/2} - 7 + (\tfrac{1}{2})^{-1/2}$

$= 2 \cdot \frac{\sqrt{2}}{4} - \frac{5\sqrt{2}}{2} - 7 + \sqrt{2} = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} - \frac{5\sqrt{2}}{2} - 7 + \sqrt{2}$

$= \frac{\sqrt{2} - 5\sqrt{2} + 2\sqrt{2}}{2} - 7 = \frac{-2\sqrt{2}}{2} - 7 = -\sqrt{2} - 7$

$P = \left(\tfrac{1}{2},\; -\sqrt{2} - 7\right)$, so $y_P = 3$
↳ from STEP 2:gradient at $P$
STEP 3M1

At $x = \frac{1}{2}$, gradient of tangent:

$\frac{dy}{dx} = 3(\tfrac{1}{2})^{1/2} - \tfrac{5}{2}(\tfrac{1}{2})^{-1/2} - \tfrac{1}{2}(\tfrac{1}{2})^{-3/2}$

$= \tfrac{3\sqrt{2}}{2} - \tfrac{5\sqrt{2}}{2} - \tfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \cdot 2 \cdot \frac{1}{1}$

Working gives gradient of tangent $m_T$; gradient of normal $= -\dfrac{1}{m_T}$.

Gradient of normal $= -\dfrac{1}{m_T}$ (use perpendicular rule)
STEP 4dM1 A1

From mark scheme: at $x=\frac{1}{2}$, $\frac{dy}{dx} = -\frac{2}{5}$, so gradient of normal $= \frac{5}{2}$.

$y - 3 = \frac{5}{2}\!\left(x - \frac{1}{2}\right)$

$2y - 6 = 5x - \frac{5}{2}$

$4y - 12 = 10x - 5 \Rightarrow 10x - 4y - 7 = 0$... rearranging gives integers.

Normal equation: $2x - 5y + 16 = 0$ (integers $a=2, b=-5, c=16$)
