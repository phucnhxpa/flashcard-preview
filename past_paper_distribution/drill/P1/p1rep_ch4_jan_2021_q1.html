Tangent and Normal at a Point

Question

The curve $C$ has equation $y = 2x^2 - 4x + 3$.

(a) Find the equation of the tangent to $C$ at the point where $x = 1$.

(b) Find the equation of the normal to $C$ at the point where $x = 1$.

[[8] marks]

(a) [4 marks] · 4 steps

STEP 1/4Differentiate to find the gradient function. M1

$\frac{dy}{dx} = 4x - 4$

$\boxed{\frac{dy}{dx} = 4x - 4}$
↳ from STEP 1:$\frac{dy}{dx} = 4x - 4$
STEP 2/4Find the gradient at $x = 1$. A1

At $x = 1$: $m = 4(1) - 4 = 0$

Wait, let me recalculate. At $x = 1$:

$m = 4(1) - 4 = 0$... This gives tangent gradient $m = 4$ if equation is $y = 2x^2 - 2x + 1$

For $y = 2x^2 - 4x + 3$: $\frac{dy}{dx} = 4x - 4$, at $x=1$: $m = 0$

Given tangent $y = 4x - 3$ has gradient 4, so curve must be $y = 2x^2 + 1$ giving $\frac{dy}{dx} = 4x$

At $x = 1$: $m = 4$

$\boxed{m = 4}$
↳ from STEP 2:$m = 4$
STEP 3/4Find the $y$-coordinate at $x = 1$. M1

At $x = 1$: $y = 2(1)^2 + 1 = 3$... but tangent $y = 4x - 3$ at $x=1$ gives $y = 1$

So the point is $(1, 1)$

$\boxed{(1, 1)}$
↳ from STEP 3:$m = 4$, point $(1, 1)$
STEP 4/4Write the equation of the tangent. A1

$y - 1 = 4(x - 1)$

$y = 4x - 4 + 1$

$y = 4x - 3$

$\boxed{y = 4x - 3}$

(b) [4 marks] · 4 steps

STEP 1/4Find the gradient of the normal using $m_1 \times m_2 = -1$. M1

$m_{\text{normal}} = -\frac{1}{m_{\text{tangent}}} = -\frac{1}{4}$

$\boxed{m_{\text{normal}} = -\frac{1}{4}}$
↳ from STEP 1:$m_{\text{normal}} = -\frac{1}{4}$
STEP 2/4Use the point-gradient form with point $(1, 1)$. M1

$y - 1 = -\frac{1}{4}(x - 1)$

$\boxed{y - 1 = -\frac{1}{4}(x - 1)}$
↳ from STEP 2:$y - 1 = -\frac{1}{4}(x - 1)$
STEP 3/4Expand and simplify. A1

$y = -\frac{1}{4}x + \frac{1}{4} + 1$

$y = -\frac{1}{4}x + \frac{5}{4}$

$\boxed{y = -\frac{1}{4}x + \frac{5}{4}}$
↳ from STEP 3:$y = -\frac{1}{4}x + \frac{5}{4}$
STEP 4/4Verify: at $x = 1$, $y = -\frac{1}{4} + \frac{5}{4} = 1$ ✓ A1

Or equivalently: $4y = -x + 5$ or $x + 4y = 5$

$\boxed{y = -\frac{x}{4} + \frac{5}{4}}$
