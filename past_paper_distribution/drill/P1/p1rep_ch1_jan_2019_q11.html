Curve Sketching and Intersection Points

Question — Jan 2019 Q11

(a) On Diagram 1, sketch the graphs of

   (i) $y = x(3 - x)$

   (ii) $y = x(x-2)(5-x)$

showing clearly the coordinates of the points where the curves cross the coordinate axes. [4]

(b) Show that the $x$ coordinates of the points of intersection of $y = x(3-x)$ and $y = x(x-2)(5-x)$ are given by the solutions to the equation $x(x^2 - 8x + 13) = 0$. [3]

(c) The point $P$ lies on both curves. Given that $P$ lies in the first quadrant, find, using algebra and showing your working, the exact coordinates of $P$. [5]

[12 marks total]

b & c [8 marks] · 5 steps

Set $x(3-x) = x(x-2)(5-x)$ to find intersections.
STEP 1 M1

Set the two curves equal:

$x(3-x) = x(x-2)(5-x)$

Rearrange: $x(3-x) - x(x-2)(5-x) = 0$

Factor out $x$: $x[(3-x) - (x-2)(5-x)] = 0$

Either $x = 0$ or $(3-x) = (x-2)(5-x)$
↳ from STEP 1:expand bracket
STEP 2 M1

Expand $(x-2)(5-x) = 5x - x^2 - 10 + 2x = 7x - x^2 - 10$

So: $3 - x = 7x - x^2 - 10$

$x^2 - 8x + 13 = 0$

Full equation: $x(x^2 - 8x + 13) = 0$ ✓
↳ from STEP 2:solve quadratic for P in first quadrant
STEP 3 M1

Solve $x^2 - 8x + 13 = 0$ using quadratic formula:

$x = \dfrac{8 \pm \sqrt{64 - 52}}{2} = \dfrac{8 \pm \sqrt{12}}{2}$

$x = 4 \pm \sqrt{3}$

$x = 4 - \sqrt{3}$ (first quadrant, $\approx 2.27$) or $x = 4 + \sqrt{3} \approx 5.73$
↳ from STEP 3:find y-coordinate at P
STEP 4 M1 A1

For $P$ in first quadrant: $x = 4 - \sqrt{3}$

Use $y = x(3-x)$: $y = (4-\sqrt{3})(3-(4-\sqrt{3}))$

$= (4-\sqrt{3})(\sqrt{3}-1)$

$= 4\sqrt{3} - 4 - 3 + \sqrt{3} = 5\sqrt{3} - 7$

$P = \left(4-\sqrt{3},\; 5\sqrt{3}-7\right)$
