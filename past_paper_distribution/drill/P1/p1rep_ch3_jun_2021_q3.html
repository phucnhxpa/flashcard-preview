Jun 2021 Q3 — Show equation from cosine rule, solve for exact sides, find exact area [9m]

Question

Figure 1 shows the plan view of a flower bed in the shape of triangle $ABC$ with:

  • $AB = p$ metres
  • $AC = q$ metres
  • $BC = 2\sqrt{2}$ metres
  • angle $BAC = 60°$

(a) Show that $p^2 + q^2 - pq = 8$. [2]

Given that side $AC$ is 2 metres longer than side $AB$, use algebra to find

(b)(i) the exact value of $p$,

(b)(ii) the exact value of $q$. [5]

Using the answers to part (b),

(c) calculate the exact area of the flower bed. [2]

[9 marks]

(a) — Show p² + q² − pq = 8 [2 marks] · 1 step

Triangle $ABC$: $AB = p$, $AC = q$, $BC = 2\sqrt{2}$, $\angle BAC = 60°$
STEP 1/1 M1A1*

Formula (Cosine Rule): $a^2 = b^2 + c^2 - 2bc\cos A$

Here $a = BC = 2\sqrt{2}$, $b = p$ (side $AB$), $c = q$ (side $AC$), $A = 60°$:

$$(2\sqrt{2})^2 = p^2 + q^2 - 2pq\cos 60°$$

$$8 = p^2 + q^2 - 2pq \cdot \frac{1}{2}$$

$$8 = p^2 + q^2 - pq \quad \checkmark$$

Watch: This is a 'show that' — you must show at least one intermediate line before stating the result. $(2\sqrt{2})^2 = 4 \times 2 = 8$ is a key calculation to show.
$p^2 + q^2 - pq = 8$ ✓ (shown)

(b) — Find exact values of p and q [5 marks] · 2 steps

From (a): $p^2 + q^2 - pq = 8$; given $q = p + 2$.
STEP 1/2 M1A1

Given: $AC$ is 2 m longer than $AB$, so $q = p + 2$.

Substitute into $p^2 + q^2 - pq = 8$:

$$p^2 + (p+2)^2 - p(p+2) = 8$$

$$p^2 + p^2 + 4p + 4 - p^2 - 2p = 8$$

$$p^2 + 2p + 4 = 8$$

$$p^2 + 2p - 4 = 0$$

Watch: Expand $(p+2)^2 = p^2 + 4p + 4$ and $p(p+2) = p^2 + 2p$ carefully — sign errors here are common.
$p^2 + 2p - 4 = 0$
↳ from STEP 1:$p^2 + 2p - 4 = 0$
STEP 2/2 M1 B1(A1) A1cso

Formula (Quadratic Formula): $p = \dfrac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$ with $a=1, b=2, c=-4$

$$p = \frac{-2 \pm \sqrt{4 + 16}}{2} = \frac{-2 \pm \sqrt{20}}{2} = \frac{-2 \pm 2\sqrt{5}}{2} = -1 \pm \sqrt{5}$$

Since $p$ is a length, $p > 0$. Reject $p = -1 - \sqrt{5} < 0$.

$$p = -1 + \sqrt{5} = \sqrt{5} - 1$$

$$q = p + 2 = (\sqrt{5} - 1) + 2 = \sqrt{5} + 1$$

Watch: The question says 'exact value' — do NOT use a calculator to give decimal approximations. Keep $\sqrt{5}$ in the answer. Also: a length cannot be negative, so always reject the negative root.
$\boxed{p = \sqrt{5} - 1 \text{ m}, \quad q = \sqrt{5} + 1 \text{ m}}$

(c) — Find exact area of triangle ABC [2 marks] · 1 step

$p = \sqrt{5} - 1$, $q = \sqrt{5} + 1$, $\angle BAC = 60°$
STEP 1/1 M1A1

Formula (Area): $\text{Area} = \tfrac{1}{2}pq\sin A$

$$\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2}(\sqrt{5} - 1)(\sqrt{5} + 1)\sin 60°$$

$(\sqrt{5}-1)(\sqrt{5}+1)$ is a difference of two squares:

$$(\sqrt{5})^2 - 1^2 = 5 - 1 = 4$$

$$\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times 4 \times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} = \frac{4\sqrt{3}}{4} = \sqrt{3}$$

Watch: The key insight is recognising $(\sqrt{5}-1)(\sqrt{5}+1) = 5-1 = 4$ — a difference of squares. This gives a clean exact answer. Do NOT approximate $\sqrt{3} \approx 1.73$; the question asks for the exact area.
$\boxed{\text{Area} = \sqrt{3} \text{ m}^2}$
