Maximum at $(\frac{\pi}{6}, 5)$, minimum at $(\frac{7\pi}{6}, -1)$
STEP 1/6 M1

Strategy: The amplitude is half the vertical distance between maximum and minimum.

Formula (Amplitude): $A = \frac{\text{max} - \text{min}}{2}$

$A = \frac{5 - (-1)}{2} = \frac{6}{2}$

The amplitude measures the distance from the midline to the peak, not peak to trough.
$\boxed{A = 3}$
↳ from STEP 1:$A = 3$
STEP 2/6 A1

Strategy: The midline is the average of maximum and minimum values.

Formula (Midline): $D = \frac{\text{max} + \text{min}}{2}$

$D = \frac{5 + (-1)}{2} = \frac{4}{2}$

D shifts the entire sine curve up or down from the x-axis.
$\boxed{D = 2}$
↳ from STEP 2:$A = 3$, $D = 2$
STEP 3/6 M1

Strategy: The distance from maximum to minimum is half the period.

Formula (Period relationship): $\text{Period} = \frac{2\pi}{B}$

Distance from max to min: $\frac{7\pi}{6} - \frac{\pi}{6} = \frac{6\pi}{6} = \pi$

So period $= 2\pi$ (twice the max-to-min distance)

Common error: using max-to-min as the full period instead of half.
$\text{Period} = 2\pi$
↳ from STEP 3:Period $= 2\pi$
STEP 4/6 A1

Strategy: Rearrange the period formula to find B.

Formula (B from period): $B = \frac{2\pi}{\text{Period}}$

$B = \frac{2\pi}{2\pi} = 1$

B is the frequency coefficient; B=1 means standard sine period.
$\boxed{B = 1}$
↳ from STEP 4:$A = 3$, $B = 1$, $D = 2$
STEP 5/6 M1

Strategy: Standard sine has maximum at $\frac{\pi}{2}$. Use this to find the phase shift.

Formula (Phase at maximum): $Bx + C = \frac{\pi}{2}$ at the maximum

At $x = \frac{\pi}{6}$: $(1)(\frac{\pi}{6}) + C = \frac{\pi}{2}$

$C = \frac{\pi}{2} - \frac{\pi}{6} = \frac{3\pi - \pi}{6}$

The argument of sine equals $\frac{\pi}{2}$ at a maximum since $\sin(\frac{\pi}{2}) = 1$.
$C = \frac{2\pi}{6}$
↳ from STEP 5:$A = 3$, $B = 1$, $D = 2$, $C = \frac{2\pi}{6}$
STEP 6/6 A1

Strategy: Simplify and check that $-\pi < C \leq \pi$.

$C = \frac{2\pi}{6} = \frac{\pi}{3}$

Check: $-\pi < \frac{\pi}{3} \leq \pi$ ✓

Final equation: $y = 3\sin(x + \frac{\pi}{3}) + 2$

Always verify C is in the specified range; add or subtract $2\pi$ if needed.
$\boxed{C = \frac{\pi}{3}}$
