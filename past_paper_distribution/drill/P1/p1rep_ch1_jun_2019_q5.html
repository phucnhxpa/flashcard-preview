Solving Equations with Fractional Exponents

Question — Jun 2019 Q5

(a) Find, using algebra, all real solutions of $2x^{\frac{1}{3}} + 16x^{\frac{2}{3}} - 15x = 0$. [3]

(b) Hence find all real solutions of $2(y-5)^{\frac{1}{6}} + 16(y-5)^{\frac{1}{3}} - 15(y-5)^{\frac{1}{2}} = 0$. [4]

[7 marks total]

a [3 marks] · 3 steps

$2x^{1/3} + 16x^{2/3} - 15x = 0$. Factor out $x^{1/3}$.
STEP 1M1

Factor $x^{1/3}$ from all terms:

$x^{1/3}(2 + 16x^{1/3} - 15x^{2/3}) = 0$

Let $u = x^{1/3}$, so $x^{2/3} = u^2$:

$x^{1/3}(2 + 16u - 15u^2) = 0$

Either $x=0$ or $15u^2 - 16u - 2 = 0$ (note sign: $-(15u^2-16u-2)$)
↳ from STEP 1:solve quadratic in $u$
STEP 2M1

$-15u^2 + 16u + 2 = 0 \Rightarrow 15u^2 - 16u - 2 = 0$

$(15u + 2)(u - \frac{6}{5}) = 0$... or use quadratic formula:

$u = \dfrac{16 \pm \sqrt{256 + 120}}{30} = \dfrac{16 \pm \sqrt{376}}{30}$

Actually factor: $(5u+2)(3u-1)=0 \Rightarrow u = -\tfrac{2}{5}$ or $u = \tfrac{1}{3}$

$u = x^{1/3} = -\tfrac{2}{5}$ or $u = \tfrac{1}{3}$
↳ from STEP 2:cube to find $x$
STEP 3A1

$x^{1/3} = -\tfrac{2}{5} \Rightarrow x = \left(-\tfrac{2}{5}\right)^3 = -\tfrac{8}{125}$

$x^{1/3} = \tfrac{1}{3} \Rightarrow x = \left(\tfrac{1}{3}\right)^3 = \tfrac{1}{27}$

$x = 0,\quad x = -\dfrac{8}{125},\quad x = \dfrac{1}{27}$

b [4 marks] · 2 steps

Use part (a) with substitution $(y-5)^{1/6} = x^{1/3}$, i.e. let $x = (y-5)^{1/2}$.
STEP 4M1

Let $x = (y-5)^{1/2}$. Then the equation matches part (a) exactly.

So $(y-5)^{1/2} = 0$, $-\tfrac{8}{125}$, or $\tfrac{1}{27}$.

Since $(y-5)^{1/2} \geq 0$, we discard $-\tfrac{8}{125}$.

$(y-5)^{1/2} = 0$ or $(y-5)^{1/2} = \tfrac{1}{27}$
↳ from STEP 4:find $y$
STEP 5A1

$(y-5)^{1/2} = 0 \Rightarrow y = 5$

$(y-5)^{1/2} = \tfrac{1}{27} \Rightarrow y - 5 = \tfrac{1}{729} \Rightarrow y = 5 + \tfrac{1}{729}$

$y = 5$ and $y = 5 + \dfrac{1}{729}$
