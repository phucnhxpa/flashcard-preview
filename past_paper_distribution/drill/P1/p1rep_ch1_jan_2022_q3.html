Surds — Expanding and Solving Linear Equations

Question — Jan 2022 Q3 (show all stages; no calculator)

(i) $f(x) = (x + \sqrt{2})^2 + (3x - 5\sqrt{8})^2$

Express $f(x)$ in the form $ax^2 + bx\sqrt{2} + c$ where $a$, $b$ and $c$ are integers to be found. [3]

(ii) Solve the equation $3(4y - 3\sqrt{3}) = 5y + \sqrt{3}$, giving your answer in the form $p + q\sqrt{3}$ where $p$ and $q$ are simplified fractions to be found. [4]

[7 marks total]

i [3 marks] · 3 steps

$f(x) = (x+\sqrt{2})^2 + (3x - 5\sqrt{8})^2$. Note $\sqrt{8} = 2\sqrt{2}$.
STEP 1M1

Simplify: $5\sqrt{8} = 5 \cdot 2\sqrt{2} = 10\sqrt{2}$

Expand $(x+\sqrt{2})^2 = x^2 + 2x\sqrt{2} + 2$

$(x+\sqrt{2})^2 = x^2 + 2x\sqrt{2} + 2$
↳ from STEP 1:expand second bracket
STEP 2M1

Expand $(3x - 10\sqrt{2})^2 = 9x^2 - 60x\sqrt{2} + 200$

$(3x-10\sqrt{2})^2 = 9x^2 - 60x\sqrt{2} + 200$
↳ from STEP 2:combine
STEP 3A1

$f(x) = (x^2 + 2x\sqrt{2} + 2) + (9x^2 - 60x\sqrt{2} + 200)$

$= 10x^2 - 58x\sqrt{2} + 202$

$f(x) = 10x^2 - 58x\sqrt{2} + 202$, so $a=10, b=-58, c=202$

ii [4 marks] · 3 steps

Solve $3(4y - 3\sqrt{3}) = 5y + \sqrt{3}$
STEP 4M1

Expand: $12y - 9\sqrt{3} = 5y + \sqrt{3}$

Collect $y$ terms: $12y - 5y = \sqrt{3} + 9\sqrt{3}$

$7y = 10\sqrt{3}$
↳ from STEP 4:divide and express in form $p + q\sqrt{3}$
STEP 5A1

$y = \dfrac{10\sqrt{3}}{7} = 0 + \dfrac{10}{7}\sqrt{3}$

$y = 0 + \dfrac{10}{7}\sqrt{3}$, so $p = 0,\; q = \dfrac{10}{7}$
