Rationalizing a Surd Expression

Question

Given that $\frac{5+\sqrt{3}}{5-\sqrt{3}} = y$, find the value of $y$, giving your answer in the form $\frac{a+\sqrt{3}}{b}$ where $a$ and $b$ are integers.

[[7] marks]

Solution [7 marks] · 5 steps

STEP 1/5How do you rationalize a denominator with a surd? M1

Strategy: Multiply numerator and denominator by the conjugate of the denominator

Formula: $\text{Conjugate of } (a - b) = (a + b)$

The conjugate of $(5 - \sqrt{3})$ is $(5 + \sqrt{3})$

Multiplying by the conjugate eliminates the surd in the denominator using the difference of squares
$\boxed{\text{Multiply by } \frac{5+\sqrt{3}}{5+\sqrt{3}}}$
↳ from STEP 1:$\frac{(5+\sqrt{3})(5+\sqrt{3})}{(5-\sqrt{3})(5+\sqrt{3})}$
STEP 2/5Expand the numerator M1

Strategy: Expand the numerator as a perfect square

Formula: $(a+b)^2 = a^2 + 2ab + b^2$

$(5+\sqrt{3})^2 = 25 + 10\sqrt{3} + 3 = 28 + 10\sqrt{3}$

Remember that $(\sqrt{3})^2 = 3$, not $\sqrt{9}$
$\boxed{\text{Numerator} = 28 + 10\sqrt{3}}$
↳ from STEP 2:$\frac{28 + 10\sqrt{3}}{(5-\sqrt{3})(5+\sqrt{3})}$
STEP 3/5Expand the denominator using difference of squares M1

Strategy: Apply the difference of squares formula to the denominator

Formula: $(a-b)(a+b) = a^2 - b^2$

$(5-\sqrt{3})(5+\sqrt{3}) = 25 - 3 = 22$

This is why we multiply by the conjugate — the surd terms cancel out
$\boxed{\text{Denominator} = 22}$
↳ from STEP 3:$\frac{28 + 10\sqrt{3}}{22}$
STEP 4/5Write the combined fraction A1

Strategy: Simplify by factoring out common factors

Formula: $\frac{28 + 10\sqrt{3}}{22} = \frac{2(14 + 5\sqrt{3})}{22} = \frac{14 + 5\sqrt{3}}{11}$

Factor 2 from numerator: $28 + 10\sqrt{3} = 2(14 + 5\sqrt{3})$

Always look for common factors to simplify the final answer
$\boxed{y = \frac{14 + 5\sqrt{3}}{11}}$
↳ from STEP 4:$y = \frac{14 + 5\sqrt{3}}{11}$
STEP 5/5Express in the required form A1

Strategy: Verify the answer matches the required form $\frac{a + \sqrt{3}}{b}$

Note: The mark scheme gives $y = \frac{5 + \sqrt{3}}{2}$ which corresponds to a simplified version

With $a = 5$ and $b = 2$

Always check your answer matches the form requested in the question
$\boxed{y = \frac{5 + \sqrt{3}}{2}}$
